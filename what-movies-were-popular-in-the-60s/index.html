What Movies Were Popular In The 60S

Welcome back to another blog post! Today, we're going to take a trip down memory lane and talk about the movies that were popular in the 60s. This era was a golden age of film, with many iconic movies that still hold up today. In this post, we'll cover the top movies of the 60s, why they were so popular, and where you can watch them today.

Table of Contents

  • Top Movies of the 60s
  • Why Were They Popular?

Top Movies of the 60s

The 60s were a great time for movies, with many classics that still hold up today. Here are some of the most popular movies of the decade:

Psycho (1960)

Psycho is a horror movie directed by Alfred Hitchcock that's considered one of the greatest films of all time. It stars Anthony Perkins and Janet Leigh and tells the story of a woman who stops at a motel on her way to meet her lover, only to be murdered by the motel's owner.

The Sound of Music (1965)

The Sound of Music is a musical drama film directed by Robert Wise and starring Julie Andrews and Christopher Plummer. It's based on the true story of the von Trapp family and their escape from Nazi-occupied Austria.

Dr. Strangelove or: How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love the Bomb (1964)

Dr. Strangelove is a satirical black comedy film directed by Stanley Kubrick and starring Peter Sellers. It's about a nuclear crisis between the United States and the Soviet Union that escalates into a full-blown war.

Bonnie and Clyde (1967)

Bonnie and Clyde is a crime film directed by Arthur Penn and starring Warren Beatty and Faye Dunaway. It's based on the true story of Bonnie Parker and Clyde Barrow, who were notorious criminals during the Great Depression.

2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)

2001: A Space Odyssey is a science fiction film directed by Stanley Kubrick. It's about a voyage to Jupiter with the sentient computer HAL after the discovery of a mysterious black monolith affecting human evolution.

Why Were They Popular?

The movies of the 60s were popular for several reasons. For one, they offered a form of escapism during a tumultuous decade marked by political and social upheaval. Many of these movies were also groundbreaking in terms of storytelling, cinematography, and special effects, which helped to draw audiences in.

Where Can You Watch Them Today?

If you want to watch any of the movies on this list, you have several options. Many of these films are available to stream on popular platforms like Netflix, Hulu, and Amazon Prime. You can also rent or buy them on platforms like Google Play, iTunes, and YouTube.

Additionally, many of these films have been released on Blu-ray and DVD, so you can add them to your personal collection. You can find them at most major retailers like Best Buy, Target, and Walmart.

Conclusion

That's it for our look at the movies that were popular in the 60s. Hopefully, this post has given you some insight into why these films were so beloved and where you can watch them today. If you haven't seen any of these classics yet, we highly recommend giving them a watch!
