Wedding Dresses For 50 Year Old Woman A Guide To Finding The Perfect Dress

Written by Petter Oct 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a 50-year-old woman who's getting married, you might be wondering what kind of wedding dress to wear. After all, you want to look and feel your best on your special day. Fortunately, there are plenty of options available that will flatter your figure and suit your personal style. In this guide, we'll explore some of the best wedding dresses for 50-year-old women, as well as tips for choosing the right dress and caring for your hair on your big day.

How to Choose the Right Wedding Dress

When choosing a wedding dress, there are several factors to consider. First, you'll want to think about the style of the dress. Some popular options for 50-year-old women include A-line dresses, sheath dresses, and tea-length dresses. These styles are timeless and elegant, and they flatter a variety of body types.

Another important factor to consider is the fabric of the dress. Lighter fabrics like chiffon, tulle, and lace can be flattering and comfortable, especially if you're getting married in the summer. Heavier fabrics like satin and taffeta can also be beautiful, but they may be more suited to a winter wedding.

Finally, you'll want to consider the color of the dress. While white is a traditional color for wedding dresses, many 50-year-old women prefer to wear dresses in softer shades like blush, ivory, or champagne. These colors can be more flattering and less harsh than pure white.

Step by Step Guide to Finding the Perfect Wedding Dress

Now that you know what factors to consider when choosing a wedding dress, here's a step-by-step guide to finding the perfect dress:

Step 1: Consider Your Personal Style

Think about the clothes you typically wear and the styles that make you feel confident and comfortable. Use this as a starting point when shopping for a wedding dress.

Step 2: Research Different Styles

Look online and in bridal magazines for inspiration. Make note of the styles and fabrics that appeal to you.

Step 3: Try on Dresses

Visit bridal shops and try on a variety of dresses. Don't be afraid to try on styles that you wouldn't normally wear. You might be surprised by what looks good on you!

Step 4: Bring a Friend or Family Member

Having someone else's opinion can be helpful when choosing a wedding dress. Bring a trusted friend or family member with you to help you make your decision.

Tips for Choosing the Right Wedding Dress

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a wedding dress:

Tip 1: Focus on Fit

A well-fitting dress can make all the difference. Make sure the dress you choose fits you properly and flatters your figure.

Tip 2: Don't Be Afraid to Alter

If you find a dress you love but it doesn't fit perfectly, don't be afraid to have it altered. A skilled seamstress can make small adjustments to ensure the dress fits you like a glove.

Tip 3: Accessorize Thoughtfully

Accessories can make or break a wedding dress. Choose accessories that complement the style of your dress and enhance your overall look.

FAQ About Wedding Dresses for 50 Year Old Woman

Q: What kind of wedding dress is best for a 50-year-old woman?

A: A-line dresses, sheath dresses, and tea-length dresses are all great options for 50-year-old women. These styles are timeless and elegant, and they flatter a variety of body types.

Q: What color wedding dress should a 50-year-old woman wear?

A: While white is a traditional color for wedding dresses, many 50-year-old women prefer to wear dresses in softer shades like blush, ivory, or champagne. These colors can be more flattering and less harsh than pure white.

Q: Should a 50-year-old woman wear a veil with her wedding dress?

A: Whether or not to wear a veil is a personal preference. If you decide to wear a veil, choose a style that complements the style of your dress.

Hair Care Tips for Your Wedding Day

In addition to choosing the right wedding dress, it's important to take care of your hair on your big day. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

Tip 1: Plan Ahead

Make sure you have plenty of time to get your hair done on your wedding day. Schedule a trial run with your hairstylist ahead of time to make sure you're happy with the look.

Tip 2: Don't Wash Your Hair on Your Wedding Day

Washing your hair on your wedding day can make it harder for your stylist to work with. Instead, wash your hair the night before and use a light conditioner.

Tip 3: Use Heat-Protectant Products

If you'll be using hot tools like a curling iron or flat iron on your hair, be sure to use heat-protectant products to prevent damage.

Tip 4: Consider Hair Accessories

A hair accessory like a flower crown, tiara, or veil can add a beautiful touch to your wedding day look. Just be sure to choose an accessory that complements your dress and hairstyle.

With these tips and ideas in mind, you're sure to find the perfect wedding dress and look stunning on your special day.
