Face Framing Bangs For Curly Hair A Guide

Written by Petter Oct 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle that can give your curly hair a fresh and stylish look? Consider adding face framing bangs to your curly hair! This hairstyle can enhance your facial features, add volume to your curls, and create a playful and youthful vibe. In this article, we will guide you through the process of getting face framing bangs for curly hair, including tips, steps, and hair care advice. Let's get started!

Description

Face framing bangs are a type of bangs that are shorter in the middle and longer on the sides. They are designed to frame the face and highlight the cheekbones, eyes, and jawline. Face framing bangs can come in different styles, such as blunt, wispy, choppy, or layered, depending on your hair texture and face shape. For curly hair, face framing bangs can be a great way to showcase your natural curls and add some definition to your face.

How to Get Face Framing Bangs for Curly Hair

The first step to getting face framing bangs for curly hair is to find a hairstylist who has experience working with curly hair. Curly hair requires special care and attention, so make sure to ask for recommendations or do some research online. Once you find a hairstylist, schedule a consultation to discuss your hair goals and preferences. Your hairstylist can recommend the best type of face framing bangs for your curly hair, taking into account your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the basic steps to getting face framing bangs for curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with curly hair products to enhance your natural curls and minimize frizz.
  2. Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser attachment to gently dry your curls and create volume.
  3. Section off the hair that you want to cut for your face framing bangs. Your hairstylist can help you determine the right length and shape for your face.
  4. Cut your hair using scissors or a razor, depending on the desired style. Your hairstylist can use different techniques, such as point-cutting or slide-cutting, to create texture and movement.
  5. Style your face framing bangs using a curling iron or a flat iron, depending on the look you want to achieve. You can also use styling products, such as mousse or hairspray, to hold your curls in place and add shine.

Tips for Maintaining Face Framing Bangs for Curly Hair

Once you get face framing bangs for curly hair, you need to take good care of them to keep them looking fresh and healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain the shape.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls gently. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to moisturize your hair and prevent dryness.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent tangles.

FAQ

Here are some common questions and answers about face framing bangs for curly hair:

  • Q: Can I get face framing bangs if I have short curly hair? A: Yes, you can! Your hairstylist can create shorter bangs that complement your hair length and face shape.
  • Q: Will face framing bangs make my hair look thinner? A: No, if done correctly, face framing bangs can actually add volume and dimension to your curls.
  • Q: How do I style my face framing bangs if I want to wear my hair up? A: You can use bobby pins or hair clips to secure your bangs to the sides or the back of your head. You can also twist your bangs and pin them to create a cute updo.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair with Face Framing Bangs

Curly hair requires special care and attention to keep it healthy and beautiful. Here are some hair care tips for curly hair with face framing bangs:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for curly hair. These products can help enhance your curls and minimize frizz.
  • Avoid using heat tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often, as they can damage your hair and make it dry and brittle.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to nourish and moisturize your curls. You can use a hair mask or a hot oil treatment for extra hydration.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as it can disturb your curls and create frizz. Instead, let your curls air dry or use a diffuser attachment to dry your hair gently.

With these tips and tricks, you can rock your face framing bangs for curly hair with confidence and style. Remember to embrace your natural curls and have fun experimenting with different hairstyles and looks!
