How Not To Cut Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Sep 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be both beautiful and challenging to manage. Proper hair care is crucial to prevent frizz and breakage, but what about haircuts? Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair, and one mistake can ruin your hair's natural texture. In this guide, we will discuss how not to cut curly hair and provide tips and tricks for maintaining healthy, bouncy curls.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Curly Hair

Before we dive into how not to cut curly hair, let's first discuss the basics of cutting curly hair. Unlike straight hair, curly hair is prone to shrinkage, unevenness, and frizz. Therefore, it's essential to cut curly hair while it's dry, so you can see how the curls fall and how much length you need to trim. Additionally, avoid using razors or thinning shears, as they can damage the hair cuticle and cause frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. Do not use a brush, as it can cause breakage.
  3. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer. Do not use a brush or comb to dry your hair, as it can disrupt your curls' natural pattern.
  4. Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back.
  5. Start with the back sections and trim your hair while it's dry. Use scissors and make small cuts to avoid cutting off too much length.
  6. Move on to the front sections and repeat the process.
  7. Check the length and shape of your hair in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments but avoid overcutting.
  8. Finish with a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for cutting curly hair:

  • Trim your hair every 8-12 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Do not cut your hair while it's wet, as it will look shorter when it dries.
  • Invest in high-quality scissors specifically designed for cutting curly hair.
  • Ask your stylist to cut your hair while it's dry to ensure they can see the natural curls' shape and length.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair:

Can I cut my own curly hair?

While it's possible to cut your own curly hair, it's best to leave it to a professional stylist who has experience working with curly hair. Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair and can be challenging to do correctly.

How can I prevent my curls from shrinking after a haircut?

To prevent your curls from shrinking after a haircut, make sure to cut your hair while it's dry and avoid using razors or thinning shears. Additionally, use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair gently and avoid using heat styling tools.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential to maintain healthy, bouncy curls. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair's natural oils.
  • Condition your hair every time you wash it to keep it hydrated and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.

By following these tips and avoiding common mistakes, you can maintain healthy, bouncy curls and avoid damaging your hair.

In conclusion, cutting curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair, and one mistake can ruin your hair's natural texture. By cutting your hair while it's dry, avoiding razors or thinning shears, and following proper hair care techniques, you can maintain healthy, bouncy curls and avoid damaging your hair.
