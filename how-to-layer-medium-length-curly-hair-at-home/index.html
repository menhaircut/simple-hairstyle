How To Layer Medium Length Curly Hair At Home

Written by Oliver Jul 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your medium length curly hair looking flat and lifeless? Layering your hair can add volume, shape, and dimension to your curls. While going to a salon for a haircut may seem like the only option, you can actually layer your hair at home with a few simple steps. In this blog post, we will guide you through how to layer medium length curly hair at home.

How to Layer Medium Length Curly Hair at Home

Before you start layering your hair, it is important to have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Follow these steps to layer your hair at home:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a product that is formulated for curly hair to keep your curls defined and moisturized. Rinse your hair thoroughly and gently squeeze out excess water with a towel. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel as this can cause frizz.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots. This will help prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Start by cutting the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to snip the ends of the hair at an angle. This will create a soft, layered effect. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way up to the top layer.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you have finished cutting your hair, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your layers are even and balanced.

Tips for Layering Medium Length Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering your medium length curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Cut your hair when it is dry to ensure that you can see the true length and shape of your curls.
  • When cutting the top layer of your hair, angle the scissors downwards to create a softer, more natural look.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and angles to find the perfect look for your hair.

FAQs About Layering Medium Length Curly Hair

Can I layer my hair if it is very curly?

Yes, you can layer your hair regardless of how curly it is. In fact, layering can help enhance and define your curls.

How often should I layer my hair?

It is recommended to layer your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your curls looking their best.

Can I layer my hair if it is damaged?

We recommend consulting with a hair stylist if your hair is damaged before attempting to layer it at home. They can advise you on the best course of action to repair and strengthen your hair.

Hair Care for Layered Medium Length Curly Hair

Now that you have successfully layered your hair, it is important to take care of it to keep your curls healthy and defined.

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner formulated for curly hair to keep your curls moisturized and defined.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid using a brush which can cause breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl defining cream to enhance and define your curls.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and prevent split ends.

Layering your medium length curly hair at home is a great way to give your curls a new lease of life. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut without leaving your home. Remember to take care of your hair to keep your curls looking their best.
