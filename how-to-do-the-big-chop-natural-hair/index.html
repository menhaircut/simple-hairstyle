How To Do The Big Chop Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Are you ready to embrace your natural hair? The big chop is a bold and empowering decision that many women make to transition from chemically treated hair to their natural texture. If you're considering doing the big chop, this guide will walk you through the process and give you some tips for taking care of your new hair.

  2. How to Do the Big Chop
  3. Step-by-Step Guide

Description

The big chop is the process of cutting off all of your chemically treated hair, leaving only your natural hair. This can be a scary and intimidating decision, but it can also be incredibly liberating. When you do the big chop, you're taking a stand for your natural beauty and embracing your unique texture.

How to Do the Big Chop

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're ready to do the big chop, follow these steps:

  1. Decide on the length you want to cut your hair. You can go for a short buzz cut or leave a little bit of length on top.
  2. Wash and detangle your hair. It's important to start with clean, product-free hair so your stylist can see your natural texture.
  3. Go to a stylist who is experienced with natural hair. They will be able to give you a clean, even cut and help you style your new hair.
  4. Cut off all of your chemically treated hair. This can be a scary moment, but remember that you're making a positive change for your hair and your health.
  5. Style your new hair. Your stylist can help you find a style that works for your face shape and hair texture. You may also want to experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair.

Congratulations, you've done the big chop! Now it's time to take care of your new hair.

Tips for Taking Care of Your New Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your new natural hair:

  • Moisturize regularly. Natural hair can be dry, so it's important to add moisture to your hair routine. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Protect your hair at night. Use a satin or silk bonnet or pillowcase to prevent breakage and preserve your style.
  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid using heat tools and avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension and breakage.
  • Experiment with styles. Natural hair is incredibly versatile, so have fun experimenting with different styles and products.

FAQ

How often should I wash my natural hair?

It depends on your hair type and texture, but most people with natural hair wash their hair once a week or every two weeks. You can also co-wash (use conditioner instead of shampoo) in between washes to refresh your hair.

How do I deal with shrinkage?

Shrinkage is a common issue for natural hair. To combat shrinkage, you can stretch your hair using twists or braids, or use a blow dryer with a diffuser attachment on low heat.

How do I prevent breakage?

To prevent breakage, be gentle with your hair and avoid tight hairstyles. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and always use a heat protectant if you're using heat tools.

Hair Care

Taking care of your natural hair is crucial to keeping it healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for maintaining your natural hair:

  • Deep condition your hair regularly to add moisture and prevent breakage.
  • Trim your hair every few months to get rid of split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and heat tools that can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock your natural hair with confidence and style.
