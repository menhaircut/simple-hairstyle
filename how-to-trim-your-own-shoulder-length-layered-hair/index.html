How To Trim Your Own Shoulder Length Layered Hair Step By Step Guide

If you have shoulder length layered hair and you're looking for a way to save some money and time by trimming it yourself, then you've come to the right place. In this article, we will guide you through the steps of trimming your own shoulder length layered hair, and provide helpful tips and tricks to make the process easier.

How to Trim Your Own Shoulder Length Layered Hair

Before we begin, it's important to note that trimming your own hair can be a bit tricky, especially if you've never done it before. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut right at home.

Tools You'll Need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clip or tie
  • Handheld mirror or vanity mirror

Things to Keep in Mind:

  • Don't trim too much at once
  • Cut small sections at a time
  • Use a comb to help guide your scissors
  • Always cut with the scissors pointing upwards

Step by Step Guide:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it's completely dry before you begin trimming. Wet hair can be deceptive and may look longer than it actually is.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using a comb. Start by separating your hair down the middle, then create two more sections on each side of your part. Use a hair clip or tie to secure each section.

Step 3: Start with the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of your hair and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to trim the ends, cutting upwards at an angle. Be sure to cut small sections at a time, and avoid cutting too much at once.

Step 4: Move on to the Middle Layer

Once you've finished trimming the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take down the hair clip or tie that's holding the middle section. Comb the hair straight down, and trim the ends using the same technique as before.

Step 5: Finish with the Top Layer

Finally, take down the hair clip or tie holding the top section of your hair. Comb it straight down and trim the ends using the same technique as before. You can also add some layers to the top section, if desired.

Tips for Trimming Your Own Hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
  • Don't cut your hair too short, you can always trim more later
  • Use a handheld mirror or vanity mirror to check the back of your head
  • Trim your hair in a well-lit area
  • If you're unsure, start with small sections and work your way up to larger ones

FAQs:

Q: Can I trim my hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to trim your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be deceptive and may look longer than it actually is.

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep split ends at bay.

Q: Can I use regular household scissors to trim my hair?

A: No, it's important to use sharp hair scissors to avoid split ends.

Hair Care:

Now that you've successfully trimmed your own shoulder length layered hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools too often to avoid damage
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet to avoid breakage

Trimming your own shoulder length layered hair can be a bit daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut right at home. Remember to take it slow, cut small sections at a time, and always cut with the scissors pointing upwards. And don't forget to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style.
