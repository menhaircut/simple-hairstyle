How To Cut Face Framing Layers For Short Hair

Written by Petter Oct 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Short hair can be tricky to style, but with the right techniques, you can create a stunning look that will turn heads. One of the best ways to add some dimension to your short hair is by cutting face framing layers. These layers will help accentuate your facial features and give you a chic, modern look.

Description

Face framing layers are layers of hair that are cut around the face to create a flattering, frame-like effect. This technique is perfect for short hair because it can help soften harsh lines and add movement to your hair. When done correctly, face framing layers can also help balance out your features and highlight your best features.

How to Cut Face Framing Layers for Short Hair

If you're ready to try out this trend, here's how to cut face framing layers for short hair:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start cutting, you'll need to prep your hair. Make sure it's clean and dry, and brush it out so it's smooth and tangle-free. If you have curly or wavy hair, you may want to straighten it first so you can see the length and shape more clearly.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Next, part your hair in the center or on the side, depending on your preference. Use a comb to create a clean, straight parting line.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Now it's time to section your hair. Use hair clips to separate the top section of your hair from the bottom section.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Section

Starting with the bottom section, take a small piece of hair and pull it forward. Hold it between your fingers and cut it at an angle, following the curve of your jawline. Repeat this process on both sides of your head until you've cut all the hair in the bottom section.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

Now it's time to cut the top section. Take a piece of hair from the front and pull it forward. Hold it between your fingers and cut it at an angle, following the curve of your cheekbone. Repeat this process on both sides of your head until you've cut all the hair in the top section.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use a comb to blend them together. This will help create a seamless, natural look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting face framing layers for short hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut slowly. You can always cut more, but you can't undo a bad cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Make sure your hands are steady and your posture is good to avoid uneven cuts.
  • Keep checking your progress in the mirror to make sure you're happy with the shape and length.

FAQ

What if I'm not confident cutting my own hair?

If you're not confident cutting your own hair, it's best to go to a professional stylist. They can help you achieve the look you want while ensuring that your hair is healthy and well-cared for.

How often should I cut my face framing layers?

It's best to cut your face framing layers every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and length.

Hair Care

After you've cut your face framing layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shine. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat when styling. You can also use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it looking its best.

Cutting face framing layers for short hair is a great way to add some dimension and style to your look. With the right techniques, you can achieve a chic, modern look that will make you feel confident and beautiful.
