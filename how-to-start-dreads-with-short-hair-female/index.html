How To Start Dreads With Short Hair Female A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a short-haired female and thinking of starting your own dreadlocks? Starting dreads with short hair can be challenging but not impossible. With the right technique, patience, and care, you can achieve beautiful and healthy dreadlocks. In this guide, we will walk you through the step-by-step process of how to start dreads with short hair female.

Table of Contents

  • Section 1: Description
  • Section 2: How to Start Dreads with Short Hair Female
  • Section 3: Step-by-Step Guide
  • Section 4: Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Hair Female
  • Section 5: FAQ
  • Section 6: Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Section 1: Description

Dreadlocks are a popular and stylish hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are formed by matting and twisting sections of hair until they become tightly knotted. While it is more common for people with longer hair to have dreadlocks, it is possible to start dreads with short hair, especially for females who have relaxed hair.

Section 2: How to Start Dreads with Short Hair Female

Starting dreads with short hair female is not as difficult as it may seem. The key is to be patient and allow your hair to grow naturally. Here are some tips to help you get started:

1. Keep Your Hair Clean

Before you start your dreadlocks, it is important to have clean hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up, and make sure your hair is completely dry before you begin.

2. Choose Your Method

There are several methods you can use to start your dreadlocks, including the backcombing method, the twist and rip method, and the crochet method. Choose the method that works best for your hair type and personal preference.

3. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small, even sections using a comb. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreadlocks to be.

4. Start Twisting

Take one section of hair and start twisting it in a clockwise direction. As you twist, the hair will naturally start to knot and form a dreadlock. Continue twisting until you have a tight knot, and then use a rubber band or hair tie to secure the end.

5. Repeat

Repeat the twisting process on each section of hair until you have completed your full head of dreadlocks. Be patient, as this process can take several hours to complete.

Section 3: Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with short hair female:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up. Rinse thoroughly and towel dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into small, even sections. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreadlocks to be.

Step 3: Choose Your Method

Choose the method you want to use to start your dreadlocks. The backcombing method, the twist and rip method, and the crochet method are all popular choices.

Step 4: Start Twisting

Take one section of hair and start twisting it in a clockwise direction. As you twist, the hair will naturally start to knot and form a dreadlock. Continue twisting until you have a tight knot, and then use a rubber band or hair tie to secure the end.

Step 5: Repeat

Repeat the twisting process on each section of hair until you have completed your full head of dreadlocks.

Section 4: Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Hair Female

Here are some tips to help you successfully start your dreadlocks:

1. Be Patient

Starting dreadlocks takes time, especially if you have short hair. Be patient and allow your hair to grow naturally.

2. Use Natural Products

Use natural products to care for your hair. Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products that can damage your hair.

3. Keep Your Hair Moisturized

Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent breakage and keep your scalp healthy. Use natural oils like coconut oil, jojoba oil, or castor oil to keep your hair moisturized.

4. Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Sleep on a satin pillowcase to prevent your hair from tangling and breaking while you sleep.

Section 5: FAQ

1. How long does it take to start dreadlocks with short hair female?

It can take anywhere from a few months to a year to start dreadlocks with short hair, depending on your hair type and the method you use.

2. Can I start dreadlocks with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair. However, you will need to be extra careful not to damage your hair during the process.

3. Do I need to cut off my relaxed hair to start dreadlocks?

No, you do not need to cut off your relaxed hair to start dreadlocks. However, it may be easier to start your dreadlocks if you have some natural hair growth.

Section 6: Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once you have started your dreadlocks, it is important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy dreadlocks:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup and keep your scalp healthy. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using conditioner.

2. Palm Roll Your Dreads

Palm roll your dreadlocks regularly to help them tighten and maintain their shape.

3. Avoid Using Heat

Avoid using heat on your dreadlocks, as it can cause damage and breakage. Instead, air dry your hair and avoid using hot styling tools.

4. Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth and prevent breakage.

Starting dreads with short hair female may seem daunting, but with the right technique and care, you can achieve beautiful and healthy dreadlocks. Remember to be patient, use natural products, and take care of your hair properly to ensure the best results.
