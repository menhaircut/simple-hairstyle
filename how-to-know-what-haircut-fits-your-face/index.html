Description

Your face shape plays a significant role in determining which haircut will suit you best. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and triangular. How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before you can choose the perfect haircut, you need to determine your face shape. Follow these steps to figure out which category you fall into:

Oval

If your face is longer than it is wide, with a rounded chin and forehead, you likely have an oval face shape. This shape is considered the most versatile, as most haircuts complement it well.

Round

A round face shape is characterized by a circular shape, with full cheeks and a rounded chin. If your face is nearly as wide as it is long, with no prominent angles, you likely have a round face shape.

Square

Square face shapes have prominent angles, with a strong jawline and forehead. If your face is roughly the same width from forehead to jaw, with a square jawline, you likely have a square face shape.

Heart

Heart-shaped faces have a wider forehead and cheekbones, with a narrower jawline and chin. If your face is wider at the temples and narrows toward the chin, you likely have a heart-shaped face.

Diamond

Diamond face shapes are characterized by high cheekbones, a narrow forehead and chin, and a pointed jawline. If your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows toward the forehead and jaw, you likely have a diamond face shape.

Triangular

A triangular face shape has a wide jawline, with a narrower forehead and cheekbones. If your face is widest at the jawline and narrows toward the forehead, you likely have a triangular face shape.

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing the perfect haircut:

Frequently Asked Questions

What haircut suits a round face?

For a round face shape, a long, layered haircut can create the illusion of length. Avoid blunt bobs, as they can make your face appear wider.

What haircut suits a heart-shaped face?

For a heart-shaped face, a chin-length bob or layered haircut works well. Avoid styles that add volume to the crown of your head, as they can make your forehead appear wider.

What haircut suits a square face?

A square face shape looks great with a long, layered haircut or a side-swept bang. Avoid blunt bobs or straight-across bangs, as they can emphasize your strong jawline.
