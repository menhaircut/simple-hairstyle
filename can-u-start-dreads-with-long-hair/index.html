Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for decades. Many people believe that dreads can only be started with short hair or by cutting off all their hair, but this is not true. You can start dreads with long hair, and it can actually be easier than starting with short hair. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with long hair.

How to Start Dreads with Long Hair

To start dreads with long hair, you will need to section your hair and twist each section. This process is called "twist and rip." Twist and rip is a popular method for starting dreads because it allows you to control the size of each dread and ensures that the dread will lock up properly.

What you'll need:

  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Dread wax (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to start dreads with long hair:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. It's essential to have clean hair when starting dreads.
  2. Section your hair into small, equal-sized sections. Use a comb to create sections that are about 1-2 inches in width.
  3. Take one section of hair and twist it from the root to the tip.
  4. After twisting the section of hair, use a hair tie to secure the end of the dread. Make sure the hair tie is tight enough to hold the twist, but not so tight that it cuts off circulation to the scalp.
  5. Repeat steps 3-4 for each section of hair until your entire head is covered in twists.
  6. After creating all the twists, you can apply dread wax to each dread. Dread wax can help your dreads lock up faster and stay in place.
  7. Let your dreads sit for a few days before washing them again.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Long Hair

Starting dreads with long hair can be a bit tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the style you want. Here are some tips to keep in mind when starting dreads with long hair:

  • Start with clean hair: It's essential to start with clean hair when creating dreads. Dirty hair can be challenging to work with and may not lock up properly.
  • Use a comb to section your hair: Sectioning your hair with a comb ensures that each section is the same size, which makes it easier to maintain your dreads.
  • Be patient: Dreads take time to form, so be patient and allow your hair to lock up naturally. Don't worry if your dreads look a bit messy at first; they will settle with time.
  • Keep your hair moisturized: Dreads can be dry and brittle, so it's important to keep your hair moisturized. Use a natural oil, like coconut oil, to keep your hair healthy.

FAQs

Can You Start Dreads with Long Straight Hair?

Yes, you can start dreads with long straight hair. You will need to use a twisting method, like the twist and rip method, to create the dreads.

How Long Does it Take to Start Dreads with Long Hair?

It can take several months to a year to start dreads with long hair, depending on your hair type and how well you maintain your dreads.

Do You Need to Cut Your Hair to Start Dreads?

No, you do not need to cut your hair to start dreads. You can start dreads with any length of hair.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you have started your dreads, you will need to take care of them properly to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining your dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly: It's important to keep your dreads clean to prevent buildup and odors. Use a residue-free shampoo designed for dreads.
  • Keep your dreads dry: Dreads can take a long time to dry, so it's essential to keep them dry to prevent mold and mildew. Use a hairdryer on the cool setting to dry your dreads.
  • Retwist your dreads: As your hair grows, your dreads will loosen. It's important to retwist your dreads regularly to keep them tight and neat.
  • Use a natural oil to moisturize your dreads: Dreads can be dry and brittle, so it's essential to keep them moisturized. Use a natural oil, like coconut oil, to keep your dreads healthy.

Starting dreads with long hair is possible and can be easier than starting with short hair. Follow the steps and tips in this article to achieve the style you want. Remember to take care of your dreads properly to keep them looking their best.
