How To Layer Your Own Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair and want to add some dimension and texture to your look, layering is the way to go. While getting a professional haircut can be expensive, you can layer your own hair at home with a few simple tools and techniques. In this guide, we'll take you through the process of layering your own short hair, step-by-step.

  4. Cutting Your Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you start layering your hair, make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Handheld mirror

You may also want to have a spray bottle filled with water to mist your hair if it gets too dry or difficult to manage.

Preparation

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare your hair. Wash and dry your hair thoroughly, and brush out any tangles. If your hair is particularly curly or unruly, you may want to straighten it with a flat iron to make it easier to cut.

Next, decide on the length and style of your layers. Look up pictures of layered haircuts online to get inspiration and ideas. Keep in mind that shorter layers will add more volume and texture to your hair, while longer layers will create a more subtle, blended look.

Sectioning Your Hair

Once you've decided on the length and style of your layers, it's time to section your hair. Start by parting your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.

Clip the two front sections of your hair out of the way, leaving the back sections loose. Take one of the back sections and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers, and lift the hair up and away from your head at a 90-degree angle. This will create a guide for your first layer.

Cutting Your Hair

Using your scissors, make small, diagonal cuts into the hair above your fingers. These cuts should be about 1/4 inch apart, and should create a slightly angled, layered effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair in this way, moving up your hair in 1/2 inch increments.

Once you've finished one section of hair, unclip the next section and repeat the process. Be sure to cut both sides of your hair evenly, and to keep the layers as symmetrical as possible.

Blending Your Layers

After you've finished cutting all of your layers, it's time to blend them together. Comb your hair straight down, and use your scissors to make small, vertical cuts into the ends of your hair. This will soften the edges of your layers and create a more natural look.

Once you've finished blending, use your hands to tousle and style your hair. You may want to use a texturizing spray or hair wax to add even more volume and texture.

Tips for Layering Your Own Hair

  • Start with small cuts, and work your way up gradually. You can always cut more hair, but you can't undo a bad haircut!
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • If you're not sure how short to cut your layers, start with longer layers and work your way up.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can shrink as it dries, and you may end up cutting off more than you intended.
  • Be patient! Layering your own hair can take some practice, but with time and patience, you'll get the hang of it.

FAQs

How often should I layer my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to maintain your layered look. Generally, you should aim to get your hair layered every 6-8 weeks.

Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

Yes, but it may be more difficult to get an even cut. Consider straightening your hair before you start cutting, or seek the help of a professional stylist.

Can I layer my hair if it's already been layered?

Yes, but be careful not to cut too much hair, as this can create uneven layers and a choppy, unflattering look.

Hair Care After Layering

After you've layered your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a deep conditioner once a week, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair regularly to maintain your layers, and protect your hair from the sun and wind with a hat or scarf.

With these tips and techniques, you can layer your own short hair at home and achieve a stylish, textured look. Happy cutting!
