How Do You Get Rid Of Orange Brassy Hair Naturally

Are you struggling with orange brassy hair after dyeing your hair blonde? Don't worry, it's a common problem, and there are natural ways to fix it. In this article, we'll discuss how to get rid of orange brassy hair naturally.

Description

Orange brassy hair occurs when blonde hair turns orange or yellowish after dyeing. This happens because the hair was not lightened enough before applying the blonde dye. The orange color is due to the underlying pigment in the hair, which can be warm or cool. Warm pigments are orange and yellow, while cool pigments are blue and green.

To fix orange brassy hair, you can use natural remedies that are safe and effective. These remedies can help tone down the orange and yellow hues and restore your blonde hair's natural shade.

How to Get Rid of Orange Brassy Hair Naturally

The following are natural ways to get rid of orange brassy hair:

Vinegar Rinse

Vinegar is an excellent natural remedy for orange brassy hair. It has acidic properties that can remove the orange and yellow tones from your hair. To use vinegar, mix equal parts of vinegar and water and apply the mixture to your hair. Leave it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it off with water.

Tea Rinse

Tea is another natural remedy that can help tone down the orange and yellow hues in your hair. Brew a strong cup of tea and let it cool down. Then, pour the tea over your hair and leave it on for 30 minutes before rinsing it off with water.

Lemon Juice

Lemon juice is an excellent natural remedy for orange brassy hair. It has acidic properties that can help remove the orange and yellow tones from your hair. To use lemon juice, mix equal parts of lemon juice and water and apply the mixture to your hair. Leave it on for 30 minutes before rinsing it off with water.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of orange brassy hair naturally:

  1. Mix equal parts of vinegar and water or lemon juice and water.
  2. Apply the mixture to your hair and massage it in.
  3. Leave the mixture on for 10-30 minutes, depending on the severity of the orange hue.
  4. Rinse your hair with water.
  5. Repeat the process once a week until you achieve the desired shade.

Tips for Preventing Orange Brassy Hair

Here are some tips to prevent orange brassy hair:

  • Use a toner after dyeing your hair blonde to remove any orange or yellow tones.
  • Avoid leaving bleach on your hair for too long, as it can damage your hair and cause it to turn orange.
  • Use a purple shampoo to maintain your blonde hair's shade and prevent it from turning orange.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to turn orange.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting rid of orange brassy hair:

1. Can I use baking soda to get rid of orange brassy hair?

Yes, you can use baking soda to get rid of orange brassy hair. Mix equal parts of baking soda and water and apply the mixture to your hair. Leave it on for 20 minutes before rinsing it off with water.

2. Can I use apple cider vinegar instead of white vinegar?

Yes, you can use apple cider vinegar instead of white vinegar. Apple cider vinegar has the same acidic properties that can help remove the orange and yellow tones from your hair.

Hair Care for Colored Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your colored hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as they can damage your hair and cause it to fade faster.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to fade faster.

By following these tips, you can keep your blonde hair looking fresh and vibrant and prevent it from turning orange or brassy.
