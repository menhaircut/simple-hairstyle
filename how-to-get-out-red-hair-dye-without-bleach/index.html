How To Get Out Red Hair Dye Without Bleach

Written by Eveline Aug 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your red hair dye and want to switch things up? Are you worried about damaging your hair with bleach? Don't worry, there are ways to get rid of red hair dye without using bleach. In this article, we will discuss how to do it step by step and provide some tips and FAQs to help you along the way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Out Red Hair Dye Without Bleach
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Removing Red Hair Dye
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care after Removing Red Hair Dye

How to Get Out Red Hair Dye Without Bleach

The first step in getting rid of red hair dye without bleach is to use a clarifying shampoo. Clarifying shampoos are designed to remove build-up from your hair and scalp, which can help to fade the red dye. You can also use a dandruff shampoo, as it typically has a higher concentration of clarifying agents.

Another option is to use a mixture of baking soda and shampoo. Mix equal parts of baking soda and your regular shampoo, apply it to your hair, and let it sit for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out. Baking soda has natural cleansing properties that can help to remove red hair dye.

If the above methods don't work, you can try using vitamin C tablets. Crush up about 10-15 vitamin C tablets and mix them with hot water to create a paste. Apply the paste to your hair, cover it with a shower cap, and let it sit for an hour before rinsing it out. The acid in the vitamin C can help to strip the color from your hair.

Step by Step Guide

1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo or dandruff shampoo.

2. If the red dye is still noticeable, mix equal parts of baking soda and shampoo and apply it to your hair. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.

3. If the red dye is still stubborn, crush up about 10-15 vitamin C tablets and mix them with hot water to create a paste. Apply the paste to your hair, cover it with a shower cap, and let it sit for an hour before rinsing it out.

4. Repeat the above steps as necessary until the red dye has faded to your desired level.

Tips for Removing Red Hair Dye

- Use a clarifying shampoo or dandruff shampoo once a week to prevent build-up of hair dye.

- Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can make the red dye more difficult to remove.

- If you have sensitive skin, do a patch test before using any of the above methods.

- Use a deep conditioning treatment after removing the red hair dye to restore moisture and prevent damage.

FAQs

Q: Will using these methods damage my hair?

A: These methods are generally safe for your hair, but they can be drying. Be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment after removing the red hair dye.

Q: How long will it take to remove the red hair dye?

A: The amount of time it takes to remove the red hair dye will depend on the intensity of the dye and the method you use. It could take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks.

Hair Care after Removing Red Hair Dye

After removing the red hair dye, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain its health. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools and opt for air-drying instead. Consider using a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent future damage to your hair.

Now that you know how to get out red hair dye without bleach, you can switch up your look without damaging your hair. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair and use a deep conditioning treatment to maintain its health.
