How To Bring An Old Wig Back To Life

Written by Luffy Oct 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of throwing away expensive wigs because they look old and worn out? Don't worry; you can bring them back to life with a few simple steps. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how to bring an old wig back to life. We will cover everything from cleaning to styling, so you can have your wig looking brand new again. Let's get started!

Description

Wigs are a great way to change up your look without having to commit to a haircut or color. However, wigs can be expensive, and over time they can start to look old and worn out. You might think that it's time to throw it away, but there are ways to bring it back to life. Whether it's a synthetic or human hair wig, you can revive it with a few simple steps.

How to

Step 1: Detangle the Wig

The first step in reviving an old wig is to detangle it. Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to carefully comb through the wig, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Be gentle to avoid damaging the hair fibers.

Step 2: Wash the Wig

Once your wig is detangled, it's time to wash it. Fill a sink or basin with cool water and add a small amount of wig shampoo. Gently swish the wig around in the water, being careful not to rub or twist the hair. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water and gently squeeze out any excess water.

Step 3: Condition the Wig

After washing, it's important to condition the wig to keep it soft and manageable. Apply a small amount of wig conditioner to the hair, avoiding the roots. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes, then rinse it out with cool water.

Step 4: Dry the Wig

Once the wig is clean and conditioned, it's time to dry it. Gently blot the wig with a towel to remove excess water, then place it on a wig stand or mannequin head to air dry. Avoid using a hairdryer, as heat can damage the fibers.

Step 5: Style the Wig

Once your wig is dry, it's time to style it. If it's a synthetic wig, use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to style it. If it's a human hair wig, you can use heat styling tools like a curling iron or straightener. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to the hair.

Tips

- Use wig-specific products to avoid damaging the fibers
- Avoid using hot water or heat on synthetic wigs
- Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape
- Don't sleep in your wig, as this can cause tangling and damage
- Avoid brushing or combing wet hair, as this can cause breakage

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my wig?

A: It depends on how often you wear it. If you wear it every day, you should wash it every 2-4 weeks. If you wear it less frequently, you can wash it every 6-8 weeks.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my wig?

A: No, you should use wig-specific products to avoid damaging the fibers. Regular shampoo and conditioner can strip the hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.

Q: How do I store my wig?

A: Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape. Keep it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.

Hair Care

Reviving an old wig is a great way to save money and keep your favorite wigs looking like new. However, it's important to take care of your wigs to ensure they last as long as possible. Always use wig-specific products, avoid heat and hot water, and store your wig properly to maintain its shape. With a little bit of care, your wigs will look great for years to come.
