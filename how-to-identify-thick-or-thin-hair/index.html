How To Identify Thick Or Thin Hair

Written by Christine Jul 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Knowing whether you have thick or thin hair can help you choose the right products and styling techniques to keep your hair looking its best. In this article, we'll show you how to identify whether you have thick or thin hair, and provide tips on how to care for your hair accordingly.

Table of Contents

  • How to Identify Thick or Thin Hair
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

How to Identify Thick or Thin Hair

There are a few ways to determine whether you have thick or thin hair:

1. Check the Circumference

One way to determine the thickness of your hair is to measure the circumference of a ponytail. If your ponytail is greater than two inches in circumference, you likely have thick hair. If it's less than two inches, you likely have thin hair.

2. Look at Individual Strands

Another way to determine the thickness of your hair is to look at individual strands. Hold a strand of hair between your fingers and run your fingers down the length of it. If you can feel the strand easily, you likely have thin hair. If you can barely feel the strand, you likely have thick hair.

3. Consider Density

The density of your hair can also help determine whether it's thick or thin. If you have a lot of hair on your head, you likely have thick hair. If you can see your scalp easily, you likely have thin hair.

Step by Step

To determine whether you have thick or thin hair:

  1. Measure the circumference of a ponytail to determine thickness.
  2. Examine individual strands of hair to determine thickness.
  3. Consider the density of your hair to determine thickness.

Tips

If you have thick hair:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy conditioners that can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add even more volume.

If you have thin hair:

  • Use a lightweight volumizing shampoo to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair with a round brush to add volume.

FAQ

What causes thick or thin hair?

Thick or thin hair can be caused by a combination of genetics, diet, and lifestyle factors. Hormones can also play a role in hair thickness.

Can hair thickness change over time?

Yes, hair thickness can change over time due to hormonal changes, diet, and lifestyle factors.

Hair Care

Regardless of whether you have thick or thin hair, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote healthy hair.

By following these tips and correctly identifying your hair type, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
