Best Haircut For Fat Face Flattering Styles For Your Face Shape

Written by Bardi Nov 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have a round or chubby face, finding the best haircut for your face shape can be challenging. You want a style that will complement your features and make your face look slimmer and more defined. In this article, we'll share some of the best haircuts for a fat face and tips on how to style them. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Best Haircuts for Fat Faces
  • Tips for Styling Your Hair

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into the best haircuts for a fat face, let's first determine your face shape. To do this, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back so your face is fully visible. Then, use a measuring tape to measure the widest part of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Once you have these measurements, compare them to the following face shapes:

  • Round face: Your face is almost as wide as it is long, with full cheeks and a rounded chin.
  • Oval face: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a slightly curved jawline and forehead.
  • Square face: Your face is almost as wide as it is long, with a strong, angular jawline and forehead.
  • Heart-shaped face: Your face is wider at the forehead and tapers down to a pointy chin.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can choose a haircut that will flatter your features and make your face look more defined.

Best Haircuts for Fat Faces

Now that you know your face shape, let's take a look at some of the best haircuts for a fat face:

1. Long Layers

Long layers are a great option for a fat face because they create movement and texture, which can help to elongate your face. Layers that frame your face can also help to slim down your cheeks and define your jawline.

2. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs are a versatile option for a fat face because they can be worn with a variety of hairstyles. They can help to balance out the width of your face and draw attention to your eyes, which can make your face look slimmer.

3. Bob Haircut

A bob haircut can be a flattering option for a fat face if it's done right. A chin-length bob is a good choice because it can help to elongate your face, while layers near the bottom can help to create movement and texture.

4. Pixie Cut

A pixie cut can be a daring choice for a fat face, but it can also be a very flattering one. A short, choppy pixie cut can help to create volume at the crown of your head, which can help to balance out the width of your face.

Tips for Styling Your Hair

Once you've chosen a haircut that flatters your face shape, it's important to style your hair in a way that complements your features. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid straight, blunt cuts: These can make your face look wider and rounder.
  • Use volumizing products: Products like mousse and root lift can help to create volume and texture, which can help to elongate your face.
  • Try a deep side part: A deep side part can help to create the illusion of a slimmer face.
  • Avoid too much volume at the sides: Too much volume at the sides of your head can make your face look wider.

FAQs

What hairstyle makes a fat face look thinner?

Hairstyles with layers can help to create movement and texture, which can make your face look slimmer. Side-swept bangs can also help to balance out the width of your face.

What face shape is best for a bob?

A bob haircut can be flattering on a variety of face shapes, but it's especially flattering on those with an oval or heart-shaped face.

Can a pixie cut be flattering on a fat face?

Yes, a pixie cut can be a flattering option for a fat face if it's done right. A short, choppy pixie cut can help to create volume at the crown of your head, which can help to balance out the width of your face.

Hair Care Tips for a Healthy Mane

Regardless of your face shape, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly: How often you wash your hair will depend on your hair type, but it's important to keep your scalp and hair clean.
  • Use a conditioner: A conditioner can help to moisturize your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair over time. Try to limit your use of these tools, or use a heat protectant spray.
  • Get regular haircuts: Regular haircuts can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know the best haircut for a fat face and how to style it, it's time to head to the salon and try out a new look. Remember, the key is to choose a style that flatters your features and makes you feel confident and beautiful.
