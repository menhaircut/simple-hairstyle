How to Remove Hair from Dog Eye - Tips and Tricks | 2023 Guide

If you're a dog owner, you know that taking care of them can be a lot of work. One issue that can come up is hair getting in their eyes. Not only is this uncomfortable for your pup, but it can also lead to more serious problems like infections or corneal ulcers. In this guide, we'll go over how to remove hair from your dog's eyes safely and effectively.

Table of Contents

  • How to Remove Hair from Dog Eye
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care to Prevent Hair in the Eyes

How to Remove Hair from Dog Eye

Before getting started, it's important to note that if your dog has a lot of hair around their eyes, it's best to have a professional groomer trim it back. This will make it easier to keep the hair out of their eyes in the future. If there are only a few stray hairs, here's how to remove them:

1. Gather Supplies

You'll need a pair of grooming scissors and a pair of tweezers. Make sure they're clean and sharp.

2. Calm Your Dog

It's important to keep your dog calm during the process. Talk to them in a soothing voice and give them treats to distract them.

3. Cut the Hair

Using the grooming scissors, carefully trim the hair around your dog's eyes. Make sure to cut only a little bit at a time and be mindful of the sharp scissors near their eyes. If your dog is particularly squirmy, you may need a second person to help hold them still.

4. Tweeze the Hair

For any hairs that are too short to cut, use the tweezers to pluck them out. This can be a bit uncomfortable for your dog, so be gentle and take breaks if needed.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to removing hair from your dog's eye:

  1. Gather supplies: grooming scissors and tweezers.
  2. Calm your dog with treats and soothing words.
  3. Cut the hair around the eye with grooming scissors.
  4. Use tweezers to pluck out any remaining hairs.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips to make the process easier:

  • Have a second person hold your dog still if needed.
  • Trim the hair in a well-lit area.
  • Take breaks if your dog gets uncomfortable.
  • Use a grooming brush to keep the hair around the eyes tangle-free.

FAQ

Can I use human scissors to trim my dog's hair?

No, it's best to use grooming scissors specifically designed for pets. Human scissors can be too sharp and cause injury to your dog.

What if my dog won't stay still?

It may be helpful to have a second person hold your dog still while you trim their hair. You can also try using treats to distract them.

Can I use tweezers to remove hair from inside my dog's eye?

No, never use tweezers inside your dog's eye. If hair is stuck inside their eye, take them to the vet immediately.

Hair Care to Prevent Hair in the Eyes

To prevent hair from getting in your dog's eyes in the future, here are some hair care tips:

  • Regular grooming and trimming around the eyes.
  • Using a grooming brush to keep hair tangle-free.
  • Tying long hair back with a bow or clip.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to safely and effectively remove hair from your dog's eyes. Remember to always keep your dog calm and comfortable during the process, and seek the help of a professional groomer if needed.
