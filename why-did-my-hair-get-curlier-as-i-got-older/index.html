Why Did My Hair Get Curlier As I Got Older

Written by Frank Oct 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, our bodies go through several changes, including changes in our hair. For some people, their hair becomes curlier as they get older. If you're one of these people, you may be wondering why this is happening. In this article, we're going to explore some of the reasons why your hair may be getting curlier as you get older.

Table of Contents

  1. Genetics
  2. Hormones
  3. Health
  4. Hair Care
  5. FAQ

Genetics

One of the most significant factors that may be causing your hair to get curlier as you get older is genetics. If your parents or grandparents have curly hair, there's a good chance that you may inherit this trait. This is because the shape and texture of our hair are determined by our genes. So if you have curly hair genes, your hair is more likely to become curlier as you age.

Hormones

Hormones also play a role in hair texture changes. As we age, our hormone levels change, which can affect the way our hair looks and feels. For example, as women go through menopause, their estrogen levels decrease, which can lead to drier, more brittle hair. This can cause the hair to become more curly, as the strands become more tightly coiled.

Health

Your overall health can also impact the texture of your hair. If you're not getting enough nutrients, your hair may become dry, brittle, and more prone to breakage. This can cause your hair to become curlier, as the strands become more tightly coiled to compensate for the damage. Additionally, certain health conditions, such as thyroid disorders, can also impact the texture of your hair.

Hair Care

The way you care for your hair can also impact its texture. If you're using harsh shampoos and styling products, this can cause your hair to become dry and damaged. This can cause your hair to become more curly, as the strands become more tightly coiled to compensate for the damage. Additionally, if you're using heat styling tools, this can also damage your hair and cause it to become more curly.

How to Take Care of Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, it's essential to take care of it properly to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for taking care of curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair.
  • Don't brush your hair when it's dry.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

FAQ

Can stress cause my hair to become curlier?

Stress can impact the texture of your hair, but it's unlikely that it would cause your hair to become curlier. Instead, stress can cause your hair to become dry and brittle, which can affect the way it looks and feels.

Can I make my hair less curly?

If you're unhappy with the texture of your hair, there are a few things you can do to make it less curly. One option is to use a straightening treatment, such as a keratin treatment. Another option is to use a flat iron to straighten your hair. However, it's essential to be careful when using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.

Why does my hair get frizzy?

Frizz occurs when the cuticles of your hair become raised, causing the strands to become tangled and rough. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, damage, and using harsh hair products. To prevent frizz, it's essential to use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner and to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.

What are some hairstyles for curly hair?

If you have curly hair, there are many hairstyles that you can try. Some popular styles include:

  • Curls with a side part
  • A messy bun
  • A half-up, half-down style
  • A curly ponytail
  • Braids or twists

Overall, there are several reasons why your hair may be getting curlier as you get older. By taking care of your hair and using the right products and styling techniques, you can keep your curls looking healthy, shiny, and beautiful.
