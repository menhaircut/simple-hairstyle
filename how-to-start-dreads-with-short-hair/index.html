How To Start Dreads With Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have short hair and want to start dreads, don't worry! It's definitely possible to achieve the look you want with a little patience and some know-how. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of starting dreads with short hair, including tips and tricks on how to care for your new style.

How to Start Dreads with Short Hair

Starting dreads with short hair is a bit different than with longer hair, but it's still possible to achieve great results. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Your hair should be at least 2-3 inches long.
  • You'll need to section your hair into small, even parts.
  • You'll need to twist or backcomb each section to create the dreadlocks.
  • You'll need to maintain your dreads regularly to keep them clean and tidy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to starting dreads with short hair:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly and let it dry completely.
  2. Section your hair into small, even parts. Use rubber bands or clips to keep each section separate.
  3. Take one section at a time and backcomb it. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends.
  4. Twist the section in one direction until it starts to form a coil.
  5. Use a dread wax or gel to hold the coil together. Be sure to use a small amount, as too much can make your hair greasy.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 for each section of hair.
  7. Once all sections are twisted and waxed, let your hair sit for a few days to allow the dreads to set.
  8. After a few days, remove the rubber bands or clips and gently pull apart any sections that have fused together.
  9. Continue to maintain your dreads regularly with shampoo, conditioner, and regular twisting.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Hair

Starting dreads with short hair can be a bit more challenging than with longer hair, but it's definitely doable. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Be patient. It can take several months to achieve the look you want.
  • Use a good quality dread wax or gel to help hold your dreads together.
  • Consider using a crochet hook to help tighten up your dreads.
  • Be gentle when washing and drying your hair to avoid pulling out your new dreads.
  • Don't be afraid to seek out advice from other people who have started dreads with short hair.

FAQ About Dreads with Short Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads with short hair:

Q: How long does my hair need to be to start dreads?
A: Your hair should be at least 2-3 inches long to start dreads. Q: Can I start dreads with relaxed hair?
A: Yes, you can start dreads with relaxed hair. Just be sure to avoid using any products that contain harsh chemicals that can damage your hair. Q: How often should I wash my new dreads?
A: You should wash your new dreads at least once a week, but no more than twice a week. Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup.

Hair Care for New Dreads

Caring for your new dreads is essential to keeping them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using any products that contain harsh chemicals or residue, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair dryer on a low setting to help dry your dreads.
  • Be gentle when washing and drying your hair to avoid pulling out your new dreads.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase to help protect your dreads while you sleep.

Starting dreads with short hair can be a bit of a challenge, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely achievable. Remember to be patient, take good care of your new dreads, and seek out advice from others who have successfully started dreads with short hair. Good luck!
