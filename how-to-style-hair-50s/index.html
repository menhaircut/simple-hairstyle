How To Style Hair 50S A Comprehensive Guide

Are you a vintage lover looking to rock a 50s-inspired hairstyle? Look no further! In this guide, we'll take you through step-by-step instructions on how to style hair 50s, along with some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice. Let's get started!

How to Style Hair 50s

Before we get into the specifics of styling hair 50s, it's important to understand the general characteristics of this era's hairstyles. The 50s was all about volume, curls, and pin-ups. Think Marilyn Monroe and Audrey Hepburn.

To achieve this look, you'll need some tools, such as hot rollers or a curling iron, bobby pins, and hairspray. You'll also need to choose a hairstyle that suits your face shape and hair texture. Some popular 50s hairstyles include the bouffant, victory rolls, and the classic bob.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now let's get into the nitty-gritty of how to style hair 50s. Here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before styling. This will ensure your hair is free of any product buildup, making it easier to work with.

2. Apply heat protectant spray

Before using any hot tools, apply a heat protectant spray to your hair. This will help minimize damage and keep your hair healthy.

3. Use hot rollers or a curling iron

Depending on the hairstyle you choose, you'll need to use either hot rollers or a curling iron to create curls. For example, if you're going for a bouffant, you'll need to use hot rollers to create volume at the crown of your head.

4. Pin your curls in place

Once you've created your curls, use bobby pins to pin them in place. This will help your curls hold their shape and prevent them from falling out throughout the day.

5. Apply hairspray

Finish off your look by applying hairspray to hold everything in place. Be sure to use a high-hold hairspray to ensure your hairstyle lasts all day.

Tips for the Perfect 50s Hairstyle

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect 50s hairstyle:

  • Use a rat-tail comb to create precise parts and sections in your hair
  • Apply mousse to your roots before blow-drying to create extra volume
  • Invest in high-quality hot rollers or a curling iron for the best results
  • Practice makes perfect - don't get discouraged if your first attempt doesn't turn out perfectly

FAQs

What hair length is best for 50s hairstyles?

50s hairstyles can be achieved with any hair length, but they tend to look best on medium to long hair.

How can I make my 50s hairstyle last all day?

Use high-hold hairspray and bobby pins to keep your curls in place. You can also sleep with a silk scarf over your hair to help preserve the style overnight.

Hair Care for 50s Hairstyles

Styling hair 50s can be damaging to your hair if you're not careful. Be sure to take care of your hair by using a deep conditioning treatment once a week and avoiding excessive heat styling.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock a stunning 50s-inspired hairstyle in no time. Happy styling!
