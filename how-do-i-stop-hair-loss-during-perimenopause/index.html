How To Stop Hair Loss During Perimenopause

Written by William Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a woman going through perimenopause, you may have noticed that your hair is thinning or falling out more than usual. This can be a frustrating and stressful experience, but there are ways to prevent or slow down hair loss during this time. In this article, we'll go over some tips and strategies to help you keep your hair healthy and full.

Causes of Hair Loss During Perimenopause

Perimenopause is the transitional period leading up to menopause, which usually occurs in a woman's late 40s or early 50s. During this time, hormone levels fluctuate and can cause a number of symptoms, including hair loss. The main hormone involved in hair loss during perimenopause is estrogen. Estrogen helps to keep hair in the growing phase for longer, but as levels decline, hair may start to shed more than usual.

Other factors that can contribute to hair loss during perimenopause include stress, poor nutrition, genetics, and certain medications. Thyroid problems and autoimmune conditions can also cause hair loss, so it's important to rule out these underlying issues with a doctor.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss During Perimenopause

While you can't completely prevent hair loss during perimenopause, there are some things you can do to slow it down or minimize its effects:

1. Eat a healthy diet

Make sure you're getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet to support healthy hair growth. Some nutrients that are particularly important for hair health include biotin, iron, and vitamin D.

2. Manage stress

Stress can cause hair loss, so try to find ways to manage it, such as exercise, meditation, or deep breathing.

3. Avoid harsh styling practices

Avoid tight hairstyles, hot tools, and chemical treatments that can damage hair and contribute to breakage.

4. Use gentle hair products

Choose shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are gentle and free of harsh chemicals that can irritate the scalp or damage hair.

5. Consider hormone therapy

If your hair loss is severe or causing significant distress, hormone therapy may be an option. This involves taking estrogen or other hormones to help balance your levels and reduce hair loss.

Hair Care During Perimenopause

In addition to the tips listed above, there are some specific hair care practices that can help keep your hair healthy and strong during perimenopause:

1. Wash your hair regularly

Washing your hair regularly can help remove any buildup that can weigh it down and make it look thinner. However, don't overdo it - washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause dryness and breakage.

2. Use a gentle shampoo

Choose a shampoo that is gentle and formulated for your hair type. Look for products that are free of sulfates, which can be harsh and drying.

3. Condition your hair regularly

Conditioning your hair can help keep it hydrated and prevent breakage. Choose a conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type and use it after every wash.

4. Avoid heat styling

Hot tools like curling irons, straighteners, and hair dryers can damage hair and cause breakage. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and if you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can hair loss during perimenopause be reversed?

While it's difficult to completely reverse hair loss during perimenopause, there are things you can do to slow it down or minimize its effects. Following the tips listed above can help keep your hair healthy and strong.

Will hormone therapy help with hair loss during perimenopause?

Hormone therapy may be an option for women experiencing severe hair loss during perimenopause. However, it's important to talk to your doctor about the risks and benefits of hormone therapy, as it can increase the risk of certain health problems.

How long does hair loss during perimenopause last?

Hair loss during perimenopause can last for several years, but it usually improves after menopause when hormone levels stabilize.

In conclusion, hair loss during perimenopause is a common and frustrating experience, but there are ways to prevent or slow it down. By following a healthy diet, managing stress, and taking good care of your hair, you can keep it looking healthy and full. If you're experiencing severe hair loss or other symptoms of perimenopause, talk to your doctor about your options for treatment.
