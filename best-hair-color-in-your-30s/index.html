Best Hair Color In Your 30S How To Choose And Maintain

In this article, we will guide you through the process of selecting your ideal hair color, as well as how to maintain it and keep your locks healthy and shiny. Let's dive in!

How to Choose the Best Hair Color in Your 30s

When selecting a hair color in your 30s, it's essential to consider your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair color. You want to choose a shade that complements your features and enhances your natural beauty. Here are some tips to help you pick the best hair color for you:

Consider Your Skin Tone

If you have warm undertones, go for hair colors with golden or honey hues, such as caramel or chestnut brown. If you have cool undertones, opt for ashy or cool-toned colors, such as platinum blonde or ash brown. If you have neutral undertones, you can pull off both warm and cool tones.

Look at Your Eye Color

Your eye color can also help guide you in choosing a hair color. If you have blue or green eyes, go for warm, golden hues like honey blonde or auburn. If you have brown eyes, you can pull off most hair colors, but warm, rich tones like chocolate brown or copper red can really make your eyes pop.

Consider Your Natural Hair Color

If you want a low-maintenance hair color, consider staying within a few shades of your natural hair color. This will allow for a more natural grow-out and require fewer touch-ups. If you want a drastic change, consider a balayage or ombre technique that gradually fades into your new hair color.

Step-by-Step Guide to Coloring Your Hair at Home

If you're on a budget or prefer to color your hair at home, here's a step-by-step guide to achieving your best hair color in your 30s:

Step 1: Choose Your Hair Color

Refer back to the tips above and choose a hair color that complements your features and natural hair color. Make sure to also read reviews and choose a high-quality hair color brand.

Step 2: Do a Strand Test

Before applying the hair color all over your head, do a strand test to ensure you like the color and that you're not allergic to any of the ingredients. Apply the hair color to a small section of hair and wait 24 hours before proceeding.

Step 3: Protect Your Skin and Clothing

Apply petroleum jelly to your hairline and ears to prevent the hair color from staining your skin. Also, wear old clothing or a smock to avoid ruining your clothes.

Step 4: Mix and Apply the Hair Color

Follow the instructions on the hair color box to mix the color and developer. Apply the hair color to your roots first and work your way down to the ends. Make sure to saturate your hair evenly.

Step 5: Wait and Rinse

Follow the recommended processing time on the hair color box, then rinse the hair color out thoroughly with cool water. Apply the included conditioner to your hair and let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing out.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Color

Once you've achieved your best hair color in your 30s, you want to make sure to maintain it for as long as possible. Here are some tips for keeping your hair color vibrant and healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for color-treated hair
  • Avoid hot tools and excessive heat styling, as it can fade your hair color
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner weekly to keep your locks hydrated and healthy
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater, as they can also fade your hair color
  • Touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair color looking fresh

FAQs About Hair Coloring in Your 30s

Is it safe to color my hair in my 30s?

Yes, it's safe to color your hair in your 30s as long as you use a high-quality hair color brand and follow the instructions carefully. If you're pregnant or breastfeeding, it's essential to consult with your doctor before coloring your hair.

Can I color my hair at home?

Yes, you can color your hair at home, but make sure to choose a high-quality hair color brand and follow the instructions carefully. If you're unsure or want a drastic change, it's best to consult with a professional hairstylist.

How often should I touch up my roots?

It's recommended to touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair color looking fresh. If you have a high-maintenance hair color, such as platinum blonde or bright red, you may need to touch up your roots more frequently.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy and Shiny Locks

Lastly, here are some hair care tips to keep your locks healthy and shiny, regardless of your hair color:

  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote hair growth
  • Avoid brushing wet hair, as it can cause breakage
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle hair gently
  • Avoid using hot tools daily, as it can damage your hair
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth

That concludes our blog post about the best hair color in your 30s! We hope you found this helpful and informative. Remember to choose a hair color that makes you feel confident and beautiful, and take good care of your locks to keep them healthy and shiny. Thanks for reading!
