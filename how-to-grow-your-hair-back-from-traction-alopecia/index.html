How To Grow Your Hair Back From Traction Alopecia

Written by Eveline Aug 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Traction alopecia is a type of hair loss that occurs when hair is pulled too tightly for a prolonged period. This kind of hair loss is common among people who frequently wear tight hairstyles such as braids, cornrows, and weaves. If you're experiencing traction alopecia, don't panic. There are ways to grow your hair back. In this post, we'll show you how to grow your hair back from traction alopecia.

How to

Stop Wearing Tight Hairstyles

The first step to growing your hair back from traction alopecia is to stop wearing tight hairstyles. Tight hairstyles put too much tension on the hair follicles, which can cause your hair to fall out. If you must wear your hair in a tight style, make sure you take breaks in between to give your hair a rest.

Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help to stimulate blood flow to your hair follicles, which can promote hair growth. You can use your fingers or a scalp massager to massage your scalp for about 10 minutes every day. This will help to increase circulation and encourage hair growth.

Use Essential Oils

Essential oils such as peppermint oil, lavender oil, and rosemary oil can help to promote hair growth. You can mix a few drops of these oils with a carrier oil such as coconut oil or olive oil and massage the mixture into your scalp. These oils help to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.

Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you're eating foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals such as iron, zinc, and biotin. These nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth. You can find these nutrients in foods such as leafy greens, nuts, whole grains, and lean proteins.

Take Supplements

If you're not getting enough nutrients from your diet, you can take supplements to help promote hair growth. Biotin supplements are popular for promoting healthy hair growth. You can also take supplements that are rich in vitamins and minerals such as iron and zinc.

Step by Step

If you're ready to start growing your hair back from traction alopecia, follow these steps:

  1. Stop wearing tight hairstyles
  2. Massage your scalp for 10 minutes every day
  3. Use essential oils to promote hair growth
  4. Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals
  5. Take supplements if necessary

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you grow your hair back from traction alopecia:

  • Don't use too much heat on your hair
  • Avoid using chemical treatments on your hair
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a silk or satin scarf
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

FAQ

What is traction alopecia?

Traction alopecia is a type of hair loss that occurs when hair is pulled too tightly for a prolonged period. This kind of hair loss is common among people who frequently wear tight hairstyles such as braids, cornrows, and weaves.

Can traction alopecia be reversed?

Yes, traction alopecia can be reversed. By following the steps outlined in this post, you can grow your hair back from traction alopecia.

How long does it take to grow hair back from traction alopecia?

The length of time it takes to grow your hair back from traction alopecia varies from person to person. It can take anywhere from a few months to a year for your hair to grow back.

Hair Care

It's essential to take care of your hair to prevent hair loss. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using chemical treatments on your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

Growing your hair back from traction alopecia takes time and patience. By following these tips, you can promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension on your hair follicles. With time, your hair will grow back, and you'll have healthy, beautiful hair once again.
