How To Layer Cut Your Own Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to update your hairstyle and add some dimension to your locks, layering your hair is a great option. While going to a salon can be expensive, you can easily layer cut your own hair at home with a few basic tools and some patience. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a layered look that will have heads turning. Let's get started!

How To

What You'll Need:

- A pair of hair cutting scissors
- A comb
- A mirror
- Hair clips or elastics
- A spray bottle filled with water (optional)

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you begin, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to cut and will give you a better idea of how your hair will look once it's styled. If you have curly hair, consider straightening it beforehand to make the cutting process easier.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair down the middle and clip one side up. Take a small section of hair at the back of your head and comb it straight down. This will be your guide for the rest of your cut.

Step 3: Cut Your First Layer

Using your hair cutting scissors, cut a small section of hair at the length you want your shortest layer to be. It's important to only cut a small section at a time to avoid cutting too much hair. Use your guide section to determine how much hair to cut.

Step 4: Continue Cutting Layers

Once you've cut your first layer, take another section of hair and use the previous layer as your guide to cut the new layer. Continue cutting layers around your head, making sure to keep them even on both sides.

Step 5: Blend Your Layers

After you've cut all your layers, it's time to blend them together. Take your comb and comb through your hair, making sure each layer blends seamlessly into the next. If you notice any uneven spots, trim them with your scissors.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Now that you've layered your hair, it's time to style it. You can use a straightener or curling iron to add some texture and dimension to your locks. Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.

Tips

- Always cut your hair when it's dry to avoid cutting too much.
- Use your guide section to determine the length for each layer.
- Take your time and cut small sections at a time. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
- If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider practicing on a wig or mannequin head first.

FAQ

Can I layer cut my hair if it's curly?

Absolutely! In fact, layering curly hair can enhance its natural texture and give it more volume. Just make sure to cut your hair when it's dry and use your curls as a guide when cutting.

How often should I layer cut my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to change up your style. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so you may want to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers.

Hair Care

To keep your layered haircut looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type and avoid using too much heat when styling. Use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools, and consider getting regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh.

Layer cutting your own hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and steps, you can achieve a salon-worthy look without breaking the bank. Happy cutting!
