What Hair To Use For Dread Extensions A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Nov 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some length and volume to your dreadlocks, dread extensions are a great option. However, choosing the right type of hair can make all the difference in the final result. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about what hair to use for dread extensions.

Description: Understanding Dread Extensions

Dread extensions are artificial hair that is attached to your existing dreadlocks. These extensions can be made from a variety of materials, including synthetic and human hair. The extensions are added to the existing dreadlocks using a variety of methods, including crochet, braiding, and fusion.

How To: Choosing the Right Type of Hair for Dread Extensions

When it comes to choosing the right type of hair for dread extensions, there are a few things you need to consider:

Hair Texture

The texture of the hair you choose will determine how well it blends with your existing dreadlocks. If you have coarse hair, you'll want to choose hair that is similarly coarse. If you have fine hair, you'll want to choose hair that is finer in texture.

Hair Length

The length of the hair you choose will determine how long your dread extensions will be. If you want longer extensions, you'll need to choose longer hair.

Hair Color

The color of the hair you choose will determine how well it matches your existing dreadlocks. If you have dark hair, you'll want to choose hair that is similarly dark. If you have light hair, you'll want to choose hair that is lighter in color.

Step-by-Step Guide: Installing Dread Extensions

Once you've chosen the right type of hair for your dread extensions, it's time to install them. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you begin, make sure your hair is clean and dry. If you need to wash your hair, do so the day before you plan to install your extensions.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into small sections where you want to add the extensions.

Step 3: Attach the Extensions

Using your chosen attachment method (crochet, braiding, or fusion), attach the extensions to your existing dreadlocks. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully to avoid damaging your hair or the extensions.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once your extensions are in place, you can style your hair as usual. However, be gentle with your new extensions to avoid damaging them.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dread Extensions

Now that your dread extensions are in place, it's important to take good care of them. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly to keep your dread extensions clean and free from buildup. However, be gentle when washing your hair to avoid damaging the extensions.

Tip 2: Moisturize Your Hair

Dread extensions can be prone to dryness, so it's important to moisturize your hair regularly. Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair hydrated.

Tip 3: Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on your dread extensions, as this can cause them to come loose or break. Be gentle when styling your hair, and avoid using heat styling tools.

FAQs: Frequently Asked Questions About Dread Extensions

Q: How long do dread extensions last?

A: Dread extensions can last anywhere from a few weeks to several months, depending on the type of hair used and how well they are maintained.

Q: Can I use synthetic hair for my dread extensions?

A: Yes, you can use synthetic hair for your dread extensions. However, keep in mind that synthetic hair may not blend as well with your natural hair as human hair.

Q: Can I install dread extensions myself?

A: It is possible to install dread extensions yourself, but it's recommended that you have them installed by a professional to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care: Taking Care of Your Natural Hair

While dread extensions can be a great way to add length and volume to your hair, it's important not to neglect your natural hair. Here are some tips for taking care of your natural hair:

Tip 1: Moisturize Your Hair

Just like your dread extensions, your natural hair needs to be moisturized regularly. Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair hydrated.

Tip 2: Avoid Overstyling Your Hair

Overstyling your hair can cause damage and breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools and be gentle when styling your hair.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair at Night

Protect your hair at night by sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase, or by wearing a satin bonnet or scarf.

Conclusion

Choosing the right type of hair for your dread extensions is crucial for achieving the look you want. By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy beautiful, healthy dreadlocks for years to come.
