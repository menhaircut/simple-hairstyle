How To Thin Out Natural Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you have thick, unruly curls that are difficult to manage? Thinning out your natural curly hair can help to reduce volume and make it more manageable. In this article, we’ll show you how to thin out your natural curly hair in a few easy steps. From tools and techniques to tips and care, we’ve got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You’ll Need
  • How to Thin Out Natural Curly Hair
  • Tips for Thinning Out Curly Hair

Tools You’ll Need

Before you start thinning out your natural curly hair, you’ll need a few tools:

  • Thinning shears
  • Regular hair cutting shears
  • Comb
  • Hair clips

It’s essential to use the right tools when thinning out your curly hair to avoid damaging or breaking it.

How to Thin Out Natural Curly Hair

Follow these simple steps to thin out your natural curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual. Use a hydrating conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips.
  3. Start thinning out your hair by using the thinning shears. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut off some of the hair with the thinning shears, starting from the middle of the section and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process throughout your hair, focusing on the areas where you want to reduce volume.
  4. Use the regular hair cutting shears to trim any uneven ends or stray hairs.
  5. Comb your hair again to ensure that it’s even and well-blended.

Take your time and be patient when thinning out your curly hair. It’s better to start with a little hair and work your way up to the desired volume rather than cutting too much hair and ending up with a thin, frizzy mess.

Tips for Thinning Out Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your curly hair:

  • Start with dry hair to get a better idea of how much hair you need to remove.
  • Use the thinning shears sparingly to avoid over-thinning your hair.
  • Focus on the areas where you want to reduce volume, such as the crown or the sides of your head.
  • Don’t cut too much hair at once. Start with small sections and work your way up to the desired volume.
  • Be extra careful when using thinning shears near the ends of your hair. You don’t want to create split ends or damage your hair.

FAQs

Can I thin out my curly hair at home?

Yes, you can thin out your curly hair at home with the right tools and techniques. However, if you’re not confident in your skills or are worried about damaging your hair, it’s best to visit a professional stylist.

How often should I thin out my curly hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how much volume you want to reduce. Generally, it’s recommended to thin out your curly hair every 6-8 weeks.

Will thinning out my curly hair make it look flat?

No, thinning out your curly hair shouldn’t make it look flat if done correctly. It will help to reduce volume and make your curls more manageable without losing their shape.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Out Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind after thinning out your curly hair:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Air dry your hair instead of using a blow dryer to prevent frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair nourished and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve the perfect balance of volume and manageability for your natural curly hair. Remember to be patient and take your time, and you’ll have gorgeous, healthy curls in no time!
