How Do You Keep Your Eyebrows Dry After Microblading

Written by Eveline Sep 12, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you've recently undergone microblading, you know that it's important to keep your eyebrows dry during the healing process. Moisture can interfere with the pigment's ability to settle into the skin and can lead to fading or even infection. In this article, we'll discuss how to keep your eyebrows dry after microblading, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and FAQ.

  • How To Keep Your Eyebrows Dry After Microblading
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Keeping Your Eyebrows Dry
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Microblading

How To Keep Your Eyebrows Dry After Microblading

The first and most important step in keeping your eyebrows dry after microblading is to avoid getting them wet. This means no showering, washing your face, or sweating for the first few days after the procedure. You'll also want to avoid any activities that might cause you to get water or sweat on your face, such as swimming, hot tubs, or saunas.

Another important step is to avoid using any products on your eyebrows for the first week after microblading. This includes makeup, moisturizers, and sunscreen. These products can interfere with the healing process and prevent the pigment from settling properly.

Finally, you'll want to be careful when you sleep. Try to avoid sleeping on your stomach or side, as this can cause you to sweat and rub your eyebrows against your pillow. Instead, sleep on your back and use a clean, soft pillowcase.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here is a step-by-step guide to keeping your eyebrows dry after microblading:

  1. Avoid getting your eyebrows wet for the first few days after microblading.
  2. Avoid using any products on your eyebrows for the first week.
  3. Be careful when you sleep and try to sleep on your back.
  4. Avoid sweating or getting water on your face for at least a week.
  5. If you must wash your face, be very gentle and avoid your eyebrows.

Tips for Keeping Your Eyebrows Dry

Here are some additional tips for keeping your eyebrows dry after microblading:

  • Wear a shower cap or use a washcloth to protect your eyebrows while showering.
  • Avoid touching your eyebrows or scratching them during the healing process.
  • Avoid direct sunlight or tanning beds, as this can cause the pigment to fade.
  • Use a straw when drinking to avoid getting water or other liquids on your face.
  • Be patient and wait for your eyebrows to fully heal before getting them wet or using products on them.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about keeping your eyebrows dry after microblading:

Q: Can I wash my face during the healing process?

A: Yes, but you should avoid your eyebrows and be very gentle when washing your face.

Q: Can I wear makeup after microblading?

A: No, you should avoid using any products on your eyebrows for the first week after microblading.

Q: Can I exercise after microblading?

A: You should avoid sweating or getting water on your face for at least a week after microblading.

Hair Care After Microblading

After microblading, you'll want to be careful when washing and styling your hair. Try to avoid getting shampoo or conditioner on your eyebrows, and be gentle when brushing or blow-drying your hair. You should also avoid using any hair products that contain alcohol or harsh chemicals, as these can irritate the skin and interfere with the healing process.

By following these tips and taking good care of your eyebrows after microblading, you can ensure that your new look lasts for as long as possible.
