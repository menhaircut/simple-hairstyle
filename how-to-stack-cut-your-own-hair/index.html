How To Stack Cut Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 26, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every time you need a trim? Learning how to stack cut your own hair can save you time and money while still giving you a professional-looking haircut. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to achieve a stacked haircut at home.

Description

Stacking your hair involves cutting the top layers shorter than the bottom ones, giving your hair a graduated look. This technique is suitable for all hair lengths, from short to long, and works well for adding volume and movement to your hair.

How To Stack Cut Your Own Hair

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to cut. Avoid using any styling products as they can weigh down your hair and alter its shape.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into two sections – the top section and the bottom section. The bottom section should be larger than the top one, as this will be the foundation for your stacked haircut.

Step 3: Cut the bottom section

Starting from the back of your head, cut the bottom section of your hair straight across. Be sure to keep the length consistent throughout the section. Use a mirror to check the length and adjust if necessary.

Step 4: Cut the top section

Take a small section of hair from the top layer, hold it between your fingers, and cut it at an angle. The length of the hair in this section should be shorter than the bottom section. Repeat this step with each section of hair on the top layer, working your way towards the front of your head.

Step 5: Blend the layers

Using a pair of scissors, blend the layers together by cutting into the hair at an angle. This will create a seamless transition between the layers and give your hair a natural-looking finish.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when stack cutting your own hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired to avoid making mistakes.
  • Check the length of your hair after each section to ensure consistency.

FAQ

Is stack cutting suitable for all hair types?

Yes, stack cutting works well for all hair types, from straight to curly. However, the technique may need to be adjusted based on your hair’s natural texture.

Can I stack cut my own hair if it’s very long?

Yes, you can stack cut long hair, but it may be more challenging to do on your own. Consider enlisting the help of a friend or family member to make the process easier.

Hair Care

After you’ve finished cutting your hair, be sure to take care of it properly to maintain its health and shine. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that’s suitable for your hair type, and avoid using heating tools too often. Regular trims every 4-6 weeks can also help keep your hair looking fresh and healthy.

Learning how to stack cut your own hair can be a game-changer for your beauty routine. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a stunning stacked haircut without ever leaving your home. Happy cutting!
