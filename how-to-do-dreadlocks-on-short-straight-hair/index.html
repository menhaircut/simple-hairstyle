How To Do Dreadlocks On Short Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to do dreadlocks on short straight hair. Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are a symbol of culture, art, and individuality. However, many people believe that dreadlocks are only suitable for people with naturally curly or kinky hair. This is not true! Anyone can get dreadlocks, regardless of their hair type. In this guide, we will show you how to do dreadlocks on short straight hair. We will also provide tips on how to care for your dreadlocks to keep them healthy and looking great.

How to Do Dreadlocks on Short Straight Hair

Before we begin, there are a few things you need to know. First, dreadlocks take time and patience to create. You cannot get them overnight. Second, you will need to choose a method that works best for your hair type and texture. Third, you will need to have the right tools and products for the job. With that said, let's get started!

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start the dreadlocking process, you need to wash your hair thoroughly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup. This will help your hair lock better. Rinse your hair with warm water and towel dry it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small, equal parts. You can use a rat tail comb to create neat sections. Make sure that each section is no bigger than the size of a pencil.

Step 3: Apply Dread Wax

Apply a small amount of dread wax to each section of your hair. Dread wax helps to bind the hair together, making it easier to form dreadlocks. Be careful not to use too much wax, as this can make your hair look greasy and attract dirt.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Take a section of hair and hold it firmly at the root. Use a metal comb to backcomb the hair in a downward motion towards the root. This will create a tangled, matted effect. Repeat this process on each section of hair.

Step 5: Twist Your Hair

After backcombing, twist the hair in a clockwise direction. This will help to tighten the dreadlocks and give them a more uniform shape.

Step 6: Secure Your Dreadlocks

Use a small rubber band to secure the end of each dreadlock. This will prevent the hair from unraveling.

Step 7: Let Your Hair Dry

Allow your hair to air dry for several hours. Do not use a hair dryer, as this can cause your hair to become frizzy and damage your dreadlocks.

Step 8: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Once your dreadlocks are dry, you can remove the rubber bands. Use a crochet hook to pull loose hair into the dreadlocks. This will help to tighten them and give them a more polished look. You can also use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair once a week.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreadlocks

Here are some tips to help you maintain your dreadlocks:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your dreadlocks from becoming frizzy
  • Avoid using heavy oils or greasy products on your hair, as this can attract dirt and cause buildup
  • Avoid using a hair dryer or flat iron on your dreadlocks, as this can cause damage and weaken them
  • Avoid using regular shampoo or conditioners on your dreadlocks, as they can leave behind residues and weaken them
  • Visit a professional stylist every few months to have your dreadlocks maintained and tightened

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreadlocks:

Can I get dreadlocks on short straight hair?

Yes, you can! Follow the steps in our guide to create dreadlocks on short straight hair.

Do I need to use wax to create dreadlocks?

No, you don't. Wax can help to bind the hair together, but it's not necessary. You can also use a locking gel or cream to create dreadlocks.

How long does it take to create dreadlocks?

It depends on your hair type and texture. It can take anywhere from a few hours to several days to create dreadlocks.

How do I remove dreadlocks?

You can remove dreadlocks by cutting them off, but this can damage your hair. It's best to visit a professional stylist who can remove them safely.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Dreadlocks require special care to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips on how to care for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair once a week with a residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using heavy oils or greasy products on your hair
  • Avoid using a hair dryer or flat iron on your dreadlocks
  • Avoid using regular shampoo or conditioners on your dreadlocks
  • Use a crochet hook to pull loose hair into the dreadlocks to keep them tight
  • Visit a professional stylist every few months to have your dreadlocks maintained and tightened

Now that you know how to do dreadlocks on short straight hair, you can experiment with different styles and designs. Remember to be patient and take good care of your dreadlocks to keep them healthy and looking great!
