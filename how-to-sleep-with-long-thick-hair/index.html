How To Sleep With Long Thick Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Jul 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Long, thick hair is a blessing, but it can also be a curse when it comes to getting a good night's sleep. Tossing and turning can lead to tangled hair and even breakage, which can be frustrating and damaging. Fortunately, there are some simple tips and tricks you can use to help you sleep more comfortably and keep your hair healthy. In this article, we'll share our top tips on how to sleep with long thick hair.

  • How to Sleep with Long Thick Hair
  • Step by Step Guide

How to Sleep with Long Thick Hair

The key to sleeping comfortably with long thick hair is to keep it in place and prevent it from getting tangled or damaged while you sleep. Here are some tips on how to do it:

1. Brush Your Hair Before Bed

Before you go to bed, take a few minutes to brush your hair thoroughly. This will help remove any tangles or knots and make it easier to manage while you sleep.

2. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Silk pillowcases are gentle on your hair and help prevent breakage and frizz. They also help keep your hair in place while you sleep, reducing the risk of tangling or knots. If you don't have a silk pillowcase, you can use a silk scarf or wrap your hair in a silk bonnet.

3. Tie Your Hair in a Loose Bun or Braid

Tying your hair in a loose bun or braid can help prevent it from getting tangled or damaged while you sleep. Make sure it's not too tight, as this can cause breakage and discomfort.

4. Use Hair Oil or Serum

Using a hair oil or serum before bed can help keep your hair hydrated and prevent tangles and breakage. Apply a small amount of oil or serum to the ends of your hair and work it through with your fingers.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to sleep with long thick hair:

Step 1: Brush Your Hair

Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to remove any tangles or knots from your hair.

Step 2: Apply Hair Oil or Serum

Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair and work it through with your fingers.

Step 3: Tie Your Hair in a Loose Bun or Braid

Tie your hair in a loose bun or braid, making sure it's not too tight.

Step 4: Use a Silk Pillowcase

Use a silk pillowcase or wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet to help keep it in place and prevent tangling or knots.

Step 5: Get Some Restful Sleep

Get a good night's sleep and wake up with healthy, tangle-free hair.

Tips for Sleeping with Long Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for sleeping with long thick hair:

1. Avoid Sleeping with Wet Hair

Sleeping with wet hair can cause breakage and damage. Make sure your hair is dry before you go to bed.

2. Use a Satin or Silk Scarf

If you don't have a silk pillowcase, you can use a satin or silk scarf to wrap your hair and protect it while you sleep.

3. Keep Your Hair Loose

Try to avoid tight hairstyles, as they can cause breakage and discomfort. Keep your hair loose and relaxed while you sleep.

4. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair hydrated and prevent tangles and breakage. Apply a small amount to your hair before bed.

5. Invest in a Good Quality Pillow

A good quality pillow can help support your head and neck and reduce the risk of hair tangling and breakage.

FAQs

Q. Can sleeping with long thick hair cause breakage?

A. Yes, sleeping with long thick hair can cause breakage if it gets tangled or pulled while you sleep.

Q. How often should I brush my long thick hair?

A. It's best to brush your hair twice a day, once in the morning and once before bed.

Q. How can I prevent my hair from tangling while I sleep?

A. You can prevent your hair from tangling by tying it in a loose bun or braid, using a silk pillowcase, and using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.

Hair Care Tips for Long Thick Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for long thick hair:

1. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Using a wide-toothed comb can help prevent breakage and damage when you're brushing your hair.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends and breakage.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause breakage. Try to avoid using heat on your hair and use heat protectant products if you do.

4. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

5. Eat a Balanced Diet

A healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can sleep more comfortably and keep your long thick hair healthy and beautiful. Sweet dreams!
