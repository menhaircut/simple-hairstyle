How To Hide Hair Loss At The Crown Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Jul 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss at the crown of your head? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many men and women experience hair loss in this area, which can be frustrating and make you feel self-conscious. Fortunately, there are several ways to hide hair loss at the crown and boost your confidence. In this article, we'll share some effective tips and tricks to help you conceal hair loss and feel great about your hair again.

How to Hide Hair Loss at the Crown: The Basics

Before we dive into specific tips, let's review some basic principles for hiding hair loss at the crown:

  • Choose the right hairstyle: Certain hairstyles can minimize the appearance of hair loss at the crown. Avoid slicked-back styles that expose your scalp, and opt for hairstyles with more volume and texture.
  • Use hair products wisely: Products like volumizing mousse and dry shampoo can help add volume and texture to your hair, but be careful not to overdo it. Too much product can make your hair look greasy or flat.
  • Consider hair extensions: If you're willing to invest in hair extensions, they can be a great way to add volume and length to your hair and conceal hair loss. Just be sure to choose high-quality extensions that match your natural hair color and texture.

How to Hide Hair Loss at the Crown: Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, let's go through some specific steps you can follow to hide hair loss at the crown:

Step 1: Wash and condition your hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are gentle and nourishing. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, which can further damage your hair and scalp.

Step 2: Apply volumizing mousse

After you've washed and conditioned your hair, apply a small amount of volumizing mousse to your roots. This will help add volume and texture to your hair, making it appear fuller and thicker.

Step 3: Blow-dry your hair

Use a blow dryer and a round brush to dry your hair, focusing on the crown area. Lift sections of hair with the brush and blow-dry them upwards to create more volume.

Step 4: Use a hair concealer

If you have noticeable hair loss at the crown, consider using a hair concealer product. These products come in spray or powder form and can be applied directly to the scalp to cover up bald spots and create the illusion of fuller hair.

Step 5: Style your hair

Once you've applied a hair concealer, style your hair as desired. Avoid slicked-back styles that expose your scalp, and opt for hairstyles with more volume and texture. Use a light-hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

While these tips can help you hide hair loss at the crown, it's important to also take care of your hair and scalp to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools
  • Massage your scalp to improve blood flow and stimulate hair growth
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Frequently Asked Questions

Can hair loss at the crown be reversed?

While there is no guaranteed way to reverse hair loss, there are several treatments that may help. These include medications like minoxidil and finasteride, as well as hair transplant surgery.

Can hair extensions make hair loss worse?

When applied correctly by a professional, hair extensions should not make hair loss worse. However, if they are applied too tightly or improperly, they can damage your natural hair and scalp.

Are hair concealers safe to use?

Most hair concealers are safe to use and do not cause any harm to your hair or scalp. However, it's important to choose a high-quality product and follow the instructions carefully to avoid any potential side effects.

Conclusion

Hiding hair loss at the crown can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, it's possible to achieve a fuller, more confident hairstyle. Remember to choose the right hairstyle, use hair products wisely, and consider hair extensions or concealers if needed. And always prioritize the health of your hair and scalp to promote healthy growth and prevent further hair loss.
