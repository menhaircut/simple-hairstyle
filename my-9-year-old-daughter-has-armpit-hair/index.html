My 9 Year Old Daughter Has Armpit Hair A Guide To Understanding And Caring For Your Child

Written by Christine Sep 24, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a parent of a young girl, you may be surprised to discover that your 9 year old daughter has armpit hair. While this may be unsettling at first, it's actually a natural part of puberty and a sign that your child is growing up. In this guide, we'll explore what armpit hair is, why it develops, and how you can help your child navigate this new phase of life.

What is Armpit Hair?

Armpit hair, also known as axillary hair, is the hair that grows in the underarm area of the body. It typically begins to develop during puberty, which is when the body undergoes significant hormonal changes. The hair is usually thicker and coarser than the hair on other parts of the body and serves as a natural barrier to sweat and bacteria.

Why Does Armpit Hair Develop?

Armpit hair develops as a result of hormonal changes in the body. During puberty, the body begins to produce higher levels of androgens, which are male hormones that are found in both males and females. These hormones stimulate the growth of hair in the underarm area, as well as other parts of the body like the pubic area and legs.

How to Talk to Your Child About Armpit Hair

If your 9 year old daughter has armpit hair, it's important to have an open and honest conversation with her about what's happening to her body. Here are some tips for talking to your child about armpit hair:

Be Honest

Explain to your child that armpit hair is a natural part of puberty and that it's nothing to be ashamed of. Let her know that everyone goes through this phase of life and that it's normal.

Be Empathetic

Remember what it was like to go through puberty yourself and try to put yourself in your child's shoes. Let her know that you understand what she's going through and that you're there to support her.

Be Available

Encourage your child to come to you with any questions or concerns she may have about her body. Let her know that you're always available to talk and that there's nothing she can't ask you.

How to Care for Armpit Hair

Now that your child has armpit hair, it's important to help her learn how to care for it. Here are some tips for caring for armpit hair:

Wash Regularly

Encourage your child to wash her armpits regularly to keep them clean and free from bacteria. Use a gentle soap and warm water to avoid irritation.

Shave or Wax (Optional)

If your child is uncomfortable with her armpit hair, she may choose to shave or wax it off. However, this is completely optional and should only be done if she wants to.

Deodorant

As your child enters puberty, she may begin to sweat more and develop body odor. Encourage her to use a deodorant to help control the odor and keep her feeling fresh.

FAQ About Armpit Hair in 9 Year Old Girls

Is it normal for a 9 year old girl to have armpit hair?

Yes, it's completely normal for a 9 year old girl to have armpit hair. It's a sign that her body is going through puberty and is developing as it should.

Should my child shave her armpit hair?

Shaving armpit hair is completely optional and should only be done if your child wants to. It's important to remember that armpit hair serves a natural purpose and is nothing to be ashamed of.

How can I help my child feel more comfortable with her armpit hair?

Encourage your child to talk openly about her feelings and concerns. Let her know that she's not alone and that you're there to support her. Remind her that armpit hair is a natural part of growing up and that it's nothing to be embarrassed about.

Is it safe for my child to use deodorant?

Yes, it's safe for your child to use deodorant. However, it's important to choose a gentle, natural deodorant that won't irritate her skin.

Conclusion

While it may be surprising to discover that your 9 year old daughter has armpit hair, it's important to remember that it's a natural part of growing up. By having an open and honest conversation with your child and helping her learn how to care for her armpit hair, you can help her navigate this new phase of life with confidence and ease.
