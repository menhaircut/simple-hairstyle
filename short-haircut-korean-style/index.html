Short Haircut Korean Style The Ultimate Guide In 2023

Written by Frank Jul 22, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a fresh and trendy haircut, then you might want to consider the short haircut Korean style. This haircut has been gaining popularity in recent years, thanks to K-pop idols and Korean celebrities who have been sporting this look. In this guide, we will show you how to get the short haircut Korean style, provide tips on how to maintain it, and answer some frequently asked questions about this hairstyle.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get the Short Haircut Korean Style
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining the Short Haircut Korean Style
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Short Haircut Korean Style

How to Get the Short Haircut Korean Style

The short haircut Korean style is a versatile and modern haircut that can suit different face shapes and hair types. To get this haircut, you can either go to a salon or do it yourself at home. However, we recommend going to a salon, especially if you're not familiar with cutting hair.

When you go to a salon, show your hair stylist some pictures of the short haircut Korean style that you like. This will give them an idea of what you want and they can customize the haircut to suit your face shape and hair type. Communication is key, so make sure to tell your stylist what you want and ask for their opinion and advice.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you want to try cutting your hair at home, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash your hair and towel dry it.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips.
  3. Start cutting the back of your head first, using a pair of sharp scissors. Cut the hair in a straight line, starting from the nape of your neck and working your way up. Use a comb to hold the hair in place and cut it evenly.
  4. Move to the sides of your head and cut the hair to the desired length.
  5. Cut the bangs, if you want to have them. You can either have straight bangs or side-swept bangs.
  6. Trim the hair around your ears and the nape of your neck.
  7. Style your hair using a hair dryer and some hair products, such as wax or gel.

Tips for Maintaining the Short Haircut Korean Style

Now that you have the short haircut Korean style, here are some tips on how to maintain it:

  • Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks to keep the haircut looking fresh and neat.
  • Use hair products, such as wax or gel, to style your hair and keep it in place.
  • Wash your hair regularly, but avoid using too much shampoo as it can dry out your scalp and hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, as they can damage your hair.

FAQs

1. Will the short haircut Korean style suit my face shape?

Yes, the short haircut Korean style can suit different face shapes, such as oval, round, and heart-shaped. However, it's important to consult with your hair stylist to customize the haircut to suit your face shape and hair type.

2. How do I style my short haircut Korean style?

You can style your short haircut Korean style using hair products, such as wax or gel. You can also blow dry your hair using a hair dryer to add volume and texture. Experiment with different styles, such as a messy look or a sleek look.

3. How often should I wash my hair?

You should wash your hair regularly, but avoid using too much shampoo as it can dry out your scalp and hair. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or as needed depending on your hair type and lifestyle.

Hair Care for Short Haircut Korean Style

To keep your hair healthy and shiny, here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and pollution by wearing a hat or using a hair serum with UV protection.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals, such as biotin and omega-3, which can promote hair growth and strength.

With these tips and tricks, you can rock the short haircut Korean style with confidence and style. Remember to consult with your hair stylist for the best results and enjoy your new look!
