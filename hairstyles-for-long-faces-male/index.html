Hairstyles For Long Faces Male How To Choose The Right One

Written by Frank Sep 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a long face, you might feel like your hairstyle options are limited. However, there are actually plenty of hairstyles that can flatter your features and make you look your best. In this article, we'll go over some tips for choosing the right hairstyle for your long face and suggest some popular options for men.

Description: What is a Long Face?

Before we dive into hairstyles, let's define what we mean by a "long face." A long face is one that is longer than it is wide, with a high forehead and a pronounced jawline. This face shape is sometimes referred to as "oblong" or "rectangular." If you're not sure if you have a long face, measure your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline to see if they are roughly the same width.

How to Choose the Right Hairstyle for a Long Face

When choosing a hairstyle for a long face, the goal is to balance out the proportions of your face and create the illusion of width. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Avoid hairstyles that are too long or too short

Hairstyles that are very short or very long can exaggerate the length of your face. Instead, opt for a medium length that falls somewhere between your chin and your shoulders.

2. Add volume to the sides

Adding volume to the sides of your hair can create the illusion of width. A side-swept fringe or layers that frame your face can also help balance out your features.

3. Avoid center parts

A center part can make your face look even longer. Instead, try a side part or a tousled, messy style that doesn't have a defined part.

4. Consider facial hair

Facial hair can also help balance out your features. A well-groomed beard or mustache can draw attention away from your jawline and create a more balanced look.

Popular Hairstyles for Long Faces Male

Now that you know what to look for in a hairstyle, here are some popular options for men with long faces:

1. The Side Part

The side part is a classic hairstyle that works well on men with long faces. It adds volume to the sides of your hair and draws attention away from your forehead and jawline.

2. The Textured Crop

The textured crop is a modern, messy style that can work well on men with long faces. The layers and texture add volume to the sides of your hair, while the messy fringe creates a more balanced look.

3. The Quiff

The quiff is a versatile hairstyle that can be adapted to suit different face shapes. For men with long faces, a voluminous quiff can add width to your hair and balance out your features.

4. The Buzz Cut

If you're looking for a low-maintenance hairstyle, the buzz cut can work well on men with long faces. Just make sure to keep some length on top to avoid exaggerating the length of your face.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a long face?

Avoid hairstyles that are very short or very long, as well as center parts. These styles can exaggerate the length of your face.

What hairstyles can help balance out a long face?

Hairstyles with volume on the sides, such as a side part, textured crop, or quiff, can help balance out a long face. Facial hair can also help create a more balanced look.

Can I still wear a fringe if I have a long face?

A side-swept fringe can work well on men with long faces, as it adds volume to the sides of your hair and balances out your features. Avoid a straight, blunt fringe that can make your face look even longer.

Hair Care Tips for Men with Long Faces

1. Use a volumizing shampoo

A volumizing shampoo can help add body and volume to your hair, which can balance out the proportions of your face.

2. Avoid heavy styling products

Heavy styling products can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. Instead, opt for lightweight products that add texture and volume.

3. Get regular trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends. They can also help maintain the shape of your hairstyle and prevent it from becoming too long or too short.

4. Protect your hair from heat damage

If you use heat styling tools, such as a hair dryer or straightener, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

5. Consider adding highlights or lowlights

Adding highlights or lowlights to your hair can create the illusion of depth and texture, which can balance out your features.

By following these tips and choosing a hairstyle that flatters your long face, you can look stylish and confident no matter what the occasion.
