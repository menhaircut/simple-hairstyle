How To Contour A Long Face Shape Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Jun 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Contouring is a makeup technique that can help enhance your facial features by creating the illusion of shadows and highlights. For those with a long face shape, contouring can help balance out the proportions of the face and create a more oval appearance. In this article, we'll be sharing tips and tricks on how to contour a long face shape, so you can achieve a flawless makeup look that complements your natural beauty.

How to Contour a Long Face Shape

Contouring a long face shape involves creating shadows in certain areas of the face to help shorten the appearance of the face. This is done by applying a darker shade of foundation or contour powder to the following areas:

Step 1: Prep Your Skin

Before you begin contouring, it's important to prep your skin with a good moisturizer and primer. This will help create a smooth canvas for your makeup and ensure that it lasts longer throughout the day.

Step 2: Apply Foundation

Apply your foundation as you normally would, making sure to blend it out evenly across your face. Choose a shade that matches your skin tone to avoid any harsh lines or visible contrasts.

Step 3: Highlight Your Features

Use a highlighter to brighten up your features and bring attention to certain areas. Apply it to the top of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and your cupid's bow.

Step 4: Contour Your Face

Now it's time to contour your face. Use a contour powder or a darker shade of foundation to create shadows in the following areas:

  • Under your cheekbones
  • Along your hairline
  • On the sides of your nose
  • Under your jawline

Blend out the contour powder or foundation using a brush or sponge, making sure to blend it seamlessly into your skin.

Step 5: Add Blush

Finish off your look by adding a pop of color to your cheeks with a blush. Choose a shade that complements your skin tone and apply it to the apples of your cheeks, blending it out towards your temples.

Tips for Contouring a Long Face Shape

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when contouring a long face shape:

  • Use a light hand when applying your contour powder or foundation. You can always build up the intensity gradually.
  • Blend out your contouring to avoid any harsh lines or visible contrasts.
  • Choose a shade of contour powder or foundation that is only one or two shades darker than your skin tone to avoid looking too harsh or unnatural.
  • Use a fluffy brush to apply your contour powder or foundation for a more natural look.
  • Consider using a cream contour instead of a powder for a more seamless blend.

FAQ About Contouring a Long Face Shape

Can contouring really make a long face shape appear more oval?

Yes, contouring can help balance out the proportions of a long face shape and create a more oval appearance. By creating shadows in certain areas of the face, you can visually shorten the appearance of the face.

Do I need to contour my entire face?

No, you don't need to contour your entire face. Focus on the areas that you want to create shadows in, such as under your cheekbones and along your hairline.

What type of brush should I use to apply my contour powder or foundation?

Use a fluffy brush for a more natural look. A dense brush may apply too much product and make your contour look too harsh.

Hair Care Tips for a Long Face Shape

In addition to contouring, there are also certain hairstyles that can help complement a long face shape. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid hairstyles that add height to the top of your head, as this can make your face look even longer.
  • Opt for hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face, such as loose waves or curls.
  • Consider getting bangs to help shorten the appearance of your forehead.
  • Avoid hairstyles that are too slicked back or tight, as this can elongate your face even further.

With these tips and tricks, you can contour your long face shape like a pro and achieve a flawless makeup look that complements your natural beauty. Remember to take your time, blend out your contouring, and have fun experimenting with different makeup techniques.
