How To Use Thinning Shears For Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Nov 08, 2023 · 5 min read

Thinning shears are a great tool for hair stylists and home users alike. They can help you achieve a more natural-looking haircut, reduce bulk, and create a softer, more textured look. However, using thinning shears can be tricky, especially if you're new to hair cutting. In this guide, we'll show you how to use thinning shears for hair in a few easy steps.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Use Thinning Shears for Hair

Thinning shears are used to remove bulk from hair and create a softer, more natural look. They have teeth on one blade that help to thin out the hair as you cut. Here are the basic steps for using thinning shears:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Before you start using thinning shears, you need to section the hair you want to thin out. Use hair clips to keep the sections in place and make sure you don't accidentally cut more hair than you intended to.

Step 2: Comb the Hair

Once you have your sections in place, comb the hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you get an even result.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Shears Correctly

Hold the thinning shears in your dominant hand and use your other hand to guide the hair. Make sure that you're holding the shears correctly – the blade with teeth should be on top and the smooth blade should be on the bottom.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting the hair with the thinning shears by taking small sections at a time. Use a gentle, snipping motion to remove some of the hair from the section. Repeat this process until you've thinned out the hair to your liking.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you need a more detailed guide on how to use thinning shears, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will make it easier to see where you need to thin out the hair and ensure that you get an even result.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. You can create as many or as few sections as you like, depending on the thickness of your hair and the style you want to achieve.

Step 3: Start with the Bottom Layers

Start thinning out your hair from the bottom layers. This will ensure that you don't accidentally cut too much hair from the top layers and create an uneven result.

Step 4: Comb the Hair

Once you have a section of hair ready, comb it to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you get an even result.

Step 5: Hold the Thinning Shears Correctly

Hold the thinning shears in your dominant hand and use your other hand to guide the hair. Make sure that you're holding the shears correctly – the blade with teeth should be on top and the smooth blade should be on the bottom.

Step 6: Start Cutting

Start cutting the hair with the thinning shears by taking small sections at a time. Use a gentle, snipping motion to remove some of the hair from the section. Repeat this process until you've thinned out the hair to your liking.

Step 7: Check Your Progress

As you work through each section of hair, check your progress in the mirror. Make sure that you're achieving the look you want and that you're not cutting too much hair from one section.

Step 8: Finish the Cut

Once you've thinned out all the sections of hair, use regular scissors to trim the ends and create the final shape of your haircut. Be sure to blend any uneven areas and check your work in the mirror.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Here are some additional tips to help you use thinning shears effectively:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to thicker sections.
  • Use a comb to help guide the hair into the thinning shears.
  • Hold the thinning shears at a slight angle to create a more natural look.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair from one section or create uneven areas.
  • Practice on a mannequin head or with a friend before trying to cut your own hair.

FAQs

Can you use thinning shears on all hair types?

Yes, you can use thinning shears on all hair types, but they work best on thick, coarse, or curly hair. If you have fine or thin hair, use thinning shears sparingly to avoid creating too much of a choppy or uneven look.

Can you use thinning shears to create layers?

Yes, you can use thinning shears to create layers in your hair. Start with the bottom layers and work your way up, thinning out each section as you go. Be sure to blend the layers together for a natural look.

How often should you use thinning shears on your hair?

You can use thinning shears on your hair as often as you like, but it's best to wait at least a few weeks between haircuts. This will give your hair time to grow back and ensure that you don't thin out too much hair at once.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair looking its best after using thinning shears, follow these hair care tips:

  • Shampoo and condition your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and shiny.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain your style.

By following these tips and using thinning shears correctly, you can achieve a more natural, textured look for your hair.
