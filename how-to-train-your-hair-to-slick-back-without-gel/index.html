How To Train Your Hair To Slick Back Without Gel

Are you tired of using gel to slick back your hair? Do you want to achieve a natural slicked-back hairstyle? You've come to the right place! In this article, we will show you how to train your hair to slick back without gel.

How to Train Your Hair to Slick Back Without Gel

Before we start, it's important to understand that training your hair to slick back without gel takes time and patience. You won't achieve the desired results overnight, but with consistent effort and proper hair care, you will see progress.

The first step is to find the right haircut for your hair type and face shape. A shorter haircut with tapered sides and a longer top works best for slicked-back hair. It's also important to have the right length of hair. Your hair needs to be long enough to slick back but not too long that it falls forward.

Next, start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup. Then, condition your hair to keep it moisturized and healthy. Avoid using hot water as it can dry out your hair and make it harder to style.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to train your hair to slick back without gel:

Step 1: Wet Your Hair

Start by wetting your hair with cold water. This will help to close the hair cuticles and make it easier to slick back.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Cream

Instead of gel, use a styling cream to help hold your hair in place. Apply a small amount of styling cream to your hair, focusing on the roots and the crown. Use a comb to distribute the cream evenly.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair Back

Using a fine-toothed comb, comb your hair back in the direction you want it to go. Start from the crown and work your way down to the sides. Make sure to comb your hair thoroughly to avoid any bumps or tangles.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a blow dryer on a low heat setting, dry your hair while combing it back. This will help to set your hair in place and make it easier to style. Make sure to keep the blow dryer at a safe distance from your hair to avoid heat damage.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Once your hair is dry, finish off with a light hold hairspray to keep it in place. Hold the hairspray can at arm's length and spray evenly over your hair.

Tips for Slicking Back Your Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect slicked-back hairstyle:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair before styling.
  • Don't use too much styling cream as it can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair in the direction you want it to go for best results.
  • Apply hairspray in a well-ventilated area to avoid inhaling the fumes.
  • Try different hairstyles to find the one that suits you best.

FAQs

Can I slick back my hair without any product?

It's possible to slick back your hair without any product, but it may not hold as well. Using a styling cream or pomade can help to keep your hair in place and add shine.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more often. If you have dry hair, you can wash it less frequently. Generally, washing your hair every 2-3 days is recommended.

Hair Care for Slicked-Back Hair

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to follow a proper hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any product buildup.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you can train your hair to slick back without gel and achieve a natural, stylish look. Remember, it takes time and patience, but the end result is worth it!
