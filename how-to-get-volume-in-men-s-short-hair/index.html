How To Get Volume In Men s Short Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your short hair looking flat and lifeless? Do you want to add some volume and texture to your locks? Look no further! In this article, we'll provide step-by-step instructions on how to get volume in men's short hair. From products to techniques, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  1. Products for Volume
  2. Blow Drying Techniques
  3. Styling Tips
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care Tips

Products for Volume

Before we dive into the techniques for adding volume to short hair, it's important to start with the right products. Here are some of our top picks:

1. Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Start your hair care routine with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products are specifically designed to add volume and fullness to your hair, so they're a great place to start.

2. Texturizing Spray

Texturizing sprays add grip and texture to your hair, which can help create volume. Spray it onto damp hair and blow dry for maximum effect.

3. Styling Paste

Styling paste is a great product for adding texture and hold to your hair. Use a small amount to add volume and definition to your hair style.

Blow Drying Techniques

One effective way to add volume to short hair is by blow drying it. Here are some tips to get the most out of your blow dryer:

1. Use a Round Brush

Using a round brush while blow drying can help create lift and volume. Use it to lift your hair at the roots and direct the air flow of the blow dryer towards your hair.

2. Blow Dry Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down can also help create volume. Bend over at the waist and blow dry your hair from the roots to the ends, using your fingers to lift and separate your hair as you go.

3. Use Cool Air

Using cool air at the end of your blow drying session can help set the volume and keep your hair looking full throughout the day.

Styling Tips

Styling your hair in the right way can also help create volume. Here are some tips:

1. Use your Fingers

Instead of using a comb or brush, try using your fingers to style your hair. This will help create a more natural, textured look with plenty of volume.

2. Embrace Messy Styles

Messy hair styles are great for adding volume and texture to short hair. Try a messy, side-swept style or a textured quiff for a more voluminous look.

3. Consider a Haircut

If you're really struggling to add volume to your hair, consider getting a haircut that's specifically designed to add volume. A layered cut or a choppy, textured style can work wonders.

FAQ

1. Can I add volume to my hair without blow drying it?

Yes! While blow drying can be an effective way to add volume, there are other techniques you can use. Try using a texturizing spray or a styling paste to add grip and fullness to your hair.

2. Will using too much product weigh my hair down?

Yes, using too much product can definitely weigh your hair down. Start with a small amount and work your way up if needed.

3. How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people may need to wash their hair every day, while others can go a few days between washes. Use your own judgment and pay attention to how your hair looks and feels.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind:

1. Don't Overwash

Overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it more difficult to add volume. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day.

2. Use a Heat Protectant

If you're using hot tools like a blow dryer or flat iron, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Get Regular Haircuts

Regular haircuts can help keep your hair healthy and looking its best. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Follow these tips and techniques, and you'll be well on your way to adding volume and fullness to your short hair. Experiment with different products and styles to find what works best for you. Good luck!
