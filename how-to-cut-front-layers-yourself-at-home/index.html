How To Cut Front Layers Yourself At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with a little practice and patience, you can master the art of layering your hair at home. In this guide, we will focus on how to cut front layers yourself at home. Not only is this a great way to save money, but you can also get the exact look you want without having to explain it to a stylist. So, let's get started!

How to Cut Front Layers Yourself at Home

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, there are a few things you will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Clips to section your hair

Once you have all of these items, you are ready to start cutting your front layers!

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will give you a better idea of how your hair will look once it's styled. Make sure to comb your hair out so it's easy to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair down the middle. Then, take a section of hair from the top of your head and clip it away. This will be the hair you use to cut your layers.

Step 3: Cut Your First Layer

Starting from the center of your forehead, take a small section of hair from the clipped section. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. This will create a soft, layered look. Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle until you reach the end of your hair.

Step 4: Cut the Other Side

Repeat step three on the other side of your head. Make sure to take small sections of hair and cut at an angle to create a consistent look.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Using your comb, blend the layers you just cut into the rest of your hair. This will give your hair a natural, seamless look.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Take a mirror and hold it up to the back of your head. Check to see if your layers are even on both sides. If not, go back and make small adjustments until you are happy with the result.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Now that your layers are cut, it's time to style your hair. Use a curling iron or straightener to add some texture and volume. You can also use a hair dryer and round brush to give your hair some bounce.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Front Layers

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own front layers:

  • Start small and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Always cut at an angle to create a soft, layered look.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Take breaks if you get tired. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and it's important to take breaks to avoid mistakes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own layers if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own layers if you have curly hair. However, it's important to remember that curly hair can shrink up once it dries, so be careful not to cut too much.

How often should I cut my front layers?

How often you cut your front layers depends on how fast your hair grows and how much you want to maintain your layers. As a general rule, you should cut your layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain your look.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Front Layers

After cutting your own front layers, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat tools every day to prevent damage.
  • Trim any split ends to keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

Cutting your own front layers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With a little patience and practice, you can get the exact look you want without ever leaving your home. So, grab your scissors and get to cutting!
