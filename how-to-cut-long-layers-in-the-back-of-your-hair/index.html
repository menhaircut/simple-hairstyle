How To Cut Long Layers In The Back Of Your Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 20, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your long hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers to your hair can give it the volume and movement it needs, but it can be tricky to cut them yourself. In this article, we'll show you how to cut long layers in the back of your hair in a few simple steps.

Table of Contents

  2. How to Cut Long Layers in the Back of Your Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Long layers are a great way to add depth and texture to your hair. They can be cut in a variety of ways, but for this tutorial, we'll be focusing on how to cut long layers in the back of your hair. This style is perfect for those with long hair who want to add some volume and movement to their locks.

How to Cut Long Layers in the Back of Your Hair

Cutting long layers in the back of your hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it's actually quite simple. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section your hair

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut long layers in the back of your hair:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and style.
  2. Section your hair into two parts: the top section and the bottom section. Clip the top section away for now.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom section and comb it straight down. This will be your guide for the rest of your layers.
  4. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends straight across. This will be your shortest layer.
  5. Take another small section of hair from the bottom section and hold it up to the previous layer. Cut this section so it's slightly longer than the first layer.
  6. Repeat this process, taking small sections of hair and cutting them slightly longer than the previous layer, until you reach the desired length.
  7. Once you've finished the bottom section, unclip the top section and repeat the same process.
  8. Take small sections of hair from the top section and cut them to match the length of the bottom layers.
  9. Blend the layers by taking small sections of hair and cutting them at an angle. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.
  10. Once you've finished cutting all the layers, style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Long Layers in the Back of Your Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting long layers in the back of your hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to add more layers than to take them away.
  • Cut the hair when it's dry. This will give you a better idea of how the layers will look once styled.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to cut small amounts and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Be patient. Cutting long layers in the back of your hair can take some time, but it's worth it for the end result.

FAQs

Can I cut long layers in the back of my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can still cut long layers in the back of your hair if it's curly. Just make sure to cut it when it's dry and to take into account how the curls will affect the length.

What's the best way to maintain long layered hair?

Regular trims are important to keep your long layered hair looking healthy and free of split ends. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair and avoid brushing it when it's wet to prevent breakage.

Can I cut my own hair if it's long?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's long. Just make sure to follow the steps carefully and take your time.

Hair Care Tips for Long Layered Hair

Here are some tips to keep your long layered hair looking its best:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles, as they can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

Now that you know how to cut long layers in the back of your hair, you can give your locks the volume and movement they deserve. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the steps carefully. Happy cutting!
