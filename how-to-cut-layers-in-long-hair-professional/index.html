How To Cut Layers In Long Hair Professional

Written by Frank Jul 29, 2023 · 4 min read

If you want to add some dimension and movement to your long hair, layers are the perfect solution. However, cutting layers in long hair can be a daunting task, especially if you are not a professional hairdresser. In this blog post, we will guide you through the steps of cutting layers in long hair professionally, including some tips and hair care advice.

Description

The layering technique involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create a layered effect. This technique works particularly well for long hair, as it adds volume and texture, making it easier to style. However, cutting layers in long hair requires some skill and knowledge of the right techniques, tools, and products.

How to Cut Layers in Long Hair Professional: Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before starting the layering process, it's important to wash and dry your hair thoroughly. This will help you see the natural fall and texture of your hair, making it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then creating a second part from your ears to the back of your head. This will create four sections: two in the front and two in the back.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Starting with the bottom section of your hair, take small sections and comb them straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends, making sure to cut at an angle to create a soft, blended effect. Repeat this process for the rest of the sections, making sure to cut the hair at different lengths to create a layered effect.

Step 4: Move to the Next Layer

Once you have cut the first layer, move on to the second layer, repeating the same process as before. Make sure to blend the layers together to avoid any harsh lines or choppy edges.

Step 5: Finish with the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top layer of your hair, taking small sections and cutting them at different lengths to create a natural-looking layered effect.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Long Hair Professional

When cutting layers in long hair, it's important to keep the following tips in mind:

  • Use sharp, high-quality scissors to prevent split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut slowly and carefully to avoid any mistakes.
  • Make sure to cut at an angle to create a blended effect.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven layers.
  • Always use a comb to guide your scissors, and comb your hair regularly to ensure an even cut.

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair to maintain my layers?

To maintain your layered hairstyle, it's recommended to get a trim every six to eight weeks. This will help prevent split ends and keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.

Can I cut layers in my own hair?

While it's possible to cut your own layers, it's recommended to seek the help of a professional hairdresser. Cutting layers in long hair requires some skill and knowledge, and it's easy to make mistakes if you're not experienced.

Hair Care Advice

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best after cutting layers, it's important to follow a few hair care tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and split ends.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair smooth and frizz-free.
  • Avoid brushing your hair while it's wet, as this can lead to breakage and damage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking layered hairstyle at home. However, if you're not confident in your cutting skills, it's always best to seek the help of a professional hairdresser.
