What s The Difference Between Skin Fade And Taper

Written by Christine Sep 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to men's hairstyles, there are many different terms that can be confusing. Two of those terms are skin fade and taper. While they may sound similar, they are actually two different hairstyles. In this article, we will explain the difference between skin fade and taper.

Table of Contents:

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description:

A skin fade is a hairstyle that involves cutting the hair very short on the sides and back of the head. The hair gradually gets shorter as it moves towards the top of the head. The hair on the top of the head can be any length, but it is usually longer than the sides and back. The hair is tapered down to the skin, hence the name "skin fade."

A taper is also a hairstyle that involves cutting the hair shorter on the sides and back of the head. However, the hair is not cut as short as in a skin fade. The hair gradually gets shorter as it moves towards the top of the head, but it does not taper down to the skin. Instead, the hair is left longer on the sides and back, creating a more natural look.

How to:

To achieve a skin fade, the hair is usually cut with clippers starting at the bottom of the head and working upwards. The clippers are gradually adjusted to a shorter length as they move up the head. The hair on the top of the head is then cut to the desired length with scissors.

To achieve a taper, the hair is also cut with clippers, but the length is not adjusted as drastically. The hair on the top of the head is then cut to the desired length with scissors.

Step by step:

How to get a skin fade:

  1. Start with longer hair on the top of the head.
  2. Use clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back of the head.
  3. Gradually adjust the length of the clippers as you move up the head.
  4. Cut the hair on the top of the head to the desired length with scissors.

How to get a taper:

  1. Start with longer hair on the top of the head.
  2. Use clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back of the head.
  3. Do not adjust the length of the clippers as drastically as in a skin fade.
  4. Cut the hair on the top of the head to the desired length with scissors.

Tips:

When getting a skin fade or a taper, it is important to communicate with your barber or hairstylist to ensure you get the desired look. Bring in pictures or explain in detail what you want. Also, make sure to use proper hair care products to maintain the style.

FAQ:

Q: Which is better, a skin fade or a taper?

A: It depends on personal preference and what looks best on the individual. Some people prefer the more dramatic look of a skin fade, while others prefer the more natural look of a taper.

Q: Can I get a skin fade or a taper with any hair type?

A: Yes, both styles can be achieved with any hair type. However, the length and texture of the hair may affect the final result.

Hair Care:

To maintain a skin fade or a taper, it is important to use proper hair care products, such as shampoo and conditioner. Also, regular trims are necessary to keep the style looking fresh. Using a styling product, such as pomade or wax, can also help keep the hair in place.

In conclusion, while skin fade and taper may sound similar, they are actually two different hairstyles. Skin fade involves cutting the hair very short on the sides and back of the head, while taper involves cutting the hair shorter but not as drastically. It is important to communicate with your barber or hairstylist to ensure you get the desired look and to use proper hair care products to maintain the style.
