How To Make My Thick Hair Thinner A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. It takes longer to dry, styling can be a hassle, and it can feel heavy and overwhelming. Luckily, there are ways to make your thick hair thinner without sacrificing its healthy shine. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make your thick hair thinner.

How to Make My Thick Hair Thinner

Step 1: Get a Haircut

The first step to making your thick hair thinner is to get a haircut. Talk to your stylist about thinning shears or texturizing techniques that can help remove bulk without shortening your hair. A professional stylist can also recommend a cut that is best for your hair type and face shape.

Step 2: Use the Right Products

Using the right products can make all the difference in making your thick hair thinner. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are designed for thinning hair, as these products help to cleanse the scalp and encourage hair growth. Avoid heavy styling products like gels and pomades, as these can weigh your hair down and make it look even thicker.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help to make it look thinner. Use a blow dryer on a low heat setting and a round brush to help smooth out your hair and remove excess moisture. Avoid using a straightener or curling iron, as these can make your hair look thicker and more voluminous.

Step 4: Try a Keratin Treatment

If you want to make your thick hair thinner for the long-term, consider getting a keratin treatment. Keratin treatments can help to smooth out your hair and reduce frizz, making it look thinner and more manageable. However, be aware that keratin treatments can be expensive and time-consuming.

Tips for Making Your Thick Hair Thinner

Here are some additional tips to help you make your thick hair thinner:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as this can help to reduce breakage and damage.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners, as these can damage your hair and make it look thicker.
  • Consider getting highlights or lowlights, as these can help to break up the thickness of your hair and make it look thinner.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, as this can help to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQs About Making Your Thick Hair Thinner

Q: Will thinning shears damage my hair?

A: No, thinning shears should not damage your hair if used correctly. However, it's important to have a professional stylist use them, as using them improperly can cause damage.

Q: How often should I get a haircut to make my thick hair thinner?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to thin it out. Generally, getting a haircut every 6-8 weeks is a good rule of thumb.

Q: Can I make my thick hair thinner without cutting it?

A: Yes, using the right products and styling techniques can help to make your thick hair look thinner. However, getting a haircut is the most effective way to remove bulk and reduce thickness.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair can be beautiful and healthy with the right care. Here are some tips for taking care of your thick hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools on a regular basis.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and healthy.
  • Get regular haircuts to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you can make your thick hair thinner and more manageable. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and it will reward you with its natural beauty.
