Is Having Layers Good For Thick Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have thick hair, you may be wondering if having layers is a good option for you. Layers in haircuts can add dimension and movement to your hair, but can they work for thick hair? In this article, we will discuss whether having layers is good for thick hair, how to get layered haircuts, tips for styling layered hair, and more.

Description

Layers can work wonders for thick hair by removing some of the weight and bulk, creating movement and texture, and enhancing the natural waves or curls of your hair. However, not all layers are created equal, and it's crucial to choose the right type of layering technique and length to suit your hair type, face shape, and styling preferences.

How to Get Layered Haircuts

To get layered haircuts for thick hair, you should consult with a professional hairstylist who has experience in cutting and styling thick hair. They can recommend the best layering technique and length for your hair type and create a customized look that flatters your features and enhances your hair's natural texture. Some popular layering techniques for thick hair include:

Long Layers

Long layers are ideal for thick hair as they remove some of the weight and add movement without sacrificing the length. Long layers can be done with a razor or scissors, and they can be blended seamlessly into your hair to create a natural-looking flow. Long layers work well for wavy or curly hair as they enhance the bounce and definition of the curls.

Short Layers

Short layers are perfect for adding volume and texture to thick hair, especially if you have a round or square face shape. Short layers are typically cut around the crown area and can be done with a razor or scissors. Short layers work well for straight or slightly wavy hair as they create a tousled, edgy look.

Step by Step

If you want to try layering your thick hair at home, here are some step-by-step instructions:

Step 1:

Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and lift.

Step 2:

Blow-dry your hair until it's about 80% dry, using a round brush to add volume to the roots.

Step 3:

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back.

Step 4:

Starting with the back section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers at the length you want to cut it.

Step 5:

Cut the hair at an angle, using scissors or a razor, to create a layered effect. Repeat this process for every section of hair, working from the back to the front.

Step 6:

Once you've finished cutting all the layers, use a texturizing spray or pomade to add definition and separation to the layers.

Tips

Here are some tips for styling and maintaining layered hair:

  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add body to your hair before blow-drying it.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush, such as a round brush or paddle brush, to style your layered hair.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to prevent split ends and add shine.
  • Get regular trims every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your layered haircut.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques, such as braiding or twisting, to create different looks with your layered hair.

FAQ

1. How do I know if layers will suit my face shape?

Layers can work for most face shapes, but it's important to choose the right length and placement of the layers to flatter your features. Consult with a hairstylist to find the best layering technique for your face shape.

2. Can layers make my hair look thinner?

No, layers can actually make thick hair appear fuller and more voluminous by removing some of the weight and adding movement and texture. However, if the layers are too short or choppy, they can make your hair look thinner.

3. How often should I wash my layered hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or work out regularly, you may need to wash your hair more often. If you have dry or curly hair, you can wash it less frequently to avoid stripping the natural oils. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days and use a dry shampoo in between washes to refresh your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your layered hair healthy and shiny, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the natural oils from your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet as it can cause damage and breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers instead.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and pollution by wearing a hat or scarf and using a hair serum with UV protection.

In conclusion, having layers can be a great option for thick hair as it can add movement, texture, and dimension to your hair. Whether you go for long layers or short layers, make sure to consult with a professional stylist and follow the proper hair care and styling tips to keep your layered hair looking its best.
