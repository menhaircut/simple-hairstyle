How To Cut Pixie Fringe A Step By Step Guide

Description

Pixie cuts have been a popular haircut for women for years, and with good reason. They are chic, trendy, and easy to maintain. One of the defining features of a pixie cut is the fringe, which is cut short and styled in a variety of ways. If you're looking to cut your own pixie fringe, this guide will show you how.

How to Cut Pixie Fringe

Before you start cutting your fringe, you'll need to decide what kind of fringe you want. There are a few different styles to choose from:

  • Straight fringe
  • Side-swept fringe
  • Textured fringe

Once you've decided on a style, it's time to get started. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own pixie fringe:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. This will give you a better idea of how your fringe will look when it's styled.
  2. Use the comb to separate the section of hair that you want to cut. For a straight fringe, this will be a section of hair that is about 1 inch wide and extends from the hairline to just above the eyebrows. For a side-swept fringe, the section of hair should be wider on one side than the other.
  3. Hold the section of hair firmly between your fingers and comb it straight down over your forehead.
  4. Using the scissors, make small, vertical cuts into the hair. Start at the center of the section and work your way outward. This will create a textured, choppy look.
  5. If you want a straight fringe, cut the hair straight across. For a side-swept fringe, angle the scissors slightly to create a diagonal line.
  6. Check your work in the mirror. If you need to make any adjustments, do so carefully.
  7. Repeat on the other side.

Tips for Cutting Pixie Fringe

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own pixie fringe:

  • Start with less hair than you think you need. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can create uneven cuts and damage your hair.
  • If you're not confident in your ability to cut your own fringe, consider asking a friend or a professional stylist for help.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting pixie fringe:

How often should I trim my pixie fringe?
It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your fringe starts to look unkempt. As a general rule, you should trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks.
What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my fringe?
If you accidentally cut too much hair, don't panic. There are a few ways to fix a botched fringe, such as using hair extensions or styling your hair in a different way.
Can I cut my fringe when it's wet?
It's best to cut your fringe when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to work with and may result in uneven cuts.

Hair Care Tips for Pixie Fringe

Now that you've cut your own pixie fringe, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair serum or oil to keep your hair moisturized and frizz-free.
  • Get regular trims to keep your fringe looking fresh.

Cutting your own pixie fringe can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and a little practice, it's definitely doable. Just remember to take your time, start with less hair than you need, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
