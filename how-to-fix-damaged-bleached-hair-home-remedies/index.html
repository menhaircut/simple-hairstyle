How To Fix Damaged Bleached Hair Home Remedies

If you've recently bleached your hair, you may have noticed that it's become dry, brittle, and damaged. Bleaching can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to break easily. Fortunately, there are many home remedies that you can use to fix damaged bleached hair. In this article, we'll discuss some of the best home remedies for repairing damaged bleached hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix Damaged Bleached Hair
  • Step-by-Step Home Remedies
  • Tips for Preventing Further Damage
  • FAQs

How to Fix Damaged Bleached Hair

Before we get into the home remedies, it's important to understand why your hair is damaged in the first place. When you bleach your hair, you're essentially stripping it of its natural color. This process can also strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Here are some home remedies that can help repair the damage:

1. Coconut Oil

Coconut oil is a great natural conditioner that can help repair damaged bleached hair. Apply it to your hair and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before washing it out. You can also leave it on overnight for a deeper conditioning treatment.

2. Avocado

Avocado contains healthy fats and vitamins that can help repair damaged hair. Mash up a ripe avocado and apply it to your hair for 20-30 minutes before washing it out.

3. Eggs

Eggs are high in protein, which is essential for healthy hair. Mix one or two eggs with a tablespoon of olive oil and apply it to your hair for 30 minutes before washing it out.

4. Apple Cider Vinegar

Apple cider vinegar can help restore the pH balance of your hair and scalp, which can help repair damage. Mix one part apple cider vinegar with two parts water and pour it over your hair after shampooing. Rinse it out with cool water.

Step-by-Step Home Remedies

Here are step-by-step instructions for using these home remedies:

1. Coconut Oil

  1. Warm up a few tablespoons of coconut oil until it's melted.
  2. Apply the oil to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Leave the oil on for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for a deeper treatment.
  4. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

2. Avocado

  1. Mash up a ripe avocado until it's smooth.
  2. Apply the avocado to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Leave the avocado on for 20-30 minutes.
  4. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

3. Eggs

  1. Beat one or two eggs in a bowl.
  2. Add a tablespoon of olive oil and mix well.
  3. Apply the egg mixture to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  4. Leave the mixture on for 30 minutes.
  5. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

4. Apple Cider Vinegar

  1. Mix one part apple cider vinegar with two parts water.
  2. Shampoo your hair as usual.
  3. Pour the apple cider vinegar mixture over your hair, focusing on the ends.
  4. Rinse your hair with cool water.

Tips for Preventing Further Damage

Here are some tips to help prevent further damage to your bleached hair:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons and straighteners.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair, as it's more fragile and prone to breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.
  • Wear a hat or scarf when you're out in the sun to protect your hair from UV damage.

FAQs

1. Can I use these remedies on color-treated hair?

Yes, these remedies are safe to use on color-treated hair.

2. How often should I use these remedies?

You can use these remedies once a week, or as often as needed to repair your damaged hair.

3. How long will it take to see results?

You should start to see results after a few uses of these remedies, but it may take several weeks to fully repair your damaged hair.

Hair Care for Damaged Bleached Hair

Once you've repaired your damaged bleached hair, it's important to take good care of it to prevent further damage. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for dry or damaged hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, or use them sparingly.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals.

By following these home remedies and tips for hair care, you can repair your damaged bleached hair and keep it healthy and beautiful.
