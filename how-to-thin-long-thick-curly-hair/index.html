How To Thin Long Thick Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by William Nov 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Having long, thick, curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it can be beautiful and voluminous, it can also be difficult to manage and style. Thinning out your hair can help make it more manageable, but it can be a daunting task to undertake. In this post, we will provide you with a complete guide on how to thin long thick curly hair.

Description

Thinning long thick curly hair involves removing some of the hair's bulk to make it more manageable. This is typically done by removing some of the hair's length and/or using thinning shears to cut into the hair to remove some of the weight. It is important to note that thinning hair can make it more prone to breakage, so it is essential to take proper care of your hair after thinning it.

How to Thin Long Thick Curly Hair

Thinning long thick curly hair can be done in a few different ways. You can either cut your hair to remove some length or use thinning shears to remove some of the hair's weight. It is important to consider the amount of hair that you want to thin out before deciding which method to use. If you are not comfortable cutting your hair, it is best to seek the help of a professional hairstylist.

1. Cutting Your Hair

If you decide to cut your hair, it is essential to use sharp scissors to prevent damaging your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair to make it easier to cut.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles and separate it into sections.
  3. Take a section of hair and hold it between your fingers at the length that you want to cut it.
  4. Cut the hair in a straight line, making sure to cut all the way through the section.
  5. Continue cutting your hair in sections until you have removed the desired amount of hair.

It is important to note that cutting your hair will result in a shorter length, so this method is not ideal if you want to keep your hair long.

2. Using Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are scissors that have one straight blade and one serrated blade. They are designed to remove some of the hair's weight without affecting the overall length. Here are the steps to follow:

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles and separate it into sections.
  3. Take a section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  4. Use the thinning shears to cut into the section of hair, starting from the bottom and working your way up.
  5. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair until you have removed the desired amount of weight.

It is essential to use thinning shears correctly to avoid damaging your hair. It is best to seek the help of a professional hairstylist if you are not comfortable using them.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin long thick curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Separate your hair into sections.
  4. Decide on the amount of hair that you want to thin out.
  5. Choose the method that you want to use to thin out your hair (cutting or thinning shears).
  6. If you are cutting your hair, cut off the desired amount of hair in sections.
  7. If you are using thinning shears, use them to remove some of the hair's weight in sections.
  8. Check your hair to ensure that it is thinned out evenly.
  9. Style your hair as desired.
  10. Take proper care of your hair to prevent breakage.

Tips for Thinning Long Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning long thick curly hair:

  • Start by thinning out a small amount of hair and gradually increase it if necessary.
  • Use sharp scissors or thinning shears to prevent damaging your hair.
  • Take frequent breaks to avoid over-thinning your hair.
  • Seek the help of a professional hairstylist if you are not comfortable thinning out your hair.
  • Take proper care of your hair after thinning it to prevent breakage.

FAQs

How often should I thin out my long thick curly hair?

It is best to thin out your hair every 2-3 months or as needed. Thinning out your hair too frequently can result in excessive breakage.

Will thinning out my hair make it easier to manage?

Yes, thinning out your hair can make it easier to manage as it reduces the hair's bulk and weight.

Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

Thinning out your hair will not make it look thinner, but it will reduce the hair's bulk and weight.

Hair Care for Thinned Hair

It is essential to take proper care of your hair after thinning it to prevent breakage. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Thinning out long thick curly hair can be a daunting task, but it can make your hair more manageable and easier to style. By following the steps and tips outlined in this post, you can achieve the perfect balance between volume and manageability.
