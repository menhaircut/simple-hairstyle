How To Tone Brassy Asian Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As an Asian with colored hair, you may have encountered the problem of brassy tones. Brassy hair is a common issue for colored hair, especially when you have dark hair that's been lightened. Brassy tones can make your hair look dull and unattractive. However, you don't have to live with brassy hair. In this article, we'll show you how to tone brassy Asian hair and keep it looking vibrant and healthy.

  1. How to Tone Brassy Asian Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Toning Brassy Hair

How to Tone Brassy Asian Hair

Toning brassy hair involves the use of toners that help neutralize the unwanted warm tones in your hair. Toners are hair products that help change the color of your hair by neutralizing the brassy tones. To tone brassy Asian hair, you'll need to use a toner that's specifically formulated for your hair type and color.

Choose the Right Toner

The first step in toning brassy Asian hair is to choose the right toner. You'll need to select a toner that's designed to neutralize the warm tones in your hair. There are many toners available on the market, so it's important to choose a toner that's specifically formulated for your hair type and color.

When selecting a toner, you'll need to consider the following:

  • Your hair type
  • The level of brassiness in your hair
  • The desired shade of your hair
  • The type of toner

Prep Your Hair

Before applying the toner, you'll need to prep your hair. This involves washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove all traces of shampoo.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to tone brassy Asian hair:

  1. Prep your hair by washing it with a clarifying shampoo.
  2. Dry your hair completely.
  3. Mix the toner according to the instructions on the package.
  4. Apply the toner to your hair, starting from the roots and working your way down to the tips.
  5. Leave the toner on your hair for the recommended time.
  6. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.
  7. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair to restore moisture.

Tips for Toning Brassy Hair

Here are some tips to help you tone brassy Asian hair:

  • Use a toner that's specifically formulated for your hair type and color.
  • Don't leave the toner on your hair for too long, as it can cause damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment after toning to restore moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as they can cause further damage.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip it of its natural oils.

FAQs

How often should I tone my brassy Asian hair?

You should tone your hair once every 4-6 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly it fades.

Can I tone my hair at home?

Yes, you can tone your hair at home using a toner kit. However, if you're not experienced in coloring your hair, it's best to have it done by a professional.

What should I do if my hair turns green after toning?

If your hair turns green after toning, it's because the toner was left on for too long or the wrong toner was used. You'll need to see a professional stylist to correct the color.

Hair Care Tips for Colored Hair

Here are some tips to help you care for your colored hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping the color from your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as they can cause damage and fade the color.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.

By following these tips and using the right toner, you can keep your brassy Asian hair looking vibrant and healthy. Remember to take good care of your colored hair to maintain its beauty and shine.
