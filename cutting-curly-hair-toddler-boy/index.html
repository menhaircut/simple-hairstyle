Cutting Curly Hair Toddler Boy A How To Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 30, 2023 · 5 min read

As a parent, you want the best for your child. This includes their appearance, which is why cutting curly hair toddler boy can be a daunting task. However, with the right technique and tools, you can give your little one a stylish and comfortable haircut. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of cutting curly hair toddler boy and provide tips for hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Curly Hair Toddler Boy
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair Toddler Boy
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair Toddler Boy

How to Cut Curly Hair Toddler Boy

Cutting curly hair toddler boy requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. Curly hair tends to shrink when dry, so it’s important to take that into consideration when cutting. Here are the basic steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair and Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure your toddler’s hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be misleading and cause you to cut too much. You’ll also need the right tools, including sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips or ties.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use hair clips or ties to section the hair. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up. This will help you see what you’re doing and prevent you from cutting too much at once.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Using sharp scissors, cut the hair in small sections. Start with the ends and work your way up. Don’t cut straight across, as this can create a blunt look. Instead, cut at an angle to create layers and texture.

Step 4: Check for Balance

After cutting each section, check for balance. Make sure both sides are even and adjust if necessary. Don’t worry if it’s not perfect, as curly hair tends to hide imperfections.

Step 5: Trim the Bangs

If your toddler has bangs, trim them last. Use the same technique as above and cut at an angle to create texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed guide on how to cut curly hair toddler boy:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Wash your toddler’s hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry and let it air dry completely. Don’t use a hair dryer, as it can cause frizz.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use hair clips or ties to section the hair. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up. Take your time and make sure each section is neat and even.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Using sharp scissors, cut the hair in small sections. Start with the ends and work your way up. Don’t cut straight across, as this can create a blunt look. Instead, cut at an angle to create layers and texture.

When cutting the sides, use your fingers to hold the hair in place. Don’t pull too tight, as this can cause the hair to spring back and create an uneven cut. Instead, gently hold the hair and cut at an angle.

Step 4: Check for Balance

After cutting each section, check for balance. Make sure both sides are even and adjust if necessary. Use a comb to compare the length on both sides.

Step 5: Trim the Bangs

If your toddler has bangs, trim them last. Use the same technique as above and cut at an angle to create texture. Make sure the bangs are even and not too short.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair Toddler Boy

Here are some tips to make cutting curly hair toddler boy easier:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Cut in small sections to prevent mistakes.
  • Don’t cut too much at once, as curly hair tends to shrink when dry.
  • Cut at an angle to create layers and texture.
  • Use hair clips or ties to section the hair.
  • Check for balance after cutting each section.
  • Trim the bangs last and cut at an angle.

FAQs

How often should I cut my toddler’s curly hair?

It’s recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake?

Don’t worry if you make a mistake, as curly hair tends to hide imperfections. If it’s a minor mistake, you can leave it and fix it next time. If it’s a major mistake, you can take your toddler to a professional stylist to fix it.

Can I cut my toddler’s curly hair when it’s wet?

No, it’s best to cut curly hair when it’s dry. Wet hair can be misleading and cause you to cut too much.

Hair Care for Curly Hair Toddler Boy

Here are some tips for hair care to keep your toddler’s curly hair healthy and shiny:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Don’t wash the hair every day, as it can cause dryness.
  • Detangle the hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer, as it can cause frizz.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to moisturize the hair.
  • Avoid using hair products with alcohol, as it can dry out the hair.

Cutting curly hair toddler boy can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right technique and tools, you can give your little one a stylish and comfortable haircut. Just remember to take your time, cut in small sections, and check for balance. And don’t forget to follow our hair care tips to keep your toddler’s curly hair healthy and shiny.
