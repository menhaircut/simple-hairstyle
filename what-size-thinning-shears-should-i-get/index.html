What Size Thinning Shears Should I Get

Written by Robby Oct 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are essential for hairdressers and stylists who want to achieve textured and layered hairstyles. They are used to remove bulk and create movement in the hair. However, choosing the right size of thinning shears can be confusing, especially for beginners. In this article, we will discuss what size thinning shears you should get and provide tips on how to use them.

  • Step-by-Step Guide on Using Thinning Shears

How to Choose the Right Size Thinning Shears

Thinning shears come in different sizes, usually measured in inches. The most common sizes are 5.5 inches, 6 inches, and 6.5 inches. The size you choose depends on the length and thickness of the hair you want to thin out.

If you have short hair, a 5.5-inch or 6-inch pair of thinning shears will be suitable. These sizes are also ideal for thinning out the ends of long hair. However, if you have thick and long hair, a 6.5-inch pair of thinning shears will be more effective. This size will allow you to remove more hair in one go, reducing the time and effort needed to thin out the hair.

Step-by-Step Guide on Using Thinning Shears

Using thinning shears requires technique and precision to achieve the desired result. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to use them:

  1. Start by sectioning the hair you want to thin out. Use hair clips to separate the top layer of hair from the bottom layer.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers, about an inch away from the ends.
  3. Open the thinning shears and place them horizontally on the hair, about an inch away from your fingers. Close the shears about halfway, making sure the teeth are facing upwards.
  4. Gently slide the thinning shears down the hair, stopping halfway. This will remove some of the hair, creating texture and movement.
  5. Repeat steps 2 to 4 on different sections of hair, making sure to vary the thickness and length of the sections for a natural look.
  6. Once you have thinned out the bottom layer of hair, release the top layer and repeat steps 2 to 5.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Here are some tips to help you use thinning shears effectively:

  • Only use thinning shears on dry hair. Wet hair can stretch and make it difficult to control the amount of hair you want to thin out.
  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always remove more hair if needed, but you can't put it back once it's gone.
  • Use the thinning shears sparingly. Over-thinning can create a choppy and uneven look.
  • Clean and oil your thinning shears regularly to maintain their sharpness and prevent rusting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

A: Thinning shears can be used on most hair types, but they work best on thick and coarse hair. If you have fine or thin hair, thinning shears may cause too much hair to be removed, making your hair look sparse.

Q: How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much volume you want to maintain. If you want to keep your hair looking textured and layered, use thinning shears every 6 to 8 weeks.

Q: Can I use thinning shears to cut my own hair?

A: It's not recommended to use thinning shears on your own hair, especially if you're not a trained stylist. You risk cutting too much hair and creating an uneven look.

Hair Care Tips

Thinning shears can help you achieve the perfect hairstyle, but they can also damage your hair if not used properly. Here are some hair care tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and soft.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or peroxide, as they can weaken your hair and cause breakage.

Choosing the right size of thinning shears can make a big difference in the outcome of your hairstyle. Remember to choose a size that matches the length and thickness of your hair and to use them sparingly. With practice and patience, you can create a beautiful and textured look that will turn heads.
