Why Is My Hair Curlier Some Days

Written by Frank Sep 02, 2023 · 3 min read
Why is My Hair Curlier Some Days?

Have you ever noticed that your hair is curlier on some days than others? This can be frustrating if you're trying to achieve a certain hairstyle, but there are a few reasons why this may be happening. In this article, we will explore why your hair may be curlier some days and provide tips on how to manage your curly locks.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

Before we dive into why your hair may be curlier some days, it's important to determine your hair type. There are four main hair types:

  • Straight
  • Wavy
  • Curly
  • Coily

If you're not sure what your hair type is, try the hair typing system created by Andre Walker. This system categorizes hair into four types and then further breaks them down into subcategories. Knowing your hair type can help you better understand why your hair may be curlier some days.

Why is My Hair Curlier Some Days?

1. Humidity

Humidity can have a big impact on your hair. When there is a lot of moisture in the air, it can cause your hair to become frizzy and curly. This is because water molecules are attracted to the protein in your hair, which causes the hair to expand and curl. If you live in a humid climate, you may notice that your hair is curlier on those days.

2. Product Build-Up

If you use a lot of hair products, they can build up on your hair over time. This can weigh down your hair and make it look limp and lifeless. However, if you skip washing your hair for a day or two, the natural oils in your hair can help to revive your curls and make them look curlier.

3. Hormones

Hormones can also play a role in how curly your hair is. During puberty, for example, many people experience changes in their hair texture. Hormonal changes during pregnancy can also cause your hair to become curlier. If you notice that your hair is curlier some days, it may be due to hormonal changes in your body.

Tips for Managing Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, you may be looking for ways to manage it. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to help define your curls
  • Try using a diffuser when blow-drying your hair

FAQs

1. Can genetics play a role in how curly my hair is?

Yes, genetics can play a big role in determining your hair texture and curl pattern.

2. Can stress cause my hair to become curlier?

Stress can cause a lot of changes in your body, including changes to your hair. It's possible that stress could cause your hair to become curlier.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly. This means using the right products and techniques to keep your curls looking their best. With a little bit of effort, you can have beautiful, bouncy curls every day.
