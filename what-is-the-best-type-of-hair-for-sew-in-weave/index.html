What Is The Best Type Of Hair For Sew In Weave

When it comes to sew in weaves, choosing the right type of hair can make all the difference. Whether you're looking for a natural look or something more dramatic, the type of hair you choose will impact the overall look and feel of your sew in. In this blog post, we'll explore the best type of hair for sew in weaves, including how to choose the right hair, how to install a sew in weave, and tips for maintaining your new look.

How to Choose the Right Hair

When it comes to choosing the right hair for your sew in weave, there are a few factors to consider. The first is the quality of the hair. You want to choose hair that is soft, shiny, and smooth. Look for hair that is made from 100% human hair, as this will give you the most natural look and feel.

The next factor to consider is the texture of the hair. Do you want straight hair, curly hair, or something in between? The texture of the hair you choose will depend on your personal preference and the style you're trying to achieve. If you're not sure what texture to choose, talk to your stylist for recommendations.

Another factor to consider is the length of the hair. Do you want long hair, short hair, or something in between? Again, the length of the hair you choose will depend on your personal preference and the style you're trying to achieve.

Finally, consider the color of the hair. Do you want hair that matches your natural color, or do you want to try something different? If you're not sure what color to choose, talk to your stylist for recommendations.

Step by Step Guide to Installing a Sew In Weave

Installing a sew in weave can be a bit daunting if you've never done it before, but with the right tools and a little bit of patience, it's a relatively straightforward process. Here's a step by step guide to installing a sew in weave:

  1. Wash and condition your natural hair.
  2. Cornrow your natural hair, making sure that the braids are flat and close to the scalp.
  3. Measure and cut the hair extensions to the desired length.
  4. Sew the hair extensions onto the cornrows using a needle and thread.
  5. Cut and style the hair extensions to achieve the desired look.

Remember to be gentle when installing your sew in weave, as pulling too hard can cause damage to your natural hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Sew In Weave

Once you've installed your sew in weave, it's important to take good care of it to ensure that it lasts as long as possible. Here are some tips for maintaining your sew in weave:

  • Wash your sew in weave regularly, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your sew in weave, as this can cause damage to the hair.
  • Wrap your hair at night to prevent tangling and frizz.
  • Don't leave your sew in weave in for too long. Generally, it's recommended that you remove your sew in weave after 8 weeks to prevent damage to your natural hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use synthetic hair for a sew in weave?

A: It's not recommended to use synthetic hair for a sew in weave, as it doesn't look as natural and can be more difficult to style.

Q: How long does a sew in weave last?

A: A sew in weave can last anywhere from 6-8 weeks, depending on how well you take care of it.

Hair Care for Your Sew In Weave

When it comes to caring for your sew in weave, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, make sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils. Second, avoid using heat styling tools on your sew in weave, as this can cause damage to the hair. Finally, make sure to wrap your hair at night to prevent tangling and frizz.

Overall, choosing the right type of hair for your sew in weave is crucial for achieving the look you want. By considering factors like texture, length, and color, you can find the perfect hair for your sew in. And with proper care and maintenance, your sew in weave will look great for weeks to come!
