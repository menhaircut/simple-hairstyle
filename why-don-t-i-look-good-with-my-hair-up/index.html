Why Don t I Look Good With My Hair Up

If you're someone who feels like they can never pull off a hairstyle with their hair up, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this issue, and it can be frustrating when you're trying to switch up your look. In this blog post, we'll be discussing some possible reasons why you don't look good with your hair up and what you can do about it.

Possible Reasons Why You Don't Look Good with Your Hair Up

There could be several reasons why you don't feel like you look good with your hair up. Here are a few possibilities:

Face Shape

The shape of your face can play a big role in how certain hairstyles look on you. If you have a round face, for example, pulling your hair up tightly may accentuate the roundness and make your face appear wider. On the other hand, if you have a long face, wearing your hair up can elongate your face even more.

Hair Texture

The texture of your hair can also affect how it looks when it's up. If you have fine hair, for example, it may be difficult to get it to stay in place without falling flat. On the other hand, if you have thick hair, it may be hard to keep it from looking too bulky or overwhelming when it's all pulled up.

Length of Hair

The length of your hair can also play a role in how it looks when it's up. If your hair is too short, it may not be long enough to style in a way that flatters your face shape. If it's too long, it may be too heavy to stay up or look too overwhelming when it's all pulled back.

How to Make Your Hair Look Good Up

If you're determined to make your hair look good up, there are a few things you can try:

Experiment with Different Styles

Don't be afraid to experiment with different updo styles to see what works best for you. Try a messy bun, a sleek ponytail, or a braided updo. You may be surprised at how different styles can flatter your face shape and hair type.

Accessorize

Accessories can also make a big difference when it comes to styling your hair up. Try adding a headband, scarf, or hairpin to your updo to give it some extra flair.

Get Professional Help

If you're still struggling to find a style that works for you, consider getting professional help. A hairstylist can give you personalized advice based on your face shape, hair type, and length of hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair Up

If you want to try styling your hair up at home, here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair to remove any buildup or oil that may weigh it down.

Step 2: Apply Styling Products

Apply a styling product like mousse or gel to your hair to help it stay in place.

Step 3: Decide on a Style

Decide on the updo style you want to try. You can try a messy bun, a sleek ponytail, or a braided updo.

Step 4: Gather Your Hair

Gather your hair and use a hair tie to secure it in place. Make sure it's not too tight, as this can cause headaches and damage to your hair.

Step 5: Finish with Accessories

Finish off your updo with accessories like a headband, scarf, or hairpin.

Tips for Making Your Hair Look Good Up

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when styling your hair up:

  • Use bobby pins to secure any loose hairs in place.
  • Try teasing your hair at the crown to add volume.
  • Use dry shampoo to add texture and grip to your hair.
  • Experiment with different hair accessories to add some extra flair to your updo.

Frequently Asked Questions About Hair Up

Q: Can anyone pull off an updo?

A: While certain face shapes and hair types may be more challenging to style in an updo, anyone can pull off this look with a little experimentation and practice.

Q: How can I keep my hair up all day?

A: Use a strong hair tie and bobby pins to keep your hair in place. You can also use hairspray to help hold your style in place.

Q: What should I do if my hair is too short for an updo?

A: Try a half-up, half-down style or experiment with adding hair extensions to give your hair some extra length.

Hair Care Tips for Styling Your Hair Up

Styling your hair up can cause damage if you're not careful. Here are a few hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Don't pull your hair too tight, as this can cause tension headaches and damage to your hair follicles.
  • Use a hair tie that won't snag or damage your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Be gentle when taking down your updo to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock an updo that flatters your face shape and hair type. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and accessories to find the perfect look for you!
