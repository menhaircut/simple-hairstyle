How To Use Thinning Scissors On Short Hair Yourself

Written by Robby Jun 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Thinning scissors are a great tool to have for anyone who wants to create a layered, textured look on short hair. They are also perfect for removing bulk from thick hair. However, using thinning scissors on short hair can be tricky for beginners. In this post, we’ll show you how to use thinning scissors on short hair yourself, step by step. We’ll also give you some tips on how to care for your hair and your scissors, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Description

Thinning scissors, also known as texturizing scissors, have teeth on one or both blades. These teeth can be fine or wide, and they remove some of the hair while leaving some of it in place. This creates a softer, more natural look than regular scissors can achieve. Thinning scissors are particularly useful for people with thick hair or short hair, as they can remove bulk without making the hair look too thin or choppy.

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Short Hair Yourself

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair before using thinning scissors. This will make it easier to see how much hair you need to remove and ensure that the scissors work smoothly.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will help you work on one section at a time and prevent you from cutting too much hair at once.

Step 3: Comb your hair

Using a comb, comb the hair in the section you want to thin. This will make it easier to see which strands are thicker and need to be thinned out.

Step 4: Hold the scissors correctly

Hold the thinning scissors with the teeth pointing towards the hair. This will ensure that the scissors cut the hair in the right way and don’t damage it.

Step 5: Start cutting

Start cutting the hair in the section you want to thin, using short, quick strokes. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can create an uneven look. Instead, cut small amounts of hair at a time and check your progress frequently.

Step 6: Blend the hair

Use a comb to blend the hair you’ve cut with the hair you haven’t cut. This will create a seamless, natural look.

Step 7: Repeat on other sections

Repeat steps 2 to 6 on the other sections of your hair, until you achieve the desired look.

Tips

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don’t cut too much hair at once, as this can create an uneven look.
  • Use a comb to blend the hair and create a natural look.
  • Practice on a small section of hair before working on your entire head.

FAQ

Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

Thinning scissors can be used on all hair types, but they are particularly useful for people with thick hair or short hair.

Can thinning scissors damage your hair?

Thinning scissors can damage your hair if they are not used correctly. Make sure to use sharp scissors, cut small amounts of hair at a time, and blend the hair after cutting to avoid creating an uneven look.

How often should you use thinning scissors on your hair?

You can use thinning scissors on your hair as often as you like, but it’s best to wait a few weeks between each use to avoid over-thinning your hair.

Hair Care

After using thinning scissors on your hair, it’s important to care for your hair and your scissors. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and strong.
  • Use a heat protectant when using hot tools on your hair.
  • Store your thinning scissors in a dry, clean place to prevent rust and damage.

With these tips and steps, you can now use thinning scissors on short hair yourself. Remember to be patient and practice on a small section of hair before working on your entire head. Take care of your hair and your scissors, and you’ll achieve a beautiful, natural look.
