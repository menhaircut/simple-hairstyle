How To Hide Thin Edges With Box Braids

Written by Eveline Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Box braids are a popular protective hairstyle that can last for weeks, but many people with thin edges worry about the braids exposing their hairline. Fortunately, there are several ways to hide thin edges with box braids, and in this blog post, we will provide step-by-step instructions and tips for achieving the perfect braided look without any worries.

Description

Box braids are a type of protective hairstyle that involves braiding hair into small, square-shaped sections. They can be done with synthetic or natural hair, and are often a popular choice for people who want to keep their hair protected and low-maintenance for several weeks. However, some people with thin edges may be worried about the braids exposing their hairline or causing damage to their already fragile hair.

How to Hide Thin Edges with Box Braids

Step-by-Step

Here are the steps to hide thin edges with box braids:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair thoroughly. This will ensure that your hair is clean and moisturized, which will help to prevent breakage and damage to your edges.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair and scalp. This will help to keep your hair moisturized and nourished, which is important when wearing protective styles like box braids.
  3. Section your hair into small, square-shaped sections using a rat-tail comb. Make sure that each section is even in size and neatly parted.
  4. Begin braiding your hair from the root, using the three-strand braiding technique. Make sure to braid tightly but not too tightly, as this can cause damage to your hair and scalp.
  5. Continue braiding each section of hair until all of your hair is braided. Make sure to leave some space between each braid to allow for movement and to prevent breakage.
  6. Once all of your hair is braided, you can choose to style your braids in a variety of ways. You can wear them down, in a bun, or in a ponytail.
  7. To hide thin edges, you can use a crochet needle to add extensions to your braids. This will add thickness and volume to your hairline, making your edges less noticeable.
  8. You can also use edge control or gel to smooth down your edges and create a sleek, polished look. This will help to keep your hairline looking neat and tidy.
  9. Finally, make sure to tie a silk or satin scarf around your hairline at night to prevent breakage and maintain the health of your hair and edges.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for hiding thin edges with box braids:

  • Choose a braiding pattern that is gentle on your hairline. Avoid braiding your hair too tightly or using a pattern that pulls on your edges.
  • Use high-quality extensions that match your natural hair color and texture. This will ensure that your braids look natural and blend seamlessly with your own hair.
  • Take breaks between protective styles to give your hair and edges a chance to rest and recover.
  • Don't forget to moisturize your hair and scalp regularly, even when wearing protective styles like box braids. This will help to prevent breakage and keep your hair healthy and nourished.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hiding thin edges with box braids:

Q: Will box braids damage my hairline?
A: Box braids can cause damage to your hairline if they are done too tightly or if you wear them for too long. It's important to choose a braiding pattern that is gentle on your hairline and to take breaks between protective styles to allow your hair and edges to rest and recover. Q: Can I wear my box braids in a ponytail?
A: Yes, you can wear your box braids in a ponytail, but make sure not to pull too tightly on your edges. Use a soft hair tie or scrunchie to avoid causing damage to your hairline.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential when wearing box braids or any other protective style. Here are some tips for keeping your hair and edges healthy and nourished:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly, using products that are gentle and moisturizing.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase or wearing a silk or satin scarf.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and nutrients to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and using the techniques outlined in this blog post, you can achieve a beautiful and healthy box braided look while also protecting your edges and promoting hair growth. Remember to be gentle with your hair and to take breaks between protective styles to give your hair and edges a chance to rest and recover. Happy braiding!
