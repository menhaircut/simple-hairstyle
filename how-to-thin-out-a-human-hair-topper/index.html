How To Thin Out A Human Hair Topper Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jun 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're new to wearing hair toppers, you might find that some of them can be a bit too thick for your liking. Fortunately, thinning out a human hair topper is relatively easy and can be done at home with a few basic tools. In this article, we'll go over the steps you need to take to thin out a human hair topper, as well as some tips on how to care for your hairpiece afterwards.

How to Thin Out a Human Hair Topper: Step by Step

Before you begin, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb or brush
  • A razor comb

Once you have your tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair topper

Before you start thinning out your hair topper, you'll want to wash it with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Allow it to air dry or use a blow dryer on a low heat setting. This will help you see the natural fall and movement of the hair.

Step 2: Comb out any tangles

Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to gently remove any tangles or knots in the hair. Be careful not to pull too hard or damage the hair.

Step 3: Section off the hair

Using small hair clips or rubber bands, section off the hair you want to thin out. This will help you work on one section at a time.

Step 4: Use the razor comb to thin out the hair

Take the razor comb and use it to gently thin out the hair in each section. Work from the ends of the hair upwards, taking small sections at a time. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as you can't put it back.

Step 5: Check for balance

As you work, check that the hair is balanced and even on all sides. You don't want to thin out one section more than another, as this can create an uneven look.

Step 6: Blend the layers

Once you've thinned out the individual sections, use the scissors to blend the layers together. This will help create a more natural look.

Step 7: Style your hair topper

Once you're happy with the thinning, style your hair topper as desired. You may find that it looks more natural and is easier to manage now that it's been thinned out.

Tips for Thinning Out a Human Hair Topper

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when thinning out your human hair topper:

  • Start with small sections and work your way up. You can always thin out more hair later, but you can't put it back once it's been cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Thinning out a hair topper can be time-consuming, but it's worth it for the natural look it can create.
  • Use a razor comb instead of regular scissors. A razor comb will help create a more natural look and prevent any blunt lines.
  • Check your work frequently to ensure balance and evenness.

FAQs About Thinning Out a Human Hair Topper

Can I thin out a synthetic hair topper?

No, you should not attempt to thin out a synthetic hair topper. Synthetic hair is made from plastic fibers and cannot be cut or thinned like human hair.

Do I need to thin out my hair topper?

It's a personal preference, but some people find that hair toppers can be too thick and heavy. Thinning out the hair can help create a more natural look and make it easier to manage.

How often should I thin out my hair topper?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you wear your hair topper. You may find that you only need to thin it out once a year or every few months.

Caring for Your Thinned Out Hair Topper

Now that you've thinned out your hair topper, it's important to care for it properly to ensure it lasts as long as possible. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair topper regularly using gentle products.
  • Store your hair topper on a wig stand or mannequin head when you're not wearing it.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage the hair.
  • Brush or comb your hair topper gently to avoid pulling on the hair.

Thinning out a human hair topper is a great way to create a more natural look and make it easier to manage. Follow these steps and tips to get the best results.
