How To Put Your Hair Up With Hair Sticks A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of using the same old hair ties and bobby pins to put your hair up? Why not try using hair sticks instead? Not only are they easy to use, but they also give your hair a unique and elegant look. In this article, we'll show you how to put your hair up with hair sticks in just a few simple steps.

  1. How to Use Hair Sticks
  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Use Hair Sticks

Hair sticks are long, thin, decorative sticks that are used to hold hair in place. They come in various shapes, sizes, and materials, such as wood, metal, and plastic. Using hair sticks is easy, and you don't need any special skills or tools. Here are the basic steps:

  1. Make a ponytail or bun with your hair.
  2. Hold the hair stick horizontally and insert it through the hair at the base of the ponytail or bun.
  3. Rotate the hair stick vertically and weave it through the hair until it comes out the other side.
  4. Adjust the hair as needed and push the hair stick in further if necessary.
  5. Repeat with another hair stick if desired.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of using hair sticks, let's go through the steps in more detail:

Step 1: Make a Ponytail or Bun

The first step is to make a ponytail or bun with your hair. You can do this by gathering your hair at the base of your neck or on the top of your head and securing it with a hair tie. Make sure the ponytail or bun is tight and secure.

Step 2: Hold the Hair Stick Horizontally

Take one of the hair sticks and hold it horizontally with both hands. Position the hair stick at the base of the ponytail or bun, right above the hair tie.

Step 3: Insert the Hair Stick

Insert the hair stick through the hair at the base of the ponytail or bun. Make sure the hair stick goes through the hair tie as well. Hold the hair stick in place with one hand.

Step 4: Rotate the Hair Stick Vertically

Rotate the hair stick vertically so that it is perpendicular to your head. Weave the hair stick through the hair until it comes out the other side. Make sure the hair stick goes through all the layers of hair.

Step 5: Adjust the Hair and Push the Hair Stick In Further

Adjust the hair as needed so that it is evenly distributed around the hair stick. If the hair stick feels loose, push it in further until it is secure.

Step 6: Repeat with Another Hair Stick

If you want to use another hair stick, repeat the same steps on the other side of the ponytail or bun. Make sure the hair sticks are inserted at the same angle and depth so that they are even.

Tips for Using Hair Sticks

Here are some tips to help you use hair sticks more effectively:

  • Choose hair sticks that are appropriate for your hair type and length. Longer hair sticks are better for thicker hair, while shorter ones are better for finer hair.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, such as braids and twists, to see what works best with hair sticks.
  • Use hair sticks as a decorative accessory by choosing ones with beads, crystals, or other embellishments.
  • Secure the hair sticks with a hair tie or clip to prevent them from falling out.

FAQ

Can I use hair sticks on wet hair?

No, it is not recommended to use hair sticks on wet hair as it can cause breakage and damage. Make sure your hair is completely dry before using hair sticks.

How do I remove hair sticks?

To remove hair sticks, gently pull them out of your hair in the opposite direction that you inserted them. Do not yank or twist them, as this can cause hair breakage.

How do I clean hair sticks?

To clean hair sticks, wipe them with a damp cloth or use a mild soap and water. Do not soak them in water or use harsh chemicals, as this can damage the material.

Hair Care Tips

Using hair sticks regularly can put stress on your hair, so it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using hair sticks on a daily basis, and alternate with other hair accessories to give your hair a break.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using hair sticks on hair that is already damaged or fragile.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Now that you know how to put your hair up with hair sticks, it's time to try it out for yourself! With a little practice, you'll be able to create beautiful updos that are both stylish and functional.
