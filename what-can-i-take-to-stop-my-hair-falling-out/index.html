What Can I Take To Stop My Hair Falling Out

Written by Oliver Nov 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're concerned about hair loss, you're not alone. Many people experience thinning hair or bald spots, and it can be a distressing experience. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. In this post, we'll cover what you can take to stop your hair falling out, including supplements, medications, and lifestyle changes.

How to Prevent Hair Loss

Before we dive into specific remedies, let's talk about the basics of preventing hair loss. There are a few key steps you can take to keep your hair healthy:

Eat a Balanced Diet

Getting enough vitamins and minerals is crucial for healthy hair growth. Make sure you're eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean protein. Some foods that are particularly good for hair health include:

  • Salmon and other fatty fish
  • Eggs
  • Spinach and other leafy greens
  • Walnuts and other nuts
  • Sweet potatoes

Manage Stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it's important to find healthy ways to manage stress in your life. This might include exercise, meditation, or talking to a therapist.

Avoid Excessive Heat and Chemicals

Excessive heat from styling tools and chemicals from hair treatments can damage your hair and contribute to hair loss. Try to limit your use of styling tools and opt for gentle, natural hair care products.

Supplements for Hair Growth

There are several supplements that may help promote healthy hair growth. Keep in mind that supplements aren't regulated by the FDA, so it's important to do your own research and talk to your doctor before starting any new supplements.

Biotin

Biotin is a B vitamin that's important for healthy hair, skin, and nails. It's found in foods like eggs, nuts, and whole grains, but you can also take it as a supplement. Some studies suggest that biotin supplements may help reduce hair loss and promote hair growth.

Vitamin D

Vitamin D is important for overall health, and some research suggests that it may also promote healthy hair growth. You can get vitamin D from sunlight, food sources like fatty fish and fortified dairy products, or supplements.

Zinc

Zinc is a mineral that's important for healthy hair growth. It's found in foods like oysters, beef, and pumpkin seeds, but you can also take it as a supplement.

Medications for Hair Growth

There are a few medications that may help prevent hair loss and promote hair growth. Keep in mind that these medications may have side effects, so it's important to talk to your doctor before starting any new medications.

Finasteride

Finasteride is a medication that's used to treat male pattern baldness. It works by blocking the hormone that causes hair loss. Finasteride is only available by prescription and may have side effects like decreased sex drive and erectile dysfunction.

Minoxidil

Minoxidil is a topical medication that's used to treat hair loss. It's available over-the-counter and works by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles. Minoxidil may cause scalp irritation or unwanted hair growth in other areas of the body.

Lifestyle Changes for Hair Growth

In addition to supplements and medications, there are several lifestyle changes you can make to promote healthy hair growth.

Exercise Regularly

Regular exercise can improve circulation and promote healthy hair growth. Aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise most days of the week.

Get Enough Sleep

Sleep is important for overall health, and it may also promote healthy hair growth. Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night.

Reduce Alcohol and Tobacco Use

Excessive alcohol and tobacco use can contribute to hair loss. Try to limit your alcohol intake and quit smoking if you're a smoker.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about preventing hair loss:

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can contribute to hair loss. Finding healthy ways to manage stress, like exercise or meditation, may help prevent hair loss.

Can hair loss be reversed?

It depends on the cause of the hair loss. In some cases, hair loss can be reversed with lifestyle changes or medication. In other cases, hair loss may be permanent.

Can hair loss be a sign of a health problem?

Yes, hair loss can be a sign of an underlying health problem like thyroid disease or anemia. If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to talk to your doctor.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Avoid brushing wet hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

In conclusion, there are several steps you can take to prevent hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Whether you choose to take supplements, medications, or make lifestyle changes, it's important to talk to your doctor and do your own research before starting any new treatments.
