How To Dry Curly Hair So It Doesn t Frizz A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 22, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that drying it can be a challenge. When you don't dry your hair properly, it can lead to frizz, which is the last thing you want. So, how can you dry your curly hair without causing it to frizz? In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to dry your curly hair so it doesn't frizz.

Table of Contents

  • How to Dry Curly Hair So It Doesn't Frizz
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Drying Curly Hair
  • FAQs

How to Dry Curly Hair So It Doesn't Frizz

The key to drying curly hair without causing frizz is to be gentle. Here are some steps you can follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: After washing your hair, gently squeeze out the excess water with a microfiber towel. Do not rub your hair with the towel as this can cause frizz.

Step 2: Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help detangle it and prevent frizz.

Step 3: Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

Step 4: Apply a hair serum or oil to your hair to help lock in moisture and prevent frizz.

Step 5: Using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer, dry your hair on a low heat setting. Hold the diffuser close to your hair and use a scrunching motion to encourage your curls.

Step 6: Once your hair is about 80% dry, turn off the blow dryer and let your hair air dry the rest of the way. This will help prevent frizz.

Tips for Drying Curly Hair

Tip 1: Use a microfiber towel to dry your hair instead of a regular towel. Microfiber towels are gentler on hair and help prevent frizz.

Tip 2: Apply a leave-in conditioner or detangler to your hair before drying. This will help make it easier to detangle and prevent frizz.

Tip 3: Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help dry your hair without causing frizz.

Tip 4: Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying. This can cause frizz.

Tip 5: Once your hair is dry, use a curl-defining cream or gel to help define your curls and prevent frizz.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people with curly hair can go several days without washing, while others may need to wash their hair every day. Experiment to see what works best for you.

Q: Can I dry my curly hair with a regular towel?

A: It's best to avoid using a regular towel on curly hair as it can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out the excess water.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Tip 1: Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized.

Tip 2: Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often as this can cause damage and frizz.

Tip 3: Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz and breakage.

Tip 4: Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it healthy and moisturized.

Tip 5: Avoid using products with alcohol in them as they can dry out your hair and cause frizz.

By following these tips and steps, you can dry your curly hair without causing frizz. Remember to be gentle and use the right products to keep your curls looking their best.
