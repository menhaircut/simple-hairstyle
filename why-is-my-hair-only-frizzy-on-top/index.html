Why Is My Hair Only Frizzy On Top

Written by Robby Jun 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're reading this, chances are you're dealing with frizzy hair on the top of your head. And you're not alone! Many people struggle with frizz, especially in humid climates or after washing their hair. But why is it that your hair is only frizzy on top? In this article, we'll break down the causes of top-of-the-head frizz and give you tips for how to combat it.

What Causes Frizz?

Frizz is caused by a lack of moisture in the hair. When the hair is dry, the cuticle (the outermost layer of the hair) becomes rough and raised, which allows moisture to escape and makes the hair look frizzy. Humidity exacerbates frizz because it attracts moisture to the hair, causing the cuticle to expand and become even more rough.

Why is My Hair Only Frizzy on Top?

There are a few reasons why your hair might be frizzy on top:

1. Overwashing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. If you're washing your hair every day or every other day, try cutting back to every 2-3 days.

2. Using the Wrong Shampoo

Some shampoos are too harsh for certain hair types, which can lead to dryness and frizz. Look for a shampoo that is specifically formulated for your hair type (e.g. curly, dry, color-treated, etc.) and avoid shampoos that contain sulfates.

3. Using Hot Tools Too Often

Using hot tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can damage the hair and lead to frizz. Try to limit your use of hot tools and always use a heat protectant spray before styling.

4. Sleeping on a Cotton Pillowcase

Cotton pillowcases can absorb moisture from the hair, leading to dryness and frizz. Try switching to a silk or satin pillowcase, which will be gentler on your hair and help retain moisture.

5. Genetics

Sometimes, frizz is simply a result of genetics. If your hair is naturally curly or wavy, it may be prone to frizz, especially in humid climates.

How to Combat Frizz

Now that you know what causes frizz, here are some tips for how to combat it:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for a shampoo and conditioner that are formulated to moisturize your hair. Avoid products that contain sulfates, as these can be drying.

2. Apply a Hair Mask Once a Week

A weekly hair mask can help restore moisture to your hair and combat frizz. Look for a mask that contains hydrating ingredients like coconut oil, avocado oil, or shea butter.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized throughout the day. Apply it to damp hair after washing and styling.

4. Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

The oils from your hands can transfer to your hair and make it look greasy and frizzy. Try to avoid touching your hair too much and use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with natural bristles to detangle.

5. Embrace Your Natural Texture

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, embrace it! Trying to straighten or tame your hair too much can lead to damage and frizz. Instead, use products that enhance your natural texture and embrace the volume and bounce.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a hair oil to combat frizz?

A: Yes! Hair oils like argan oil, jojoba oil, and coconut oil can help moisturize your hair and combat frizz. Just be careful not to use too much, as this can make your hair look greasy.

Q: Will cutting my hair help combat frizz?

A: It depends on the length and texture of your hair. Generally, shorter hair is less prone to frizz because it is lighter and less likely to get weighed down. However, if you have long, curly hair, cutting it too short could actually make the frizz worse. Talk to your stylist about the best haircut for your hair type.

Q: Can I use a hair serum to combat frizz?

A: Yes! Hair serums are designed to smooth the hair and combat frizz. Look for a serum that is lightweight and non-greasy, and apply it to damp hair before styling.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are some general hair care tips to help keep your hair healthy and frizz-free:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with natural bristles to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using hot tools too often
  • Cut back on washing your hair
  • Avoid using hair ties with metal clasps, as these can damage your hair
  • Wear a hat or use an umbrella when it's raining or humid outside

By following these tips and using the right products, you can combat frizz and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful!
