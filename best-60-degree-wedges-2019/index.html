Best 60 Degree Wedges 2019 A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 22, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a golf enthusiast, you know that having the right equipment is crucial to performing your best on the course. One essential piece of equipment is the wedge, which is used to make short-range shots that require precision and accuracy. Of the different types of wedges available, the 60 degree wedge is a popular choice for its versatility and ability to handle a variety of shots. In this guide, we'll explore the best 60 degree wedges of 2019 and what makes them stand out.

How to Choose the Right 60 Degree Wedge

Before we dive into the best options, it's important to understand what to look for when choosing a 60 degree wedge. Here are some factors to consider:

Shaft Material

The shaft material will affect the weight and stiffness of the wedge. Some common materials include steel and graphite.

Bounce Angle

The bounce angle determines how the wedge interacts with the ground. A higher bounce angle is better for soft turf, while a lower angle is better for firm turf.

Sole Grind

The sole grind affects how the club interacts with the turf and the ball. Different grinds are better for different types of shots, so it's important to choose one that suits your playing style.

Top 60 Degree Wedges of 2019

Here are our top picks for the best 60 degree wedges of 2019:

1. Titleist Vokey SM7 Wedge

The Titleist Vokey SM7 Wedge is a popular choice among professionals and amateurs alike. It features a progressive center of gravity that helps to produce more consistent spin and control on shots. The SM7 also has a variety of sole grinds to choose from, allowing you to customize the wedge to your playing style.

2. Callaway Mack Daddy 4 Wedge

The Callaway Mack Daddy 4 Wedge is another excellent option. It features a unique groove-in-groove technology that helps to increase spin and control on shots. The Mack Daddy 4 also has a wide range of loft and bounce options, making it a versatile choice for different types of shots.

3. TaylorMade Milled Grind Hi-Toe Wedge

The TaylorMade Milled Grind Hi-Toe Wedge is a great choice for players looking for more versatility around the green. Its high toe design allows for more options when it comes to shot selection, making it easier to get out of tricky situations. The Milled Grind technology also helps to produce consistent spin and control on shots.

Tips for Using a 60 Degree Wedge

Now that you have an idea of what to look for in a 60 degree wedge and some of the best options on the market, here are some tips for using it effectively:

1. Practice Different Shots

A 60 degree wedge is a versatile club that can be used for a variety of shots, including chips, pitches, and bunker shots. Practice different types of shots to get a feel for how the club interacts with the ball and the turf.

2. Experiment with Loft and Bounce

Loft and bounce are essential factors in determining how a wedge performs. Experiment with different loft and bounce options to find the combination that suits your playing style and the course conditions.

3. Use Proper Technique

Using proper technique is crucial for getting the most out of your wedge. Make sure to maintain a consistent grip, stance, and swing when using the club.

FAQs About 60 Degree Wedges

1. What is a 60 degree wedge used for?

A 60 degree wedge is typically used for short-range shots around the green that require precision and accuracy.

2. How far can you hit a 60 degree wedge?

The distance you can hit a 60 degree wedge will depend on a variety of factors, including your swing speed and the type of shot you're making. On average, a 60 degree wedge can be hit between 50 and 90 yards.

3. Should I use a 60 degree wedge?

If you're looking to improve your short-range game and have a versatile club that can handle a variety of shots, a 60 degree wedge is a great choice.

Conclusion

Choosing the right 60 degree wedge is essential for improving your short-range game and performing your best on the course. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced player, there's a wedge out there that can help take your game to the next level. Consider the factors we've discussed in this guide and try out some of our top picks to find the one that works best for you.
