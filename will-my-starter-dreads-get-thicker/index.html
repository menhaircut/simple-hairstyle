Will My Starter Dreads Get Thicker

Written by Christine Nov 01, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Starting your dreadlock journey can be an exciting and daunting experience. You may have a lot of questions about how your dreads will grow and change over time. One common question that many people have is, "Will my starter dreads get thicker?" In this article, we'll explore this question in detail and provide you with some tips and advice on how to care for your starter dreads.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Dreadlocks are formed by matting and twisting hair together. Over time, the hair will continue to lock and tighten, creating thicker and more defined dreads. However, the thickness of your starter dreads will depend on several factors, including the thickness of your natural hair, the method used to start your dreads, and how well you care for your hair.

How to

If you want your starter dreads to get thicker, there are a few things you can do to encourage the locking process. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid washing your hair too often

Washing your hair too often can cause your dreads to loosen and unravel. It's best to wash your hair no more than once a week, using a residue-free shampoo specifically designed for dreadlocks.

2. Keep your hair dry

Moisture can also cause your dreads to loosen and unravel. Be sure to dry your hair thoroughly after washing and avoid getting your hair wet in the rain or while swimming.

3. Use a palm rolling technique

Palm rolling is a technique that involves rolling your dreads between your palms to encourage the hair to lock and tighten. This can help your starter dreads to get thicker over time.

Step by Step

Here's how to palm roll your dreads:

  1. Take one dread at a time and roll it between your palms.
  2. Start at the root of the dread and work your way down to the tip.
  3. Repeat this process for each dread, rolling them in the same direction.
  4. Do this once or twice a week, but be careful not to overdo it, as too much rolling can cause your dreads to weaken and break.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your starter dreads:

  • Be patient. It can take several months or even a year for your dreads to fully mature and thicken.
  • Avoid using wax or other heavy products on your hair, as these can cause buildup and prevent your dreads from locking properly.
  • Consider using a locking accelerator spray or oil to help speed up the locking process.
  • Avoid sleeping on wet hair, as this can cause your dreads to become misshapen or flat.

FAQ

Q: Can I speed up the locking process?

A: While there is no way to speed up the locking process, there are things you can do to encourage your dreads to lock more quickly. These include using a locking accelerator spray or oil, palm rolling your dreads, and avoiding products that can prevent your dreads from locking properly.

Q: Will my starter dreads get thinner over time?

A: No, your dreads should not get thinner over time. In fact, they should continue to thicken and become more defined as they lock and tighten.

Hair Care

Caring for your starter dreads is essential to keeping them healthy and helping them to thicken over time. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair no more than once a week using a residue-free shampoo.
  • Dry your hair thoroughly after washing.
  • Avoid using heavy products, such as wax or gel, on your hair.
  • Palm roll your dreads once or twice a week to encourage locking.
  • Avoid sleeping on wet hair.

In conclusion, your starter dreads have the potential to become thicker and more defined over time. By following the tips and advice in this article, you can encourage the locking process and help your dreads to reach their full potential.
