What Islam Says About Hair Color Understanding The Religious Perspective

Written by William Oct 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Islam is a religion that is guided by principles and teachings that are meant to guide the behavior of its followers. One of the areas that it addresses is hair color, and what is acceptable and what is not. This article is going to explore what Islam says about hair color and related issues.

What is Islam?

Islam is a religion that was founded by Prophet Muhammad in the 7th century in the Arabian Peninsula. It is the second-largest religion in the world, with over 1.8 billion followers. The religion is based on the belief in one God, Allah, and the teachings of the Quran. The religion has five pillars that are meant to guide the behavior of its followers. These include the declaration of faith, prayer, charity, fasting, and pilgrimage to Mecca.

What Does Islam Say About Hair Color?

In Islam, changing the color of one's hair is not forbidden, but it is discouraged. It is believed that changing one's hair color is a way of altering God's creation, which is frowned upon. Some Islamic scholars believe that changing one's hair color is only permissible if it is done to cover grey hair or if it is done for medical reasons. However, if one chooses to color their hair for aesthetic reasons, they should choose a color that is close to their natural hair color.

How to Color Your Hair According to Islamic Teachings

If you choose to color your hair, it is important to do it in a way that is in line with Islamic teachings. Here are some guidelines:

  • Choose a color that is close to your natural hair color.
  • Avoid colors that are too bright or bold.
  • Do not use henna that has been mixed with harmful chemicals.
  • Do not expose your hair to excessive heat during the coloring process.
  • Do not use products that contain alcohol or other harmful chemicals.

Step-by-Step Guide on Coloring Your Hair

If you have decided to color your hair, here is a step-by-step guide on how to do it:

  1. Choose a color that is close to your natural hair color.
  2. Read the instructions on the hair dye package carefully.
  3. Put on gloves to protect your hands from the dye.
  4. Apply the dye to your hair, making sure to cover all areas evenly.
  5. Leave the dye on your hair for the recommended time.
  6. Rinse your hair thoroughly with water.
  7. Use a mild shampoo to wash your hair, and then apply conditioner.
  8. Rinse your hair again, making sure to remove all traces of the dye.

Tips for Maintaining Colored Hair

If you have colored your hair, it is important to take steps to maintain it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for colored hair.
  • Avoid excessive heat, such as blow-drying or using hot styling tools.
  • Avoid exposure to sunlight for extended periods of time.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.

FAQs

Q: Is it haram to color your hair?

A: No, it is not haram to color your hair, but it is discouraged.

Q: Can I use henna to color my hair?

A: Yes, henna is permissible to use to color your hair, as long as it does not contain harmful chemicals.

Q: Do I have to cover my hair when I color it?

A: No, it is not necessary to cover your hair when coloring it.

Final Thoughts

In conclusion, Islam does not prohibit coloring your hair, but it is discouraged. If you choose to color your hair, it is important to do it in a way that is in line with Islamic teachings. Choose a color that is close to your natural hair color, and avoid colors that are too bold or bright. Be sure to use products that are safe and avoid exposure to harsh chemicals and excessive heat.
