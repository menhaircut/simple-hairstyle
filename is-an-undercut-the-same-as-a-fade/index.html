Is An Undercut The Same As A Fade

Written by Frank Aug 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are trying to keep up with the latest hairstyle trends, you've probably heard of the terms "undercut" and "fade." While these two hairstyles may look similar, they have some distinct differences. In this article, we will discuss the difference between an undercut and a fade and help you decide which one is right for you.

Description

An undercut is a hairstyle where the hair on the sides and back of the head is shaved or cut very short, while the hair on the top of the head is left longer. This creates a contrast between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back. A fade, on the other hand, is a gradual change in hair length from the top of the head down to the sides and back. The hair gradually gets shorter as it moves down the head, creating a fade effect.

How To

If you want to get an undercut, you will need to have a barber shave or cut the sides and back of your head very short. The length of the hair on top can vary, but it is usually longer than the hair on the sides and back. To get a fade, your barber will start with longer hair on top and gradually blend it into shorter hair on the sides and back. The length of the fade can vary from a short fade to a long fade, depending on your preference.

Step by Step

To get an undercut, follow these steps:

  1. Decide on the length of hair you want on top of your head.
  2. Visit a barber and ask for an undercut.
  3. The barber will shave or cut the sides and back of your head very short.
  4. The barber will leave the hair on top of your head longer.
  5. You can style the hair on top of your head however you like.

To get a fade, follow these steps:

  1. Decide on the length of hair you want on top of your head.
  2. Visit a barber and ask for a fade.
  3. The barber will start with longer hair on top and gradually blend it into shorter hair on the sides and back.
  4. The barber will use clippers or scissors to create the fade effect.
  5. You can style the hair on top of your head however you like.

Tips

Here are some tips for maintaining your undercut or fade:

  • Visit your barber regularly to maintain the length of your hair.
  • Use hair products like pomade or hair wax to style your hair on top.
  • If you have an undercut, consider shaving or trimming the sides and back yourself to save money.
  • If you have a fade, consider getting it touched up every two weeks to keep it looking fresh.

FAQ

Q: Can I get an undercut or fade if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can get an undercut or fade with curly hair. However, it may be more difficult to style the hair on top if it is very curly.

Q: Which is easier to maintain, an undercut or a fade?

A: Both hairstyles require regular maintenance to keep them looking fresh. However, an undercut may be easier to maintain because the sides and back are shaved or cut very short.

Q: Can I still tie my hair back if I have an undercut or fade?

A: Yes, you can still tie your hair back if you have an undercut or fade. However, the sides and back may be too short to tie back, depending on the length of your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your undercut or fade looking fresh, you should wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. You should also use a hair product like pomade or hair wax to style your hair on top. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it and make it look frizzy. Finally, make sure to visit your barber regularly to maintain the length of your hair.

Now that you know the difference between an undercut and a fade, you can decide which hairstyle is right for you. Whether you prefer a bold, edgy look with an undercut or a more subtle, gradual look with a fade, both hairstyles are sure to turn heads.
