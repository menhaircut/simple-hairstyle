How To Cut Your Own Thick Hair Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune to get your hair cut at the salon? Do you want to try something new and cut your own thick hair short? With the right tools and techniques, cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own thick hair short.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you have thick hair. But with the right approach, you can achieve a stylish and professional-looking cut at home. Before you start, make sure you have the necessary tools and equipment, including sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. You'll also need to choose a haircut that suits your face shape and personal style.

How to Cut Your Own Thick Hair Short

Step 1: Clean and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see the natural shape and texture of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type, and towel dry your hair thoroughly.

Step 2: Choose Your Haircut

Decide on the style of haircut you want to achieve. Look at pictures online or in magazines to get inspiration. Keep in mind your face shape and personal style. If you're not sure what would look good on you, consult a hairstylist or use virtual haircut apps to try out different styles.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair by dividing it into four equal parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Use hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair by working on one section at a time. Use the comb to separate a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair to the desired length, using the comb as a guide. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Check for Symmetry

Check for symmetry by comparing each section of hair to the other. Make sure the lengths are even and adjust as necessary. Don't worry if it's not perfect – a slightly uneven cut can add texture and character to your hair.

Step 6: Trim the Ends

Trim the ends of your hair to remove any split ends or damaged hair. Hold the scissors vertically and snip off the ends of your hair. This will give your hair a clean and polished finish.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and work your way up to a shorter length if you're not sure how much to cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Take breaks if you need to rest your arms or hands.
  • Don't rush – take your time to ensure a precise cut.
  • Experiment with different techniques and styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if I have thick hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair even if you have thick hair. Just make sure you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully.

How do I choose a haircut that suits my face shape?

There are many resources available online that can help you choose a haircut that suits your face shape. Look at pictures of celebrities or consult a hairstylist for advice.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and the style of haircut you have. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy and manageable hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and vitality. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. Invest in a good quality hairbrush or comb to avoid damaging your hair, and consider using hair masks or treatments to keep your hair looking shiny and healthy.

Now that you know how to cut your own thick hair short, it's time to give it a try! With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a stylish and professional-looking haircut at home.
