How To Give Your Straight Hair Volume A Step By Step Guide

If you have straight hair, you may have noticed that it can often fall flat and lack volume. But don't worry, there are plenty of ways to add volume and body to your hair. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to give your straight hair volume.

Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

The first step to giving your straight hair volume is to use the right shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically designed to add volume and body to your hair. These products usually contain ingredients like keratin, biotin, and collagen, which can help strengthen and plump up your hair.

Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

One of the easiest ways to add volume to your straight hair is to blow dry it upside down. This technique helps lift your roots and create volume at the crown of your head. Here's how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair and apply a root lifting spray to your roots.
  3. Flip your head upside down and start blow drying your hair with a round brush.
  4. Focus the blow dryer on your roots, lifting them up with your brush.
  5. Dry the rest of your hair as usual, using the round brush to create volume throughout your hair.

Use a Root Lifting Spray

A root lifting spray can help give your hair extra lift and volume at the roots. Look for a product that is designed to add volume and body to your hair. Here's how to use it:

  1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair and apply a root lifting spray to your roots.
  3. Blow dry your hair upside down, focusing on your roots.

Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair can create instant volume and body. Here's how to do it:

  1. Section off the top layer of your hair.
  2. Take a small section of hair from underneath and backcomb it with a teasing brush.
  3. Repeat this process with small sections of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head.
  4. Once you've teased all the sections, gently smooth the top layer of your hair over the teased sections.

Add Volume with Rollers

Using rollers is a great way to add volume to your straight hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair and apply a root lifting spray to your roots.
  3. Divide your hair into sections and roll them up with velcro rollers.
  4. Blow dry your hair with the rollers in place, then remove the rollers once your hair is dry.

Try a Volumizing Powder

A volumizing powder can help add volume and texture to your hair. Here's how to use it:

  1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair and apply a root lifting spray to your roots.
  3. Apply a small amount of volumizing powder to your roots, then massage it in with your fingers.
  4. Style your hair as usual.

Use a Curling Iron

Using a curling iron can add volume and texture to your straight hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair and apply a root lifting spray to your roots.
  3. Take small sections of hair and curl them with a curling iron.
  4. Once you've curled all the sections, gently brush through your hair with your fingers to soften the curls and create volume.

Get a Haircut

Getting a haircut can also help add volume to your straight hair. Ask your stylist for a layered cut, which can create movement and volume throughout your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every day. If you have dry hair, you can probably wash it every other day.

Can I use volumizing products on colored hair?

Yes, but be sure to use products that are specifically designed for color-treated hair.

Will blow drying my hair upside down damage it?

No, as long as you use a heat protectant spray and don't hold the blow dryer too close to your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Volume

Here are some additional tips to help keep your hair looking voluminous and healthy:

  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to detangle your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and strength.
  • Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.

By following these tips and using the right products and techniques, you can give your straight hair the volume and body it deserves.
