What To Do When One Side Of Your Hair Sticks Out Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Oct 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're reading this, chances are you're no stranger to the frustration of having one side of your hair stick out like a sore thumb. Whether you're dealing with a cowlick, frizz, or just unruly hair, it can be a real pain to deal with. But fear not! In this article, we'll go over some helpful tips and tricks to help you tame that stubborn strand and get your hair looking its best.

Description: Why Does One Side of My Hair Stick Out?

Before we dive into how to fix the problem, let's take a look at what might be causing it in the first place. There are a few common culprits:

  • Cowlicks: These are areas where the hair grows in a different direction than the rest of your hair, resulting in a noticeable bump or wave.
  • Fine hair: If you have fine hair, it's more likely to stick out and be difficult to manage.
  • Frizz: Humidity, heat, and other factors can cause your hair to frizz and stand up.

How to: Quick Fixes for One Side of Hair Sticking Out

Now that you know what might be causing your hair to stick out, let's talk about some quick fixes you can try:

1. Use Hair Products

There are a variety of hair products that can help you tame unruly hair. Some good options include:

  • Hairspray: A light mist of hairspray can help keep your hair in place.
  • Gel: A small amount of gel can help smooth down flyaways and keep your hair looking neat.
  • Pomade: Pomade can help add weight to your hair, which can help keep it from sticking out.

2. Use a Hair Straightener

If your hair is sticking out because of a cowlick or wave, using a hair straightener can help. Simply apply some heat protectant spray to your hair, then use the straightener to smooth down the offending strand.

3. Use a Bobby Pin

If all else fails, you can always use a bobby pin to hold your hair in place. Just be sure to use a pin that matches your hair color so it doesn't stand out.

Step by Step: How to Fix One Side of Hair Sticking Out

If you have a little more time and want a more permanent solution, here's a step-by-step guide to fixing one side of your hair sticking out:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup that might be contributing to the problem.

Step 2: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair straight using a round brush. This will help smooth down any waves or cowlicks.

Step 3: Use a Flat Iron

Use a flat iron to further smooth down any stubborn strands.

Step 4: Apply Hair Serum

Apply a small amount of hair serum to your hair to add shine and weight, which can help keep your hair in place.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Finish off with a light mist of hairspray to keep everything in place.

Tips: How to Avoid One Side of Hair Sticking Out

Of course, the best way to deal with one side of your hair sticking out is to prevent it from happening in the first place. Here are a few tips:

  • Get Regular Haircuts: Regular haircuts can help prevent split ends, which can make your hair more prone to sticking out.
  • Use Heat Protectant Spray: Using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools can help prevent damage that can make your hair more difficult to manage.
  • Avoid Overwashing: Overwashing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, which can make it more prone to frizz and sticking out.

FAQ: Common Questions About One Side of Hair Sticking Out

Q: Can I use a hairbrush to fix one side of my hair sticking out?

A: Yes, a hairbrush can be helpful for smoothing down unruly strands. Just be sure to use a brush with soft bristles to avoid damaging your hair.

Q: Can genetics cause one side of my hair to stick out?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in how your hair behaves. If you're prone to cowlicks, for example, it's likely due to your genetics.

Q: Can I use a hair mask to help prevent one side of my hair sticking out?

A: Yes, a hair mask can be a great way to nourish your hair and keep it healthy, which can help prevent frizz and sticking out.

Hair Care: Taking Care of Your Hair to Prevent One Side from Sticking Out

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent it from sticking out. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a Gentle Shampoo: Harsh shampoos can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it more prone to frizz and sticking out.
  • Avoid Heat Damage: Using hot tools on your hair can damage it, making it more difficult to manage. Try to limit your use of hot tools, or use a heat protectant spray before using them.
  • Protect Your Hair from the Elements: Sun, wind, and other environmental factors can damage your hair, making it more prone to sticking out. Wear a hat or use a scarf to protect your hair when you're outside.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to tame that stubborn strand and get your hair looking its best. Happy styling!
