Can Vitamin B12 Deficiency Cause Itchy Scalp

Written by Daniel Nov 16, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're experiencing an itchy scalp, you may be wondering what could be causing it. One possible cause that you may not have considered is a vitamin B12 deficiency. In this article, we'll explore the link between vitamin B12 deficiency and itchy scalp, as well as some tips for maintaining healthy hair and scalp.

  • How Does Vitamin B12 Deficiency Cause Itchy Scalp?

What is Vitamin B12?

Vitamin B12 is an essential nutrient that plays a key role in many bodily functions, including the production of red blood cells and the maintenance of a healthy nervous system. It is found naturally in animal products such as meat, fish, and dairy, but can also be obtained through supplements or fortified foods.

How Does Vitamin B12 Deficiency Cause Itchy Scalp?

When your body is deficient in vitamin B12, it can lead to a condition called pernicious anemia. This condition can cause a range of symptoms, including fatigue, weakness, and tingling or numbness in the hands and feet. One lesser-known symptom of pernicious anemia is itchy scalp.

This is because vitamin B12 plays a role in the production of healthy skin cells, including those on the scalp. When your body is deficient in this nutrient, it can lead to the development of dry, itchy, and flaky scalp.

Other Symptoms of Vitamin B12 Deficiency

In addition to itchy scalp, vitamin B12 deficiency can cause a range of other symptoms, including:

  • Fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Dizziness
  • Pale skin
  • Tingling or numbness in the hands and feet
  • Muscle weakness
  • Memory problems

How to Prevent Vitamin B12 Deficiency

The best way to prevent vitamin B12 deficiency is to ensure that you're getting enough of this nutrient through your diet or supplements. If you're vegan or vegetarian, it's especially important to make sure you're getting enough vitamin B12, as it is primarily found in animal products.

If you suspect that you may be deficient in vitamin B12, it's important to talk to your doctor. They may recommend blood tests or other diagnostic tests to determine if you have a deficiency and what the best course of treatment may be.

Tips for Maintaining a Healthy Scalp

In addition to making sure you're getting enough vitamin B12, there are several other things you can do to maintain a healthy scalp:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Avoid using hot tools such as hair dryers and straighteners
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals such as hair dyes and bleaches
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet rich in vitamins and nutrients
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water

FAQs

Can vitamin B12 deficiency cause hair loss?

Yes, vitamin B12 deficiency can cause hair loss, as it plays an important role in the production of healthy hair follicles.

What foods are high in vitamin B12?

Animal products such as meat, fish, and dairy are high in vitamin B12. Some fortified cereals and plant-based milks may also contain vitamin B12.

Is itchy scalp a sign of a more serious condition?

In some cases, itchy scalp can be a sign of a more serious condition such as psoriasis, eczema, or seborrheic dermatitis. If your symptoms persist or worsen, it's important to talk to your doctor.


Overall, vitamin B12 deficiency can cause a range of symptoms, including itchy scalp. By making sure you're getting enough of this important nutrient and taking steps to maintain a healthy scalp, you can keep your hair and skin looking and feeling their best.
