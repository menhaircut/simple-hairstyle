How To Cut Ends Of Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be a beautiful and unique feature, but it also requires a little more maintenance than straight hair. One of the most important aspects of curly hair care is regular trimming, especially the ends. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut ends of curly hair.

How to Cut Ends of Curly Hair

The first step in cutting curly hair ends is to make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a spray bottle filled with water, and a comb. You may also want to use hair clips to section your hair.

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry because you can see the shape more clearly. Wet hair can also appear longer than it actually is, so you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. You can do this by using hair clips or simply parting your hair down the middle and then from ear to ear. This will help you focus on one section at a time and prevent you from cutting too much.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Once you have sectioned your hair, comb it thoroughly. This will help you see any tangles or knots that may need to be removed before you start cutting.

Step 3: Spray with Water

Spritz your hair with water to make it slightly damp. This will make it easier to cut and prevent split ends from flying all over the place.

Step 4: Trim the Ends

Using your scissors, trim off the ends of your hair. Start with small snips and work your way up, adjusting the length as you go. Remember, you can always cut more off, but you can't put it back on.

Step 5: Repeat

Repeat this process on each section of your hair until you have trimmed all of the ends.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair Ends

  • Invest in a good pair of professional hair cutting scissors. They will make a huge difference in the quality of your cut.
  • Always start with small snips and work your way up. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're not confident in your own abilities, ask a friend or family member to help you.
  • Trim your hair regularly. Curly hair tends to split more easily, so it's important to keep up with regular trims.
  • Consider visiting a professional stylist for a trim every few months. They can help you maintain the shape and health of your curls.

FAQs

How often should I trim my curly hair?

It depends on the individual, but every 3-4 months is a good rule of thumb. If your hair is prone to split ends or you use a lot of heat styling tools, you may need to trim more frequently.

Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

You can, but it's not recommended. Wet hair appears longer than it actually is, so you may end up cutting more than you intended. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry.

Should I cut my hair myself or visit a professional stylist?

It's up to you, but if you're not confident in your own abilities, it's best to visit a professional stylist. They can help you maintain the shape and health of your curls.

Curly Hair Care

Trimming your curly hair ends is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. Here are some other tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your curls and make them look greasy.

By following these tips and regularly trimming your curly hair ends, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful.
