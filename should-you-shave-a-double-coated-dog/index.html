Should You Shave A Double Coated Dog

Description

Double coated dogs have two layers of fur, an outer layer of long, coarse hair and an inner layer of soft, fluffy hair. These two layers work together to protect the dog from extreme temperatures, keeping them warm in the winter and cool in the summer. However, many dog owners wonder if they should shave their double coated dog, especially during the warmer months. In this article, we'll discuss the pros and cons of shaving a double coated dog and provide tips for hair care.

How to Shave a Double Coated Dog

Before you decide to shave your double coated dog, it's important to understand the risks involved. Shaving a double coated dog can cause the fur to grow back unevenly, which can lead to skin irritation and even sunburn. Additionally, shaving can damage the undercoat, which can lead to matting and tangling.

Step by Step Guide

If you've decided to shave your double coated dog, here's a step by step guide to follow:

  1. Brush your dog thoroughly to remove any loose hair and tangles.
  2. Use clippers to trim the fur to a length of about 1/2 inch.
  3. Use scissors to trim any areas that the clippers can't reach, such as the paws and face.
  4. Bathe your dog to remove any loose hair and debris.
  5. Dry your dog thoroughly with a towel and blow dryer.

Tips for Shaving a Double Coated Dog

Here are some tips to keep in mind when shaving a double coated dog:

  • Consult with a professional groomer or veterinarian before shaving your dog.
  • Invest in high-quality clippers and scissors to ensure a smooth cut.
  • Don't shave your dog too close to the skin, as this can cause irritation and sunburn.
  • Be patient and take breaks if your dog becomes anxious or restless.
  • Consider leaving some fur on your dog's body to protect them from the sun.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about shaving double coated dogs:

Can shaving a double coated dog cause health problems?

Shaving a double coated dog can lead to skin irritation, sunburn, and uneven fur growth. Additionally, it can damage the undercoat, which can lead to matting and tangling.

When is it okay to shave a double coated dog?

It's only okay to shave a double coated dog if it's necessary for medical reasons or if the dog is severely matted or tangled. Otherwise, it's best to leave the fur intact to protect the dog from extreme temperatures.

What are the best clippers and scissors for shaving a double coated dog?

The best clippers and scissors for shaving a double coated dog are high-quality and have sharp blades. It's important to invest in good tools to ensure a smooth cut and to prevent injury to the dog's skin.

Hair Care for Double Coated Dogs

If you've decided not to shave your double coated dog, it's important to take care of their fur to keep them healthy and comfortable.

  • Brush your dog regularly to remove any loose hair and tangles.
  • Bathe your dog every 6-8 weeks to remove any dirt and debris.
  • Use a conditioner to keep the fur soft and shiny.
  • Trim the fur around the paws, ears, and face to prevent matting and tangling.
  • Provide your dog with a comfortable and cool place to rest during hot weather.

In conclusion, shaving a double coated dog should only be done if it's necessary for medical reasons or if the dog is severely matted or tangled. Otherwise, it's best to leave the fur intact to protect the dog from extreme temperatures. If you do decide to shave your dog, be sure to consult with a professional groomer or veterinarian and invest in high-quality clippers and scissors.
