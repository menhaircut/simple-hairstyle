How To Stop Hereditary Hair Fall 10 Effective Tips

Written by Eveline Sep 22, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair fall that runs in your family? Don't worry, there are ways to prevent it. In this article, we will share with you 10 effective tips on how to stop hereditary hair fall. Read on to learn more!

Description

Hereditary hair fall is a common problem that affects many people around the world. It is caused by genetic factors that can make your hair follicles shrink, weaken, and eventually fall out. If you are experiencing this problem, you may notice that your hair is thinning, shedding, or receding. Fortunately, there are ways to stop hereditary hair fall and promote hair growth. In this article, we will show you how to prevent hair fall naturally and effectively.

How to Stop Hereditary Hair Fall: Step by Step

Step 1: Understand the Causes of Hereditary Hair Fall

The first step in stopping hereditary hair fall is to understand its underlying causes. As mentioned earlier, this type of hair fall is genetic and can be passed down from one generation to another. It is caused by a hormone called DHT, which can shrink hair follicles and shorten the hair growth cycle. Other factors that can contribute to hereditary hair fall include stress, poor nutrition, and hormonal imbalances.

Step 2: Get a Proper Diagnosis

If you are experiencing hair fall, it is important to get a proper diagnosis from a hair specialist or dermatologist. They can help you identify the cause of your hair fall and recommend the right treatment or medication. Some common tests that they may perform include a hair analysis, blood tests, and a scalp biopsy.

Step 3: Follow a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is essential for preventing hair fall and promoting hair growth. Make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals. Some foods that are good for hair health include eggs, fish, nuts, berries, and leafy greens. Avoid processed foods, sugary drinks, and alcohol, as they can weaken your hair and make it prone to falling out.

Step 4: Practice Good Hair Care

Good hair care is crucial for preventing hereditary hair fall. Make sure to use a mild shampoo and conditioner that are free from harsh chemicals. Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage and damage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to untangle your hair gently. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, such as braids, ponytails, or buns.

Step 5: Use Hair Growth Products

There are many hair growth products available in the market that can help you stop hereditary hair fall. These products contain ingredients that can stimulate hair growth and strengthen your hair follicles. Some popular hair growth products include minoxidil, finasteride, and biotin supplements. Make sure to consult with your doctor before using any hair growth products, as they can have side effects.

Step 6: Try Natural Remedies

There are many natural remedies that can help you prevent hereditary hair fall. Some effective remedies include applying aloe vera gel, coconut oil, onion juice, or green tea to your scalp. These ingredients can nourish your hair follicles and promote hair growth. You can also try massaging your scalp with essential oils, such as peppermint, lavender, or rosemary.

Step 7: Reduce Stress

Stress can be a major contributor to hereditary hair fall. When you are stressed, your body produces hormones that can interfere with your hair growth cycle. To reduce stress, try practicing relaxation techniques, such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing. Make sure to get enough sleep and exercise regularly, as these can also help you manage stress.

Step 8: Avoid Heat and Chemical Treatments

Heat and chemical treatments can damage your hair and make it prone to falling out. Avoid using hot tools, such as straighteners, curlers, or blow dryers, as they can weaken your hair and cause breakage. Also, avoid chemical treatments, such as coloring, perming, or relaxing, as they can damage your hair follicles and make your hair brittle.

Step 9: Consider Hair Transplant Surgery

If you have severe hereditary hair fall that cannot be treated with medication or natural remedies, you may want to consider hair transplant surgery. This procedure involves transplanting healthy hair follicles from one part of your scalp to another. It is a safe and effective way to restore your hairline and promote hair growth.

Step 10: Stay Positive and Patient

Finally, it is important to stay positive and patient when trying to stop hereditary hair fall. Hair growth takes time and patience, and it may take several months or even years to see results. Don't get discouraged if you don't see immediate improvement, and keep following a healthy diet and hair care routine.

Tips for Preventing Hereditary Hair Fall

Here are some additional tips that can help you prevent hereditary hair fall:

  • Avoid smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your scalp hydrated
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or helmets that can trap heat and sweat
  • Take breaks from using hot tools and chemical treatments
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and breakage

FAQs

1. What is hereditary hair fall?

Hereditary hair fall is a type of hair loss that is caused by genetic factors. It can be passed down from one generation to another and is characterized by a gradual thinning, shedding, or receding of hair.

2. Can hereditary hair fall be prevented?

Yes, hereditary hair fall can be prevented by following a healthy diet, practicing good hair care, using hair growth products, trying natural remedies, reducing stress, and avoiding heat and chemical treatments. In some cases, hair transplant surgery may also be an option.

3. How long does it take to see results from hair growth products?

The time it takes to see results from hair growth products may vary depending on the product and the individual. Some products may take several months to produce noticeable results, while others may work faster. It is important to follow the manufacturer's instructions and consult with your doctor if you have any concerns.

Hair Care Tips for Hereditary Hair Fall

Here are some additional hair care tips that can help you prevent hereditary hair fall:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip your scalp of natural oils
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair
  • Avoid over-brushing or over-styling your hair, as it can cause breakage and damage
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and promote healthy growth

By following these tips and steps, you can stop hereditary hair fall and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to stay patient and positive, and consult with your doctor if you have any concerns. Good luck!
