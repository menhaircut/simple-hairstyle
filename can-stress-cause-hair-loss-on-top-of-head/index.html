Can Stress Cause Hair Loss On Top Of Head

Written by Robby Sep 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Stress is a common problem for everyone in today's fast-paced world. It is a feeling of emotional or physical tension that can cause various health problems. One of the most common problems that people face due to stress is hair loss. Hair loss can occur due to many reasons, but can stress cause hair loss on top of head? Let's find out.

Description

Hair loss is a common problem that affects millions of people around the world. It is a condition that can be caused due to various factors such as genetics, age, hormonal changes, and stress. Hair loss due to stress is called telogen effluvium. It is a condition where the hair follicles enter the resting phase prematurely, resulting in hair loss.

How to

If you are experiencing hair loss due to stress, there are a few things that you can do to prevent further hair loss. Here are some of the things that you can do:

1. Reduce stress

The first thing that you need to do is to reduce stress. You can do this by practicing relaxation techniques such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises. These techniques can help you relax and reduce stress levels.

2. Eat a healthy diet

Eating a healthy diet is essential for maintaining healthy hair. Make sure that you are eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals. Include foods that are rich in protein, iron, and biotin in your diet.

3. Take supplements

If you are not getting enough nutrients from your diet, you can take supplements. Supplements such as biotin, iron, and vitamin D can help promote healthy hair growth.

Tips

FAQ

Q. Can stress cause hair loss on top of head?

A. Yes, stress can cause hair loss on top of the head.

Q. How long does it take for hair to grow back after telogen effluvium?

A. It can take up to 6 months for hair to grow back after telogen effluvium.

Hair Care

Here are some hair care tips that can help prevent hair loss:

1. Use a mild shampoo

Use a mild shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp.

2. Avoid harsh styling products

Avoid using harsh styling products such as gels, sprays, and hair dyes that can damage your hair.

3. Avoid tight hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids and ponytails that can pull on your hair and cause damage.

4. Protect your hair from the sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a sunscreen for your hair.
