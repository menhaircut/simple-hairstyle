How To Dread Straight Hair Naturally A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 20, 2023 · 5 min read

Dreading straight hair can be a challenge, but it’s not impossible. With the right techniques and a little bit of patience, you can achieve dreadlocks that are natural-looking and healthy. In this guide, we’ll show you how to dread straight hair naturally.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Dread Straight Hair Naturally
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Dreading Straight Hair Naturally
  5. FAQ About Dreading Straight Hair Naturally
  6. Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Description

Dreadlocks are a hairstyle that involves matting or braiding hair into tight, rope-like strands. This hairstyle has been around for centuries and is popular among different cultures, including African, Caribbean, and Rastafarian. While dreadlocks are traditionally associated with curly or kinky hair, people with straight hair can also achieve this look.

Dreading straight hair naturally involves a different process than dreading curly or kinky hair. Straight hair needs more time and effort to lock up, but the result can be just as beautiful and unique.

How to Dread Straight Hair Naturally

The key to dreading straight hair naturally is to let your hair do its thing. Avoid using any products that will make your hair appear artificial, such as waxes or gels. Instead, use natural methods to encourage your hair to mat and lock up.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to dread straight hair naturally:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition your hair to remove any buildup or oils.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use a comb to separate your hair into sections that are about the same size. The sections should be no wider than the width of your finger.
  3. Twist each section. Take one section of hair and twist it tightly in one direction. You can do this with your fingers or a comb. The tighter the twist, the faster your hair will dread.
  4. Backcomb the twisted section. Use a comb to backcomb the twisted section of hair. Start at the roots and comb towards the ends. This will create knots and tangles in your hair, which will eventually turn into dreadlocks.
  5. Repeat steps 3-4 for each section of hair. Make sure to twist and backcomb each section of hair in the same direction.
  6. Leave your hair alone. Once you’ve twisted and backcombed your hair, leave it alone. Don’t brush or comb it, as this will undo the knots and tangles that you’ve created.
  7. Wash your hair sparingly. It’s important to keep your hair clean, but washing it too often can delay the dreading process. Try to wash your hair no more than once a week.
  8. Be patient. Dreading straight hair naturally takes time. It can take several months for your hair to fully lock up and form dreadlocks.

Tips for Dreading Straight Hair Naturally

Here are some tips to help you dread straight hair naturally:

  • Don’t use any products that contain wax, gel, or other artificial ingredients. These products can prevent your hair from locking up naturally.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer or heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and delay the dreading process.
  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage and prevent your hair from dreading.
  • Consider using a natural sea salt spray to encourage your hair to lock up. Mix sea salt with water in a spray bottle and mist your hair daily.
  • Be patient. Dreading straight hair naturally takes time, so don’t get discouraged if your hair doesn’t lock up right away.

FAQ About Dreading Straight Hair Naturally

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreading straight hair naturally:

  • Can I dread straight hair without backcombing it? Yes, you can dread straight hair without backcombing it. Some people use the twist and rip method or the neglect method to dread their hair.
  • Can I wash my hair while it’s dreading? Yes, you can wash your hair while it’s dreading. Just be sure to use a gentle, natural shampoo and avoid rubbing your hair too vigorously.
  • Do I need to cut my hair to dread it? No, you don’t need to cut your hair to dread it. You can dread hair of any length, although it may take longer for shorter hair to lock up.
  • Can I dye my hair while it’s dreading? Yes, you can dye your hair while it’s dreading. Just be sure to use a natural, ammonia-free hair dye and avoid getting dye on your scalp.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once your hair has locked up into dreadlocks, it’s important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often. Use a gentle, natural shampoo and avoid rubbing your hair too vigorously.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or conditioners, as these can weigh down your hair and prevent it from locking up.
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten loose hairs and help your dreadlocks form more tightly.
  • Avoid using rubber bands or hair ties, as these can cause breakage and damage your hair.
  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on your dreadlocks, as this can cause them to unravel.

Dreading straight hair naturally can be a rewarding and unique experience. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve natural-looking dreadlocks that are healthy and beautiful.
