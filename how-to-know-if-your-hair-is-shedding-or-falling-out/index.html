How To Know If Your Hair Is Shedding Or Falling Out

Description

It's normal to lose some hair every day. However, if you notice that you're losing more hair than usual, you might be wondering if it's shedding or falling out. Shedding is a normal process where old hair falls out to make way for new hair. Hair loss, on the other hand, can be due to various reasons such as genetics, stress, or medical conditions.

How to

If you want to know if your hair is shedding or falling out, there are a few things you can do. First, pay attention to the amount of hair you're losing. If you're shedding, you'll notice a few hairs coming out when you comb or wash your hair. However, if you're losing hair, you'll notice clumps of hair coming out.

You can also check the texture of the hair that's coming out. If it's shedding, the hair will have a white bulb at the end, which means it's falling out naturally. If it's falling out, the hair will be shorter and won't have a white bulb at the end.

Step by step

Here are the steps you can follow to know if your hair is shedding or falling out:

  1. Pay attention to the amount of hair you're losing when combing or washing your hair.
  2. Check the texture of the hair that's coming out.
  3. If the hair has a white bulb at the end, it's shedding. If it doesn't have a white bulb and is shorter, it's falling out.

Tips

If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to take care of your hair and scalp. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids, weaves, or ponytails that can pull on your hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat on your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and vitamins that are essential for hair growth.
  • Manage your stress levels as stress can contribute to hair loss.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal to lose hair every day?
A: Yes, it's normal to lose some hair every day.

Q: What causes hair loss?
A: Hair loss can be due to various reasons such as genetics, stress, medical conditions, or hormonal changes.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair and scalp is essential for preventing hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Use conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair as it can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using too many hair products.

By following these tips and knowing the difference between shedding and hair loss, you can take better care of your hair and prevent further damage.
