Curly Hair With A Perm The Ultimate Guide For 2023

Description

Curly hair with a perm is a popular hairstyle choice among women. It offers a low-maintenance way to achieve voluminous curls that last for months. A perm is a chemical process that changes the structure of the hair, creating curls or waves. With a perm, you can achieve a range of curly styles, from tight ringlets to loose waves.

How To

If you're considering getting a perm, it's important to find a stylist who specializes in curly hair. They will be able to advise you on the best type of perm for your hair type and desired style. Before getting a perm, it's important to have healthy hair, so make sure to take care of your hair in the weeks leading up to your appointment.

Types of Perms:

There are two main types of perms: cold perms and hot perms. Cold perms use a chemical solution to create curls in the hair, while hot perms use heat. Cold perms are generally gentler on the hair and can be a good choice for those with fine or damaged hair. Hot perms can create tighter curls and are a good choice for those with thicker hair.

Preparing for a Perm:

Before getting a perm, it's important to avoid washing your hair for a few days. This will help protect your scalp from the chemicals in the perm solution. Make sure to also avoid using any styling products or heat tools on your hair in the days leading up to your appointment.

Step By Step

Step 1: Consultation

The first step in getting a perm is to schedule a consultation with a stylist who specializes in curly hair. During the consultation, you and your stylist will discuss your hair type, desired style, and any concerns you may have. Your stylist will also assess the health of your hair to ensure it's in good condition for a perm.

Step 2: Preparing Your Hair

Before your appointment, make sure to avoid washing your hair for a few days. On the day of your appointment, your stylist will shampoo your hair to remove any buildup or oils. They may also apply a protective cream around your hairline and ears to protect your skin from the perm solution.

Step 3: Applying the Perm Solution

Once your hair is prepared, your stylist will apply the perm solution to your hair. They will use rods or rollers to create the desired curl pattern. The solution will be left on your hair for a set amount of time, depending on the curl pattern you want and the strength of your hair.

Step 4: Neutralizing the Hair

After the perm solution has been applied, your stylist will rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any excess solution. They will then apply a neutralizing solution to your hair to stop the chemical process and set the curls. This solution will also help to restore the pH balance of your hair.

Step 5: Styling Your Hair

Once your hair has been neutralized, your stylist will remove the rods or rollers from your hair and apply a conditioning treatment. They will then style your hair using a diffuser or other styling tools to enhance your curls and create the desired look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you care for your curly hair with a perm:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as this can damage the curls.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braid to help preserve your curls overnight.

FAQ

Q: How long does a perm last?

A: A perm can last anywhere from 3 to 6 months, depending on how quickly your hair grows and how well you take care of it.

Q: Can I color my hair after getting a perm?

A: It's best to wait at least a week after getting a perm before coloring your hair. Coloring your hair too soon after a perm can cause damage and weaken the curls.

Hair Care

To keep your curly hair with a perm looking its best, it's important to follow a good hair care routine. This includes using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat tools, and getting regular trims to prevent split ends.

It's also important to deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy. You can use a store-bought deep conditioner or make your own by mixing a quarter cup of olive oil with a quarter cup of honey.

With the right care and maintenance, you can enjoy beautiful, curly hair with a perm for months to come.
