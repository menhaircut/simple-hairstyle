How To Make Big Curly Hair Smaller Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jul 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have big curly hair, you know the struggle of trying to tame your mane. While voluminous curls can be beautiful, sometimes you may want to make your hair look smaller and more manageable. Luckily, there are a few tricks you can try to create a more subdued look. In this post, we'll take you through the steps you need to follow to make your big curly hair smaller.

Description

Big curly hair can be a blessing, but it can also be a curse. While it's great to have hair with volume and bounce, it can be difficult to manage. If you're tired of your hair taking up too much space, it's time to learn how to make your big curly hair smaller. In this post, we'll show you how to do just that.

How to Make Big Curly Hair Smaller: Step by Step

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

The first step to making your big curly hair smaller is to start with clean hair. Use a clarifying or volumizing shampoo to remove any buildup, and follow up with a lightweight conditioner. Rinse your hair thoroughly, and then gently pat it dry with a towel.

Step 2: Apply a Smoothing Serum

Next, apply a smoothing serum to your hair. This will help to tame frizz and make your curls more manageable. Use a dime-sized amount of serum, and apply it evenly throughout your hair. Be sure to focus on the ends, where hair tends to be the most unruly.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair with a Diffuser Attachment

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. This will help to keep your curls intact while also reducing frizz. Start by drying the roots of your hair, and then work your way down to the ends. Use a low heat setting and be sure to move the diffuser around to avoid overheating any one area.

Step 4: Use a Curling Iron to Define Your Curls

If you want to make your curls smaller and more defined, use a curling iron to touch up any areas that need it. Use a small-barreled curling iron, and wrap small sections of hair around the barrel. Hold the curling iron in place for a few seconds, and then release the curl. Repeat this process until all of your curls are the size and shape you want them to be.

Tips to Make Big Curly Hair Smaller

Here are some additional tips to help you make your big curly hair smaller:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I make my big curly hair smaller without using heat?

A: Yes, you can. Instead of using a curling iron, try using foam rollers or flexi rods to create smaller curls. You can also try using a hair tie to create a loose bun, which will help to define your curls without using heat.

Q: How often should I wash my big curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair prefer to wash their hair every day, while others only wash their hair once a week. Experiment with different washing schedules to find what works best for you.

Hair Care for Big Curly Hair

If you have big curly hair, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and hydrated:

  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can damage your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your big curly hair smaller and more manageable. Remember to take good care of your hair, and experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you.
