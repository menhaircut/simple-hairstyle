How To Become A Mobile Hairdresser In The Uk

Written by Christine Aug 30, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are passionate about hairstyling and want to take your skills on the road, becoming a mobile hairdresser in the UK can be a great career choice. As a mobile hairdresser, you can offer your services to clients in the comfort of their own homes or workplaces, providing them with a convenient and personalized experience.

  • How to Become a Mobile Hairdresser UK
  • Step by Step Guide

Description

A mobile hairdresser is a hairstylist who offers their services outside of a traditional salon setting. Instead of working in a shop, they travel to their clients' homes or workplaces to provide haircuts, styling, coloring, and other hair-related services. This type of service is becoming increasingly popular in the UK, as it provides clients with a more personalized and convenient experience.

How to Become a Mobile Hairdresser UK

To become a mobile hairdresser in the UK, you will need to follow these steps:

Step 1: Get Trained and Certified

The first step to becoming a mobile hairdresser is to get the necessary training and certification. You can enroll in a hairdressing course at a college or training center, or you can find an apprenticeship with a licensed hairdresser. Once you have completed your training, you will need to pass an exam to become a certified hairdresser.

Step 2: Build Your Skills and Portfolio

As a mobile hairdresser, you will need to be skilled in a variety of hair-related services, including cutting, coloring, styling, and more. You can build your skills by practicing on friends and family members, or by taking additional courses and workshops. It's also important to build a portfolio of your work, which you can use to showcase your skills and attract potential clients.

Step 3: Get the Necessary Equipment

To become a mobile hairdresser, you will need to invest in the necessary equipment, including hairdressing tools, products, and a portable salon chair. You will also need to have reliable transportation, such as a car or van, to travel to your clients' locations.

Step 4: Register Your Business

Before you can start offering your services as a mobile hairdresser, you will need to register your business with the appropriate authorities. This may include registering for a business license, tax ID number, and insurance.

Step 5: Market Your Services

Once you are ready to start offering your services, you will need to market yourself to potential clients. You can create a website or social media page to showcase your services and portfolio, and you can also advertise in local newspapers or on community noticeboards.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to becoming a mobile hairdresser in the UK:

Step 1: Get trained and certified in hairdressing.

Step 2: Build your skills and portfolio by practicing on friends and family members.

Step 3: Invest in the necessary equipment, including hairdressing tools, products, and a portable salon chair.

Step 4: Register your business with the appropriate authorities.

Step 5: Market your services to potential clients through a website, social media, or local advertising.

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you succeed as a mobile hairdresser in the UK:

  • Offer competitive prices to attract clients.
  • Be punctual and reliable to build client trust.
  • Invest in high-quality equipment to provide the best possible service.
  • Stay up-to-date with the latest hair trends and techniques.
  • Provide excellent customer service to build a loyal client base.

FAQs

Q: Do I need a license to become a mobile hairdresser in the UK?

A: Yes, you will need to be a certified hairdresser and register your business with the appropriate authorities before offering your services as a mobile hairdresser.

Q: How much can I earn as a mobile hairdresser in the UK?

A: The amount you can earn as a mobile hairdresser will depend on factors such as your location, skills, and the demand for your services. However, mobile hairdressers in the UK can earn anywhere from £20,000 to £50,000 per year.

Hair Care Tips

As a mobile hairdresser, it's important to educate your clients on proper hair care techniques. Here are some tips you can share:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent damage and dryness.
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair to prevent breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat by wearing a hat or using a heat protectant spray.
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and steps, you can become a successful mobile hairdresser in the UK and provide your clients with a convenient and personalized hairdressing experience.
