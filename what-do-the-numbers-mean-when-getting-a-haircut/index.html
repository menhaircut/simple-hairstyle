What Do The Numbers Mean When Getting A Haircut

Written by Oliver Aug 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who's ever gotten a haircut, chances are you've heard your barber or stylist throw around numbers while discussing your desired style. What do these numbers mean? How can they help you get the haircut you want? In this article, we'll break down what the numbers mean and how you can use them to communicate with your stylist.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When you go to get a haircut, your stylist or barber may ask you what number you want. This number refers to the guard on the clippers they use to cut your hair. Clippers have different guards that can be attached to them to achieve different lengths of hair.

For example, a number 1 guard will cut your hair to 1/8 of an inch, while a number 8 guard will leave your hair 1 inch long. The higher the number, the longer the hair will be left.

How to

When you're getting a haircut, it's important to communicate clearly with your stylist or barber. Knowing what number guard you want can help you achieve the desired length of hair. Here's how to use the numbers:

  1. Decide on the length of hair you want. Do you want a buzz cut? A crew cut? A longer style?
  2. Look at a chart of clipper guard sizes to determine what number corresponds with your desired length.
  3. Tell your stylist or barber what number you want. For example, you may say "I want a number 4 on the sides and back, and a scissor cut on top."
  4. Be open to your stylist's suggestions. They may recommend a different number or technique to achieve the look you want.

Step by step

If you're still unsure of how to use the numbers, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Find a chart of clipper guard sizes online or ask your stylist for one.
  2. Determine what length you want your hair to be. For example, if you want a buzz cut, you may want a number 1 guard.
  3. Tell your stylist or barber what number you want. Be specific about which areas of your head you want cut with that guard.
  4. If you're unsure about which number to use, ask your stylist for their recommendation.
  5. During the haircut, communicate with your stylist if you want any adjustments made.
  6. After the haircut, take a look in the mirror and make sure you're happy with the length and style.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using clipper guard numbers:

  • Don't be afraid to ask your stylist for their opinion. They're professionals and can help you achieve the look you want.
  • If you're trying a new style or length, start with a longer guard and work your way down. It's easier to take off more hair than to add it back on.
  • Take care of your hair after the cut by using appropriate products and getting regular trims to maintain the style.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about clipper guard numbers:

What if I don't know what length I want?

Don't worry! Your stylist can help you determine what length will look best based on your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.

What if I don't like the length of my hair after the cut?

Let your stylist know as soon as possible. They may be able to make adjustments to fix the length or suggest a different style that will work better for you.

Can I use clipper guards at home?

Yes! Clipper guards can be purchased at most beauty supply stores and are easy to attach to clippers. Just make sure to follow the instructions carefully and start with a longer guard if you're unsure of the length you want.

Hair Care

After you've gotten your haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Use appropriate products for your hair type and style. For example, if you have curly hair, use a product designed for curls to enhance them.
  • Get regular trims to keep the style looking fresh and to prevent split ends.
  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often. Over-washing can strip the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.

Now that you know what the numbers mean when getting a haircut, you can communicate more effectively with your stylist or barber to achieve the desired length and style. Don't be afraid to ask questions or make adjustments during the cut, and take care of your hair afterwards to keep it looking its best.
