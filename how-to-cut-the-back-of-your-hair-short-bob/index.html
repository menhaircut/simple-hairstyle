How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair Short Bob

Written by Robby Oct 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not a professional stylist. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can easily cut the back of your hair into a chic short bob. Follow these step-by-step instructions to achieve the perfect cut.

Description

A short bob is a classic and timeless hairstyle that is both chic and low maintenance. This style features hair that is cut straight around the head, usually at chin or ear-length. The back of the hair is typically shorter than the front, creating a subtle angle that frames the face. If you're looking to achieve this style at home, follow these steps to learn how to cut the back of your hair short bob.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair Short Bob

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair, making it easier to cut.

Step 3: Section Off Your Hair

Using your comb, create a horizontal parting across the back of your head, dividing the hair into two sections. Clip the top section of hair up and out of the way.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Section

Take the bottom section of hair and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to cut the hair straight across, making sure to trim any uneven ends. The length of the hair should be shorter in the back and gradually longer towards the front.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Use your comb to blend the hair from the back to the sides, creating a smooth and seamless look.

Step 6: Cut the Top Section

Release the top section of hair and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, making sure to blend it with the back section.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting, comb through your hair and check for any uneven spots or choppy layers. Make any necessary adjustments until your hair is even and well-blended.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up, as it's easier to trim more hair than to grow it back.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp to prevent any split ends or damage to your hair.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can appear shorter once it dries.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself, consider consulting with a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair into a short bob if it's curly?

Absolutely! A short bob can work well with curly hair. It's important to cut it when it's dry and to leave some length to avoid any unwanted shrinkage.

Do I need to cut my hair straight across?

No, you can create a more textured or layered look by cutting your hair at an angle or point-cutting the ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain the style. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and avoid any frizz. Regular trims every 6-8 weeks will also help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these simple steps, you can easily cut the back of your hair into a chic short bob. Whether you're looking for a low-maintenance style or just want to switch things up, this classic look is sure to turn heads.
