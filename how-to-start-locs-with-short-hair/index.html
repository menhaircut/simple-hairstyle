How To Start Locs With Short Hair

Are you ready to change up your hairstyle and start locs with your short hair? It may seem daunting at first, but with the right guidance, you can achieve your desired loc style. In this article, we will guide you through the process of starting locs with short hair, including step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Start Locs with Short Hair

Starting locs with short hair is possible, but it may take some time and patience. The first step is to decide what type of locs you want, whether it's traditional or freeform. Traditional locs are more uniform in size and shape, while freeform locs are more organic and natural-looking.

Once you have decided on the type of locs you want, you will need to gather the necessary tools, including a comb, brush, and styling gel. You may also want to consider using a starter loc kit, which includes all the necessary tools and products.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to start locs with short hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will remove any dirt or buildup and prepare your hair for the loc process.
  2. Section your hair into small, even parts using a comb. The size of the sections will depend on the type of locs you want.
  3. Apply styling gel to each section of hair, paying close attention to the roots. This will help the hair stay in place and form into locs.
  4. Use a comb or brush to twist each section of hair. You can either do this manually or use a tool like a latch hook.
  5. Repeat the twisting process for each section of hair until your entire head is covered in twisted sections.
  6. Allow the hair to dry completely. You can use a hair dryer on a low setting to speed up the process.
  7. Once the hair is dry, gently unravel each twist to reveal the newly formed locs.
  8. Continue to care for and maintain your locs as they grow and mature.

Tips for Starting Locs with Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you start locs with short hair:

  • Be patient. It may take several months or even a year for your locs to fully mature.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair and prevent it from forming into locs.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting locs with short hair:

  • Can you start locs with relaxed hair?
  • How often should you wash your hair when starting locs?
  • Do you need to retwist your locs?
  • Can you color your hair with locs?

Hair Care for Locs with Short Hair

Here are some tips for caring for your locs with short hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo. Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils that can cause buildup.
  • Retwist your locs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent unraveling.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or swim cap.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling wands, as they can damage your hair.

Starting locs with short hair may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve your desired loc style. Remember to be patient, take good care of your hair, and enjoy the journey!
