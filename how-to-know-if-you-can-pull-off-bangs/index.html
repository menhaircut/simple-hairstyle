How To Know If You Can Pull Off Bangs

Written by Eveline Jul 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you contemplating getting bangs but are unsure whether they will suit you? Bangs can be a great way to switch up your look, but they require some careful consideration before committing to the cut. In this article, we will give you tips on how to know if you can pull off bangs and what to consider before making the chop.

Face Shape

The first consideration before getting bangs is your face shape. Some bang styles may suit certain shapes more than others. For example, if you have a round face, side-swept bangs can help elongate your face, while full blunt bangs may make your face appear wider. Similarly, if you have a heart-shaped face, full bangs can balance out a wider forehead. Look up hairstyles for your face shape to get an idea of what will work best for you.

Hair Texture

Your hair texture is another important factor to consider. Bangs require regular maintenance, and if you have curly or wavy hair, they may require more effort to style than if you have straight hair. If you have fine hair, full blunt bangs may appear sparse and wispy. In this case, consider a more textured or side-swept style.

Hair Type

Consider your hair type as well. If you have thick hair, full blunt bangs can look great, but may require more effort to style. If you have thin hair, side-swept bangs can add volume and texture. If you have naturally oily hair, full blunt bangs may require more frequent washes to prevent them from looking greasy.

Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is another factor to consider before getting bangs. If you lead an active lifestyle or live in a humid climate, bangs may require more upkeep to prevent them from sticking to your forehead or becoming frizzy. Additionally, if you wear glasses, consider how they will sit with your new bangs. You may need to adjust the length or style to ensure they don't interfere with your frames.

Face Framing Bangs

If you're unsure about committing to full bangs, consider face framing bangs instead. These are shorter, wispy bangs that frame your face and can be styled to the side or pushed back. They are lower maintenance than full bangs and can be a great way to ease into a new look.

Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs are a versatile option that can work with many different face shapes and hair types. They are longer than full bangs and are swept to the side, creating a more natural look. They can be styled with a round brush for a sleek look or left to air dry for a more textured look.

Full Bangs

Full bangs are a bold choice that can completely transform your look. They require more maintenance than other bang styles, as they need to be trimmed regularly to avoid becoming too long or scruffy. They can be styled straight, textured, or with a slight curve to soften the look.

Hair Care

Once you've decided on a bang style, it's important to take care of your hair to keep your bangs looking fresh. Regular trims are essential to maintaining the shape and length of your bangs. Use a heat protectant spray before styling with hot tools and avoid over-washing your hair to prevent your bangs from becoming greasy.

FAQ

How do I know if I have the right face shape for bangs?

There are many online resources that can help you determine your face shape. Look up hairstyles for your face shape to get an idea of what will work best for you.

How often do I need to trim my bangs?

For full bangs, aim to trim them every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh. For face framing or side-swept bangs, you can go a little longer between trims, around 6-8 weeks.

What should I do if I don't like my new bangs?

If you're not happy with your new bangs, don't panic. Speak to your stylist about adjusting the length or style to better suit your preferences. Alternatively, you can clip them back or pin them to the side until they grow out.

Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with bangs?

Absolutely! Side-swept bangs can be easily incorporated into a ponytail or updo, while full bangs can be pinned back or swept to the side.

How can I prevent my bangs from becoming greasy?

Avoid over-washing your hair and use a dry shampoo between washes to absorb excess oil. You can also use a blotting sheet or powder to absorb any excess oil on your forehead.

By considering your face shape, hair texture, hair type, and lifestyle, you can determine whether bangs will suit you. Whether you opt for face framing, side-swept, or full bangs, remember to take care of your hair to keep your new look fresh.
