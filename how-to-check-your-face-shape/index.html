How To Check Your Face Shape A Guide For Perfect Hairstyles

Written by Daniel Jul 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Knowing your face shape is essential when it comes to choosing the perfect hairstyle. It can be difficult to determine your face shape, but it's important to get it right. In this article, we'll show you how to check your face shape and provide some tips on how to choose the best hairstyles for your face shape.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Check Your Face Shape
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Choosing Hairstyles
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Different Face Shapes

How to Check Your Face Shape

The first step in determining your face shape is to pull back your hair and stand in front of a mirror. Make sure your face is clean and free of any makeup or accessories. Here are some things to look for when checking your face shape:

  • The width of your forehead
  • The width of your cheekbones
  • The length of your face
  • The shape of your jawline

Once you've taken note of these features, you can start to determine your face shape.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide to help you determine your face shape:

Step 1: Measure Your Forehead

Use a tape measure to measure the width of your forehead from one temple to the other.

Step 2: Measure Your Cheekbones

Measure the width of your cheekbones at their widest point.

Step 3: Measure Your Jawline

Measure from the tip of your chin to the base of your ear, then multiply that number by two to get the length of your jawline.

Step 4: Measure Your Face Length

Measure from the top of your forehead to the bottom of your chin.

Step 5: Determine Your Face Shape

Using the measurements you've taken, you can now determine your face shape:

  • Oval: Your face length is greater than the width of your cheekbones, and your forehead is wider than your jawline.
  • Round: Your face length and cheekbone width are roughly equal, and your forehead and jawline are also roughly equal in width.
  • Square: Your face length, forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all roughly equal in width.
  • Heart: Your forehead is wider than your cheekbones, and your chin comes to a point.
  • Diamond: Your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your forehead and jawline are narrower.
  • Triangle: Your jawline is wider than your forehead and cheekbones.

Tips for Choosing Hairstyles

Once you've determined your face shape, you can start choosing hairstyles that will flatter your features. Here are some tips:

  • Oval: Almost any hairstyle will flatter an oval face shape.
  • Round: Choose hairstyles that add height to the top of your head, such as a high ponytail or topknot.
  • Square: Soften your features with layered hairstyles or side-swept bangs.
  • Heart: Balance your features with hairstyles that add volume at the chin, such as a chin-length bob.
  • Diamond: Soften your cheekbones with hairstyles that add volume at the sides, such as a side-swept pixie cut.
  • Triangle: Balance your features with hairstyles that add volume at the top of your head, such as a messy bun or textured updo.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if my face shape doesn't fit any of these categories?

While these are the most common face shapes, there are some that don't fit into any of these categories. If you're having trouble determining your face shape, consult with a hairstylist.

Can I change my face shape with makeup?

You can use contouring techniques with makeup to make your face appear more oval or heart-shaped, but you can't actually change your face shape.

Can I wear any hairstyle I want?

While you can wear any hairstyle you want, choosing a hairstyle that flatters your face shape can make a big difference in how you look.

Hair Care for Different Face Shapes

Once you've determined your face shape and chosen a flattering hairstyle, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Oval: Keep your hair healthy with regular trims and deep conditioning treatments.
  • Round: Use volumizing products to add height to your hair, and avoid hairstyles that are too short or too long.
  • Square: Keep your hair long and layered, and avoid blunt cuts that can make your features appear harsh.
  • Heart: Keep your hair chin-length or longer, and add volume at the chin with layers or curls.
  • Diamond: Avoid hairstyles that add volume at the sides, and keep your hair short or medium length.
  • Triangle: Keep your hair long and layered, and add volume at the top of your head with teasing or volumizing products.

By following these tips, you can choose the perfect hairstyle for your face shape and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
