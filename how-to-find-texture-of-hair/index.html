How To Find Texture Of Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Oct 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Understanding your hair texture is essential to maintaining healthy and beautiful hair. However, it can be challenging to determine your hair's texture, especially if you have relaxed hair. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of finding your hair texture, including how to identify different hair types, and provide you with tips on how to care for your hair based on its texture.

Table of Contents

  • What is Hair Texture?
  • How to Find Hair Texture
  • Hair Texture Types
  • Hair Care Tips Based on Texture
  • Frequently Asked Questions

What is Hair Texture?

Hair texture refers to the thickness or diameter of individual hair strands. It is an essential characteristic of hair that can affect how your hair looks, feels, and behaves. There are three primary hair texture types: fine, medium, and coarse.

How to Find Hair Texture

There are a few ways to determine your hair texture:

Visual Test

The easiest way to determine your hair texture is to visually inspect your hair strands. Take a single strand of hair and hold it up to the light. If the strand is barely visible or nearly transparent, you have fine hair. If the strand is visible and has some texture to it, you have medium hair. If the strand is thick and easily visible, you have coarse hair.

Touch Test

You can also determine your hair texture by feeling your hair strands. Take a single strand of hair and run it through your fingers. If the strand feels smooth and barely there, you have fine hair. If the strand feels rougher and thicker, you have medium hair. If the strand feels thick and wiry, you have coarse hair.

Curl Pattern Test

Another way to determine your hair texture is by examining your curl pattern. If you have tight, defined curls, you likely have coarse hair. Loose, undefined curls or waves indicate medium hair. Fine hair typically has little to no curl pattern.

Hair Texture Types

As mentioned earlier, there are three primary hair texture types:

Fine Hair

Fine hair has a small diameter and can be easily damaged. It is often oily at the roots and can appear flat and lifeless. If you have fine hair, you may want to avoid heavy hair products and consider using volumizing shampoos and conditioners to add body.

Medium Hair

Medium hair has a slightly larger diameter than fine hair and is more resilient. It can hold styles well and can have a natural shine. If you have medium hair, you can use a wide range of hair products, but you may want to avoid products that are too heavy or too light.

Coarse Hair

Coarse hair has the largest diameter and is the most resilient. It can be difficult to style and can appear dry and dull. If you have coarse hair, you may want to use deep conditioning treatments and avoid heat styling tools that can further damage your hair.

Hair Care Tips Based on Texture

Here are some hair care tips based on your hair texture:

Fine Hair Care Tips

  • Use lightweight shampoos and conditioners to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products like waxes and gels.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as they can damage your hair.

Medium Hair Care Tips

  • Use a variety of hair products, including mousses, gels, and hairsprays.
  • Avoid using too much product that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant before using heat styling tools.

Coarse Hair Care Tips

  • Use deep conditioning treatments to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Use heavier hair products like oils and creams.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can hair texture change over time?

A: Yes, hair texture can change over time due to factors like aging, hormonal changes, and hair treatments.

Q: Can I have more than one hair texture?

A: Yes, it is common to have different hair textures in different parts of your head. For example, you may have fine hair at the crown of your head and coarse hair at the nape of your neck.

Q: Can I change my hair texture?

A: No, you cannot change your hair texture permanently. However, you can use hair treatments and styling techniques to temporarily alter the appearance of your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair texture and personal preference. Fine hair may need to be washed more frequently, while coarse hair can go longer between washes.

By following these tips, you can determine your hair texture and learn how to care for your hair accordingly. Remember, everyone's hair is unique, so it's crucial to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you.
