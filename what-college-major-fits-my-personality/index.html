What College Major Fits My Personality

Written by Christine Sep 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're like most people, you want to choose a college major that fits your personality. After all, you'll be spending a lot of time studying and working in your chosen field, so it's important to find something that you love and that motivates you. But with so many options to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide which major is right for you. In this article, we'll explore some tips and strategies to help you find the perfect fit.

Table of Contents:

  • Personality Assessment
  • Interests and Passions
  • Career Prospects
  • Advice from Experts
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Personality Assessment

One of the first steps you can take to find a major that fits your personality is to take a personality assessment. There are many different types of assessments available, but some of the most popular ones include the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator, the Big Five Personality Traits, and the Enneagram. These assessments can help you identify your strengths, weaknesses, and preferences, which can then be used to guide your major selection.

For example, if you're an extroverted person who enjoys being around others, you might consider a major in communications, marketing, or psychology. On the other hand, if you're introverted and enjoy working independently, you might consider a major in computer science, mathematics, or engineering.

Interests and Passions

Another important factor to consider when choosing a major is your interests and passions. Think about the activities that you enjoy doing in your spare time or the topics that you find yourself reading about or researching. These can be strong indicators of the type of major that would be a good fit for you.

For example, if you love animals and are passionate about animal welfare, you might consider a major in biology, veterinary science, or environmental science. If you're interested in politics and social justice, you might consider a major in sociology, political science, or anthropology.

Career Prospects

While it's important to choose a major that you love and that fits your personality, it's also important to consider the career prospects for that major. After all, you want to choose a major that will lead to a fulfilling and rewarding career.

Research the different career paths that are available for graduates in your chosen major, and consider the job outlook and earning potential in that field. You can also talk to professionals in the field or do informational interviews to get a better sense of what the day-to-day work is like in that career.

Advice from Experts

Finally, don't be afraid to seek out advice from experts in the field. Talk to your academic advisor, professors, and career counselors to get their input on which majors might be a good fit for your personality and interests. You can also attend career fairs, networking events, and other industry events to learn more about different majors and career paths.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I'm still not sure which major is right for me?

If you're still struggling to choose a major, consider taking some classes in different subjects to see which ones you enjoy the most. You can also talk to upperclassmen, alumni, and professionals in different fields to get their perspective on which majors might be a good fit for your personality and interests.

What if I choose the wrong major?

It's not uncommon for students to change their major at some point during their college career. If you find that you're unhappy with your chosen major, talk to your academic advisor about your options for switching to a different major or exploring different career paths.

What if I don't have a clear idea of my interests or passions?

If you're not sure what you're passionate about, consider taking some time to explore different hobbies, activities, and subjects. You can also talk to a career counselor or take a personality assessment to get a better sense of your strengths and preferences.

Conclusion

Choosing a college major that fits your personality can be a challenging process, but by taking a thoughtful and strategic approach, you can find a major that is both fulfilling and rewarding. Remember to consider your personality, interests, and career prospects when making your decision, and don't be afraid to seek out advice from experts in the field.
