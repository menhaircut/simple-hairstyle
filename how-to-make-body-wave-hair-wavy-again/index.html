How To Make Body Wave Hair Wavy Again A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your body wave hair losing its bounce and becoming flat? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll show you how to make body wave hair wavy again in just a few simple steps. From using the right products to adopting the right techniques, we'll guide you through everything you need to know to get your hair back to its wavy, bouncy self.

Description

Body wave hair is a popular hairstyle that gives your hair texture and volume. However, over time, your hair may lose its wave and become flat. This can be frustrating, but there are ways to bring back the bounce in your hair. With the right products and techniques, you can make your body wave hair wavy again without having to spend hours at the salon.

How to Make Body Wave Hair Wavy Again

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in making your body wave hair wavy again is to wash it with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup and prepare your hair for the next steps. After washing, apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair to add moisture and nourishment.

Step 2: Apply a Wave Enhancing Product

Once you've washed and conditioned your hair, it's time to apply a wave enhancing product. Look for a product that's specifically designed to enhance waves and curls. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, using your fingers to work it through from root to tip.

Step 3: Scrunch Your Hair

After applying the wave enhancing product, scrunch your hair to create waves. Use your fingers to push your hair up towards your scalp, scrunching as you go. This will help to create volume and texture in your hair.

Step 4: Diffuse Your Hair

Next, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair. This will help to set the waves and create volume. Hold the diffuser close to your hair and move it around in circular motions, focusing on the roots and mid-lengths of your hair.

Step 5: Finish with a Texturizing Spray

Once your hair is dry, finish with a texturizing spray to add definition and hold to your waves. Spray the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair again, creating more waves and texture.

Tips

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before washing it.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and make it harder to hold waves.
  • Consider getting a trim to remove any split ends, which can make your hair look flat and lifeless.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my body wave hair?

A: It's recommended that you wash your body wave hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from becoming too dry or damaged.

Q: Can I make my body wave hair wavy again without using heat?

A: Yes, you can make your body wave hair wavy again without using heat. Follow the steps outlined in this article, using a wave enhancing product and a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to create waves.

Hair Care

To keep your body wave hair looking wavy and bouncy, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from becoming too dry. Additionally, avoid using hot tools and opt for air-drying or diffusing your hair instead. Finally, get regular trims to remove split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and full of life.
