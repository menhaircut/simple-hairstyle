How To Do Dreadlocks With Short Hair At Home

Written by Oliver Jul 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your short hair and want to try something new and exciting? Dreadlocks might be the perfect hairstyle for you! While many people associate dreadlocks with long hair, it is possible to achieve this look even if you have short hair. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to do dreadlocks with short hair at home. We will also include some tips and FAQs to help you achieve the best results possible.

Step-by-Step Guide

Before we dive into the steps, make sure you have the following items:

  • Dread wax or gel
  • A comb with small teeth
  • Rubber bands
  • A spray bottle with water

Now, letâs get started:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

This step is essential to remove any dirt or oil from your hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to get rid of any product buildup. Rinse your hair thoroughly and let it air dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to divide your hair into small sections. The sections should be about the same size as the dreadlocks you want to achieve. Secure each section with a rubber band.

Step 3: Apply Dread Wax or Gel

Take a small amount of dread wax or gel and rub it between your palms. Apply it to each section of your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots, use the comb to backcomb each section of hair. This will create knots and tangles that will eventually form into dreadlocks. Be gentle and donât pull too hard to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 5: Roll Your Hair

Once you have backcombed each section, use your palms to roll them back and forth. This will further tighten the knots and help the dreadlocks form.

Step 6: Secure Your Dreadlocks

Remove the rubber bands from each section and twist the hair in one direction. Use another rubber band to secure the dreadlock at the root. Repeat this process for each section.

Step 7: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Now that you have your dreadlocks, it's important to maintain them. Use a spray bottle to mist your hair with water every day. This will keep your dreadlocks hydrated and prevent them from drying out. You can also apply a small amount of dread wax or gel to the roots to keep them tight.

Tips for Achieving the Perfect Dreadlocks

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Be patient - it may take a few months for your dreadlocks to fully form.
  • Don't wash your hair too often - this can cause your dreadlocks to unravel.
  • Use a satin pillowcase - this will prevent your hair from frizzing while you sleep.
  • Avoid using too much product - this can weigh down your dreadlocks and make them look greasy.

FAQs About Dreadlocks with Short Hair

Can you get dreadlocks with short hair?

Yes, you can! While it may take longer to achieve than with long hair, it is still possible to create dreadlocks with short hair.

How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for your dreadlocks to fully form. It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain them.

Do you need to use special shampoo for dreadlocks?

It's recommended to use a shampoo specifically designed for dreadlocks, as regular shampoo can leave residue that can cause your dreadlocks to unravel. Look for a residue-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Dreadlocks

To keep your dreadlocks looking their best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons - this can damage your hair and make it difficult to maintain your dreadlocks.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf to protect your hair while you sleep.
  • Avoid swimming in chlorinated water - this can cause your hair to dry out and become brittle.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a month to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

With these tips and steps, you can achieve the perfect dreadlocks with short hair at home. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair to keep your dreadlocks looking their best. Have fun and enjoy your new look!
