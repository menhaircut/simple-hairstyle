How To Make Curly Hair Straight For Black Guys A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a black guy with curly hair who wants to achieve a straighter look? You're not alone. Many men with curly hair struggle to find the right techniques and products to achieve the straight hair they desire. In this guide, we'll provide you with a step-by-step process on how to make curly hair straight for black guys. Let's get started!

How to Make Curly Hair Straight for Black Guys: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with a Clean Scalp

The first step in achieving straight hair is to start with a clean scalp. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid any leftover product that could weigh down your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Before using any heating tools on your hair, it's important to apply a heat protectant to prevent damage. Look for a product that is specifically designed for curly hair to ensure the best results. Apply the heat protectant evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a blow dryer with a concentrator attachment, start drying your hair. Make sure to use a round brush to help smooth out your hair and achieve a straighter look. Work in small sections, starting from the back and moving forward. Use the low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 4: Straighten Your Hair

Once your hair is completely dry, it's time to straighten it. Use a flat iron with adjustable heat settings to ensure that you're not using too much heat. Start from the back and work your way forward. Use a comb to help guide the flat iron through your hair, and make sure to go over each section multiple times to ensure that it's completely straight.

Step 5: Apply a Serum

To finish off your straightened look, apply a serum to add shine and reduce frizz. Look for a serum that is specifically designed for curly hair to ensure that it doesn't weigh down your hair. Apply the serum evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Tips for Making Curly Hair Straight for Black Guys

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the straight hair you desire:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before blow drying.
  • Make sure to use a flat iron with ceramic plates to prevent damage.
  • Don't use too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Avoid using heating tools on a daily basis to prevent damage.
  • Consider getting a keratin treatment to help make your hair more manageable.

FAQs About Making Curly Hair Straight for Black Guys

Q: Can I use a relaxer to straighten my hair?

A: While a relaxer can be used to straighten hair, it's important to note that it can be damaging to your hair and scalp. If you choose to use a relaxer, make sure to follow the instructions carefully and avoid leaving it on for too long.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It's best to avoid straightening your hair on a daily basis to prevent damage. Aim to straighten your hair no more than once a week, and use a heat protectant each time.

Q: Can I achieve a straight look without using heating tools?

A: While heating tools are the most effective way to achieve a straight look, there are other methods you can try. Consider using a blow dryer with a round brush to help smooth out your hair, or try a keratin treatment to make your hair more manageable.

Hair Care for Black Guys with Curly Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Wash your hair no more than once or twice a week to avoid drying it out.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to help hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid using a brush.

By following these tips and using the right techniques and products, you can achieve the straight hair you desire. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid using too much heat to prevent damage. Good luck!
