How To Cut Your Own Long Hair At The Back

Written by Oliver Sep 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon for a simple trim? Don't worry, you can easily cut your own long hair at the back with a few simple steps. In this article, we'll guide you through the process and provide some tips for maintaining your hair post-cut.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially when it comes to the back. However, with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. This article will provide step-by-step instructions to help you confidently cut your own long hair at the back.

How to Cut Your Own Long Hair at the Back

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. It's also helpful to have a spray bottle of water to keep your hair damp while cutting.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before starting the cut. This will help you see your hair's natural texture and prevent any surprises after the cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into two equal parts by creating a part from ear to ear. Clip the top section of hair out of the way for now.

Step 4: Determine Your Desired Length

Stand in front of a mirror with the handheld mirror behind your head to see the back of your hair. Use the comb to guide your hair to the desired length and make a small snip. It's better to start with a small cut and work your way up to your desired length.

Step 5: Cut in Small Sections

Cut your hair in small sections, working from the bottom up. Make sure to cut straight across, and avoid cutting at an angle. Continuously check the length in the handheld mirror to ensure you're achieving your desired length.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

After you've cut the bottom section, unclip the top section and blend the layers by cutting at a slight angle. This will create a natural-looking, layered effect.

Step 7: Check for Any Unevenness

Once you've finished the cut, check for any unevenness. Use the scissors to make small adjustments as needed.

Tips

  • Start with a small cut and work your way up to your desired length. It's better to take your time and be cautious than to cut too much off at once.
  • Use a comb to guide your hair while cutting, and cut straight across rather than at an angle.
  • Check your hair's length frequently in the handheld mirror to ensure you're achieving your desired look.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient while cutting your hair.

FAQ

Is it hard to cut your own hair?

With the right tools and techniques, cutting your own hair can be easy and even enjoyable. It may take some practice, but it's definitely doable.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, try not to cut too much more off. It's always better to take your time and make small adjustments. If you're really unhappy with the result, you can always visit a salon for a professional fix.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and nourished. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair. A regular trim every 6-8 weeks can help maintain your hair's shape and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own long hair at the back may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Just take your time and be patient, and you'll have a beautiful cut in no time!
