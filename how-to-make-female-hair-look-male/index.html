How To Make Female Hair Look Male A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to make your female hair look more masculine, you've come to the right place. Whether you're dressing up for a costume party or simply want to change up your look for a while, there are a number of ways to achieve a more masculine hairdo. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of creating a male hairstyle from your female hair, step by step.

Description

When it comes to making female hair look male, the key is to focus on creating a shorter, more angular cut. Men's hair tends to be shorter and more textured than women's hair, so you'll need to take this into consideration when choosing a style. Depending on the length of your hair, you may need to cut it quite short to achieve a masculine look. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can create a style that is both flattering and masculine.

How to Make Female Hair Look Male

Step 1: Choose a Style

The first step in making female hair look male is to choose a style that suits your face shape and features. Some popular male hairstyles include the buzz cut, crew cut, and undercut. If you're not sure which style to choose, consider consulting with a hairstylist who specializes in men's haircuts.

Step 2: Gather Your Tools

Once you've chosen a style, you'll need to gather the tools you'll need to create it. This may include scissors, clippers, trimmers, a comb, and styling products such as gel or pomade.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, it's important to wash and dry it thoroughly. This will help to remove any dirt and oil from your hair, making it easier to cut and style. Once your hair is clean, use a towel to blot it dry.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. If you're using scissors, begin by cutting the hair at the nape of your neck. Work your way up to the crown of your head, cutting the hair in small sections. If you're using clippers, start by using a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard as you move up the sides and back of your head.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to style it. Use a comb and styling product to create the look you're going for. You may want to experiment with different products and techniques to find the best look for you.

Tips

When making female hair look male, it's important to take your face shape and features into consideration. A style that looks great on one person may not be as flattering on another. Be sure to choose a style that highlights your best features and complements your face shape.

It's also important to take good care of your hair after you've cut it. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated, and avoid using heat styling tools too frequently.

FAQ

Q: Can I make my long hair look masculine?
A: Yes, you can. However, you may need to cut it quite short to achieve a truly masculine look.

Q: Do I need to go to a salon to make my hair look masculine?
A: No, you can do it yourself at home with the right tools and techniques. However, if you're not confident in your abilities, it may be a good idea to visit a hairstylist who specializes in men's haircuts.

Hair Care

After you've made your female hair look male, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and moisturized, and avoid using heat styling tools too frequently. You may also want to consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to give your hair an extra boost of hydration.

With these tips and techniques, you can create a masculine hairstyle from your female hair that looks great and suits your style. Whether you're looking to change up your look for a while or simply want to experiment with different styles, there are plenty of options to choose from. So go ahead and give it a try!
