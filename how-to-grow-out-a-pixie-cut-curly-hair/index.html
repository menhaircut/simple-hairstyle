How To Grow Out A Pixie Cut Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of your pixie cut and want to grow your curly hair out? Growing out a pixie cut can be a daunting task, especially if you have curly hair. But don't worry, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to grow out a pixie cut curly hair.

How to Grow Out a Pixie Cut Curly Hair

Growing out a pixie cut when you have curly hair can be a challenge, but it's not impossible. Here are some tips on how to grow out a pixie cut curly hair:

1. Be Patient

Growing out a pixie cut takes time, especially if you have curly hair. It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year before you start to see significant growth. So, be patient and don't get discouraged if it takes longer than you expected.

2. Get Regular Trims

While growing out your hair, it's important to get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage. Aim for a trim every 6 to 8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and promote growth.

3. Use Hair Accessories

When your hair is in the awkward stage of growing out, hair accessories can be your best friend. Headbands, hair clips, and hair ties can help keep your hair out of your face and make the growing out process a little more bearable.

4. Experiment with Styles

Just because your hair is growing out doesn't mean you can't have fun with it. Experiment with different styles, like braids, twists, and updos, to keep your hair looking fresh and stylish while it grows out.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Decide on Your Desired Length

Before you start growing out your pixie cut, decide on your desired length. This will help you set goals and stay motivated throughout the growing out process.

Step 2: Get Regular Trims

As mentioned earlier, getting regular trims is essential to promoting healthy hair growth. Make sure to schedule appointments every 6 to 8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Step 3: Experiment with Styles

Try out different styles, like braids, twists, and updos, to keep your hair looking stylish and fresh while it grows out. You can also consult with a hairstylist to find styles that work best for your hair type and face shape.

Step 4: Be Patient

Growing out a pixie cut takes time, so be patient and don't get discouraged if it takes longer than expected. Remember, healthy hair growth is a gradual process.

Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut Curly Hair

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Curly hair can be prone to tangles, especially when growing out a pixie cut. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair and prevent breakage.

2. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioners can help keep your hair hydrated and prevent breakage. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing and conditioning to keep it healthy and strong.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be damaging to your hair, especially when growing it out. Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, and opt for heatless styling techniques like air-drying and braiding.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals to keep your hair strong and healthy.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to grow out a pixie cut?

A: It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year to grow out a pixie cut, depending on your hair type and how fast it grows.

Q: How often should I trim my hair while growing out a pixie cut?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6 to 8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Q: Can I still style my hair while growing out a pixie cut?

A: Yes, you can still style your hair while growing out a pixie cut. Experiment with different styles, like braids, twists, and updos, to keep your hair looking stylish and fresh.

Hair Care Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut Curly Hair

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair can be prone to dryness, so use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture to your hair and prevent breakage. Aim to deep condition your hair at least once a week.

3. Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Satin pillowcases are gentle on your hair and can help prevent breakage while you sleep. Swap out your cotton pillowcase for a satin one to keep your hair healthy and strong.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles can be damaging to your hair, especially when growing it out. Avoid styles like tight braids and ponytails, and opt for looser styles that won't put too much tension on your hair.

With these tips and tricks, growing out a pixie cut curly hair doesn't have to be a daunting task. Remember to be patient, get regular trims, and experiment with styles to keep your hair looking stylish and healthy as it grows out.
