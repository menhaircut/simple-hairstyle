Best Haircut For Oblong Face Female Flatter Your Features With The Right Cut

If you have an oblong face, you may have noticed that certain hairstyles just don't seem to suit you. But fear not, as there are plenty of fabulous haircuts that can flatter your features and make the most of your unique face shape. In this article, we'll explore the best haircut for oblong face female and provide you with some tips on how to keep your tresses looking their best.

What is an Oblong Face Shape?

An oblong face shape is longer than it is wide, with a high forehead and a narrow chin. This face shape is sometimes called a rectangular face shape or a long face shape. If you have an oblong face, you may also have a slightly angular jawline and prominent cheekbones.

When choosing a haircut for an oblong face, the goal is to create the illusion of width and balance out the length of the face. This can be achieved by adding volume and texture to the sides of the head and keeping the length of the hair around the chin or collarbone.

Best Haircuts for Oblong Face Female

Now that we know what an oblong face shape is, let's explore some of the best haircuts for oblong face female:

1. Long Layers

Long layers are a great option for oblong faces, as they add volume and movement to the hair. The layers should start around the collarbone or chest area, and the ends of the hair should be slightly curled or flipped outwards to create the illusion of width.

2. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs are a flattering choice for oblong faces, as they help to shorten the length of the face and draw attention to the eyes. The bangs should be cut diagonally and swept to one side, with the longest point hitting just above the eyebrows.

3. Bob

A bob is a classic haircut that can work well with an oblong face shape. The key is to keep the length of the hair at or above the chin, and to add some texture and volume to the sides of the head. A layered bob or an asymmetrical bob can also be a great option.

4. Shag

A shag haircut is another great option for oblong faces, as it adds volume and movement to the hair. The layers should start around the cheekbones or jawline, and the ends of the hair should be slightly curled or tousled to create a messy, effortless look.

How to Style Your Oblong Face Haircut

Once you've chosen the perfect haircut for your oblong face, it's important to know how to style it to flatter your features. Here are some tips:

1. Add Volume

To create the illusion of width, it's important to add volume to the sides of your head. You can do this by blow-drying your hair with a round brush, or by using a volumizing spray or mousse.

2. Curl or Wave Your Hair

Adding curls or waves to your hair can also help to create the illusion of width. You can use a curling iron or a flat iron to create loose, beachy waves, or you can opt for a more defined curl using hot rollers or a perm.

3. Avoid Flat Styles

Flat, straight styles can make an oblong face shape look even longer. Instead, opt for textured styles that add volume and movement to the hair.

Tips for Oblong Face Haircare

Here are some additional tips for caring for oblong face hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and damage.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

If you use heated styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Aim to deep condition your hair once a week.

FAQ

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have an oblong face?

Avoid hairstyles that add height to the top of your head, such as slicked-back ponytails or high buns. These styles can make your face look even longer.

Can I wear my hair straight if I have an oblong face?

Yes, you can wear your hair straight if you have an oblong face. Just be sure to add some volume and texture to the sides of your head to create balance.

Should I get a haircut that's all one length if I have an oblong face?

No, it's best to avoid haircuts that are all one length if you have an oblong face. This can make your face look even longer. Instead, opt for layered styles that add volume and texture to the sides of your head.

With the right haircut and styling techniques, an oblong face can be just as beautiful as any other face shape. Whether you prefer long layers, side-swept bangs, or a shag haircut, there's a style out there that will flatter your features and make you feel confident and beautiful.
