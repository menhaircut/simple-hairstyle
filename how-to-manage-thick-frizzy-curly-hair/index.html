How To Manage Thick Frizzy Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Aug 27, 2023 · 5 min read

Thick frizzy curly hair is notoriously difficult to manage. However, with the right techniques and products, you can achieve beautiful and healthy curls. In this article, we will provide you with tips and steps on how to manage thick frizzy curly hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Thick frizzy curly hair is characterized by its coarse texture, high volume, and tendency to frizz. It can be challenging to manage because it requires a lot of moisture and hydration to maintain its shape and definition. However, with the right hair care routine, you can achieve beautiful and healthy curls that are manageable and easy to style.

How to

The first step in managing thick frizzy curly hair is to find the right products that work for your hair type. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair and contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil. These ingredients will provide your hair with the moisture and hydration it needs to maintain its shape and definition.

Shampoo and Conditioner

When washing your hair, use a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy. Follow up with a conditioner that is rich in moisture and proteins to help strengthen your hair and prevent breakage.

Deep Conditioning

Deep conditioning your hair once a week is essential to maintaining healthy and hydrated curls. Look for a deep conditioner that contains ingredients like avocado oil, honey, and aloe vera, which will provide your hair with the moisture it needs to stay soft and manageable.

Styling Products

When styling your hair, use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl enhancers, gels, and creams. These products will help define your curls and reduce frizz. Apply the products to damp hair and use a diffuser to dry your hair gently, avoiding the use of a hairdryer as much as possible.

Step by Step

Here are the steps you can follow to manage your thick frizzy curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and follow up with a moisturizing conditioner.
  2. Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  3. Apply curl-enhancing products, such as gels or creams, to damp hair.
  4. Gently dry your hair with a diffuser to reduce frizz.
  5. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  6. Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair.
  7. Sleep with a satin pillowcase or wrap your hair in a silk scarf to prevent frizz.
  8. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.
  9. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair serum with SPF.
  10. Stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to promote healthy hair growth.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you manage your thick frizzy curly hair:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel, which can cause frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair before styling to provide extra moisture and hydration.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, such as braids, buns, and twists, to find what works best for your hair.

FAQ

How often should I wash my thick frizzy curly hair?

You should wash your hair once or twice a week, depending on your hair type and how quickly it gets oily. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy.

Can I use a hair straightener on my thick frizzy curly hair?

While you can use a hair straightener on your curly hair, it's best to avoid it as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair. Instead, try heatless styling techniques, such as braids or buns, to achieve different hairstyles.

What should I do if my hair is still frizzy after styling?

If your hair is still frizzy after styling, try using a small amount of hair oil or serum to smooth down any flyaways. You can also use a boar bristle brush to gently brush your hair and distribute the natural oils throughout your hair.

Hair Care

Managing thick frizzy curly hair requires a lot of care and attention. However, with the right hair care routine, you can achieve beautiful and healthy curls that are easy to manage and style. Remember to use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, deep condition your hair once a week, and trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth. Stay hydrated, eat a healthy diet, and protect your hair from the sun to maintain its health and beauty.
