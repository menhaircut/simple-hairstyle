How To Make Easy Bun Hairstyles For Long Hair

Are you tired of wearing your hair down all the time? Do you want to switch up your look with an easy and chic hairstyle? Look no further than the classic bun! Here are some easy ways to create bun hairstyles for long hair.

Table of Contents

  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How To

The bun hairstyle is a classic and versatile look that can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. To make an easy bun hairstyle for long hair, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Elastic hair bands
  • Bobby pins
  • Hairbrush
  • Hairspray

Step 1: Brush Your Hair

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to create a smooth and polished bun hairstyle.

Step 2: Tie Your Hair Into a Ponytail

Gather your hair into a high or low ponytail, depending on where you want the bun to sit. Secure the ponytail with an elastic hair band.

Step 3: Twist and Wrap Your Hair

Take the ponytail and twist it into a tight rope. Then, wrap the twisted hair around the base of the ponytail, tucking the ends underneath. Secure the bun with bobby pins.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

Use hairspray to smooth any flyaways and hold the bun in place. You can also pull out a few strands of hair around your face to create a softer look.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to creating different types of bun hairstyles for long hair:

Low Bun

  1. Part your hair down the middle and brush it back into a low ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail into a tight rope and wrap it around the base of the ponytail.
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins and hairspray.

Messy Bun

  1. Start with a low ponytail and tease the hair at the crown of your head to create volume.
  2. Twist the ponytail into a loose rope and wrap it around the base of the ponytail.
  3. Pull out a few strands of hair to create a messy, undone look.
  4. Secure the bun with bobby pins and hairspray.

Braided Bun

  1. Create a Dutch braid on one side of your head and secure it with an elastic hair band.
  2. Gather the rest of your hair into a low ponytail and twist it into a bun.
  3. Wrap the Dutch braid around the bun and secure it with bobby pins.
  4. Finish with hairspray.

Tips

  • If you have fine hair, try using a hair donut to create a fuller-looking bun.
  • For a sleeker look, use a comb to smooth down any flyaways before securing the bun.
  • Experiment with different bun placements, such as a side bun or a high bun, to find the most flattering style for your face shape.

FAQ

Q: Can I make a bun hairstyle with short hair?

A: It may be more difficult to create a bun with short hair, but you can try using hair extensions or a hairpiece to add length and volume to your hair.

Q: How can I make my bun last all day?

A: Use plenty of hairspray to hold the bun in place, and consider using bobby pins or hair sticks to secure it more firmly.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and strong, make sure to follow a regular hair care routine that includes shampooing, conditioning, and regular trims to prevent split ends.

When styling your hair, be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, which can cause breakage and damage. Use heat protectant products when using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners, and try to limit heat styling as much as possible.
