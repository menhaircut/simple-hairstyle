Description

Short layers in medium hair are a great way to add volume, texture, and movement to your hair. Cutting short layers can also give your hair a fresh new look without having to cut too much length. However, it's important to know the right technique and tools to use when cutting short layers in medium hair. In this article, we will guide you step by step on how to cut short layers in medium hair.

How to Cut Short Layers in Medium Hair

The first thing you need to do is gather all the necessary tools. You will need hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's important to use hair cutting scissors as they are sharper and can give you a cleaner cut. Once you have all your tools ready, you can start cutting your short layers.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing your hair and letting it dry completely. It's easier to cut hair when it's dry as wet hair tends to stretch and can give you an inaccurate cut.

Step 2: Section your hair into four parts using hair clips. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then from the top of your ears to the back of your head. This will create four sections - two in the front and two in the back.

Step 3: Start with the back sections first. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle finger and cut it at an angle. The angle should be towards your face, so the layers blend in well. Cut the hair in small sections, so you have better control over the length.

Step 4: Move on to the front sections. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle finger and cut it at an angle. The angle should be towards your face, so the layers blend in well. Cut the hair in small sections, so you have better control over the length.

Step 5: Once you have cut all the sections, you can check to see if they are even. Comb your hair straight down and check for any uneven spots. If you notice any uneven spots, you can trim them with your scissors.

Tips for Cutting Short Layers in Medium Hair

Tip 1: Start by cutting small sections of hair. It's easier to control the length when you cut small sections at a time.

Tip 2: Always cut at an angle towards your face. This will help the layers blend in well.

Tip 3: Use sharp hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can give you an uneven cut.

Tip 4: Don't cut too much length. Cutting short layers is a great way to add volume and texture to your hair without having to cut too much length.

FAQs

Q1: Can I cut short layers in curly hair?

A1: Yes, you can cut short layers in curly hair. However, it's important to cut the layers when your hair is dry, so you can see the curl pattern and avoid cutting too much length.

Q2: How often should I cut my short layers?

A2: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain your hair. On average, you should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your short layers.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Layers in Medium Hair

After cutting your short layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the volume and texture. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add more volume to your hair. Avoid using too much heat on your hair as it can damage your hair and cause split ends. You can also use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

Cutting short layers in medium hair is a great way to add volume and texture to your hair without having to cut too much length. With the right technique and tools, you can achieve a fresh new look that complements your face shape and hair type. Follow our step by step guide and tips to cut your short layers at home and maintain your hair with proper care.
