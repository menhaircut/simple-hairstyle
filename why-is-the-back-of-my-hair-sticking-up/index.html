Why Is The Back Of My Hair Sticking Up Tips And Tricks To Tame Flyaway Hair

Written by Aprilia Nov 17, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of dealing with flyaway hair that won't stay in place? Do you find yourself constantly smoothing down the back of your hair? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with hair that sticks up in the back, and it can be frustrating to deal with. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why the back of your hair might be sticking up and provide tips and tricks to help you tame those flyaways.

Description

The back of the hair sticking up is a common issue among people with different hair types, lengths and textures. It can be caused by a variety of factors, from dryness and breakage to static and humidity. Whatever the reason, it can be challenging to deal with, especially if you're trying to achieve a sleek, polished look. Fortunately, there are several ways to address this issue and get your hair looking smooth and flyaway-free.

How to Tame Flyaway Hair

If you're dealing with hair that sticks up in the back, there are a few things you can do to tame those flyaways. Here are some tips to try:

1. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Dryness is a common cause of flyaway hair. If your hair is dry and brittle, it's more likely to stick up and be difficult to manage. Using a leave-in conditioner can help add moisture and hydration to your hair, making it smoother and more manageable. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to the back of your hair, focusing on the ends, and then comb it through gently.

2. Apply Hair Oil

Hair oils are a great way to add shine and smoothness to your hair. They can also help control frizz and flyaways. Apply a small amount of hair oil to the back of your hair, focusing on the ends, and then comb it through gently. Avoid applying too much, as this can make your hair look greasy.

3. Use a Smoothing Cream

If you're dealing with particularly stubborn flyaways, a smoothing cream can help. Apply a small amount of smoothing cream to the back of your hair, focusing on the areas where your hair tends to stick up. Then, use a comb or your fingers to smooth the hair down.

Step by Step Guide to Taming Flyaway Hair

1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair as usual. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly, as any leftover product can contribute to flyaways.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to the back of your hair, focusing on the ends, and then comb it through gently.

3. Apply a small amount of hair oil to the back of your hair, focusing on the ends, and then comb it through gently.

4. Apply a small amount of smoothing cream to the back of your hair, focusing on the areas where your hair tends to stick up. Then, use a comb or your fingers to smooth the hair down.

5. If you're using heat styling tools, make sure to apply a heat protectant spray to your hair before styling. This will help prevent damage and breakage, which can contribute to flyaway hair.

Tips for Preventing Flyaway Hair

Prevention is key when it comes to flyaway hair. Here are some tips to help prevent your hair from sticking up in the first place:

1. Use a Humidifier

Dry air can contribute to flyaway hair, especially in the winter months. Using a humidifier can help add moisture to the air, which can in turn help keep your hair hydrated and less prone to flyaways.

2. Avoid Over-Styling Your Hair

Using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals can damage your hair and make it more prone to flyaways. Try to avoid over-styling your hair and opt for more natural hairstyles instead.

3. Protect Your Hair While Sleeping

Sleeping on a cotton pillowcase can cause friction and breakage, which can contribute to flyaway hair. Try sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase instead, and consider wrapping your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet to protect it while you sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why is the back of my hair sticking up?

A: There are several reasons why the back of your hair might be sticking up, including dryness, breakage, static, and humidity.

Q: Can I use hairspray to tame flyaway hair?

A: Yes, hairspray can help tame flyaway hair. However, be sure to choose a hairspray that is lightweight and won't weigh your hair down.

Q: Can I use a hair mask to help prevent flyaway hair?

A: Yes, using a hair mask once a week can help add moisture and hydration to your hair, which can in turn help prevent flyaway hair.

Hair Care Tips for Flyaway Hair

When it comes to dealing with flyaway hair, proper hair care is key. Here are some tips to help keep your hair healthy and flyaway-free:

1. Avoid Using Harsh Chemicals

Harsh chemicals can damage your hair and make it more prone to flyaways. Try to avoid using chemical treatments like relaxers, and opt for more natural hair care products instead.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can contribute to flyaway hair. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb can help prevent breakage and damage to your hair. Avoid using a brush, as this can cause friction and make your hair more prone to flyaways.

In conclusion, dealing with flyaway hair can be frustrating, but there are several ways to tame those flyaways and get your hair looking smooth and polished. By using the tips and tricks outlined in this article, you can say goodbye to sticking up hair and hello to a sleek, flyaway-free mane.
