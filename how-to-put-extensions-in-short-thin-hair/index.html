How To Put Extensions In Short Thin Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of having short and thin hair? Do you want to add volume and length to your hair without waiting for it to grow naturally? If so, hair extensions might be the perfect solution for you. However, putting extensions in short thin hair can be a bit tricky. In this article, we will guide you through the process of putting extensions in short thin hair step by step. We will also provide you with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you achieve the best results.

How to Put Extensions in Short Thin Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's first discuss the different types of hair extensions that are suitable for short thin hair:

  • Clip-in extensions: These are the easiest and most convenient type of extensions to use. They come in small wefts with clips attached to them. You can simply clip them onto your hair without any special tools or adhesive.
  • Tape-in extensions: These are thin wefts of hair that are attached to your natural hair with a special tape. They are discreet and can be used in small sections to add volume and length to your hair.
  • Sew-in extensions: These are wefts of hair that are sewn onto your natural hair. They are the most secure type of extensions and can last for several months.

Now that you know the different types of extensions, let's move on to the step-by-step guide.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is how to put extensions in short thin hair:

Step 1: Prepare your hair

Wash and condition your hair before putting in extensions. Make sure your hair is free of tangles and knots. Blow-dry your hair completely before adding extensions.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into small sections using a comb. Clip the top layers of your hair away from the bottom layers.

Step 3: Attach the extensions

Take a weft of hair and position it under the section of hair you want to add it to. Use a clip to secure the weft to your natural hair. Repeat this process for all the sections of hair you want to add extensions to.

Step 4: Blend the extensions

Use a brush or comb to blend the extensions with your natural hair. Make sure there are no visible lines or bumps where the extensions are attached.

Step 5: Style your hair

You can now style your hair as desired. Use a curling iron, straightener, or other styling tools to achieve your desired look.

Tips for Putting Extensions in Short Thin Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when putting extensions in short thin hair:

  • Choose the right type of extensions for your hair. Clip-in extensions are the easiest to use and are suitable for short thin hair.
  • Match the color of the extensions to your natural hair color for a seamless look.
  • Trim the extensions if necessary to match the length of your natural hair.
  • Avoid putting too much tension on your natural hair when attaching the extensions to prevent damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about putting extensions in short thin hair:

Can I put extensions in very short hair?

It is possible to put extensions in very short hair, but you will need to choose the right type of extensions and attach them carefully to avoid any damage to your natural hair.

How long do extensions last?

The lifespan of extensions depends on the type of extensions and how well they are maintained. Clip-in extensions can last for up to a year, while sew-in extensions can last for several months.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Extensions in Short Thin Hair

Here are some tips to help you take care of your extensions and natural hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage to your extensions.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently as they can damage your natural hair and extensions.
  • Brush your hair gently to avoid pulling on the extensions.
  • Remove the extensions before going to bed to prevent tangling.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently put extensions in short thin hair and achieve the look you desire. Remember to take care of your extensions and natural hair to maintain their health and beauty.
