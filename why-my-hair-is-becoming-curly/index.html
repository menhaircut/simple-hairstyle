Why Is My Hair Becoming Curly

Written by Christine Sep 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you are someone who has always had straight hair, you may be surprised to find your hair becoming curly. This can be a result of various factors such as genetics, hormonal changes, external factors, or even hair damage. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind why your hair is becoming curly and how you can take care of your curls to keep them looking their best.

Description

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. Straight hair has a round follicle while curly hair has a more oval-shaped follicle. This shape of the follicle determines the natural curl pattern of the hair.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

There are different kinds of curly hair, ranging from wavy to coily. To determine your hair type, wash and condition your hair and let it air dry without using any styling products. Here are the four main types of curly hair:

1. Type 2: Wavy Hair

Wavy hair has a loose curl pattern and is characterized by an S-shaped wave. It is the least curly of all the curly hair types and is easy to manage.

2. Type 3: Curly Hair

Curly hair has a tighter curl pattern than wavy hair and is characterized by ringlets or corkscrew curls. It can be prone to frizz and requires more maintenance than wavy hair.

3. Type 4: Coily Hair

Coily hair has a very tight curl pattern and is characterized by small, tightly coiled curls. It is the most fragile of all the curly hair types and requires a lot of moisture and care.

4. Type 4A, 4B, and 4C: Afro Hair

Afro hair has a very tight curl pattern and is characterized by tightly coiled curls that form a tight, dense afro. It is the most fragile of all the curly hair types and requires a lot of moisture and care.

Step by Step Guide on How to Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a lot of care to keep it looking its best. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to care for your curly hair:

1. Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that is specifically formulated for curly hair. These products will help to hydrate and define your curls without stripping them of their natural oils.

2. Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while it is still wet. This will help to prevent breakage and damage to your curls.

3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help lock in moisture and define your curls. Use a product that is specifically formulated for your hair type.

4. Style Your Hair

Style your hair using a product that is specifically formulated for your hair type. Use a diffuser to dry your hair to help define your curls.

5. Protect Your Hair While You Sleep

Use a satin or silk pillowcase to protect your hair while you sleep. These materials will help to prevent friction and breakage, which can cause damage to your curls.

Tips for Taking Care of Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your curly hair:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons. These tools can cause damage to your curls and make them harder to manage.

2. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment on your hair once a week to help keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

3. Avoid Brushing Your Hair

Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause breakage and damage to your curls. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage. This will help to keep your curls looking healthy and defined.

FAQ

Q: Why is my hair becoming curly?

A: Your hair may be becoming curly due to various factors such as genetics, hormonal changes, external factors, or even hair damage.

Q: What is the best way to care for curly hair?

A: The best way to care for curly hair is to use products that are specifically formulated for your hair type, detangle your hair while it is wet, and avoid using heat styling tools.

Q: What is the best way to define my curls?

A: The best way to define your curls is to use a leave-in conditioner and a styling product that is specifically formulated for your hair type.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking their best. Remember to use products that are specifically formulated for your hair type, avoid heat styling tools, and detangle your hair while it is wet to prevent breakage and damage. With a little extra care, you can have healthy, defined curls that you'll love.
