How To Thin Thick Hair Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have thick hair and want to try out a bob hairstyle? While a bob haircut looks great on thick hair, it can be overwhelming if your hair is too thick. You may feel like your hair is taking over your face, and it can be hard to manage. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to thin thick hair bob in a few easy steps.

Description

If you have thick hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. It can take a lot of time to style, and it can be hard to find a haircut that suits your hair type. A bob haircut is a great option if you want to try something new, but it can be overwhelming if your hair is too thick. Thinning your hair can make it easier to style and manage, and it can also give you a more flattering look.

How to Thin Thick Hair Bob

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in thinning your hair is to wash it. This will remove any dirt and oil from your hair, making it easier to cut. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type. If you have thick hair, look for a shampoo and conditioner that is designed to add volume and thickness.

Step 2: Dry Your Hair

Once you've washed your hair, it's time to dry it. Use a blow dryer to dry your hair until it is about 80% dry. This will make it easier to cut. If you have curly hair, you may want to use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help define your curls.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small sections. Use hair clips to hold each section in place. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 4: Thin Your Hair

Now it's time to thin your hair. Use thinning shears to cut into each section of hair. Start at the top of each section and work your way down to the bottom. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. You can always go back and thin your hair more if needed.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you've thinned your hair, it's time to style it. You can blow dry your hair straight or use a curling iron to add some waves. Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.

Tips

- Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair before cutting it.

- Don't cut too much hair at once.

- If you're not sure how much hair to cut, start with a small amount and work your way up.

- If you're not comfortable cutting your hair yourself, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can you thin your hair too much?

Yes, you can thin your hair too much. If you thin your hair too much, it can look stringy and unhealthy. It's important to only thin your hair as much as needed.

How often should you thin your hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. If you have thick hair that grows quickly, you may need to thin it every few months. If you have thinner hair that grows more slowly, you may only need to thin it once a year.

Can thinning your hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning your hair will not make it grow back thicker. It will only make it easier to manage and style.

Hair Care

After you've thinned your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. You can also use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Thinning your hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and stylish. By following these simple steps, you can thin your hair and get the perfect bob haircut for your thick hair.
