How To Cut Guy Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Oct 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Long hair has become increasingly popular among men in recent years. However, maintaining long hair can be a challenge, especially when it comes to cutting it. In this article, we will provide a comprehensive guide on how to cut guy long hair in a relaxed and easy-to-follow manner. We will cover everything from the tools you need to the steps you should take to achieve the perfect cut.

  2. Preparing Your Hair
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair
  5. Blending Your Hair

Tools You Will Need

Before you begin cutting your long hair, you will need to gather the necessary tools. These include:

  • Sharp shears
  • Clippers with guards
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Water spray bottle
  • Mirror

Make sure that your shears and clippers are sharp and in good condition to ensure a clean cut.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure that it is clean and dry. Comb out any tangles or knots, and remove any hair ties or clips that you may be using to keep your hair in place. It is important to cut your hair when it is dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in an uneven cut.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is crucial to ensuring an even and symmetrical cut. Start by dividing your hair into four sections: top, sides, and back. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section, leaving only the section you are working on loose.

Top Section

Begin by taking a 1-inch section of hair from the top of your head. Hold the section between your index and middle fingers and cut the ends with your shears. Repeat this process, working your way from the front to the back of your head, until you have cut all of the hair in the top section.

Sides Section

Next, unclip the side sections of your hair and comb them forward. Take a small section of hair from the front and clip the rest of the hair away. Use your clippers with the appropriate guard to trim the hair to your desired length. Repeat this process, moving towards the back of your head.

Back Section

Finally, unclip the back section of your hair and comb it straight down. Use your clippers with the appropriate guard to trim the hair to your desired length.

Cutting Your Hair

Once you have sectioned your hair, it is time to start cutting. Begin by cutting the hair in the back section, working your way up to the sides and top sections.

When cutting your hair, it is important to work slowly and carefully. Take small sections of hair at a time and cut them evenly. Use your comb to hold the hair in place and cut straight across with your shears.

Blending Your Hair

After you have finished cutting your hair, it is important to blend the different sections together. Use your clippers to create a fade between the sides and the back of your head. Blend the top section with the sides by cutting the hair at an angle.

Finishing Touches

Once you have blended your hair, it is time to add the finishing touches. Use your shears to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. Check the length of your hair in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Long Hair

  • Start with a longer length and cut gradually to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Always use sharp shears and clippers to ensure a clean cut.
  • Work slowly and carefully, taking small sections of hair at a time.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • If you are unsure of how to cut your hair, seek the help of a professional stylist.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I cut my long hair?

It is recommended to cut your long hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own long hair?

Yes, you can cut your own long hair. However, it is recommended to seek the help of a professional stylist if you are unsure of how to cut your hair.

How do I know what length to cut my hair?

It is important to have a clear idea of what length you want before you start cutting your hair. Use pictures or consult with a stylist to determine the best length for your face shape and hair type.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your long hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain its health and shape. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using hot tools such as blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage your hair. Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Cutting guy long hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily and effectively. Remember to work slowly and carefully, taking small sections of hair at a time. Use the tips and techniques outlined in this article to achieve the perfect long hair cut.
