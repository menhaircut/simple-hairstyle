The Best Way To Thin Out Long Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Do you have long, thick hair that's difficult to manage? Thinning out your hair can make styling easier and more manageable. In this article, we'll show you the best way to thin out long thick hair. We'll cover everything from how to do it, tips to make it easier, and hair care after thinning. So, let's get started!

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Thin Out Long Thick Hair

Thinning out long thick hair is a process that involves removing some of the hair's density to make it more manageable. There are several ways to do this, including using thinning scissors, a razor, or a hair thinning brush. However, the best way to do it is by using thinning shears. Thinning shears are scissors with teeth on one blade that cut some of the hair while leaving the rest intact.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to thin out long thick hair using thinning shears:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back.
  3. Start with the back section and comb through it to remove any tangles.
  4. Take a small section of hair and hold it with your fingers.
  5. Hold the thinning shears with the teeth facing upwards and cut into the section of hair in a zig-zag motion.
  6. Repeat this process, moving through each section of hair until you've thinned out your entire head.

Remember to only cut a small amount of hair at a time to avoid removing too much hair. You can always go back and cut more if needed.

Tips for Thinning Out Long Thick Hair

Thinning out long thick hair can be a daunting task, but these tips can help make it easier:

  • Start small and work your way up. Only cut a little bit of hair at a time.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair damp while cutting. This will help you see where you need to cut and prevent any accidents.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head while cutting. It can be hard to see what you're doing back there, but a mirror can help.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. Thinning out long thick hair can be time-consuming, so ask a friend or family member to help out.

FAQs

Can I thin out my hair with regular scissors?

No, regular scissors will not give you the same effect as thinning shears. Thinning shears are designed to cut some of the hair while leaving the rest intact, which is what makes them perfect for thinning out long thick hair.

How often should I thin out my long thick hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. Generally, it's recommended to thin out long thick hair every 2-3 months.

Will thinning out my hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning out your hair will not make it grow back thicker. It will only remove some of the density, making it more manageable.

Hair Care After Thinning

After thinning out your long thick hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.
  • Avoid using too many hair products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Thinning out long thick hair can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily and safely. Follow these tips and you'll have healthy, manageable hair in no time!
