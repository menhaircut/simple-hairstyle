How To Cut Round Layers Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 09, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune at the salon just to get your hair cut? Do you want to learn how to cut round layers yourself? Look no further! In this article, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut round layers yourself. We’ll also include some tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice to help you achieve the perfect look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Round Layers Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Round Layers
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Advice

How to Cut Round Layers Yourself

Cutting round layers yourself can be intimidating, but it’s not as difficult as you might think. Here are the basic steps to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Use a comb to part your hair down the middle.

Step 2: Divide your hair into four sections, two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Take one section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about two inches from the end.

Step 4: Cut the hair at an angle, making sure to cut less than you think you need. You can always cut more, but you can’t add hair back.

Step 5: Repeat steps 3 and 4 on the remaining sections of hair.

Step 6: Blend the layers by combing through your hair and cutting any stray pieces that are sticking out.

Step 7: Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Round Layers

Now that you know the basic steps, here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect round layers:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Don’t use kitchen scissors or any other type of scissors that aren’t meant for cutting hair.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to cut less and go back for more than to cut too much and have to start over.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don’t be afraid to pause and come back to it later.
  • Look in the mirror often. Make sure you’re cutting evenly and that your layers are blending properly.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting round layers:

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

Q: Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

A: It’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry. Wet hair can stretch, making it difficult to get an accurate cut.

Q: Should I cut my hair straight across or at an angle?

A: To achieve round layers, it’s best to cut your hair at an angle.

Hair Care Advice

Once you’ve cut your round layers, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that’s suited for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider getting a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray.

Now that you know how to cut round layers yourself, you can save money and achieve the perfect look from the comfort of your own home. Happy cutting!
