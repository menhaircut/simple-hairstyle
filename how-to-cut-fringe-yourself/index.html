How To Cut Fringe Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 12, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you looking to freshen up your hairstyle without breaking the bank? Cutting your own fringe may be just the solution you need. Not only is it a cost-effective way to change up your look, but it can also be a fun and empowering experience. Follow these simple steps to learn how to cut fringe yourself.

How to Cut Fringe Yourself

Before you grab your scissors, it's important to have a clear idea of what you want your fringe to look like. Take some time to research different styles and find one that best suits your face shape and hair type. Once you've found your inspiration, it's time to get started.

Step 1: Gather your tools

Make sure you have the necessary tools on hand before you begin. You'll need sharp hairdressing scissors, a comb, and clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into the desired fringe section. Be sure to comb it straight down and clip the rest of your hair away from the section you'll be cutting.

Step 3: Decide on the length

Using your fingers or a comb, determine how long you want your fringe to be. It's always best to start longer and gradually cut shorter as you go.

Step 4: Start cutting

Using your scissors, make small cuts at an angle. Start from the center and work your way outwards. Be sure to keep checking the length as you go.

Step 5: Check for symmetry

Once you've finished cutting, release the rest of your hair and check for symmetry. If one side is longer than the other, make small adjustments until they're even.

Step 6: Style your fringe

Now that you've cut your fringe, it's time to style it. Use a blow dryer and brush to smooth out any kinks and create the desired shape.

Tips for Cutting Fringe Yourself

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own fringe:

  • Start longer than you think you want, you can always go shorter.
  • Use sharp hairdressing scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Be patient and take your time, rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Do not cut too much hair at once, small cuts are better.

FAQs About Cutting Fringe Yourself

Can I cut my fringe with regular scissors?

No, it's best to use sharp hairdressing scissors to ensure a clean cut.

Do I need to wet my hair to cut fringe?

No, it's not necessary to wet your hair before cutting fringe. However, if your hair is particularly unruly, you may find it easier to work with if it's damp.

How often should I trim my fringe?

It's recommended to trim your fringe every four to six weeks to maintain the desired length.

Hair Care for Fringe

Now that you've cut your fringe, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and health. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. Finally, schedule regular trims to keep your fringe looking fresh.

There you have it, a simple guide to cutting fringe yourself. With a little patience and practice, you'll be able to achieve the perfect fringe in no time. Remember, start longer than you think you want and take small cuts, and most importantly, have fun!
