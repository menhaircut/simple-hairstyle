How To Style Textured Pixie Step By Step Guide

If you're looking for a low-maintenance, yet stylish haircut, a textured pixie may be the perfect choice for you. This trendy haircut can be customized to suit your face shape and hair texture, and it's easy to style every day. In this guide, we'll show you how to style a textured pixie in just a few simple steps.

What is a Textured Pixie?

A textured pixie is a short haircut that's usually no more than two inches long. The hair is cut in layers to create volume and movement, and the ends are tapered to give the hair a textured look. This haircut is perfect for those with fine or thin hair who want to add some texture and volume to their hair.

How to Style a Textured Pixie

Styling a textured pixie is easy and takes just a few minutes. Here are the basic steps:

Step 1: Apply Styling Product

Start with clean, damp hair and apply a styling product, such as a mousse or a styling cream, to add texture and hold. Use a small amount of product, as too much can weigh down your hair.

Step 2: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair, lifting the roots as you go. This will add volume and texture to your hair. Use a cool setting on your blow dryer to set the style in place.

Step 3: Style Your Hair

Using your fingers, style your hair into the desired shape. You can add more texture by twisting and tousling your hair with your fingers. For a more polished look, use a flat iron to straighten your hair and create a more defined style.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling a textured pixie:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start with clean, damp hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a small amount of styling product, such as a mousse or a styling cream, to your hair. This will add texture and hold to your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair, lifting the roots as you go. This will add volume and texture to your hair. Use a cool setting on your blow dryer to set the style in place.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Using your fingers, style your hair into the desired shape. You can add more texture by twisting and tousling your hair with your fingers. For a more polished look, use a flat iron to straighten your hair and create a more defined style.

Tips for Styling a Textured Pixie

Here are some tips to help you style your textured pixie:

  • Use a small amount of styling product, as too much can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and texture.
  • Use your fingers to tousle and twist your hair for a more textured look.
  • Use a flat iron to create a more defined style.
  • Try different styling products to find the one that works best for your hair.

FAQ

Can I style my textured pixie with a side part?

Yes, you can style your textured pixie with a side part. Use a comb to create a part on one side of your head and style your hair accordingly.

How often should I get my hair cut?

It's recommended that you get your hair cut every six to eight weeks to keep your textured pixie looking fresh and stylish.

Can I color my hair if I have a textured pixie?

Yes, you can color your hair if you have a textured pixie. However, it's important to use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent your color from fading.

Hair Care Tips for a Textured Pixie

Here are some hair care tips to keep your textured pixie looking its best:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it looking fresh and healthy.

With these styling and hair care tips, you can keep your textured pixie looking stylish and healthy every day. Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find the look that works best for you.
