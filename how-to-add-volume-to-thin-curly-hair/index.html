How To Add Volume To Thin Curly Hair Tips And Tricks In 2023

Written by Oliver Sep 10, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have thin curly hair, you know the struggle of getting your hair to look voluminous and full. It can be frustrating when your curls fall flat and lack body, making your hair appear lifeless and dull. However, with some simple tips and tricks, you can add volume to your thin curly hair and achieve bouncy and voluminous curls. In this blog post, we will show you how to add volume to thin curly hair in 2023.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Add Volume to Thin Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thin Curly Hair

How to Add Volume to Thin Curly Hair

Here are some simple steps to help you add volume to your thin curly hair:

1. Use the Right Products

Using the right products can make all the difference when it comes to adding volume to your thin curly hair. Look for products that are designed to add volume and body, such as volumizing shampoos, conditioners, and mousses. Avoid heavy, oily, or greasy products that can weigh down your curls.

2. Blow-Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow-drying your hair upside down is a great way to add volume and lift to your curls. This technique works by lifting the roots of your hair away from your scalp, creating a voluminous look. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer and scrunch your hair upwards while drying.

3. Use Hot Rollers

Hot rollers are a great tool for adding volume and body to your curls. Roll your hair in sections using hot rollers and leave them in for around 20 to 30 minutes. Once you remove the rollers, gently brush out your curls with a wide-tooth comb for a voluminous look.

4. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair is a simple technique that can add instant volume and lift to your curls. Use a teasing comb or brush to gently backcomb the roots of your hair. Be careful not to tease your hair too much, as this can damage your hair and cause breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you add volume to your thin curly hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using too much conditioner, as this can weigh down your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Volumizing Mousse

Apply a volumizing mousse to your damp hair, focusing on the roots. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Blow-Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer and dry your hair upside down. Scrunch your hair upwards while drying to create volume and lift.

Step 4: Use Hot Rollers

Roll your hair in sections using hot rollers and leave them in for around 20 to 30 minutes. Once you remove the rollers, gently brush out your curls with a wide-tooth comb for a voluminous look.

Step 5: Tease Your Hair

Use a teasing comb or brush to gently backcomb the roots of your hair. Be careful not to tease your hair too much, as this can damage your hair and cause breakage.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you add volume to your thin curly hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this can prevent breakage and damage.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it appear dull and lifeless.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove buildup and product residue from your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or serums, as these can weigh down your curls and make them appear flat.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a curling iron to add volume to my thin curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use a curling iron to add volume to your thin curly hair. However, be careful not to use too much heat, as this can damage your hair. Use a heat protectant spray before using a curling iron.

Q: Can I add volume to my thin curly hair without using heat?

A: Yes, you can add volume to your thin curly hair without using heat. Try using hot rollers or flexi rods instead of a curling iron or blow dryer.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain healthy and voluminous curls:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as bleach or hair dyes, as these can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can add volume to your thin curly hair and achieve bouncy and voluminous curls. Experiment with different techniques and products to find what works best for your hair type and texture. With a little bit of practice, you can achieve the voluminous curls you've always wanted.
