How To Big Chop Transitioning Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 20, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're considering going natural, big chopping your transitioning hair is one of the most popular options. The big chop involves cutting off all of your relaxed hair, leaving only your natural hair. This process can be daunting, but with the right information and preparation, you can have a successful big chop.

How to Prepare for the Big Chop

The big chop is a major hair change, so it's important to prepare yourself mentally and physically. Here are some tips to help you prepare for the big chop:

1. Research

Before you go in for the big chop, do some research to find the right stylist who specializes in natural hair. Look for reviews and pictures of their previous work to ensure you're in good hands.

2. Set Realistic Expectations

Understand that your hair will be shorter after the big chop. Set realistic expectations for what your hair will look like and how you'll style it.

3. Get Your Products Ready

Start investing in natural hair care products that will work best for your new hair texture. Look for products that are free of harsh chemicals and sulfates.

Step-by-Step Guide to the Big Chop

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you through the big chop process:

1. Wash Your Hair

Before the big chop, wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.

2. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four sections, using hair clips to keep them separate.

3. Cut Your Hair

Starting with one section, use scissors to cut off the relaxed hair. Continue this process for each section until all the relaxed hair is removed.

4. Moisturize Your Hair

After the big chop, your natural hair will need moisture. Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair.

Tips for Caring for Your Natural Hair

Once you've big chopped, it's important to care for your natural hair properly. Here are some tips to help you care for your natural hair:

1. Moisturize Your Hair

Your natural hair needs moisture to stay healthy. Use a leave-in conditioner, oil, or cream to keep your hair moisturized.

2. Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to protect your hair at night. This will help prevent breakage and frizz.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Q: Will my hair grow back after the big chop?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after the big chop.

Q: How long will it take for my hair to grow back after the big chop?

A: The rate at which your hair grows back will depend on your hair type and how well you take care of it. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month.

Q: Can I style my hair after the big chop?

A: Yes, you can style your hair after the big chop. There are many different styles you can try, including twist-outs, braid-outs, and wash-and-go styles.

Conclusion

The big chop is a major hair change, but it can also be a liberating experience. With the right preparation and care, you can have healthy, beautiful natural hair. Remember to be patient with your hair and enjoy the journey.
