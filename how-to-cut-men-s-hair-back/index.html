How To Cut Men s Hair Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to cut men's hair back! Whether you're a professional barber or just looking to give your friend or partner a trim, this article will provide you with all the tips and techniques you need to achieve a great result. In this guide, we'll cover everything from the tools you'll need to the different cutting techniques you can use, so let's get started!

Table of Contents:

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping the Hair
  • Cutting Techniques
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for a Great Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools for the job. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Cape or towel
  • Water spray bottle
  • Clipper guards (optional)

Make sure your tools are clean and sharp before you start cutting.

Prepping the Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to prep the hair to ensure a smooth and even cut:

  • Wash the hair and towel-dry it until it's slightly damp.
  • Comb the hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  • Section the hair into manageable sections using clips or elastic bands.
  • Use a water spray bottle to dampen each section of hair as you work on it.
  • Cover the person with a cape or towel to protect their clothes from hair clippings.

Cutting Techniques

Using Scissors:

Scissors are best for cutting longer hair or creating texture. Here's how to use them:

  • Hold the hair section between your fingers and cut in a straight line, using the tips of the scissors.
  • Use a comb to lift and guide the hair as you cut.
  • Cut small sections at a time, and check your progress regularly.
  • Use thinning scissors to create texture and remove bulk.

Using Clippers:

Clippers are best for cutting shorter hair or creating a buzz cut. Here's how to use them:

  • Attach a clipper guard to the clippers to choose your desired length.
  • Start at the nape of the neck and move the clippers upwards, against the direction of hair growth.
  • Continue using the clippers to cut around the sides and back of the head, using the guard to maintain an even length.
  • Use scissors to blend in any longer sections of hair on top of the head.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting, it's time to add the finishing touches:

  • Use the clippers or scissors to trim any stray hairs around the ears and neck.
  • Use a comb to check for any uneven sections, and trim as necessary.
  • Style the hair as desired using products such as gel or pomade.

Tips for a Great Haircut

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve a great haircut:

  • Start with a longer guard or section of hair, and work your way shorter if necessary.
  • Take breaks to step back and check your progress in a mirror.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once - it's better to make several small cuts than one big one.
  • Use a sharper angle with scissors to create layers and texture.
  • Remember to clean your tools after each use.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair using these techniques?

Yes, you can use these techniques to cut your own hair, but it's always best to have someone else do it for you to ensure an even cut.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic - it's just hair! You can always go shorter if necessary, or visit a professional barber to fix any mistakes.

How often should I cut my hair?

This depends on your hair type and style, but most men should aim to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain their style and keep their hair healthy.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair looking and feeling its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

And that's it - our complete guide on how to cut men's hair back! We hope you found this article helpful, and feel confident in your ability to give a great haircut. Remember, practice makes perfect, so keep practicing and experimenting with different techniques to find what works best for you. Happy cutting!
