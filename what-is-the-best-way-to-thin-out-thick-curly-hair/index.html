What Is The Best Way To Thin Out Thick Curly Hair

Written by Daniel Aug 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Thick curly hair can be beautiful, but it can also be a challenge to manage. One way to make it easier to style and maintain is by thinning it out. But what is the best way to thin out thick curly hair? In this article, we’ll explore different methods for thinning out thick curly hair, along with tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Out Thick Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Out Thick Curly Hair

How to Thin Out Thick Curly Hair

There are several ways to thin out thick curly hair, including:

  • Using thinning shears
  • Getting a razor cut
  • Using a texturizing product
  • Getting a chemical treatment

Each of these methods has its pros and cons, so it’s important to choose the one that works best for your hair type and desired style.

Using Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a type of scissors with teeth that allow you to remove some of the bulk from your hair without changing its overall length. This method is best for those who want to keep their curls intact but need to reduce the volume.

Getting a Razor Cut

A razor cut is a technique that uses a razor blade to create soft, textured layers in your hair. This method can be used to thin out thick curly hair while also adding movement and definition.

Using a Texturizing Product

Texturizing products, such as mousses, gels, and sprays, can be used to add texture and reduce the volume of thick curly hair. These products work by coating the hair shaft and separating the curls, making them look more defined and less bulky.

Getting a Chemical Treatment

Chemical treatments, such as a relaxer or a keratin treatment, can be used to permanently or temporarily straighten thick curly hair, making it easier to manage and style. However, these treatments can also damage the hair if not done correctly, so it’s important to choose a reputable salon and stylist.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you’ve decided to thin out your thick curly hair, here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Blow-dry your hair with a diffuser attachment until it’s about 80% dry.
  3. Section your hair into four parts, using hair clips to keep them separate.
  4. Starting with one section at a time, take small sections of hair and use thinning shears, a razor blade, or a texturizing product to thin out the hair.
  5. Repeat with each section until you’ve achieved your desired level of thinning.
  6. Style your hair as usual and enjoy your new, more manageable curls!

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your thick curly hair:

  • Start with a small amount of thinning and gradually increase if needed.
  • Don’t thin out too much hair at once, as this can lead to unevenness and loss of curl definition.
  • Choose a method that works best for your hair type and desired style.
  • Consider consulting with a stylist for advice and guidance.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

FAQs

Can I thin out my thick curly hair at home?

While it’s possible to thin out your hair at home using thinning shears or a razor blade, it’s recommended to consult with a stylist for best results and to avoid damaging your hair.

Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner overall?

Thinning out your hair can reduce the volume and make it easier to manage, but it shouldn’t make your hair look noticeably thinner overall.

How often should I thin out my thick curly hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. It’s generally recommended to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your desired level of thinning.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Thick curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using products with alcohol or sulfates, as these can dry out your hair.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and healthy.

Thinning out thick curly hair can be a game-changer for those who struggle with managing their curls. By choosing the best method for your hair type and style, and taking good care of your hair, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls that are easy to style and maintain.
