What Religion Has Really Long Hair

Written by Daniel Jul 19, 2023 · 5 min read

Long hair is often associated with femininity and beauty, and many religions around the world encourage their followers to grow their hair long. In this blog post, we'll explore the religions that have a tradition of growing long hair and discuss some tips for caring for long hair.

  2. How to Grow Long Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide

Religions with Long Hair Tradition

There are several religions that have a tradition of growing long hair. These include:

Judaism

In Judaism, hair is seen as a symbol of spiritual strength and is often left uncut, particularly among Hasidic Jews. Orthodox Jewish men may also grow long sideburns, called payot or peyos, as a reminder to follow God's commandments.

Sikhism

Sikhs are required to keep their hair long and uncut as a symbol of their commitment to the Sikh faith. Men often wear their hair in a topknot called a joora, while women may wear their hair in a braid. Sikh men may also cover their hair with a turban.

Rastafarianism

Rastafarians believe that their hair is a symbol of their connection to God and their African heritage. They often grow long dreadlocks and may wear a head covering called a turban.

Hinduism

Hindus believe that the hair is an important part of the body and should not be cut without a specific reason, such as mourning the death of a loved one. Many Hindu men grow their hair long and may wear it in a topknot or braid.

How to Grow Long Hair

Growing long hair takes time and patience, but there are some things you can do to encourage hair growth:

Eat a healthy diet

A balanced diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Foods that are high in protein, such as eggs, fish, and nuts, can also help strengthen hair.

Avoid heat styling

Using hot tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can damage hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try to limit your use of these tools or use them on a low heat setting.

Get regular trims

While it may seem counterintuitive, getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow longer. Trimming off split ends can prevent breakage and keep your hair healthy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are some steps you can take to grow long, healthy hair:

Step 1: Start with healthy hair

Before you start growing your hair out, make sure it's healthy. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot tools or chemical treatments on your hair.

Step 2: Be patient

It can take several months or even years to grow your hair to the desired length. Be patient and avoid getting discouraged if your hair doesn't grow as quickly as you'd like.

Step 3: Protect your hair

Avoid exposing your hair to harsh elements like sun, wind, and chlorine. Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun, and use a swim cap when swimming in chlorinated water.

Step 4: Use a wide-tooth comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent pulling and breaking your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Long Hair

Once you've grown your hair long, here are some tips for keeping it healthy:

Condition regularly

Long hair can become dry and brittle, so it's important to condition it regularly. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and shiny.

Avoid tight hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Try to avoid wearing your hair in tight styles too often.

Use a heat protectant

If you do use hot tools on your hair, be sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum to prevent damage.

FAQs

Q: Can I still dye my hair if I want to grow it long?

A: Yes, you can still dye your hair if you want to grow it long. Just be sure to use a gentle, ammonia-free hair dye and avoid over-processing your hair.

Q: Is it true that brushing your hair 100 times a day can make it grow longer?

A: No, this is a myth. Brushing your hair too much can actually cause breakage and damage to your hair.

Q: Should I use a boar bristle brush to brush my hair?

A: Boar bristle brushes are gentle on hair and can help distribute natural oils throughout your hair, but they can also be expensive. A regular plastic or nylon brush can work just as well.

Hair Care Products for Long Hair

Here are some hair care products that can help keep your long hair healthy:

Deep conditioning treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and shiny.

Leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help detangle your hair and prevent breakage.

Heat protectant spray or serum

Use a heat protectant spray or serum before using hot tools on your hair.

Wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to prevent pulling and breaking your hair.

In conclusion, there are several religions that have a tradition of growing long hair, and there are many things you can do to encourage hair growth and keep your hair healthy. Whether you're growing your hair for religious reasons or simply because you love long hair, taking care of your hair is important for maintaining its beauty and strength.
