How to Make a 60s Hair Bump | Step-by-Step Guide

The 60s hair bump is a classic hairstyle that has made a comeback in recent years. This iconic hairstyle was popularized by celebrities like Brigitte Bardot and Jackie Kennedy. If you want to learn how to make a 60s hair bump, keep reading for a step-by-step guide.

How to Make a 60s Hair Bump

The 60s hair bump is a simple hairstyle that can be achieved in a few easy steps. Here's how:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Separate the top section of your hair from the rest of your hair and clip it up.
  3. Backcomb the crown section of your hair using a fine-toothed comb. Start at the roots and work your way towards the ends.
  4. Spray the backcombed section of your hair with hairspray to keep it in place.
  5. Smooth the top section of your hair over the backcombed section and secure it with bobby pins.
  6. Tease the ends of your hair to create volume.
  7. Finish with a final spritz of hairspray to set the style in place.

Tips for Making a 60s Hair Bump

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect 60s hair bump:

  • Use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb your hair for the best results.
  • If your hair is fine or thin, use a volumizing mousse before backcombing to add texture and volume.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different hair accessories like headbands or scarves to add a touch of 60s glamour to your hairstyle.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about making a 60s hair bump:

  1. What hair length is best for a 60s hair bump? The 60s hair bump works best on medium to long hair.
  2. Can I make a 60s hair bump with bangs? Yes, you can still make a hair bump with bangs. Just make sure to separate the top section of your hair so that your bangs are not included.
  3. How long does a 60s hair bump last? With the right hair products and techniques, a 60s hair bump can last all day or night.

Hair Care for a 60s Hair Bump

To keep your 60s hair bump looking its best, here are some hair care tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent it from becoming too dry or damaged.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add texture and volume to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Use a hairspray with a strong hold to keep your hair bump in place.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve the perfect 60s hair bump for any occasion. Have fun experimenting with different hairstyles and accessories to make this classic look your own.
