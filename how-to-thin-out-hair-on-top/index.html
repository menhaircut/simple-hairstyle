How To Thin Out Hair On Top Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Sep 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have thick hair, you know how hard it can be to get a good haircut. Hairdressers often struggle to thin out hair on top without creating an uneven look. But don't worry, in this article we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you achieve that perfect, even look you're after.

Description

Thinning out hair on top is a common hair care technique that can help reduce the weight of thick hair, making it more manageable and easier to style. It involves removing some of the hair's bulk without sacrificing length, creating a more even look that's perfect for those looking to achieve a sleeker or more modern hairstyle.

How to Thin Out Hair on Top

There are several ways to thin out hair on top, depending on your hair type and the look you're after. Here are a few popular options:

1. Razoring

Razoring is a technique that involves using a razor to thin out hair on top. The razor removes some of the bulk without creating harsh lines or unevenness. This method is best for those with thick, coarse hair.

2. Point Cutting

Point cutting is a method in which the stylist uses scissors to cut into the hair at an angle, creating texture and reducing bulk. This technique works best for those with fine or thin hair.

3. Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a special type of scissors that have teeth on one blade. These teeth help to remove some of the bulk while leaving the length intact. This method is great for those with medium to thick hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to thinning out hair on top:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair, but don't blow dry it completely. You want your hair to be slightly damp so that it's easier to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to divide your hair into sections. Start at the crown of your head and work your way down to the nape of your neck. Pin each section up with a hair clip.

Step 3: Thin Out Your Hair

Choose the method you want to use to thin out your hair, and start at the back of your head. Take small sections of hair and begin thinning them out, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Be careful not to remove too much hair at once.

Step 4: Blend Your Hair

Once you've thinned out the back of your head, move to the sides and front. Be sure to blend the thinned-out sections with the rest of your hair for a natural look.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished thinning out your hair, style it as desired. You'll likely find that your hair is more manageable and easier to style now that it's been thinned out.

Tips for Thinning Out Hair on Top

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when thinning out hair on top:

1. Don't Overdo It

It's important not to remove too much hair at once. Start slowly and work in small sections to ensure you don't thin out your hair too much.

2. Use the Right Tools

Make sure you have the right tools for the job. Whether you're using razors, scissors, or thinning shears, make sure they're sharp and in good condition.

3. Leave It to the Professionals

If you're not confident in your ability to thin out your hair on top, it's best to leave it to the professionals. A good hairdresser will be able to thin out your hair without creating an uneven look.

FAQ

Can I Thin Out My Hair at Home?

Yes, you can thin out your hair at home. However, it's important to be careful not to remove too much hair at once. If you're not confident in your ability to thin out your hair, it's best to leave it to the professionals.

How Often Should I Thin Out My Hair?

The frequency with which you should thin out your hair depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should aim to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Will Thinning Out My Hair Damage It?

No, thinning out your hair won't damage it as long as it's done correctly. However, if you're not careful, you can end up removing too much hair and creating an uneven look.

Hair Care

After thinning out your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Good Shampoo and Conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, as these can damage your hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair, so it's best to avoid them as much as possible. If you must use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Thinning out hair on top is a great way to achieve a sleeker, more modern look. Whether you do it at home or leave it to the professionals, be sure to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
