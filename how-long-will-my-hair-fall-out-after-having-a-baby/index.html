How Long Will My Hair Fall Out After Having A Baby Tips And Faqs

Written by Aprilia Oct 20, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a new mom, you might be experiencing hair loss after giving birth. This is a common phenomenon that affects many women. In this blog post, we'll explore how long will my hair fall out after having a baby, what causes it, and what you can do about it. Keep reading to learn more.

How to Know if You're Experiencing Postpartum Hair Loss

Postpartum hair loss, also known as telogen effluvium, typically occurs about two to four months after giving birth. You might notice more hair falling out than usual when you brush your hair or take a shower. Your hair might also feel thinner or look less full.

It's important to note that postpartum hair loss is different from female pattern baldness, which is a genetic condition that affects women later in life. If you're not sure whether you're experiencing postpartum hair loss, consult with a dermatologist.

What Causes Postpartum Hair Loss?

During pregnancy, your body produces higher levels of estrogen, which can prolong the growth phase of your hair. This means that you might experience less hair shedding during pregnancy. However, after giving birth, your estrogen levels drop, and your hair enters a resting phase. This resting phase is followed by a shedding phase, which is when you might notice more hair falling out.

Other factors that can contribute to postpartum hair loss include stress, hormonal changes, and nutrient deficiencies.

How Long Will My Hair Fall Out After Having a Baby?

Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for about six months to a year. However, every woman is different, so it's difficult to predict exactly how long it will last. Some women might notice more hair loss than others, and some might experience it for a shorter or longer period of time.

It's important to note that postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition, and your hair will eventually grow back.

Tips for Dealing with Postpartum Hair Loss

Here are some tips for dealing with postpartum hair loss:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid pulling it back tightly or using harsh chemicals.
  • Consider a shorter haircut. A shorter style can make your hair look fuller and hide any thinning areas.
  • Eat a healthy diet. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and other nutrients that are important for hair growth.
  • Manage stress. Stress can contribute to hair loss, so try to find ways to relax and reduce your stress levels.
  • Use volumizing products. Products that add volume to your hair can help make it look fuller.

Hair Care Tips for New Moms

Here are some general hair care tips for new moms:

  • Wash your hair regularly. This can help keep your scalp healthy and prevent buildup.
  • Use a gentle shampoo. Look for a shampoo that's free of harsh chemicals and sulfates.
  • Condition your hair. Conditioning can help keep your hair hydrated and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid heat styling. Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair from UV damage.

FAQ

Q: Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

A: No, postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition, and your hair will eventually grow back.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause postpartum hair loss?

A: No, breastfeeding is not directly linked to postpartum hair loss. However, hormonal changes that occur during breastfeeding can contribute to hair loss.

Q: Can I use hair growth products to treat postpartum hair loss?

A: Some hair growth products might help stimulate hair growth, but it's important to consult with a dermatologist before using any new products.

Q: When should I see a dermatologist for postpartum hair loss?

A: If you're experiencing excessive hair loss or you're concerned about your hair loss, consult with a dermatologist. They can help determine the cause of your hair loss and recommend treatments.

Overall, postpartum hair loss is a common condition that affects many new moms. By following the tips and advice in this blog post, you can help manage your hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
