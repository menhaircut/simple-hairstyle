Can You Pray Salah With Fake Hair

Written by Christine Jun 09, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are someone who wears wigs, hair extensions, or other types of fake hair, you may be wondering if it is permissible to pray salah while wearing them. This is a common question among Muslim women who want to maintain their appearance while also fulfilling their religious obligations. In this article, we will discuss whether or not you can pray salah with fake hair, as well as some tips on how to care for your hair to ensure it remains in good condition.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Tips

FAQ

Is it permissible to pray salah with fake hair?

Yes, it is permissible to pray salah with fake hair. Wearing wigs, hair extensions, or other types of fake hair does not invalidate your salah as long as the hair covers your head and neck and does not obstruct your forehead from touching the ground during sujood.

Do I need to remove my fake hair before salah?

No, you do not need to remove your fake hair before salah. As long as your hair covers your head and neck and does not obstruct your forehead from touching the ground during sujood, you can pray salah with fake hair.

What types of fake hair can I wear for salah?

You can wear any type of fake hair for salah as long as it covers your head and neck and does not obstruct your forehead from touching the ground during sujood. This includes wigs, hair extensions, and hijabs.

How to Pray Salah with Fake Hair

Praying salah with fake hair is easy and straightforward. Here are the steps you need to follow:

  1. Ensure that your fake hair covers your head and neck and does not obstruct your forehead from touching the ground during sujood.
  2. Make wudu (ablution) as normal.
  3. Stand facing the qiblah (direction of the Kaaba in Mecca).
  4. Recite the opening takbir (Allahu Akbar).
  5. Recite Surah Al-Fatihah and another surah from the Quran.
  6. Bow down into ruku (bowing position) and recite the appropriate dua.
  7. Stand up straight and recite the appropriate dua.
  8. Prostrate into sujood (prostration position) and recite the appropriate dua.
  9. Sit up straight and recite the appropriate dua.
  10. Prostrate into sujood again and recite the appropriate dua.
  11. Sit up straight and recite the appropriate dua.
  12. Complete the salah by making the tasleem (saying salaam to the right and left).

Tips for Praying Salah with Fake Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when praying salah with fake hair:

  • Make sure your fake hair is securely fastened to your head to avoid any mishaps during salah.
  • Choose a wig or hair extension that is comfortable and lightweight, so it does not distract you during salah.
  • Consider wearing a headband or scarf underneath your fake hair to keep it in place and prevent slipping.
  • Practice proper hair care to ensure your fake hair remains in good condition and does not look disheveled during salah.
  • Be mindful of your movements during salah to avoid accidentally shifting or moving your fake hair.

Hair Care for Fake Hair

To ensure your fake hair remains in good condition and looks natural during salah, it is essential to practice proper hair care. Here are some tips:

  • Brush your hair regularly to prevent tangles and knots.
  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid damaging the hair fibers.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, as they can damage the hair fibers.
  • Store your fake hair in a cool, dry place to prevent it from becoming damaged or discolored.
  • Avoid exposing your fake hair to sunlight or heat, as it can cause the hair fibers to become brittle and dry.

In conclusion, it is permissible to pray salah with fake hair as long as it covers your head and neck and does not obstruct your forehead from touching the ground during sujood. By following the tips and practicing proper hair care, you can ensure your fake hair remains in good condition and does not distract you during salah.
