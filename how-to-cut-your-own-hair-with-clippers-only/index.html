How To Cut Your Own Hair With Clippers Only A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the salon or barbershop for a simple haircut? With the right tools and a little practice, you can learn how to cut your own hair with clippers only. This guide will walk you through the process, from selecting the right clippers to finishing your cut with style. Let's get started!

How to Choose the Right Clippers for Your Hair

The first step in cutting your own hair with clippers is selecting the right tool for the job. Look for clippers that are designed for home use, with adjustable blades and a variety of guards for different hair lengths. If you have thick or curly hair, choose clippers with a more powerful motor to ensure a clean cut. Check out online reviews and ask friends or family members for recommendations before making your purchase.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Own Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Use a comb to remove any tangles or knots, and consider trimming any longer sections with scissors before using the clippers. If you're using a mirror, position it so you have a clear view of the back of your head.

Step 2: Attach the Guard

Select the guard that corresponds to the hair length you want to achieve, and attach it to the clippers. Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want a shorter cut.

Step 3: Start at the Sides

Begin cutting at the sides of your head, moving the clippers upwards in the direction of the hair growth. Use short, overlapping strokes to ensure an even cut. Don't worry about getting too close to the scalp at this point.

Step 4: Move to the Back

Next, move to the back of your head and repeat the process, starting at the nape of your neck and moving upwards. Use the mirror to check your progress and make sure your cut is even on both sides.

Step 5: Trim the Top

Finally, use the clippers without a guard to trim the top of your hair. Use the same short, overlapping strokes and work from front to back. Be careful not to take off too much hair, as it's easier to trim more later than to try to fix a mistake.

Step 6: Finishing Touches

Once you have the basic cut done, use scissors to trim any longer sections or to add texture to your hair. You can also use the clippers without a guard to create a fade or to trim your sideburns and neckline.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair with Clippers

- Start with a longer guard and work your way down for a more gradual cut
- Use a mirror to check your progress
- Don't try to cut too much hair at once
- Take breaks if you need to, and don't rush the process
- Practice makes perfect!

FAQ: Cutting Your Own Hair with Clippers

Can I cut my own hair with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut your own hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Clippers are designed for cutting hair and will give you a more even, professional-looking cut.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim more hair or add texture to conceal any uneven spots. If necessary, seek the help of a professional stylist to fix any major mistakes.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair with Clippers

After cutting your own hair with clippers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair with clippers only, you can save time and money by giving yourself a quick trim whenever you need it. With a little practice, you'll be a pro in no time!
