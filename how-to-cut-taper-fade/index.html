How To Cut Taper Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of paying for haircuts every few weeks? Why not learn how to cut your own hair? In this article, we’ll show you how to cut a taper fade. This popular hairstyle is characterized by a gradual decrease in hair length from the top of the head down to the neckline. It’s a versatile look that can be customized to suit any face shape or hair type.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Taper Fade
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Perfect Taper Fade
  • FAQs

How to Cut Taper Fade

The key to a successful taper fade is blending. This means gradually transitioning from one hair length to another. You’ll need to use different clipper guards and techniques to achieve the desired effect. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it. Comb your hair in the direction that it naturally falls. If you have curly or wavy hair, you may want to use a hair pick or wide-tooth comb instead.

Step 2: Choose Your Starting Point

Decide where you want the fade to begin. This will determine the length of hair you leave on top. For a high fade, start at the crown of your head. For a low fade, start just above the ears.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Use scissors or clippers to cut the hair on top to your desired length. You may want to leave it longer than the rest of your hair to create contrast.

Step 4: Create the Fade

Attach a long guard to your clippers and create a guideline around the head. This will help you keep track of where to start fading. Use a shorter guard to cut the hair below the guideline. Gradually switch to even shorter guards as you move down the head, always blending in the hair between the two lengths.

Step 5: Blend the Sides

Attach a shorter guard to your clippers and use a scooping motion to blend the hair on the sides and back of the head. Be careful not to cut too high or you’ll ruin the fade.

Step 6: Define the Hairline

Use clippers or scissors to clean up the hairline around the ears and neckline. You can create a sharp line or a more natural-looking curve.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Use a mirror to check the back and sides of your head. Make any necessary touch-ups.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Apply a small amount of hair product to your hands and run it through your hair. Style as desired.

Tips for a Perfect Taper Fade

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a scooping motion to blend the hair on the sides and back.
  • Don’t cut too high or you’ll ruin the fade.
  • Be patient and take your time. A taper fade requires precision and attention to detail.

FAQs

How long does a taper fade last?

A taper fade can last anywhere from a few days to a few weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style.

Can I cut a taper fade on my own?

Yes, you can cut a taper fade on your own if you have the right tools and skills. However, it’s always best to start with a professional haircut and learn from there.

What’s the difference between a taper fade and a regular fade?

A taper fade gradually decreases in hair length, while a regular fade has a sharp line between the longer and shorter hair. A taper fade is also more subtle and versatile than a regular fade.

Hair Care Tips

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Cutting a taper fade can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it’s definitely doable. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips for a perfect taper fade every time. Happy cutting!
