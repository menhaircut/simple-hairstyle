How To Cut Face Framing Layers On Medium Length Hair

If you are looking for a new hairstyle that will flatter your features and add some dimension to your hair, then cutting face framing layers on medium length hair might just be the thing for you. In this article, we will show you how to achieve this look step by step, as well as provide some tips and hair care advice to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.

How to Cut Face Framing Layers on Medium Length Hair

Before we get started, you will need a few tools to achieve this look:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Now that you have your tools, let's get started on cutting your face framing layers:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into three parts. Divide your hair into a top, middle, and bottom section. Clip the top section out of the way so it doesn't get in the way of the cutting process.

Step 2: Cut the Bottom Layers

Start with the bottom section of your hair. Comb through your hair and make sure it is free of tangles. Take a small section of hair, about 1-2 inches wide, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut the hair at a slight angle, starting from the middle of the hair strand and cutting towards the ends. This will create a graduated effect, with the hair being shorter towards the back and longer towards the front.

Continue cutting small sections of hair in this manner until you have completed the bottom layer of your hair.

Step 3: Cut the Middle Layers

Let down the middle section of your hair and repeat the same process as above. Cut small sections of hair at a slight angle, starting from the middle of the hair strand and cutting towards the ends. This will create a seamless transition from the bottom layer to the middle layer.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layers

Finally, let down the top section of your hair and repeat the same process as above. Cut small sections of hair at a slight angle, starting from the middle of the hair strand and cutting towards the ends. This will create a seamless transition from the middle layer to the top layer.

Step by Step

To recap, here are the steps to cutting face framing layers on medium length hair:

  1. Section your hair into three parts.
  2. Cut the bottom layers.
  3. Cut the middle layers.
  4. Cut the top layers.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting face framing layers on medium length hair:

  • Make sure your hair is dry or slightly damp. Cutting wet hair can cause it to shrink when it dries, resulting in a shorter haircut than you intended.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.

FAQ

How often should I get my hair cut?

It is recommended to get your hair cut every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Will cutting layers make my hair look thinner?

No, cutting layers can actually make your hair look thicker and add volume.

Can I cut my own hair?

We recommend going to a professional stylist to get the best results. However, if you are confident in your cutting skills, you can cut your own hair at home.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are a few hair care tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

Cutting face framing layers on medium length hair is a great way to add some dimension to your hair and flatter your features. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve this look at home or with the help of a professional stylist. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting it to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
