How To Cut Your Hair In Layers Yourself

Written by Petter Aug 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Cutting your hair in layers can add texture, volume, and movement to your hairstyle. However, going to a salon can be expensive, and time-consuming. If you want to save some money and take control of your hair, you can learn how to cut your hair in layers yourself. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about how to cut your hair in layers, from the tools you need to the step-by-step process, and tips for hair care.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Need
  • How to Cut Your Hair in Layers Yourself
  • Tips for Cutting Your Hair in Layers
  • FAQ

Tools You Need

Before you start cutting your hair in layers, you need to make sure you have the right tools. Here are the things you need to prepare:

  • Scissors: You will need a pair of sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Don't use regular scissors as they can damage your hair.
  • Comb: A wide-toothed comb is recommended for detangling your hair.
  • Hair Ties: You may want to use hair ties to section off your hair while cutting.
  • Mirror: A large mirror will help you see the back of your head while cutting.

How to Cut Your Hair in Layers Yourself

Now that you have your tools ready, let's move on to the step-by-step process of cutting your hair in layers.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. Towel-dry your hair and let it air dry or blow-dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to part your hair down the middle, and then divide your hair into two equal sections. Secure each section with a hair tie.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Start with the bottom layer. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head, comb it straight down, and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Cut the hair straight across with your scissors. Make sure to cut the hair evenly and don't cut too much at once.

Step 4: Cut the Next Layer

Once you finish cutting the first layer, release the hair tie from the next section above it. Comb the hair straight down, and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair to the same length as the first layer.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

When you finish cutting all the layers, you need to blend them together. Take a section of hair from the top layer, comb it down, and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at a 45-degree angle, so it blends with the layers below it.

Step 6: Check Your Work

After finishing cutting your hair in layers, check your work by looking in the mirror from different angles. If you notice any uneven spots, use your scissors to fix them.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair in Layers

Cutting your hair in layers yourself can be tricky, but these tips can help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.
  • Cut your hair in a well-lit room, so you can see what you're doing.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and cut slowly to avoid mistakes.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair in layers with curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your hair in layers even if you have curly hair. However, it's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, so you can see how the layers will look.

How often should I cut my hair in layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your layers to last. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

What if I mess up while cutting my hair in layers?

Don't panic if you make a mistake while cutting your hair in layers. You can always fix it by cutting more hair or going to a professional stylist for help.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair in Layers

After cutting your hair in layers, you need to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips for hair care after cutting your hair in layers:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and be gentle when brushing.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layers.

Cutting your hair in layers yourself can be a fun and challenging experience. With the right tools, techniques, and tips, you can achieve a stylish and trendy hairstyle without breaking the bank. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and practice until you get the hang of it.
