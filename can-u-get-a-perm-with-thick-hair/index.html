Can You Get A Perm With Thick Hair

Written by Oliver Nov 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have thick hair, you may be wondering if you can get a perm. The short answer is yes, you can! However, there are a few things you need to consider before you take the plunge. In this article, we will discuss how to get a perm with thick hair, the steps involved, tips, FAQs and hair care.

Description

A perm is a chemical treatment that changes the structure of your hair. It can make straight hair curly or wavy, or enhance the curls in already curly hair. Thick hair is great for perming because it can hold the curls well. However, it's important to note that the process can damage your hair if it's not done correctly.

How to Get a Perm with Thick Hair

If you've decided to get a perm, there are a few things you need to do before you go to the salon:

1. Consult with a Professional

It's important to consult with a professional stylist who has experience with perming thick hair. They will be able to assess your hair type and determine if a perm is right for you. They will also be able to recommend the best type of perm for your hair.

2. Prepare Your Hair

Before getting a perm, make sure your hair is healthy and in good condition. This means avoiding any chemical treatments or heat styling for at least a week before your appointment. You should also avoid washing your hair for a day or two before your appointment, as this will help protect your scalp from the chemicals.

3. Choose Your Perm Solution

There are different types of perm solutions available, including alkaline, acid, and exothermic. Your stylist will be able to recommend the best one for your hair type and desired results.

4. Get the Perm

Once you're in the salon, your stylist will apply the perm solution to your hair. They will then wrap your hair in perm rods and apply a neutralizer to set the curls. The entire process can take a few hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to getting a perm with thick hair:

Step 1: Consult with a Professional

Find a stylist who has experience with perming thick hair. They will be able to assess your hair type and determine if a perm is right for you.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Avoid any chemical treatments or heat styling for at least a week before your appointment. You should also avoid washing your hair for a day or two before your appointment.

Step 3: Choose Your Perm Solution

Your stylist will recommend the best perm solution for your hair type and desired results.

Step 4: Get the Perm

Your stylist will apply the perm solution to your hair, wrap your hair in perm rods, and apply a neutralizer to set the curls.

Step 5: Care for Your Hair

After getting a perm, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. This means avoiding heat styling, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and applying a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

Tips for Getting a Perm with Thick Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best possible results:

1. Choose the Right Perm Solution

Make sure to choose the right perm solution for your hair type and desired results. Your stylist will be able to recommend the best one for you.

2. Don't Overprocess Your Hair

Overprocessing your hair can cause damage and breakage. Make sure to follow your stylist's instructions and don't leave the perm solution on for too long.

3. Take Care of Your Hair

After getting a perm, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. This means avoiding heat styling, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and applying a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

FAQ

1. Will a perm damage my hair?

A perm can damage your hair if it's not done correctly. Make sure to consult with a professional stylist who has experience with perming thick hair.

2. How long does a perm last?

A perm can last anywhere from six to twelve months, depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you take care of it.

3. Can I color my hair after getting a perm?

It's best to wait at least two weeks before coloring your hair after getting a perm. This will give your hair time to recover from the chemical treatment.

Hair Care

After getting a perm, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause the curls to fall out. Avoid using curling irons, flat irons, and blow dryers.

2. Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is specifically formulated for permed hair. This will help keep your curls looking their best.

3. Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

In conclusion, if you have thick hair, you can get a perm. However, it's important to consult with a professional stylist who has experience with perming thick hair. Make sure to prepare your hair, choose the right perm solution, and take care of your hair after the treatment. Follow these tips and you'll have beautiful, bouncy curls in no time!
