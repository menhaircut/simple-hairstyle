How To Thin Thick Hair With Thinning Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it's great to have a full head of hair, it can also be difficult to manage and style. Thinning scissors are a great tool for those with thick hair, as they can help to remove some of the bulk and make hair easier to style. In this article, we'll show you how to thin thick hair with thinning scissors, including tips on how to use them and how to care for your hair afterwards.

Description: What are Thinning Scissors?

Thinning scissors are a type of hair cutting scissors that are designed to remove some of the bulk from thick hair. They have teeth that are spaced out, which allows them to cut hair in a way that removes some of the thickness without making the hair look choppy or uneven. Thinning scissors are a great tool for those with thick hair, as they can make it easier to style and manage.

How to Thin Thick Hair with Thinning Scissors

Step 1: Choose the Right Scissors

Not all thinning scissors are created equal. When choosing a pair, look for scissors that have teeth that are spaced out evenly and are not too sharp. This will ensure that the hair is cut evenly and not too thin in certain areas.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before using thinning scissors, you should wash and dry your hair. This will help to remove any tangles or knots, making it easier to cut. It's also important to make sure your hair is completely dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use a comb to section it into manageable sections. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 4: Start Thinning

When you're ready to start thinning, hold the scissors at a 45-degree angle and start cutting into the hair. Make sure to only cut a small amount of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much. You can always go back and cut more, but it's difficult to add hair back once it's been cut.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut some of the hair, use your fingers or a comb to blend the hair together. This will help to create a natural look and avoid any choppy areas.

Step 6: Repeat

Continue cutting and blending until you've thinned out all of the sections of hair. Take breaks as needed to avoid getting tired or making mistakes.

Tips for Thinning Hair with Thinning Scissors

Thinning scissors can be a great tool for those with thick hair, but there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more, but it's difficult to add hair back once it's been cut.
  • Take breaks as needed to avoid getting tired or making mistakes.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair in one area, as this can create an uneven look.
  • Use a comb or your fingers to blend the hair as you go to create a natural look.

FAQs About Thinning Scissors

Q: Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

A: Thinning scissors are best for those with thick hair. If you have fine or thin hair, it's best to avoid using thinning scissors as they can make the hair look even thinner.

Q: How often should I thin my hair?

A: It's best to thin your hair every 6-8 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows and how much thickness you want to remove.

Q: Can thinning scissors damage my hair?

A: Thinning scissors are generally safe to use, but it's important to use them correctly and not cut too much hair at once. If you're unsure about how to use them, it's best to consult a professional stylist.

Hair Care Tips After Using Thinning Scissors

After using thinning scissors, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage the hair.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning scissors can be a great tool for those with thick hair, but it's important to use them correctly and take care of your hair afterwards. By following these tips and steps, you'll be able to thin your hair and keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
