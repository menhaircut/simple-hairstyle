How To Fix A Too Short Dress Tips And Tricks

Written by William Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have a favorite dress that is just a little too short for your liking? Maybe you bought it online and didn't realize the length, or perhaps it shrunk in the wash. Whatever the reason, don't let a too-short dress gather dust in your closet. With a few simple fixes, you can make it wearable and stylish again. In this article, we'll show you how to fix a too short dress in relaxed English language, step by step.

How to

Before we dive into the details, here are the basic steps for fixing a too short dress:

  1. Determine the desired length
  2. Gather the necessary materials
  3. Decide on a fix: add fabric, remove hem, or layer
  4. Measure and cut fabric (if necessary)
  5. Attach fabric (if necessary)
  6. Hem or finish edges

Step by Step

Let's break down each step in more detail.

1. Determine the desired length

The first step in fixing a too short dress is to decide on the desired length. Do you want it to hit at the knee, mid-thigh, or ankle? Try on the dress with shoes and accessories to get a better idea of what length will work best.

2. Gather the necessary materials

Depending on the fix you choose, you may need some additional materials. Here are some options:

  • Fabric in a matching or complementary color
  • Hemming tape or fusible web
  • Sewing machine or needle and thread
  • Scissors

3. Decide on a fix: add fabric, remove hem, or layer

There are a few ways to fix a too short dress, depending on the style and fabric. Here are some options:

  • Add fabric: Cut a strip of fabric in a matching or complementary color, and sew it to the hemline of the dress. This will add length without changing the overall style.
  • Remove hem: If the dress has a thick hemline, you may be able to remove it and re-hem at the desired length.
  • Layer: If the dress is too short to wear alone, try layering it over leggings or skinny jeans.

4. Measure and cut fabric (if necessary)

If you're adding fabric to the hemline, measure the length you need to add and cut your fabric accordingly. Make sure to add an extra inch or two for hemming and attaching.

5. Attach fabric (if necessary)

Sew the strip of fabric to the hemline of the dress, right sides together. You can use a sewing machine or needle and thread, depending on your skill level and preference.

6. Hem or finish edges

Once the fabric is attached, hem the bottom edge to finish the look. You can use hemming tape or fusible web for a no-sew option, or sew a traditional hem with a needle and thread or sewing machine.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fixing a too short dress:

  • Choose a fabric that matches the dress in color and weight
  • Use a straight stitch for a clean look
  • Try on the dress with shoes and accessories to get a better idea of the final look
  • Take your time and measure carefully

FAQ

Here are some common questions about fixing a too short dress:

Can I use hemming tape instead of sewing?

Yes, hemming tape is a great no-sew option for fixing a too short dress. Make sure to follow the instructions carefully and use a hot iron to activate the adhesive.

Can I add a contrasting color fabric?

Yes, adding a contrasting color fabric can add interest and style to a too short dress. Just make sure the colors complement each other and the overall look is cohesive.

How can I make sure the dress looks balanced?

Try to add length evenly around the dress, rather than just in one area. This will help the dress look balanced and intentional.

Hair Care

While you're fixing your too short dress, why not give your hair some extra love? Here are some tips for healthy, shiny locks:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using hot tools every day
  • Use a heat protectant spray when you do use hot tools
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

With these tips and tricks, you can fix a too short dress and look great while doing it. Happy sewing!
