Can You Get Dreads With Relaxed Hair

Written by Aprilia Nov 02, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to getting dreadlocks, many people often wonder if it's possible to achieve this unique hairstyle with relaxed hair. The answer is yes, it is possible. However, there are a few things to keep in mind.

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Dreads with Relaxed Hair

Description

Dreadlocks, or simply dreads, are a popular hairstyle that involves the intentional matting and twisting of hair. While traditionally associated with natural hair textures, dreads can also be achieved with relaxed hair. However, it's important to understand that getting dreads with relaxed hair may take longer and require more maintenance than with natural hair.

How to Get Dreads with Relaxed Hair

Getting dreads with relaxed hair involves the same basic process as with natural hair, but there are a few key differences. Here are the general steps:

Step 1: Grow Out Your Hair

The length of your hair is important when it comes to getting dreads with relaxed hair. Your hair should be at least four inches long before you begin the process. This will give you enough hair to work with and will ensure that your dreads are secure.

Step 2: Stop Relaxing Your Hair

In order to get dreads with relaxed hair, you will need to stop relaxing your hair. This means no more chemical treatments, flat ironing, or blowouts. You want your hair to be as natural as possible so that it can easily mat and twist.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to get dreads with relaxed hair:

  1. Grow out your hair to at least four inches in length.
  2. Stop relaxing your hair and avoid any heat styling or chemical treatments.
  3. Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  4. Separate your hair into sections and backcomb each section to create knots.
  5. Twist each section of hair in a clockwise direction until it tightens and forms a dread.
  6. Secure each dread with a rubber band or hair tie.
  7. Allow your dreads to set for several weeks to ensure they are secure.
  8. Remove the rubber bands or hair ties and palm roll each dread to help it stay in shape.
  9. Maintain your dreads by washing them regularly and retwisting any loose hairs.

Tips for Maintaining Dreads with Relaxed Hair

Maintaining dreads with relaxed hair requires a bit more effort than with natural hair. Here are some tips to help you keep your dreads looking their best:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Retwist any loose hairs to keep your dreads secure.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent your dreads from getting tangled while you sleep.
  • Avoid excessive sweating or getting your dreads wet too often, as this can lead to mildew or odors.

FAQ

Can you get dreads with relaxed hair?

Yes, it is possible to get dreads with relaxed hair. However, it may take longer and require more maintenance than with natural hair.

How do you maintain dreads with relaxed hair?

To maintain dreads with relaxed hair, you should wash them regularly, avoid heavy hair products, retwist any loose hairs, use a satin or silk pillowcase, and avoid excessive sweating or getting your dreads wet too often.

How long does it take to get dreads with relaxed hair?

The time it takes to get dreads with relaxed hair can vary depending on your hair type and length. On average, it can take anywhere from six months to a year to achieve fully mature dreads.

Hair Care for Relaxed Hair Dreads

If you have relaxed hair and are considering getting dreads, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid chemical treatments, heat styling, and excessive manipulation of your hair.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Protect your hair with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet while you sleep.
  • Trim any damaged or split ends regularly to prevent further damage.

With proper care and maintenance, you can achieve and maintain dreads with relaxed hair. It may take some extra effort, but the end result can be a unique and stylish hairstyle that you'll love.
