How To Cut Children s Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 26, 2023 · 6 min read

Curly hair can be a joy to behold, but it can also be a challenge to maintain. When it comes to cutting children's curly hair, the task can seem daunting. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give your little one a stylish and easy-to-manage haircut. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut children's curly hair in a way that ensures a beautiful and healthy look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Children's Curly Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Children's Curly Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Cut Children's Curly Hair

Cutting children's curly hair requires some specific techniques to ensure that the curls are not damaged or pulled straight. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Detangle

Start by washing your child's hair and detangling it with a wide-tooth comb. It's best to cut curly hair when it's wet and detangled, as this will give you a more accurate idea of the length and shape of the curls.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using clips, starting at the nape of the neck and working your way up. This will help you to cut the hair evenly and avoid any uneven patches.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Using sharp scissors, trim the hair in each section to the desired length. It's best to cut curly hair in smaller sections to avoid taking off too much at once. Remember that curly hair will shrink when it dries, so it's better to cut it slightly longer than you want it to be.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

If your child has layered hair, blend the layers using a technique called point-cutting. This involves cutting into the hair vertically, rather than horizontally, to create a more natural-looking finish.

Step 5: Finish the Haircut

Once you've cut all the sections, check for any uneven patches or straggly ends. Trim these carefully with the scissors until you're happy with the overall look.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to cutting children's curly hair:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You'll need a few basic tools to cut curly hair, including:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as this will help you to create clean, precise cuts.

Step 2: Wash and Detangle

Shampoo and condition your child's hair as usual, and then apply a leave-in conditioner or detangling spray to help you comb through the curls. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Use hair clips to divide the hair into manageable sections. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up, clipping each section out of the way as you go.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Working on one section at a time, spritz the hair with water to dampen it. Use the scissors to trim the ends of the hair, cutting across the section in a straight line. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as curly hair can be unpredictable and may spring up shorter than you expect.

Continue cutting each section in this way, checking the length and shape as you go. If you're unsure about how much to cut, start with a small amount and work your way up.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

If your child has layered hair, use the point-cutting technique to blend the layers together. Hold the scissors vertically and cut into the hair at an angle, snipping off small amounts of hair at a time. This will create a more natural-looking finish and prevent any choppy or uneven layers.

Step 6: Check for Uneven Patches

Once you've cut all the sections, check for any uneven patches or straggly ends. Trim these carefully with the scissors until you're happy with the overall look.

Tips for Cutting Children's Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you cut your child's curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to create clean, precise cuts.
  • Start with small amounts of hair and work your way up, as curly hair can be unpredictable.
  • Cut curly hair in smaller sections to avoid taking off too much at once.
  • Cut curly hair when it's wet and detangled, as this will give you a more accurate idea of the length and shape of the curls.
  • Trim curly hair slightly longer than you want it to be, as it will shrink when it dries.
  • Blend layers using the point-cutting technique to create a more natural-looking finish.

FAQ

How often should I cut my child's curly hair?

This depends on how fast your child's hair grows, but most curly hair types benefit from a trim every 6-8 weeks. Regular trims help to prevent split ends and keep the hair healthy.

Can I cut my child's curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your child's curly hair yourself with the right tools and techniques. However, if you're unsure or nervous about doing it yourself, it's best to take your child to a professional stylist.

Should I cut curly hair when it's dry or wet?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's wet and detangled, as this will give you a more accurate idea of the length and shape of the curls. Cutting curly hair when it's dry can result in uneven patches and frizz.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires some special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage the hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or detangling spray to help you comb through the curls.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.
  • Apply a curl-enhancing product like mousse or gel to damp hair to define the curls and reduce frizz.

Cutting children's curly hair can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's a task that can be easily accomplished. Follow the steps outlined in this guide, and your child will have a stylish and healthy-looking haircut in no time!
