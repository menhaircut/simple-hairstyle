Why Is My Hair Curlier In The Front

Written by Petter Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered why your hair seems to have different curl patterns in different areas? Specifically, why is your hair curlier in the front? This is a common question that many people have, and the answer lies in a combination of genetics, hair care practices, and styling techniques. In this blog post, we will explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide tips on how to manage your curly front hair.

What Causes Curly Hair?

Before we dive into the specifics of why hair is curlier in the front, let's first understand what causes curly hair in general. The shape of your hair follicles determines whether your hair is straight or curly. Straight hair has round hair follicles, while curly hair has oval-shaped hair follicles. The more oval-shaped your hair follicles are, the curlier your hair will be.

Why Is Hair Curlier in the Front?

Now, let's get to the heart of the matter. Why is your hair curlier in the front? There are a few reasons for this:

Genetics

One of the primary reasons why your hair may be curlier in the front is genetics. Your hair follicles are determined by your DNA, and some people are simply born with more oval-shaped follicles in the front of their heads. If your parents or grandparents have curly hair, it's likely that you inherited the same genes and will have curlier hair in the front as well.

Hair Care Practices

Another factor that can contribute to curly hair in the front is your hair care practices. If you frequently wash your hair, use heat styling tools, or use harsh chemicals like relaxers or perms, your hair can become damaged and more prone to curling. This can cause the front of your hair to become curlier than the rest.

Styling Techniques

The way you style your hair can also contribute to the front being curlier. For example, if you frequently wear your hair in a tight ponytail or bun, this can cause the front of your hair to curl more due to the tension on those hair follicles. Similarly, if you frequently part your hair on one side, the hair on that side may become curlier than the other side.

How to Manage Curly Front Hair

If you're struggling to manage your curly front hair, there are a few things you can do to help:

Use Gentle Hair Care Products

To prevent further damage and breakage, it's important to use gentle hair care products that are free of harsh chemicals. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are specifically formulated for curly hair, as these will help to moisturize and define your curls.

Avoid Heat Styling

To minimize damage to your hair, avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. If you must use these tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting on low to minimize damage.

Try Different Styling Techniques

If you're looking for ways to style your curly front hair, try experimenting with different techniques like twist outs, braid outs, or finger coils. These methods can help to define your curls and create a more polished look.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Whether your hair is curlier in the front or all over, there are a few tips you can follow to keep your curls looking their best:

  • Moisturize regularly using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make my hair less curly in the front?

While you can't change the shape of your hair follicles, you can use styling techniques and products to minimize the appearance of curls in the front. Talk to your stylist about the best options for your hair type.

Can I straighten my curly front hair?

Yes, you can straighten your curly front hair using heat styling tools like flat irons or blow dryers. However, it's important to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting on low to prevent damage to your hair.

What causes frizz in curly hair?

Frizz is caused by a lack of moisture in the hair. To prevent frizz, make sure to moisturize your hair regularly using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil, and avoid brushing your hair when it's dry.

Conclusion

Curly front hair is a common occurrence that can be caused by genetics, hair care practices, and styling techniques. By following the tips and techniques outlined in this blog post, you can help to manage your curly front hair and keep your curls looking their best.
