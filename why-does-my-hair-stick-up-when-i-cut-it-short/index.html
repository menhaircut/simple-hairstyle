Why Does My Hair Stick Up When I Cut It Short

Written by Bardi Sep 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing a bad hair day after getting a new short haircut? Wondering why your hair seems to be sticking up at odd angles? This is a common complaint among people who have recently cut their hair short. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide tips on how to tame your unruly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When you cut your hair short, you are essentially removing the weight that was previously keeping your hair down. This weight was provided by the longer strands of hair, which helped to pull the shorter hairs down and keep them in place. Without this weight, the shorter hairs are free to stand up and stick out in all directions.

Another factor that contributes to your hair sticking up after a short haircut is the natural growth pattern of your hair. Each hair follicle has its own unique angle and direction of growth, which can cause the hair to stand up or stick out if cut too short. This is why some people may experience more extreme cases of hair sticking up than others.

How To

If you're struggling with hair that won't lay flat after getting a short haircut, there are a few things you can try to help tame your locks:

Use Styling Products

One of the easiest ways to tame unruly hair is by using styling products. Look for products that are designed to add weight and hold to your hair, such as pomade, wax, or gel. Apply a small amount to your hair and style as desired.

Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help to smooth down any flyaways and add volume and body to your locks. Use a round brush to help shape your hair as you blow dry, and finish with a blast of cool air to set the style in place.

Try a Different Haircut

If you're really struggling with hair that won't lay flat, it may be worth considering a slightly longer haircut that provides more weight and volume to your hair. Talk to your stylist about different options that may work better for your hair type and face shape.

Step By Step

Here are some step-by-step tips for taming hair that won't lay flat after a short haircut:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any excess oils or product buildup that may be weighing your hair down.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair, focusing on the areas where your hair is sticking up. Use your fingers to work the product through your hair, and style as desired.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a blow dryer to dry your hair, using a round brush to help smooth down any flyaways. Finish with a blast of cool air to set the style in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for taming hair that won't lay flat after a short haircut:

  • Try using a hair serum or oil to add shine and smoothness to your hair.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.
  • Consider getting regular trims to help maintain the shape and style of your short haircut.

FAQ

Q: Will my hair eventually lay flat after a short haircut?

A: Yes, as your hair grows out, it will become heavier and easier to manage. In the meantime, try using styling products or blow drying your hair to help tame any unruly locks.

Q: Can I use a flat iron to straighten my hair after a short haircut?

A: Yes, a flat iron can help to smooth down any flyaways and straighten your hair. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, manageable locks. Here are some tips for keeping your hair in top condition:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is tailored to your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools too frequently, as this can cause damage and breakage to your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.

By following these tips, you can help to keep your hair looking great, even after a short haircut.
