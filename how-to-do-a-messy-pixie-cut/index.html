How To Do A Messy Pixie Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a bold and edgy haircut that will turn heads, a messy pixie cut might be just what you need. This short and choppy style is perfect for those who want to embrace their natural texture and add a touch of spunk to their look. In this article, we'll show you how to do a messy pixie cut from start to finish. From choosing the right length to styling your new 'do, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Choosing the Right Length
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Styling Your Messy Pixie Cut
  • Tips and Tricks
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

Choosing the Right Length

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to decide on the length you want. A messy pixie cut can range from super short to chin-length, so think about what will work best for your face shape and hair texture. If you have a round face, a longer pixie cut might be more flattering, while those with an oval face can pull off a shorter cut.

When it comes to hair texture, a messy pixie cut works well with wavy or curly hair, as these textures add volume and movement to the style. However, if you have straight hair, don't worry - a pixie cut can still work for you. Just be prepared to spend a bit more time styling it to get that messy, bedhead look.

Cutting Your Hair

Once you've decided on the length you want, it's time to start cutting your hair. If you're not confident in your cutting skills, it's best to go to a professional stylist. However, if you're feeling brave, you can do it yourself.

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Use hair clips or elastics to keep each section in place.

Next, take a pair of sharp scissors and start cutting your hair. Begin at the back of your head, cutting small sections at a time. Work your way up to the crown of your head, being careful not to cut too much at once.

Once you've cut the back, move on to the sides and the top. Again, work in small sections and use your fingers as a guide to create a choppy, textured look. Don't worry about being too precise - the beauty of a messy pixie cut is in its imperfection.

Styling Your Messy Pixie Cut

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. To achieve a messy, bedhead look, start by applying a texturizing spray or mousse to your damp hair. This will help create volume and texture.

Next, use a blow dryer and your fingers to rough-dry your hair. Don't worry about being too neat - the goal is to create a messy look. Once your hair is dry, use a flat iron or a curling wand to create loose waves or curls.

If you want to add even more volume and texture, use a dry shampoo or a sea salt spray. These products will give your hair that effortless, tousled look.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few tips to help you get the most out of your messy pixie cut:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. This will make cutting your hair much easier.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styling products. Every hair type is different, so what works for someone else might not work for you.
  • If you're not sure about cutting your hair yourself, go to a professional stylist. They will be able to give you advice on the best length and style for your face shape and hair texture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about messy pixie cuts:

  • Will a messy pixie cut work on curly hair?

    • Yes! A messy pixie cut is perfect for those with curly hair, as it allows you to embrace your natural texture.

  • How can I make my messy pixie cut last longer?

    • Use a texturizing spray or mousse to add volume and texture, and avoid washing your hair every day. Dry shampoo can be a lifesaver for those days when you don't have time to wash your hair.

  • What should I do if I don't like my messy pixie cut?

    • If you're not happy with your haircut, don't panic. It will grow back! In the meantime, you can experiment with different styling products and techniques to find a look you love.

Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

Now that you have a messy pixie cut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair. If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair. Stick to lightweight styling products like mousse or texturizing spray.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock a messy pixie cut like a pro. Whether you're looking for a dramatic change or just want to switch up your look, a messy pixie cut is a great option for those who want to stand out from the crowd.
