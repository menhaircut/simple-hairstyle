How To Cut Short Choppy Layered Hair

Written by Robby Oct 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not a professional hair stylist. But with the right tools and techniques, you can create a short, choppy layered cut that looks chic and stylish. In this article, we'll guide you through how to cut short choppy layered hair and answer some frequently asked questions about this trendy haircut.

Description

The short choppy layered haircut is a popular style that is perfect for those who want a low maintenance, edgy look. This cut typically features layers of different lengths that are cut at an angle to create a choppy, textured effect. It's a versatile cut that can be styled in a variety of ways and is suitable for all hair types.

How to Cut Short Choppy Layered Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. Here are the steps you need to follow to cut short choppy layered hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut. If you have curly or wavy hair, it's best to let it dry naturally to avoid altering the natural texture.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Using your fine-tooth comb, section off your hair into four equal parts: the top, sides, and back. Clip each section out of the way to keep it separate.

Step 3: Start Cutting the Layers

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and cut it at an angle, creating a short layer. Continue to work your way up, taking small sections and cutting them at different angles to create a choppy, layered effect. Repeat this process with the side sections and the top section of your hair.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Using your scissors, blend the layers together by cutting into the hair at an angle. This will help to create a more natural, seamless look.

Step 5: Add Texture

To add even more texture to your hair, you can use your scissors to point-cut into the ends of your hair. This will create a choppy, textured effect that looks edgy and stylish.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, you can style it in a variety of ways. You can add some volumizing mousse to create a messy, tousled look or use a straightening iron for a sleek, polished look. Experiment with different styles to find the one that suits you best.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short choppy layered hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting off too much at once.
  • Use your fingers to hold the hair in place while cutting, instead of using a comb.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet or damp, as this can make it appear shorter than it actually is.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different angles and lengths to create a unique look.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short choppy layered hair:

Is this cut suitable for all hair types?

Yes, this cut is suitable for all hair types, including straight, wavy, curly, and coily hair.

Can I cut my own hair at home?

While it's always best to visit a professional hair stylist, you can cut your own hair at home if you're confident and have the right tools.

How often should I get my hair cut?

It's recommended to get your hair cut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and shape of your cut.

Hair Care

To keep your short choppy layered haircut looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized, and avoid using too much heat when styling. Regular trims can also help to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting short choppy layered hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, especially when you see the final result. With these tips and techniques, you can create a trendy, edgy look that's perfect for any occasion. So grab your scissors and get cutting!
