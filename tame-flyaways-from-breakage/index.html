Taming Flyaways From Breakage A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 10, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're someone who struggles with flyaways caused by breakage, you know how frustrating it can be. Not only do they make your hair look messy, but they can also be damaging in the long run. In this guide, we'll explore how to tame flyaways from breakage and keep your hair healthy and strong.

Description

Flyaways are those small, wispy strands of hair that often stick up or out from your head. They can be caused by a variety of factors, including breakage, humidity, and static electricity. When it comes to flyaways caused by breakage, the problem is often rooted in damage to the hair shaft. This damage can be caused by a number of things, including heat styling, chemical treatments, and rough handling.

How to Tame Flyaways from Breakage

If you're dealing with flyaways caused by breakage, there are a few things you can do to tame them and prevent further damage.

Step 1: Moisturize Your Hair

Dry, brittle hair is more prone to breakage, which can lead to flyaways. To combat this, it's important to keep your hair moisturized. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and consider incorporating a leave-in conditioner into your routine.

Step 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be incredibly damaging to your hair, leading to breakage and flyaways. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible. Otherwise, consider air-drying your hair or using heat-free styling techniques.

Step 3: Be Gentle with Your Hair

Rough handling can cause breakage, which can lead to flyaways. Be gentle with your hair when brushing or combing, and avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.

Tips for Taming Flyaways

Here are a few additional tips for taming flyaways caused by breakage:

Tip 1: Use a Smoothing Serum

A smoothing serum can help to tame flyaways and keep your hair looking sleek. Look for a serum that contains nourishing ingredients like argan oil or shea butter.

Tip 2: Use a Boar Bristle Brush

A boar bristle brush can help to distribute your scalp's natural oils throughout your hair, keeping it moisturized and healthy. Brush your hair gently from root to tip to help tame flyaways.

Tip 3: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to reduce friction and prevent breakage while you sleep, which can lead to fewer flyaways in the morning.

FAQs About Taming Flyaways from Breakage

Q. How long does it take to tame flyaways?

A. The amount of time it takes to tame flyaways can vary depending on the severity of the breakage. With consistent hair care and the right products, you should start to see an improvement in a few weeks.

Q. Can I still color my hair if I have flyaways?

A. Yes, but it's important to be gentle with your hair and avoid over-processing. Consider a salon treatment that can help to strengthen your hair and prevent further breakage.

Q. Can I use hairspray to tame flyaways?

A. Yes, but be careful not to use too much, as it can make your hair look greasy or weighed down. Look for a lightweight hairspray that won't leave residue on your hair.

Hair Care for Preventing Breakage and Flyaways

Preventing breakage is key to taming flyaways. Here are a few tips for keeping your hair healthy and strong:

Tip 1: Avoid Over-Processing

Chemical treatments like coloring and perming can be damaging to your hair. Try to limit these treatments and give your hair time to recover between appointments.

Tip 2: Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help to nourish your hair from the inside out. Be sure to eat plenty of protein, healthy fats, and vitamins like biotin and vitamin E.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can be damaging to your hair, leading to breakage and flyaways. Consider wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

In conclusion, taming flyaways caused by breakage requires a combination of proper hair care and the right products. By moisturizing your hair, avoiding heat styling, and being gentle with your hair, you can help to prevent breakage and tame flyaways. With a little patience and consistency, you'll be able to achieve healthy, beautiful hair that's free from flyaways.
