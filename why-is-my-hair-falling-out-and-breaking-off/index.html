Why Is My Hair Falling Out And Breaking Off

Written by Robby Jul 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you noticed that your hair is falling out and breaking off? This can be a frustrating and concerning problem for many people. There are several reasons why your hair may be falling out and breaking off, from genetics to lifestyle factors. In this article, we will explore the common causes of hair loss and breakage and provide tips on how to maintain healthy hair.

Table of Content

  • Genetics
  • Stress
  • Nutrition
  • Hair Care
  • FAQs

Genetics

One of the most common causes of hair loss and breakage is genetics. If your family has a history of hair loss or thinning, you may be more likely to experience similar issues. Male and female pattern baldness is a genetic condition that affects millions of people worldwide. This condition causes hair to thin and fall out in specific areas of the scalp.

If you suspect that genetics may be the cause of your hair loss and breakage, it is essential to speak with a doctor or dermatologist. They can provide guidance on potential treatments and preventative measures.

Stress

Stress can also be a significant contributor to hair loss and breakage. When you experience high levels of stress, your body produces a hormone called cortisol. Elevated cortisol levels can cause hair to fall out and become brittle.

To reduce stress, consider incorporating relaxation techniques into your daily routine. This may include meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises. It is also essential to get enough sleep and exercise regularly.

Nutrition

Your diet can also impact the health of your hair. If you are not getting enough essential vitamins and nutrients, your hair may become weak and brittle. Foods that are high in protein, vitamins A, C, and E, and minerals such as iron and zinc can promote healthy hair growth.

If you suspect that your nutrition may be contributing to your hair loss and breakage, consider speaking with a nutritionist or dietitian. They can provide guidance on a healthy eating plan that supports healthy hair.

Hair Care

Improper hair care can also cause hair to fall out and break off. Using harsh chemicals, such as relaxers and dyes, can weaken hair and cause it to break. Overuse of heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can also cause hair to become brittle and break.

To maintain healthy hair, consider using gentle hair care products and limiting the use of heat styling tools. It is also important to avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, which can pull on hair and cause breakage.

FAQs

How much hair loss is normal?

It is normal to lose 50-100 hairs per day. If you are experiencing more significant hair loss, it may be a sign of an underlying issue.

Can hair loss be reversed?

Depending on the cause of hair loss, it may be reversible. Consulting with a doctor or dermatologist can help determine the appropriate course of treatment.

Will cutting my hair help with breakage?

Cutting hair can help with split ends, but it will not necessarily prevent breakage. It is important to maintain a healthy hair care routine to prevent breakage.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use gentle hair care products
  • Avoid harsh chemicals, such as relaxers and dyes
  • Limit the use of heat styling tools
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on hair
  • Eat a balanced diet that supports healthy hair growth
  • Get enough sleep and exercise regularly to reduce stress
  • Speak with a doctor or dermatologist if you suspect an underlying medical issue

In conclusion, hair loss and breakage can be caused by a variety of factors, from genetics to lifestyle habits. Maintaining a healthy hair care routine, practicing stress-reducing techniques, and eating a balanced diet can promote healthy hair growth. If you are experiencing significant hair loss or breakage, it is important to consult with a doctor or dermatologist to determine the appropriate course of treatment.
