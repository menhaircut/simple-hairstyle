How To Dread Short Hair By Yourself A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking to sport some dreadlocks but have short hair, don't worry! You don't need long locks to achieve the look you're after. This article will guide you through the process of how to dread short hair by yourself.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Dread Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Dreading Short Hair

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that have been around for centuries. They can be seen on people of all ages, genders, and races. Dreadlocks are created by allowing hair to mat and tangle together. This creates thick, rope-like locks that can be styled in various ways. While dreadlocks are commonly associated with longer hair, it is possible to create dreadlocks on short hair as well. In this article, we'll show you how to do just that!

How to Dread Short Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's first talk about the materials you'll need to create dreadlocks on short hair:

  • Dread wax or locking gel
  • A metal comb
  • Hair elastics
  • Optional: a crochet hook

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your materials ready, let's get started on creating your own dreadlocks:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly and dry it completely.
  2. Section your hair into small, even parts. Use hair elastics to secure each section.
  3. Take a small amount of dread wax or locking gel and apply it to the roots of each section. Use your fingers to work the wax or gel through your hair.
  4. Using a metal comb, backcomb each section of hair. Start at the tips and work your way up to the roots. This will create tangles and knots that will eventually turn into dreadlocks.
  5. Once you've backcombed each section, twist them tightly in one direction. This will help the knots to stay in place.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 for each section of hair.
  7. Optional: If you want to speed up the dreadlocking process, you can use a crochet hook to pull loose hairs into the dreadlocks. This will also help to tighten the knots.
  8. Leave your hair alone for a few days to allow the knots to set in. Avoid washing your hair during this time.
  9. After a few days, you can gently wash your hair using a residue-free shampoo. Be sure to avoid scrubbing your scalp too vigorously, as this can cause the knots to come undone.
  10. Continue to care for your dreadlocks using the tips outlined below.

Tips for Dreading Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when dreading short hair:

  • Be patient. It can take several months for your dreadlocks to fully form.
  • Avoid using too much wax or gel, as this can lead to buildup and make it harder for your hair to lock up.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and accessories for your dreadlocks.
  • Be gentle when washing and styling your hair to avoid pulling out any knots or causing breakage.

FAQs

Can I create dreadlocks on short hair?

Yes! While it may take longer for your hair to fully lock up, it is possible to create dreadlocks on short hair.

What's the difference between dread wax and locking gel?

Dread wax is a thick, heavy wax that helps to hold hair in place and create knots. Locking gel is a lighter gel that helps to promote hair growth and keep hair moisturized while it's in the locking process.

Can I wash my hair while it's in the dreadlocking process?

Avoid washing your hair for the first few days after creating your dreadlocks. After that, you can gently wash your hair using a residue-free shampoo. Be gentle when washing and avoid scrubbing your scalp too vigorously.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once you've created your dreadlocks, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips to help you care for your dreadlocks:

  • Avoid using regular shampoo or conditioner, as these can leave behind residue that can make it harder for your hair to lock up. Instead, use a residue-free shampoo and a vinegar rinse to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can cause damage to your hair and make it harder for your hair to lock up.
  • Be sure to keep your scalp clean and moisturized. You can use a natural oil like coconut or jojoba oil to keep your scalp moisturized.
  • Avoid using hair elastics or tight headbands, as these can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and taking good care of your dreadlocks, you can enjoy your new hairstyle for years to come!
