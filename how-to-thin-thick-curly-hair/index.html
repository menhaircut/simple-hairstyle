How To Thin Thick Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it's great to have a full head of bouncy curls, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One of the most challenging aspects of thick curly hair is the volume. If you're struggling to manage your thick curly hair, you may want to consider thinning it out. In this article, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about how to thin thick curly hair.

Description

Thinning thick curly hair involves removing some of the bulk from your hair to make it more manageable. This can be done with the help of a professional stylist or by using a thinning shear at home. Thinning your hair can help reduce frizz, make your curls more defined, and make your hair easier to style.

How to Thin Thick Curly Hair

Thinning thick curly hair can be done in a few different ways. Here are some of the most popular methods:

  • Thinning shears: Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth on one side and a straight blade on the other. They're used to remove some of the hair without cutting it all off. Thinning shears are best used by a professional stylist.
  • Razor cutting: Razor cutting involves using a razor blade to slice through the hair. This method can create a softer, more textured look, but it's best left to a professional stylist.
  • Layering: Layering involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create depth and movement. This can help reduce bulk and make your hair more manageable.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're going to thin your thick curly hair at home, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  2. Blow-dry your hair until it's about 80% dry. This will help you see the natural shape of your curls.
  3. Section your hair into four equal parts, using hair clips to keep them separate.
  4. Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  5. Use thinning shears to cut the hair at a slight angle, taking care not to cut too much off.
  6. Move on to the next section and repeat the process until you've thinned out all four sections of your hair.
  7. Style your hair as desired, using a curl cream or gel to define your curls.

Tips for Thinning Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning your thick curly hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more hair off, but you can't put it back on.
  • Use good quality thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't cut too much hair off at once. Thinning your hair should be a gradual process.
  • Leave the top layer of your hair untouched to maintain the length and prevent your hair from looking too thin.
  • Always cut your hair when it's dry so you can see the natural shape of your curls.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning thick curly hair:

  • Can thinning your hair cause damage?

    • Thinning your hair can cause damage if it's done improperly. That's why it's important to use good quality thinning shears and to start with a small amount of hair.

  • How often should you thin your hair?

    • How often you should thin your hair depends on how quickly it grows and how much you want to thin it. It's best to consult with a professional stylist to determine the best schedule for your hair.

  • Can you thin your hair without cutting it?

    • No, thinning your hair involves cutting some of it off to reduce bulk.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your thick curly hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair, as regular towels can cause frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning your thick curly hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and reduce frizz. Whether you choose to do it yourself or go to a professional stylist, be sure to take it slow and use good quality tools. With the right technique and care, you can have beautiful, bouncy curls that are easy to style.
