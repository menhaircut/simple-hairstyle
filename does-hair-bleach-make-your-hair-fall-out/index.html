Does Hair Bleach Make Your Hair Fall Out

Written by Petter Oct 04, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are someone who loves to experiment with your hair, then you might have considered bleaching your hair at some point in your life. Bleaching your hair is a popular way to get a new look, but it also comes with some risks. One of the most common questions people ask when considering bleaching their hair is, "Does hair bleach make your hair fall out?"

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Bleach Your Hair
  3. Step by Step Guide to Bleaching Your Hair
  4. Tips for Bleaching Your Hair
  5. FAQs

Description

Bleaching your hair involves stripping your hair of its natural color by using chemicals that break down the melanin in your hair. This process can be damaging to your hair, and if not done correctly, it can cause your hair to fall out. However, if done correctly, you can achieve a beautiful look without any damage to your hair.

How to Bleach Your Hair

Bleaching your hair is not something that you should do on a whim. It requires a lot of preparation and research to ensure that you do it correctly. Here are the steps to bleach your hair:

Step by Step Guide to Bleaching Your Hair

Step 1: Choose the right bleach for your hair type.

Step 2: Do a strand test to see how your hair reacts to the bleach.

Step 3: Prepare your hair by washing it and detangling it.

Step 4: Apply the bleach to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

Step 5: Leave the bleach on your hair for the recommended amount of time.

Step 6: Rinse the bleach out of your hair thoroughly.

Step 7: Tone your hair to remove any brassy tones.

Step 8: Condition your hair to restore moisture.

Tips for Bleaching Your Hair

Bleaching your hair can be a daunting task, but these tips can help you achieve the results you want while minimizing the risk of damage to your hair:

  • Use a deep conditioner before and after bleaching your hair to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid washing your hair for a few days after bleaching to give it time to recover.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy.
  • Don't bleach your hair too often, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Consider going to a professional stylist if you're not confident in your ability to bleach your hair at home.

FAQs

Does hair bleach make your hair fall out?

Hair bleach can cause your hair to fall out if it's not done correctly. However, if done correctly, it's unlikely to cause any damage to your hair.

Can I bleach my hair if it's already damaged?

Bleaching your hair when it's already damaged can cause further damage and breakage. It's best to wait until your hair is healthy before bleaching it.

How often can I bleach my hair?

It's best to avoid bleaching your hair too often, as it can cause damage and breakage. Experts recommend waiting at least six weeks between bleaching sessions.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent further damage. Here are some tips for caring for your hair after bleaching:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause further damage to your hair.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it's more vulnerable to breakage.
  • Consider using a hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and protected.

In conclusion, bleaching your hair can cause damage and even hair fall, but it's not always the case. By following the proper steps and taking care of your hair after bleaching, you can achieve the look you want without any damage to your hair.
