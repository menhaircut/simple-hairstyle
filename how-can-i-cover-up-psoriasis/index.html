How Can I Cover Up Psoriasis Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Daniel Jun 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune skin condition that affects millions of people worldwide. It can cause red, scaly patches on the skin that can be both uncomfortable and unsightly. If you have psoriasis, you may be wondering how you can cover it up and feel more confident in your skin. In this article, we'll provide you with tips, tricks, and hair care advice to help you cover up psoriasis and feel more comfortable.

Description of Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a skin condition that causes skin cells to grow too quickly, leading to a buildup of cells on the surface of the skin. This buildup can cause red, scaly patches that are often itchy and painful. Psoriasis can occur anywhere on the body, but it is most commonly found on the scalp, elbows, knees, and lower back.

How to Cover Up Psoriasis

If you have psoriasis, there are several ways you can cover it up and feel more confident. Here are some tips:

1. Use Makeup

Makeup can be a great way to cover up psoriasis on the face or other visible areas of the body. Look for makeup that is specifically designed for sensitive skin, as psoriasis can be easily irritated by harsh chemicals. Use a green-tinted color corrector to neutralize the redness, then apply a foundation that matches your skin tone.

2. Wear Clothing That Covers Your Psoriasis

If your psoriasis is on your arms, legs, or other visible areas of the body, consider wearing clothing that covers it up. Long-sleeved shirts, pants, and dresses can be a great way to hide psoriasis while still looking stylish.

3. Use Topical Treatments

Topical treatments, such as creams and ointments, can be used to reduce the appearance of psoriasis. Look for products that contain salicylic acid, which can help to soften and remove the scales. You can also use moisturizers to keep your skin hydrated and reduce itching.

4. Try Self-Tanning Products

Self-tanning products can be a great way to cover up psoriasis on the legs or other areas of the body. Look for products that are specifically designed for sensitive skin, and apply them evenly to achieve a natural-looking tan.

Step-by-Step Guide to Covering Up Psoriasis

Here is a step-by-step guide to covering up psoriasis:

Step 1: Cleanse Your Skin

Start by cleansing your skin with a gentle cleanser. This will help to remove any dirt or oil that could interfere with your makeup or topical treatments.

Step 2: Apply Your Topical Treatment

If you are using a topical treatment, apply it now. This will help to reduce the appearance of psoriasis and prepare your skin for makeup.

Step 3: Apply Your Makeup

If you are using makeup to cover up psoriasis, apply it now. Start with a green-tinted color corrector to neutralize the redness, then apply a foundation that matches your skin tone.

Step 4: Use Self-Tanning Products

If you are using self-tanning products, apply them now. Be sure to apply them evenly to achieve a natural-looking tan.

Step 5: Wear Clothing That Covers Your Psoriasis

If your psoriasis is on your arms, legs, or other visible areas of the body, wear clothing that covers it up.

Tips for Covering Up Psoriasis

Here are some additional tips for covering up psoriasis:

1. Be Gentle

Psoriasis can be easily irritated, so be gentle when applying makeup or topical treatments. Avoid rubbing or scrubbing your skin, as this can make your psoriasis worse.

2. Moisturize

Keeping your skin moisturized can help to reduce itching and discomfort. Use a moisturizer that is specifically designed for sensitive skin to avoid irritating your psoriasis.

3. Use Sunscreen

Psoriasis can be exacerbated by sun exposure, so be sure to use a sunscreen with a high SPF when you are outside.

FAQ About Covering Up Psoriasis

Q: Can I Cover Up Psoriasis on My Scalp?

A: Yes, you can cover up psoriasis on your scalp by using a hat or headscarf. You can also use a shampoo that is specifically designed for psoriasis to help reduce redness and flaking.

Q: Can I Cover Up Psoriasis on My Nails?

A: Yes, you can cover up psoriasis on your nails by using nail polish or acrylic nails. However, be sure to avoid harsh chemicals that could irritate your psoriasis.

Q: Can I Cover Up Psoriasis on My Face?

A: Yes, you can cover up psoriasis on your face by using makeup. Look for products that are specifically designed for sensitive skin, and be sure to avoid harsh chemicals that could irritate your psoriasis.

Hair Care for Psoriasis

If you have psoriasis on your scalp, there are several things you can do to help manage it:

1. Use a Shampoo for Psoriasis

Look for a shampoo that is specifically designed for psoriasis. These shampoos contain ingredients that can help to reduce redness and flaking.

2. Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and hair sprays. These products can irritate your psoriasis and make it worse.

3. Be Gentle

Be gentle when brushing and styling your hair, as psoriasis can be easily irritated. Use a soft-bristled brush and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.


By following these tips and tricks, you can cover up psoriasis and feel more confident in your skin. Remember to be gentle with your skin, moisturize regularly, and avoid harsh chemicals that could irritate your psoriasis. With the right care and attention, you can manage your psoriasis and enjoy healthy, beautiful skin.
