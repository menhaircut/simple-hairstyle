What Causes Sudden Excessive Shedding In Dogs

Written by Bardi Jun 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you noticing more hair than usual around your house? Is your dog leaving behind clumps of fur on your furniture? If so, your dog may be experiencing sudden excessive shedding, which can be caused by a variety of factors. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why your dog may be shedding excessively and provide tips on how to manage it.

Stress

One of the most common causes of sudden excessive shedding in dogs is stress. Dogs can experience stress from a variety of sources, such as changes in routine, new environments, or separation anxiety. When a dog is stressed, their body releases cortisol, which can cause hair to fall out in clumps.

Managing your dog's stress levels can help reduce shedding. Providing your dog with a comfortable environment, plenty of exercise, and attention can help reduce stress. If your dog has separation anxiety, consider crate training or hiring a dog walker to help ease their anxiety.

Diet

A dog's diet can also affect their shedding. If your dog is not getting the proper nutrients, their hair may become brittle and fall out more easily. Additionally, certain foods can cause allergies, which can lead to excessive shedding.

Make sure your dog is getting a balanced diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals. Consult with your veterinarian about the best food for your dog's breed and age. If your dog has food allergies, your veterinarian may recommend a special diet.

Allergies

Allergies can cause sudden excessive shedding in dogs. Dogs can be allergic to a variety of things, such as pollen, mold, and food. When a dog is allergic to something, their body releases histamines, which can cause itching and hair loss.

If you suspect your dog has allergies, take them to the veterinarian for an allergy test. Your veterinarian may recommend medication or a special diet to manage your dog's allergies.

Infections and Diseases

Sudden excessive shedding can also be a symptom of an underlying infection or disease. Infections such as ringworm, mange, and bacterial infections can cause hair loss. Diseases such as cancer, hypothyroidism, and Cushing's disease can also cause hair loss.

If you notice your dog is shedding excessively and also has other symptoms such as lethargy, weight loss, or skin irritation, take them to the veterinarian for a check-up. Your veterinarian may recommend tests to diagnose any underlying infections or diseases.

How to Manage Excessive Shedding

While excessive shedding can be frustrating, there are steps you can take to manage it. Here are some tips:

  • Brush your dog daily to remove loose hair
  • Bathe your dog regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a deshedding tool to remove excess hair
  • Vacuum and clean your home regularly to remove hair
  • Consult with your veterinarian about supplements to promote healthy coat and skin

Tips for Reducing Shedding

Here are some additional tips for reducing shedding:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your dog's coat
  • Provide your dog with a healthy diet and plenty of water
  • Keep your dog's stress levels low
  • Provide your dog with regular exercise
  • Consider switching to a hypoallergenic dog breed

FAQs

Q: Is it normal for dogs to shed?

A: Yes, it is normal for dogs to shed. However, excessive shedding can be a sign of an underlying issue.

Q: How often should I brush my dog?

A: You should brush your dog at least once a week. Dogs with longer hair may require more frequent brushing.

Q: Can diet affect my dog's shedding?

A: Yes, diet can affect your dog's shedding. A balanced diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals can help reduce shedding.

Hair Care for Dogs

Proper hair care is essential for managing shedding in dogs. Here are some tips:

  • Brush your dog's coat regularly to remove loose hair
  • Bathe your dog every 2-3 months with a gentle shampoo
  • Trim your dog's hair regularly to prevent matting
  • Consult with your veterinarian about supplements to promote healthy skin and coat

By following these tips and consulting with your veterinarian, you can help manage your dog's shedding and keep their coat healthy and shiny.
