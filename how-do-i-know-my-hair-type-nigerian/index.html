How Do I Know My Hair Type Nigerian A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Knowing your hair type is essential in determining what products and styling techniques work best for your hair. For Nigerians, it can be challenging to identify their hair type due to the diversity of the country's population. However, with proper guidance, it's easy to determine your hair type. In this article, we'll guide you on how to know your hair type Nigerian, including tips and hair care routines to keep your hair healthy and beautiful.

How to Know Your Hair Type Nigerian

Before diving into the different hair types, it's essential to understand that Nigerians have different hair textures. Hair texture refers to the thickness or diameter of an individual strand of hair, while hair type refers to the curl pattern. Here are the steps to know your hair type Nigerian:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in determining your hair type is to wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo. This helps to remove any buildup or product residue that may affect the results. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Air Dry Your Hair

After washing, avoid using a towel to dry your hair. Instead, let your hair air dry to prevent any damage or frizz. Once your hair is dry, avoid brushing or combing it, as this can disrupt your natural curls and affect the results.

Step 3: Examine Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, examine it to determine your hair type. Here are the different hair types Nigerian:

Types of Hair Nigerian

Type 1: Straight Hair

People with straight hair have hair that is naturally straight and does not curl or wave. Straight hair is typically shiny and smooth, and it's not prone to frizz. This hair type is common among Nigerians of Asian and Caucasian descent.

Type 2: Wavy Hair

Wavy hair has a slight curl pattern, with loose waves that fall in an S-shape. This hair type is common among mixed-race Nigerians and those of Hispanic descent. Wavy hair is prone to frizz and requires proper care to maintain its shape.

Type 3: Curly Hair

Curly hair has a distinct curl pattern, with curls that range from loose to tight. This hair type is common among Nigerians of African descent. Curly hair is prone to dryness, and it requires regular moisturizing to prevent breakage and maintain its shape.

Type 4: Coily Hair

Coily hair has a tight curl pattern, with coils that range from small to tight. This hair type is common among Nigerians of African descent. Coily hair is prone to dryness, and it requires regular moisturizing to maintain its shape and prevent breakage.

Tips for Hair Care Nigerian

Regardless of your hair type, here are some essential hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly using natural oils, such as coconut oil and olive oil.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions, such as extreme cold and wind.
  • Avoid using chemical treatments, such as relaxers and perms, as they can damage your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to know your hair type Nigerian:

1. How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash your hair every other day. If you have dry hair, you can wash your hair once or twice a week.

2. Can I change my hair type?

No, you cannot change your hair type. Your hair type is determined by your genetics, and it cannot be altered.

3. Can I use the same hair products for different hair types?

No, you cannot use the same hair products for different hair types. Each hair type requires different products and styling techniques to maintain its shape and health.

Conclusion

Knowing your hair type Nigerian is essential in determining what products and techniques work best for your hair. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can identify your hair type and take the necessary steps to keep your hair healthy and beautiful. Remember, proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy hair, regardless of your hair type.
