What Happens To Your Scalp When You Go Bald

Written by Eveline Aug 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our blog about what happens to your scalp when you go bald. Losing hair can be a difficult experience, and it's important to understand the changes that occur on your scalp when you lose your hair. In this post, we will explore the causes of baldness, the changes that happen to your scalp, and how to care for your scalp when you go bald.

Table of Contents:

  • Causes of Baldness
  • Changes to Your Scalp When You Go Bald
  • Hair Care Tips for Bald Scalps
  • FAQs About Baldness

Causes of Baldness

Baldness can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, age, and hormonal changes. Male pattern baldness, the most common type of baldness, is typically caused by genetics. This type of baldness is characterized by a receding hairline and thinning hair on the crown of the head. Female pattern baldness is also caused by genetics but is less common than male pattern baldness.

Other factors that can cause baldness include stress, poor nutrition, and certain medical conditions such as alopecia areata. Chemotherapy and radiation therapy can also cause hair loss, although this hair loss is usually temporary.

Changes to Your Scalp When You Go Bald

When you go bald, the hair follicles on your scalp shrink and eventually die. This means that your scalp will have fewer hair follicles, and the ones that remain will be smaller in size. As a result, the skin on your scalp may become thinner and more sensitive.

Without hair to protect it, your scalp will also be more exposed to the elements, such as the sun and wind. This can lead to dryness, flakiness, and irritation. In some cases, baldness can also cause an increase in oil production on the scalp, which can lead to an oily scalp and dandruff.

Hair Care Tips for Bald Scalps

When you go bald, it's important to take care of your scalp to prevent dryness, flakiness, and irritation. Here are some tips for caring for your bald scalp:

  • Keep your scalp clean by washing it regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Moisturize your scalp with a non-greasy, lightweight moisturizer to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products on your scalp.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun by wearing a hat or using a sunscreen specifically designed for the scalp.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate blood flow and promote healthy hair growth.

FAQs About Baldness

Can baldness be reversed?

There is currently no cure for baldness, but there are treatments available that can help slow down or stop hair loss. These treatments include medications, hair transplants, and scalp micropigmentation.

Does wearing a hat cause baldness?

No, wearing a hat does not cause baldness. However, wearing a hat that is too tight can cause hair breakage and damage to the hair follicles, which can lead to hair loss.

Is baldness hereditary?

Yes, baldness can be hereditary. Male pattern baldness is passed down through the X chromosome, which means that it is more likely to be inherited from the mother's side of the family. Female pattern baldness is also hereditary but is less common than male pattern baldness.

Can stress cause baldness?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss, but it is usually temporary. The hair loss usually stops once the stress has been resolved.

In conclusion, going bald can be a challenging experience, but understanding the changes that occur on your scalp can help you take better care of your scalp and prevent further hair loss. By following the hair care tips outlined in this post, you can keep your scalp healthy and promote healthy hair growth. If you have any concerns about your hair loss, be sure to consult with a dermatologist or hair loss specialist.
