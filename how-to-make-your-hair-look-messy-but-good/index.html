How To Make Your Hair Look Messy But Good

Written by Petter Oct 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you want to achieve that effortless, tousled look that screams I woke up like this, then you need to know how to make your hair look messy but good. This hairstyle is perfect for those who want to achieve a laid-back, carefree look that exudes confidence and style. Whether you have long or short hair, straight or curly, there are ways to achieve this look that will work for you. Here are some tips and tricks to help you master the art of messy-but-good hair.

Description

Messy hair has been in fashion for a while now, and it's not hard to see why. It's a perfect way to achieve a relaxed and effortless look without spending hours styling your hair. But achieving that perfect messy-but-good look can be a challenge. You don't want to look like you just rolled out of bed, but you also don't want to look like you spent hours trying to make your hair look messy. The key is to strike the right balance between a relaxed and polished look.

How to

The first step in achieving messy-but-good hair is to start with a good base. This means washing your hair and applying a styling product that will give your hair texture and hold. You can use a mousse, texturizing spray, or a sea salt spray to achieve this. Once you have applied your styling product, it's time to start creating your messy-but-good look.

Step-by-Step

1. Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser attachment to create natural-looking waves and curls.

2. Part your hair to the side or create a messy center part.

3. Use a curling iron or wand to create loose waves or curls. Don't worry about making them perfect, the goal is to create a natural-looking wave.

4. Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create volume.

5. Apply a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add texture and hold to your hair.

6. Finish off with a light hairspray to hold your look in place.

Tips

- Don't overdo it with the product. Use just enough to create texture and hold without weighing down your hair.

- Use a light touch when curling your hair. The goal is to create natural-looking waves, not perfect curls.

- Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create volume. Don't use a brush or comb, as this will create a more polished look.

- If you have bangs, you can create a messy-but-good look by sweeping them to the side and tousling them with your fingers.

FAQ

Q: Can I achieve a messy-but-good look with short hair?

A: Yes, you can achieve a messy-but-good look with short hair. Use a texturizing product to create volume and tousle your hair with your fingers to create a messy look.

Q: How often should I wash my hair to achieve a messy-but-good look?

A: It's best to wash your hair every other day to achieve a messy-but-good look. This will allow your hair to maintain the natural oils that will help create texture and hold.

Hair Care

When going for a messy-but-good look, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

- Avoid using heat styling tools every day to prevent damage to your hair.

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.

- Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve a messy-but-good look that will have you looking effortlessly stylish and chic. So go ahead and embrace your natural waves and curls, and give this hairstyle a try.
