How To Slick Back African American Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking for a sleek and sophisticated hairstyle, slicking back your hair can be a great option. However, if you have African American hair, you may be wondering how to achieve this look without damaging your hair or causing breakage. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to slick back your hair and provide some helpful tips for maintaining healthy hair.

How to Slick Back African American Hair

Slicking back African American hair requires a bit of work, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and sleek look. Here are some steps to get you started:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you begin, make sure your hair is clean and free of any product buildup. Shampoo and condition your hair as usual, then towel dry gently.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

To help protect your hair from damage and keep it moisturized, apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair. This will also help make it easier to comb through your hair later on.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb, gently comb your hair from root to tip to remove any tangles or knots. This will also help distribute the leave-in conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Apply a Styling Gel or Pomade

Apply a small amount of styling gel or pomade to your hair, starting at the back of your head and working your way forward. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, making sure to focus on the roots and ends.

Step 5: Comb Your Hair Back

Using a fine-tooth comb, comb your hair straight back from your forehead. Make sure to comb your hair in one direction to create a smooth and sleek look.

Step 6: Style as Desired

Once you've slicked back your hair, you can style it as desired. You can keep it simple and slicked back, or you can add some volume to your hair by using a blow dryer and round brush to create a slight wave or curl.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you slick back your African American hair:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to protect and moisturize your hair.
  3. Comb your hair with a wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles.
  4. Apply a styling gel or pomade to your hair.
  5. Comb your hair straight back with a fine-tooth comb.
  6. Style as desired using a blow dryer and round brush.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair while slicking it back:

  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and cause it to look greasy.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using a blow dryer or other heat styling tools.
  • Limit the use of heat styling tools to avoid damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

FAQs

Q: Can I slick back my hair if it's short?

A: Yes, you can slick back short hair by using a small amount of product and combing your hair straight back from your forehead.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Q: What kind of styling product should I use?

A: You can use a styling gel, pomade, or hair wax to slick back your hair. It's important to choose a product that works well with your hair type and provides the hold and shine you're looking for.

Hair Care Products for Slicked Back Hair

If you're looking for hair care products to help maintain your slicked back hairstyle, here are some options to consider:

  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Styling gel or pomade
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hair wax
  • Blow dryer
  • Round brush

By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a sleek and stylish look with your slicked back African American hair. Just remember to take care of your hair by using the right products and techniques, and you'll be sure to turn heads with your new hairstyle.
