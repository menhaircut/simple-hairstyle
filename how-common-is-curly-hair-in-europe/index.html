How Common Is Curly Hair In Europe - A Comprehensive Guide

Curly hair has been a popular hairstyle for centuries, and it is a common sight in many parts of the world. However, when it comes to Europe, there is a common misconception that straight hair is the norm. In this article, we will explore the truth about how common curly hair is in Europe, along with some tips on how to take care of curly hair.

Table of Content

  • What Percentage of Europeans Have Curly Hair?
  • Why Do Europeans Have Straight Hair?
  • How to Take Care of Curly Hair
  • FAQs

What Percentage of Europeans Have Curly Hair?

Contrary to popular belief, curly hair is quite common in Europe. In fact, according to a study by the World Atlas of Hair, around 45% of Europeans have straight hair, while 40% have wavy hair, and 15% have curly hair. This means that almost one in every six Europeans has curly hair, which is a significant proportion.

In addition to this, curly hair is more common in certain regions of Europe, such as the Mediterranean, where around 50% of the population has curly hair. Similarly, in countries like Spain, Italy and Greece, curly hair is considered to be the norm.

Why Do Europeans Have Straight Hair?

The reason why many people believe that straight hair is the norm in Europe is that historically, people with straight hair were considered to be more attractive and desirable. This is because straight hair was associated with wealth and status, as it was easier to maintain and style.

However, this does not mean that Europeans are genetically predisposed to having straight hair. In fact, the texture of one's hair is determined by a combination of genetic and environmental factors, such as climate, diet, and hair care practices.

How to Take Care of Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it looking healthy and defined. Here are some tips to help you take care of your curly hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip the hair of its natural oils, which can make curly hair dry and frizzy. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair moisturized.

2. Condition regularly

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it is essential to condition it regularly to keep it hydrated. Use a deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

Curly hair is prone to tangles, so use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. Do not use a brush, as it can break your hair and cause frizz.

4. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools can damage curly hair and make it frizzy. Avoid using blow dryers, straighteners, and curling irons on your hair.

FAQs

1. Is curly hair common in Europe?

Yes, curly hair is quite common in Europe. Around 15% of Europeans have curly hair, while 40% have wavy hair.

2. Why do Europeans have straight hair?

Historically, straight hair was considered to be more attractive and desirable, which is why people with straight hair were considered to be of higher status. However, this does not mean that Europeans are genetically predisposed to having straight hair.

3. How do I take care of my curly hair?

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and defined. Use a sulfate-free shampoo, condition regularly, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid heat styling tools.

4. Can I straighten my curly hair?

Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but it is essential to use a heat protectant spray and avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can damage your hair.

In conclusion, curly hair is quite common in Europe, and almost one in every six Europeans has curly hair. Whether you have curly hair or not, it is essential to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great.
