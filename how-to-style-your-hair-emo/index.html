How To Style Your Hair Emo A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to style your hair emo! Whether you're a long-time fan of the emo style or just looking to switch things up, this guide will give you all the tips and tricks you need to perfect the look. From selecting the right hair products to creating the perfect fringe, we've got you covered. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Selecting the Right Hair Products
  2. Cutting Your Hair
  3. Coloring Your Hair
  4. Styling Your Fringe
  5. Adding Texture to Your Hair
  6. Accessorizing Your Hair
  7. Maintaining Your Hair
  8. Final Thoughts

Selecting the Right Hair Products

Before you can style your hair emo, you need to make sure you have the right products. Start by selecting a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type. If you have oily hair, look for a clarifying shampoo that will remove excess oil. If you have dry hair, a moisturizing conditioner will help keep your hair hydrated.

Next, you'll need to choose a styling product. Emo hair typically requires a strong hold product, such as a hair gel or pomade. Look for a product that is designed to add volume and texture to your hair.

Cutting Your Hair

The emo style is all about layers and texture, so you'll need to get a haircut that reflects that. When you're at the salon, ask your stylist for choppy layers and a side-swept fringe. The fringe should be long enough to cover your forehead, but not so long that it gets in your eyes.

If you're cutting your hair at home, be sure to use sharp scissors and take your time. Start by sectioning your hair and cutting each section individually. Emo hair is all about the imperfections, so don't worry too much about making it look perfect.

Coloring Your Hair

Emo hair is often characterized by its bold, vibrant colors. If you're feeling daring, consider dyeing your hair a bright shade, such as red, blue, or purple. Keep in mind, though, that dyeing your hair can be damaging, so be sure to use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

If you're not ready to commit to a full dye job, consider adding highlights or lowlights to your hair. This will give your hair dimension and add to the overall emo look.

Styling Your Fringe

The fringe is a key component of the emo look, so you'll want to make sure it's styled just right. Start by blow-drying your fringe using a round brush. This will help add volume and texture to your hair.

Next, use a hair straightener to create a slight curve in your fringe. This will give it that classic emo swoop. If you have bangs that are too long, you can trim them yourself using a pair of scissors.

Adding Texture to Your Hair

Emo hair is all about texture, so you'll want to add some to your own hair. Start by applying a small amount of styling product to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair, creating a messy, textured look.

If you have straight hair, consider using a curling iron to create loose, messy waves. This will add even more texture to your hair.

Accessorizing Your Hair

Emo hair is often accessorized with hair clips, bows, and other hair accessories. Consider adding some to your own hair to complete the look. You can also experiment with different hair styles, such as braids or messy buns.

Maintaining Your Hair

To keep your emo hair looking great, you'll need to maintain it properly. Be sure to wash your hair regularly using a color-safe shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can be damaging to your hair.

You may also want to consider getting regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Final Thoughts

Styling your hair emo takes some effort, but the result is a bold, unique look that's sure to turn heads. With the right products, tools, and techniques, you can create the perfect emo hairstyle that reflects your personality and individuality.

Remember, the key to the emo look is all about embracing imperfection and making it your own. So, don't be afraid to experiment and have fun with your hair!
