How To Cut Bangs For Round Face At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Nov 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have a round face and are looking for a way to change up your hairstyle, cutting bangs may be the perfect solution. But before you grab a pair of scissors and start snipping away, it's important to know what type of bangs will flatter your face shape. In this article, we'll show you how to cut bangs for round face at home, including tips on choosing the right style and maintaining your new look.

Choosing the Right Bangs for a Round Face

When it comes to bangs for round faces, there are a few styles that work particularly well. Avoid straight, blunt bangs that cut across your forehead, as these can make your face appear even rounder. Instead, opt for side-swept bangs that angle diagonally across your forehead, or choppy, textured bangs that frame your face.

Another option is to go for curtain bangs, which are parted down the middle and frame your face on either side. These work particularly well if you have a longer face, as they can help balance out your features. Consult with your hairstylist or do some research online to find the best bangs for your face shape.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your bangs, you'll need a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or bobby pins
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Comb your hair straight down, ensuring that it's free of tangles. Use hair clips or bobby pins to pin back the rest of your hair, leaving only the section you want to cut as your bangs.

Using the spray bottle, mist the section of hair you want to cut with water. This will help prevent your hair from becoming too static and easier to cut.

Cutting Your Bangs

Now it's time to start cutting. Begin by combing your hair straight down again, and use your fingers to separate the section you want to cut from the rest of your hair. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use your other hand to hold the scissors at a slight angle.

Start by cutting a small amount of hair at a time. Remember, you can always trim more later. Cut at a diagonal angle to create side-swept bangs, or use a choppy technique to create textured bangs.

Continue cutting until you've achieved the desired length and style. Make sure to keep checking your progress in the mirror, and take breaks as needed to avoid cutting too much at once.

Styling Your Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs, it's time to style them. Blow-dry your bangs using a round brush, angling them to the side if you've opted for side-swept bangs. You can also use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or waves.

If you want to add some texture, use a bit of texturizing spray or dry shampoo. Remember, the key to styling your bangs is to keep them looking natural and effortless.

Maintaining Your Bangs

It's important to maintain your bangs to keep them looking their best. Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them at the desired length, and use hair clippers to remove any split ends. You can also use a bit of hair wax or pomade to keep your bangs in place throughout the day.

Tips for Cutting Bangs at Home

Cutting bangs at home can be intimidating, but it's also a great way to save money and experiment with your look. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with small cuts, and trim a little at a time.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take breaks as needed to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Check your progress in the mirror frequently.
  • Don't try to cut your bangs when you're in a rush or feeling stressed.

FAQs

Can I cut bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut bangs even if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so you may want to cut your bangs a bit longer than you would if you had straight hair.

How do I know if bangs will look good on me?

The best way to know if bangs will look good on you is to consult with your hairstylist. They can help you determine the best style for your face shape and hair type. You can also experiment with clip-in bangs or try on wigs to get an idea of how you'll look with bangs.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs?

If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don't panic. You can always trim them a bit shorter, or try styling them differently to hide any mistakes. If all else fails, you can always visit a hairstylist to have your bangs fixed.

Hair Care for Bangs

To keep your bangs looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat when styling. You can also use a bit of dry shampoo to keep your bangs looking fresh between washes.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy beautiful, flattering bangs that complement your round face shape.
