Why Do Dogs Lose Large Patches Of Hair

Written by Christine Aug 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

As a dog owner, it's important to keep an eye on your furry friend's coat. A dog's coat can tell you a lot about their overall health and wellbeing. Unfortunately, it's not uncommon for dogs to lose large patches of hair. This can be a sign of an underlying health issue that needs to be addressed. In this article, we'll explore some of the reasons why dogs lose hair and what you can do to help them.

Reasons Why Dogs Lose Hair

There are several reasons why dogs may lose large patches of hair. Some of the most common reasons include:

1. Allergies

Dogs can develop allergies to a variety of things, including certain foods, medications, and environmental factors like pollen and dust. Allergies can cause your dog to scratch and bite at their skin, leading to hair loss. Allergies can also cause inflammation and irritation of the skin, which can lead to hair loss.

2. Parasites

Parasites like fleas, ticks, and mites can cause hair loss in dogs. These pests can irritate the skin and cause your dog to scratch and bite, leading to hair loss. In addition, some parasites like mange mites can burrow into the skin and cause severe hair loss and skin damage.

3. Infections

Bacterial, fungal, and yeast infections can all cause hair loss in dogs. These infections can cause inflammation and irritation of the skin, leading to hair loss. In addition, some infections like ringworm can cause circular patches of hair loss.

4. Hormonal Imbalances

Hormonal imbalances like hypothyroidism and Cushing's disease can cause hair loss in dogs. These conditions can disrupt the normal growth cycle of your dog's hair, leading to hair loss.

5. Stress

Stress can cause hair loss in dogs, just like it can in humans. Dogs that are stressed may excessively lick or chew at their fur, leading to hair loss. In addition, stress can weaken the immune system, making dogs more susceptible to infections and other health issues that can cause hair loss.

How to Help a Dog with Hair Loss

If you notice that your dog is losing large patches of hair, it's important to take them to the vet for a proper diagnosis. Your vet can determine the underlying cause of your dog's hair loss and recommend a treatment plan. Some common treatments for hair loss in dogs include:

1. Medications

If your dog has an infection or parasite infestation, your vet may prescribe medications like antibiotics or antifungal drugs to help treat the underlying issue.

2. Change in Diet

If your dog has a food allergy or intolerance, your vet may recommend a change in diet to help alleviate symptoms and reduce hair loss.

3. Supplements

Your vet may recommend supplements like omega-3 fatty acids or biotin to help improve your dog's coat health and promote hair regrowth.

4. Environmental Changes

If your dog is allergic to certain environmental factors like pollen or dust, your vet may recommend environmental changes like air filters or hypoallergenic bedding to help reduce symptoms and promote hair regrowth.

Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Hair Loss in Dogs

If your dog is experiencing hair loss, here's a step-by-step guide to help you address the issue:

Step 1: Take Your Dog to the Vet

The first step in treating hair loss in dogs is to take them to the vet. Your vet can determine the underlying cause of the hair loss and recommend a treatment plan.

Step 2: Follow Your Vet's Treatment Plan

Your vet may recommend medications, dietary changes, supplements, or environmental changes to help address your dog's hair loss. It's important to follow their recommendations closely to ensure the best possible outcome.

Step 3: Monitor Your Dog's Progress

As your dog undergoes treatment, monitor their progress closely. Keep an eye on their coat to see if hair is regrowing and watch for any signs of improvement or worsening of symptoms.

Step 4: Follow Up with Your Vet

After your dog completes their treatment plan, follow up with your vet to ensure that their hair loss has been fully addressed. Your vet may recommend ongoing maintenance or monitoring to ensure that the issue does not recur.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss in Dogs

While some causes of hair loss in dogs are out of your control, there are some things you can do to help prevent hair loss in your furry friend:

1. Keep Up with Regular Grooming

Regular grooming can help keep your dog's coat healthy and prevent tangling and matting that can lead to hair loss. Brush your dog regularly and bathe them as needed.

2. Use High-Quality Products

When it comes to grooming products, use high-quality, gentle products that are specifically designed for dogs. Avoid using human shampoo or other products that may be too harsh for your dog's skin.

3. Keep Your Dog's Living Space Clean

Clean your dog's living space regularly to help reduce the risk of infections and infestations that can cause hair loss. Wash bedding and toys regularly and vacuum frequently.

Frequently Asked Questions About Dog Hair Loss

Q: Is hair loss in dogs normal?

A: Some shedding is normal in dogs, but large patches of hair loss are not. If you notice that your dog is losing hair, it's important to take them to the vet for a proper diagnosis.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss in dogs?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss in dogs. Dogs that are stressed may excessively lick or chew at their fur, leading to hair loss.

Q: What should I do if my dog is losing hair?

A: If your dog is losing hair, take them to the vet for a proper diagnosis. Your vet can determine the underlying cause of the hair loss and recommend a treatment plan.

Proper Hair Care for Dogs

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your dog's coat healthy and preventing hair loss. Here are some tips for proper hair care for dogs:

1. Brush Your Dog Regularly

Regular brushing helps keep your dog's coat healthy and prevents tangling and matting that can lead to hair loss. Brush your dog at least once a week, or more often if they have a long coat.

2. Bathe Your Dog as Needed

Regular bathing helps keep your dog's coat clean and healthy. However, avoid bathing your dog too frequently, as this can strip their coat of natural oils and cause dryness and irritation.

3. Use High-Quality Grooming Products

When it comes to grooming products, use high-quality, gentle products that are specifically designed for dogs. Avoid using human shampoo or other products that may be too harsh for your dog's skin.

4. Keep Your Dog's Living Space Clean

Clean your dog's living space regularly to help reduce the risk of infections and infestations that can cause hair loss. Wash bedding and toys regularly and vacuum frequently.

If you notice that your dog is losing large patches of hair, it's important to take them to the vet for a proper diagnosis. With the right treatment and care, you can help your furry friend get back to their healthy, happy self.
