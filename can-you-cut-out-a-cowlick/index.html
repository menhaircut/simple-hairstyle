Can You Cut Out A Cowlick A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Aug 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cowlicks can be a real pain to deal with, and if you're someone who has one, you know just how frustrating it can be to style your hair. Cowlicks are sections of hair that grow in a different direction than the rest of your hair, causing a visible bump or swirl. In this article, we'll answer the question "can you cut out a cowlick?" and provide you with tips on how to manage and style your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Cut Out a Cowlick
  • Step By Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing a Cowlick
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

A cowlick is a section of hair that grows in a different direction than the rest of your hair, causing a visible bump or swirl. They are commonly found at the crown of the head, but can also appear at the hairline or in other areas. Cowlicks can be caused by genetics, but can also be exacerbated by improper hair care or styling.

Can You Cut Out a Cowlick?

The short answer is no, you cannot cut out a cowlick. Since cowlicks are caused by the direction in which your hair grows, cutting it won't do anything to change its growth pattern. However, there are ways to manage and style your cowlick to make it less noticeable.

Step By Step Guide

Here are some steps you can take to manage and style your cowlick:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair in the opposite direction of your cowlick, using a round brush to smooth it out.
  3. Apply a styling product, like a gel or wax, to your hair and comb it through.
  4. Use a hair dryer to dry your hair while using a brush to smooth it out.
  5. If your cowlick is still visible, try using a flat iron to straighten it out.
  6. Finish with a hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Managing a Cowlick

Here are some additional tips to help you manage your cowlick:

  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find one that works best for your cowlick. Some hairstyles, like a side part, can help to conceal it.
  • Don't overuse styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make your cowlick more noticeable.
  • If you have a cowlick at your hairline, try blow drying it straight down to help conceal it.
  • Consider getting a shorter haircut, as longer hair can make cowlicks more difficult to manage.

FAQ

Can a cowlick go away on its own?

No, cowlicks are a natural part of your hair growth pattern and won't go away on their own. However, they can become less noticeable with proper hair care and styling.

Can you use a hair straightener to get rid of a cowlick?

Yes, a hair straightener can be used to help straighten out a cowlick. However, be careful not to use it too often, as it can damage your hair.

Can you train a cowlick to go in a different direction?

No, you cannot train a cowlick to go in a different direction. However, you can manage it with proper styling techniques.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help manage your cowlick:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and manageable.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as this can exacerbate your cowlick.

Conclusion

While you can't cut out a cowlick, there are ways to manage and style your hair to make it less noticeable. Experiment with different hairstyles and styling products to find what works best for you, and remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and manageable.
