The Best Hair Extensions For Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 10, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking to add length or volume to your curly hair? Hair extensions can be a great solution, but with so many types of extensions available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one for your curls. In this article, we’ll go over the best types of hair extensions for curly hair, how to care for them, and some frequently asked questions.

  • How to Choose the Right Hair Extensions for Curly Hair

Types of Hair Extensions for Curly Hair

When it comes to hair extensions for curly hair, there are a few types to choose from:

Tape-In Extensions

Tape-in extensions are a popular choice for curly hair because they lie flat against the scalp and blend seamlessly with natural curls. They’re made of wefts with adhesive strips that are attached to small sections of your hair. They’re easy to apply and remove, but they need to be retaped every 6-8 weeks.

Sew-In Extensions

Sew-in extensions are also known as weaves. They’re applied by braiding your natural hair into cornrows and sewing the extensions onto the braids. They’re a durable option that can last up to two months, but they can be heavy and uncomfortable for some people.

Clip-In Extensions

Clip-in extensions are a temporary solution that’s easy to apply and remove. They’re made of wefts with small clips that attach to your natural hair. They’re ideal for special occasions or when you want to switch up your look without committing to permanent extensions.

How to Choose the Right Hair Extensions for Curly Hair

Choosing the right hair extensions for curly hair can be tricky, but there are a few things to consider:

  • Texture: Look for extensions that match the texture of your natural curls. If your curls are tight and kinky, choose extensions with a similar texture. If your curls are loose and wavy, choose extensions with a looser curl pattern.
  • Length: Decide how much length you want to add to your hair. Tape-in and sew-in extensions can add significant length, while clip-in extensions are best for adding volume or highlights.
  • Color: Choose extensions that match your natural hair color or a shade lighter for a highlighted effect.
  • Budget: Hair extensions can be expensive, so decide on a budget before you start shopping. Clip-in extensions are the most affordable option, while sew-in extensions are the most expensive.

Step-by-Step Guide to Applying Hair Extensions on Curly Hair

Here’s a basic guide to applying hair extensions on curly hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup.
  2. Section your hair into small sections and tease the roots to create a base for the extensions.
  3. Apply the extensions one weft at a time, starting at the nape of your neck and working your way up. For tape-in extensions, remove the backing from the adhesive strip and press the weft onto your hair. For sew-in extensions, sew the weft onto your braids. For clip-in extensions, open the clip and attach it to your natural hair.
  4. Blend the extensions with your natural hair by gently combing through the curls.
  5. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Extensions

Here are a few tips for maintaining your hair extensions:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out the extensions.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on the extensions to prevent damage.
  • Brush your extensions gently with a wide-tooth comb to avoid tangling.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair to prevent matting.
  • Get regular touch-ups to maintain the appearance of your extensions.

FAQs About Hair Extensions for Curly Hair

Q: Can I dye my hair extensions?

A: It depends on the type of extensions. Some extensions can be dyed, while others cannot. Check with the manufacturer before dyeing your extensions.

Q: How long do hair extensions last?

A: The lifespan of hair extensions depends on the type of extensions, how well they’re maintained, and how often they’re used. Tape-in extensions can last up to two months with proper care, while sew-in extensions can last up to two months.

Q: Can I swim with hair extensions?

A: Yes, you can swim with hair extensions, but it’s important to rinse them thoroughly with clean water after swimming in saltwater or chlorine.

Hair Care for Curly Hair with Extensions

Curly hair with extensions requires special care to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out the extensions.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on the extensions to prevent damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair to prevent matting.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the health of your natural hair.

Now that you know the best types of hair extensions for curly hair, how to care for them, and some frequently asked questions, you’re ready to rock your new look!
