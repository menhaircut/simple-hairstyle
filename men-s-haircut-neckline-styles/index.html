Men s Haircut Neckline Styles A Guide To Different Options

Written by Eveline Jun 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to getting a men's haircut, one of the most important decisions to make is what neckline style to go for. The neckline is the part of the haircut that frames the back of the neck, and there are several different options to choose from depending on your personal style and preferences. In this guide, we'll take a look at some of the most popular men's haircut neckline styles and how to achieve them.

Tapered Neckline

A tapered neckline is a popular and versatile option that works well for a variety of hair lengths and styles. With a tapered neckline, the hair gradually gets shorter as it approaches the neck, creating a clean and polished look. This style is often used for more formal haircuts, but can also be adapted for a more casual look.

To achieve a tapered neckline, your barber or stylist will use clippers to gradually fade the hair from the back of the head down to the neck. The length of the fade can be adjusted to suit your preferences, with shorter fades creating a more dramatic look and longer fades producing a more subtle effect.

Blocked Neckline

A blocked neckline is a more traditional style that creates a clean and defined edge at the back of the neck. With a blocked neckline, the hair is cut in a straight line across the back of the neck, creating a distinct shape that contrasts with the rest of the haircut.

To achieve a blocked neckline, your barber or stylist will use clippers or scissors to create a straight line across the back of the neck. This style works well with shorter haircuts and can be adjusted to suit your personal style preferences.

Rounded Neckline

A rounded neckline is a softer and more natural-looking option that creates a curved edge at the back of the neck. With a rounded neckline, the hair is cut in a gentle curve that follows the shape of the neckline, creating a more organic and relaxed look.

To achieve a rounded neckline, your barber or stylist will use scissors or clippers to create a gentle curve at the back of the neck. This style works well with longer haircuts and can be adjusted to suit your personal preferences.

How to Achieve Different Neckline Styles

The best way to achieve different neckline styles is to work with a skilled barber or stylist who can help you achieve the look you want. When discussing your haircut with your stylist, be sure to mention the type of neckline you're interested in and ask for their recommendations on how to achieve it.

Depending on the style you're going for, your barber or stylist may use clippers, scissors, or a combination of both to achieve the desired effect. They may also use different techniques, such as fading or blending, to create a seamless transition between the neckline and the rest of the haircut.

Tips for Maintaining Your Neckline

Once you've achieved your desired neckline style, it's important to maintain it between haircuts to keep it looking sharp. Here are a few tips for maintaining your neckline:

  • Trim the hair around your neckline regularly to prevent it from growing out of shape.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair and scalp healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh styling products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

FAQs about Men's Haircut Neckline Styles

Q: What neckline style is best for a short haircut?

A: A blocked neckline is a classic choice for a short haircut, as it creates a clean and defined edge that complements the short length of the hair.

Q: Can I change my neckline style between haircuts?

A: Yes, you can change your neckline style between haircuts by discussing your preferences with your barber or stylist. Keep in mind that it may take a few haircuts to achieve your desired neckline style if you're making a significant change.

Q: How often should I get my neckline trimmed?

A: It's a good idea to get your neckline trimmed every 2-3 weeks to keep it looking sharp and well-maintained.

Hair Care Tips for a Healthy Neckline

In addition to maintaining your neckline style between haircuts, it's important to take care of your hair and scalp to promote healthy growth and prevent damage. Here are a few tips for maintaining a healthy neckline:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to remove buildup and keep your scalp clean.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as blow dryers or straighteners, on a regular basis as this can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and working with a skilled barber or stylist, you can achieve a neckline style that complements your personal style and keeps you looking sharp and well-groomed.
