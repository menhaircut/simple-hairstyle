How to Cut Curtain Bangs on Curly Hair

Curtain bangs are a popular hairstyle trend that can add a touch of glamour to any look. These bangs are characterized by their parted, wispy, and face-framing appearance. Cutting curtain bangs on curly hair can seem daunting, but with the right technique, it can be done easily.

Materials You'll Need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Optional: mirror, hair elastics

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut curtain bangs on curly hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would. You want your hair to be in its natural state before you start cutting.
  2. Part your hair in the middle. Use a comb to create a straight part down the middle of your head.
  3. Separate the hair you want to cut into a triangle shape. Use your comb to create a triangle shape at the front of your head. This is where you will cut your bangs.
  4. Clip the rest of your hair back. Use hair clips to secure the rest of your hair away from the section you're cutting.
  5. Spray the section of hair with water. This will help to make it easier to cut.
  6. Comb the section of hair straight down. Make sure it's as smooth as possible.
  7. Hold the hair between your fingers where you want to cut. Start by cutting a little bit at a time. Remember, you can always cut more but you can't put it back.
  8. Cut in a straight line across. Be careful not to cut too much, especially near the edges.
  9. Repeat on the other side. Make sure both sides are even.
  10. Style your bangs as desired. You can leave them as is or use a curling iron or flat iron to style them.

Tips for Cutting Curtain Bangs on Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own curtain bangs:

  • Start with small cuts. It's better to take your time and cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Don't cut too close to the edges. You want to avoid cutting too close to your hairline or ears.
  • Consider your hair type. If you have very curly hair, you may need to cut your bangs a bit longer to account for shrinkage.
  • Don't cut your bangs when they're wet. Wet hair can appear longer than it is when it dries, so it's better to cut when your hair is dry.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curtain bangs on curly hair:

Do I need to go to a salon to cut my curtain bangs?

No, you can cut your own curtain bangs at home. Just make sure you have the right tools and follow the instructions carefully.

Can I cut my curtain bangs when my hair is wet?

We recommend cutting your bangs when your hair is dry. Wet hair can appear longer than it is when it dries, so it's better to cut when your hair is dry.

What if I mess up my bangs?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a salon to have them fixed or try to fix them yourself by trimming them little by little.

Hair Care for Curly Hair with Bangs

Now that you've cut your curtain bangs, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling your hair too often. This can cause damage and dry out your hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

With these tips and Brad Mondo's guide, you'll be able to cut your own curtain bangs on curly hair with confidence. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have beautiful, face-framing bangs in no time!
