Are Scissors Better Than Clippers A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to haircuts, one of the biggest debates in the industry is whether scissors or clippers are better. While both tools have their own advantages, the answer ultimately depends on the individual's hair type, style preference, and skill level of the stylist. In this article, we'll dive deep into the topic and provide you with a comprehensive guide on the pros and cons of using scissors and clippers for haircuts.

How To Decide Between Scissors and Clippers?

Before we dive into the pros and cons of each tool, it's important to understand the difference between them. Scissors are used for cutting individual strands of hair, while clippers are used for cutting large sections of hair at once. When deciding between scissors and clippers, consider the following factors:

  • Hair type (thick, thin, curly, straight)
  • Haircut style (long, short, layered, textured)
  • Skill level of the stylist
  • Personal preference

Pros and Cons of Scissors

Pros:

  • Precision cutting for more control and accuracy
  • Allows for more creativity and versatility in haircut styles
  • Suitable for all hair types and lengths

Cons:

  • Requires more skill and practice to use effectively
  • Takes longer to cut hair compared to clippers
  • Not ideal for cutting large sections of hair

Pros and Cons of Clippers

Pros:

  • Efficient and fast for cutting large sections of hair
  • Easier to use for beginners and less skilled stylists
  • Can create clean, precise lines for specific haircut styles

Cons:

  • Less precise than scissors, which can result in uneven cuts
  • Not suitable for all hair types and lengths
  • Can create a uniform, robotic look rather than a natural texture

Step-By-Step Guide for Cutting Hair with Scissors

If you've decided to use scissors for your haircut, follow these steps:

  1. Wash and dry the hair thoroughly.
  2. Section the hair into manageable parts.
  3. Start at the back of the head and work your way up.
  4. Hold the hair with your non-cutting hand and use the scissors to cut the desired length.
  5. Continue cutting small sections of hair until the desired style is achieved.
  6. Blend the hair together using thinning shears or a razor if necessary.
  7. Finish the haircut by trimming any stray hairs and checking for evenness.

Step-By-Step Guide for Cutting Hair with Clippers

If you've decided to use clippers for your haircut, follow these steps:

  1. Wash and dry the hair thoroughly.
  2. Choose the appropriate clipper guard size based on the desired length.
  3. Start at the back of the head and work your way up.
  4. Use the clippers to cut the hair in long, smooth strokes.
  5. Repeat the process until the desired length is achieved.
  6. Clean up the edges using scissors or a trimmer.
  7. Finish the haircut by trimming any stray hairs and checking for evenness.

Tips for Cutting Hair with Scissors or Clippers

No matter which tool you choose, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Always start with clean, dry hair.
  • Invest in high-quality scissors or clippers for the best results.
  • Practice on mannequin heads or willing volunteers before cutting a client's hair.
  • Take breaks and rest your hands to avoid fatigue and cramping.
  • Use a comb to guide your cutting and keep the hair even.

FAQ About Scissors and Clippers

Q: Can you use scissors and clippers together?

A: Yes, many stylists use both tools to achieve a specific style or texture.

Q: Is it better to cut wet or dry hair?

A: It's generally easier to cut wet hair, but some stylists prefer cutting dry hair for more precision.

Q: How often should you replace your scissors or clippers?

A: It depends on how often you use them and how well you maintain them. Generally, scissors should be sharpened every 6-12 months and clippers should have their blades replaced every 6-12 months.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain the style and health of the hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner suited for your hair type and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims every 6-8 weeks can also help keep the hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

When it comes to the age-old debate of scissors vs clippers, there's no clear winner. Both tools have their own advantages and disadvantages, and the choice ultimately comes down to personal preference and the client's hair type and style. By following the tips and guidelines in this article, you can feel confident in your ability to use either tool effectively and achieve a great haircut.
