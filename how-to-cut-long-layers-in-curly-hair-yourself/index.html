How To Cut Long Layers In Curly Hair Yourself

Written by Petter Jun 20, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon every few months just to get your curly hair trimmed? Well, fear not! With a few tips and tricks, you can learn how to cut long layers in curly hair yourself. Follow this step-by-step guide and get ready to rock your new haircut!

Description

Curly hair can be tricky to cut, especially when it comes to layering. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. This guide will teach you everything you need to know about cutting long layers in curly hair yourself, including tips for maintaining your new haircut.

How to Cut Long Layers in Curly Hair Yourself

Step 1: Gather your supplies

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary supplies on hand. You will need:

  • A pair of sharp scissors
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A comb
  • A mirror

Step 2: Wash and detangle your hair

Start by washing your hair and detangling it with a wide-tooth comb. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 3: Divide your hair into sections

Divide your hair into four sections: two on top and two on the bottom. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Start cutting

Start with one section at a time. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the ends at a slight angle, moving upwards towards the top of your head. Continue cutting small sections of hair, moving from the back to the front of your head.

Step 5: Check your progress

After you finish one section, let down the next section and repeat the process. Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure your layers are even.

Step 6: Finish with the top sections

Once you have finished the bottom sections, move on to the top sections. Use the same technique to cut your layers, but make sure to blend them with the bottom layers.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair damp while cutting.
  • Make sure to cut at a slight angle to create a natural-looking layer.
  • When in doubt, cut less rather than more. You can always go back and trim more later.

FAQ

Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is. However, if you prefer to cut your hair when it's wet, make sure to cut less than you would if it were dry.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to trim your curly hair every 3-4 months to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, it's important to use sharp hair cutting scissors to achieve a clean cut and prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, make sure to take good care of it to maintain your new look. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Instead, opt for air-drying your hair or using a diffuser. You can also use hair masks and oils to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Cutting long layers in curly hair yourself may seem intimidating at first, but with a little practice, you can become a pro. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. You got this!
