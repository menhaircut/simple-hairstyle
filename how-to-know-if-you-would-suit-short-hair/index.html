How To Know If You Would Suit Short Hair Tips And Tricks

Short hair is a bold and daring choice, and it's not for everyone. If you're considering a major chop, it's important to know if you would suit short hair. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know to make an informed decision, including how to determine your face shape, the different types of short hairstyles, and how to care for your new 'do.

  • Determining Your Face Shape
  • Types of Short Hairstyles

Determining Your Face Shape

Before deciding on a short hairstyle, it's important to determine your face shape. This will help you choose a cut that flatters your features and brings out your best qualities. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and long.

To determine your face shape, start by pulling your hair back and looking in the mirror. Use a tape measure to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, measure the length of your face from your hairline to your chin. Use these measurements to identify your face shape:

  • Oval: Your face length is greater than the width of your cheekbones, and your forehead is wider than your jawline.
  • Round: Your face length is similar to the width of your cheekbones, and your forehead and jawline are also similar in width.
  • Square: Your face length, cheekbones, and jawline are similar in width, and your forehead is also wide.
  • Heart: Your forehead is wider than your cheekbones, and your chin is pointed.
  • Diamond: Your cheekbones are wider than your forehead and jawline, and your chin is pointed.
  • Long: Your face length is greater than the width of your cheekbones, and your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are similar in width.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can start looking for short hairstyles that will flatter your features.

Types of Short Hairstyles

There are many different types of short hairstyles to choose from, including pixie cuts, bobs, and shags. When choosing a short hairstyle, it's important to consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.

  • Pixie Cut: A pixie cut is a short, cropped hairstyle that's perfect for those with oval or heart-shaped faces. It's also a great choice for those with fine hair, as it adds volume and texture.
  • Bob: A bob is a classic short hairstyle that's versatile and flattering on almost any face shape. There are many different variations of the bob, including the blunt bob, the layered bob, and the asymmetrical bob.
  • Shag: A shag is a layered, choppy haircut that's perfect for those with wavy or curly hair. It's also a great choice for those with round or square faces, as it adds texture and movement.

When choosing a short hairstyle, make sure to bring pictures to your stylist and discuss your options. They will be able to recommend a cut that works best for your face shape and hair texture.

Caring for Short Hair

Short hair requires a different type of care than long hair. Here are some tips for keeping your short hair healthy and stylish:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Short hair tends to get oily faster than long hair, so make sure to wash it every other day.
  • Use the right products: Use a shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for your hair type. If you have fine hair, use a volumizing shampoo to add texture and body.
  • Style with heat: Use a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron to style your short hair. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims: Short haircuts require regular maintenance to keep them looking fresh. Make sure to schedule regular trims with your stylist.

FAQs

Q: Will short hair make my face look rounder?

A: It depends on the cut. If you have a round face, a pixie cut or bob with layers can actually make your face appear slimmer. Avoid blunt cuts or styles that add volume to the sides of your face.

Q: Can I still wear my hair up with a short haircut?

A: Yes, there are many ways to style short hair, including updos and braids. Talk to your stylist about different styling options that work with your haircut.

Q: How often should I get my short hair cut?

A: It depends on the style and how fast your hair grows. As a general rule, plan on getting a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your cut looking fresh.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be able to determine if short hair is right for you. Remember to consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style when choosing a cut, and be sure to take good care of your new 'do.
