Can Hair Transplant Come From Other Parts Of The Body

Written by Christine Jul 12, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're experiencing hair loss, you may have considered hair transplant surgery. But did you know that hair transplants can come from other parts of your body? In this article, we'll explore the different types of hair transplants and how they work.

Types of Hair Transplants

There are two main types of hair transplants: follicular unit transplantation (FUT) and follicular unit extraction (FUE).

Follicular Unit Transplantation (FUT)

FUT involves removing a strip of skin from the back of your scalp, where the hair is densest. The strip is then divided into small grafts, each containing one to four hairs. These grafts are then transplanted into the balding areas of your scalp.

Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE)

FUE involves removing individual hair follicles from the donor area using a small, circular punch tool. These follicles are then transplanted into the recipient area using a special needle.

Can Hair Transplant Come from Other Parts of the Body?

Yes, hair transplants can come from other parts of the body. This is known as body hair transplant (BHT) or body hair-to-head transplant (BHHT). BHT is typically used when the donor area on the scalp is limited, or when the patient has insufficient hair in the donor area.

Where Can Hair Transplant Come from?

The hair used for BHT can come from various parts of the body, including:

  • Beard
  • Chest
  • Back
  • Abdomen
  • Arms and legs

How Does Body Hair Transplant Work?

The process of BHT is similar to that of FUE. The hair follicles are extracted from the donor area using a special punch tool, and then transplanted into the recipient area using a needle.

However, there are some differences between BHT and FUE:

  • The hair follicles in the donor area of the body are typically thicker and coarser than those on the scalp.
  • The hair growth cycle on the body is different from that on the scalp, which can affect the success of the transplant.
  • BHT requires a higher level of skill and expertise than FUE, as the hair follicles are more difficult to extract and transplant.

FAQ About Can Hair Transplant Come from Other Parts of the Body

1. Is BHT a good option for everyone?

No, BHT is not suitable for everyone. It is generally recommended for patients who have limited hair in the donor area of the scalp, or who have already had multiple hair transplant surgeries.

2. How long does BHT take?

The duration of BHT depends on the number of hair follicles that need to be transplanted. It can take several hours or even multiple sessions to complete the procedure.

3. Is BHT painful?

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, so you shouldn't feel any pain. However, you may experience some discomfort and swelling after the surgery.

Hair Care After Body Hair Transplant

After the surgery, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure the best results. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid touching the transplanted area for the first few days.
  • Avoid strenuous physical activity for at least a week.
  • Avoid exposure to the sun and other sources of heat.
  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to wash your hair.
  • Avoid using hair products for the first few weeks.

Overall, hair transplant surgery can be an effective way to restore hair growth. If you're considering BHT, be sure to consult with a qualified hair transplant surgeon to determine if it's the right option for you.
