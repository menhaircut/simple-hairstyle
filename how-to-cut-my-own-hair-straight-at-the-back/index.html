How To Cut My Own Hair Straight At The Back The Ultimate Guide

Written by Daniel Oct 25, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to the back. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a straight and even cut without having to visit a salon. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps and provide helpful tips and tricks to ensure a successful cut every time.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Blending Your Hair
  • Tips for a Successful Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You'll Need

Before starting, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Handheld mirror
  • Hairspray (optional)

Prepping Your Hair

To ensure a clean and even cut, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would, and avoid using any styling products that may interfere with the cutting process. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is crucial to achieving a straight and even cut. Start by dividing your hair down the middle, creating two equal sections. Then, using a comb, pull the hair forward over your shoulders and create a part in the back of your head, dividing the hair into two even sections. Secure each section with a hair tie or clip.

Cutting Your Hair

Starting with one section at a time, use your comb to create a straight line across the bottom of your hair, trimming any uneven ends with your scissors. Be sure to cut slowly and carefully, checking your progress in the handheld mirror to ensure a straight cut. Repeat on the other section.

Blending Your Hair

After cutting the bottom of your hair, it's important to blend the hair from the back to the sides. Using your comb, create a diagonal line from the bottom of your hair to the top. Cut any excess hair on the diagonal line, being careful not to cut too much. Repeat on the other side, blending the hair until there are no visible lines.

Tips for a Successful Cut

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check your progress and ensure a straight cut.
  • If you're unsure about cutting the back of your hair, enlist the help of a friend or family member.
  • Consider using clippers to create a fade for a more professional look.
  • Use hairspray to hold your hair in place while cutting.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can still cut your own hair if it's curly. Just be sure to cut it while it's dry to avoid any surprises when it dries.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't panic! Hair grows back, and mistakes can often be fixed with a little creativity. If you're unsure, seek the advice of a professional stylist.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to care for it properly to ensure healthy growth. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat tools or styling products that may damage your hair. Consider scheduling regular trims to maintain your new look.

Cutting your own hair can be a challenging task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can also be a rewarding experience. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a straight and even cut every time, without having to leave the comfort of your own home.
