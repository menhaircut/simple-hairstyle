Is Fringe Suitable For Round Face

Written by Robby Oct 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a round face, you may be wondering if fringe is the right choice for you. The good news is that with the right cut and style, fringe can actually be very flattering for those with round faces. In this article, we'll take a look at how to choose the right fringe for your face shape, as well as some tips for caring for and styling your new look.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When it comes to choosing a fringe for a round face, the key is to choose a cut that will help elongate the face and create the illusion of more length. This means avoiding fringes that are too short or too blunt, as these can actually make the face appear rounder.

Instead, opt for a longer, more layered fringe that falls just above the eyebrows. This will help to draw attention to the eyes and create a more angular look. You can also consider adding some texture to your fringe by adding in some soft, subtle layers or opting for a side-swept fringe that falls to the side of the face.

How to

The first step in choosing the right fringe for your round face is to consult with your stylist. They will be able to help you choose a cut and style that will work best for your face shape and hair type.

Once you've chosen your fringe, it's important to keep it well-maintained. This means getting regular trims to keep the length and layers looking fresh, as well as using a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Step by step

When styling your fringe, start by blow-drying it in the direction you want it to fall. If you're going for a side-swept fringe, use a round brush to help create volume and movement.

You can also add some texture to your fringe by using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo. This will help to create a more natural, tousled look that can be both playful and sophisticated.

Tips

When choosing a fringe for a round face, it's important to consider your hair type and texture as well. If you have fine hair, for example, you may want to avoid heavy, blunt fringes that can weigh your hair down and make it look limp.

Instead, opt for a more layered, textured fringe that will add volume and movement to your hair. You can also consider adding in some subtle highlights or lowlights to add some depth and dimension to your look.

FAQ

Can I still wear a blunt fringe with a round face?

While a blunt fringe can be a bit tricky to pull off with a round face, it's not impossible. The key is to keep the fringe longer and more layered, with a slight curve at the ends. This will help to create the illusion of more length and add some movement to your look.

Should I opt for a side-swept fringe or a straight fringe?

Both side-swept and straight fringes can work well with a round face, but it really comes down to personal preference. If you prefer a more dramatic look, a straight fringe can be a great option. If you prefer something more playful and casual, a side-swept fringe can be a great choice.

Hair Care

To keep your fringe looking its best, it's important to use a quality shampoo and conditioner that will help to nourish and hydrate your hair. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help protect your hair from heat damage and add some shine and moisture.

When blow-drying your fringe, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to help prevent damage and breakage. And always use a low heat setting to avoid drying out your hair and causing split ends.

With the right cut and care, fringe can be a great way to add some personality and style to your look, no matter what your face shape may be.
