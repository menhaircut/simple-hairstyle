How To Bleach Asian Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Oct 28, 2023 · 5 min read

Bleaching Asian hair at home can be a tricky process, but it is possible with the right tools and techniques. In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to bleach Asian hair at home. We will cover everything from preparation to aftercare, so you can achieve the perfect blonde shade without damaging your hair. Follow these steps and tips for a successful at-home bleaching process.

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Bleaching Process
  • Using Toner
  • Aftercare
  • Tips for Successful Bleaching
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Bleached Hair

Preparation

Before bleaching your hair, there are a few important things to consider. Firstly, assess the condition of your hair. Bleaching can be damaging, so if your hair is already dry or damaged, you may want to wait or consult with a hair professional.

Next, gather all the necessary tools and materials. You will need:

  • Bleach powder
  • Developer
  • Gloves
  • Brush
  • Bowl
  • Old clothes or hair cape
  • Shower cap

It is also important to do a strand test before you bleach your entire head to check for any adverse reactions or damage. Simply mix a small amount of bleach powder and developer and apply to a small section of hair. Leave it on for the recommended time and check for any breakage or damage.

Bleaching Process

Once you have prepared your hair and materials, it is time to start the bleaching process. Follow these steps:

  1. Section your hair into four parts and clip each section up.
  2. Mix the bleach powder and developer according to the instructions on the packaging.
  3. Put on gloves and apply the bleach mixture to each section of hair, starting from the back and working your way to the front.
  4. Be sure to saturate each section evenly and avoid getting any bleach on your scalp or skin.
  5. Once all sections are covered, put on a shower cap to help the bleach process. The recommended processing time can vary depending on the brand and strength of bleach, so be sure to check the packaging.
  6. Check your hair every 10-15 minutes to monitor the progress. Do not leave the bleach on for too long, as it can cause damage or breakage.
  7. When your hair has reached the desired level of lightness, rinse it thoroughly with lukewarm water. Do not use hot water, as it can damage your hair further.

Using Toner

After bleaching, you may notice that your hair has a yellow or brassy tint. This is normal and can be corrected with toner. Toner is a semi-permanent hair color that helps neutralize unwanted tones and achieve a cooler shade of blonde.

To use toner:

  1. Choose a toner that is specifically formulated for your hair type and desired shade.
  2. Mix the toner and developer according to the instructions on the packaging.
  3. Apply the toner evenly to your hair, starting from the roots and working your way to the tips.
  4. Leave the toner on for the recommended time and rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water.

Aftercare

After bleaching and toning your hair, it is important to take proper care of it to maintain its health and color. Here are some tips for aftercare:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 24 hours after bleaching or toning.
  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid further damage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools such as blow dryers, straighteners, or curling irons.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

Tips for Successful Bleaching

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect blonde shade:

  • Choose a high-quality bleach powder and developer for best results.
  • Be patient and do not rush the process. Bleaching can take time, but it is important to avoid leaving the bleach on for too long.
  • Avoid overlapping bleach on previously bleached or highlighted hair, as this can cause breakage.
  • Consider using a lower volume developer if you have previously bleached or damaged hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about bleaching Asian hair:

Can I bleach my hair at home?

Yes, you can bleach your hair at home, but it is important to follow the proper steps and use high-quality products to avoid damage.

How long should I leave the bleach on?

The recommended processing time can vary depending on the brand and strength of bleach, so be sure to check the packaging. Do not leave the bleach on for too long, as it can cause damage or breakage.

What should I do if my hair turns orange or yellow after bleaching?

This is normal and can be corrected with toner. Choose a toner that is specifically formulated for your hair type and desired shade.

Hair Care Tips for Bleached Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for bleached hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools such as curling irons or straighteners, as they can further damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid breakage.
  • Do not pull or tug on your hair, as this can cause breakage or damage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or hair dyes on your bleached hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can successfully bleach your Asian hair at home and achieve the perfect blonde shade. Remember to take proper care of your hair after bleaching to maintain its health and color.
