How To Use Hair Clip With Thick Hair

Written by Luffy Jun 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick hair, you know how difficult it can be to style it. It takes a lot of effort and time to make your hair look presentable. One of the easiest ways to manage your hair is by using hair clips. However, using hair clips with thick hair can be tricky. In this article, we will show you how to use hair clips with thick hair and provide you with some tips for maintaining healthy hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Hair Clip with Thick Hair
  • Step by Step Process
  • Tips for Using Hair Clips with Thick Hair
  • FAQs

How to Use Hair Clip with Thick Hair

Using hair clips with thick hair can be challenging, but with the right technique, it can help you manage your hair easily. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Choose the Right Hair Clip

When you have thick hair, choosing the right hair clip is essential. Look for clips that are sturdy and have a strong grip. Avoid using small clips that cannot hold your hair properly. Large clips or claw clips are ideal for thick hair as they provide a strong hold.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Before using the hair clip, section your hair into smaller portions. Use a comb to make a part, and then clip the rest of your hair away. This will make it easier to manage your hair.

Step 3: Position the Clip Correctly

Once you have sectioned your hair, position the clip correctly. Place the clip at the base of the section of hair and clip it in place. Make sure that the clip is secure and tight enough to hold your hair in place.

Step 4: Use Multiple Clips

If you have a lot of hair, it is best to use multiple clips to manage it. Section your hair into smaller portions and use clips to hold each section in place. This will make it easier to style your hair and ensure that each section stays in place.

Step by Step Process

Here is a step-by-step process for using hair clips with thick hair:

  1. Choose the right hair clip
  2. Section your hair
  3. Position the clip correctly
  4. Use multiple clips to manage your hair

Tips for Using Hair Clips with Thick Hair

Here are some tips for using hair clips with thick hair:

  • Choose the right size clip for your hair
  • Use clips that have a strong grip
  • Section your hair into smaller portions
  • Position the clip at the base of the section of hair
  • Use multiple clips to manage your hair
  • Don't use too many clips as it can damage your hair
  • Remove the clips gently to avoid pulling your hair

FAQs

Can hair clips damage my hair?

Using hair clips with thick hair can be beneficial, but using too many clips can damage your hair. Avoid using clips that are too tight, as they can pull your hair and cause breakage. Use clips that have a strong grip, but don't use too many.

How do I remove hair clips without damaging my hair?

To remove hair clips, gently open the clip and release the hair. Do not pull the clip out of your hair as it can damage your hair. If the clip is difficult to remove, use a detangling spray to help release the hair.

What is the best way to store hair clips?

When storing hair clips, avoid placing them in a damp or humid area. Keep them in a dry place, such as a drawer or on a hair clip holder. This will help prevent rust and prolong the life of your hair clips.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Maintaining healthy hair is essential, especially if you have thick hair. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using too many styling products
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals

Using hair clips with thick hair can be challenging, but with the right technique, it can help you manage your hair easily. Follow our step-by-step guide, use the right size clip, and section your hair properly to achieve the best results. Don't forget to take care of your hair by following our hair care tips.
