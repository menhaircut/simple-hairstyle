What To Do If Your Hair Is Falling Out From Bleach

Written by Eveline Oct 29, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss after bleaching your hair? Don't worry; you're not alone. Bleaching your hair can cause damage to your hair, which can lead to hair loss. In this article, we'll discuss what causes hair loss from bleach and what you can do to prevent it from happening.

Table of Contents

  1. What Causes Hair Loss from Bleach?
  2. How to Prevent Hair Loss from Bleach
  3. How to Treat Hair Loss from Bleach
  4. Hair Care Tips to Prevent Hair Loss from Bleach
  5. FAQs

What Causes Hair Loss from Bleach?

Bleaching your hair can cause damage to your hair shaft, which can lead to hair loss. The bleach can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it brittle and prone to breakage. If the bleach is left on for too long, it can also burn your scalp, which can lead to hair loss.

How to Prevent Hair Loss from Bleach

The best way to prevent hair loss from bleach is to avoid bleaching your hair altogether. However, if you must bleach your hair, there are a few things you can do to minimize the damage:

  • Use a lower volume developer (10 or 20) instead of a higher volume developer (30 or 40).
  • Don't leave the bleach on for too long. Follow the instructions on the packaging carefully.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to maintain its health and strength.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as they can further damage your hair.

How to Treat Hair Loss from Bleach

If you're already experiencing hair loss from bleach, there are a few things you can do to treat it:

  • Stop bleaching your hair immediately.
  • Visit a hair professional who can assess the damage and recommend the best course of action.
  • Consider cutting your hair shorter to remove the damaged ends.
  • Use a hair mask or treatment to help repair the damage and promote hair growth.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Hair Loss from Bleach

Here are some additional hair care tips to help prevent hair loss from bleach:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos and conditioners that contain sulfates.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.

FAQs

Can hair grow back after bleach damage?

Yes, hair can grow back after bleach damage. However, it may take some time, and you need to take steps to promote hair growth and prevent further damage.

Can bleach cause permanent hair loss?

In some cases, bleach can cause permanent hair loss. This is why it's essential to follow the instructions on the packaging carefully and avoid leaving the bleach on for too long.

Can hair loss from bleach be reversed?

Yes, hair loss from bleach can be reversed in some cases. However, it depends on the severity of the damage and how quickly you take action to treat it.

What's the best way to prevent hair loss from bleach?

The best way to prevent hair loss from bleach is to avoid bleaching your hair altogether. However, if you must bleach your hair, use a lower volume developer, don't leave the bleach on for too long, and deep condition your hair regularly.

In conclusion, bleach can cause hair loss if not used correctly. If you're experiencing hair loss from bleach, stop bleaching your hair immediately and seek professional help. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can prevent hair loss from bleach and promote healthy hair growth.
