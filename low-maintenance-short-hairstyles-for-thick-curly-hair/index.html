Low Maintenance Short Hairstyles For Thick Curly Hair

Written by Eveline Oct 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Thick curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it’s voluminous and full of life. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. That’s where low maintenance short hairstyles come in. They’re perfect for busy people who don’t have a lot of time to spend on their hair. Here are some of the best low maintenance short hairstyles for thick curly hair.

Bob Cut

The bob cut is a classic hairstyle that works well for thick curly hair. It’s easy to maintain and can be customized to suit your face shape and personal style. A chin-length bob is perfect for those who want a shorter hairstyle without sacrificing too much length. It’s also a great option for those who want to show off their curls.

To style a bob cut, simply scrunch your hair with a curl-enhancing product and let it air dry. You can also blow dry your hair with a diffuser attachment for extra volume. To maintain your bob cut, get regular trims every 6-8 weeks.

Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a bold and edgy hairstyle that can work well for those with thick curly hair. It’s a low maintenance option that requires minimal styling. A short pixie cut can help to emphasize your curls and show off your natural texture.

To style a pixie cut, apply a curl-enhancing product and let your hair air dry. You can also use a diffuser attachment to enhance your curls. To maintain your pixie cut, get regular trims every 4-6 weeks.

Shag Cut

The shag cut is a layered hairstyle that works well for thick curly hair. It’s a low maintenance option that requires minimal styling. The layers help to add volume and texture to your hair, while also making it easier to manage.

To style a shag cut, apply a curl-enhancing product and let your hair air dry. You can also use a diffuser attachment to enhance your curls. To maintain your shag cut, get regular trims every 6-8 weeks.

Faux Hawk

The faux hawk is a trendy and edgy hairstyle that can work well for those with thick curly hair. It’s a low maintenance option that requires minimal styling. The sides are shaved or cut short, while the top is left longer to create a faux hawk effect.

To style a faux hawk, apply a curl-enhancing product and let your hair air dry. You can also use a diffuser attachment to enhance your curls. To maintain your faux hawk, get regular trims every 4-6 weeks.

Tips for Low Maintenance Hairstyles

  • Use a curl-enhancing product to help define your curls.
  • Air dry your hair whenever possible to minimize heat damage.
  • Use a diffuser attachment when blow drying your hair to enhance your curls.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your hair.
  • Consider using a satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz and breakage.

FAQ

What is the best haircut for thick curly hair?

The best haircut for thick curly hair is one that works with your natural texture and face shape. Short hairstyles like the bob, pixie, shag, and faux hawk are all great options for low maintenance styling.

What products should I use on my thick curly hair?

You should use a curl-enhancing product to help define your curls. Look for products that are designed specifically for curly hair, like curl creams, gels, and mousses.

How often should I get my hair trimmed?

You should get your hair trimmed every 4-8 weeks, depending on the length and style of your hair. Regular trims help to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to help prevent dryness and frizz.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to help keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can cause heat damage.

Low maintenance short hairstyles are perfect for those with thick curly hair. They’re easy to manage and require minimal styling. Whether you opt for a bob, pixie, shag, or faux hawk, there’s a low maintenance style that will work for you. Just remember to use the right products and get regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.
