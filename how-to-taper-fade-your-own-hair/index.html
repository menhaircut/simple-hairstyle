How To Taper Fade Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking to give yourself a fresh haircut, a taper fade might be the way to go. This style is popular among men of all ages and can be achieved at home with a little practice. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to taper fade your own hair.

Description

A taper fade is a haircut that gradually fades from short to longer hair. It's typically shorter on the sides and back and longer on the top. This style has become increasingly popular in recent years and can be seen on men of all ages and professions. It's a versatile cut that can be dressed up or down and is easy to maintain.

How to Taper Fade Your Own Hair

To achieve a taper fade on your own, you'll need a few tools:

  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before starting the process. It's also important to have a clear idea of how you want your hair to look when you're finished. Look up pictures of taper fades online or in magazines to get an idea of what you like.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve a taper fade:

  1. Start with the longest guard on your clippers and cut the top of your hair to the desired length. Use scissors to trim any longer pieces.
  2. Using a shorter guard, cut the sides and back of your hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the comb to help guide the clippers.
  3. Blend the sides and top together using a guard in between the two lengths you've already used. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the comb to blend the two lengths together.
  4. Use scissors to trim any longer pieces on the top of your head.
  5. Switch to a shorter guard and trim the sides and back again, starting at the bottom and working your way up.
  6. Blend the sides and top again using a guard in between the two lengths you've already used.
  7. Repeat steps 4-6 until you've achieved the desired length and blend.
  8. Clean up the neckline and sideburns with clippers or scissors.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for a Successful Taper Fade

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you attempt a taper fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard. It's easier to take off more hair than it is to put it back on.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and it's important to take breaks if you start to feel fatigued.
  • Use multiple mirrors to get a full view of your head as you cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Cutting your own hair can be challenging and it's okay to ask a friend or family member for assistance.

FAQ

Here are a few commonly asked questions about taper fades:

Can you taper fade long hair?

Yes, you can taper fade long hair. However, it may be more challenging to achieve a smooth blend.

How often should I get a taper fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade grows out. On average, most people get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

While it's possible to use regular scissors, it's best to use hair cutting scissors. These are sharper and more precise, making it easier to achieve the desired cut.

Hair Care After the Taper Fade

Once you've achieved your taper fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Style your hair with pomade or hair wax to keep it in place.
  • Visit a professional stylist for touch-ups every few weeks.

With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a sleek and stylish taper fade at home. Follow these steps and tips for a successful cut.
