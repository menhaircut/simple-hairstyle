The Best Way To Start Dreads With Short Hair

Written by Eveline Nov 27, 2023 · 3 min read

If you have short hair, you may be wondering if it's possible to start dreadlocks. The good news is that it is! In fact, starting dreads with short hair can be easier than starting with longer hair. In this article, we'll go over the best way to start dreads with short hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Dreads with Short Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreads

How to Start Dreads with Short Hair

Before we dive into the step by step guide, let's first go over the basics of starting dreads with short hair. The best method for starting dreads with short hair is the twist and rip method. This method involves twisting the hair and then ripping it apart to create sections that will eventually become dreads. This method works well for short hair because it creates sections that are small enough to form tight, neat dreads.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start, you'll need a few tools to help you along the way:

  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Rubber bands
  • Dread wax (optional)

Step by Step Guide

Now that you have your tools, let's get started!

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in starting dreads with short hair is to wash your hair. This will remove any dirt or product buildup and make your hair easier to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into small, even sections. The sections should be about the size of a pencil or smaller. Use hair clips to keep each section separated.

Step 3: Twist and Rip

Starting at the root of each section, twist the hair in one direction until it starts to coil on itself. Then, grab the hair with your other hand and rip it apart. Repeat this twisting and ripping motion until the entire section is twisted and knotted. Secure the end of the section with a rubber band to keep it in place.

Step 4: Repeat

Continue twisting and ripping each section until your entire head is covered. Be sure to keep the sections small and even to create neat, uniform dreads.

Step 5: Maintenance

Once your dreads are formed, you'll need to maintain them by regularly washing and separating them. You can also use dread wax to help keep them neat and tidy.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Hair

Here are a few tips to help you along the way:

  • Be patient – it can take several months for your dreads to fully form
  • Avoid using conditioner or any products that contain silicone
  • Wear a hat or wrap your hair at night to prevent frizz
  • Use a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup

FAQs

Can I start dreads with very short hair?

Yes, you can! The twist and rip method works well for hair as short as 2-3 inches.

Do I need to use dread wax?

No, dread wax is optional. Some people find it helpful for keeping their dreads neat and tidy, but it's not necessary.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once your dreads are formed, it's important to take good care of them. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Separate your dreads regularly to prevent them from growing together
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils on your dreads
  • Wear a swim cap when swimming to prevent your dreads from getting tangled

Starting dreads with short hair is possible and can be a great way to rock a new look. Use the twist and rip method, be patient, and take good care of your dreads to ensure they look their best.
