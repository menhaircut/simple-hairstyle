How Should Curly Hair Be Layered A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Nov 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it looks beautiful, it can be difficult to manage and style. One of the best ways to make curly hair more manageable is by layering it. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how should curly hair be layered.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Layer Curly Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Layering Curly Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Layered Curly Hair

Description

Layering curly hair involves cutting the hair in a way that creates layers or different lengths. This helps to reduce volume and weight in the hair, making it more manageable and adding texture and movement. Layering also helps to define curls and add volume to the hair.

How to Layer Curly Hair

The best way to layer curly hair is by getting a professional haircut from a hairstylist who specializes in curly hair. It is important to find someone who has experience working with curly hair as they will know how to cut and style it best.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to layer curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any product buildup and make your hair easier to work with.
  2. Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser. This will help to enhance your natural curls and make them easier to see.
  3. Section your hair into four parts: the front, the back, and two sides.
  4. Starting at the back, take a small section of hair and twist it. Cut the ends of the hair that stick out from the twist. This will create a layered effect.
  5. Repeat this process on each section of hair, working your way from the back to the front.
  6. When you have finished cutting, shake out your hair to see how it looks. If you want more layers, you can go back and cut some more.

Tips for Layering Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering curly hair:

  • Always use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and damage to the hair.
  • Avoid thinning scissors as they can create a frizzy look.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to start with a little and add more if necessary.
  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before cutting. Wet hair can shrink as it dries, making your layers uneven.
  • Be patient and take your time. Layering curly hair can be a time-consuming process, but the results are worth it.

FAQs

Q: How often should you layer curly hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. Generally, you should get a haircut every six to eight weeks to maintain your layers.

Q: Can you layer curly hair at home?

A: While it is possible to layer curly hair at home, we recommend getting it done by a professional. They have the experience and knowledge to create the best layers for your hair type and texture.

Q: Will layering curly hair make it frizzy?

A: No, if done correctly, layering curly hair can actually reduce frizz by removing excess weight from the hair.

Hair Care for Layered Curly Hair

Once you have layered your curly hair, it is important to take care of it properly to maintain its shape and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle it when it is wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance your curls and keep them moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Sleep on a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

By following these tips and getting your hair layered by a professional, you can enjoy beautiful, manageable, and healthy curly hair.
