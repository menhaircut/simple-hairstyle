What Frame Shape Is Best For My Face

Written by Petter Jul 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Choosing the right frame shape for your face can be tricky, especially with so many options available. The right frame can enhance your best features and complement your overall look, while the wrong frame can make you look unbalanced or outdated. In this article, we'll cover how to determine your face shape and which frame shapes are best suited for each shape.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before you can choose the right frame shape, you need to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: round, square, oval, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, look in a mirror and:

  • Measure your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline with a flexible tape measure
  • Take note of the length of your face from forehead to chin
  • Compare your measurements to the following guidelines:

Round Face: Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are similar in width. Length is shorter than width.

Square Face: Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are similar in width. Length is equal to width.

Oval Face: Forehead is slightly wider than chin. Cheekbones are high. Length is greater than width.

Heart Face: Forehead is wider than chin. Cheekbones are high. Length is greater than width.

Diamond Face: Cheekbones are widest part of face. Forehead and jawline are narrow and roughly equal in width. Length is greater than width.

Oblong Face: Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly equal in width. Length is greater than width.

Which Frame Shapes are Best for Each Face Shape?

Round Face

If you have a round face, you want to choose frames that will help elongate your face and make it appear slimmer. Look for frames that are wider than your face and have angular lines, such as rectangular or square frames. Avoid round frames, which will only emphasize the roundness of your face.

Square Face

If you have a square face, you want to soften your strong jawline and make your face appear more rounded. Look for frames that are wider than your cheekbones and have curved or round edges, such as oval or round frames. Avoid square or geometric frames that will only emphasize the squareness of your face.

Oval Face

If you have an oval face, you can wear almost any frame shape. Look for frames that are proportional to your face and don't overwhelm your features. Avoid frames that are too large or too small.

Heart Face

If you have a heart-shaped face, you want to balance your wide forehead with your narrow chin. Look for frames that are wider at the bottom than the top, such as cat-eye or butterfly frames. Avoid frames that are wider at the top than the bottom, which will only emphasize your forehead.

Diamond Face

If you have a diamond face, you want to balance your narrow forehead and jawline with your wide cheekbones. Look for frames that are wider than your cheekbones and have curved edges, such as oval or cat-eye frames. Avoid frames that are too narrow, which will only emphasize your narrow features.

Oblong Face

If you have an oblong face, you want to make your face appear shorter and wider. Look for frames that are wider than your cheekbones and have decorative or contrasting temples, such as aviator or butterfly frames. Avoid frames that are too narrow or too small.

Tips for Choosing the Right Frame Shape

  • Consider your personal style and the occasion for which you'll be wearing the frames
  • Try on multiple frame shapes to see which ones look best on you
  • Choose frames that complement your hair color and skin tone
  • Make sure the frames fit comfortably and securely on your face
  • Consult with an optician or eyewear specialist for additional advice and recommendations

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I wear any frame shape if I have an oval face?

Yes, if you have an oval face, you can wear almost any frame shape. Just make sure the frames are proportional to your face and don't overwhelm your features.

Can I wear round frames if I have a round face?

Avoid round frames if you have a round face, as they will only emphasize the roundness of your face. Look for frames with angular lines, such as rectangular or square frames, that will help elongate your face and make it appear slimmer.

Can I wear oversized frames if I have a small face?

Avoid oversized frames if you have a small face, as they will overwhelm your features. Look for frames that are proportional to your face and don't appear too large or too small.

Hair Care Tips for Wearing Frames

  • Choose frames that won't interfere with your hairstyle, such as frames with nose pads or adjustable temples
  • Make sure your frames don't rub against your hair, which can cause breakage and damage
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and strong
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair
  • Consult with a hairstylist for additional advice and recommendations

Choosing the right frame shape for your face can be a daunting task, but by following these guidelines and tips, you can find a frame that enhances your best features and complements your personal style.
