What To Do With Thick Frizzy Hair Tips And Tricks

Thick frizzy hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can tame your locks and achieve the smooth, sleek look you desire. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on what to do with thick frizzy hair to make it more manageable and healthy.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How To
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for managing thick frizzy hair. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Limit the use of hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

How To

Here are some techniques on what to do with thick frizzy hair:

1. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help to smooth it out and reduce frizz. Start by applying a heat protectant spray to your damp hair. Then, blow dry your hair using a round brush, pulling the hair taut as you go.

2. Use a Hair Mask

Using a hair mask once a week can help to nourish and hydrate your hair. Apply the mask to your hair, focusing on the ends, and leave it on for the recommended time before rinsing it out.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioners can help to tame frizz and keep your hair hydrated. Apply the product to your damp hair, focusing on the ends, and style as desired.


Step-by-Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on what to do with thick frizzy hair:

1. Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioning it to keep it moisturized.

2. Towel Dry Your Hair

Use a towel to gently dry your hair, being careful not to rub it vigorously, as this can cause frizz.

3. Apply a Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray to your damp hair to protect it from heat damage.

4. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush, pulling the hair taut as you go.

5. Use a Hair Mask

Apply a hair mask to your hair, focusing on the ends, and leave it on for the recommended time before rinsing it out.

6. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair, focusing on the ends, and style as desired.


Tips

Here are some additional tips on what to do with thick frizzy hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can transfer oils from your hands to your hair, making it greasy.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz and protect your hair from breakage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about what to do with thick frizzy hair:

1. Can I use a straightening iron on my thick frizzy hair?

While straightening irons can be used on thick frizzy hair, it's best to avoid using them too often, as they can cause heat damage.

2. Can I use hair oil on my thick frizzy hair?

Yes, hair oil can be used on thick frizzy hair to help smooth and hydrate it. Apply a small amount to your hair, focusing on the ends.

3. How often should I wash my thick frizzy hair?

It's best to wash your thick frizzy hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of natural oils.

Managing thick frizzy hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, you can keep your hair healthy and looking its best. Remember to take care of your hair by avoiding heat styling tools, using hair masks and leave-in conditioners, and getting regular trims. Use these tips and tricks on what to do with thick frizzy hair to achieve the smooth, sleek look you desire.
