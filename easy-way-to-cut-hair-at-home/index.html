Easy Way To Cut Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Cutting your own hair at home can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a simple and cost-effective way to maintain your hairstyle. In this guide, we'll go over the easy way to cut hair at home, including how to prepare, what tools you'll need, and step-by-step instructions for a basic trim.

Preparing to Cut Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare your workspace and yourself. Choose a well-lit area with a clean surface, and put on a comfortable shirt that you don't mind getting hair on. Make sure you have a clear idea of the style you want to achieve, and gather any reference photos or videos that can help guide you.

Tools You'll Need

To cut your hair at home, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror

You may also want to invest in hair-cutting shears, which are specially designed for cutting hair and will give you a cleaner, more precise cut. These can be purchased online or at your local beauty supply store.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before you start cutting it. This will ensure that your hair is free of any tangles or product buildup, which can make it harder to cut evenly. If you have curly hair, consider cutting it while it's still damp, as this can help you see the natural shape of your curls and avoid cutting too much off.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Use your comb to divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle, and then divide each half into two more sections. Secure each section with a hair tie or clip, so that you can work on one section at a time without getting tangled up.

Step 3: Trim the Ends

Take one section of hair at a time, and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers, and use your scissors to trim off any split ends or damaged pieces. Work your way around your head, taking care to cut the same amount off of each section so that your hair stays even.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After you've trimmed all of your sections, it's important to check for evenness. Use your comb to part your hair down the middle again, and compare the two halves. If one side is longer than the other, trim it until it matches. You can also check the length of your hair around your face and ears, to make sure that it's all the same length.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home

  • Start with small cuts, and work your way up. It's easier to take more off than to put it back on!
  • Use hair clips or ties to keep your hair out of the way while you're working, and only release one section at a time.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair-cutting shears, which will give you a cleaner, more precise cut.
  • If you're cutting bangs, start with a longer length and work your way up gradually, rather than cutting too much off all at once.
  • Remember that hair grows back, so don't stress too much about making it perfect. Have fun and experiment!

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut your hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Regular scissors are not as sharp as hair-cutting shears, and can result in a jagged, uneven cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

How often you cut your hair depends on your individual hair type and style. As a general rule, it's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep split ends at bay and maintain your style.

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair at home. Just be sure to cut it while it's still damp, so that you can see the natural shape of your curls and avoid cutting too much off.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair at Home

After you've cut your hair at home, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain your style. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized, and consider using a hair oil or leave-in conditioner to prevent split ends. Avoid using heat tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as these can damage your hair and cause breakage.

Conclusion

Cutting your own hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience, as long as you take the time to prepare properly and use the right tools and techniques. By following the easy way to cut hair at home outlined in this guide, you can maintain your style and save money on salon appointments. Remember to have fun and experiment with different styles, and don't stress too much about making it perfect!
