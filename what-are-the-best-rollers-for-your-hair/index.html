What Are The Best Rollers For Your Hair

Written by Robby Jul 09, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking for ways to add volume, curl or straighten your hair, rollers are a great option. But with so many types of rollers on the market, it can be difficult to know which ones are the best for your hair type and styling needs. In this article, we'll explore the different types of rollers available and provide tips on how to choose the best ones for your hair.

  2. How to Choose the Best Rollers for Your Hair

Types of Rollers

Rollers come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and materials. Here are some of the most common types:

Velcro Rollers

Velcro rollers are a popular choice because they don't require clips or pins to secure them in place. They have a rough surface that grips the hair, making them easy to use and ideal for adding volume and body to fine or limp hair. Velcro rollers come in a range of sizes, from small to large, so you can create different types of curls or waves.

Hot Rollers

Hot rollers are heated before use and can create long-lasting curls or waves. They come in various sizes and materials, like ceramic or tourmaline, which can help reduce heat damage to your hair. Hot rollers are suitable for all hair types, but they may not work well on very thick or coarse hair.

Flexi Rods

Flexi rods are made of a flexible foam material and come in different sizes, from thin to thick. They're great for creating tight, defined curls or waves without using heat. Flexi rods can be used on all hair types, but they work best on hair that's been stretched or straightened beforehand.

Perm Rods

Perm rods are similar to flexi rods but are made of harder plastic. They're commonly used to create permanent curls in hair that's been chemically treated, but they can also be used to create temporary curls or waves. Perm rods come in various sizes, and the larger the rod, the looser the curl or wave will be.

How to Choose the Best Rollers for Your Hair

When choosing rollers, consider the following factors:

Hair Type

The type of rollers you choose will depend on your hair type. If you have fine or thin hair, velcro rollers or flexi rods are a good choice because they won't weigh down your hair. If you have thick or coarse hair, hot rollers or perm rods may work better because they can provide more heat and hold.

Roller Size

The size of the rollers will determine the size of the curls or waves you create. Smaller rollers will create tighter curls, while larger rollers will create looser waves. Consider the length of your hair, too. Shorter hair may need smaller rollers to create volume, while longer hair can handle larger rollers for more dramatic curls or waves.

Material

The material of the rollers can affect the outcome of your hairstyle. Velcro rollers are great for adding volume, but they may not hold curls as well as hot rollers or perm rods. Hot rollers and perm rods can create more defined curls, but they can also be damaging if not used correctly.

How to Use Rollers

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to use rollers:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. Apply a heat protectant spray if using hot rollers or a curling iron.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. The number of sections will depend on the size of your rollers and the amount of hair you have.
  3. Take a small section of hair and place the roller at the end. Roll the hair up towards the scalp, making sure it's smooth and taut.
  4. Use a clip or pin to secure the roller in place.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 for each section of hair.
  6. Leave the rollers in for at least 20-30 minutes, or until the hair is completely dry.
  7. Remove the rollers gently and style as desired.

Tips for Using Rollers

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your rollers:

  • For tighter curls, use smaller rollers and leave them in for longer.
  • For looser waves, use larger rollers and leave them in for a shorter amount of time.
  • Apply a styling product, like mousse or hairspray, before and after using rollers to help hold the style.
  • Don't brush or comb your hair immediately after removing the rollers. Use your fingers to gently separate the curls or waves instead.
  • For added volume, tease the roots of your hair with a comb or brush before using rollers.

FAQ

Can I use rollers on wet hair?

No, it's not recommended to use rollers on wet hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before using rollers to avoid damaging your hair or creating an uneven style.

How long should I leave the rollers in?

The amount of time you leave the rollers in will depend on the type of rollers you're using and the desired outcome. Generally, leaving the rollers in for at least 20-30 minutes is recommended.

Can I use rollers on short hair?

Yes, you can use rollers on short hair. Use smaller rollers to create volume or curls, and larger rollers to create waves.

Hair Care Tips for Using Rollers

When using rollers, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain a healthy appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Don't use rollers too often. Using hot rollers or curling irons frequently can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot rollers or curling irons to reduce heat damage.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair before and after using rollers.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray or styling product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Now that you know what the best rollers for your hair are, it's time to start experimenting with different styles and techniques. Whether you want to add volume, create curls, or straighten your hair, rollers are a versatile tool that can help you achieve your desired look.
