How To Do A Low Skin Fade

Written by Daniel Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish haircut, a low skin fade may be just what you need. This popular haircut features tapered sides that gradually fade to the skin, creating a clean and sharp look. In this article, we'll show you how to do a low skin fade step by step. We'll also give you some tips on how to care for your hair after the cut. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Do a Low Skin Fade
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

How to Do a Low Skin Fade

Before we start, you'll need the right tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, hair scissors, a comb, and a mirror. If you're not confident in your skills, it may be best to go to a professional barber instead.

Here's how to do a low skin fade:

Step 1: Determine the Length

The first step is to determine the length of the fade. A low skin fade typically starts around the ear and gradually fades to the skin. Use your clippers to cut the hair to the desired length. It's best to start with a longer guard and work your way down to a smaller one.

Step 2: Create a Guide

Next, create a guide for the fade. Use your comb to separate the longer hair from the shorter hair. This will help you create a smooth transition between the two lengths.

Step 3: Start Fading

Now it's time to start fading. Use your clippers to create a fade line around the head. Start with a larger guard and work your way down to a smaller one. Be sure to blend the hair as you go to create a seamless transition.

Step 4: Blend the Sides

Once you've created the fade line, it's time to blend the sides. Use your scissors to blend the hair between the fade line and the longer hair on top. Be sure to blend the hair evenly to avoid any harsh lines.

Step 5: Finish the Cut

Finally, finish the cut by trimming the hair on top. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. You can also use a razor to create a clean and sharp look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect low skin fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a smaller one
  • Use a comb to create a guide for the fade
  • Blend the hair evenly to avoid any harsh lines
  • Use a razor to create a clean and sharp look
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you if you're not confident in your skills

FAQ

Can I do a low skin fade on myself?

It's possible to do a low skin fade on yourself, but it can be difficult. It's best to go to a professional barber if you're not confident in your skills.

How often should I get a low skin fade?

You should get a low skin fade every 2-4 weeks to maintain the look.

Can I style my hair after a low skin fade?

Yes, you can style your hair after a low skin fade. Use a pomade or wax to create your desired style.

Hair Care

After you've done your low skin fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated
  • Avoid using hot tools such as blow dryers and flat irons
  • Visit your barber regularly to maintain the look

That's it! With these tips, you can achieve a clean and stylish low skin fade. Remember to take care of your hair to maintain the look. If you're not confident in your skills, it's best to go to a professional barber. Good luck!
