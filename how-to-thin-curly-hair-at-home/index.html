How To Thin Curly Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 26, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Curly hair can sometimes be difficult to manage, especially when it's thick and abundant. If you're looking to thin out your curly hair, you don't have to go to a salon to do it. With the right tools and techniques, you can do it at home. In this article, we'll show you how to thin curly hair at home in a few simple steps.

What You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need a few things:

  • Scissors
  • Thinning shears
  • Comb or brush
  • Hair clips

How to Thin Curly Hair at Home

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing your hair with your regular shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair until it's damp, and let it air dry completely.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start by parting your hair down the middle, and then divide it into smaller sections. The sections should be about 1-2 inches wide.

Step 3: Start thinning your hair

Take one section of hair and comb it out. Hold the scissors or thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair, starting at the ends. Work your way up the section, cutting off small pieces of hair as you go. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 4: Check your progress

After you've thinned out each section of hair, check your progress in a mirror. If your hair still looks too thick, repeat the process until you achieve your desired look.

Tips for Thinning Curly Hair at Home

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when thinning curly hair at home:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always thin out more hair, but you can't put it back once it's been cut.
  • Use thinning shears instead of regular scissors. Thinning shears are designed to remove hair without leaving sharp edges.
  • Don't thin your hair too much. If you thin your hair too much, it can look stringy and damaged.
  • Be patient. Thinning curly hair can take time, especially if you have a lot of hair.

FAQs

Can I thin my curly hair if it's already thin?

If your curly hair is already thin, you don't need to thin it any further. Thinning your hair too much can cause it to look damaged and unhealthy.

How often should I thin my curly hair?

It's best to thin curly hair every 6-8 weeks. Thinning your hair too often can cause it to become damaged and prone to breakage.

Can I use regular scissors to thin my curly hair?

You can use regular scissors to thin your curly hair, but thinning shears are recommended. Regular scissors can leave sharp edges and cause your hair to look uneven.

Hair Care for Thinned Curly Hair

After thinning your curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.

Thinning curly hair at home is easy with the right tools and techniques. Follow these tips, and you'll have beautiful, manageable hair in no time.
