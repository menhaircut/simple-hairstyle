How To Cut Volume Into Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your long, flat hair? Do you want to add more volume and dimension to your locks? Look no further than this step-by-step guide on how to cut volume into long hair. With a few simple techniques and the right tools, you can transform your hair from dull to full in no time.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Volume into Long Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Volume into Long Hair
  • FAQ

How to Cut Volume into Long Hair

Cutting volume into long hair involves layering and texturizing the hair to create movement and body. This can be achieved through a variety of cutting techniques, including:

  • Long layers
  • Face-framing layers
  • Graduated layers
  • Texturizing

These techniques are best performed by a professional hairstylist, but with the right tools and a steady hand, you can achieve a similar effect at home.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut volume into your long hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb through any tangles and ensure your hair is completely detangled.
  2. Section your hair into four equal parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section away to keep it separate.
  3. Starting with the back sections, take a 1-2 inch section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut into the hair at a slight angle, removing length and creating a soft layer. Repeat this process for each section, working from the bottom towards the crown of your head.
  4. Move to the front sections and repeat step three, but this time angle the scissors slightly towards your face for a face-framing effect.
  5. Once you have finished cutting the layers, use texturizing shears to add texture and movement to your hair. Take small sections of hair and snip into the ends with the texturizing shears, creating a choppy, layered effect.
  6. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your new voluminous look!

Tips for Cutting Volume into Long Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting volume into long hair:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors and texturizing shears. Blunt or dull scissors can damage your hair and result in an uneven cut.
  • Start with a small amount of hair when cutting layers. You can always take more off, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Take breaks as needed. Cutting long hair can be time-consuming and tiring, so don't be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.
  • Practice patience. Cutting volume into long hair requires precision and attention to detail. Take your time and don't rush the process.

FAQ

Can I cut volume into my own long hair?

While it is possible to cut volume into your own long hair, it is recommended that you visit a professional hairstylist for the best results. They have the training and experience to create the perfect cut for your hair type and face shape.

Will cutting volume into my long hair damage it?

When done correctly, cutting volume into long hair should not damage it. However, using dull or blunt scissors can cause split ends and breakage. It is important to use sharp, high-quality scissors and to take your time when cutting.

Hair Care After Cutting Volume into Long Hair

After cutting volume into your long hair, it is important to properly care for your hair to maintain its health and volume. This includes using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible, and getting regular trims to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve the voluminous, full-bodied hair you've always wanted. Remember to take your time, be patient, and invest in high-quality tools for the best results.
