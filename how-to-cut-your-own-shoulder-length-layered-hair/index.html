How to Cut Your Own Shoulder Length Layered Hair | Tips and FAQ

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle without breaking the bank, cutting your own hair is a great option. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a shoulder length layered haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll go over the steps, tips, and frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair.

How to Cut Your Own Shoulder Length Layered Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure you have the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also important to work in a well-lit area with a mirror.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it straight. This will make it easier to cut and style.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, separate your hair into four sections: top, sides, and back. Clip each section to keep them separate.

Step 3: Cut the Back Section

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair, hold it between your fingers, and cut the ends at an angle. Repeat until you've cut all the hair in the back section.

Step 4: Cut the Side Sections

Move on to the side sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front and hold it between your fingers. Cut the ends at an angle, then repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

Finally, cut the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front, hold it between your fingers, and cut the ends at an angle. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've cut all the hair on the top section.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry to get a more accurate length.
  • Don't cut your hair straight across - cut at an angle for a more layered look.
  • Take breaks and step away from the mirror to avoid over-cutting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own layered hair?

Yes, you can cut your own layered hair with the right tools and techniques. Follow our step-by-step guide for best results.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, it's best to use hair cutting scissors as they're designed to give a clean cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and free of split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and style. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, and consider using a hair mask once a week for extra hydration.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own shoulder length layered hair at home. Happy cutting!
