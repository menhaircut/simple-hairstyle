How To Curl Hair For Black Guys A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Dec 01, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you a black guy looking to add some curls to your hair? Maybe you're tired of the same old look and want to switch things up a bit. Whatever your reason may be, curling your hair is a great way to add some texture and volume. In this article, we'll show you how to curl hair for black guys in a relaxed English language. We'll cover everything from the tools you need to how to care for your curls. So, let's get started!

Tools You Need

Before you start curling your hair, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Curling iron: Choose a curling iron with a barrel size that works for your hair length and the type of curls you want. A 1-inch barrel is a good starting point.
  • Heat protectant spray: Protect your hair from heat damage by applying a heat protectant spray before curling.
  • Wide-tooth comb: Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before curling.
  • Hair clips: Section your hair with hair clips to make it easier to curl.
  • Hairspray: Finish off your curls with some hairspray to hold them in place.

Preparation

Before you start curling your hair, you'll need to prepare it. Here's what you should do:

  1. Wash your hair: Start with clean hair. Wash your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  2. Dry your hair: Dry your hair with a towel until it's damp. Avoid using a hair dryer as it can damage your hair.
  3. Apply heat protectant: Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to protect it from the heat of the curling iron.

Section 1: Curling the Back of Your Head

Start by sectioning the back of your hair. Take a small section of hair and clamp the curling iron near the roots. Twist the curling iron away from your face and hold for a few seconds. Release the curling iron and let the curl fall naturally. Repeat this process until you've curled all the sections at the back of your head.

Section 2: Curling the Sides of Your Head

Next, move on to the sides of your head. Take a small section of hair and clamp the curling iron near the roots. Twist the curling iron towards your face and hold for a few seconds. Release the curling iron and let the curl fall naturally. Repeat this process until you've curled all the sections on the sides of your head.

Section 3: Curling the Top of Your Head

Finally, move on to the top of your head. Take a small section of hair and clamp the curling iron near the roots. Twist the curling iron away from your face and hold for a few seconds. Release the curling iron and let the curl fall naturally. Repeat this process until you've curled all the sections on the top of your head.

Tips for Curling Hair for Black Guys

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when curling your hair:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to avoid heat damage to your hair.
  • Don't hold the curling iron on your hair for too long as it can damage your hair.
  • Use hair clips to section your hair to make it easier to curl.
  • Experiment with different barrel sizes to achieve different types of curls.
  • Finish off your curls with some hairspray to hold them in place.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some of the most frequently asked questions about curling hair for black guys:

Can you curl relaxed hair?

Yes, you can curl relaxed hair. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray to avoid heat damage.

What's the best curling iron barrel size for black guys?

The best curling iron barrel size for black guys depends on your hair length and the type of curls you want. A 1-inch barrel is a good starting point.

How long do curls last?

Curls can last anywhere from a few hours to a few days depending on your hair type, the type of curls, and the products you use.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair as it can dry out your curls.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry as it can cause frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to curl hair for black guys, it's time to give it a try. Remember to use a heat protectant spray, section your hair, and experiment with different barrel sizes to achieve the perfect curls. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at curling your hair in no time!
