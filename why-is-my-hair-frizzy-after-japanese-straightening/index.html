Description

Japanese straightening, also known as thermal reconditioning, is a hair treatment that uses heat and chemicals to permanently straighten hair. It involves applying a solution to the hair, which breaks down the protein bonds that give hair its natural shape. The hair is then straightened with a flat iron and neutralized to lock in the straightness.

While Japanese straightening can give you smooth, straight hair that lasts for months, it can also cause frizz. This is because the treatment can weaken the hair and make it more susceptible to damage from humidity and other environmental factors.

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can further damage your hair and contribute to frizz.
  • Use a hair serum or oil to tame frizz and add shine.

  • Make sure your hair is in good condition before the treatment. If your hair is already damaged, Japanese straightening can make it worse.
  • Follow the aftercare instructions provided by your stylist, which may include avoiding washing your hair for a few days after the treatment and using specific products.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for managing frizz after Japanese straightening:

  • Avoid exposing your hair to humidity, as this can cause frizz. Consider using a hair scarf or hat in humid weather.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dye or bleach, as these can further damage your hair and contribute to frizz.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Proper hair care is essential for managing frizz after Japanese straightening. Here are some tips:
