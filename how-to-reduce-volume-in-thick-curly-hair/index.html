How To Reduce Volume In Thick Curly Hair

Written by William Nov 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have thick curly hair, you know the struggle of dealing with unruly and voluminous locks. While some people love the volume, others want to reduce it for a more manageable and sleek look. In this blog post, we will discuss how to reduce volume in thick curly hair and provide you with useful tips and tricks to make your hair more manageable.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Thick curly hair tends to have a lot of volume due to the natural shape of the curls. This can make it difficult to manage and style, especially if you prefer a more sleek and polished look. However, there are several ways to reduce volume in thick curly hair without damaging or altering the natural texture of your hair.

How to

Here are some ways to reduce volume in thick curly hair:

1. Use a Diffuser

A diffuser attachment on your hair dryer can help to evenly distribute heat and reduce frizz, which can contribute to volume. Use the diffuser on a low heat setting and scrunch your hair as you dry it to encourage your curls to form without adding too much volume.

2. Try a Hair Serum

A hair serum can help to tame frizz and add shine to your hair, while also reducing volume. Apply a small amount of serum to your hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths, and avoid applying it to the roots.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush can help to detangle your hair without creating too much volume. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently combing through any tangles.

4. Cut Your Hair in Layers

Layered haircuts can help to reduce volume in thick curly hair by removing some of the bulk. Ask your hairstylist for long layers that will help to frame your face and reduce volume without sacrificing length.

5. Avoid Heavy Products

Heavy styling products can weigh down your hair and contribute to volume. Instead, use lightweight products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as a curl defining cream or mousse.

Step by step

Here are the steps to reduce volume in thick curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a hair serum to your damp hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  4. Divide your hair into sections and apply a lightweight curl defining cream or mousse to each section.
  5. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting, scrunching your hair as you go.
  6. Once your hair is dry, use a small amount of hair oil to add shine and reduce frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for reducing volume in thick curly hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can create frizz and volume.
  • Try using a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and contribute to volume.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and contribute to volume.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can contribute to volume.

FAQ

1. Can I still have volume in my hair if I reduce it?

Yes, you can still have volume in your hair even if you reduce it. The goal of reducing volume is to make your hair more manageable and sleek, but you can still have some volume if you prefer.

2. How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference, but most people with thick curly hair only need to wash their hair once or twice a week.

3. Can I use a brush on my curly hair?

It is best to avoid using a brush on curly hair, as this can create frizz and contribute to volume. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for reducing volume in thick curly hair. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals such as hair dyes and relaxers.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can reduce volume in your thick curly hair and achieve a more manageable and sleek look. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type and personal style.
