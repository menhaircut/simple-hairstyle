How To Do Your Own Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Nov 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

In this article, we will guide you through the process of doing your own fade haircut. Whether you are looking to save some money or simply prefer the convenience of cutting your own hair at home, this guide will help you achieve a professional-looking fade. We will cover everything from the tools you need to the techniques you should use, so read on to learn more!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Making the First Cut
  • Blending Your Hair
  • Cleaning Up Your Cut
  • Tips for a Great Fade

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Electric hair clippers
  • Clipper guards (sizes #1, #2, #3, #4, #5, #6, #7, #8)
  • Barber shears
  • A comb
  • A handheld mirror
  • A regular mirror
  • A towel or cape to catch hair clippings

Make sure your clippers are well-oiled and your guards are clean and in good condition before you begin.

Preparing Your Hair

Start by washing and towel-drying your hair. Comb it out so it's tangle-free and easy to work with. If you have longer hair, you may want to trim it down with scissors before starting with the clippers.

Next, decide how high up you want your fade to start. This will determine where you make your first cut. If you're not sure, start with a higher guard (like a #4 or #5) and work your way down until you find a length you like.

Making the First Cut

Using your clippers and the appropriate guard, make a straight cut around your head at the height you've chosen. This will be the top line of your fade. Use the handheld mirror to check that the line is straight and even all the way around.

Next, switch to a slightly shorter guard (like a #3 or #4) and make another pass around your head, going up about an inch from the first line. This will be your second line, and it should be slightly curved to create a smooth transition between the two lengths.

Continue working your way down with progressively shorter guards (like a #2 or #3, then a #1 or #2) until you've reached the desired length for the fade. Remember to keep the lines slightly curved to create a natural-looking transition.

Blending Your Hair

To blend the different lengths of your hair, switch to a shorter guard (like a #1) and use an upward flicking motion with the clippers to create a fade between the two lengths. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using different guards as needed to create a smooth transition.

You may also want to use your barber shears to blend any areas that need extra attention. Use the comb to lift the hair up and cut off any longer pieces that are sticking out.

Cleaning Up Your Cut

Once you've finished the fade, use the clippers to trim any stray hairs around your ears and neck. Use the regular mirror to check the back of your head and make any final adjustments.

When you're done, use the handheld mirror to check your new haircut from all angles. If you're happy with it, give yourself a pat on the back!

Tips for a Great Fade

  • Start with a higher guard and work your way down slowly until you find a length you like.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check your haircut from all angles as you go.
  • Take your time and be patient. A good fade takes practice!
  • Don't be afraid to blend with your barber shears if needed.
  • Use a towel or cape to catch hair clippings and make cleanup easier.

FAQ

Can I do a fade haircut on myself?

Yes, you can! It may take some practice, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a great-looking fade haircut at home.

What's the difference between a fade and a taper?

A fade is a gradual transition from longer to shorter hair, while a taper is a gradual transition from shorter to longer hair. Fades typically start higher up on the head and are shorter overall.

Do I need to wash my hair before cutting it?

It's a good idea to wash and towel-dry your hair before cutting it. This will make it easier to work with and help you achieve a more even cut.

Hair Care After Your Cut

Now that you've done your own fade haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair (like blow dryers or straighteners).
  • Get regular trims to maintain your fade and keep your hair healthy.

Congratulations on mastering the art of the fade haircut! With practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking cut in the comfort of your own home.
