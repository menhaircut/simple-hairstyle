How To Stop Naturally Curly Hair Going Frizzy Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Bardi Nov 27, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy hair every time you let your natural curls out? If so, you're not alone. Many people with naturally curly hair struggle with frizz, especially in humid weather or after washing their hair. Fortunately, there are several ways to prevent frizz and keep your curls looking smooth and defined. In this blog post, we'll share our top tips for stopping naturally curly hair from going frizzy, as well as some hair care advice to keep your locks healthy and hydrated.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prevent Naturally Curly Hair from Going Frizzy
  • Hair Care Tips for Naturally Curly Hair
  • FAQs: Frequently Asked Questions

How to Prevent Naturally Curly Hair from Going Frizzy

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Instead, opt for a sulfate-free shampoo that will gently cleanse your hair without stripping it of moisture. Look for shampoos that contain moisturizing ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, or argan oil.

2. Condition, condition, condition

Curly hair needs lots of moisture to stay healthy and frizz-free. After shampooing, be sure to use a conditioner that is specifically formulated for curly hair. Apply it generously, focusing on the ends of your hair where it tends to be driest. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes before rinsing it out with cool water.

3. Don't towel-dry your hair

Towel-drying your hair can rough up the cuticles and cause frizz. Instead, gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt. You can also try plopping your hair, which involves wrapping it in a t-shirt or microfiber towel and letting it air dry.

4. Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help keep your curls moisturized and frizz-free all day long. Look for one that is lightweight and doesn't leave your hair feeling greasy or weighed down. Apply it to damp hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

5. Use a diffuser when blow-drying

If you must blow-dry your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute heat evenly and prevent frizz. Start by applying a heat protectant spray to your hair, then use the diffuser on a low heat setting. Hold the diffuser close to your scalp and scrunch your hair gently as you dry it.

6. Avoid touching your hair too much

The more you touch your hair, the more likely it is to frizz. Try to avoid playing with your hair throughout the day, and resist the urge to brush or comb it too often. Instead, use your fingers to gently shape your curls and add volume where you need it.

Hair Care Tips for Naturally Curly Hair

1. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week

In addition to your regular conditioner, use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls healthy and hydrated. Look for one that contains nourishing ingredients like shea butter, avocado oil, or honey. Apply it to damp hair and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

2. Sleep on a satin pillowcase

Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and lead to frizz, especially if you toss and turn during the night. Switch to a satin or silk pillowcase instead, which will be gentler on your hair and help prevent tangles and breakage.

3. Trim your hair regularly

Split ends and breakage can make your curls look frizzy and unruly. To keep your hair looking its best, get a trim every 8-12 weeks. This will help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.

4. Avoid using heat styling tools too often

Heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons can damage your hair and cause frizz. Try to limit your use of these tools, and always use a heat protectant spray before styling. If you must use heat, opt for a low or medium heat setting, and never use it on wet hair.

FAQs: Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Can I use gel or mousse on my curly hair?

A. Yes, you can use gel or mousse to help define your curls and prevent frizz. Look for products that are specifically formulated for curly hair, and apply them to damp hair before styling.

Q. How often should I wash my curly hair?

A. It depends on your hair type and how much oil your scalp produces. Some people with curly hair can go several days between washes, while others prefer to wash their hair every other day. Experiment to find a routine that works for you.

Q. Can I brush my curly hair?

A. It's best to avoid brushing your curly hair, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair when it's wet, and avoid touching it too much when it's dry.

With these tips and tricks, you can say goodbye to frizzy hair and hello to beautiful, defined curls. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and give it the love and care it deserves.
