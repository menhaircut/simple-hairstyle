How To Hide Weave Tracks With Short Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Sep 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of constantly worrying about your weave tracks showing through your short hair? Look no further! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to hide weave tracks with short hair. We will also give you tips on how to take care of your hair while wearing a weave. Let's get started!

How to Hide Weave Tracks with Short Hair

First and foremost, it's important to choose the right type of weave for your hair. Opt for a weave that closely matches the texture and color of your natural hair. This will make it easier to blend your weave with your hair, making it less likely for your tracks to show.

Once you've chosen the right type of weave, it's time to install it. We recommend going to a professional stylist to ensure that your weave is installed properly. However, if you choose to install your weave at home, make sure to follow the instructions carefully.

To hide your weave tracks, you can use a few different techniques. One popular method is to use a closure or a frontal. These are pieces of hair that are installed at the front of your head, covering your natural hairline and the tracks of your weave.

If you don't want to use a closure or a frontal, another technique is to braid your natural hair in a specific pattern. This pattern will help to create a foundation for your weave, making it easier to hide your tracks. Your stylist can help you determine the best braiding pattern for your hair.

Another technique is to use hair extensions to cover your tracks. You can braid your natural hair and then sew in the extensions, covering the braids and the tracks of your weave.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the different techniques for hiding your weave tracks, here is a step-by-step guide on how to do it:

  1. Choose the right type of weave for your hair.
  2. Install your weave, either at home or with the help of a professional stylist.
  3. Use a closure or a frontal to cover your tracks, or braid your natural hair in a specific pattern.
  4. If you choose to use hair extensions, braid your natural hair and sew in the extensions.
  5. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for hiding your weave tracks:

  • Use a high-quality weave to ensure that it blends well with your natural hair.
  • Don't install your weave too tightly, as this can cause hair damage and make it more likely for your tracks to show.
  • Use a fine-toothed comb to blend your natural hair with your weave.
  • Use hair products, such as edge control or hair spray, to tame any flyaways or frizz.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hiding weave tracks with short hair:

Q: Can I hide my weave tracks without using a closure or a frontal?

A: Yes, you can braid your natural hair in a specific pattern to create a foundation for your weave, making it easier to hide your tracks.

Q: Will hiding my weave tracks damage my hair?

A: Hiding your weave tracks should not damage your hair as long as you install your weave properly and don't make it too tight.

Q: How long can I keep my weave in?

A: We recommend keeping your weave in for no longer than six to eight weeks to prevent hair damage and breakage.

Hair Care

While wearing a weave, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair while wearing a weave:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, such as flat irons or curling irons.
  • Don't sleep with wet hair or with a tight scarf or cap on your head.
  • Take breaks between weaves to allow your hair to rest and recover.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to hide your weave tracks with short hair with ease. Remember to take care of your hair while wearing a weave to prevent damage and breakage. Happy styling!
