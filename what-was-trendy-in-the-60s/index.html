What Was Trendy In The 60S

Written by Frank Oct 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a fan of vintage fashion, you'll likely know that the 60s were a period of great style experimentation. From bold prints to mini skirts, the era is known for its daring and playful aesthetic. In this article, we'll explore some of the key trends from the period, and offer tips on how to incorporate them into your own wardrobe.

What Were the Key Fashion Trends of the 60s?

1. Mini Skirts

Perhaps the most iconic trend of the 60s, mini skirts were a statement of female liberation and a rejection of traditional gender roles. Today, mini skirts are still a popular choice for those who want to show off their legs and embrace a playful, youthful look. Pair a mini skirt with a simple blouse and heels, or dress it down with sneakers and a graphic tee for a casual yet stylish outfit.

2. Bold Prints

The 60s were all about bold, eye-catching prints, from psychedelic swirls to graphic florals. If you want to channel this trend, consider incorporating a statement print into your outfit, either through a dress or a statement piece like a coat or blazer. To keep the look modern, pair your print with simple, neutral pieces.

3. Go-Go Boots

Popularized by fashion icons like Twiggy and Nancy Sinatra, go-go boots were a staple of 60s fashion. Today, they're still a fun and playful addition to any outfit. Pair them with a mini skirt or dress for a retro-inspired look, or wear them with skinny jeans and a sweater for a more casual take on the trend.

4. Mod Style

The mod style of the 60s was all about clean lines, geometric shapes, and bold colors. To channel this look, opt for structured pieces like shift dresses and A-line skirts, and choose bright, eye-catching hues like orange, pink, and turquoise. To keep the look modern, add statement jewelry or a bold lip.

How to Incorporate 60s Fashion into Your Wardrobe Today

If you're looking to add some 60s-inspired pieces to your wardrobe, there are a few key things to keep in mind. Second, focus on incorporating one or two statement pieces into your outfit, rather than going head-to-toe retro. Step-by-Step: How to Create a 60s-Inspired Look

Here's a step-by-step guide to creating a 60s-inspired look:

Step 1: Choose a Statement Piece

Start by choosing a statement piece that channels the 60s aesthetic, like a mini skirt or a bold print blouse.

Step 2: Keep the Rest Simple

To avoid looking like you're in costume, keep the rest of your outfit simple and modern. Opt for neutral colors and classic shapes that complement your statement piece.

Step 3: Add Accessories

To complete your look, add a few carefully chosen accessories that evoke the 60s vibe. Consider a pair of go-go boots, a chunky necklace, or a bold headband.

Tips for Styling 60s Fashion

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when styling 60s fashion:

1. Don't Overdo It

While it's fun to incorporate retro pieces into your outfit, don't overdo it â you don't want to look like you're wearing a costume.

2. Mix and Match

Experiment with different trends and styles, and don't be afraid to mix and match to create your own unique look.

3. Keep It Modern

To avoid looking outdated, balance retro pieces with modern, on-trend pieces.

FAQs About 60s Fashion

Q: What fabrics were popular in the 60s?

Popular fabrics in the 60s included polyester, cotton, and wool.

Q: What hairstyles were popular in the 60s?

Popular hairstyles in the 60s included the beehive, the bouffant, and the pixie cut.

Q: What makeup trends were popular in the 60s?

Popular makeup trends in the 60s included bold eyeliner, thick lashes, and bright lipstick.

How to Care for 60s-Inspired Hair

If you're looking to create a 60s-inspired hairstyle, there are a few key things to keep in mind. First, invest in a good quality hairspray to help hold your style in place. Second, consider using hot rollers or a curling iron to create volume and texture. Finally, be gentle when brushing and styling your hair, as excessive pulling and tugging can lead to breakage and damage.

Conclusion

The 60s were a period of great style experimentation, and many of the trends from the era continue to inspire fashion today. Whether you're a fan of mini skirts, bold prints, or go-go boots, there are plenty of ways to incorporate 60s fashion into your wardrobe in a modern and stylish way. So have fun with it, mix and match, and express yourself through fashion!
