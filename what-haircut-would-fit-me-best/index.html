What Haircut Would Fit Me Best Your Ultimate Guide

Written by Christine Jun 24, 2023 · 5 min read

Choosing the right haircut can be a daunting task. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to decide which one will suit your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of determining what haircut would fit you the best. From face shape analysis to hair texture considerations, we’ve got you covered. Let’s dive in.

Description

The first step in determining what haircut would suit you best is to evaluate your face shape. There are seven main face shapes, including round, oval, square, heart, diamond, oblong, and triangle. Depending on your face shape, certain haircuts will be more flattering than others. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to opt for a haircut that adds height and volume to the top of your head to create the illusion of a longer face.

How to

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, compare these measurements to see which is the widest. Once you have determined your face shape, you can start looking at hairstyles that will complement your features.

Round Face

If you have a round face, you may want to consider a layered haircut that adds height and volume to the top of your head. A side-swept fringe can also help to elongate your face. Avoid blunt cuts that end at your chin, as they can make your face appear wider.

Oval Face

An oval face is considered the most balanced face shape, and most haircuts will look good on you. You can consider a long bob, a pixie cut, or even a shaggy haircut.

Square Face

If you have a square face, you may want to opt for a soft, layered haircut that frames your face. A side part can also help to soften your features. Avoid straight, blunt cuts that end at your jawline, as they can accentuate the squareness of your face.

Heart Face

If you have a heart-shaped face, you may want to consider a bob or a layered haircut that ends at your collarbone. Side-swept bangs can also help to balance out your features. Avoid short haircuts that end above your chin, as they can make your forehead appear larger.

Diamond Face

If you have a diamond-shaped face, you may want to consider a bob or a pixie cut that adds volume to the top of your head. Soft layers can also help to balance out your features. Avoid hairstyles that are too long, as they can elongate your face.

Oblong Face

If you have an oblong face, you may want to consider a long bob or a shoulder-length haircut with layers. Bangs can also help to shorten the length of your face. Avoid hairstyles that are too long or too short, as they can make your face appear even longer.

Triangle Face

If you have a triangle-shaped face, you may want to consider a layered haircut that adds volume to the top of your head. A side part can also help to balance out your features. Avoid hairstyles that are too short, as they can accentuate the width of your jawline.

Step by Step

Now that you know your face shape and the types of haircuts that will suit you best, it’s time to find a hairstylist who can help you achieve your desired look. When selecting a hairstylist, look for someone who has experience working with your hair type and who can offer personalized recommendations based on your face shape and lifestyle.

During your consultation, be sure to bring in pictures of hairstyles that you like and be honest about your hair care routine and styling preferences. Your hairstylist will work with you to create a customized haircut that flatters your features and suits your lifestyle.

Tips

  • Consider your hair texture when selecting a haircut. For example, if you have curly hair, you may want to opt for a layered haircut that helps to define your curls.
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles. Hair grows back, and you can always try something new if you don’t love your current haircut.
  • Invest in high-quality hair care products that will help to maintain your haircut and keep your hair healthy and shiny.

FAQ

How often should I get a haircut?

It’s recommended that you get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain your hairstyle and keep your hair healthy. However, the frequency of haircuts will depend on your hair type and the length of your hair.

Can I still get a haircut if I have damaged hair?

Yes, you can still get a haircut if you have damaged hair. However, it’s important to communicate with your hairstylist about your hair’s condition so that they can recommend the best course of action.

How can I maintain my haircut at home?

To maintain your haircut at home, invest in high-quality hair care products that are tailored to your hair type and texture. Follow a regular hair care routine that includes shampooing, conditioning, and styling your hair.

Hair Care

After getting a new haircut, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. This includes using high-quality hair care products that are tailored to your hair type and texture, avoiding heat styling tools whenever possible, and protecting your hair from environmental damage. Regular trims can also help to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.

In conclusion, choosing the right haircut can help to enhance your features and boost your confidence. By analyzing your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle, you can find a haircut that suits you best. Remember to communicate with your hairstylist, experiment with different styles, and maintain your hair’s health and appearance with regular care. Happy hairstyling!
