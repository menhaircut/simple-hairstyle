Men s Hair Long In Front Shorter In Back The Ultimate Guide

Written by Christine Nov 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

In recent years, the men's hair trend of having longer hair in the front and shorter hair in the back has become increasingly popular. This style, sometimes referred to as the "mullet," has been sported by many celebrities and influencers. If you're considering trying out this look for yourself, then you've come to the right place. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about men's hair long in front shorter in back, including how to achieve the style, tips for maintaining it, and common FAQs. Let's get started!

How to Achieve Men's Hair Long in Front Shorter in Back

The first step to achieving this look is to grow out your hair. You'll want to have at least a few inches of hair in the front and on top of your head, with the length gradually decreasing towards the back. Once you have the desired length, it's time to visit a trusted hairstylist who can help you achieve the cut. Here's what you can expect:

Step 1: Consultation

Your hairstylist will likely begin by discussing your desired length and style. They may also recommend certain products or techniques to help you achieve the look.

Step 2: Cutting the Hair

The hairstylist will start by trimming the back of your hair to the desired length. They will then gradually work their way towards the front, cutting the hair at an angle to create a layered look. The length in the front should be significantly longer than the back.

Step 3: Styling the Hair

Once the cut is complete, your hairstylist will likely style your hair to accentuate the long-front, short-back look. This may involve using products such as hair gel or wax to create a textured or tousled look.

Tips for Maintaining Men's Hair Long in Front Shorter in Back

Now that you have the cut, it's important to take proper care of your hair to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Regular trims: To keep the style looking fresh, you'll need to schedule regular trims with your hairstylist every 4-6 weeks.
  • Use quality hair products: Invest in quality hair products such as shampoo, conditioner, and styling products to keep your hair healthy and looking great.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools such as hair dryers and straighteners can damage your hair and make it difficult to maintain the style.
  • Protect your hair: If you're spending time in the sun, be sure to protect your hair with a hat or hair sunscreen.

FAQs About Men's Hair Long in Front Shorter in Back

Q: Is the mullet back in style?

A: Yes, the mullet has made a comeback in recent years, with many celebrities and influencers sporting the look.

Q: Is the mullet easy to maintain?

A: While the mullet does require regular trims, it can be relatively low-maintenance. With the right hair products and care, you can keep the style looking great.

Q: Can anyone pull off the mullet?

A: The mullet can be a versatile look, but it may not be suitable for everyone. It's best to consult with a hairstylist to determine if the look is right for you.

Hair Care for Men's Hair Long in Front Shorter in Back

Proper hair care is crucial for maintaining the health and appearance of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid using hot water, which can damage your hair.
  • Use a conditioner to help detangle your hair and keep it soft.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or bleach on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve and maintain the men's hair long in front shorter in back look with ease. Remember to consult with a trusted hairstylist, invest in quality hair products, and take proper care of your hair to keep it looking great.
