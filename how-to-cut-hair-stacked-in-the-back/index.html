How To Cut Hair Stacked In The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a fresh and trendy haircut that can give your hair more volume and movement? Then you might want to try a stacked haircut! This style is characterized by shorter layers at the back of the head, which create a stacked effect and add texture and depth to the hair.

If you're interested in learning how to cut hair stacked in the back, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll provide you with a detailed guide on how to achieve this look. We'll cover everything from the tools you need to the techniques you should use. Let's get started!

What You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it dry naturally or blow-drying it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips or elastics to secure each section.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the back section. Comb the hair straight down and cut a straight line across the bottom. This will create the foundation for the rest of the haircut.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the top section of the back and comb it down. Cut a horizontal line across the middle of the hair, using the bottom layer as a guide. This layer should be shorter than the bottom layer.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the final section of the back and comb it down. Cut a horizontal line across the top of the hair, using the middle layer as a guide. This layer should be shorter than the middle layer.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Use your scissors to blend the layers together, creating a seamless transition from one length to the next. Focus on the edges and make sure there are no harsh lines.

Step 7: Cut the Sides

Release the front sections of your hair and comb them down. Cut the sides to the desired length, using the back as a guide. You can use clippers for a more precise cut.

Step 8: Check for Balance

Use a mirror to check that both sides of your hair are even and balanced. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 9: Add Texture

If you want to add more texture to your hair, you can use thinning scissors or a razor to create soft, wispy layers.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron or flat iron to add curls or straighten your hair. Finish with hairspray or other styling products.

Tips for Cutting Hair Stacked in the Back

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't undo a bad cut.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair damp while you're cutting it. This will make it easier to control and cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will appear longer than it actually is. Cut it when it's dry to get a more accurate length.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to ensure a clean cut.

FAQs

What is a stacked haircut?

A stacked haircut is a style where the back of the hair is cut into shorter layers, creating a stacked effect. This adds volume and texture to the hair.

What face shape is best for a stacked haircut?

A stacked haircut can work for any face shape, but it's especially flattering for those with a round or heart-shaped face. It can help balance out a wider forehead or round cheeks.

How often should I get a stacked haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. Generally, you should get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

Now that you know how to cut hair stacked in the back, you can try this trendy and versatile style for yourself. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a beautiful and flattering haircut that will make you feel confident and stylish.
