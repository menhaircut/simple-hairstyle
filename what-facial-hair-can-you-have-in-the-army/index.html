What Facial Hair Can You Have In The Army

If you're planning to join the army, one of the things you may be wondering about is what kind of facial hair is allowed. The answer to this question depends on a few different factors, including your rank and the specific branch of the army you're in. In this blog post, we'll go over everything you need to know about what facial hair you can have in the army.

Description

The army has strict grooming standards for its soldiers, and this includes rules about facial hair. The reason for these rules is to ensure that all soldiers present a uniform and professional appearance. How to

First of all, it's important to understand that if you're a new recruit, you will be required to be clean-shaven during basic training. This means that you won't be allowed to have any facial hair at all until you've completed your training.

Once you've completed basic training, the rules about facial hair will depend on your rank and the branch of the army you're in. Generally, the rules are as follows:

Step by Step

Army Regulations

According to Army Regulation 670-1, male soldiers are not allowed to have beards or goatees. However, mustaches are allowed as long as they are neatly trimmed and do not extend beyond the corners of the mouth. The mustache cannot be longer than half an inch and cannot extend down the chin or neck.

Air Force Regulations

In the Air Force, mustaches are allowed as long as they are neatly trimmed and do not extend beyond the corners of the mouth. Beards and goatees are not allowed, and all other facial hair must be clean-shaven. However, if a medical condition prevents a soldier from being clean-shaven, they may be permitted to have a beard or goatee.

Navy Regulations

In the Navy, mustaches are allowed as long as they are neatly trimmed and do not extend beyond the corners of the mouth. Beards and goatees are not allowed, and all other facial hair must be clean-shaven. Exceptions may be made for medical or religious reasons, but the sailor must request permission through the proper channels.

Tips

If you're unsure about the rules for your specific branch of the army, it's always best to consult with your superior officer or refer to the official grooming standards for your branch.

Additionally, it's important to take good care of your facial hair if you are allowed to have it. This means regularly washing and grooming it to keep it looking neat and presentable.

FAQ

Q: Can I have a beard in the army?

A: Generally, no. Beards are not allowed in most branches of the army. However, exceptions may be made for medical or religious reasons.

Q: Can I have a mustache in the army?

A: Yes, mustaches are allowed in most branches of the army as long as they are neatly trimmed and do not extend beyond the corners of the mouth.

Q: Can I have a goatee in the army?

A: No, goatees are not allowed in most branches of the army.

Hair Care

If you are allowed to have facial hair in the army, it's important to take good care of it. This means washing it regularly with a gentle cleanser and using a conditioner to keep it soft and manageable. You should also trim it regularly to keep it looking neat and well-groomed.

Overall, the rules about facial hair in the army are designed to promote a professional appearance and maintain uniformity among soldiers. If you're unsure about the rules for your specific branch of the army, be sure to consult with your superiors and follow the official grooming standards to ensure that you are in compliance.
