How To Cut Long Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Cutting long layers is a great way to add volume, movement, and texture to long hair. Whether you have straight, wavy, or curly hair, layers can help to enhance your natural texture and make your hair look fuller and more dynamic. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut long layers at home, without the need for a professional stylist. Follow these steps to achieve a beautiful, layered haircut that will make you look and feel your best.

What You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • Hair clips

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair with your favorite shampoo and conditioner, then let it air dry or blow dry it using a low heat setting. Comb your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Use your comb to divide your hair into three or four sections. Clip each section up and out of the way, leaving only one section loose for cutting.

Step 3: Decide on Your Length

Decide on the length of your layers. If you want subtle layers, start with a length that is only slightly shorter than the rest of your hair. If you want more dramatic layers, start with a length that is several inches shorter than the rest of your hair. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.

Step 4: Cut the First Layer

Starting at the front of your hair, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at the desired length. Be sure to cut at an angle, rather than straight across, to create a more natural look. Repeat this process for the entire section, cutting all the way around your head.

Step 5: Cut the Remaining Layers

Release the next section of hair and clip the already-cut hair out of the way. Use the same technique to cut the next layer, making sure that it is slightly shorter than the previous layer. Repeat this process for all remaining sections of hair, working your way from front to back.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once all the layers have been cut, use your scissors to blend them together. Take small sections from each layer and trim them so that they blend seamlessly with the layers above and below. This will create a more natural, layered look.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Take a step back and look at your hair in the mirror. Check to make sure that the layers are even and that there are no jagged edges or uneven spots.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, using a curling iron, straightener, or other styling tool to enhance your layers and create volume and texture.

Tips for Cutting Long Layers

  • Take your time and start with a conservative length. You can always cut more if you want shorter layers.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean to avoid split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, especially if you're new to cutting your own hair.
  • Don't be afraid to ask a friend or family member for help, especially when cutting the back of your head.

FAQ

Can I cut my own long layers?

Yes, you can cut your own long layers at home. Just make sure you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully.

How often should I cut my long layers?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's a good idea to trim your layers every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to have it fixed. Alternatively, you can try to blend the mistake into your layers or create a new style that works with the mistake.

Hair Care Tips for Long Layers

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Cutting long layers at home is a great way to save money and achieve a beautiful, layered haircut. Follow these steps and tips to get the look you want, and don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and styles. With a little practice and patience, you can become a pro at cutting your own hair.
