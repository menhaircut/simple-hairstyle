How To Style Wet Hair So It Dries Straight

Written by Daniel Nov 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you may find that your locks tend to dry with a lot of frizz and texture. If you want to achieve a straight, sleek look with your hair, it's essential to learn how to style wet hair so it dries straight. In this article, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step and offer some tips to help you get the best results.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Styling Wet Hair

How to Style Wet Hair So It Dries Straight

The first step in styling wet hair for a straight look is to choose the right products. You'll need a heat protectant spray, a straightening serum or cream, and a blow dryer with a concentrator nozzle attachment. Here's how to get started:

1. Start with clean, damp hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo and conditioner to remove any product buildup or residue. Blot your hair with a towel to remove excess water, but don't rub or twist your hair, as this can cause frizz.

2. Apply a heat protectant spray

Spray a heat protectant product evenly throughout your hair to protect it from damage caused by heat styling tools. Make sure to coat each section of hair thoroughly.

3. Apply a straightening serum or cream

Apply a straightening serum or cream to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

4. Use a concentrator nozzle attachment

Attach a concentrator nozzle to your blow dryer to help direct the airflow and prevent frizz. Use a round brush to smooth out each section of hair as you blow dry it, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.

5. Finish with a blast of cool air

When you're finished blow drying, switch your dryer to the cool setting and give your hair a final blast of cool air to help set the style and reduce frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to styling wet hair so it dries straight:

Step 1: Wash and condition your hair

Start with clean, damp hair. Use a clarifying shampoo and conditioner to remove any buildup or residue from your hair. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water to help seal the cuticle and reduce frizz.

Step 2: Apply a heat protectant spray

Spray a heat protectant product evenly throughout your hair to protect it from damage caused by heat styling tools. Make sure to coat each section of hair thoroughly.

Step 3: Apply a straightening serum or cream

Apply a straightening serum or cream to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Section your hair

Section your hair into manageable pieces using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to blow dry each section and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Step 5: Use a concentrator nozzle attachment

Attach a concentrator nozzle to your blow dryer to help direct the airflow and prevent frizz. Use a round brush to smooth out each section of hair as you blow dry it, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.

Step 6: Finish with a blast of cool air

When you're finished blow drying, switch your dryer to the cool setting and give your hair a final blast of cool air to help set the style and reduce frizz.

Tips for Styling Wet Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you style wet hair so it dries straight:

  • Use a good quality hair dryer with a concentrator nozzle attachment
  • Blot your hair with a microfiber towel to remove excess water and reduce frizz
  • Work in small sections to ensure that each section is properly dried and straightened
  • Avoid brushing your hair while it's wet, as this can cause breakage and damage
  • Use a high-quality straightening serum or cream to help protect your hair and reduce frizz

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my hair without heat?

A: Yes, you can straighten your hair without heat by using a straightening brush or by wrapping your hair tightly around your head and securing it with pins or clips while it dries.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It's best to limit heat styling to no more than once a week to prevent damage and breakage.

Q: What should I do if my hair is still frizzy after blow drying?

A: Try using a smoothing cream or serum to help tame frizz and flyaways. Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause further frizz.

Hair Care Tips for Straight Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your straightened hair:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils and cause dryness
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your hair's natural oils
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis, as this can cause damage and breakage
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment at least once a month to keep your hair moisturized and shiny

With these tips and techniques, you can style wet hair so it dries straight and achieve a sleek, polished look. Remember to use high-quality products, work in small sections, and be patient as you blow dry each section of hair. With practice, you'll be able to achieve the perfect straight style every time!
