Can U Start Dreads With Relaxed Hair: A Step-by-Step Guide

Are you someone who has relaxed hair but wants to start dreadlocks? You may be wondering if it's possible to start dreads with relaxed hair. The answer is yes! In this article, we'll guide you through the process of starting dreads with relaxed hair. We'll cover everything from how to start dreads, to tips for maintaining them, and everything in between. So, let's dive in!

Description

Starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair can be challenging, but it's not impossible. It requires patience, dedication, and the right techniques to achieve the desired results. Dreads are a great way to express your individuality and embrace your natural hair. Whether you have type 3 or type 4 hair, starting dreads with relaxed hair is a journey that requires commitment.

How To

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

The first step to starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair is to prepare your hair. You'll need to stop relaxing your hair and allow it to grow out for at least 2-3 inches. This is because relaxed hair is weaker and more prone to breakage, which can hinder the locking process. It's also important to keep your hair clean and moisturized, as dry hair can make it difficult for the hair to lock.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

The next step is to section your hair. You can do this by parting your hair into small to medium-sized sections using a comb. Make sure the sections are even in size, as this will ensure that your dreads are uniform in size and shape. You can also use rubber bands or clips to keep the sections in place while you're working on them.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

The next step is to backcomb your hair. This involves teasing your hair with a comb in a backward motion to create knots or tangles. This process helps to create the "dreaded" look. However, be careful not to backcomb too aggressively, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

Step 4: Palm Roll Your Hair

After backcombing your hair, you'll need to palm roll your hair. This involves rolling your hair between your palms to smooth out any tangles or knots. This process helps to create a tighter, more defined dreadlock. You can also use a locking gel or wax to help hold your dreads in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on your hair, as this can cause buildup and make it difficult for your hair to lock.
  • Keep your hair moisturized and hydrated by using a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Be patient and allow your hair to lock naturally. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Visit a professional loctician for maintenance and upkeep of your dreads.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

Can you start dreads with relaxed hair?

Yes, it's possible to start dreadlocks with relaxed hair. However, it requires patience and dedication to achieve the desired results.

How long does it take to start dreads with relaxed hair?

The time it takes to start dreadlocks with relaxed hair varies depending on the individual's hair type and texture. It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year or more for the hair to fully lock.

Can you wash your hair with dreads?

Yes, you can wash your hair with dreads. However, it's important to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using heavy oils or products on your hair.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential when starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, strong hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Moisturize your hair with a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth.

Starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair is a journey that requires patience and dedication. With the right techniques and proper hair care, you can achieve the desired results and enjoy your beautiful, natural dreads.
