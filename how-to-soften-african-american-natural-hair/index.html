How To Soften African American Natural Hair

Are you tired of dealing with rough and dry African American natural hair? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many African American women struggle with keeping their hair soft and manageable. In this article, we'll provide you with tips and tricks on how to soften your natural hair.

Description

African American hair is naturally dry and prone to breakage. This is due to the hair's natural curl pattern which makes it difficult for natural oils to travel down the hair shaft. Therefore, it's important to take extra care of your hair to keep it soft and manageable. In this article, we'll show you how to soften your African American natural hair.

How to Soften African American Natural Hair

There are many ways to soften your African American natural hair. Here are some methods you can try:

1. Moisturize Your Hair

The key to softening African American natural hair is to keep it moisturized. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized throughout the day.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair once a week can help soften and hydrate your hair. Use a deep conditioner that contains natural ingredients such as avocado, coconut oil, or shea butter.

3. Use a Hair Mask

A hair mask can help nourish and soften your hair. Apply a hair mask once a week and leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour. Rinse it off with warm water and follow up with a conditioner.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to soften your African American natural hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Deep Condition Your Hair

Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour. Rinse it off with warm water.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to keep it moisturized throughout the day.

Step 4: Use a Hair Mask

Apply a hair mask once a week and leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour. Rinse it off with warm water and follow up with a conditioner.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Tips for Softening African American Natural Hair

Here are some additional tips for softening your African American natural hair:

1. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help reduce friction and prevent breakage. This can help keep your hair soft and manageable.

2. Protect Your Hair at Night

Wear a satin bonnet or scarf at night to protect your hair from rubbing against your pillowcase. This can help keep your hair soft and smooth.

3. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle. Wash your hair no more than once a week to keep it soft and healthy.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about softening African American natural hair:

1. Can I use a hair straightener to soften my hair?

No, using a hair straightener can actually damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. It's best to avoid heat styling tools and use natural methods to soften your hair.

2. How often should I deep condition my hair?

You should deep condition your hair once a week to keep it soft and hydrated.

3. Can I use a regular conditioner as a leave-in conditioner?

No, regular conditioners are not formulated to be used as leave-in conditioners. Use a product specifically designed as a leave-in conditioner.

Hair Care for Softened African American Natural Hair

Once you've softened your African American natural hair, it's important to maintain it. Here are some tips for caring for softened hair:

1. Keep Your Hair Hydrated

Continue to use moisturizing products to keep your hair hydrated and soft.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Avoid using heat styling tools and use a heat protectant spray if you must use them.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can soften your African American natural hair and keep it healthy and manageable.
