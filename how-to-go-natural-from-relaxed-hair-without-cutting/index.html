How To Go Natural From Relaxed Hair Without Cutting

Written by Bardi Oct 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of relaxing your hair every few weeks and dealing with the damage that comes with it? Going natural might be the solution you’ve been looking for. However, the thought of cutting off your relaxed hair may seem daunting. The good news is that there are ways to transition to natural hair without having to do the big chop. In this article, we will show you how to go natural from relaxed hair without cutting.

How to

The key to transitioning to natural hair without cutting is to be patient and gentle with your hair. Here are the steps you can follow:

Step 1: Stop Relaxing Your Hair

The first step in transitioning to natural hair is to stop relaxing your hair. This might be the most challenging step for some, but it’s necessary if you want to go natural. You can start by stretching your relaxer appointments for longer periods until you stop relaxing your hair altogether.

Step 2: Moisturize Your Hair

Relaxed hair is often dry and brittle, so it’s essential to keep your hair moisturized during the transition process. Use a deep conditioner at least once a week and a leave-in conditioner after every wash. You can also use natural oils like coconut oil, jojoba oil, or argan oil to seal in moisture.

Step 3: Style Your Hair

As your hair grows, you’ll need to find ways to style it to blend the relaxed and natural textures. Braid-outs, twist-outs, and bantu knots are great styles that can help you achieve a seamless transition. You can also use accessories like headbands, scarves, and hats to switch up your look.

Step 4: Trim Your Ends

As your natural hair grows, it’s essential to trim your ends regularly to get rid of the relaxed ends. You can do this yourself or visit a stylist who specializes in natural hair. Trimming your ends will help prevent breakage and split ends.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you transition to natural hair without cutting:

  • Be patient. Going natural is a process that takes time.
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping with a satin bonnet or pillowcase.
  • Avoid heat styling tools like flat irons and blow dryers.
  • Use products specifically designed for natural hair.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to transition to natural hair?

A: The length of time it takes to transition to natural hair varies from person to person. It can take anywhere from six months to two years or more.

Q: Can I still wear my hair straight during the transition process?

A: Yes, you can still wear your hair straight, but it’s essential to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. Try roller sets or flexi rods as a heatless alternative.

Q: Can I color my hair during the transition process?

A: It’s best to avoid coloring your hair during the transition process as it can cause additional damage to your hair.

Hair Care

Transitioning to natural hair requires a lot of care and attention. Here are some additional tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Wash your hair once a week with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth.

Transitioning to natural hair without cutting can be a challenging but rewarding journey. With patience, care, and the right products, you can achieve beautiful and healthy natural hair.
