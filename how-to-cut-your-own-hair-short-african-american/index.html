How To Cut Your Own Hair Short African American A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Nov 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're an African American woman looking to save some money on haircuts or simply want to try something new, cutting your own hair at home can be a great option. However, cutting short hair can be tricky, especially if you're doing it for the first time. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair short African American, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you along the way.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Short African American

Before we begin, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be difficult and may not produce the same results as going to a professional stylist. However, with the right tools and technique, you can still achieve a great look. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Mirror or two mirrors (one in front and one behind you)

Step 2: Decide on Your Haircut

Next, decide on the type of haircut you want. Do you want a bob, a pixie cut, or something in between? Look for inspiration online or in magazines, and decide on the length and style that you want.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before cutting it. This will help you see your hair's natural texture and make it easier to cut.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, back, and two sides. Use hair clips or elastics to keep each section in place.

Step 5: Cut the Back

Start with the back section. Use your clippers to trim the hair at the nape of your neck, starting with a longer guard and working down to a shorter guard. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length.

Step 6: Cut the Sides

Next, move on to the side sections. Use your clippers to trim the hair around your ears, again starting with a longer guard and working down to a shorter guard. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length.

Step 7: Cut the Top

Finally, move on to the top section. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, holding the hair between your fingers and cutting straight across. Gradually work your way around the top section, checking your progress in the mirror.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair short African American:

  1. Wet your hair and comb it straight back.
  2. Use your clippers to trim the hair at the nape of your neck, starting with a longer guard and working down to a shorter guard. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length.
  3. Move on to the side sections. Use your clippers to trim the hair around your ears, again starting with a longer guard and working down to a shorter guard. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length.
  4. Divide the top section of your hair into two sections, from ear to ear. Clip the front section away from the back section.
  5. Starting at the back of the head, use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, holding the hair between your fingers and cutting straight across.
  6. Gradually work your way around the top section, checking your progress in the mirror.
  7. Release the front section of your hair and comb it forward.
  8. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, holding the hair between your fingers and cutting straight across.
  9. Gradually work your way around the front section, checking your progress in the mirror.
  10. Check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Short Hair

Cutting short hair can be challenging, but here are some tips to help you along the way:

  • Start with a longer guard and work down to a shorter guard to prevent cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length.
  • Check your progress in the mirror frequently.
  • Take your time and be patient. It's better to cut a little at a time than to cut too much at once.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair short African American:

Is it easy to cut your own hair short African American?

No, cutting your own hair can be difficult, especially if you're doing it for the first time. However, with the right tools and technique, you can still achieve a great look.

What tools do I need to cut my own hair short African American?

You'll need scissors, clippers, a comb, hair clips or elastics, and a mirror or two mirrors (one in front and one behind you).

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my own hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your own hair, don't panic. Try to even out the mistake by cutting the rest of your hair to the same length. If the mistake is too drastic, you may need to seek professional help.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your own hair short African American, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own hair short African American at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and check your progress frequently. Happy cutting!
