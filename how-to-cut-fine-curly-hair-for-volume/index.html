Description

Fine curly hair is characterized by its thin strands and natural curl pattern. This type of hair can be prone to frizz and can lack volume and body. By cutting fine curly hair properly, you can add volume and create a more defined curl pattern. The key is to use techniques that enhance the natural curl, while also removing excess weight that can weigh down the hair.

Layering involves cutting the hair at different lengths, which helps to create volume and movement. To do this, start by sectioning the hair into four or five parts, depending on the thickness of the hair. Then, starting at the back of the head, take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, starting at the mid-lengths and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process for each section of hair, being careful not to cut too much off at once.
