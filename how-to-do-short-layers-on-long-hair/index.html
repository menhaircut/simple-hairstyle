How To Do Short Layers On Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some volume and texture to your long hair, short layers are a great option. Not only do they add dimension to your hair, but they also make it easier to style. In this article, we'll show you how to do short layers on long hair in a few simple steps.

Description

Short layers on long hair involve cutting the top layers of hair shorter than the bottom layers. This creates a layered look that adds volume and texture to your hair. Short layers are ideal for people with long, straight hair who want to add some movement and depth to their hair. They also work well for people with curly hair who want to reduce bulk and frizz.

How to

To achieve short layers on long hair, you will need:

  • A comb
  • A pair of sharp scissors
  • A mirror

Here are the steps to follow:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and style.
  2. Part your hair down the middle and comb it straight down.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and hold it straight up.
  4. Using your scissors, cut the hair in a straight line, about an inch or two from the ends. This will create the first layer.
  5. Continue taking small sections of hair from the top of your head and cutting them in a straight line until you've created as many layers as you want.
  6. Once you've finished cutting, comb your hair and check to make sure the layers are even.
  7. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim the layers until they're all the same length.
  8. Style your hair as desired.

Step-by-Step

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will make it easier to cut and style. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and smooth.

Step 2: Part your hair down the middle

Use a comb to part your hair down the middle. Make sure the part is straight and even.

Step 3: Take a small section of hair from the top of your head

Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and hold it straight up. This will be the first section you cut.

Step 4: Cut the hair in a straight line

Using your scissors, cut the hair in a straight line, about an inch or two from the ends. Make sure the cut is even and straight.

Step 5: Continue cutting small sections of hair

Continue taking small sections of hair from the top of your head and cutting them in a straight line until you've created as many layers as you want. Remember to cut each section at the same length.

Step 6: Check for evenness

Once you've finished cutting, comb your hair and check to make sure the layers are even. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim the layers until they're all the same length.

Step 7: Style your hair

Style your hair as desired. Short layers on long hair work well with loose waves, curls, or a straight and sleek look.

Tips

  • Start with a small amount of hair and cut gradually. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will appear shorter when it dries.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your short layers.

FAQ

Can you do short layers on curly hair?

Yes, short layers work well on curly hair. They help to reduce bulk and frizz, while adding shape and definition to your curls.

How many layers should I have on my long hair?

This depends on your personal preference and the thickness of your hair. Generally, it's best to start with a few layers and gradually add more if needed.

Will short layers make my hair look thinner?

No, short layers actually add volume and texture to your hair. They create movement and dimension, making your hair appear fuller and thicker.

Hair Care

To keep your short layers looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. Instead, opt for air drying or using a diffuser to enhance your natural curls. Lastly, protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf when outside.
