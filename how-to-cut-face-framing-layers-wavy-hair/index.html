How To Cut Face Framing Layers For Wavy Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 10, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have wavy hair, adding face framing layers can enhance your natural texture and give you a fresh new look. In this article, we’ll give you step-by-step instructions on how to cut face framing layers for wavy hair, as well as tips, FAQs, and hair care advice. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Face Framing Layers for Wavy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Cutting Face Framing Layers
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

Step-by-Step Instructions

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You’ll need sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. Here’s how to cut face framing layers for wavy hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it with a diffuser. You want your natural waves to be present when you cut your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use the comb to part your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference. Then, clip the top section of your hair up and away from your face.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Start by cutting the first layer of face framing hair. Take a small section of hair from the front of your face and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting from your cheekbone and angling down towards your chin. Repeat this step on the other side of your face.

Step 4: Cut the Second Layer

Release the top section of your hair and section off the next layer of face framing hair. Repeat the same process as before, cutting at an angle from your cheekbone towards your chin.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut both layers, use the comb to blend them together by combing the hair downwards and trimming any uneven pieces. You want the layers to look seamless and natural.

Tips for Cutting Face Framing Layers

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect face framing layers for wavy hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little bit at a time. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and a jagged cut.
  • Cut your hair when it’s dry to avoid any surprises when your hair shrinks up as it dries.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to err on the side of caution and cut less than you think you need.

FAQs

How often should I cut my face framing layers?

It’s recommended to trim your face framing layers every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Can I cut my own face framing layers?

Yes, you can cut your own face framing layers, but it’s always best to have a professional do it if you’re unsure or have never done it before.

Will face framing layers work for all face shapes?

Yes, face framing layers can work for all face shapes. The key is to adjust the length and angle of the layers to flatter your face shape.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

Now that you’ve cut your face framing layers, it’s important to take care of your wavy hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your natural waves without causing damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can dry out your hair and cause damage.

With these tips and tricks, you can cut face framing layers for your wavy hair and keep it healthy and beautiful. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun experimenting with your new look!
