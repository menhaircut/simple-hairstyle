Is Unnatural Hair Color Unprofessional

Written by Daniel Jun 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we enter the year 2023, the world of fashion and beauty continues to evolve. One of the most popular trends that has emerged in recent years is the use of unnatural hair colors. From pastel pinks to electric blues, many people are choosing to express themselves through their hair. However, this begs the question: is unnatural hair color unprofessional? In this blog post, we will explore the topic and provide you with tips and tricks for rocking colorful locks while still maintaining a professional image.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Unnatural hair colors have gained popularity in recent years, especially among younger people. Hair dyes now come in a wide range of colors, from pastels to neons to metallics. While these colors can be a fun and creative way to express yourself, they can also be seen as unprofessional in certain settings.

Many workplaces have a dress code in place that includes guidelines for hair and makeup. While it may not explicitly say that unnatural hair colors are not allowed, it could be seen as an unspoken rule. Additionally, some industries, such as finance or law, have a conservative culture that values traditional appearances.

How to

If you want to rock an unnatural hair color but also maintain a professional image, there are a few things you can do:

1. Check your workplace dress code

Before dyeing your hair, check if your workplace has a dress code in place. If it does, make sure to follow the guidelines for hair and makeup.

2. Choose a subtle color

If you're unsure about whether an unnatural hair color is appropriate, choose a subtle shade. Pastels and muted colors are more likely to be accepted in professional settings.

3. Style your hair appropriately

Even if you have an unnatural hair color, you can still style your hair in a professional manner. Keep your hair neat and tidy, and avoid wild hairstyles that could be seen as unprofessional.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to dyeing your hair an unnatural color:

1. Choose your color

There are many unnatural hair colors to choose from, so pick one that you love and that will suit your skin tone.

2. Bleach your hair

If you have dark hair, you will need to bleach it first to get the most vibrant color. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully and do a patch test first.

3. Apply the dye

Once your hair is bleached, apply the dye according to the instructions on the packet. Be sure to wear gloves and protect your clothes and skin.

4. Rinse and condition

Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water and apply a conditioning treatment to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Tips

Here are some tips for maintaining your unnatural hair color:

1. Use color-safe shampoo and conditioner

Regular shampoo and conditioner can strip the color from your hair, so use products that are specifically designed for colored hair.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make the color fade faster, so try to avoid using them as much as possible.

3. Touch up your roots

If you have a bright or vibrant hair color, your roots will start to show quickly. Touch them up regularly to keep your color looking fresh.

FAQ

Q: Is it unprofessional to have unnatural hair color?

A: It depends on the workplace and industry. Some workplaces have a dress code that includes guidelines for hair and makeup, while others are more relaxed. If you're unsure, it's best to check with your employer or HR department.

Q: Will an unnatural hair color affect my job prospects?

A: It's possible. While many employers are becoming more accepting of unconventional appearances, some may still view unnatural hair colors as unprofessional. It's always best to err on the side of caution, especially if you're interviewing for a job.

Hair Care

Regardless of whether you have an unnatural hair color or not, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Wash your hair regularly

Washing your hair regularly helps to remove dirt and oil, which can clog your hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

2. Use a wide-toothed comb

Using a wide-toothed comb helps to prevent damage and breakage, especially if your hair is wet.

3. Get regular trims

Getting regular trims helps to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, while unnatural hair colors can be a fun and creative way to express yourself, they may not always be appropriate in professional settings. If you're unsure about whether an unnatural hair color is acceptable, it's best to check with your employer or HR department. By following our tips and tricks, you can rock colorful locks while still maintaining a professional image.
