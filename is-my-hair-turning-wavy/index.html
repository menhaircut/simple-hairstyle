Is My Hair Turning Wavy How To Identify And Care For Wavy Hair

Written by Luffy Jun 05, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you noticing a change in your hair texture? Maybe your once straight hair is starting to form loose waves. This can be a confusing and frustrating experience, but don't worry, you're not alone. In this article, we'll explore the question "is my hair turning wavy?" and provide tips for identifying and caring for your new wavy locks.

How to Identify Wavy Hair

Identifying wavy hair can be tricky, especially if you've had straight hair your entire life. Here are some common characteristics of wavy hair:

  • Slight bends or waves throughout the hair
  • Natural volume and body
  • Can frizz easily
  • May have a mix of straight and wavy sections

If you're still unsure if your hair is turning wavy, try air-drying your hair and see if any waves form. You can also consult with a hairstylist for their professional opinion.

Why Is My Hair Turning Wavy?

There are a few reasons why your hair may be turning wavy:

  • Genetics: Your hair texture is determined by your genetics, and it's possible that your hair is naturally wavy but was previously straightened through heat styling or chemical treatments.
  • Hormonal changes: Hormonal changes, such as pregnancy or menopause, can impact the texture of your hair.
  • Damage: Over-styling or chemical treatments can damage your hair, causing it to become wavy or curly.

How to Care for Wavy Hair

Now that you've identified your wavy hair, it's important to adjust your hair care routine to keep your locks healthy and defined. Here are some tips for caring for wavy hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a hydrating conditioner to the ends of your hair to keep it moisturized.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it's wet.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry to prevent frizz.
  • Apply a curl defining cream or gel to enhance your natural waves.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent further damage.

FAQ About Wavy Hair

Can I still straighten my wavy hair?

Yes, you can still straighten your wavy hair, but it's important to use heat protectant and not overdo it with heat styling tools. Remember, your hair is more prone to damage now that it's wavy.

What should I do if my waves are frizzy?

Frizz is common with wavy hair, but you can combat it by applying a smoothing serum or oil to your hair after styling. Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day to prevent added frizz.

Final Thoughts

Is your hair turning wavy? Embrace your new texture and adjust your hair care routine to keep your waves healthy and defined. Remember, wavy hair is beautiful and unique, and with the right care, it can be your favorite feature.
