How To Cut Layers Into Shoulder Length Curly Hair

Written by Eveline Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can be challenging to cut layers into it. Layers add dimension and movement to curly hair, but if not done correctly, they can cause frizz and unwanted volume. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut layers into shoulder length curly hair. We will also provide you with tips, FAQs, and hair care tips to maintain your curly hair's health and style.

  2. Step by Step Guide

How to Cut Layers into Shoulder Length Curly Hair

Before you start cutting your curly hair, it is essential to have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a spray bottle filled with water, and a wide-tooth comb. Here is how to cut layers into shoulder length curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Once your hair is washed, detangle it using a wide-tooth comb. Make sure to remove all knots and tangles from your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts. Use a hair clip to hold each section in place. Start by dividing your hair in half, from the front to the back. Then, divide each half in half again, from the ear to the crown of your head.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Take one section of hair and spray it lightly with water. Comb through the section, ensuring that there are no tangles. Then, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way around your head.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all the sections of your hair, it is time to blend the layers. Take a comb and comb through your hair. Look for any uneven areas and trim them as necessary.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You can use a diffuser to enhance your curls or blow-dry your hair straight.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut layers into shoulder length curly hair:

  1. Wash and detangle your hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts.
  3. Spray each section with water and comb through it.
  4. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up.
  5. Repeat step 4 for each section of hair.
  6. Blend the layers using a comb.
  7. Trim any uneven areas as necessary.
  8. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you cut layers into curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Cut at an angle to create soft layers.
  • Be patient and take your time.
  • Blend the layers well to avoid any choppy or uneven areas.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers into curly hair:

Q: Can I cut my curly hair myself?

A: Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself, but it is best to have a professional do it if you are unsure.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It is recommended to cut curly hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

Q: Will cutting layers cause my curly hair to frizz?

A: Cutting layers should not cause your curly hair to frizz if done correctly. However, if you cut too much or cut unevenly, it can result in frizz and unwanted volume.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining healthy and beautiful curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day.

Cutting layers into shoulder length curly hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful and stylish look. Remember to be patient and take your time, and always consult a professional if you are unsure. With proper care and maintenance, your curly hair will stay healthy and beautiful for years to come.
