What Music Did They Listen To In The 1960S

Introduction

The 1960s was a decade of significant cultural and social change around the world. One of the most significant changes was the emergence of new music genres that reflected the changing times. The 1960s was a time of protest, civil rights movements, and the Vietnam War. It was also a time of great music, with many legendary artists and bands creating timeless classics that continue to be popular today. In this article, we will explore the music that was popular in the 1960s.

Rock Music

Rock music was one of the most popular genres in the 1960s. It was a time when many legendary rock bands emerged, such as The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, and Led Zeppelin. These bands created classic rock songs that continue to be popular today. The Beatles, for example, had numerous hits in the 1960s, such as "Hey Jude," "Let It Be," and "A Hard Day's Night." The Rolling Stones also had several hits, including "Satisfaction," "Paint It Black," and "Jumpin' Jack Flash." Led Zeppelin, on the other hand, had hits such as "Stairway to Heaven," "Whole Lotta Love," and "Kashmir."

Folk Music

Folk music was another popular genre in the 1960s. It was a time when many folk artists emerged, such as Bob Dylan, Joan Baez, and Pete Seeger. These artists created classic folk songs that reflected the times and the social issues of the day. Bob Dylan, for example, had hits such as "Blowin' in the Wind," "The Times They Are A-Changin'," and "Like a Rolling Stone." Joan Baez had hits such as "Diamonds and Rust" and "We Shall Overcome." Pete Seeger had hits such as "Where Have All the Flowers Gone" and "Turn! Turn! Turn!"

Psychedelic Music

Psychedelic music was a new genre that emerged in the 1960s. It was a time of experimentation and exploration, and psychedelic music reflected this. Bands such as The Doors, Jimi Hendrix Experience, and Pink Floyd created classic psychedelic songs that were unlike anything else that had been heard before. The Doors had hits such as "Light My Fire," "Break On Through," and "The End." Jimi Hendrix Experience had hits such as "Purple Haze," "Hey Joe," and "All Along the Watchtower." Pink Floyd had hits such as "Interstellar Overdrive," "See Emily Play," and "Arnold Layne."

Soul Music

Soul music was another popular genre in the 1960s. It was a time when many legendary soul artists emerged, such as Aretha Franklin, Otis Redding, and James Brown. These artists created classic soul songs that continue to be popular today. Aretha Franklin had hits such as "Respect," "Chain of Fools," and "Think." Otis Redding had hits such as "Sittin' On the Dock of the Bay," "Try a Little Tenderness," and "I've Been Loving You Too Long." James Brown had hits such as "Papa's Got a Brand New Bag," "I Got You (I Feel Good)," and "It's a Man's Man's Man's World."

Pop Music

Pop music was also popular in the 1960s. It was a time when many pop artists emerged, such as The Beach Boys, The Supremes, and The Four Seasons. These artists created classic pop songs that continue to be popular today. The Beach Boys had hits such as "Good Vibrations," "Help Me, Rhonda," and "Surfin' USA." The Supremes had hits such as "Baby Love," "Stop! In the Name of Love," and "You Can't Hurry Love." The Four Seasons had hits such as "Sherry," "Big Girls Don't Cry," and "Walk Like a Man."

Jazz Music

Jazz music was also popular in the 1960s. It was a time when many legendary jazz artists emerged, such as Miles Davis, John Coltrane, and Dave Brubeck. These artists created classic jazz songs that continue to be popular today. Miles Davis had hits such as "So What," "All Blues," and "Round Midnight." John Coltrane had hits such as "My Favorite Things," "Giant Steps," and "A Love Supreme." Dave Brubeck had hits such as "Take Five," "Blue Rondo a la Turk," and "Unsquare Dance."

Conclusion

The 1960s was a time of significant cultural and social change, and music played a significant role in reflecting these changes. In this article, we explored the popular music genres of the 1960s, including rock, folk, psychedelic, soul, pop, and jazz music. Many legendary artists and bands emerged during this period, creating timeless classics that continue to be popular today. Whether you're a fan of rock, folk, or jazz music, the 1960s had something for everyone.
