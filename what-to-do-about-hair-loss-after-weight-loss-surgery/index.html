What To Do About Hair Loss After Weight Loss Surgery

Written by Frank Jun 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss after weight loss surgery? You're not alone. Many people who undergo bariatric surgery, such as gastric bypass or sleeve gastrectomy, may experience temporary hair loss due to the rapid weight loss and changes in their body. However, there are steps you can take to help minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Here's what you need to know.

How to

1. Increase your protein intake

Protein is essential for healthy hair growth, so make sure you're getting enough in your diet. Try to consume at least 60-80 grams of protein per day. Good sources of protein include lean meats, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, and nuts.

2. Take your vitamins

Make sure you're taking a daily multivitamin that contains iron, biotin, and other essential vitamins and minerals for hair growth. Talk to your doctor about any additional supplements you may need.

3. Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as dyes, perms, or relaxers. These can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Stick to gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners.

4. Be gentle with your hair

Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to detangle your hair. Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, as these can cause tension and breakage.

5. Consider hair extensions

If you're self-conscious about your hair loss, consider getting hair extensions. These can help add volume and thickness to your hair while it's growing back.

Step by Step

1. Speak with your doctor

If you're experiencing hair loss after weight loss surgery, speak with your doctor. They can help determine if there are any underlying medical issues that may be causing your hair loss.

2. Increase your protein intake

Eat a diet rich in protein to promote healthy hair growth.

3. Take your vitamins

Take a daily multivitamin that contains iron, biotin, and other essential vitamins and minerals for hair growth.

4. Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as dyes, perms, or relaxers.

5. Be gentle with your hair

Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's wet, and avoid tight hairstyles.

6. Consider hair extensions

If you're self-conscious about your hair loss, consider getting hair extensions.

Tips

- Be patient. Hair loss after weight loss surgery is usually temporary and will improve over time.

- Protect your hair from the sun and heat by wearing a hat or using a heat protectant spray.

- Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.

- Drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Q: How long does hair loss after weight loss surgery last?

A: Hair loss after weight loss surgery is usually temporary and will improve over time. It can take several months for your hair to grow back.

Q: Can medication help with hair loss after weight loss surgery?

A: Your doctor may prescribe medication to help promote hair growth, but this is usually only necessary in severe cases.

Q: Will hair loss after weight loss surgery affect my ability to exercise?

A: No, hair loss after weight loss surgery should not affect your ability to exercise.

Hair Care

Remember, hair loss after weight loss surgery is usually temporary and will improve over time. In the meantime, take care of your hair by eating a healthy diet, taking your vitamins, and avoiding harsh chemicals and hairstyles. Be patient and gentle with your hair, and consider getting hair extensions if you're self-conscious about your hair loss. If you're concerned about your hair loss, speak with your doctor.
