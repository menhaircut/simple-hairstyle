How To Protect Hair Before Blow Drying Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jul 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Blow drying your hair is a quick and easy way to get the perfect hairstyle, but it can also cause damage to your hair. The high heat from the blow dryer can dry out your hair, leaving it frizzy and weak. That's why it's important to take steps to protect your hair before blow drying it. In this article, we'll give you some tips and tricks on how to protect your hair before blow drying it.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Protect Hair Before Blow Drying
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips and Tricks
  4. FAQ

How to Protect Hair Before Blow Drying

The first step to protecting your hair before blow drying it is to make sure it's clean and conditioned. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for your hair type. This will help to prevent damage and breakage.

After washing your hair, gently towel dry it to remove excess water. Don't rub your hair vigorously with the towel, as this can cause damage to the hair cuticles. Instead, use a soft, microfiber towel to gently pat your hair dry.

Next, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. This will help to protect your hair from the heat of the blow dryer. Be sure to apply the heat protectant evenly throughout your hair, paying special attention to the ends.

Finally, use a wide-toothed comb or a detangling brush to gently comb out any tangles in your hair. This will prevent breakage and damage when you blow dry your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to protect your hair before blow drying it:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  2. Gently towel dry your hair
  3. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair
  4. Use a wide-toothed comb or detangling brush to comb out any tangles
  5. Blow dry your hair using a low heat setting
  6. Use a cool shot button on your blow dryer to cool down your hair and set your style

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help protect your hair before blow drying:

  • Use a high-quality blow dryer that has multiple heat and speed settings
  • Don't blow dry your hair when it's soaking wet - let it air dry for a few minutes first
  • Blow dry your hair in sections to ensure that each section is thoroughly dry
  • Use a round brush to add volume and texture to your hair while blow drying

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about protecting your hair before blow drying:

How often should I blow dry my hair?

It's best to limit blow drying to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair.

What should I do if my hair feels dry and damaged after blow drying?

If your hair feels dry and damaged after blow drying, use a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair. You can also try using a leave-in conditioner to help protect your hair from heat damage.

Is it better to air dry my hair instead of blow drying it?

Air drying your hair is the healthiest option, but blow drying can be a quick and easy way to get the perfect hairstyle.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help keep your hair healthy and strong:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or detangling brush to gently comb out any tangles
  • Don't use hot tools on your hair every day - limit use to once or twice a week
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth

By following these tips and tricks, you can protect your hair from damage and keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
