How To Box Braid Your Own Hair Boy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Box braids are a popular protective hairstyle for both boys and girls. They are low-maintenance and can last for weeks, making them an ideal choice for busy parents and kids. If you're a boy and want to learn how to box braid your own hair, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll take you through the steps of creating your own box braids, from start to finish.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Braiding Your Own Hair
  4. FAQs

How to Box Braid Your Own Hair Boy

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over the basics of box braids. Box braids are individual plaits that are sectioned off into squares, or boxes. They can be done on any hair type, but work best on hair that is at least 2 inches long. Here's what you'll need to get started:

  • Rat-tail comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Box braid hair extensions
  • Scissors (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any dirt or product buildup, making it easier to braid. Make sure to detangle your hair while it's wet, using a wide-tooth comb.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your rat-tail comb, section your hair into small, equal-sized squares. Use hair clips or ties to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Add the Extensions

Take one of your box braid extensions and fold it in half. Place the folded end at the root of one of your hair sections, and use your rat-tail comb to create a small hole in the center of the section. Thread the folded end of the extension through the hole, and pull it through until the extension is even on both sides.

Step 4: Start Braiding

Divide the section of hair into three equal parts, and begin braiding as you would a regular three-strand braid. As you braid, add in more hair from the section, along with the extension. Keep braiding until you reach the end of the hair, then secure the braid with a hair tie or clip.

Step 5: Repeat

Repeat steps 3 and 4 for each of your hair sections, until your entire head is braided. Make sure to keep the braids even in size and tension, to prevent any discomfort or unevenness.

Tips for Braiding Your Own Hair

  • Start with clean, detangled hair to make the braiding process easier.
  • Use a rat-tail comb to create even sections, and hair clips or ties to keep them in place.
  • Take your time and braid slowly, to ensure even tension and a neat finish.
  • Use hair extensions that match your hair color or desired look.
  • Trim any excess extension hair at the end of the braid, for a clean finish.

FAQs

Can boys wear box braids?

Yes, box braids are a versatile hairstyle that can be worn by anyone, regardless of gender. Boys can rock box braids just as well as girls.

How long do box braids last?

Box braids can last anywhere from 4-8 weeks, depending on how well they are maintained. To make them last longer, avoid excessive pulling, tugging, or manipulation, and keep them moisturized and clean.

Hair Care for Box Braids

Once your box braids are in, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your braids every 2-3 weeks, using a sulfate-free shampoo and warm water.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your braids.
  • Keep your scalp moisturized with a light oil, such as jojoba or almond oil.
  • Protect your braids at night by wearing a satin or silk bonnet or scarf.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you're ready to box braid your own hair boy. Whether you're looking for a new protective style or just want to switch up your look, box braids are a great choice for boys of all ages.
