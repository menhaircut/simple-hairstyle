How To Fade Your Own Hair Male A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Fading your own hair as a male can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done at home. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of how to fade your own hair male style. From the necessary tools to the step-by-step process, we have everything you need to know to achieve a professional-looking fade.

Table of Contents:

  • Tools Needed
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Using the Clippers
  • Creating the Fade
  • Blending the Hair
  • Touching Up the Fade
  • Tips for Fading Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Fading

Tools Needed:

Before you begin, you will need the following tools:

  • Clippers with multiple guards
  • Comb
  • Hair scissors
  • Handheld mirror
  • Hair cutting cape or towel
  • Clippers oil
  • Disinfectant spray

Make sure your clippers have multiple guards to help you achieve different lengths of hair. You will also need hair scissors to trim the top of your hair.

Preparing Your Hair:

Before you start fading your hair, it is important to prepare it properly. Start by washing and drying your hair. Comb it straight and make sure there are no tangles. Use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head clearly.

Next, put on your hair cutting cape or towel to protect your clothes from hair clippings. You can also use a damp towel around your neck to keep yourself cool during the process.

Using the Clippers:

Start by selecting the clipper guard you want to use. For a fade, start with the longest guard and work your way down to the shortest guard. Make sure the clipper is oiled and clean before you start.

Begin at the bottom of your hairline and work your way up. Use long, even strokes with the clippers to create a straight line. Move the clippers up and down to cut the hair evenly. Make sure to use a comb to lift the hair and hold it in place while you are cutting.

Cutting the Sides:

When cutting the sides, start at the bottom and work your way up. Use the same technique as before, moving the clippers up and down to cut the hair evenly. Use the longest guard first and work your way down to the shortest guard.

Cutting the Back:

When cutting the back of your hair, use the handheld mirror to see clearly. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the same technique as before.

Creating the Fade:

Once you have cut the sides and back of your hair, it is time to create the fade. Change to a shorter guard and start at the bottom of your hairline. Use shorter strokes to create a faded effect. Blend the hair from the shorter length into the longer length by moving the clippers up and down.

Continue to change the guard length and blend the hair until you have achieved the desired fade effect.

Blending the Hair:

Use hair scissors to trim the top of your hair. Make sure to blend the top of your hair into the fade by trimming it gradually. Use your fingers to blend the hair together and make sure there are no harsh lines.

Touching Up the Fade:

After you have finished the fade, use the handheld mirror to check for any missed spots. Use the clippers to touch up any areas that need it.

Tips for Fading Your Own Hair:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to the shortest guard.
  • Use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head clearly.
  • Take your time and work in sections to achieve the best results.
  • Use hair scissors to blend the top of your hair into the fade.
  • Check for any missed spots with the handheld mirror and touch up as needed.

FAQs:

How often should I fade my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade grows out. On average, you should fade your hair every 2-3 weeks to maintain the style.

Do I need to use clippers to fade my hair?

Clippers are the best tool to use for fading your hair, but you can also use scissors if you are experienced with them.

Can I fade my hair if it is curly?

Yes, you can fade your hair if it is curly. Just make sure to use the right guard length and take your time to achieve the desired effect.

Hair Care After Fading:

After you have faded your hair, it is important to take care of it properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and straighteners to prevent damage. Get regular trims to keep the style looking fresh.

Now that you know how to fade your own hair male style, you can achieve a professional-looking fade at home. With the right tools and techniques, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. Just remember to take your time and work in sections to achieve the best results. Happy fading!
