Are Frontal Wigs Hard To Maintain

Written by Eveline Aug 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Frontal wigs have become increasingly popular in recent years. Not only do they look natural, but they also offer a versatile styling option for those who want to switch up their hair without committing to a permanent change. However, many people wonder – are frontal wigs hard to maintain? In this article, we’ll break down everything you need to know about taking care of your frontal wig.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Maintain Your Frontal Wig
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Frontal Wig
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Frontal Wigs

Description

Frontal wigs are made with a lace closure that spans from ear to ear, covering the entire hairline. This creates a natural-looking hairline and allows for versatile styling options. However, these wigs require a bit more maintenance than traditional wigs due to their delicate construction.

How to Maintain Your Frontal Wig

While frontal wigs may require some extra maintenance, it’s not necessarily difficult. Here are some basic steps to follow to keep your wig looking its best:

1. Detangle your wig regularly

Use a wide-tooth comb or a wig brush to gently detangle your wig daily. This will prevent tangling and keep the hair looking smooth.

2. Wash your wig regularly

Wash your wig every two to three weeks, or as needed. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid rubbing or tangling the hair. Rinse thoroughly with cool water.

3. Store your wig properly

When not in use, store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head. This will help maintain the wig’s shape and prevent tangling.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide to maintaining your frontal wig:

1. Detangle your wig

Start by detangling your wig with a wide-tooth comb or wig brush. Work from the ends of the hair up to the roots, gently working out any tangles or knots.

2. Wash your wig

Fill a sink or basin with cool water and add a small amount of gentle shampoo. Immerse your wig in the water and gently swirl it around, being careful not to rub or tangle the hair. Rinse thoroughly with cool water, then repeat with conditioner.

3. Dry your wig

Gently blot your wig with a towel to remove excess water, then place it on a wig stand or mannequin head to air dry. Avoid using a hair dryer, as this can damage the hair.

4. Style your wig

Once your wig is dry, you can style it as desired. Use a heat protectant spray if using heat tools, and avoid using high heat settings.

Tips for Maintaining Your Frontal Wig

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when maintaining your frontal wig:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent tangling and breakage while sleeping.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh down the hair and cause tangling.
  • Consider investing in a wig cap to protect your natural hair and help keep the wig in place.

FAQs

Here are some common questions people have about maintaining their frontal wig:

Q: How often should I wash my frontal wig?

A: It’s recommended to wash your wig every two to three weeks, or as needed.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my wig?

A: While you can use regular shampoo and conditioner, it’s recommended to use a gentle, sulfate-free formula to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.

Q: Can I swim with my frontal wig?

A: It’s not recommended to swim with your wig, as chlorine and saltwater can damage the hair. If you must swim with your wig, wear a swim cap and rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water afterwards.

Hair Care for Frontal Wigs

To keep your frontal wig looking its best, it’s important to take good care of the hair. Here are some basic hair care tips:

  • Avoid using high heat on the hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a month to keep the hair moisturized.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair, as this can cause tangling and breakage.

Overall, while frontal wigs may require some extra maintenance, they can be a great way to switch up your look without committing to a permanent change. By following these tips and taking good care of your wig, you can keep it looking natural and beautiful for months to come.
