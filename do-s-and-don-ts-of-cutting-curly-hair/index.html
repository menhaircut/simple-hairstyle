Do's and Don'ts of Cutting Curly Hair

Curly hair can be tricky to cut, but with the right techniques, it can be a breeze. In this article, we will be discussing the do's and don'ts of cutting curly hair to help you achieve the perfect cut for your clients.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs about Cutting Curly Hair
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Cut Curly Hair

When cutting curly hair, it's important to keep in mind that each curl is unique and should be treated as such. Curly hair tends to shrink up when dry, so it's important to cut it while it's wet. Start by sectioning the hair into manageable sections and detangling it with a wide-tooth comb.

Do:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Use a sharp pair of shears to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Take your time and pay attention to each individual curl.

Don't:

  • Cut curly hair when it's dry as it will not give an accurate length.
  • Use a razor as it will damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Cut too much off at once, as it may result in an uneven cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut curly hair:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Section the hair into manageable parts using hair clips.

Step 2: Detangle the Hair

Detangle the hair using a wide-tooth comb to ensure that it's free of knots and tangles.

Step 3: Wet the Hair

Wet the hair with a spray bottle until it's damp.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Start cutting the hair in small sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Style the hair as desired and let it air dry.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Tip #1: Use a Good Pair of Shears

Invest in a good pair of shears to avoid damaging the hair. A sharp pair of shears will give you a clean cut and reduce the risk of split ends.

Tip #2: Cut the Hair When Wet

Cutting curly hair when it's wet will give you a more accurate length as it tends to shrink up when dry.

Tip #3: Take Your Time

Don't rush the process. Take your time and pay attention to each individual curl to ensure a clean and even cut.

FAQs about Cutting Curly Hair

Q: Can I cut curly hair when it's dry?

A: No, it's recommended to cut curly hair when it's wet to achieve an accurate length.

Q: Should I use a razor to cut curly hair?

A: No, using a razor on curly hair can cause split ends and damage the hair.

Q: How often should I cut curly hair?

A: It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Tip #1: Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep it hydrated.

Tip #2: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons as they can cause damage and dry out the hair.

Tip #3: Use a Diffuser

When blow-drying curly hair, use a diffuser attachment to help maintain its natural shape and prevent frizz.

With these tips and techniques, cutting curly hair can be a breeze. Remember to take your time and pay attention to each individual curl to achieve a clean and even cut.
