How To Wear A Thick Headband With Short Hair

Are you looking for a way to add some style to your short hair? A thick headband is a great accessory that can add both fashion and function to your look. However, styling a headband with short hair can be a bit tricky. In this article, we will show you how to wear a thick headband with short hair, step by step.

Description

A thick headband is a versatile accessory that can be worn with different hairstyles. It can add volume to thin hair, keep hair away from your face during workouts or outdoor activities, and can make a fashion statement. However, wearing a headband with short hair can be a bit challenging since there is less hair to work with. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will show you how to wear a thick headband with short hair in a way that flatters your face shape and enhances your overall look.

How to Wear a Thick Headband with Short Hair

Before we begin, let's talk about the different types of headbands available. A thick headband is typically wider than a regular headband and can be made of different materials such as fabric, leather, or plastic. When choosing a headband, consider the color, texture, and style that will complement your outfit.

Now, let's get started on how to wear a thick headband with short hair:

Step by Step Guide

  1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Place the headband over your head, positioning it where you want it to sit. You can wear it at the hairline, above the ears, or at the crown of your head.
  3. Adjust the headband so that it fits snugly but not too tight. If it's too loose, it will slip off, and if it's too tight, it will cause headaches and discomfort.
  4. Take a small section of hair from one side of your head and tuck it behind the headband. Repeat on the other side.
  5. For a sleeker look, you can use a hair gel or pomade to smooth down any flyaways or baby hairs.
  6. Alternatively, you can leave some hair out in front of the headband for a more casual and relaxed vibe.
  7. If you want to add more volume to your hair, you can tease the hair at the crown of your head before wearing the headband.
  8. Finish with a hairspray to keep the headband in place and to add some extra hold to your hairstyle.

Tips for Wearing a Thick Headband with Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you wear a thick headband with short hair:

  • Experiment with different headband styles such as braided, patterned, or embellished headbands.
  • Choose a headband that complements your face shape. For example, if you have a round face, opt for a headband with angular shapes or sharp lines to create the illusion of length.
  • If you have a short bob, try wearing a headband with a messy bun or a low ponytail.
  • Accessorize with earrings or a necklace that complement the headband.
  • Don't be afraid to play around with different hairstyles and headband positions until you find the look that works best for you.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about wearing a thick headband with short hair:

  • Can I wear a thick headband with bangs? Yes, you can wear a headband with or without bangs. If you have bangs, you can either tuck them behind the headband or leave them out in front.
  • What type of headband is best for short hair? A thick headband is a great choice for short hair since it adds volume and texture. However, you can also wear a thin headband or a scarf headband depending on your personal style.
  • How do I keep the headband from slipping off? Choose a headband with a non-slip grip or add some bobby pins to secure it in place.
  • Can I wear a headband to work or formal events? Yes, a headband can be a stylish accessory for any occasion. Just make sure to choose a headband that complements your outfit and the dress code.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your short hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent over-drying.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.

Now that you know how to wear a thick headband with short hair, it's time to experiment with different styles and have fun with your new look. Remember, the key to wearing a headband with short hair is to keep it simple and choose a headband that complements your personal style.
