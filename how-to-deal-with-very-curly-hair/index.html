How To Deal With Very Curly Hair A Guide For 2023

Written by Luffy Jul 01, 2023 · 5 min read

Dealing with very curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, it can also be a beautiful asset. In this guide, we will walk you through how to care for and style your curly hair, addressing common concerns and offering tips for keeping your locks healthy and vibrant. Read on for a step-by-step approach to dealing with very curly hair.

How to Deal with Very Curly Hair

Before we get into the specifics of dealing with very curly hair, it's important to understand what makes curly hair unique. Curly hair tends to be dryer, more prone to breakage, and more susceptible to frizz than straight hair. However, with the right care and styling techniques, curly hair can be healthy, bouncy, and beautiful.

The first step to dealing with very curly hair is to identify your hair type. There are several different types of curly hair, ranging from loose waves to tight coils. Knowing your hair type will help you choose the right products and techniques for your hair.

Identifying Your Hair Type

The most common way to identify your hair type is to use the Andre Walker Hair Typing System. This system categorizes hair into four types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each type is then further divided into subcategories based on the tightness of the curl or wave pattern. To identify your hair type, simply examine your hair and compare it to the descriptions and pictures provided by the system.

Once you've identified your hair type, you can tailor your hair care routine to meet your specific needs. Here are some tips for caring for very curly hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Cleanse

The first step in any hair care routine is to cleanse your hair. When dealing with very curly hair, it's important to use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Focus on your scalp, massaging the shampoo into your roots, and avoid scrubbing your hair too vigorously. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Condition

After cleansing, it's important to condition your hair to restore moisture and prevent tangles. Choose a conditioner that is specifically formulated for curly hair, and apply it generously to the lengths and ends of your hair. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Leave the conditioner in your hair for a few minutes before rinsing it out with cool water.

Step 3: Style

When styling very curly hair, it's important to use products that will enhance your curls without weighing them down. Start by applying a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your damp hair. Then, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair gently, scrunching your curls as you go. Once your hair is dry, you can use a curling iron or wand to define individual curls or add volume.

Tips for Curly Hair Care

In addition to the basic steps outlined above, there are several tips and tricks that can help you care for your curly hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can cause damage to your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective hair product

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about dealing with very curly hair:

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: This can vary depending on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with very curly hair prefer to wash their hair only once or twice a week, while others may need to wash it more frequently. Listen to your hair and adjust your routine as needed.

Q: Should I use a sulfate-free shampoo?

A: Yes, sulfate-free shampoos are generally gentler on curly hair and won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, but it's important to use heat styling tools sparingly and to use a heat protectant product to prevent damage to your hair.
