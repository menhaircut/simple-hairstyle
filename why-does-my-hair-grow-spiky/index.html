Why Does My Hair Grow Spiky

Written by Oliver Jun 08, 2023 · 3 min read
Why Does My Hair Grow Spiky? | Hair Care Tips

If you're one of those individuals who struggle with spiky hair, you may feel frustrated and disheartened. It can be challenging to style and manage, and you may wonder why your hair grows this way. In this article, we'll explore some of the reasons why your hair may grow spiky and provide you with some helpful tips on how to care for it.

Table of Contents

  • Genetics
  • Product Buildup
  • Humidity
  • Heat Damage

Genetics

Genetics play a significant role in how your hair grows and behaves. Some individuals are genetically predisposed to having spiky hair. If your parents or grandparents have spiky hair, it's possible that you inherited this trait from them. Unfortunately, there's not much you can do to change your genetics, but you can learn how to manage your hair with the right products and styling techniques.


Product Buildup

Product buildup can also cause your hair to grow spiky. When you use too many styling products, such as gels, mousses, and hairsprays, they can accumulate on the hair shaft and weigh it down, causing it to grow upwards instead of laying flat. To prevent product buildup, use only the necessary products and wash your hair regularly to remove any excess buildup.


Humidity

Humidity can also affect how your hair grows. When the air is humid, the moisture in the air can penetrate the hair shaft and cause it to swell, resulting in spiky hair. To combat this, try using a humidity-resistant hair product or style your hair in an updo or braids to keep it contained.


Heat Damage

Heat damage from styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can also cause your hair to grow spiky. When you apply heat to your hair, it can weaken the hair shaft and cause it to break or split, resulting in frizzy and spiky hair. To prevent heat damage, use a heat protectant spray and limit your use of styling tools.


Over-Styling

Over-styling your hair can also cause it to grow spiky. When you constantly manipulate your hair with heat and styling products, you can damage the hair shaft and cause it to grow in a spiky pattern. To prevent over-styling, limit your use of styling tools and try to embrace your hair's natural texture.


Hair Care Tips

Now that you know some of the reasons why your hair may grow spiky, here are some tips on how to care for it:

  • Use only necessary styling products and wash your hair regularly to prevent product buildup
  • Try using a humidity-resistant hair product or style your hair in an updo or braids to combat humidity
  • Use a heat protectant spray and limit your use of styling tools to prevent heat damage
  • Avoid over-styling and embrace your hair's natural texture

FAQ

Q: Can spiky hair be fixed?

A: While you can't change your genetics, you can manage spiky hair with the right products and styling techniques.

Q: Should I use a brush or comb on spiky hair?

A: It's best to use a wide-tooth comb on spiky hair to prevent tangles and breakage.

Q: How often should I wash spiky hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently, whereas if you have dry hair, you can wash it less often.
