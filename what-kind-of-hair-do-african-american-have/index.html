What Kind of Hair Do African Americans Have? A Guide to Relaxed Hair

African Americans have diverse hair types that come in different textures, lengths, and colors. However, many African Americans have curly, coily, or kinky hair that can be difficult to manage. In this article, we will discuss what kind of hair African Americans have, how to care for it, and tips for maintaining healthy hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

African American hair is unique because of its texture, which is usually curly, coily, or kinky. This texture is caused by the shape of the hair follicle, which is oval or flat instead of round. This shape makes it difficult for natural oils to travel down the hair shaft, which can lead to dryness and breakage.

Many African Americans choose to relax their hair to make it more manageable. Relaxers are chemical treatments that straighten the hair by breaking down the protein bonds that give it its texture. Relaxed hair is easier to comb, style, and maintain, but it requires careful care to prevent damage and breakage.

How to

If you have relaxed hair, it's important to follow a hair care routine that includes shampooing, conditioning, and moisturizing. Here are some tips for caring for relaxed hair:

Step by step

  1. Wash your hair once a week with a gentle shampoo that doesn't contain sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  2. Use a deep conditioner after washing your hair to restore moisture and prevent breakage.
  3. Moisturize your hair daily with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to prevent dryness and breakage.
  4. Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair.
  5. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and set the temperature to a low or medium setting.
  6. Avoid using tight hair accessories like rubber bands and hair ties, which can cause breakage.
  7. Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and keep it healthy.
  8. Avoid over-processing your hair with relaxers or other chemical treatments, which can weaken your hair and cause breakage.
  9. Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.
  10. Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for relaxed hair:

  • Wrap your hair in a satin or silk scarf at night to prevent breakage and maintain your hairstyle.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or greases on your hair, which can clog your pores and cause breakouts on your scalp.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product that contains SPF.

FAQ

Q: Can African Americans have straight hair?

A: Yes, some African Americans have naturally straight hair, but this is less common than curly, coily, or kinky hair.

Q: Is it bad to relax your hair?

A: Relaxing your hair can be damaging if not done correctly or if done too frequently. It's important to follow a hair care routine that includes regular conditioning and moisturizing to prevent breakage and maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care

Caring for African American hair can be challenging, but it's important to maintain healthy hair to prevent breakage and damage. If you have relaxed hair, follow a hair care routine that includes regular shampooing, conditioning, and moisturizing. Avoid heat styling tools, tight hair accessories, and heavy oils or greases. Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and maintain healthy hair.
