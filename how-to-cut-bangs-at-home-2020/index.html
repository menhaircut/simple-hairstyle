Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking for a change? Cutting bangs at home might be the answer you're looking for! With the pandemic still in full swing, many of us have had to resort to DIY haircuts. If you're not sure where to start, don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut bangs at home in 2020, along with some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

How to Cut Bangs at Home

Cutting bangs at home can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Here are the things you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • A mirror
  • A hair clip or elastic band

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your bangs at home:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be tricky to cut, so it's best to work with dry hair.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut. Use a fine-tooth comb to separate the hair you want to cut from the rest of your hair. You can clip or tie back the rest of your hair to keep it out of the way.
  3. Decide on the length of your bangs. It's best to start with longer bangs and gradually cut more if needed. Use your fingers to measure the length you want, and mark it with a comb.
  4. Start cutting. Use sharp hair cutting scissors to cut the hair at the mark you made with the comb. Cut small sections at a time, and be sure to cut straight across.
  5. Check your progress. Use a mirror to check the length and shape of your bangs. If you need to cut more, do so in small sections.
  6. Style your bangs. Once you're happy with the length and shape of your bangs, style them as desired. You can use a straightener or curling iron to add some texture.

Tips for Cutting Bangs at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect bangs:

  • Start with longer bangs. It's always better to cut less hair than to cut too much.
  • Cut small sections at a time. This will help you avoid any mistakes.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Don't cut too close to the roots. Leave some space between the bottom of your bangs and your scalp.
  • Be patient. Cutting bangs can be time-consuming, but it's worth it to take your time and get it right.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting bangs at home:

  • Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair? Yes, you can cut bangs if you have curly hair. Just be sure to cut them when your hair is dry and in its natural state.
  • How often should I trim my bangs? It's best to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.
  • What do I do if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs? Don't panic! If you make a mistake, try to even out the hair by cutting small sections at a time. If all else fails, you can always pin your bangs back until they grow out.

Hair Care After Cutting Bangs at Home

Once you've cut your bangs at home, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly. Clean hair will help your bangs look fresh and clean.
  • Blow dry your bangs. Using a blow dryer can help your bangs look smooth and polished.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much. This can cause them to become oily and lose their shape.
  • Use a heat protectant when styling. This will help prevent damage and split ends.

Cutting bangs at home can be a fun and rewarding experience, as long as you take your time and follow the right steps. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve the perfect bangs in no time!
