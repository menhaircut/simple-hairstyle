How To Straighten Short Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling to straighten your short, thick hair? Don't worry, you're not alone! Many people with thick hair find it challenging to straighten their locks, but with the right techniques and tools, it's possible to achieve sleek, straight hair. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to straighten short thick hair, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Straighten Short Thick Hair

Straightening short, thick hair can be challenging, but it's not impossible. The key is to use the right tools and techniques to achieve sleek, straight locks. Here's what you'll need:

  • Flat iron
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hairbrush
  • Hair clips

Before you start straightening your hair, make sure that it's clean and dry. You don't want to apply heat to wet hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. Once your hair is dry, apply a heat protectant spray to protect your locks from heat damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools and products ready, here's a step-by-step guide on how to straighten short thick hair:

  1. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to straighten your hair one section at a time.
  2. Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and run a hairbrush through it to detangle any knots.
  3. Place the flat iron at the roots of your hair and slowly glide it down to the ends. Be sure to apply even pressure to avoid kinks or bends in your hair.
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 for each section of hair, working your way up to the top of your head.
  5. Once you've straightened all of your hair, run a hairbrush through it to smooth out any bumps or kinks.
  6. Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to add shine and tame any flyaways.

And there you have it! With these simple steps, you can achieve sleek, straight hair that lasts all day.

Tips for Straightening Short Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when straightening short thick hair:

  • Choose a flat iron with adjustable heat settings. Thick hair requires more heat than thin hair, so make sure that you can adjust the heat to suit your hair type.
  • Don't use too much tension when straightening your hair. This can cause breakage and damage to your locks.
  • Use a comb or hairbrush to detangle your hair before straightening it.
  • Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair before using a flat iron.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about straightening short thick hair:

Q: Can I straighten my hair if it's wet?

A: No, it's not recommended to straighten wet hair. Wet hair is more prone to damage and breakage when exposed to heat.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It's best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to avoid damage and breakage.

Q: How do I prevent frizz when straightening my hair?

A: Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to your hair after straightening to add shine and prevent frizz.

Hair Care for Short Thick Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of short thick hair:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips for hair care and straightening short thick hair, you can achieve sleek, straight locks that look and feel healthy.
