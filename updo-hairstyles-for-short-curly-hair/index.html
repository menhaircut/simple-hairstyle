Updo Hairstyles For Short Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by William Aug 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short curly hair, you might feel like your styling options are limited. But fear not! There are plenty of updo hairstyles that work great for short curly hair. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for creating the perfect updo, step-by-step instructions for our favorite styles, and answers to common questions about short curly hair. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How To

The first step in creating an updo hairstyle for short curly hair is to start with clean, dry hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and a moisturizing conditioner to hydrate your curls. Once your hair is dry, apply a styling product that's designed for curly hair. This will help define your curls and make them easier to work with.

Next, decide on the type of updo you want to create. There are many options, including buns, twists, braids, and ponytails. Consider your hair length and texture when choosing a style. For example, if you have very short hair, a braided updo might not be possible.

Once you've chosen a style, gather the necessary tools and accessories. You'll likely need bobby pins, hair ties, and possibly a hair donut or other styling tool. It's also helpful to have a mirror and some extra styling product on hand in case you need to touch up your curls.

Step By Step

Bun

To create a bun updo for short curly hair, follow these steps:

  1. Start by pulling your hair into a high ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun shape.
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins, tucking any loose ends into the bun.
  4. Use your fingers to gently pull out some curls around your face and at the nape of your neck to create a soft, romantic look.

Twist

To create a twisted updo for short curly hair, follow these steps:

  1. Part your hair down the middle and separate into two equal sections.
  2. Starting at the front of your head, twist one section of hair tightly towards the back of your head.
  3. Secure the twisted section with bobby pins, then repeat on the other side.
  4. Use your fingers to gently pull out some curls around your face and at the nape of your neck to create a soft, romantic look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when creating updo hairstyles for short curly hair:

  • Use bobby pins that match your hair color to help them blend in.
  • Don't be afraid to add a little volume to your curls by gently teasing them with a comb or brush.
  • Experiment with different styles and accessories to find what works best for your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or oil to help keep your curls hydrated and defined throughout the day.

FAQ

Can you create an updo with very short curly hair?

Yes, but your options may be more limited. Consider a simple bun or twist, or try adding a decorative hair accessory to dress up your hair.

How do I keep my updo in place all day?

Use plenty of bobby pins and hair ties to secure your updo, and consider using a hairspray or other setting product to help hold it in place.

What should I do if my curls become frizzy or lose their shape throughout the day?

Carry a small bottle of styling product with you and use it to touch up your curls as needed. You can also try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help define your curls and reduce frizz.

Hair Care

To keep your short curly hair looking its best, be sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for curly hair. Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy. Avoid using heat styling tools whenever possible, and use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help define your curls. Finally, be sure to trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep your curls looking their best.

We hope this article has inspired you to try some new updo hairstyles for short curly hair. With a little practice and some helpful tips, you'll be able to create beautiful, stylish looks that showcase your natural curls.
