Why Does My Hair Go Bushy When I Brush It

Written by Aprilia Jul 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of brushing your hair only to have it turn into a frizzy bush? You're not alone! Many people experience this frustrating phenomenon, and there are several reasons why it happens.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When you brush your hair, you're essentially pulling through the strands with a bristled tool. This action can cause the hair to become static, which makes it stand on end and creates the appearance of a bushy, frizzy mess. Additionally, if your hair is already dry and damaged, brushing can exacerbate the problem by breaking and splitting the hair.

How to

There are several things you can do to prevent your hair from going bushy when you brush it:

Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Instead of using a brush, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair. This will help prevent static and breakage, and will also help distribute natural oils throughout your hair.

Brush Your Hair When It's Wet

When your hair is wet, it's more pliable and less likely to break or become static. Use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your hair while it's wet, and then let it air dry or use a hair dryer on a low, cool setting.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to brushing your hair without making it go bushy:

  1. Start by using a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair while it's still wet.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help prevent breakage and static.
  3. Use a paddle brush to gently brush your hair in sections, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  4. Be gentle! Avoid tugging or pulling on your hair, as this can cause breakage and create static.
  5. Finish by using a little bit of hair serum or oil to smooth down any flyaways and add shine.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent bushy hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of natural oils and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners, as these can also cause breakage and static.
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillowcase, as these materials are gentler on hair and can prevent static.
  • Try using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and repair damaged hair.

FAQ

Q: Can brushing your hair too much cause it to go bushy?

A: Yes, brushing your hair too much can cause breakage and static, which can make your hair look bushy and frizzy.

Q: Is there a certain type of brush that's better for preventing bushy hair?

A: Yes, a paddle brush with boar bristles is a good choice for preventing static and breakage.

Q: Are there any hairstyles that are less prone to bushy hair?

A: Yes, hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails can help prevent bushy hair by keeping the hair contained and protected from the elements.

Hair Care

Overall, the key to preventing bushy hair is to be gentle and take good care of your hair. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle wet hair, avoid over-brushing and hot tools, and nourish your hair with regular conditioning treatments. With a little bit of care and attention, you can keep your hair looking healthy, shiny, and frizz-free!
