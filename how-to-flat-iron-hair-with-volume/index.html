How To Flat Iron Hair With Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Flat ironing your hair is a great way to achieve a sleek and smooth look, but what if you want to add volume to your hair at the same time? Flat ironing hair with volume may seem like a daunting task, but with the right technique and tools, it's actually quite easy to achieve. In this article, we'll show you how to flat iron hair with volume, from start to finish.

Table of Contents

  • How to Flat Iron Hair with Volume
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Flat Ironing Hair with Volume
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Flat Ironing Hair with Volume

How to Flat Iron Hair with Volume

Before we get started, it's important to note that flat ironing hair with volume works best on hair that is already thick and/or curly. If you have fine, straight hair, you may not be able to achieve the same level of volume with this technique.

The key to flat ironing hair with volume is to use a technique called "bumping." This involves pulling the flat iron away from your head as you reach the ends of your hair, which creates a slight curve or "bump" in the hair.

Tools You Will Need:

  • Flat iron
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Sectioning clips
  • Round brush
  • Blow dryer

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help create a good foundation for adding volume to your hair.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant Spray

Before flat ironing your hair, it's important to protect it from heat damage. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair, making sure to evenly distribute it throughout your strands.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using sectioning clips, divide your hair into small sections. This will make it easier to flat iron your hair and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush and blow dryer, start at the roots and work your way down to the ends of each section of hair. This will help create volume and smooth out any kinks or tangles.

Step 5: Flat Iron Your Hair

Starting with the bottom section of your hair, take a small section and clamp the flat iron near the roots. As you glide the flat iron down the length of your hair, gradually pull it away from your head to create a slight bump. Continue this technique on each section of hair, working your way up to the top of your head.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

To ensure that your hair stays in place and maintains its volume, finish with a light mist of hairspray.

Tips for Flat Ironing Hair with Volume

  • Use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.
  • Divide your hair into small sections to make it easier to flat iron.
  • Use a round brush and blow dryer to create volume before flat ironing.
  • Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends of each section of hair.
  • Gradually pull the flat iron away from your head as you reach the ends of your hair to create a slight bump.
  • Finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold your hair in place.

FAQs

Q: Can I flat iron hair with volume on hair that is already straight?

A: While it is possible to create volume on straight hair, this technique works best on hair that is already thick and/or curly.

Q: What is the best type of flat iron to use for this technique?

A: Look for a flat iron with ceramic plates, as these distribute heat evenly and are less likely to damage your hair.

Hair Care for Flat Ironing Hair with Volume

To maintain your hair's volume and prevent damage, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair when brushing.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve sleek, smooth hair with volume that will turn heads wherever you go!
