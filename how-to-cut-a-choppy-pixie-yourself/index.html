How To Cut A Choppy Pixie Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 09, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon every few weeks to maintain your choppy pixie cut? With the right tools and techniques, you can easily cut your own hair at home. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut a choppy pixie yourself, saving you time and money.

How To

Before getting started, gather all the necessary tools and products. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. Make sure your hair is clean and dry before cutting.

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 1: Trim the Back

Begin with the back section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length, making sure to cut straight across. Continue cutting small sections until the entire back section is trimmed.

Step 2: Cut the Sides

Next, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length, angling the scissors slightly downwards to create a choppy effect. Repeat on the other side.

Step 3: Trim the Top

Now it's time to trim the top section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length, angling the scissors slightly downwards to create a textured look. Continue cutting small sections until the entire top section is trimmed.

Step 4: Blend the Sections

Once all sections are trimmed, blend them together by lightly snipping into any harsh lines or uneven areas. Use your fingers to blend the hair together and create a natural, choppy look.

Tips

When cutting, always start with a longer length and gradually trim more if needed. It's easier to take off more hair than to add it back on.

Use a mirror to check the back of your head and ensure an even cut.

Take breaks if your arms or hands get tired, as this can affect your cutting precision.

FAQ

How often should I cut my choppy pixie?

It's recommended to trim your choppy pixie every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Can I cut my choppy pixie while it's wet?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink and change shape as it dries.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

Don't panic! It's always better to err on the side of caution and start with longer lengths. If you accidentally cut too much, wait a few weeks for it to grow back, or visit a professional stylist for a fix.

Hair Care

After cutting your choppy pixie, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and texture. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and hydrated. Apply a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Now that you know how to cut a choppy pixie yourself, you can enjoy a fresh, stylish look without ever leaving your home. Happy cutting!
