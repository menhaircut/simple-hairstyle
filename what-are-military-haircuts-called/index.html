What Are Military Haircuts Called

If you're looking for a new, short hairstyle, you might be considering a military haircut. But what are military haircuts called in relaxed English? In this article, we'll explore the different names for military haircuts, how to get one, and how to care for your new short cut.

Names for Military Haircuts

Military haircuts are known by several names in relaxed English. Some of the most popular names include:

  • Crew cut
  • High and tight
  • Flat top
  • Butch cut
  • Ivy League
  • Buzz cut

Each name refers to a slightly different variation of the military haircut. For example, the crew cut is a very short cut with tapered sides and back, while the flat top is a short cut with a flat, horizontal top.

How to Get a Military Haircut

If you're interested in getting a military haircut, you have a few options. You can go to a barber or hair salon and ask for one of the specific styles mentioned above. Or, if you're feeling bold, you can try cutting your hair yourself using clippers and a guide comb.

Barber or Hair Salon

To get a military haircut at a barber or hair salon, simply show them a picture of the style you want and ask for that cut. The stylist will use clippers and scissors to create the desired look. They may also use a straight razor to clean up the edges and neckline.

DIY Military Haircut

If you want to cut your own military haircut, you'll need a few tools:

  • Clippers
  • Guide comb
  • Scissors
  • Straight razor (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting a Military Haircut

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting your own military haircut:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use the clippers with a #2 or #3 guard to cut the sides and back of your head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the guide comb to ensure an even cut.
  3. Use the clippers with a #4 or #5 guard to cut the top of your head. Again, use the guide comb to ensure an even cut.
  4. Blend the sides and top together using the clippers and guide comb.
  5. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs and blend the sides and top even more.
  6. Use the clippers without a guard to trim the edges and neckline.
  7. Clean up any stray hairs with a straight razor (optional).

Tips for Maintaining a Military Haircut

To keep your military haircut looking sharp, follow these tips:

  • Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks.
  • Wash your hair every other day with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a light hair gel or pomade to style your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.

FAQs about Military Haircuts

Is a military haircut easy to maintain?

Yes, a military haircut is very easy to maintain. It requires regular trims to keep the shape, but it doesn't require much styling or product.

What face shape is best for a military haircut?

A military haircut looks great on most face shapes, but it's especially flattering on round or square faces.

Can women get military haircuts?

Yes, women can definitely get military haircuts. In fact, some military haircuts are specifically designed for women, such as the pixie cut.

Hair Care Tips for Military Haircuts

While a military haircut is low-maintenance, you still need to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.

Here are some hair care tips for military haircuts:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons, which can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, which can strengthen your hair from the inside out.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind with a hat or scarf.

By following these tips, you can keep your military haircut looking sharp and stylish all year round.
