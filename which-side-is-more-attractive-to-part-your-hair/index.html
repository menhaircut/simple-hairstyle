Which Side Is More Attractive To Part Your Hair

Written by Robby Aug 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to styling your hair, one of the most important decisions you'll make is which side to part it on. The right part can flatter your face shape, enhance your features, and give you a polished, put-together look. But with so many options to choose from, how do you know which side is more attractive to part your hair?

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

The side that you choose to part your hair can have a big impact on your overall look. It can change the way your face appears, and can even make your hair look fuller or more voluminous. Plus, the right part can help to balance out asymmetrical features and create a more harmonious appearance.

But with so many different ways to part your hair, it can be tough to know which one is right for you. Some people prefer to part their hair in the middle, while others opt for a deep side part. And then there are the more unconventional choices, like the zig-zag part or the diagonal part.

So, which side is more attractive to part your hair? The answer may surprise you.

How To

Before we dive into the specifics of which side to part your hair on, let's cover some basic terminology. When we talk about a part, we're referring to the place where your hair is separated and combed in opposite directions. A middle part is when the hair is divided down the center of the head, while a side part is when the hair is parted to one side or the other.

Now, let's get into the nitty-gritty of which side is more attractive to part your hair.

Face Shape

One of the most important factors to consider when choosing a part is your face shape. Different parts can help to balance out or emphasize certain features, depending on the shape of your face.

If you have a round face, a deep side part can help to elongate your features and create a more angular appearance. On the other hand, if you have a square face, a middle part can help to soften your features and create a more feminine look.

If you have an oval face, you're in luck! You can pretty much pull off any part you like. Just be sure to avoid a part that's too severe, as it can make your face appear longer.

Hair Texture

The texture of your hair can also play a role in which side is more attractive to part your hair on. If you have fine or thin hair, a middle part can help to create the illusion of more volume and fullness. If you have thick or curly hair, a deep side part can help to manage the volume and create a more polished look.

Step by Step

Now that you know which factors to consider when choosing a part, let's walk through the steps of actually creating the part.

  1. Start by combing your hair straight back from your forehead.
  2. Determine where you want your part to be. You can use the arch of your eyebrow or the point where your hair naturally falls to guide you.
  3. Using the end of a comb, create a clean, straight line down the center of your head for a middle part, or to one side for a deep side part.
  4. Gently comb the hair on either side of the part in opposite directions.
  5. Style as desired.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when choosing and creating your part:

  • Experiment with different parts to find the one that flatters your face shape and hair texture the most.
  • Use a tail comb or the end of a brush to create a clean, precise part.
  • Don't be afraid to switch up your part from time to time for a fresh, new look.
  • Consider using a styling product, like a volumizing mousse or texturizing spray, to help hold your part in place.

FAQ

Is it bad to part your hair in the same spot every day?

Not necessarily. However, constantly parting your hair in the same spot can cause breakage and damage over time. It's a good idea to switch up your part every once in a while to give your hair a break.

Can I change my part if I have bangs?

If you have bangs, it can be a little trickier to change your part. You may need to train your bangs to lay in a different direction by blow-drying them in the opposite direction of your usual part.

Hair Care

No matter which side you choose to part your hair on, it's important to take good care of your locks. Be sure to use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-styling with heat tools. Regular trims can also help to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.

So, which side is more attractive to part your hair? Ultimately, the answer depends on your unique features and preferences. But with these tips and tricks, you'll be able to find the perfect part for you in no time.
