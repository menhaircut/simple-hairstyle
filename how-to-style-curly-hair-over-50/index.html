How To Style Curly Hair Over 50 A Guide For The Mature Woman

Written by Eveline Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, our hair changes along with the rest of our body. Many women over 50 have naturally curly hair, which can be both a blessing and a challenge. Curly hair has volume, texture, and bounce, but it can also be frizzy, dry, and hard to manage. If you're looking for tips on how to style your curly hair over 50, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll share our best advice on how to embrace your curls and look fabulous at any age.

How to

Step 1: Start with a Good Haircut

The foundation of any great hairstyle is a great haircut. If you have curly hair, it's important to find a stylist who knows how to work with your texture. Look for someone who specializes in curly hair or has experience with mature clients. A good haircut will enhance your curls, flatter your face shape, and be easy to style at home.

Step 2: Use the Right Products

Curly hair needs moisture, so it's important to use products that hydrate and define your curls. Look for shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are specifically designed for curly hair. Avoid sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils, and opt for gentle, moisturizing formulas instead.

Step 3: Embrace Your Natural Texture

One of the best things about curly hair is its natural texture. Instead of fighting your curls, embrace them. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls and create volume. Scrunch your hair with your hands to encourage your curls to clump together. Don't be afraid to let your hair air dry, either.

Step 4: Experiment with Different Styles

Just because you have curly hair doesn't mean you're limited to one style. There are many different ways to wear curly hair, from loose waves to tight ringlets. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you. Try a messy bun, a half-up, half-down style, or a side braid. Don't be afraid to play with accessories, either. A scarf, headband, or hair clip can add a pop of color and personality to your look.

Tips

- Don't over-wash your hair. Curly hair tends to be dry, so you don't need to shampoo every day.

- Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause frizz.

- Apply styling products to wet hair to help define your curls.

- Avoid heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair.

FAQ

Q: How often should I trim my curly hair?

A: It's a good idea to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and keep your curls looking their best.

Q: What's the best way to refresh my curls on non-wash days?

A: Use a spray bottle filled with water and a little bit of leave-in conditioner to refresh your curls. Scrunch your hair with your hands to revive your curls.

Q: Can I color my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can color your curly hair, but it's important to use a gentle, non-damaging formula. Talk to your stylist about your options and choose a color that complements your skin tone and hair texture.

Hair Care

Taking care of your curly hair is essential to keeping it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for keeping your curls in top shape:

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

- Sleep on a satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

- Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

- Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.

With the right haircut, products, and styling techniques, you can rock your curly hair over 50 and look fabulous at any age. Embrace your natural texture, experiment with different styles, and take good care of your hair. Your curls will thank you.
