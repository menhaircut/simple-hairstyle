Hairstyles For Long Hair Pulled Back A Guide For Relaxed Looks

Written by Daniel Jun 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Long hair can be a blessing and a curse. It’s gorgeous when it’s down and flowing, but sometimes we just want to get it out of our face and off our neck. That’s where hairstyles for long hair pulled back come in. In this guide, we’ll take you through several relaxed looks that are perfect for everyday wear or special occasions. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Pull Back Long Hair
  • Hairstyles for Long Hair Pulled Back
  • Tips for Styling Long Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Long Hair

How to Pull Back Long Hair

Pulling back long hair is easy once you get the hang of it. Here are the basic steps:

  1. Brush your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Gather your hair at the nape of your neck or on top of your head.
  3. Secure your hair with an elastic band or hair tie.
  4. Adjust the style as needed to make sure it’s comfortable and secure.

That’s it! Now let’s move on to some actual hairstyles.

Hairstyles for Long Hair Pulled Back

The Classic Ponytail

The ponytail is a timeless hairstyle that’s perfect for long hair. It’s simple, easy, and can be dressed up or down. To create a classic ponytail:

  1. Brush your hair back and gather it at the nape of your neck.
  2. Tie your hair with an elastic band or hair tie.
  3. Wrap a small section of hair around the elastic band to hide it.
  4. Secure the wrapped hair with a bobby pin.

That’s it! You can leave your ponytail sleek and smooth, or add some texture with waves or braids.

The Messy Bun

The messy bun is a popular hairstyle for long hair pulled back because it’s easy to create and looks effortlessly chic. To create a messy bun:

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail and wrap it around the base to create a bun.
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins.
  4. Pull out a few pieces of hair around your face to create a relaxed, messy look.

The key to a good messy bun is to not overthink it. Let it be messy and imperfect!

The Half-Up, Half-Down

The half-up, half-down hairstyle is a great option for long hair because it keeps some hair out of your face without pulling it all back. To create a half-up, half-down style:

  1. Section off the top half of your hair, from your temples to the crown of your head.
  2. Pull that section back and secure it with an elastic band or hair tie.
  3. Fluff up the section to create volume.

You can leave the bottom half of your hair down or add some waves or curls to create more texture.

Tips for Styling Long Hair

When it comes to styling long hair pulled back, there are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use high-quality hair ties and bobby pins to prevent breakage and damage.
  • Experiment with different textures, like waves or braids, to add interest to your hairstyle.
  • Don’t pull your hair back too tightly, as this can cause headaches and damage to your hairline.
  • Consider using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair.

FAQs

What’s the best hairstyle for long hair pulled back?

It really depends on your personal style and the occasion. The classic ponytail, messy bun, and half-up, half-down style are all great options.

How can I keep my hair from getting tangled when it’s pulled back?

Brush your hair before pulling it back, and make sure to use a high-quality hair tie or elastic band to prevent tangles and breakage.

Is it bad to pull your hair back every day?

Pulling your hair back every day can cause damage to your hairline and lead to breakage. It’s important to switch up your hairstyles and give your hair a break from being pulled back.

Hair Care for Long Hair

Long hair requires a little extra care to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to prevent breakage when brushing.
  • Choose a shampoo and conditioner that’s formulated for long hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Following these tips will help keep your long hair looking beautiful and healthy.

Final Thoughts

Hairstyles for long hair pulled back are a great option for when you want to get your hair out of your face and off your neck. The classic ponytail, messy bun, and half-up, half-down styles are all easy to create and look effortlessly chic. Just remember to take care of your hair and switch up your hairstyles to prevent damage. Happy styling!
