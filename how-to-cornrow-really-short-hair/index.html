How To Cornrow Really Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cornrows are a classic and versatile hairstyle that can be worn by people of all ages and hair types. However, if you have really short hair, you may think that cornrows are not an option for you. But, that's not true! With the right technique and some practice, you can cornrow even the shortest of hair. In this article, we'll show you how to cornrow really short hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. Let's get started!

How to Cornrow Really Short Hair

Cornrowing short hair can be challenging, but it's not impossible. Here are the basic steps to cornrow really short hair:

1. Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cornrowing, make sure your hair is clean, detangled, and moisturized. You can use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized and to make it easier to handle.

2. Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using a rat-tail comb. The sections should be no wider than the width of your finger. Use hair clips to secure the sections that you are not working on.

3. Start Cornrowing

Take a small section of hair from the front and divide it into three strands. Cross the right strand over the middle strand, then cross the left strand over the middle strand. This is the basic cornrow pattern.

Continue cornrowing, adding hair from each section as you go. Make sure to keep the strands tight and close to the scalp. You can use gel or edge control to help keep the hair in place.

4. Finish Your Cornrows

When you reach the end of the section, braid the remaining hair to the end and secure it with a small hair elastic. Repeat the same process with the remaining sections until you have cornrowed your entire head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cornrow really short hair:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair to keep it moisturized.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Step 4: Finish Your Cornrows

Tips for Cornrowing Short Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cornrowing short hair:

  • Use a rat-tail comb to create small sections.
  • Start with a small section in the front and work your way backward.
  • Use gel or edge control to keep the hair in place.
  • Make sure to keep the strands tight and close to the scalp.
  • Practice, practice, practice!

FAQs

Can I cornrow really short hair?

Yes, you can cornrow really short hair. It may take some practice, but it's definitely possible.

How long does it take to cornrow short hair?

The time it takes to cornrow short hair depends on the length and thickness of your hair. It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to a few hours.

Hair Care Tips for Cornrows

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining cornrows:

  • Keep your scalp clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on the cornrows.
  • Wrap your hair at night with a satin scarf or bonnet.
  • Avoid leaving cornrows in for too long (4-6 weeks is the recommended time).
  • When removing cornrows, use scissors to cut the hair elastic instead of pulling the hair.

Now that you know how to cornrow really short hair, you can try this classic hairstyle on yourself or someone else. Remember to be patient and practice, and you'll be a pro in no time!
