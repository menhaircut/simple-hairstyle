Description

A pixie haircut is a short hairstyle that is typically cut above the ears and close to the scalp. It's a popular haircut choice for women who want a low-maintenance and effortless style, while still looking chic and fashionable.

The pixie haircut gained popularity in the 1950s when Audrey Hepburn wore it in the movie Roman Holiday. Since then, it has been a go-to hairstyle for women who want to make a bold statement with their hair.

There are different variations of the pixie haircut, such as the classic pixie, textured pixie, undercut pixie, and long pixie. Each variation has its unique style and can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type.

How to Get a Pixie Haircut?

If you're ready to make the chop and get a pixie haircut, here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Find a Good Stylist

Find a good stylist who has experience cutting pixie haircuts. Look for a stylist who has a portfolio of their work and positive reviews from other clients.

Step 2: Choose Your Pixie Style

Choose the type of pixie haircut you want based on your face shape and hair type. Consult with your stylist to determine which style will suit you best.

Step 3: Prepare for the Cut

Wash your hair and avoid using any hair products before your appointment. Wear a shirt that buttons up the front or a cape to make it easier for your stylist to cut your hair.

Step 4: Get the Cut

Let your stylist work their magic and cut your hair into the pixie style you've chosen. Be sure to communicate with them throughout the process to ensure you're happy with the result.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a classic pixie haircut:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 2: Cut the Top

Start by cutting the top section of your hair. Use scissors to cut it to the desired length, starting at the front and working your way back.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Move on to the side sections and cut them to the same length as the top. Pull the hair up and cut it at an angle to create a tapered look.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Cut the back section of your hair to the same length as the sides and top. Use scissors to create a tapered look at the nape of your neck.

Step 5: Add Texture

Use texturizing shears to add texture and movement to your hair. This will give your pixie haircut a more natural and effortless look.

Tips for Styling Pixie Haircut

Here are some tips for styling your pixie haircut:

  • Use a texturizing spray to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Invest in a good quality hair dryer to achieve the perfect blowout.
  • Use a small amount of styling wax or pomade to define your hair and create a messy, tousled look.
  • Experiment with different hair accessories, such as headbands and hair clips, to add some personality to your hairstyle.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a pixie haircut suitable for all face shapes?

Yes, a pixie haircut can be customized to suit any face shape. Consult with your stylist to determine which variation of the pixie haircut will suit you best.

How often do I need to get a pixie haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your pixie haircut's shape. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks.

Can I color my pixie haircut?

Yes, you can color your pixie haircut. Consult with your stylist to determine which hair color will suit your skin tone and hair type best.

Hair Care for Pixie Haircut

Here are some tips for maintaining your pixie haircut:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to add moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your pixie haircut.

Now that you know what a pixie haircut is and how to care for it, it's time to make the chop and rock your new hairstyle with confidence!
