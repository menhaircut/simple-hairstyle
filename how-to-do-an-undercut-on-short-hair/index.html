How To Do An Undercut On Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, an undercut is a great option to consider. This trendy look involves shaving or cutting the hair underneath the top layer, creating a dramatic contrast between the two lengths. It's a versatile style that can be adapted to suit different hair types and lengths, including short hair. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of doing an undercut on short hair, including tips and advice for maintaining your new look.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Do an Undercut on Short Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips and Advice

How to Do an Undercut on Short Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, it's important to note that doing an undercut on short hair can be tricky, especially if you've never done it before. If you're not confident in your abilities, we recommend seeking the help of a professional stylist. However, if you're feeling adventurous and want to give it a try, here's what you need to know.

What You'll Need:

  • Clippers or a razor
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Scissors

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve an undercut on short hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the natural part of your hair and where you want to make the cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, create a section of hair from the temple to the nape of the neck. Clip or tie the top section of hair out of the way.

Step 3: Cut or Shave the Undercut

Using clippers or a razor, shave or cut the hair underneath the clipped section. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up towards the temple. Use a comb to keep the hair in place and create a clean, even line.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut the undercut, blend the hair so that it looks natural. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven spots.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Release the top section of hair and style as desired. You can part it to one side or in the middle, or slick it back for a more dramatic look.

Tips and Advice

Here are some tips and advice to keep in mind when doing an undercut on short hair:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter. You can always go shorter, but it's harder to add length back once you've cut it.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles.
  • If you're using clippers, start with a higher guard and work your way down to a shorter length.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and lengths.

FAQs

Can I do an undercut on curly hair?

Absolutely! The key is to work with your natural texture and create a shape that works for your curls. Talk to your stylist for advice on how to achieve the look on curly hair.

Will an undercut suit my face shape?

An undercut can work for a variety of face shapes, but it's important to consider your individual features and how they will be accentuated by the cut. Talk to your stylist for advice on what will work best for you.

How often do I need to maintain an undercut?

This will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the undercut grows out. You may need to go in for touch-ups every few weeks or months to maintain the look.

Hair Care for Undercut Styles

Undercuts require some maintenance to keep them looking fresh and stylish. Here are some tips for caring for your hair after doing an undercut:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and nourished.
  • Consider using styling products like pomade or wax to keep your hair in place and add texture.
  • Visit your stylist regularly for touch-ups and trims.

That's it! With this step-by-step guide, you should now be able to achieve an undercut on short hair. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and lengths.
