How To Start Dreads With Soft Hair A Step By Step Guide

Starting dreadlocks with soft hair can be a challenging process, but it is definitely achievable. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with soft hair, along with some tips and frequently asked questions to help you along the way.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Start Dreads with Soft Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let‚Äôs first discuss what soft hair is and how it affects the dreadlock process. Soft hair is hair that is easily manipulated and lacks texture. This type of hair can make it difficult for dreads to form and stay in place. However, with some patience and proper maintenance, you can successfully start dreads with soft hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps to follow when starting dreads with soft hair:

  1. Decide on the method: There are several methods you can use to start your dreadlocks, including the backcombing method, the twist and rip method, and the crochet method. Research each method and decide which one will work best for you and your hair type.
  2. Wash and condition your hair: It is important to start with clean hair. Use a residue-free shampoo and a lightweight conditioner to moisturize your hair.
  3. Section your hair: Use a comb to section your hair into the desired size and shape of your dreads. You can use rubber bands to hold each section in place.
  4. Start the method: Follow the instructions for your chosen method to start your dreads. Be gentle and take your time to avoid damaging your hair.
  5. Palm roll your dreads: After you have started your dreads, use the palm rolling method to help them stay in place. Rub each dread between your palms in a circular motion to smooth and tighten the hair.
  6. Maintain your dreads: To keep your dreads looking neat and healthy, maintain them regularly. This includes washing them regularly, using a residue-free shampoo, and avoiding heavy conditioners or oils that can weigh down your hair.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Soft Hair

Here are some tips to help you start your dreads with soft hair:

  • Be patient ‚Äì it may take several months for your dreads to fully form and mature.
  • Avoid using too much product in your hair, as this can cause buildup and damage.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction and help your dreads stay in place.
  • Consider using a locking gel or spray to help your dreads stay in place during the early stages of the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads with soft hair:

  • Can I start dreads with relaxed hair? Yes, you can start dreads with relaxed hair. However, it may take longer for your dreads to form and stay in place.
  • How often should I wash my dreads? It is recommended to wash your dreads every 1-2 weeks, depending on your hair type and lifestyle.
  • Can I color my hair with dreads? Yes, you can color your hair with dreads. However, it is important to use a gentle, ammonia-free hair dye and avoid getting the dye on your scalp or roots.

Hair Care for Dreads with Soft Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your dreads with soft hair:

  • Wash your dreads regularly using a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a locking gel or spray to help your dreads stay in place during the early stages of the process.
  • Avoid excessive manipulation or pulling of your dreads, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Starting dreads with soft hair can be a long and challenging process, but with some patience and proper maintenance, you can achieve the look you desire. Follow these steps and tips to start your journey to beautiful, healthy dreadlocks.
