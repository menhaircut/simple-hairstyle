How To Cut Layers In Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have short hair and are looking to add some volume and texture, cutting layers is a great way to achieve that. However, cutting layers in short hair can be a bit tricky, especially if you're doing it at home. In this article, we'll go over how to cut layers in short hair in a relaxed, easy-to-understand language.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Layers in Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in Short Hair

Description

Layers are a great way to add some movement and texture to short hair. They can help to create the illusion of volume and give your hair a more dynamic look. However, it's important to note that cutting layers in short hair can be a bit challenging, especially if you're new to hair cutting. That's why it's important to follow a step-by-step guide and take your time to ensure that you get the look you want.

How to Cut Layers in Short Hair

Before we get into the step-by-step guide, it's important to note that cutting layers in short hair is not recommended for beginners. If you're new to hair cutting or are unsure of your abilities, it's best to leave it to a professional stylist. However, if you're up for the challenge and have some experience cutting hair, here's how to cut layers in short hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important that your hair is clean and free of any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Part your hair down the middle and divide it into two equal sections.

Step 3: Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use scissors to cut the hair at an angle. This will create the first layer.

Step 4: Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle, moving towards the front of your head. Be sure to cut each section at the same angle so that the layers are even.

Step 5: Once you've finished cutting the first layer, move on to the second layer. Take a small section of hair from the first layer and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use scissors to cut the hair at a steeper angle. This will create the second layer, which will sit on top of the first layer.

Step 6: Continue cutting small sections of hair at a steeper angle, moving towards the front of your head. Be sure to cut each section at the same angle so that the layers are even.

Step 7: Once you've finished cutting the second layer, move on to the third layer. Take a small section of hair from the second layer and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use scissors to cut the hair at an even steeper angle. This will create the third layer, which will sit on top of the second layer.

Step 8: Continue cutting small sections of hair at an even steeper angle, moving towards the front of your head. Be sure to cut each section at the same angle so that the layers are even.

Step 9: Once you've finished cutting all of the layers, use a comb to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless, layered look.

Step 10: Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Short Hair

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Using regular scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Take your time and work in small sections. This will help to ensure that the layers are even and look natural.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut a little bit at a time and check your progress than to cut too much and have to start over.
  • Practice on a mannequin head or a wig before trying to cut your own hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut layers in my own short hair?

A: While it's possible to cut layers in your own short hair, it's not recommended for beginners. It's best to leave it to a professional stylist or practice on a mannequin head or wig before attempting to cut your own hair.

Q: How often should I cut my hair if I have layers?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair with Layers

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as it's less harsh on your strands.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to the ends of your hair to keep them hydrated and healthy.

Cutting layers in short hair can be a bit challenging, but with some practice and patience, it's definitely achievable. Just remember to take your time, work in small sections, and use good-quality scissors. And if you're unsure about your abilities, it's always best to leave it to a professional stylist. Happy cutting!
