How To Cut A Side Fringe For Thin Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Jul 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your thin hair looking flat and lifeless? One great way to add some dimension and style to your hair is by cutting a side fringe. Not only will it give you a new look, but it can also help frame your face and accentuate your features. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to cut a side fringe for thin hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut a Side Fringe for Thin Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting a Side Fringe for Thin Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

Description

A side fringe, also known as side-swept bangs, is a style where the hair is cut diagonally across the forehead to create a face-framing effect. This type of fringe is perfect for thin hair because it adds volume and movement to the hair, making it look fuller and thicker. It’s also a versatile style that can work with any hair length or texture.

How to Cut a Side Fringe for Thin Hair

Cutting a side fringe for thin hair can be done at home, but it’s important to have the right tools and techniques to avoid any mishaps. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • Clips to section your hair

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut a side fringe for thin hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb it straight and part it where you want your fringe to sit.
  2. Section the hair you want to cut from the rest of your hair and clip it away.
  3. Using sharp scissors, cut the hair at a slight angle, starting from the outer corner of your eyebrow and moving downwards.
  4. Make sure to cut small sections at a time, and comb through the hair after each cut to ensure evenness.
  5. Repeat on the other side, making sure to match the length and angle of the first side.
  6. Blend the fringe into the rest of your hair by lightly trimming the ends with scissors.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting a Side Fringe for Thin Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting a side fringe for thin hair:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed. You can always trim more, but you can’t add hair back once it’s been cut.
  • Use hair-thickening products like mousse or volumizing spray to give your hair more body and texture.
  • Don’t cut your fringe too short, as it may end up looking choppy and uneven.
  • If you’re unsure about cutting your own fringe, it’s best to seek the help of a professional stylist.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a side fringe for thin hair:

Should I cut my fringe when my hair is wet or dry?

It’s best to cut your fringe when your hair is dry, as wet hair can shrink and lead to a shorter fringe than intended.

How often should I trim my fringe?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your fringe starts to look uneven. On average, it’s recommended to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape.

Can I cut my fringe straight across?

Yes, you can cut your fringe straight across, but it may not be the most flattering style for thin hair. A diagonal cut can add more dimension and movement to your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

In addition to cutting a side fringe, there are other ways to care for and style thin hair:

  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Try using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add more body to your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add more volume at the roots.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair and make it look even thinner.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut a side fringe for thin hair that flatters your face shape and adds volume to your hair. Remember to take your time, start with a longer length, and seek professional help if needed.
