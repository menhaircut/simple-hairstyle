How To Part Curly Hair For Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it's a beautiful and unique hair type, it can be challenging to add volume and definition to it. One of the ways you can achieve more volume is by parting your curly hair in a specific way. In this post, we'll show you how to part curly hair for volume in a relaxed English language.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Part Curly Hair for Volume

Parting your curly hair in a specific way can help add volume to your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

1. Find your natural part

The first step is to find your natural part. Your natural part is where your hair naturally falls when you don't style it. You can find your natural part by combing your hair straight back from your forehead and seeing where it naturally falls.

2. Part your hair to the opposite side

Once you've found your natural part, part your hair to the opposite side. This will create more volume by lifting your hair at the roots. For example, if your natural part is on the left side, part your hair to the right side.

3. Use a volumizing product

Using a volumizing product can help add even more volume to your hair. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots, and then blow-dry your hair upside down. This will help lift your hair at the roots and create more volume.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to parting your curly hair for volume:

1. Start with clean, damp hair

Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated. Towel-dry your hair until it's damp.

2. Find your natural part

Comb your hair straight back from your forehead and see where it naturally falls. This is your natural part.

3. Part your hair to the opposite side

Part your hair to the opposite side of your natural part. This will create more volume by lifting your hair at the roots.

4. Apply a volumizing product

Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots. This will help lift your hair at the roots and create more volume.

5. Blow-dry your hair upside down

Flip your head upside down and blow-dry your hair. This will help lift your hair at the roots and create more volume.

6. Finish with hairspray

Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your curls in place and prevent frizz.

Tips for Adding Volume to Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for adding volume to curly hair:

  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help enhance your curls and add volume.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your curls and make them look flat.
  • Get regular trims to keep your curls healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Sleep with your hair in a pineapple or silk scarf to help preserve your curls and prevent frizz.

FAQs

Q: Can I part my curly hair in the middle?

A: You can part your curly hair in the middle, but it may not add as much volume as parting it to the opposite side of your natural part.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people with curly hair can go a week or more without washing their hair, while others need to wash their hair every few days. Experiment to find a washing schedule that works for you.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and defined. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid using a brush on your hair, as it can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as they can damage your curls. If you must use a heat tool, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Get regular trims to keep your curls healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Sleep with your hair in a pineapple or silk scarf to help preserve your curls and prevent frizz.

By following these tips and parting your curly hair to the opposite side of your natural part, you can add volume and definition to your curls. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type and lifestyle.
