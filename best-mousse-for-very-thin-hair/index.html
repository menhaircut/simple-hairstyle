Best Mousse For Very Thin Hair Boost Your Hair Volume

Written by Bardi Jun 12, 2023 · 6 min read

Thin hair can be a real challenge to style, and it can be hard to find the right products to add volume without weighing it down. That's where mousse comes in! Mousse can add body and lift to even the thinnest hair, leaving you with a fuller, bouncier look. In this article, we'll look at the best mousse for very thin hair, how to use it, and some tips for keeping your hair looking its best.

Description

If you have very thin hair, you'll want to look for a mousse that is lightweight and won't weigh your hair down. You also want a mousse that will provide a good hold without feeling sticky or crunchy. Look for mousses with volumizing ingredients such as keratin, collagen, or biotin.

Here are some of the best mousse for very thin hair:

1. Kenra Volume Mousse Extra 17

This mousse is perfect for fine, thin hair that needs a little extra volume. It provides a flexible hold without weighing down your hair, and it contains a blend of ingredients that help to strengthen and protect your hair from damage.

2. OGX Locking + Coconut Curls Decadent Creamy Mousse

This mousse is ideal for those with very thin, curly hair. It provides long-lasting hold and definition without making your hair feel crunchy or sticky. It also contains coconut oil to moisturize and nourish your hair.

3. TRESemmé Beauty-Full Volume Touchable Bounce Mousse

This mousse is designed to give you soft, touchable hair with lots of volume. It contains a unique formula that allows you to use it both before and after you blow-dry your hair, so you can customize your look to suit your style.

How to Use Mousse for Thin Hair

Using mousse can help to add volume and body to your hair, but it's important to use it correctly to get the best results. Here are some tips on how to use mousse for thin hair:

1. Start with clean, damp hair

Before you apply mousse, make sure your hair is clean and damp. You can either towel-dry your hair or use a hair dryer on a low heat setting to remove excess moisture.

2. Apply mousse evenly

Dispense a small amount of mousse into your hand and apply it evenly throughout your hair. Focus on the roots and the areas where you want to add volume.

3. Use a comb to distribute the mousse

Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the mousse evenly throughout your hair. This will ensure that every strand is coated and that you get maximum volume.

4. Blow-dry your hair

Use a round brush and a hair dryer to blow-dry your hair. Focus on the roots and use the brush to lift your hair and add volume. Use a cool shot at the end to set your style.

Step-by-Step Guide to Using Mousse

If you're new to using mousse, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get the best results:

1. Shampoo and condition your hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any buildup and give you a clean slate to work with.

2. Towel-dry your hair

Use a towel to remove excess moisture from your hair. You don't want it to be dripping wet, but you also don't want it to be completely dry.

3. Dispense a small amount of mousse

Dispense a small amount of mousse into your hand. You don't need much, especially if you have very thin hair.

4. Apply the mousse

Apply the mousse evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the roots and the areas where you want to add volume.

5. Comb your hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to comb the mousse through your hair, making sure that every strand is coated.

6. Blow-dry your hair

Use a round brush and a hair dryer to blow-dry your hair. Focus on the roots and use the brush to lift your hair and add volume. Use a cool shot at the end to set your style.

Tips for Using Mousse on Very Thin Hair

Here are some additional tips for using mousse on very thin hair:

1. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner

Using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner can help to give your hair a boost and make it easier to style.

2. Don't use too much mousse

Using too much mousse can make your hair feel heavy and greasy. Start with a small amount and add more as needed.

3. Blow-dry your hair upside down

Blow-drying your hair upside down can help to add volume and lift at the roots.

4. Use a diffuser attachment

A diffuser attachment can help to distribute heat evenly and prevent your hair from getting too hot, which can cause damage.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

In addition to using mousse, there are some other things you can do to care for your thin hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Avoid heat styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, which can cause damage to your hair.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and strengthen your hair.

4. Get regular trims

Getting regular trims can help to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

With the right mousse and hair care routine, you can give your thin hair the volume and body it deserves. Try out some of the best mousse for very thin hair and see what works best for you!
