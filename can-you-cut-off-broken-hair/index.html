Can You Cut Off Broken Hair

Written by Christine Aug 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Broken hair can be a frustrating problem for anyone. Whether you have long or short hair, broken strands can make your hair look dull, frizzy, and unmanageable. If you're dealing with broken hair, you may be wondering if you can cut it off. The short answer is yes, you can cut off broken hair. In this article, we'll go over how to cut off broken hair, step by step.

Description

Broken hair can occur for a variety of reasons, including heat damage, chemical treatments, over-styling, and harsh hair products. When hair is damaged, the outer layer of the hair shaft (the cuticle) can become rough and lifted, causing the hair to break. This can lead to split ends, frizz, and overall hair damage. While it's important to address the underlying causes of hair breakage, cutting off the damaged hair can help improve the overall health and appearance of your hair.

How to Cut off Broken Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. Here's how to cut off broken hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup. Then, towel-dry your hair and comb it out so it's smooth and tangle-free.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using hair clips, divide your hair into four equal sections: two in the front and two in the back. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 3: Cut off the Broken Ends

Starting with one section of hair, take a small section at the ends and twist it tightly. Then, use the scissors to snip off the ends that are sticking out. Repeat this process with each section of hair until you've trimmed off all the broken ends.

Step 4: Blend in the Layers

Once you've trimmed off the broken ends, you may notice that your hair is uneven or has some layers. To blend in the layers, take small sections of hair from each section and cut them to match the length of the rest of your hair.

Tips for Cutting off Broken Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting off broken hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure you're cutting evenly.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair further.
  • Don't cut off too much hair at once. It's better to trim a little bit at a time until you're happy with the length.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist for help.

FAQs

Can I Cut off Broken Hair Myself?

Yes, you can cut off broken hair yourself. However, if you're not confident in your hair-cutting skills, it's best to go to a professional stylist for help.

How Often Should I Cut off Broken Hair?

It's best to trim off broken hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent further damage.

Will Cutting off Broken Hair Make My Hair Grow Faster?

No, cutting off broken hair won't make your hair grow faster. However, it can help improve the overall health of your hair and make it look fuller and shinier.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Broken Hair

Here are some hair care tips to help prevent broken hair:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use hair products that are designed for your hair type.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.
  • Don't pull your hair back into tight ponytails or braids.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft.

Ultimately, cutting off broken hair can help improve the overall health of your hair and make it look fuller and shinier. By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can prevent further damage and keep your locks looking their best.
