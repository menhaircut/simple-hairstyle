How To Dread Permed Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have permed hair and want to try dreadlocks, you may be wondering if it's possible. The answer is yes, you can dread permed hair! In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to do it, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Dread Permed Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's first understand what dreadlocks are. Dreadlocks are a hairstyle that involves matting and twisting sections of hair to create rope-like strands. This style is popular among people of different cultures and hair types, including permed hair.

Dreading permed hair is possible, but it's important to note that the process may take longer than dreading natural hair. This is because permed hair is chemically treated and has a different texture than natural hair. However, with the right techniques and patience, you can achieve dreadlocks on permed hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps to dread permed hair:

  1. Start with clean hair. Shampoo and condition your hair with a residue-free shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using products that contain silicone, which can make your hair slippery and harder to dread.
  2. Section your hair. Divide your hair into small, manageable sections using clips or hair ties. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreadlocks you want to create.
  3. Backcomb each section. Take one section of hair and backcomb it using a fine-toothed comb. Start at the roots and comb downwards towards the ends. This will create knots and tangles in the hair, which will help the hair lock and form into a dread.
  4. Twist each section. After backcombing, twist the section of hair in one direction until it starts to coil into a dreadlock. Use your fingers to help the hair twist and coil.
  5. Secure the dread. Once you've twisted the hair into a dread, use a rubber band or hair tie to secure the end. Make sure the rubber band is not too tight, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  6. Repeat the process. Continue backcombing, twisting, and securing each section of hair until you've completed your entire head.
  7. Maintain your dreads. After you've created your dreadlocks, it's important to maintain them. This includes washing your hair regularly, using a residue-free shampoo, and re-twisting your dreads as needed.

Tips for Dreading Permed Hair

Here are some tips to help you dread permed hair:

  • Be patient. Dreading permed hair may take longer than dreading natural hair, so don't get discouraged if your hair doesn't lock right away.
  • Use the right products. Avoid using products that contain silicone, as this can make your hair slippery and harder to dread. Use residue-free shampoos and conditioners to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid heat. Minimize the use of heat styling tools, such as flat irons and blow dryers, as this can damage your hair and make it harder to dread.
  • Protect your hair at night. Wear a silk or satin scarf or bonnet at night to protect your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Get help. If you're having trouble dreading your hair, consider getting help from a professional stylist who has experience with dreading permed hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreading permed hair:

  • Can you dread permed hair? Yes, you can dread permed hair.
  • How long does it take to dread permed hair? Dreading permed hair may take longer than dreading natural hair, as permed hair has a different texture. It may take several months to a year or more to achieve fully locked dreadlocks.
  • Can you wash permed dreadlocks? Yes, you can wash permed dreadlocks. It's important to use a residue-free shampoo and to avoid over-washing, as this can cause your dreads to unravel.
  • Can you undo permed dreadlocks? Yes, you can undo permed dreadlocks, but it may take time and patience. You'll need to use a lot of conditioner and gently comb out the knots and tangles.

Hair Care for Dreaded Permed Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid over-washing, as this can cause your dreads to unravel.
  • Re-twist your dreads as needed. This will help them stay locked and prevent them from unraveling.
  • Avoid using heavy products. Avoid using products that contain heavy oils or waxes, as this can cause buildup and make your dreads feel greasy.
  • Protect your hair at night. Wear a silk or satin scarf or bonnet at night to protect your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Get regular trims. Trimming the ends of your dreads can help prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you can successfully dread permed hair. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair, and you'll have beautiful, healthy dreadlocks in no time!
