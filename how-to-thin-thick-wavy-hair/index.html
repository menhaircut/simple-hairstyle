How To Thin Thick Wavy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Sep 15, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have thick wavy hair, you know that it can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it’s voluminous and bouncy. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. One way to make your thick wavy hair more manageable is to thin it out. In this article, we’ll show you how to thin thick wavy hair in simple, easy-to-follow steps.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Thin Thick Wavy Hair
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips

Description

Thinning out your hair means removing some of the bulk and weight of your hair. This can make it easier to manage, reduce frizz, and create more defined waves. There are several methods for thinning out thick wavy hair, including using thinning shears, razor cutting, and point cutting. We’ll cover each of these methods in detail below.

How to Thin Thick Wavy Hair

Before you start thinning out your hair, it’s important to have the right tools. You’ll need a pair of thinning shears, a razor, or a pair of scissors. You may also want to use a comb or hair clips to section off your hair.

Using Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth or notches on one blade. When you cut with them, they remove some of the hair while leaving the rest intact. Here’s how to use them to thin out your thick wavy hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section off a small piece of hair.
  3. Hold the section of hair with your fingers or a comb.
  4. Using the thinning shears, cut into the section of hair about halfway up the strand. Don’t cut too much at once – start with a small amount and work your way up if you need to.
  5. Repeat on other sections of hair until you’ve thinned out your hair to your desired thickness.

Razor Cutting

Razor cutting is another method for thinning out thick wavy hair. It involves using a razor to cut the hair at an angle, which removes some of the bulk and creates texture. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section off a small piece of hair.
  3. Hold the section of hair with your fingers or a comb.
  4. Using a razor, cut into the section of hair at an angle. Be careful not to cut too much at once.
  5. Repeat on other sections of hair until you’ve thinned out your hair to your desired thickness.

Point Cutting

Point cutting is a technique that involves cutting into the hair at an angle, rather than straight across. This creates texture and removes some of the bulk. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section off a small piece of hair.
  3. Hold the section of hair with your fingers or a comb.
  4. Using scissors, cut into the section of hair at an angle, making small snips into the hair. Be careful not to cut too much at once.
  5. Repeat on other sections of hair until you’ve thinned out your hair to your desired thickness.

Step-by-Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide for thinning out your thick wavy hair:

  1. Choose the method that you want to use – thinning shears, razor cutting, or point cutting.
  2. Gather your tools – thinning shears, a razor, or scissors.
  3. Wash and dry your hair.
  4. Section off a small piece of hair.
  5. Hold the section of hair with your fingers or a comb.
  6. Use your chosen method to thin out the section of hair. Be careful not to cut too much at once.
  7. Repeat on other sections of hair until you’ve thinned out your hair to your desired thickness.

Tips

Here are some tips for thinning out your thick wavy hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up if you need to.
  • Don’t cut too much at once – you can always go back and cut more later.
  • Be careful not to cut too close to your scalp.
  • If you’re using thinning shears, make sure you’re cutting into the hair at an angle.
  • If you’re using a razor, make sure it’s sharp.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out thick wavy hair:

How often should I thin out my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. Some people may only need to thin out their hair once every few months, while others may need to do it more frequently.

Can I thin out my hair at home?

Yes, you can thin out your hair at home using thinning shears, a razor, or scissors. However, if you’re not confident in your abilities, it’s best to go to a professional stylist.

Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

Thinning out your hair can make it look less voluminous, but it won’t necessarily make it look thinner overall.

Hair Care

Thinning out your hair can make it more manageable, but it’s important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy. Here are some tips for caring for your thick wavy hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.

Thinning out your thick wavy hair can be a game-changer when it comes to managing and styling it. With the right tools and techniques, you can create a more defined, textured look that’s easy to maintain. Just remember to take care of your hair properly to keep it healthy and strong.
