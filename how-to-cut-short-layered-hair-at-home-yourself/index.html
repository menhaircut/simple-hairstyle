How To Cut Short Layered Hair At Home Yourself Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Dec 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to change up your short hairdo, layers are a great option to add dimension and movement to your locks. However, going to the salon to get your hair cut can be expensive, and sometimes you just don't have the time to schedule an appointment. That's where cutting your hair at home comes in! In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut short layered hair at home yourself.

Description

Cutting your own hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done! If you're unsure about doing it yourself, you can always ask a friend or family member to help you out. But if you're ready to give it a go, we've got you covered.

How To

Before we get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Sectioning clips (optional)

Now that you have your tools ready, let's get started!

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you're working with clean hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use your sectioning clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Top Section

Start with the top section of your hair. Comb it straight up and use your scissors to cut the ends. You can choose to cut straight across or at an angle for a more layered look.

Step 4: Cut the Side Sections

Next, move on to the left and right side sections. Comb each section straight down and cut the ends at an angle. This will create layers and add movement to your hair.

Step 5: Cut the Back Section

Finally, move on to the back section of your hair. Comb it straight down and cut the ends at an angle, just like you did with the side sections. Take your time and make sure the layers are even.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

To blend the layers, use your scissors to point cut into the ends of your hair. This will help create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 7: Use Clippers (Optional)

If you want to create a more textured look, you can use clippers to add texture to the ends of your hair. Use the clippers to cut into the ends of your hair, creating choppy layers.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it! Use a blow dryer and round brush to create volume and movement. You can also use a straightener or curling iron to add more texture.

Tips

  • Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to go slow and get it right than to rush and end up with a bad haircut.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Sharp scissors will make it easier to cut your hair and ensure that you get a clean cut.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, start with small snips and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't uncut your hair!

FAQ

Can I cut my short layered hair with regular scissors?

No, regular scissors won't give you a clean cut and may end up damaging your hair. Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors.

How often should I cut my short layered hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. On average, you should aim to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Can I add layers to my already layered hair?

Yes, you can add layers to your already layered hair. Follow the same steps as outlined in this guide, but start by cutting the longer layers first.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. You can also use hair oils or serums to add shine and prevent frizz.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can create a short layered haircut that looks great and saves you money. Happy cutting!
