Does It Hurt More To Wax Long Or Short Hair

Written by Eveline Jul 23, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're new to waxing, you might be wondering whether it hurts more to wax long or short hair. The truth is, there's no simple answer to this question. In this blog post, we'll explore the factors that can affect the pain level during waxing and provide some tips to minimize discomfort. We'll also answer some frequently asked questions about hair removal and provide some advice on how to take care of your skin post-waxing.

Table of Contents

  • Factors that Affect Pain During Waxing
  • Tips to Minimize Discomfort During Waxing
  • FAQs about Hair Removal
  • Hair Care Tips After Waxing

Factors that Affect Pain During Waxing

The length of your hair is just one of the many factors that can affect the pain level during waxing. Here are some other factors to consider:

Hormones

Fluctuations in hormones can affect the sensitivity of your skin, making waxing more painful during certain times of the month.

Skin Sensitivity

If you have sensitive skin, you may experience more pain during waxing. It's important to let your esthetician know if you have any skin conditions or allergies before your appointment.

Experience of the Esthetician

A skilled esthetician will be able to minimize pain during waxing by using the proper technique and waxing at the right temperature.

Type of Wax

The type of wax used during your appointment can also affect pain level. Hard wax is typically less painful than soft wax, as it adheres only to the hair and not the skin.

Tips to Minimize Discomfort During Waxing

While waxing can never be completely painless, there are a few things you can do to minimize discomfort:

Exfoliate

Exfoliating a day or two before your appointment can help remove dead skin cells and make waxing less painful.

Take an Over-the-Counter Pain Reliever

Taking an over-the-counter pain reliever, such as ibuprofen, before your appointment can help minimize pain during waxing.

Avoid Caffeine and Alcohol

Caffeine and alcohol can increase skin sensitivity, so it's best to avoid these substances before your appointment.

FAQs about Hair Removal

How long should my hair be before waxing?

Your hair should be at least 1/4 inch long before waxing. If it's too short, the wax won't be able to grip the hair properly.

Can I wax if I'm pregnant?

Yes, you can wax while pregnant, but it's important to let your esthetician know before your appointment.

How long will the results of waxing last?

Waxing typically lasts between 3-6 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows.

Hair Care Tips After Waxing

Moisturize

After waxing, it's important to moisturize your skin to prevent dryness and irritation.

Avoid Sun Exposure

Avoid sun exposure for at least 24 hours after waxing, as your skin may be more sensitive to UV rays.

Avoid Tight Clothing

Avoid wearing tight clothing that can rub against your skin and cause irritation.

In conclusion, whether it hurts more to wax long or short hair depends on several factors. By following our tips and advice, you can minimize discomfort during waxing and take care of your skin post-waxing. If you're still unsure about waxing, consult with your esthetician before your appointment to discuss your concerns.
