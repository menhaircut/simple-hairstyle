How Long Does Hair Fall Out After Stopping Birth Control

Written by William Aug 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're reading this article, it's likely that you've recently stopped taking birth control and are experiencing hair loss. This is a common side effect of hormonal birth control, and it can be alarming to see clumps of hair falling out in the shower or on your brush. The good news is that hair loss after stopping birth control is usually temporary and can be managed with the right hair care and lifestyle changes.

How to Prevent Hair Loss After Stopping Birth Control

The best way to prevent hair loss after stopping birth control is to prepare your body for the change. This means eating a healthy diet, getting enough sleep, and reducing your stress levels. You can also talk to your doctor about switching to a non-hormonal form of birth control, such as a copper IUD or condoms.

It's important to note that hair loss after stopping birth control is usually temporary and will eventually stop on its own. However, it can take several months for your hair to return to its normal thickness and texture.

Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Hair Loss After Stopping Birth Control

1. Be gentle with your hair. Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as bleach or hair dye, and limit your use of heat styling tools.

2. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically designed for thinning hair or hair loss.

3. Massage your scalp. This can help improve circulation and stimulate hair growth.

4. Take supplements. Biotin, iron, and vitamin D are all important for healthy hair growth.

5. Eat a healthy diet. Focus on foods that are rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals, such as eggs, nuts, and leafy greens.

Tips for Coping with Hair Loss After Stopping Birth Control

1. Experiment with hairstyles. Try out different haircuts and styles that can help camouflage thinning hair.

2. Consider a wig or hair extensions. This can be a temporary solution while you wait for your hair to grow back.

3. Talk to a therapist. Hair loss can be emotionally distressing, and it's important to take care of your mental health as well.

FAQs About Hair Loss After Stopping Birth Control

How long does hair fall out after stopping birth control?

It can take several months for hair loss to stop after stopping birth control. However, it's important to note that hair loss is usually temporary and your hair will eventually grow back.

Will my hair grow back after stopping birth control?

Yes, hair loss after stopping birth control is usually temporary and your hair will eventually grow back. However, it can take several months for your hair to return to its normal thickness and texture.

Can birth control cause permanent hair loss?

While it's rare, some forms of hormonal birth control can cause permanent hair loss. This is more likely to occur with certain types of progestin, such as levonorgestrel and norgestimate.
