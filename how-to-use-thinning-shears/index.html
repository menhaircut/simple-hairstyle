How To Use Thinning Shears A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are an essential tool for hairstylists and anyone who wants to achieve a certain hairstyle. These specialized scissors are designed to remove bulk from hair, creating a more natural and blended look. If you're new to using thinning shears, don't worry, it's not as complicated as it seems. In this article, we'll walk you through how to use thinning shears, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Thinning Shears
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Thinning Shears
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Use Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are used to remove excess hair from the head, creating a more natural and blended look. They work by cutting some hairs while leaving others untouched, which creates a textured effect. Thinning shears are often used on thick hair, but they can be used on any hair type.

What You Will Need

Before you start using your thinning shears, you need to make sure you have the following:

  • Thinning shears
  • Clip
  • Comb
  • Hair dryer (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have all the necessary tools, it's time to start using your thinning shears. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair before using thinning shears. This will help you get an accurate idea of how much hair needs to be thinned out. You can also use a hair dryer to speed up the drying process.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using clips. This will help you work on one section at a time and prevent you from cutting too much hair.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier for you to use the thinning shears.

Step 4: Use the Thinning Shears

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and start cutting the hair in a zigzag motion. Be sure to cut only a small amount of hair at a time.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've finished cutting the first section, check your progress by releasing the clip and letting the hair fall naturally. If you need to thin out more hair, repeat the previous steps.

Step 6: Repeat on Other Sections

Repeat these steps on the other sections of your hair until you've achieved the desired look.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears:

  • Use thinning shears only on dry hair.
  • Cut only a small amount of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much.
  • Use the thinning shears in a zigzag motion to create a natural look.
  • Start with the bottom layers of your hair and work your way up.
  • Be careful not to thin out too much hair, as this can lead to an uneven look.

FAQs

Can thinning shears be used on any hair type?

Yes, thinning shears can be used on any hair type, but they are most often used on thick hair.

How often should you use thinning shears?

You should use thinning shears only when necessary, as using them too often can lead to an uneven look and damaged hair.

Can thinning shears be used on wet hair?

No, thinning shears should only be used on dry hair.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals.

Now that you know how to use thinning shears, you can achieve a more natural and blended look with ease. Remember to follow these steps carefully and use the thinning shears only when necessary. With a little practice, you'll soon be a pro at using thinning shears!
