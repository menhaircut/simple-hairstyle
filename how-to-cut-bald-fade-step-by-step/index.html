How To Cut Bald Fade Step By Step A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to get a fresh haircut, the bald fade is a popular style that's sure to turn heads. This style is characterized by a gradual fade from short hair at the top to bald skin at the bottom. Although it might seem intimidating to cut a bald fade, it's actually a straightforward process that anyone can learn. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut bald fade step by step, so you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.

Description

The bald fade is a popular haircut that's characterized by a gradual fade from short hair at the top to bald skin at the bottom. This style is versatile and can be customized to suit different hair types and face shapes. Whether you're looking for a bold and edgy look or a more subtle style, the bald fade is a great option.

How to Cut Bald Fade Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Make sure your clippers are sharp and oiled before you start.

Step 2: Choose Your Guard Size

The first step in cutting a bald fade is to choose your guard size. This will determine how short you want your hair to be at the top. The most common guard sizes for a bald fade are 1, 2, or 3. If you're not sure which guard size to use, start with a larger size and work your way down.

Step 3: Start with the Top

Once you've chosen your guard size, it's time to start cutting. Start with the top of your hair and work your way down. Use your clippers to trim your hair to the desired length, using the comb to guide the clippers and ensure an even cut.

Step 4: Create the Fade

Once you've cut the top of your hair, it's time to create the fade. Start with a higher guard size and work your way down to a lower guard size. Use the clippers to create a gradual fade from the top of your hair to the bald skin at the bottom. Use the comb to blend the different lengths of hair together for a seamless transition.

Step 5: Clean Up the Edges

Once you've created the fade, it's time to clean up the edges. Use your clippers to trim any stray hairs around your ears and neck. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that you missed with the clippers.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to check your work. Use a mirror to check the back of your head and ensure that your haircut is even. Make any necessary adjustments before moving on.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. Use a styling product like pomade or wax to add texture and hold to your hair. Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.

Tips

  • Start with a larger guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting your hair too short.
  • Use the comb to guide your clippers and ensure an even cut.
  • Blend the different lengths of hair together for a seamless fade.
  • Check your work in the mirror to ensure an even cut.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find the one that works best for you.

FAQ

Can I Cut a Bald Fade at Home?

Yes, anyone can learn how to cut a bald fade at home with the right tools and techniques. However, if you're unsure about your skills, it's always a good idea to visit a professional barber.

What Guard Size Should I Use for a Bald Fade?

The most common guard sizes for a bald fade are 1, 2, or 3. However, the guard size you choose will depend on how short you want your hair to be at the top.

How Often Should I Cut My Hair for a Bald Fade?

How often you cut your hair will depend on how quickly your hair grows. Most people need to cut their hair every 2-4 weeks to maintain a bald fade.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type to keep your hair clean and healthy. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and protect it from the sun to prevent damage. With the right care, your bald fade will look great for weeks to come.
