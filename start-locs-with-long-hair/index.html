Start Locs With Long Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your long, straight hair and want to try something new? Starting locs with long hair can be a daunting task, but with the right information and steps, you can achieve the desired outcome. In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to start locs with long hair, including how to prepare your hair, the process of starting locs, tips, and frequently asked questions.

How to Prepare Your Hair for Locs

Before starting locs, it is important to prepare your hair to ensure that it is in the best possible condition. Here are some tips:

1. Get a Trim

Trimming your hair before starting locs removes any split ends and helps to prevent breakage. It also makes it easier to maintain your locs as they grow.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair helps to moisturize and strengthen it, making it less prone to breakage. Use a deep conditioner that is suitable for your hair type and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it off.

3. Stop Using Heat Tools

Heat tools such as flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more difficult to form locs. Avoid using them for at least a month before starting locs.

The Process of Starting Locs

There are several methods for starting locs, including the palm roll method, the two-strand twist method, and the interlocking method. Here are the basic steps for starting locs:

1. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using a comb or your fingers. The size of the sections will depend on the method you choose and the thickness of your hair.

2. Apply Product

Apply a locking product to each section of hair. This helps to bind the hair together and encourage the formation of locs. You can use a commercial locking product or make your own using natural ingredients such as aloe vera gel or beeswax.

3. Start the Loc

Start the loc by either palm rolling, two-strand twisting, or interlocking the hair. Repeat this process for each section of hair until all of your hair is in locs.

Tips for Starting Locs with Long Hair

Here are some tips to help you start locs with long hair:

1. Be Patient

Starting locs with long hair takes time and patience. It can take up to a year for your locs to fully mature, so be prepared for a long journey.

2. Keep Your Hair Clean

Keeping your hair clean is essential for healthy locs. Wash your hair regularly using a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and keep your scalp clean.

3. Avoid Using Heavy Products

Avoid using heavy products on your hair, as they can weigh it down and make it difficult for your locs to form. Instead, use lightweight products that won't leave residue on your hair.

FAQs

1. Can I start locs with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start locs with relaxed hair. However, it is best to wait until your hair has grown out a bit before starting locs to avoid breakage.

2. How often should I wash my locs?

You should wash your locs at least once a week to keep them clean and healthy.

3. Can I color my locs?

Yes, you can color your locs. However, it is best to wait until your locs have fully matured before coloring them to avoid damaging them.

Hair Care for Locs

Here are some hair care tips for locs:

1. Keep Your Scalp Moisturized

Keeping your scalp moisturized is essential for healthy locs. Use a natural oil such as coconut oil or olive oil to moisturize your scalp and prevent dryness.

2. Protect Your Locs at Night

Protecting your locs at night can help to prevent breakage and frizz. Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to protect your locs while you sleep.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension on your locs and lead to breakage. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles that won't pull on your locs.

In conclusion, starting locs with long hair is a process that requires patience and dedication. By following the steps and tips outlined in this article, you can achieve healthy and beautiful locs that will last for years to come.
