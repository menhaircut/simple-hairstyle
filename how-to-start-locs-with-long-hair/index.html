How To Start Locs With Long Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Are you tired of your long, relaxed hair and considering a new hairstyle? Starting locs can be a great option for those looking for a low-maintenance, unique look. However, if you have long hair, the process may seem daunting. In this guide, we will walk you through how to start locs with long hair.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Start Locs with Long Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Locs with Long Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Start Locs with Long Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's discuss what locs are and what you need to know before starting them. Locs, also known as dreadlocks, are a hairstyle where the hair is matted and knotted into rope-like strands. Starting locs with long hair requires patience and dedication, as the process can take several months to a year to fully mature.

Here are some things to keep in mind before starting locs:

  • Locs require maintenance and upkeep, including regular washing and retwisting.
  • Starting locs with long hair can be a lengthy process, so be prepared for the journey.
  • Locs can be started with different techniques, including freeform or palm rolling.
  • Be mindful of the products you use on your hair, as some can cause buildup and prevent the locs from matting properly.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have an understanding of what to expect, let's get into the step-by-step guide on how to start locs with long hair:

Step 1: Decide on a Technique

There are several techniques for starting locs, including freeform, palm rolling, and interlocking. Research each technique to determine which one is best for you and your hair type.

Step 2: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Clean hair is essential before starting locs. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue, and condition to keep your hair moisturized.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small, equal-sized parts using a comb or your fingers. The size of the sections will depend on the technique you choose.

Step 4: Begin Twisting Your Hair

If you choose the palm rolling technique, start by twisting each section of hair between your palms in a clockwise motion until it starts to matte. Repeat this process for each section until all of your hair is twisted.

Step 5: Maintain Your Locs

Once your hair is twisted, you will need to maintain your locs by washing and retwisting regularly. This will help your locs mature and prevent them from unraveling.

Tips for Starting Locs with Long Hair

Starting locs with long hair can be challenging, so here are some tips to help you along the way:

  • Be patient. Locs take time to mature, and you may experience some discomfort during the process.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.
  • Avoid using heavy products on your hair, as they can cause buildup and prevent your locs from matting properly.
  • Stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about starting locs with long hair:

How long does it take for locs to mature?

Locs can take several months to a year to fully mature, depending on your hair type and the technique used to start them.

Can I start locs with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start locs with relaxed hair. However, it may take longer for your locs to mature and you may need to avoid using chemical treatments on your hair while your locs are forming.

Can I wash my hair with locs?

Yes, you can and should wash your hair with locs. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using heavy conditioners that can cause buildup.

Hair Care for Locs

Once your locs have matured, it is important to maintain them properly to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for your locs:

  • Wash your locs regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Retwist your locs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid using heavy products on your locs, as they can cause buildup and prevent them from matting properly.
  • Protect your locs at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.

Starting locs with long hair can be a rewarding experience, but it requires patience and dedication. By following these tips and taking care of your locs properly, you can enjoy your new hairstyle for years to come.
