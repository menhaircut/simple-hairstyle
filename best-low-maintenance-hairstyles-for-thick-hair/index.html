Best Low Maintenance Hairstyles For Thick Hair

Written by Petter Sep 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it looks great styled, it can be a hassle to maintain, especially when you're short on time. Fortunately, there are plenty of low maintenance hairstyles for thick hair that look great and don't require too much upkeep. In this article, we'll explore some of the best low maintenance hairstyles for thick hair, along with tips for keeping your hair looking healthy and beautiful.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Low maintenance hairstyles are perfect for those who don't have the time or inclination to spend hours styling their hair every day. These hairstyles are easy to maintain and require minimal upkeep, making them ideal for busy people who still want to look their best. When it comes to thick hair, low maintenance hairstyles are particularly important, as thick hair can be difficult to manage and style.

How to

The key to achieving a low maintenance hairstyle for thick hair is to choose a style that works with your hair's natural texture and thickness. Some great options include:

1. Long Layers

Long layers are a great option for thick hair, as they help to reduce bulk and add movement and texture. To achieve this look, ask your stylist to cut long layers throughout your hair, starting at your chin or collarbone. You can then style your hair using a simple blowout or air dry for a natural, effortless look.

2. Bob

A classic bob is a great low maintenance hairstyle for thick hair, as it's easy to style and looks great on almost everyone. Ask your stylist to cut your hair just above your shoulders, with or without bangs, depending on your preference. You can then style your hair using a simple blowout or air dry for a natural, effortless look.

3. Pixie Cut

If you're feeling daring, a pixie cut is a great low maintenance hairstyle for thick hair. This short, stylish cut requires minimal styling and looks great on those with thick, textured hair. Ask your stylist to cut your hair short on the sides and back, leaving a little length on top for styling.

Step by step

Here are some simple steps for achieving a low maintenance hairstyle for thick hair:

1. Choose the right cut

Choose a cut that works with your hair's natural texture and thickness, such as long layers, a bob, or a pixie cut.

2. Invest in the right products

Invest in products that are designed to help manage thick hair, such as a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, a smoothing serum, and a texturizing spray.

3. Style your hair with minimal heat

To minimize damage to your hair, try to style it with minimal heat. Air dry your hair whenever possible, and use a low heat setting on your blow dryer or flat iron.

Tips

Here are some tips for maintaining a low maintenance hairstyle for thick hair:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid drying it out.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about low maintenance hairstyles for thick hair:

Q: Can I still style my hair with a low maintenance hairstyle?

A: Yes! While low maintenance hairstyles are designed to be easy to maintain, you can still style your hair if you want to. Experiment with different products and styling tools to find a look that works for you.

Q: Will a low maintenance hairstyle work for all types of thick hair?

A: While low maintenance hairstyles can work for all types of thick hair, it's important to choose a style that works with your hair's natural texture and thickness. Consult with your stylist to find a cut that will work best for you.

Hair Care

When it comes to caring for your thick hair, it's important to use products that are designed to help manage its thickness and texture. Look for volumizing shampoos and conditioners, as well as smoothing serums and texturizing sprays. Additionally, try to minimize heat styling as much as possible, as this can damage your hair over time. Finally, make sure to get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Thick hair doesn't have to be a hassle. With the right cut and a few simple styling tips, you can achieve a low maintenance hairstyle that looks great and requires minimal upkeep. Experiment with different styles and products to find a look that works for you, and don't forget to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
