What Is The Highest Grade Of Human Hair

Are you looking for the best quality human hair for your next hair extension or wig? Then you must be wondering what is the highest grade of human hair available in the market. In this article, we will explain everything you need to know about the highest grade of human hair, including the description, how-to, step-by-step, tips, FAQs, and hair care. So, let's dive in!

Description

The highest grade of human hair is the virgin hair. Virgin hair is the highest quality human hair that has not been chemically processed, dyed, or treated with any harmful chemicals. It is the purest form of human hair that is collected from a single donor and has all its cuticles intact and aligned in the same direction. Virgin hair is the most expensive type of human hair, but it is worth the investment as it lasts longer, looks more natural, and can be styled and colored like your own hair.

How to Determine the Highest Grade of Human Hair

There are a few factors to consider when determining the highest grade of human hair:

  • Source: The highest grade of human hair is sourced from a single donor, which means all the hair is of the same quality and texture. The hair should be collected from healthy donors who have not used any chemicals or treatments on their hair.
  • Texture: The highest grade of human hair should have a natural texture that matches your own hair. It should not be too shiny or silky, as this can indicate that the hair has been treated with chemicals.
  • Color: Virgin hair comes in a natural color, which can vary from dark brown to black. If the hair has been dyed or colored, it is not virgin hair.
  • Cuticles: The highest grade of human hair should have all its cuticles intact and aligned in the same direction. This makes the hair less prone to tangling and shedding.
  • Softness: Virgin hair should feel soft and silky to the touch. If the hair feels rough or coarse, it may have been chemically treated.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Highest Grade of Human Hair

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you choose the highest grade of human hair:

  1. Research different brands and suppliers that sell virgin hair.
  2. Read reviews and check the quality of the hair.
  3. Contact the supplier and ask for information about the hair, including the source, texture, color, and cuticles.
  4. Ask for a sample of the hair to check the quality and texture.
  5. Choose the texture and color that matches your own hair.
  6. Place your order and wait for the hair to arrive.
  7. Once you receive the hair, inspect it to make sure it meets your expectations.
  8. Wash and condition the hair before installing it.
  9. Style and enjoy your new hair!

Tips for Maintaining the Highest Grade of Human Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining the highest grade of human hair:

  • Wash and condition the hair regularly to keep it clean and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage the hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair gently.
  • Store the hair in a cool and dry place to prevent tangling and shedding.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or treatments on the hair.
  • Protect the hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or swimming cap.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about the highest grade of human hair:

Q: How long does virgin hair last?

A: Virgin hair can last up to two years with proper care and maintenance.

Q: Can I color or bleach virgin hair?

A: Yes, you can color or bleach virgin hair. However, it is recommended to have a professional do it to avoid damaging the hair.

Q: How much does virgin hair cost?

A: Virgin hair is the most expensive type of human hair, and the price can vary depending on the length, texture, and quality of the hair.

Q: Can I swim with virgin hair?

A: Yes, you can swim with virgin hair, but it is recommended to wear a swimming cap to protect the hair from chlorine and saltwater.

Hair Care for the Highest Grade of Human Hair

Here are some hair care tips specifically for the highest grade of human hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition the hair once a week to keep it hydrated and soft.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down the hair.
  • Wrap the hair in a silk or satin scarf at night to prevent tangling and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Now that you know everything about the highest grade of human hair, you can make an informed decision when choosing the best quality hair for your next hair extension or wig. Remember to always take good care of your hair to make it last longer and look its best!
