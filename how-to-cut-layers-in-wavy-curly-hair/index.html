How To Cut Layers In Wavy Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Oct 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Wavy curly hair can be a challenge to cut, especially if you're looking to add layers. But with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look that enhances your natural waves and curls. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting layers in wavy curly hair, from preparation to finishing touches. We'll also provide tips and answers to frequently asked questions, as well as suggestions for hair care after your cut. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Sectioning
  • Cutting Technique
  • Adding Layers
  • Blending

Preparation

Before you begin cutting your wavy curly hair, it's important to prepare your hair and tools. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for wavy or curly hair. This will help define your waves and curls and make them easier to cut. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, but avoid brushing or using a fine-tooth comb, as this can disrupt your curl pattern.

Next, gather your cutting tools. You'll need sharp hair cutting scissors, a spray bottle filled with water, and hair clips to section your hair. If you're cutting your hair yourself, you may also want to use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head.

Sectioning

The key to cutting layers in wavy curly hair is to section your hair properly. Start by parting your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, take a small section of hair at the front of your head, just above your ear, and clip the rest of your hair out of the way. This will be your first section.

Continue taking small sections of hair from the front to the back of your head, clipping each section out of the way as you go. You should end up with four sections total: two in the front and two in the back.

Cutting Technique

Now that you've sectioned your hair, it's time to start cutting. Begin with the first section at the front of your head. Spray the section with water to dampen it, then comb it straight down. Use your scissors to make small, diagonal cuts into the hair, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Be sure to cut each section at an angle, rather than straight across, to create layers that blend naturally with your curls.

Once you've finished cutting the first section, unclip the next section and repeat the process. Continue cutting each section until you've cut all four sections.

Adding Layers

If you want to add more layers to your wavy curly hair, you can do so by taking smaller sections and cutting them at steeper angles. Start by taking a small section of hair at the top of your head and combing it straight up. Use your scissors to cut into the hair at an angle, starting at the ends and working your way up. Repeat this process with small sections of hair until you've achieved your desired layering.

Blending

Once you've finished cutting your layers, it's important to blend them together for a natural look. To do this, take a small section of hair from the top of your head and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to make small, horizontal cuts into the hair, working your way down the section. This will help blend the layers together and prevent any harsh lines.

Tips for Cutting Wavy Curly Hair

  • Always cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink when it dries and affect the length of your cut.
  • Use hair cutting scissors, as regular scissors can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.
  • Take small sections of hair and cut them gradually, rather than cutting large sections all at once.
  • Be patient and take your time, as cutting wavy curly hair requires precision and attention to detail.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my wavy curly hair myself?

A: Yes, but it's important to have the right tools and technique. If you're not confident in your ability to cut your hair, it's best to visit a professional stylist.

Q: How often should I cut my wavy curly hair?

A: It depends on the length and style of your hair, but generally every 8-12 weeks is recommended to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Your Cut

After you've cut your wavy curly hair, it's important to care for it properly to maintain its health and style. Use products that are designed for wavy or curly hair, such as a leave-in conditioner or curl cream, to define your curls and prevent frizz. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and disrupt your curl pattern. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. And finally, be gentle with your hair and avoid pulling or tugging on your curls, as this can cause breakage and damage.
