How To Make Straight Hair Messy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of having perfectly straight hair that looks too polished and put-together? Do you want to achieve that effortlessly messy, undone look? Look no further! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to make straight hair messy.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Make Straight Hair Messy
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Messy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Make Straight Hair Messy

Making straight hair look messy may seem counterintuitive, but it’s actually quite simple. The key is to add texture and volume to the hair. You can achieve this by using the right styling products and techniques.

Styling Products

The right styling products are essential to achieve that messy look. Here are some products you can use:

  • Dry Shampoo: This will add texture and volume to your hair.
  • Sea Salt Spray: This will give your hair that tousled, beachy look.
  • Texturizing Spray: This will add texture and grip to your hair.
  • Hair Oil: This will add shine and tame frizz.

Styling Techniques

Now that you have the right products, here are some styling techniques you can use:

  • Messy Bun: Pull your hair back into a loose bun and use bobby pins to secure it. Pull out a few pieces of hair to frame your face.
  • Twist and Pin: Twist small sections of hair and pin them back with bobby pins. Repeat until you’ve pinned back all of your hair.
  • Braids: Braid your hair and then gently pull on the strands to create a messy, undone look.
  • Scrunching: Apply sea salt spray to damp hair and scrunch your hair with your hands. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance the waves.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to make straight hair messy:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a dry shampoo to your roots to add texture and volume.
  3. Apply a texturizing spray to the mid-lengths and ends of your hair. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair and create waves.
  4. Using a curling iron, curl random sections of your hair. Don’t curl every section, as you want to keep it looking messy and undone.
  5. Apply a hair oil to the ends of your hair to add shine and tame frizz.
  6. Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create that messy look.
  7. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Maintaining Messy Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your messy hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day. This will help preserve the texture and volume.
  • Use a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and maintain your hairstyle.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this will flatten it.
  • If your hair starts to look too oily, use dry shampoo to refresh it.

FAQs

Q: Can I achieve a messy look with short hair?

A: Absolutely! Short hair can be styled in a messy, textured look using the same products and techniques as long hair.

Q: How do I prevent my hair from looking too messy?

A: Start with a small amount of product and build it up gradually. You can always add more, but it’s harder to take away once you’ve applied too much.

Q: Can I achieve a messy look without using heat?

A: Yes! You can achieve a messy look by using braids or twists and then gently pulling on the strands to create texture.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some general hair care tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet and drink plenty of water to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.

Now that you know how to make straight hair messy, go ahead and try it out for yourself! With the right products and techniques, you can achieve that effortlessly messy, undone look in no time.
