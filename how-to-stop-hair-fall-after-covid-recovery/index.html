How To Stop Hair Fall After Covid Recovery A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair fall after recovering from Covid-19? You're not alone. Hair loss is a common side effect of Covid recovery, and it can be distressing to see clumps of hair falling out every day. However, you don't have to suffer in silence. In this article, we'll discuss how to stop hair fall after Covid recovery and offer some useful tips to help you grow your hair back.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Hair Fall After Covid Recovery
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Fall
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Routine

How to Stop Hair Fall After Covid Recovery

The first step to stopping hair fall after Covid recovery is to understand why it's happening. Covid-19 can cause significant stress on the body, leading to hormonal imbalances, nutritional deficiencies, and inflammation. All of these factors can contribute to hair loss.

The good news is that hair fall after Covid recovery is usually temporary, and your hair will grow back once your body recovers. However, there are some steps you can take to speed up the process and prevent further hair loss.

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

A healthy diet is essential for overall health, including hair health. Make sure you're eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, iron, and vitamins. Some foods that are good for hair health include:

  • Lean meat
  • Fish
  • Eggs
  • Leafy greens
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Whole grains

2. Take Supplements

If you're not getting enough nutrients from your diet, you may benefit from taking supplements. Some supplements that are good for hair health include:

  • Biotin
  • Vitamin D
  • Zinc
  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin E
  • Iron

3. Manage Stress

Stress can be a major contributor to hair loss, so it's essential to manage it effectively. Some ways to manage stress include:

  • Meditation
  • Yoga
  • Breathing exercises
  • Exercise
  • Talking to a therapist

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to stopping hair fall after Covid recovery:

1. Consult a Doctor

If you're experiencing significant hair loss, it's essential to consult a doctor. They can help rule out any underlying medical conditions and recommend treatments to help promote hair growth.

2. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid brushing or combing your hair too aggressively, as this can cause further hair loss. Use a wide-tooth comb or brush and be gentle when detangling your hair.

3. Use a Mild Shampoo

Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils. Instead, use a mild shampoo that's gentle on your hair and scalp.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners, curling irons, and hair dryers, as they can damage your hair and cause further hair loss.

5. Get Regular Haircuts

Regular haircuts can help promote hair growth by getting rid of split ends and promoting healthy hair growth.

Tips to Prevent Hair Fall

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair fall:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Avoid using chemical treatments like hair dye and bleach
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions
  • Get enough sleep and exercise regularly

FAQs

Q: How long does hair fall last after Covid recovery?

A: Hair fall after Covid recovery can last for several weeks to several months, depending on the individual. However, most people will see their hair grow back within a year.

Q: Will my hair grow back after Covid recovery?

A: Yes, most people will see their hair grow back after Covid recovery. However, it may take several months to a year for your hair to fully recover.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can be a major contributor to hair loss. When you're stressed, your body releases hormones that can disrupt the hair growth cycle and lead to hair loss.

Hair Care Routine

Here's a simple hair care routine to help promote hair growth:

1. Wash your hair with a mild shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that's free from harsh chemicals and sulfates.

2. Condition your hair

Use a conditioner that's formulated for your hair type and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing.

3. Use a hair serum or oil

Apply a hair serum or oil to the ends of your hair to help prevent breakage and promote healthy hair growth.

4. Dry your hair gently

Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, as this can cause breakage. Instead, gently pat your hair dry with a soft towel or t-shirt.

5. Style your hair gently

Avoid using heat styling tools and tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles that don't put too much stress on your hair.

Follow these tips, and you'll be well on your way to stopping hair fall after Covid recovery and promoting healthy hair growth.
