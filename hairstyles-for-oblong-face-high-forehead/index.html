Understanding Your Face Shape

Before diving into hairstyles, it's important to understand your face shape. Oblong faces are longer than they are wide, with a high forehead and a prominent chin. You may also have a slightly pointed jawline.

The key to finding the right hairstyle is to balance out your face shape. This means adding width to the sides of your face and minimizing the length of your forehead.

Hairstyles for Oblong Face High Forehead

Now that you know your face shape, let's explore different hairstyles that complement your features.

1. Bangs

Bangs are a great way to minimize the appearance of a high forehead. Side-swept bangs or blunt bangs that hit just above the eyebrows are both flattering options.

2. Layered Bob

A layered bob adds volume and width to the sides of your face, balancing out an oblong shape. It's also easy to maintain and style.

3. Long Layers

If you prefer longer hair, opt for long layers that frame your face and soften your jawline. This style works best with loose waves or curls.

4. Pixie Cut

A pixie cut with layers and texture can add volume to the crown of your head, making your face appear shorter. This style is also low-maintenance and edgy.

5. Updos

Updos such as buns or braids can create the illusion of a shorter forehead while still showcasing your length. Avoid slicked-back styles, which can elongate your face even more.

Tips for Styling Your Hair

Now that you have some hairstyle ideas, here are some tips to help you style your hair.

1. Use Products

Products such as mousse or volumizing spray can add texture and lift to your hair, making it appear fuller and wider. Avoid heavy, oily products that can weigh down your hair.

2. Experiment with Parting

Changing your part can create the illusion of a different face shape. If you usually part your hair in the middle, try a side part to add width to your face.

3. Embrace Your Natural Texture

Working with your natural texture can make styling easier and more flattering. If you have curly hair, embrace the curls with a layered cut. If you have straight hair, add volume with a textured bob.

FAQs

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have an oblong face and high forehead?

Avoid hairstyles that add height to the crown of your head, such as high ponytails or backcombing. Also, avoid straight, blunt cuts that can elongate your face even more.

Can I still wear my hair in a middle part?

While a middle part can elongate your face, it's still possible to make it work. Try adding side-swept bangs or layers to add width to your face.

Hair Care for Oblong Face High Forehead

Caring for your hair is just as important as styling it. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and strong.

1. Use Heat Protectant

When using heat tools such as a straightener or curling iron, always use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

2. Trim Regularly

Regular trims can prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy. Aim for a trim every 6-8 weeks.

3. Avoid Over-Washing

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and damage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or use dry shampoo in between washes.

With these tips and hairstyle ideas, you can find the perfect look for your oblong face and high forehead. Remember to embrace your natural features and have fun experimenting with different styles!
