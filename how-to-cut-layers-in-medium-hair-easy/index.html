How To Cut Layers In Medium Hair Easy A Step By Step Guide

Description

Medium hair is the perfect length for adding layers to create texture and volume. Cutting layers in medium hair is a great way to switch up your look without sacrificing length. However, getting the perfect layers can be tricky, especially if you don't have experience cutting hair. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut layers in medium hair easy.

How to Cut Layers in Medium Hair Easy

If you're looking for an easy way to cut layers in medium hair, you'll need a few things:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut layers in medium hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair and part it in the middle or on one side, depending on your preference.
  2. Section off the top layer of your hair, from the crown of your head to your temples, and clip it up and out of the way.
  3. Take a section of hair from the back of your head, just above your nape, and comb it straight down. Hold the section with your non-dominant hand and use your dominant hand to cut the hair at a slight angle. This will create your guide length for the rest of your layers.
  4. Continue taking sections of hair from the back of your head, combing them straight down, and cutting them at the same angle as your guide length. Each section should be slightly longer than the previous one.
  5. Once you've finished the back sections, move to the sides of your head. Take sections of hair from the front and cut them at the same angle as your guide length.
  6. Release the top layer of your hair and comb it straight up. Use your guide length to cut the top layer at the same angle, creating shorter layers on top.
  7. Trim any straggly ends or uneven lengths until you're happy with the overall shape.
  8. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your new layered look!

Tips for Cutting Layers in Medium Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in medium hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and cut gradually. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair or creating split ends.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as this can cause it to look shorter than it actually is once it dries.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting layers can be time-consuming, but the end result will be worth it!

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers in medium hair:

  • Do I need to go to a salon to get layers in my hair? No, you can cut layers in your hair at home if you have the right tools and follow the proper technique.
  • Can I cut layers in curly hair? Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. Just be sure to cut each section of hair when it's dry and in its natural state, as curly hair can shrink when it dries.
  • How often should I cut my hair to maintain my layers? It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers and keep them looking fresh.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers in Medium Hair

After cutting layers in your medium hair, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools like hair dryers, straighteners, and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.

By following these tips and techniques, you can easily cut layers in your medium hair at home and enjoy a fresh, new look!
