How To Cut Your Own Curtain Fringe Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune for a professional hair cut? Want to save money and learn how to cut your own curtain fringe? You’ve come to the right place! In this step by step guide, we’ll show you how to achieve the perfect curtain fringe in the comfort of your own home.

Description

A curtain fringe, also known as a Bardot fringe, is a versatile and stylish haircut that frames the face and adds texture to your hair. It’s a low maintenance look that can be easily styled and suits most face shapes. By following these simple steps, you can achieve a salon-worthy curtain fringe without the price tag.

How to Cut Your Own Curtain Fringe

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start, you’ll need to gather some tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp hairdressing scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off the hair you want to cut into a triangle shape. The point of the triangle should be where you want your fringe to start, and the sides of the triangle should be the length you want your fringe to be.

Step 3: Cut Your Fringe

Using your hairdressing scissors, start by cutting the middle of the triangle. Cut slowly and carefully, taking small sections at a time. Work your way outwards, cutting the sides of the triangle. Remember to keep the length of your fringe even and check your progress by holding the hair up to your face.

Step 4: Texturize Your Fringe

To add texture to your fringe, hold a small section of hair between your fingers and snip into it with your scissors. This will create a choppy, textured look that will blend in with the rest of your hair.

Step 5: Style Your Fringe

Once you’ve finished cutting your fringe, style it to your liking. You can blow dry it straight or add some waves using a curling iron. Use a styling product, such as a hair spray or wax, to hold your fringe in place.

Tips

- Start by cutting a little at a time. You can always cut more, but you can’t add hair back once it’s cut.

- Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

- Cut your fringe when your hair is dry. Wet hair can shrink when it dries, causing your fringe to look shorter than you intended.

- If you’re unsure about cutting your own fringe, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQ

Can I cut my fringe with regular scissors?

No, it’s important to use hairdressing scissors as they are designed to cut hair without damaging it. Regular scissors can cause split ends and make your hair look uneven.

How often should I cut my fringe?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your fringe starts to look too long. On average, you should aim to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks.

Can I cut my fringe if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut a curtain fringe if you have curly hair. However, it’s important to remember that curly hair can shrink when it dries, so make sure to cut your fringe when your hair is dry and styled as you normally wear it.

Hair Care

To keep your fringe looking its best, it’s important to take care of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

With these simple steps and tips, you can achieve the perfect curtain fringe without leaving your house. Give it a try and see how much you can save on haircuts!
