How To Use Thinning Shears On Short Thick Hair

Written by Aprilia Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are a great tool for those with short thick hair who want to achieve a more natural and textured look. They are designed to remove bulk and weight from your hair without sacrificing length, making them ideal for those who want to keep their hair short but still want to add some movement and dimension to their locks.

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade and a smooth edge on the other. They work by cutting some of the hair and leaving some of it uncut, creating a textured and layered look. They are different from regular shears which cut all the hair at the same length.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Short Thick Hair

Using thinning shears on short thick hair is relatively easy, but it does require some skill and patience. Here is a step-by-step guide to get you started:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. It's important to work with clean and dry hair so that you can see how much hair you need to thin out.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips or hair ties. This will make it easier for you to work with your hair and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Shears Correctly

Hold the thinning shears with the teeth facing up and the smooth edge facing down. Use your thumb and middle finger to hold the shears and your index finger to guide them.

Step 4: Start Thinning Your Hair

Start thinning your hair by holding a small section of hair between your fingers and running the thinning shears through it. Make sure to only cut a few strands of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much hair and creating uneven layers.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Check your progress as you go along to make sure that you are thinning your hair evenly. It's important to step back and look at your hair from different angles to make sure that it looks balanced and natural.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on short thick hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. It's better to thin out your hair gradually than to cut too much at once.
  • Be patient and take your time. Thinning your hair can be time-consuming, but it's worth it if you want to achieve a natural and textured look.
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet hair. Wet hair is more prone to breakage and damage, so it's better to wait until your hair is dry before using thinning shears.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning shears on short thick hair:

  • Can I use thinning shears on any type of hair?

    • Yes, thinning shears can be used on any type of hair, but they are especially useful for those with thick hair.

  • How often should I thin out my hair?

    • It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Some people may need to thin out their hair every few months, while others may only need to do it once or twice a year.

  • Can I use thinning shears to create bangs?

    • Yes, thinning shears can be used to create bangs, but it's best to leave this to a professional stylist who can ensure that your bangs are cut evenly and at the right length.

Hair Care

After using thinning shears on your hair, it's important to take good care of your locks to ensure that they stay healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.

Using thinning shears on short thick hair can be a great way to add some texture and movement to your locks. With some patience and practice, you can achieve a natural and effortless look that is sure to turn heads.
