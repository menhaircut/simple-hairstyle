How to Do Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

Medium length hair can be tricky to style, but with a little bit of practice and some helpful tips, you can achieve a variety of beautiful hairstyles. Whether you're going for a casual look or a more formal one, there are plenty of options available to you. In this article, we'll go over some of our favorite hairstyles for medium length hair and provide step-by-step instructions on how to achieve them.

Table of Contents

  • Beachy Waves
  • Braided Crown
  • Messy Bun
  • Fishtail Braid
  • Half Up Half Down

Beachy Waves

To achieve beachy waves, start by applying a heat protectant to your hair. Then, use a curling wand to curl small sections of your hair away from your face. Once you've curled all of your hair, use your fingers to gently separate the curls and create a more relaxed, beachy look. Finish with a texturizing spray to add volume and hold.

Tip:

For a more natural look, alternate the direction of your curls as you go.

Braided Crown

To create a braided crown, start by parting your hair down the middle. Then, French braid each side of your hair from the front to the back, securing each braid with a hair tie. Bring each braid up and over the top of your head, securing them with bobby pins. Finish by spraying with a strong hold hairspray to keep the braids in place.

Tip:

If you have trouble braiding your own hair, enlist the help of a friend or watch a tutorial on YouTube.

Messy Bun

To create a messy bun, start by pulling your hair back into a ponytail. Then, twist the ponytail and wrap it around the base, securing with bobby pins. Use your fingers to gently pull and loosen the bun to create a more relaxed, messy look. Finish by spraying with a light hold hairspray to keep everything in place.

Tip:

Messy buns work best on second or third day hair, as the natural oils in your hair help hold everything together.

Fishtail Braid

To create a fishtail braid, start by dividing your hair into two sections. Take a small piece of hair from the outside of the left section and cross it over to the right section. Then, take a small piece from the outside of the right section and cross it over to the left section. Repeat this pattern until you reach the end of your hair, and secure with a hair tie. Use your fingers to gently pull and loosen the braid for a more relaxed look.

Tip:

Fishtail braids work best on hair that hasn't been washed in a day or two, as the natural oils help hold the braid together.

Half Up Half Down

To create a half up half down look, start by pulling the top section of your hair back and securing it with a hair tie or clip. You can leave the bottom section of your hair straight, or add curls or waves for added texture. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep everything in place.

Tip:

Try adding a cute hair accessory like a bow or a headband to dress up your half up half down look.

FAQ

Q: What hair type is best for these hairstyles?

A: These hairstyles work best on medium length hair that is straight or wavy. If you have curly hair, some of these styles may require a bit more effort to achieve.

Q: How long do these hairstyles take to do?

A: The amount of time it takes to do these hairstyles varies depending on your skill level and the complexity of the style. Some styles may take just a few minutes, while others may take upwards of half an hour.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair in order to maintain its health and make styling easier. Here are some tips for keeping your hair in top condition:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any kind of heat styling tool.
  • Invest in a quality hairbrush or comb to prevent breakage and damage.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create a variety of beautiful hairstyles for your medium length hair. Whether you're going for a casual look or a more formal one, there are plenty of options available to you. So grab your tools and get styling!
