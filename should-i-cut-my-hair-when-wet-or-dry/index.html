Should I Cut My Hair When Wet Or Dry

Written by Petter Oct 08, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to cutting your hair, you may wonder whether it's better to do it when your hair is wet or dry. There's no one right answer, as it depends on your hair type, the style you're going for, and personal preference. In this article, we'll discuss the pros and cons of cutting wet vs. dry hair and help you determine which option is best for you.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Hair When Wet
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Hair When Dry
  • Tips for Cutting Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

Description

When you cut your hair wet, it's easier to control and see the length you're cutting. Wet hair also tends to be more elastic, making it less prone to breakage. However, when hair dries, it will shrink, and you may end up cutting off more than you intended. Additionally, if you have curly or wavy hair, cutting it wet may result in a different look once it dries.

On the other hand, cutting your hair dry allows you to see the exact length and shape you're cutting. This is especially useful if you're going for a specific style or have layers in your hair. Dry cutting also works well for people with curly or wavy hair, as you can see the natural curl pattern and avoid any surprises once the hair dries.

How to Cut Hair When Wet

If you prefer to cut your hair when it's wet, follow these steps:

  1. Wash and condition your hair, then towel dry it until it's damp.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Section your hair into manageable parts using clips or hair ties.
  4. Starting at the back, use a comb to measure the length you want to cut, then cut straight across.
  5. Move to the sides and front, following the same process.
  6. Once you've finished cutting, blow-dry your hair to see the final result.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Hair When Dry

If you prefer to cut your hair dry, follow these steps:

  1. Wash and condition your hair, then let it air dry completely.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Section your hair into manageable parts using clips or hair ties.
  4. Starting at the back, use a comb to measure the length you want to cut, then cut straight across.
  5. Move to the sides and front, following the same process.
  6. Once you've finished cutting, blow-dry your hair to see the final result.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Use a comb to measure the length you want to cut, ensuring both sides are even.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up, rather than cutting too much at once.
  • Take breaks as needed to avoid fatigue and ensure the best results.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your hair yourself, but it's important to have the right tools and techniques to ensure a clean cut. Consider watching tutorials or practicing on a small section first before attempting a full cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my hair wet or dry if I have curly hair?

It's generally recommended to cut curly hair when it's dry to avoid any surprises once the hair dries and shrinks.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

Once you've cut your hair, it's important to maintain it with proper hair care. This includes regular washing and conditioning, using heat protectant when using styling tools, and avoiding harsh chemicals and processes that can damage the hair. Consider consulting with a professional stylist for advice on the best products and techniques for your hair type and style.
