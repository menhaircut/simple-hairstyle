What Size Is A 2 Guard Haircut

Written by Oliver Jul 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you wondering what size is a 2 guard haircut? If you're a fan of hair clippers, then you might be familiar with the term "guard." A guard is an attachment that you can put on a clipper that helps you cut hair to a certain length.

In this article, we will explore what size is a 2 guard haircut, how to use it, step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care for this haircut. So, let's dive in!

Description

A 2 guard haircut is a popular hairstyle among men, and it involves using a clipper with a #2 guard attachment. This haircut is versatile and can be used to achieve different styles, from buzz cuts to fades.

The guard size is based on the length of the hair that it leaves behind. In the case of the #2 guard, it leaves around 1/4 inch or 6.35 mm of hair on the scalp. This means that if you want a 2 guard haircut, your hair will be cut to around 1/4 inch in length all around your head.

How to Use a 2 Guard Haircut

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure that you have the right tools. You will need a clipper with a #2 guard attachment, a comb, and a pair of scissors.

Here are the steps to follow:

  1. Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it.
  2. Attach the #2 guard to your clipper.
  3. Start cutting your hair from the sides, moving upwards towards the top of your head. Use the clipper in an upward motion to create an even cut.
  4. Use the comb to lift the hair and trim any stray hairs with scissors.
  5. Finally, use the clipper without the guard to trim the hair around your neck and ears.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Let's go through the steps in more detail:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. Towel-dry your hair until it's slightly damp, but not soaking wet.

Step 2: Attach the #2 Guard to Your Clipper

Attach the #2 guard to your clipper. Make sure that it's securely attached and that there are no gaps between the guard and the clipper.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair from the Sides

Start cutting your hair from the sides, moving upwards towards the top of your head. Hold the clipper at an angle, so that the guard is in contact with your scalp. Use the clipper in an upward motion to create an even cut.

Step 4: Trim Stray Hairs with Scissors

Use the comb to lift the hair and trim any stray hairs with scissors. Cut the hair in the same direction as the clipper to create an even look.

Step 5: Trim the Hair Around Your Neck and Ears

Finally, use the clipper without the guard to trim the hair around your neck and ears. This will help create a clean and polished look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect 2 guard haircut:

  • Use a comb to lift the hair and create an even cut.
  • Take your time and go slow. Rushing can lead to uneven cuts.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Trim the hair around your ears and neck for a polished look.
  • Use hair clippers with adjustable blades to achieve different lengths.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you're not comfortable cutting your own hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the 2 guard haircut:

What other guard sizes can I use?

You can use different guard sizes to achieve different lengths. The #1 guard leaves around 1/8 inch of hair, the #3 guard leaves around 3/8 inch of hair, and so on.

Can I use a 2 guard on long hair?

The 2 guard is best suited for shorter hair. If your hair is longer than 1/4 inch, you might need to use a longer guard or scissors to achieve the desired length.

How often should I get a 2 guard haircut?

The frequency of your haircuts will depend on how fast your hair grows and your personal preference. Some people get a 2 guard haircut every 2-3 weeks, while others might wait longer.

Hair Care

After getting a 2 guard haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and flat irons.
  • Apply hair oil or serum to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach and hair dyes.

And there you have it! Now you know what size is a 2 guard haircut, how to use it, step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care for this popular hairstyle. Happy clipping!
