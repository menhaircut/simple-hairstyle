How To Style Curtain Bangs With Wavy Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curtain bangs have been a popular hairstyle for a few years now, and they're not going anywhere anytime soon. They're a versatile choice that can be styled in many ways, and they're especially great for wavy hair. If you're wondering how to style curtain bangs with wavy hair, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll walk you through everything you need to know to achieve the perfect look.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Style Curtain Bangs with Wavy Hair

Curtain bangs are a type of fringe that's parted down the middle, framing the face on either side. They're called curtain bangs because they're meant to look like a set of curtains pulled to the side. Here's how to style them with wavy hair:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Before you begin styling, make sure your hair is clean and dry. If your hair is wet or damp, the bangs won't hold their shape. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner formulated for wavy hair to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

2. Prep your hair with heat protectant

Heat damage can cause wavy hair to frizz and lose its shape. To protect your hair, apply a heat protectant spray or cream to your bangs and the rest of your hair before styling.

3. Blow dry your bangs

Using a round brush, blow dry your bangs in the direction you want them to fall. If you want them to be more voluminous, blow dry them up and away from your face. If you want them to be sleeker, blow dry them straight down. Make sure to dry them completely.

4. Use a curling iron to add waves

If your hair is naturally wavy, you can skip this step. But if you want to add more defined waves to your bangs, use a small curling iron to curl them away from your face. Don't curl them too tightly, or they'll look too curly.

5. Finish with hairspray

To keep your bangs in place, spray them with a flexible hold hairspray. This will help them hold their shape without looking stiff or crunchy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling curtain bangs with wavy hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Prep your hair with heat protectant
  3. Blow dry your bangs
  4. Use a curling iron to add waves (optional)
  5. Finish with hairspray

Tips for Styling Curtain Bangs with Wavy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect look:

  • Don't overuse heat styling tools. Too much heat can damage your hair, so try to limit your use of curling irons and straighteners.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves.
  • Consider getting a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your bangs looking fresh.
  • Experiment with different parting locations to find the one that flatters your face shape the most.

FAQs

Can I style curtain bangs with curly hair?

Yes, you can definitely style curtain bangs with curly hair. Follow the same steps as you would for wavy hair, but be sure to use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls instead of using a curling iron.

How often should I wash my wavy hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or live in a humid climate, you may need to wash your hair more frequently. If your hair is dry or you live in a dry climate, you may be able to wash it less often. Generally, washing your hair every other day or every three days is a good rule of thumb for wavy hair.

What kind of products should I use on my wavy hair?

Look for products that are specifically formulated for wavy hair. Avoid anything that contains sulfates or alcohol, as these can dry out your hair. Instead, opt for products that contain moisturizing ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, or argan oil.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your wavy hair looking its best:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your ends to keep them moisturized.
  • Avoid towel-drying your hair vigorously, as this can cause breakage. Instead, gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.
  • Consider sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your hair from getting tangled or frizzy overnight.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to style your curtain bangs with wavy hair like a pro. Just remember to be gentle with your hair, use heat protectant, and experiment with different styles until you find the one that works best for you.
