How To Shave The Back And Sides Of Your Head Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Shaving the back and sides of your head is not an easy task, especially if you are doing it for the first time. It requires a lot of patience, skill, and practice. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will guide you through the process of shaving the back and sides of your head in a step-by-step manner. We will also provide you with some tips and tricks that will help you achieve the perfect shave.

Description

Shaving the back and sides of your head is a common practice among men who prefer a bald or buzzed look. This style is popular among athletes, military personnel, and those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle. While it may seem like a simple task, there are some things you need to keep in mind to avoid injuries and achieve the desired look.

How to Shave the Back and Sides of Your Head

Here's how you can shave the back and sides of your head:

Step 1: Prepare Your Tools

Before you start shaving, you need to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of clippers, a razor, shaving cream, and a mirror. Make sure your clippers and razor are sharp and clean.

Step 2: Trim Your Hair

Start by trimming your hair with clippers. Use a guard attachment that will leave your hair about 1/8 inch long. Trim your hair in the direction of hair growth. This will make it easier to shave later.

Step 3: Apply Shaving Cream

Apply shaving cream to the back and sides of your head. Make sure to cover all the areas that you want to shave. Shaving cream will help lubricate your skin and prevent razor burn.

Step 4: Shave the Back of Your Head

Start shaving the back of your head by using a razor. Use short strokes and go against the direction of hair growth. Rinse the razor after every stroke to prevent clogging. Use your free hand to stretch the skin to avoid cuts and nicks.

Step 5: Shave the Sides of Your Head

Once you are done with the back of your head, move on to the sides. Use the same technique as before, and make sure to use short strokes. Be extra careful around the ears and the hairline.

Step 6: Rinse Your Head

Rinse your head with cold water to remove any remaining shaving cream. Pat your head dry with a towel.

Step 7: Apply Aftershave

Apply aftershave to your head to soothe your skin and prevent razor burn. You can also use a moisturizer to keep your skin hydrated.

Tips for Shaving the Back and Sides of Your Head

Here are some tips that will help you achieve the perfect shave:

  • Use shaving cream or gel to lubricate your skin and prevent razor burn.
  • Use a sharp and clean razor to avoid cuts and nicks.
  • Trim your hair before shaving to make it easier.
  • Use short strokes and go against the direction of hair growth.
  • Stretch your skin with your free hand to avoid cuts.
  • Rinse your razor after every stroke to prevent clogging.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about shaving the back and sides of your head:

1. Can I shave my head with a regular razor?

Yes, you can use a regular razor to shave your head. However, make sure it is sharp and clean to avoid cuts and nicks.

2. How often should I shave my head?

It depends on your hair growth and personal preference. Some people shave their heads every day, while others do it once a week.

3. How can I prevent razor burn?

You can prevent razor burn by using shaving cream or gel, using a sharp razor, and rinsing your skin with cold water after shaving.

Hair Care Tips

Shaving your head can leave your skin dry and itchy. Here are some tips that will help you take care of your scalp:

  • Moisturize your scalp regularly to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can irritate your skin.
  • Use a sunscreen to protect your scalp from the sun.
  • Avoid touching your head too often to avoid bacteria buildup.

Shaving the back and sides of your head may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the desired look. Remember to be patient and take your time. Happy shaving!
