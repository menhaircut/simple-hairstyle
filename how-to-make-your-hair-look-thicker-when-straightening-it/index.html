How To Make Your Hair Look Thicker When Straightening It

Written by Christine Jul 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of having thin and lifeless hair? Do you want to add some volume and thickness to your locks? If you answered yes to these questions, then you've come to the right place. In this article, we will show you how to make your hair look thicker when straightening it.

Description

Straightening your hair is a great way to achieve a sleek and polished look. However, if you have fine or thin hair, the process can leave your locks looking even flatter and lifeless. Fortunately, there are a few tips and tricks you can use to make your hair look thicker and fuller when straightening it.

How to

Follow these steps to make your hair look thicker when straightening it:

Step 1: Start with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner

Before you even start straightening your hair, it's important to use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help add texture and body to your locks, making them easier to style.

Step 2: Use a root-lifting spray

After washing and conditioning your hair, apply a root-lifting spray to your roots. This will help lift your hair at the roots, giving the illusion of more volume.

Step 3: Blow-dry your hair upside down

Blow-drying your hair upside down is a great way to add volume and thickness to your locks. Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots as you blow-dry.

Step 4: Use a heat protectant spray

Before straightening your hair, apply a heat protectant spray to protect your locks from heat damage. This will also help your hair hold its style better.

Step 5: Use a high-quality straightener

Invest in a high-quality straightener with adjustable heat settings. This will allow you to customize the heat to your hair type, preventing unnecessary damage.

Step 6: Section your hair

Section your hair into small sections before straightening. This will ensure that you don't miss any sections and will help you achieve a sleek and polished look.

Step 7: Use a comb while straightening

Use a comb while straightening your hair to help guide your strands and prevent tangles. This will also help add volume and thickness to your locks.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help make your hair look thicker when straightening it:

  • Don't overdo it with products. Too much product can weigh down your hair, making it look even thinner.
  • Avoid using a hairbrush after straightening. This can cause your hair to become static and flat.
  • Consider getting a layered haircut. This will help add volume and texture to your locks.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about making your hair look thicker when straightening it:

Q: Can I use a straightener on wet hair?

A: No, you should never use a straightener on wet hair. Wet hair is more susceptible to heat damage.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It's best to limit straightening to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your locks.

Q: Can I use a straightener on colored hair?

A: Yes, you can use a straightener on colored hair. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your locks.

Hair Care

Remember, proper hair care is essential for achieving and maintaining a healthy and voluminous mane. Be sure to use a moisturizing conditioner, avoid over-washing your hair, and protect your locks from heat damage.

With these tips and tricks, you can easily make your hair look thicker when straightening it. Experiment with different products and techniques to find the perfect routine for your locks. Happy styling!
