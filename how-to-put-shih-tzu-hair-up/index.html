How To Put Shih Tzu Hair Up A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Shih Tzus are popular pets for their adorable looks and lovable personalities. However, their long, flowing hair can be a challenge to manage, especially when it comes to putting it up. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps on how to put Shih Tzu hair up, from the basics to more advanced styles.

The Basics

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over some basic tips for putting up Shih Tzu hair:

  • Use a comb or brush to gently detangle the hair before attempting to put it up. This will help prevent discomfort and pain for your pup.
  • Make sure your Shih Tzu is comfortable and relaxed before you begin. If they're anxious or fidgety, it may be more difficult to put their hair up.
  • Have all of your supplies ready before you start, including hair ties, bobby pins, and any styling products you may need.

Step 1: Brushing and Prepping

Start by brushing your Shih Tzu's hair to remove any tangles or knots. Use a comb or brush specifically designed for long hair to avoid pulling or damaging the hair. Once the hair is detangled, you can add any styling products you may need, such as hairspray or gel.

Step 2: Creating a Ponytail

Gather all of your Shih Tzu's hair together at the base of their neck and hold it in one hand. With the other hand, use a hair tie to secure the hair in a ponytail. Make sure the ponytail is tight enough to hold the hair in place, but not so tight that it causes discomfort.

Step 3: Securing the Ponytail

Once you have the ponytail in place, use bobby pins to secure any loose strands or flyaways. You can also use a small elastic band to wrap around the base of the ponytail for extra security. Make sure the pins and band are hidden from view to create a neat and polished look.

Step 4: Creating a Top Knot

To create a top knot, take a small section of hair from the top of the ponytail and twist it into a bun. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place, making sure to hide the pins from view. You can also use a small elastic band to wrap around the bun for extra security.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Once you have the top knot in place, use a brush or comb to smooth out any flyaways or bumps. You can also add any finishing touches, such as a bow or decorative clip, for added flair.

Advanced Styles

If you're looking for more advanced styles, there are plenty of options to explore. Some popular styles for Shih Tzu hair include:

  • French braid
  • Half-up, half-down style
  • Braided crown
  • Ballerina bun

These styles may require more skill and practice, but they can create stunning looks for your Shih Tzu.

Tips for Putting Up Shih Tzu Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when putting up your Shih Tzu's hair:

  • Use high-quality hair ties and bobby pins to avoid breakage or damage to the hair.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for your Shih Tzu's hair type and personality.
  • Don't leave hair ties or pins in for extended periods of time to avoid discomfort or irritation for your pup.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I put up my Shih Tzu's hair if it's short?

While it may be more difficult to put up shorter hair, it's still possible to create a ponytail or other style. Just be sure to use small hair ties and secure the hair gently to avoid discomfort.

How often should I put up my Shih Tzu's hair?

It's best to avoid putting up your Shih Tzu's hair too often, as it can cause discomfort or irritation. Aim to put up their hair only when necessary, such as for grooming or special occasions.

Shih Tzu Hair Care

Caring for your Shih Tzu's hair is essential to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for maintaining your pup's luscious locks:

  • Brush their hair regularly to avoid tangles and mats.
  • Trim their hair every few months to prevent split ends and damage.
  • Use a dog-specific shampoo and conditioner to avoid irritation or dryness.
  • Avoid using human hair products on your Shih Tzu, as they may contain harmful chemicals or irritants.

By following these tips, you can keep your Shih Tzu's hair looking and feeling its best.

Putting up Shih Tzu hair may take some practice and patience, but with these tips and techniques, you can create beautiful styles for your furry friend. Remember to always prioritize their comfort and well-being, and enjoy the bonding experience of grooming and styling your pet.
