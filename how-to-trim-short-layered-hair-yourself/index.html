How To Trim Short Layered Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of paying exorbitant amounts of money to get your short layered hair trimmed every few weeks? Or maybe you simply don't have the time to schedule regular salon appointments. Whatever your reason may be, trimming your own short layered hair is easier than you think. In this guide, we will walk you through the process step-by-step so that you can achieve a fresh, layered haircut from the comfort of your own home.

Description

Short layered hair is a versatile style that can be customized to suit any face shape, hair texture, or personal preference. It involves cutting the hair into multiple layers of varying lengths, which creates volume, movement, and texture. However, these layers need to be maintained regularly in order to prevent the hair from looking unkempt or uneven. By learning how to trim your own short layered hair, you can save time and money, and also have more control over your hairstyle.

How To

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror that you can use to see the back of your head.

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section off your hair. Use the hair clips to divide your hair into four sections: top, sides, and back. This will make it easier to work with each section individually.

Step 3: Begin with the back section. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the scissors to trim the ends, cutting at a slight angle to create a layered effect. Repeat this process for the rest of the back section, working your way up towards the crown of your head.

Step 4: Move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair from one side and hold it between your fingers. Use the scissors to trim the ends, following the same angle as before. Repeat this process for the other side section.

Step 5: Finally, move on to the top section. Take small sections of hair and hold them between your fingers. Use the scissors to trim the ends, cutting at a slightly steeper angle than before. This will create more volume and texture on the top layer.

Step-by-Step

Step 1: Comb through clean, dry hair.

Step 2: Section off hair into four parts: top, sides, and back.

Step 3: Start with the back section and trim ends at a slight angle.

Step 4: Move on to the side sections and trim ends at the same angle.

Step 5: Finish with the top section and trim ends at a steeper angle.

Tips

1. Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean, precise cut.

2. Take your time and work in small sections to avoid making mistakes.

3. If you're not sure how much to cut off, start with a small amount and work your way up.

4. Don't be afraid to experiment with different angles and lengths to achieve your desired look.

FAQ

Q: Can I trim my short layered hair with regular scissors?

A: No, it's important to use hair cutting scissors in order to achieve a clean, precise cut.

Q: How often should I trim my short layered hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

After trimming your short layered hair, it's important to take care of it properly in order to maintain its health and appearance. Here are a few tips:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.

2. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause breakage and split ends.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

4. Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.

By following these tips and regularly trimming your short layered hair, you can ensure that your hair looks and feels its best at all times.
