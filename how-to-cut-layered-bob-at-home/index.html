How To Cut Layered Bob At Home Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your hair at home can be a daunting task, especially if you're going for a layered bob. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy look at home. In this post, we'll guide you through the process of cutting a layered bob, step-by-step. We'll also share some tips and hair care advice to ensure that your new haircut looks great and stays healthy.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting.

Next, comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to section your hair and ensure that your haircut is even.

Sectioning Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Start with one of the back sections. Comb it straight down and then use your fingers to create a horizontal part at the nape of your neck. Clip the top section out of the way.

Cutting Your Hair

Start cutting at the center of your hairline, using your comb as a guide. Cut your hair straight across, making sure to keep your fingers at a slight angle to create layers.

Move to the sides and repeat the process, working your way up to the top section. Make sure to cut each section to the same length.

Texturizing Your Hair

Texturizing your hair will give it a more natural, layered look. To do this, hold your hair between two fingers and snip into the ends of your hair. Don't cut too much at once, and make sure to vary the lengths of your cuts for a more natural look.

Finishing Your Haircut

Once you've finished cutting and texturizing your hair, unclip each section and check for any uneven spots. Make any necessary adjustments.

Style your hair as desired, using a blow dryer or straightener if needed. And voila! You've just cut a layered bob at home.

Tips for Cutting a Layered Bob at Home

  • Start with small cuts, and work your way up. It's easier to take off more hair than to add it back.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your hair can be tiring, especially if you're doing it for the first time.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven cuts.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your hair if it's curly. Just make sure to cut it when it's dry, and use your fingers to create a natural-looking shape.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my hair with kitchen scissors?

No, it's not recommended to cut your hair with kitchen scissors. Make sure to use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair.

Hair Care Tips for Your Layered Bob

  • Use a heat protectant spray if you're using heat styling tools.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut a layered bob at home. Remember to take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
