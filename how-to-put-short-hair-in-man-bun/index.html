How To Put Short Hair In Man Bun A Step By Step Guide

Are you a man with short hair who wants to rock the man bun look? You're not alone! The man bun has become a popular hairstyle in recent years, and it's not just for those with long locks. With a little patience and the right technique, you can put your short hair in a man bun. In this article, we'll guide you through the process.

How to Put Short Hair in Man Bun

Before we get into the step-by-step guide, let's talk about what you'll need. You'll need a comb, hair ties, and some hair product to help hold your hair in place. Now, let's get started.

Step 1: Comb Your Hair

Make sure your hair is free of tangles by combing it out. This will make it easier to style.

Step 2: Gather Your Hair

Gather your hair at the top of your head. Use your comb to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step 3: Twist Your Hair

Twist your hair until it starts to coil into a bun shape. Use your hand to hold the bun in place.

Step 4: Secure with a Hair Tie

Take a hair tie and wrap it around the bun to secure it in place. Make sure it's tight enough to hold your hair in place, but not too tight that it causes discomfort.

Step 5: Tuck in Loose Ends

If you have any loose ends sticking out, tuck them in with bobby pins. This will help keep your bun looking neat and tidy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Let's break down the steps even further with a more detailed guide.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to style and hold the bun in place.

Step 2: Apply Hair Product

Apply a small amount of hair product, like pomade or hair clay, to your hair. This will help hold the bun in place and add texture to your hair.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Use a comb to comb your hair back and away from your face. Smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step 4: Gather Your Hair

Gather your hair at the top of your head. Use your comb to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step 5: Twist Your Hair

Twist your hair until it starts to coil into a bun shape. Use your hand to hold the bun in place.

Step 6: Secure with a Hair Tie

Take a hair tie and wrap it around the bun to secure it in place. Make sure it's tight enough to hold your hair in place, but not too tight that it causes discomfort.

Step 7: Tuck in Loose Ends

If you have any loose ends sticking out, tuck them in with bobby pins. This will help keep your bun looking neat and tidy.

Tips for Styling a Man Bun with Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you style your man bun with short hair:

  • Use hair product to add texture and hold to your hair.
  • Don't make your bun too tight, as this can cause discomfort and damage to your hair.
  • Use bobby pins to keep loose ends in place.
  • Experiment with different styles, such as a half-up, half-down look.

FAQs

Can I put my short hair in a man bun?

Yes, you can! With the right technique and a little patience, you can rock the man bun look with short hair.

What hair product should I use?

You can use a variety of hair products, such as pomade or hair clay, to add texture and hold to your hair.

How do I keep my man bun looking neat and tidy?

Use bobby pins to tuck in any loose ends and keep your bun looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care for Man Buns

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair when wearing a man bun:

  • Avoid pulling your hair too tight, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Take breaks from wearing a man bun to give your hair a rest.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently put your short hair in a man bun and rock the look with style.
