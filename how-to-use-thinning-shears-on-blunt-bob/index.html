How To Use Thinning Shears On Blunt Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to add some texture to your blunt bob hairstyle? Thinning shears might be the answer you're looking for! Thinning shears are specially designed scissors that can remove bulk and create a softer look by cutting some of the hair strands shorter than others. In this article, we'll show you how to use thinning shears on your blunt bob to achieve a more textured and layered look.

Description

A blunt bob is a classic hairstyle that features a straight cut at the ends, with no layers or texture. While this look can be chic and sophisticated, it can also be a bit too severe for some people's tastes. Thinning shears can help you achieve a softer, more relaxed version of the blunt bob, with less bulk and more movement.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Blunt Bob

Here's a step-by-step guide to using thinning shears on your blunt bob:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important to work with clean, dry hair so that you can see the true texture of your hair and how the thinning shears are affecting it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use hair clips to section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. This will make it easier to work on each section separately.

Step 3: Start at the Back

Begin with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers, about an inch away from the end.

Step 4: Cut with the Thinning Shears

Use the thinning shears to cut into the section of hair, starting at the point where you're holding it. Make small, quick cuts, moving the shears up and down the section of hair to create a textured, layered effect.

Step 5: Repeat on All Sections

Continue to work your way through all four sections of your hair, using the thinning shears to create texture and movement. Remember to work in small sections and make quick, light cuts to avoid removing too much hair at once.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Blunt Bob

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when using thinning shears on your blunt bob:

  • Work with dry hair, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut and style.
  • Start with small sections of hair, and work your way up to larger sections as you become more comfortable with the technique.
  • Use the thinning shears sparingly to avoid removing too much hair at once.
  • Be gentle with the shears, and avoid pulling or tugging on the hair.
  • Consider using a texturizing or volumizing product to enhance the effect of the thinning shears.

FAQs

What are thinning shears?

Thinning shears are scissors that are designed to remove bulk and create texture in the hair. They have teeth that are designed to cut some strands of hair shorter than others, creating a layered, textured effect.

Can I use thinning shears on any hairstyle?

Thinning shears can be used on a variety of hairstyles, but they work best on styles with longer hair. Shorter styles may not have enough hair to work with, and the thinning shears may create a choppy or uneven effect.

Do I need to be a professional hairstylist to use thinning shears?

No, you don't need to be a professional hairstylist to use thinning shears. However, it's important to be gentle and cautious when using them, as they can remove a lot of hair quickly if used improperly.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Shears

After using thinning shears on your hair, it's important to properly care for your hair to maintain its texture and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heated styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a texturizing or volumizing product to enhance the effect of the thinning shears.

Using thinning shears on your blunt bob can be a great way to add texture and movement to your hair. By following these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a soft, relaxed look that's perfect for any occasion.
