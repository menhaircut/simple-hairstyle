How to Cut Curly Hair for Beginners: Step-by-Step Guide and Tips

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be challenging to find a stylist who understands how to cut and style it properly. But with the right tools and techniques, cutting curly hair can be easy and fun. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut curly hair for beginners, from the tools you'll need to the step-by-step process.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Preparing the Hair
  3. Sectioning the Hair
  4. Cutting the Hair
  5. Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  6. FAQs
  7. Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting curly hair, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools. Here are the essential tools you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Spray bottle
  • Clips to section the hair

Once you have all of these tools, you're ready to get started.

Preparing the Hair

Before you start cutting, you'll need to prepare the hair. Here's what you should do:

  1. Wash and condition the hair, making sure to use a curly hair-friendly product.
  2. Detangle the hair using a wide-tooth comb.
  3. Blow-dry the hair using a diffuser attachment, or let it air dry.

Once the hair is dry, you're ready to start sectioning.

Sectioning the Hair

Sectioning the hair is essential when cutting curly hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by dividing the hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back.
  2. Clip each section of hair up and out of the way, except for the section you'll be cutting first.

Once you've sectioned the hair, you're ready to start cutting.

Cutting the Hair

Here's the step-by-step process for cutting curly hair:

  1. Start by cutting the hair in small sections, about 1/4 inch to 1/2 inch at a time.
  2. Hold the hair in your fingers and cut it at an angle, so the ends are slightly longer than the rest of the hair.
  3. Continue cutting small sections of hair, making sure to cut at an angle.
  4. Once you've finished cutting one section, unclip the next section and repeat the process.

Remember to cut slowly and carefully, making small cuts at a time. It's better to cut too little than too much, as you can always go back and cut more if necessary.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Make sure the hair is completely dry before cutting.
  • Don't use a razor to cut curly hair, as this can cause frizz and damage.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the head as you cut.
  • Don't pull the hair too tightly as you cut, as this can cause unevenness.
  • Consider cutting the hair while it's styled in its natural state, so you can see how it will look.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting curly hair:

Can you cut curly hair when it's wet or dry?

You can cut curly hair either wet or dry, but it's generally easier to cut it dry so you can see how the curls will fall.

How often should you cut curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Do you need special scissors to cut curly hair?

You don't necessarily need special scissors to cut curly hair, but it's important to use sharp scissors to prevent damage.

Hair Care After Cutting

After you've finished cutting curly hair, it's essential to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.
  • Avoid brushing the hair, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying to enhance the curls.
  • Consider using a microfiber towel to dry the hair, as this can reduce frizz.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut curly hair like a pro. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun with it!
