What Is Tapering In Hairdressing

Written by Luffy Nov 02, 2023 · 4 min read

Tapering is a popular hairdressing technique that involves gradually decreasing the length of hair from the top to the bottom. Essentially, it creates a fade or blended effect that makes the hair look neat and polished. Tapering is often used on the sides and back of the head, but can also be done on the top for a more layered look. In this blog post, we’ll go over everything you need to know about tapering in hairdressing.

How to Taper Hair

To taper hair, you’ll need a set of clippers with guard attachments, scissors, and a comb. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Determine the Length

Before you start cutting, decide on the length you want to taper the hair to. This will depend on the client’s preferences and the style they want to achieve. Use the clippers with the appropriate guard attachment to start cutting the hair at the desired length.

Step 2: Switch Guards

As you move down the head, gradually switch to smaller guard attachments to decrease the length of the hair. You can also use scissors to blend the different lengths together and create a more natural look.

Step 3: Blend the Edges

Once you’ve reached the desired length, it’s important to blend the edges of the taper to create a seamless transition between the different lengths. Use the scissors to carefully blend the hair and remove any harsh lines.

Step 4: Finish with Detailing

Finally, use the clippers and scissors to clean up any stray hairs and add any finishing touches to the style. This might include trimming the sideburns, shaping the hairline, or adding texture to the hair.

Tips for Tapering Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when tapering hair:

  • Start with a longer guard attachment than you think you need, as you can always switch to a shorter one later.
  • Use a comb to help guide the clippers and create a more even cut.
  • Be patient and take your time – rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Blend the different lengths of hair together thoroughly to avoid any harsh lines or choppy transitions.
  • Pay attention to the client’s preferences and adjust your technique accordingly.

FAQs About Tapering Hair

What’s the difference between a taper and a fade?

While tapering and fading are similar techniques, they have some key differences. A taper refers to gradually decreasing the length of hair from the top to the bottom, while a fade involves cutting the hair extremely short at the bottom and blending it into longer hair at the top.

What hair types work best for tapering?

Tapering can work on all different hair types, but it’s especially effective on thicker hair. It can also help add shape and dimension to fine or thin hair.

Can I taper my own hair at home?

While it’s possible to taper your own hair at home, it can be difficult to achieve a professional-looking result without the proper tools and experience. We recommend seeing a trained hairdresser for the best results.

How to Care for Tapered Hair

To keep your tapered hair looking fresh and polished, here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add moisture and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Visit your hairdresser regularly for touch-ups and maintenance.

Final Thoughts

Tapering is a versatile and stylish hairdressing technique that can work for a variety of different hair types and styles. Whether you’re looking to add shape and dimension to your hair or simply want a polished, clean-cut look, tapering is a great option to consider. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking result that will last for weeks.
