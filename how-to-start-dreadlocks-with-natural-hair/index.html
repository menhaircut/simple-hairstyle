How To Start Dreadlocks With Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you're interested in starting dreadlocks with your natural hair, you've come to the right place. In this guide, we'll take you through the process step-by-step and provide you with some tips and tricks to help you along the way. So, let's get started!

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that involves the hair becoming matted and forming into cylindrical locks. While dreadlocks were originally associated with Rastafarian culture, they are now worn by people of all races and backgrounds. Starting dreadlocks with natural hair can be a bit of a challenge, but with the right techniques and tools, anyone can do it.

How to Start Dreadlocks with Natural Hair

Starting dreadlocks with natural hair involves a process of separating and matting the hair into locks. While this process can be done on any hair type, it is important to note that some hair types may take longer to lock than others. Here are the basic steps to starting dreadlocks with natural hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or residue.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections using a rat-tail comb.
  3. Apply a locking gel or wax to each section of hair, twisting and rolling each section between your palms to encourage locking.
  4. Continue twisting and rolling each section of hair until the entire head is done.
  5. Use a crochet hook or interlocking tool to tighten and neaten each lock, if desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you along the way as you start your dreadlocks:

  • Be patient. Dreadlocks take time to form and mature, so don't expect instant results.
  • Use a natural locking gel or wax to avoid buildup and damage to your hair.
  • Cover your hair at night to prevent frizz and tangling.
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz and damage to your locks.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with natural hair:

How long does it take to start dreadlocks?

The time it takes to start dreadlocks varies depending on the hair type and texture, but it can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months.

Can I start dreadlocks on short hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks on short hair. However, it may take longer for the hair to lock and form into mature dreadlocks.

Do I need to use a locking gel or wax?

No, you don't need to use a locking gel or wax to start dreadlocks. However, using a natural locking gel or wax can help encourage locking and prevent frizz and damage to your hair.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Caring for your dreadlocks is important to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or conditioners that can cause buildup and prevent locking.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and tangling.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your dreadlocks, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Starting dreadlocks with natural hair can be a fun and rewarding process. With the right techniques and care, you can have beautiful and healthy dreadlocks in no time.
