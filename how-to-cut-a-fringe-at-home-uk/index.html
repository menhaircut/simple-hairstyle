How To Cut A Fringe At Home Uk A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 26, 2023 · 5 min read
If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without leaving the comfort of your own home, cutting a fringe might be just what you need. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and personalised look that suits your face shape and hair type. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut a fringe at home in the UK, as well as some useful tips and answers to common questions.

Description

A fringe, also known as bangs, is a section of hair that falls over the forehead. It can be cut in various lengths and styles, from a wispy side-swept fringe to a blunt full fringe. Cutting a fringe at home can save you money and time, as well as allow you to experiment with different looks. However, it's important to be cautious and patient when handling scissors near your face, and to follow proper hygiene and maintenance practices for your hair.

How to

Here are the basic steps to follow when cutting a fringe at home in the UK: 1. Gather your tools: a pair of sharp hairdressing scissors, a comb, clips, a mirror, and optionally, a spray bottle and a towel. 2. Wash and dry your hair: this will ensure that your hair is clean, smooth, and free of tangles. If you have curly or wavy hair, you might want to blow-dry it straight or leave it slightly damp to avoid shrinkage. 3. Decide on the length and style of your fringe: take into account your face shape, hair texture, and personal preferences. You can use a ruler or a piece of paper to measure the desired length of your fringe, and mark it with clips or a washable pen. 4. Section off your hair: separate the section of hair that you want to cut into a fringe, by combing it straight down from the crown of your head to the tip of your nose. You can clip the rest of your hair away to avoid cutting it by mistake. 5. Cut your fringe: hold the section of hair between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers down to the desired length. Then, use the scissors to cut across the hair in a straight line, starting from the middle and working towards the sides. Avoid cutting too much hair at once, and check the length and symmetry regularly by using two mirrors or asking for a second opinion. 6. Trim the sides and corners: if you want a softer or angled fringe, you can use the scissors to trim the sides and corners of your fringe. This will create a more natural and flattering effect, and blend the fringe with the rest of your hair. However, be careful not to overdo it, as this can result in uneven or choppy layers. 7. Style your fringe: comb or brush your fringe into the desired shape and position, and use a hair spray or serum to hold it in place. You can also use a straightener or a curler to add texture or volume to your fringe, but be gentle and avoid overheating your hair.

Step by Step

1. Wash and dry your hair. 2. Decide on the length and style of your fringe. 3. Section off your hair. 4. Cut your fringe. 5. Trim the sides and corners. 6. Style your fringe.

Tips

- Start with a longer length and gradually cut more hair, rather than cutting too much at once. - Use a comb to hold your hair taut and prevent uneven cutting. - Cut your fringe when your hair is dry, as wet hair tends to shrink and look shorter. - If you have thick or coarse hair, consider thinning it out with thinning shears or a razor to avoid a bulky or heavy fringe. - Take breaks and step back from the mirror to avoid eye strain and fatigue. - If you're not confident with cutting a fringe yourself, ask a friend or a professional hairdresser for help.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut a fringe with regular scissors? A: It's not recommended, as regular scissors are usually dull and can damage your hair or create uneven lines. Invest in a pair of hairdressing scissors that are sharp, precise, and comfortable to hold. Q: How often should I trim my fringe? A: It depends on your hair growth and desired length, but as a rule of thumb, you should trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends. Q: What if I make a mistake while cutting my fringe? A: Don't panic or try to fix it immediately. Take a deep breath, step back, and assess the situation. You can either leave it as it is and wait for it to grow out, or try to blend it with the rest of your hair by cutting some adjacent strands. If it's a major mistake or you're unhappy with the result, seek professional help.

Hair Care

Cutting a fringe can expose your forehead and hairline to the sun, pollution, and sweat. To keep your hair and skin healthy and protected, follow these tips: - Apply sunscreen or wear a hat when going outside, especially during peak hours. - Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that suit your hair type and needs. - Avoid using too much heat or styling products, as they can damage your hair and scalp. - Brush or comb your hair gently and avoid pulling or tugging on your fringe. - Trim your split ends regularly and deep-condition your hair once a week. - Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to promote hair growth and strength.

Conclusion

Cutting a fringe at home in the UK can be a fun and rewarding experience, as long as you follow the proper steps and precautions. Remember to start small, be patient, and embrace imperfection. Your fringe is a reflection of your personality and style, and can enhance your overall look and confidence. Happy cutting!
