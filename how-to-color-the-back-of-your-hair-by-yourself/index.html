How to Color the Back of Your Hair By Yourself - Tips and Tricks

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon every time you need to color your hair? Do you want to learn how to color the back of your hair by yourself? Look no further! In this article, we will give you step-by-step instructions on how to color the back of your hair without any help.

How to Color the Back of Your Hair By Yourself

Coloring the back of your hair can be a daunting task, especially if you don't have any experience. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve professional-looking results from the comfort of your own home. Here is how you can color the back of your hair by yourself:

Step 1 - Choose Your Hair Color

Before you start coloring your hair, you need to choose the right hair color. Make sure to choose a color that complements your skin tone and matches the rest of your hair. You can find a wide variety of hair colors at your local drugstore or beauty supply store.

Step 2 - Gather Your Tools

Once you have your hair color, you need to gather all the tools you will need for the job. This includes gloves, a mixing bowl, a hairbrush, a hair clip, and a timer. You may also want to use a hair dye applicator brush to make the process easier.

Step 3 - Prepare Your Hair

Before you start coloring your hair, you need to prepare it by washing it thoroughly with shampoo and conditioner. This will help remove any dirt or oil that may interfere with the coloring process. Make sure to towel-dry your hair before proceeding.

Step 4 - Mix Your Hair Color

Follow the instructions on your hair color package to mix the color. Pour the color into the mixing bowl and add the developer. Stir the mixture with the applicator brush until it is smooth and free of lumps.

Step 5 - Apply the Color

Using the applicator brush, apply the hair color to the back of your hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends. Use the hairbrush to distribute the color evenly and make sure all strands are covered.

Step 6 - Set the Timer

Set the timer according to the instructions on your hair color package. This will ensure that the color develops properly and gives you the desired results.

Step 7 - Rinse Your Hair

Once the timer goes off, rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water. Make sure to remove all the hair color from your hair. You can then apply the conditioner that came with your hair color package to help restore moisture to your hair.

Step 8 - Dry Your Hair

After rinsing your hair, towel-dry it and then blow-dry it on low heat. This will help set the color and give you a finished look.

Tips for Coloring the Back of Your Hair By Yourself

Here are some tips that can help make the process of coloring the back of your hair by yourself easier:

  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go.
  • Wear old clothes or a towel to protect your clothing from hair color stains.
  • Apply petroleum jelly or a barrier cream around your hairline to prevent the color from staining your skin.
  • Always follow the instructions on your hair color package.

FAQ About Coloring the Back of Your Hair By Yourself

Here are some frequently asked questions about coloring the back of your hair by yourself:

Do I need to bleach my hair before coloring it?

If you are going for a lighter hair color, you may need to bleach your hair first. However, if you are going for a darker color or a color that is similar to your natural hair color, you can skip the bleaching step.

How long should I leave the color on my hair?

The amount of time you should leave the color on your hair depends on the instructions on your hair color package. Follow the instructions carefully to avoid over-processing your hair.

Can I color my hair if it is damaged?

If your hair is severely damaged, it is best to avoid coloring it until it has been repaired. Coloring damaged hair can cause further damage and make your hair more prone to breakage.

Hair Care After Coloring Your Hair

After coloring your hair, it is important to take good care of it to maintain the color and keep your hair healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping the color from your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to help restore moisture.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools on a daily basis.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or applying a leave-in conditioner.

Now that you know how to color the back of your hair by yourself, you can save money and achieve professional-looking results at home. Just remember to follow the instructions carefully and take good care of your hair afterwards.
