How To Make Thin Hair Look Good In Braids

Written by Luffy Jul 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of having thin hair that just won't cooperate when you try to style it? Do you love the look of braids but feel like they just don't work with your hair type? Fear not! With a few simple tips and tricks, you can make thin hair look fabulous in braids.

Description

Thin hair can be tricky to style, but that doesn't mean you have to give up on braids. With the right techniques, you can create beautiful braids that add volume and texture to your hair. Whether you're looking for a simple braid or a more intricate style, there are plenty of options that will work with your hair type.

How to

Here's how to make thin hair look good in braids:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair before braiding. This will help to remove any excess oil or product buildup, which can weigh down your hair and make it harder to braid. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture to your hair.

2. Use a texturizing spray

Before you start braiding, spray a texturizing spray or dry shampoo onto your roots. This will give your hair some extra grip and help to create more volume and texture.

3. Choose the right braid

Not all braids are created equal when it comes to thin hair. Some styles, like a French braid, can be too tight and flat, while others, like a fishtail braid, can be too loose and messy. Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for your hair.

4. Add extensions

If you want to add more volume to your braids, consider using extensions. Clip-in extensions can be a great way to add length and fullness to your hair, making it easier to braid and style.

5. Use hair elastics strategically

When you're braiding thin hair, it's important to use hair elastics strategically. Too many elastics can weigh down your hair and make it look flat, while too few can cause your braid to unravel. Use small, clear elastics to secure your braid, and try to hide them as best you can.

6. Pull out the braid

Once you've finished braiding your hair, gently pull on the sides of the braid to loosen it up. This will create a more relaxed, boho look and add some volume to your hair.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to braiding thin hair:

1. Prep your hair

Wash and dry your hair, then apply a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to your roots.

2. Choose your braid

Select the type of braid you want to create.

3. Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections, depending on the type of braid you're creating.

4. Start braiding

Begin braiding your hair, using small, clear elastics to secure each section.

5. Pull out the braid

Once you've finished braiding, gently pull on the sides of the braid to loosen it up.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for braiding thin hair:

  • Experiment with different braids to find the one that works best for your hair type.
  • Add extensions to create more volume and length.
  • Use small, clear elastics to secure your braid.
  • Pull on the sides of the braid to create a more relaxed, boho look.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture to your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about braiding thin hair:

What type of braid is best for thin hair?

There isn't one specific type of braid that is best for thin hair. Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for your hair type.

How do I add volume to my braids?

You can add volume to your braids by using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo, and by gently pulling on the sides of the braid once you've finished braiding.

Can I use extensions with thin hair?

Yes, clip-in extensions can be a great way to add length and fullness to thin hair.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.

With these tips and tricks, you can make thin hair look fabulous in braids. Whether you're going for a simple braid or a more intricate style, there are plenty of options that will work with your hair type. So go ahead and experiment with different styles, and have fun creating beautiful braids!
