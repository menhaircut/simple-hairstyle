How To Cut A Guy s Curly Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting curly hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not familiar with the proper techniques. But with the right tools and a little know-how, you can give your guy a great haircut at home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut a guy's curly hair with scissors, from start to finish.

Table of Contents

  1. Materials You'll Need
  2. Preparation
  3. Cutting Techniques
  4. Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care After the Cut

Materials You'll Need

To cut a guy's curly hair with scissors, you'll need the following:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair comb or brush
  • Hair clips or rubber bands
  • Water spray bottle
  • Towel or cape
  • Styling products (optional)

Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure your guy's hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be difficult to cut and style, so it's best to work with dry hair. If his hair is too long, you may want to trim it with clippers before you start cutting with scissors. Use a comb or brush to detangle his hair, and section it off using hair clips or rubber bands.

Cutting Techniques

Begin by cutting the sides and back of his hair. Use a comb or brush to lift a section of hair, and cut it to the desired length. Work your way around his head, cutting each section to the same length. When you're finished with the sides and back, move on to the top of his head.

For the top of his head, use your fingers to lift a section of hair, and cut it to the desired length. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries. Work your way around his head, cutting each section to the same length.

If your guy has bangs, use scissors to trim them to the desired length. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut it straight across. For a softer look, you can also point cut the ends.

Layering Curly Hair

If your guy has thick or curly hair, you may want to add layers to give it more shape and texture. To do this, take a section of hair and hold it straight up from the head. Cut the ends of the hair at an angle, moving the scissors upwards as you cut. Continue layering the hair in this way, working your way around the head.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid pulling or damaging the hair.
  • Work with dry hair to avoid uneven cuts.
  • Start with a longer length, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.
  • Cut in small sections to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair if needed.
  • Point cut the ends for a softer, more natural look.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and styles.

FAQs

How often should I cut curly hair?

Curly hair tends to grow more slowly than straight hair, so you may be able to go longer between haircuts. It's generally recommended to get a haircut every 3-4 months to maintain healthy hair.

Can I cut my own curly hair?

Cutting your own curly hair can be challenging, as it's difficult to see and reach all the angles. It's best to have someone else cut your hair, or visit a professional stylist.

Hair Care After the Cut

After the cut, you can style your guy's hair using your favorite products. If he has curly hair, you may want to apply a curl-defining cream or gel to enhance his natural curls. Remember to keep his hair moisturized, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. With the right care, his new haircut will look great for weeks to come!
