How To Cut Medium Length Men s Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of going to the barber every few weeks for a trim? Learning how to cut medium length men's hair with clippers can save you time and money. With a little practice and the right tools, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step, and provide tips and tricks for a successful cut.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but it's not as difficult as it may seem. With a little patience and the right approach, you can achieve a clean, precise cut every time. Whether you're looking for a simple trim or a more complex style, this guide will show you how to achieve the look you want.

How to Cut Medium Length Men's Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, you'll need to gather a few essential tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. You may also want to invest in a hair cape or towel to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Step 2: Choose Your Guard Length

Clipper guards come in different sizes, which correspond to different lengths of hair. Decide on the length you want to achieve, and choose the corresponding guard. For a medium-length cut, a guard size of 3-4 is usually appropriate.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition your hair as usual, and towel dry thoroughly. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you begin cutting.

Step 4: Trim the Sides and Back

Start by trimming the sides and back of your head. Use the clippers to trim upward from the nape of your neck, using the guard to achieve your desired length. Work your way up the sides and back of your head, using the comb to guide the clippers and ensure an even cut.

Step 5: Trim the Top

Once you've trimmed the sides and back, move on to the top of your head. Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers may have missed. Work in small sections, using the comb to lift the hair and the scissors to trim the ends.

Step 6: Blend the Sides and Top

Use the clippers to blend the sides and top of your hair. Begin at the top of your head, and use the clippers to trim downward toward the sides. Use a comb to guide the clippers and ensure an even cut.

Step 7: Clean Up the Neckline

Use the clippers to trim any stray hairs on the back of your neck. Start at the center and work your way outward, using the comb to guide the clippers and ensure a clean, straight line.

Step 8: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, take a close look in the mirror to make sure everything is even and well-blended. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs that may have been missed.

Tips for a Successful Cut

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a comb to guide the clippers and ensure an even cut.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Invest in quality tools to ensure a clean, precise cut.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

Yes, with a little practice and the right tools, anyone can learn how to cut their own hair with clippers.

What size guard should I use for a medium-length cut?

A guard size of 3-4 is usually appropriate for a medium-length cut.

Do I need to wash my hair before cutting it?

Yes, it's important to start with clean, dry hair for an even, precise cut.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, be sure to wash away any loose hairs and style as desired. Regular trims are important to maintain the overall health and appearance of your hair. Consider investing in quality hair care products to keep your hair looking and feeling its best.
