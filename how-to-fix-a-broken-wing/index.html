How to Fix a Broken Wing - Step by Step Guide | Hair Care Tips

Do you have a broken wing that needs fixing? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll walk you through the steps on how to fix a broken wing. Whether you're dealing with a minor or major break, we've got tips and tricks to help you get your wing back in shape.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix a Broken Wing
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Fixing a Broken Wing
  • FAQs

How to Fix a Broken Wing

Before we dive into the step by step guide, let's go over some basic materials you'll need to fix a broken wing. You will need:

  • Small hair elastics
  • Bobby pins
  • Hair glue or hair extensions tape
  • Scissors
  • Hair spray

Step by Step Guide

Now that you have your materials, let's get started on fixing your broken wing:

  1. Brush your hair and smooth out the broken wing.
  2. Take a small hair elastic and tie it at the base of the broken wing to hold it in place.
  3. Take a small section of hair below the broken wing and twist it tightly.
  4. Place the twisted section of hair over the broken wing and secure it with bobby pins.
  5. Take another small section of hair below the first twisted section and repeat the twisting and securing process.
  6. Continue to add twisted sections of hair until you reach the end of the broken wing.
  7. Apply a small amount of hair glue or hair extensions tape to the broken wing and press it firmly against your head.
  8. Use hair spray to set the style and keep the wing in place.

Tips for Fixing a Broken Wing

  • Don't pull too hard on your hair while fixing the broken wing as it may cause further damage.
  • If you don't have hair glue or hair extensions tape, you can use a small amount of clear nail polish to hold the broken wing in place.
  • If the break is severe, consider getting professional help to avoid further damage.

FAQs

Q: How long will it take to fix a broken wing?
A: The time it takes to fix a broken wing depends on the severity of the break. Minor breaks can be fixed in a few minutes, while major breaks may take longer.

Q: Can I still wear my hair down after fixing a broken wing?
A: Yes, you can still wear your hair down after fixing a broken wing. Just make sure to use hair spray to keep it in place.

Hair Care After Fixing a Broken Wing

After fixing a broken wing, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools on the repaired wing for at least a week.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that may put stress on the repaired wing.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to fix a broken wing, you can confidently take care of your hair and keep it looking its best. Remember to take care of your hair to prevent future damage and maintain healthy locks.
