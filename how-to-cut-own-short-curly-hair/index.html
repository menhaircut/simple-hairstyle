How To Cut Your Own Short Curly Hair In 2023 A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon, or just want to try something new, cutting your own short curly hair may be the way to go. With the right tools, techniques, and a little bit of patience, you can achieve a stylish and flattering look from the comfort of your own home. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to get started.

Description

Cutting your own short curly hair can be a daunting task, especially if you've never done it before. However, with the right preparation and approach, it can also be a fun and rewarding experience. This guide will take you through the process step by step, from choosing the right tools and products to executing the perfect cut.

How To

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, you'll need to make sure you have all the necessary supplies. This includes a good pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, clips, and a mirror. You may also want to invest in a spray bottle filled with water, as well as any products you typically use on your hair (such as gel or mousse).

Step 2: Choose Your Style

Before you start cutting, you'll need to decide on the style you want to achieve. Look for inspiration online or in magazines, and consider your hair type and face shape when making your choice. If you're not sure what will work best for you, start with a simple trim and go from there.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair before you begin cutting. This will give you a better idea of how your hair naturally falls, and make it easier to create an even cut.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use your comb to create sections. Start by dividing your hair into two sections (left and right), and then create smaller sections within each side. Use your clips to secure each section in place.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the ends of your hair in small sections. Hold each section between your fingers, and cut straight across using your hair cutting scissors. Work your way around your head, cutting each section to the desired length.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

Once you've finished cutting, it's important to check your progress in the mirror. Make sure your hair is even and that you're happy with the overall shape. If you notice any areas that need touching up, go back and make any necessary adjustments.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your cut, it's time to style your hair. Use any products you like, and experiment with different techniques to find a look you love.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair, especially if you're new to cutting your own hair.
  • Use your mirror to check your progress from different angles.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's easier to cut more off later than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Take breaks if you need to, and don't rush the process.
  • If you're having trouble with a particular area, ask a friend or family member for help.

FAQ

Can I cut my own curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. However, it's important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid damaging your hair.

What kind of scissors should I use to cut my hair?

You should use hair cutting scissors, which are designed specifically for cutting hair. Regular scissors can damage your hair and make it more difficult to achieve a clean, even cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

This will depend on your hair type, but most people with short curly hair should aim to cut their hair every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

Cutting your own hair is just the first step in maintaining healthy, beautiful locks. To keep your hair looking its best, make sure to wash and condition it regularly, and use products that are designed for your hair type. Additionally, avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make it more difficult to manage.
