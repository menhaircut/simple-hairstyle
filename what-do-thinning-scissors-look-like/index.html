What Do Thinning Scissors Look Like A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning scissors are an essential tool for hairstylists and barbers. They help to create texture and volume in the hair by removing some of the bulk. But what do thinning scissors actually look like? In this article, we will provide a complete guide to the appearance of thinning scissors.

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of hair cutting tool that have special blades designed to cut some of the hair while leaving some behind. They typically have a serrated edge, which helps to create a natural look by blending the cut hair with the uncut hair. Thinning scissors are also known as blending scissors, texturizing shears, or chunking shears.

Thinning scissors come in various sizes, with some having longer blades than others. The size of the blades will depend on the type of hair being cut and the desired result. Thinning scissors can be used on both wet and dry hair, although they are typically used on dry hair for better accuracy.

How To Use Thinning Scissors

Using thinning scissors requires some skill and practice. Here are some basic steps to follow:

  1. Wash and dry the hair.
  2. Section the hair into small portions using clips.
  3. Hold the thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair.
  4. Move the scissors up and down to create texture and volume.
  5. Repeat the process on all sections of the hair.

It is important to use thinning scissors sparingly to avoid over-thinning the hair. Over-thinning can make the hair look stringy and damaged.

Step By Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to using thinning scissors:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Wash and dry the hair, then comb it out to remove any tangles. Section the hair into small portions using clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 2: Identify the Areas to Thin

Identify the areas of the hair that you want to thin out. This might be the ends of the hair or the crown of the head.

Step 3: Hold the Scissors Correctly

Hold the thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair. Use your dominant hand to hold the scissors and your other hand to hold the hair steady.

Step 4: Cut Into the Hair

Cut into the hair with the thinning scissors, moving them up and down to create texture and volume. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to over-thinning.

Step 5: Repeat the Process

Repeat the process on all sections of the hair that you want to thin out. Take your time and be patient. Thinning scissors require a bit of skill and practice to use effectively.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors:

  • Use thinning scissors sparingly to avoid over-thinning.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a comb to help guide the scissors through the hair.
  • Take breaks to prevent hand fatigue and avoid making mistakes.

FAQ

What is the difference between thinning scissors and regular scissors?

Thinning scissors have special blades designed to cut some of the hair while leaving some behind. This creates texture and volume, whereas regular scissors simply cut the hair straight across.

Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

Thinning scissors can be used on all hair types, but they are particularly effective on thick, coarse hair.

Do thinning scissors damage the hair?

Thinning scissors can damage the hair if they are used too often or too aggressively. It is important to use them sparingly and with care.

Hair Care

Using thinning scissors is just one part of a good hair care routine. To keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful, it is important to:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using thinning scissors correctly, you can achieve a beautiful, textured look for your hair.
