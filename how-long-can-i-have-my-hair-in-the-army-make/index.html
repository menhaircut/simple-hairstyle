Are you planning to join the army and wondering how long you can have your hair? Hair regulations are an essential part of the military, and it's crucial to understand them before enlisting. In this article, we'll discuss how long you can have your hair in the army make, along with other related information.

Hair Regulations in the Army

The army has strict regulations when it comes to personal appearance, including hair. These regulations are put in place to ensure uniformity and discipline among the personnel. The army's hair regulations apply to both men and women and are enforced during training, on duty, and off duty.

As per the army regulations, hair must be neat, clean, and well-groomed at all times. It should not interfere with the proper wearing of military headgear or protective masks. The hair should be conservative and not faddish, eccentric, or trendy.

How Long Can You Have Your Hair in the Army?

The length of hair allowed in the army depends on the gender and hair type of the personnel. For men, the hair should be tapered in the back and on the sides, with no length exceeding 1/8 inch. The hair should also not touch the ears or the collar of the uniform.

For women, the hair should not extend below the bottom edge of the collar at the back of the uniform. The hair should also be styled in such a way that it does not interfere with the proper wearing of a military headgear or protective mask. If the hair is longer than the authorized length, it should be pinned up or braided neatly.

Exceptions to the Hair Length Regulations

The army allows for some exceptions to the hair length regulations, depending on the situation. For example, personnel who require medical treatment that affects their hair may be granted an exception. Pregnant personnel may also be granted an exception if the hair regulations pose a risk to their health or the health of their unborn child.

Hair Care Tips for Army Personnel

Keeping your hair neat and well-groomed is essential for army personnel. Here are some hair care tips that can help:

  • Wash your hair regularly and keep it clean.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Use hair products that are authorized by the army.
  • Get regular haircuts to maintain the proper length.
  • Avoid extreme hairstyles or colors that are not authorized by the army.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have long hair in the army?

No, the army has strict regulations on hair length, and personnel are not allowed to have long hair.

Can women in the army wear their hair down?

No, women in the army are not allowed to wear their hair down. The hair should be styled in such a way that it does not interfere with the proper wearing of a military headgear or protective mask.

What happens if I violate the army's hair regulations?

If you violate the army's hair regulations, you may face disciplinary action, including counseling, reprimand, or even discharge from the army.

Can I wear a wig or hairpiece in the army?

Yes, you can wear a wig or hairpiece in the army, as long as it conforms to the army's hair regulations and does not interfere with the proper wearing of a military headgear or protective mask.


Understanding the army's hair regulations is crucial for personnel. It's important to maintain a professional appearance and adhere to the regulations to ensure uniformity and discipline. By following the guidelines and maintaining proper hair care, you can comply with the army's regulations and serve your country with pride.
