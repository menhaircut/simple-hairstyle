How To Cut Into Mullet Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a funky and edgy hairstyle, the mullet may be the perfect choice for you. This hairstyle has been around for decades, and it's still a popular choice for those who want to make a statement. In this article, we'll show you how to cut into mullet hair in easy-to-follow steps.

Description

The mullet is a hairstyle that's short at the front and sides and longer at the back. It was popularized in the 1980s by musicians and athletes, and it's still a popular choice for those who want to stand out from the crowd. The mullet can be styled in different ways, from a messy and textured look to a sleek and polished style.

How to Cut into Mullet Hair

Before you start cutting, you need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. This will make the hair easier to cut and style. Use a towel to dry the hair, and make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use the comb to section the hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. Use a hair tie or clip to keep the top section out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Section

Start cutting the bottom section of the hair. Use the comb to comb the hair straight down, and cut it straight across. You can use the comb as a guide to ensure that the hair is cut evenly.

Step 4: Cut the Top Section

Take down the top section of the hair, and use the comb to comb it straight down. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the back and working your way towards the front. The hair at the back should be longer than the hair at the front.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Use the comb to blend the layers together. Use the scissors to trim any uneven bits, and make sure that the layers are blended smoothly.

Step 6: Texturize the Hair

If you want a messy and textured look, use the scissors to cut into the hair at random angles. This will create texture and movement in the hair.

Step 7: Style the Hair

Once you've finished cutting the hair, style it as desired. You can use a hair dryer and a styling product to create the look you want.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting into mullet hair:

  • Start with a longer length, and gradually cut more if needed
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and angles
  • Always cut less than you think you need to, as you can always cut more later

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting into mullet hair:

Can I cut a mullet on my own?

Yes, you can cut a mullet on your own, but it's recommended to have someone help you, especially if you're not experienced in cutting hair.

What face shape suits a mullet?

A mullet can suit any face shape, but it's recommended to consult with a hairstylist to determine the best length and style for your face shape.

How often should I get my mullet trimmed?

It's recommended to get your mullet trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

Hair Care

To keep your mullet looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep the hair hydrated and healthy
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Cutting into mullet hair can be a fun and exciting experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can create a unique and edgy hairstyle that's sure to turn heads. Follow these steps, and don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and angles. Have fun!
