How To Fix A Bad Curly Hair Cut Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Jul 14, 2023 · 5 min read

Getting a bad haircut is a nightmare, especially if you have curly hair. A bad curly hair cut can leave you with uneven lengths, awkward layers, and frizzy ends. But don't worry, there are ways to fix a bad curly hair cut without having to wait for your hair to grow out. In this blog post, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to fix a bad curly hair cut and get your curls back on track.

  • How to Fix a Bad Curly Hair Cut
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs

How to Fix a Bad Curly Hair Cut

The first step in fixing a bad curly hair cut is to identify what went wrong. Was the length too short? Were the layers uneven? Did the stylist use the wrong technique? Once you know what needs to be fixed, you can start working on a solution.

Here are some things you can do to fix a bad curly hair cut:

  • Get a trim: If the length is too short, you may need to get a trim to even out your hair. This will help get rid of any uneven lengths and give your hair a fresh start.
  • Add layers: If your hair looks heavy and shapeless, adding layers can give it more movement and volume. However, be careful not to add too many layers, as this can make your hair look too thin and stringy.
  • Style your hair differently: If you're not happy with the cut, try styling your hair differently. Experiment with different partings, updos, and accessories to find a style that works for you.
  • Use hair products: Hair products can work wonders in fixing a bad curly hair cut. Use a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner, a leave-in conditioner, and a styling product to define your curls and control frizz.
  • Consult a new stylist: If you're still not happy with your hair, consider consulting a new stylist. Look for someone who specializes in curly hair and has a good reputation for fixing bad haircuts.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix a bad curly hair cut:

  1. Wash your hair with a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel-dry your hair gently.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-defining cream to your hair.
  4. Blow-dry your hair on a low heat setting, using a diffuser attachment to enhance your curls.
  5. If you need to trim your hair, do so using sharp scissors and cutting small sections at a time.
  6. If you want to add layers, use point cutting to create soft, natural-looking layers.
  7. Style your hair as desired using a curling iron or other styling tools.
  8. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your curls in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for fixing a bad curly hair cut:

  • Avoid using a brush on your curls, as this can create frizz and breakage.
  • Use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to dry your hair, as regular towels can rough up the hair cuticle and cause frizz.
  • Invest in a good-quality diffuser attachment for your blow dryer to enhance your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different hair products and styling techniques to find what works best for your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing a bad curly hair cut:

How long will it take for my hair to grow out?

It depends on how short the cut is and how fast your hair grows. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so it may take several months to grow out a bad haircut.

Can I fix a bad curly hair cut myself?

If you're confident in your hair-cutting skills, you can try fixing a bad curly hair cut yourself. However, it's always best to consult a professional stylist to avoid making the problem worse.

What should I do if I'm not happy with the new cut?

If you're not happy with the new cut, talk to your stylist and explain what you don't like. They may be able to make some adjustments or recommend a different style that better suits your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining healthy, beautiful curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can fix a bad curly hair cut and keep your curls looking their best. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products until you find what works for you.
