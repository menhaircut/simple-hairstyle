How To Cut Damaged Curly Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting curly hair can be a daunting task, especially if it's damaged. Whether you have heat damage, split ends or your hair has been over-processed, cutting it properly is essential to avoid further damage. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut damaged curly hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Damaged Curly Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Damaged Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Damaged Curly Hair

How to Cut Damaged Curly Hair

Before you start cutting your curly hair, it's important to consider your hair type, texture, and the degree of damage. Depending on the extent of the damage, you may need to trim a little or cut it short. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Do not cut your hair when it's wet. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Work in small sections to ensure even cutting.
  • Start with a little bit of hair and gradually cut more if needed.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Detangle Your Hair

Start by detangling your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. This will help you see the extent of the damage and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four or more parts using hair clips. This will help you work in small sections and avoid cutting too much hair at once.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair in small sections, starting from the back and working your way to the front. Use the tip of the scissors to cut off the damaged ends. Cut the hair at an angle to avoid creating blunt ends.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After cutting a section, check for evenness by comparing it to the other sections. If it's not even, trim a little more until it's even.

Step 5: Finish Up

Once you've cut all the sections, check your hair for evenness and adjust as needed. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Damaged Curly Hair

  • Don't cut too much hair at once. Work in small sections to avoid cutting too much and ending up with uneven hair.
  • Don't use regular scissors. Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid split ends and further damage.
  • Consider cutting your hair shorter. If the damage is extensive, it may be better to cut it short and start fresh.
  • Consult a professional. If you're not confident in cutting your hair, it's best to seek the help of a professional hair stylist.

FAQs

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself, but it's important to be careful and work slowly, especially if it's damaged.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and further damage.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, it's best to use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid split ends and further damage.

Hair Care for Damaged Curly Hair

Cutting your hair is only part of the solution. To keep your curly hair healthy, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or minimize their use.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to restore moisture.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry to avoid creating frizz.

By following these tips, you can keep your damaged curly hair healthy and beautiful.

Cutting damaged curly hair may seem overwhelming, but with the right tools and techniques, you can do it yourself. Remember to take your time, work in small sections and use sharp hair cutting scissors. If you're not confident, it's best to seek the help of a professional hair stylist. By taking good care of your curly hair, you can keep it healthy and beautiful for years to come.
