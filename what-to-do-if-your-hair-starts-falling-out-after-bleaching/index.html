What To Do If Your Hair Starts Falling Out After Bleaching

Written by Christine Oct 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have recently bleached your hair and noticed that it is falling out, you may be wondering what to do. Hair loss after bleaching is a common occurrence and can happen to anyone, regardless of their hair type or texture. In this article, we will discuss steps you can take to prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

Table of Contents

2. How to Prevent Further Hair Loss

Causes of Hair Loss After Bleaching

There are several reasons why your hair may be falling out after bleaching. These include:

  • Chemical damage to the hair follicles
  • Overprocessing of the hair
  • Excessive heat styling
  • Using the wrong hair products
  • Not following proper hair care routines

If you have experienced any of these issues, they may have contributed to your hair loss.

How to Prevent Further Hair Loss

If you have noticed that your hair is falling out after bleaching, there are steps you can take to prevent further damage. These include:

  • Avoiding heat styling tools
  • Using a protein treatment to strengthen the hair
  • Using a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to the hair
  • Avoiding harsh chemicals in hair products
  • Focusing on a healthy diet and lifestyle to promote hair growth

By taking these steps, you can help to prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Hair Loss After Bleaching

If you are experiencing hair loss after bleaching, follow these steps to treat the issue:

  1. Stop bleaching your hair immediately.
  2. Use a protein treatment to strengthen the hair.
  3. Use a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to the hair.
  4. Consult with a hair care professional for additional advice.
  5. Consider taking supplements to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these steps, you can help to treat hair loss after bleaching and promote healthy hair growth.

Tips for Promoting Healthy Hair Growth

In addition to treating hair loss after bleaching, there are steps you can take to promote healthy hair growth. These include:

  • Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Drinking plenty of water
  • Avoiding harsh chemicals in hair products
  • Using a heat protectant spray when heat styling the hair
  • Using a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair

By incorporating these tips into your hair care routine, you can promote healthy hair growth and prevent further damage.

Frequently Asked Questions About Hair Loss After Bleaching

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss after bleaching:

Q: Is hair loss after bleaching permanent?
A: Hair loss after bleaching is not always permanent. With proper hair care and treatment, you can often restore your hair to its previous state.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after bleaching?
A: Hair typically grows at a rate of about 0.5 inches per month. However, the rate may vary depending on your individual hair growth patterns and the extent of the damage.

Q: Can I still bleach my hair if it is falling out?
A: It is not recommended to bleach your hair if it is falling out. This can further damage the hair and lead to more hair loss.

Hair Care Routine for Bleached Hair

If you have bleached hair, it is important to follow a proper hair care routine to prevent damage and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling tools
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Use a protein treatment every two weeks
  • Avoid harsh chemicals in hair products

By following these tips, you can help to maintain healthy, beautiful bleached hair.

Conclusion

If your hair is falling out after bleaching, don't panic. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can help to prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to take care of your hair by using the right products and following a proper hair care routine. With time and patience, you can restore your hair to its former glory.
