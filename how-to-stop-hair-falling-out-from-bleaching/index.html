How To Stop Hair Falling Out From Bleaching

Written by William Jun 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you one of the many people who experience hair loss after bleaching? This can be a common problem, especially if you bleach your hair frequently or if you have naturally thin or fragile hair. But don't worry, there are steps you can take to prevent hair loss and keep your hair healthy and strong. In this article, we will show you how to stop hair falling out from bleaching, with helpful tips and advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Hair Falling Out from Bleaching
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Loss
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care to Keep Your Hair Healthy

How to Stop Hair Falling Out from Bleaching

The first step to stop hair falling out from bleaching is to limit or avoid bleaching altogether. Bleaching can damage your hair, causing it to become brittle and prone to breakage. If you must bleach your hair, do it sparingly and follow these steps:

Choose a Gentle Bleaching Product

When selecting a bleaching product, choose one that is gentle and formulated for your hair type. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals like ammonia, which can damage your hair and scalp. Look for products that contain natural oils and moisturizers to help protect your hair during the bleaching process.

Follow the Instructions Closely

Always follow the instructions carefully when bleaching your hair. Don't leave the product on your hair for longer than recommended, as this can damage your hair and scalp. Rinse your hair thoroughly after bleaching and use a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture and nutrients to your hair.

Avoid Heat Styling Tools

After bleaching your hair, avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can further damage your hair and cause it to break. Instead, let your hair air dry and use a wide-toothed comb to detangle it gently.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to stop hair falling out from bleaching:

Step 1: Limit or Avoid Bleaching

If possible, limit or avoid bleaching your hair altogether. If you must bleach your hair, do it sparingly and follow the instructions carefully.

Step 2: Choose a Gentle Bleaching Product

Select a bleaching product that is gentle and formulated for your hair type. Look for products that contain natural oils and moisturizers to help protect your hair during the bleaching process.

Step 3: Follow the Instructions Carefully

Always follow the instructions closely when bleaching your hair. Don't leave the product on your hair for longer than recommended, as this can damage your hair and scalp. Rinse your hair thoroughly after bleaching and use a deep conditioning treatment.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons after bleaching your hair. Instead, let your hair air dry and use a wide-toothed comb to detangle it gently.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

Here are some tips to help prevent hair loss from bleaching:

Avoid Overprocessing Your Hair

Don't bleach your hair too frequently or use other chemical treatments, as these can damage your hair and cause it to fall out.

Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type. Look for products that contain natural oils and moisturizers to help keep your hair healthy and strong.

Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy and strong. Include foods like fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains in your diet.

FAQs

Q. How long does it take for hair to recover from bleaching?

A. It can take several weeks or months for hair to recover from bleaching, depending on the extent of the damage. Be patient and give your hair time to heal.

Q. Can hair grow back after falling out from bleaching?

A. Yes, hair can grow back after falling out from bleaching, but it may take several months or longer. Be gentle with your hair and give it time to heal.

Q. Can I bleach my hair if I have thin or fragile hair?

A. If you have thin or fragile hair, it is best to avoid bleaching altogether, as it can damage your hair and cause it to fall out. If you must bleach your hair, do it sparingly and follow the instructions carefully.

Hair Care to Keep Your Hair Healthy

To keep your hair healthy and strong, follow these hair care tips:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to remove dirt and oil buildup.

Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair gently, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails, as these can pull on your hair and cause it to break.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and keep it looking healthy and strong.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can prevent hair loss from bleaching and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to be gentle with your hair and give it time to heal, and limit or avoid bleaching whenever possible. If you have any concerns about hair loss or damage, consult a professional stylist or dermatologist.
