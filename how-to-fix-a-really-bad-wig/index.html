How to Fix a Really Bad Wig

The first step in fixing a bad wig is to identify the problem. Is it tangled? Is the style not working for you? Once you know what the issue is, you can start working on a solution.

Some common problems with wigs include:

  • Tangled hair
  • Frizzy hair
  • Unnatural shine
  • Flat hair
  • Unflattering style

In this guide, we'll cover how to fix each of these problems and more.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to fix a really bad wig:

Step 1: Detangle the Wig

If your wig is tangled, start by gently combing through it with a wide-toothed comb. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull too hard. If the tangles are stubborn, you can use a wig detangling spray or a mixture of water and fabric softener to make the hair easier to comb through.

Step 2: Wash and Condition the Wig

Once the wig is detangled, wash it with a wig shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to use cool water and gently work the shampoo and conditioner through the hair. Rinse thoroughly and gently squeeze out the excess water.

Step 3: Style the Wig

If the wig has lost its style or is just not working for you, you can try restyling it. Use a wig styling product to set the hair in the desired style, and then use a wig brush to shape the hair. You can also use heat styling tools like a curling iron or flat iron, but be sure to use a low heat setting and protect the hair with a heat protectant spray.

Step 4: Trim the Wig

If the wig is too long or has uneven ends, you can trim it to the desired length. Use a sharp pair of scissors and cut small sections at a time, being careful not to cut too much at once. You can also take the wig to a professional stylist for a trim.

Tips for Fixing a Bad Wig

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fixing a bad wig:

  • Be gentle when detangling the hair
  • Use a wig shampoo and conditioner
  • Use cool water when washing the wig
  • Use a wig styling product to set the hair in the desired style
  • Protect the hair with a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools
  • Trim the wig slowly and carefully

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing a bad wig:

How do I fix a frizzy wig?

You can fix a frizzy wig by washing it with a wig shampoo and conditioner, and then using a wig detangling spray or a mixture of water and fabric softener to smooth out the hair. You can also use a wig styling product to set the hair in a smooth style.

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on a wig?

No, you should always use a wig shampoo and conditioner on a wig. Regular shampoo and conditioner can damage the hair and cause it to tangle or become frizzy.

How often should I wash my wig?

You should wash your wig every 6-8 wears, or as needed if it becomes dirty or starts to smell.

Hair Care for Wigs

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your wig looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Store your wig on a wig stand to keep its shape
  • Wash your wig with a wig shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot water or heat styling tools on your wig
  • Protect your wig from sunlight and heat
  • Trim your wig regularly to keep it looking neat and tidy

With these tips and our step by step guide, you can fix a really bad wig and keep it looking great for longer. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.
