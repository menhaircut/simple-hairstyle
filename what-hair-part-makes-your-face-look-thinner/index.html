What Hair Part Makes Your Face Look Thinner A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Jun 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of seeing a round or chubby face every time you look in the mirror? One simple trick you can try is changing your hair part. Yes, you read it right! The way you part your hair can make a significant difference in how your face looks. In this article, we will discuss what hair part makes your face look thinner, how to do it, and some tips and tricks to maintain the look. So, let's get started!

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

Before we dive into the details, let's understand the concept. The way you part your hair can create an illusion of a slimmer or wider face. A middle part can make your face look longer and thinner, while a side part can make it look wider. However, the placement of the part also matters. A deep side part can add more definition to your face and make it look slimmer, while a shallow side part can have the opposite effect.

How to

Now that you know the theory let's move on to the practical part. Here's how to create a hair part that makes your face look thinner:

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

Before you decide on the hair part, you should know your face shape. The most common face shapes are round, oval, square, heart, and diamond. Each shape has different features and requires a different approach to create a slimmer look.

Step 2: Find the Right Part

Once you know your face shape, you can determine the best hair part for you. Here's a quick guide:

  • Round face: A deep side part can create the illusion of length and make your face look slimmer. Avoid middle parts or shallow side parts.
  • Oval face: You can experiment with different parts, but a middle part can make your face look longer and more angular.
  • Square face: A side part can soften the features and make your face look less angular. Avoid middle parts.
  • Heart face: A side part can balance the wideness of the forehead and make your face look more symmetrical.
  • Diamond face: A middle part can make your face look longer and more balanced.

Step 3: Style Your Hair

Now that you know which part to choose, it's time to style your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Deep side part: Use a comb to create a straight line from your hairline to the crown of your head. Pin the other side of your hair back, and let the rest fall naturally. You can add some volume to the crown for a more dramatic effect.
  • Shallow side part: Use your fingers to create a soft part, and let your hair fall naturally. Avoid creating a straight line, as it can make your face look wider.
  • Middle part: Use a comb to create a straight line from your hairline to the back of your head. You can add some volume to the crown for a more flattering look.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to make your hair part look even better:

  • Add volume: Adding volume to the crown can make your face look longer and slimmer.
  • Use hair accessories: You can use hair accessories like headbands or clips to create a more defined part and add some style.
  • Experiment: Don't be afraid to try different parts and see what works best for you. You might be surprised by the results.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair parts:

  • Can a hair part make my face look wider? Yes, a shallow side part can make your face look wider, especially if you have a round face.
  • Can I change my hair part every day? Yes, you can change your hair part as often as you like. It's a simple way to switch up your look.
  • Can I create a hair part if I have bangs? Yes, you can create a side part or a middle part with bangs. Just make sure they blend in well with the rest of your hair.

Hair Care

Finally, here are some tips to take care of your hair:

  • Use a good shampoo and conditioner: Choose products that suit your hair type and address your specific concerns.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it look dull and lifeless. Try to air-dry your hair whenever possible.
  • Get regular trims: Regular trims can prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

So, there you have it – a comprehensive guide on what hair part makes your face look thinner. Remember to choose the right part for your face shape, style your hair accordingly, and take good care of your locks. With these tips, you can achieve a slimmer, more flattering look effortlessly.
