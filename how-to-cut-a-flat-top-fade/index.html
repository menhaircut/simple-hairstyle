How To Cut A Flat Top Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 02, 2023 · 4 min read

A flat top fade is a cool and classic hairstyle that never goes out of style. It's a versatile cut that works for most hair types and can be customized to suit different face shapes and personal styles. Whether you're a professional barber or just someone who wants to learn how to cut their own hair, this guide will teach you how to cut a flat top fade in relaxed English language.

  • How to Cut a Flat Top Fade
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Introduction

A flat top fade is a popular hairstyle that features short sides and a flat top. It's a versatile cut that can be customized to suit different face shapes and personal styles. If you're interested in learning how to cut a flat top fade, keep reading for a step-by-step guide.

How to Cut a Flat Top Fade

To cut a flat top fade, you'll need a few tools, including clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. You'll also need to decide on the length and style of the fade you want to create. Here are the basic steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Use a comb to part the hair where you want the fade to begin.
  2. Use clippers with the appropriate guard to trim the hair on the sides and back of the head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using a higher guard as you go.
  3. Blend the sides and back with scissors or clippers without a guard. Use a comb to lift the hair and create a natural-looking transition between the top and sides.
  4. Create the flat top by trimming the hair on the top of the head to the desired length. Use scissors to cut the hair straight across and a comb to lift the hair and create a flat surface.
  5. Blend the top with the sides and back using scissors or clippers without a guard. Use a comb to lift the hair and create a natural-looking transition.
  6. Check your work in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Style the flat top as desired using a product like pomade or wax.

Tips for Cutting a Flat Top Fade

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting a flat top fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair too quickly.
  • Use scissors to create a more natural-looking transition between the top and sides.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting a flat top fade requires precision and attention to detail.
  • Ask for feedback and make any necessary adjustments as you go.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own flat top fade?

A: Yes, you can cut your own flat top fade with the right tools and techniques. However, if you're not confident in your ability to do so, it's always best to consult a professional barber.

Q: How often should I get my flat top fade trimmed?

A: This depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade starts to grow out. On average, most people get their flat top fade trimmed every 2-4 weeks.

Q: What products should I use to style my flat top fade?

A: You can use a variety of products to style your flat top fade, including pomade, wax, gel, and hairspray. Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type and desired style.

Hair Care Tips for a Flat Top Fade

To keep your flat top fade looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners, which can damage the hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Get regular trims to keep your fade looking clean and fresh.

Cutting a flat top fade takes practice, but with the right tools and techniques, it's a stylish and versatile hairstyle that can be customized to suit your personal style. Whether you're a professional barber or just someone who wants to learn how to cut their own hair, this step-by-step guide will help you achieve the perfect flat top fade.
