Updo Hairstyles For Medium Length Hair Tutorials

Written by Frank Jun 17, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have medium length hair, you may think that your hair is too short for an updo. However, there are many updo hairstyles that work well with medium length hair. In this article, we will provide you with tutorials for updo hairstyles for medium length hair, as well as tips for hair care.

How to do an Updo Hairstyle for Medium Length Hair

Before we get into the specific updo hairstyles for medium length hair, it’s important to know the basics of how to do an updo. First, you should start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is too slippery, you may want to add some texture with a texturizing spray or dry shampoo. Then, you can start to create your updo by pulling your hair back and securing it with bobby pins or hair ties. You can also use hair accessories like clips or headbands to hold your hair in place.

Step 1: Choose Your Updo

There are many updo hairstyles that work well with medium length hair, including braids, buns, and twists. Choose the style that you like the most and that works best with your hair type and face shape.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Before you start styling, make sure that your hair is clean and dry. If you have fine or straight hair, you may want to add some texture with a texturizing spray or dry shampoo. If your hair is thick or curly, you may want to use a smoothing serum or cream to tame frizz.

Step 3: Create Your Updo

Once you’ve chosen your updo and prepped your hair, you can start to create your style. Follow the step-by-step guide below for specific updo hairstyles for medium length hair.

Step by Step Guide for Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

Braided Crown Updo

This updo hairstyle is perfect for a formal event or wedding. It’s also great for keeping your hair out of your face on a hot summer day.

Step 1: Part your hair down the middle and create two braids on each side of your head. Secure the ends with clear elastic bands.

Step 2: Pull the braids up and over the top of your head, securing them with bobby pins.

Step 3: Tuck the ends of the braids under the rest of your hair and secure with bobby pins.

Messy Bun

This updo hairstyle is perfect for a casual day or a night out with friends. It’s also great for keeping your hair out of your face while you’re working or studying.

Step 1: Pull your hair back into a ponytail and secure with a hair tie.

Step 2: Twist the ponytail around the hair tie to create a bun.

Step 3: Secure the bun with bobby pins and pull out some pieces of hair around your face to create a messy, effortless look.

Twisted Half Updo

This updo hairstyle is perfect for a date night or a night out with friends. It’s also great for keeping your hair out of your face while you’re working or studying.

Step 1: Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and twist it.

Step 2: Pull the twisted section of hair back and secure with a bobby pin.

Step 3: Repeat on the other side of your head, twisting and securing with a bobby pin.

Tips for Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

  • Use bobby pins and hair ties that match your hair color to create a seamless look.
  • Don’t be afraid to play with different textures and styles to find the updo that works best for you.
  • If you’re having trouble getting your hair to stay in place, try using a strong hold hairspray or hair gel.
  • Practice your updo a few times before wearing it out to make sure that you are comfortable with the style and that it looks the way you want it to.

FAQs about Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

What kind of hair accessories can I use for an updo?

You can use bobby pins, hair ties, clips, headbands, and hair combs to hold your hair in place.

What if my hair is too short for an updo?

If your hair is too short for an updo, you can try adding hair extensions or using a hair donut to create more volume and length.

Hair Care for Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

When you’re wearing an updo hairstyle, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent breakage and damage. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can dry out your hair and cause damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, as they can cause breakage and damage.
  • Be gentle when brushing and detangling your hair, as rough handling can also cause breakage and damage.

With these tutorials and tips, you can create beautiful updo hairstyles for medium length hair that are perfect for any occasion. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
