How To Cut Korean Hair Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you're trying to achieve a specific look like Korean hair bangs. But with the right tools and techniques, you can give yourself a salon-quality cut without leaving your home. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut Korean hair bangs from start to finish.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Bangs
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Your Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few tools. Make sure you have:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • Hair clips or bobby pins
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp - dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start cutting, you'll want to make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's also a good idea to style your hair the way you normally wear it so you can see how your new bangs will look with your everyday look.

Use your fine-tooth comb to comb your hair straight down over your forehead. Make sure your hair is evenly distributed and there are no bumps or knots.

Use your spray bottle to lightly mist your hair. This will help keep it in place while you're cutting.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is important to ensure you get an even cut. Use your comb to create a triangle-shaped section at the front of your hair. The point of the triangle should be at the center of your hairline and the base should be at the crown of your head.

Clip the rest of your hair back to keep it out of the way.

Cutting Your Bangs

Now it's time to start cutting! Take a small section of hair from the triangle shape you created. Hold your hair between your index and middle fingers and gently pull it down to the length you want your bangs to be.

Using your scissors, make a small snip at the bottom of your hair. Be careful not to cut too much - you can always cut more later.

Continue cutting small sections of hair and checking to make sure they're even. Remember, it's better to cut too little than too much!

Once you've cut all the hair in the triangle section, unclip the rest of your hair and check your work. Use your comb to blend your new bangs with the rest of your hair.

Finishing Touches

If you want a more textured look, you can point-cut your bangs. This means cutting into the ends of your hair at an angle instead of straight across.

To style your new bangs, blow dry them with a round brush to give them volume. You can also use a flat iron to straighten them or a curling iron to give them a slight wave.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs

  • Start with small snips and work your way up
  • Make sure your hair is evenly distributed before you start cutting
  • Take breaks if you need to - cutting your own hair can be tiring!
  • Don't cut too much at once - you can always cut more later

FAQs

Can I cut my own bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes! Just make sure you cut your hair when it's dry so you can see how it will look when it's styled.

Should I cut my bangs straight across or at an angle?

This is up to you! Straight across bangs are more classic, while angled bangs can give you a more modern look.

Hair Care After Your Cut

After you've cut your hair, make sure to take care of it to keep it looking great. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type and avoid using too much heat when styling.

You'll also want to trim your bangs every few weeks to keep them looking fresh. Happy cutting!
