How To Get Rid Of Brassy Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your hair color turning brassy and orange? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people with blonde, gray, or highlighted hair struggle with brassiness. But the good news is that you can fix it at home without spending a fortune at the salon. In this article, we'll show you how to get rid of brassy hair at home with easy DIY tips and tricks.

Description

Brassiness is a common hair problem that occurs when the hair turns yellow, orange, or red instead of the desired blonde or gray shade. This happens when the hair is overexposed to sunlight, chlorine, hard water, or styling products that contain alcohol or sulfates. It can also happen when the hair is bleached or colored improperly or when the color fades over time. Brassiness can be frustrating and unflattering, but there are ways to combat it at home.

How to Get Rid of Brassy Hair at Home

Before we dive into the solutions, it's important to understand the science behind brassiness. Hair color is made up of two pigments: eumelanin (black and brown) and pheomelanin (red and yellow). When hair is bleached or lightened, the eumelanin is removed, leaving behind the pheomelanin. This is why blonde and gray hair tends to turn yellow or orange over time. To get rid of brassy hair at home, you need to neutralize the warm tones with cool tones. This can be done using toners, purple or blue shampoos, or DIY remedies.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps to follow to get rid of brassy hair at home:

  1. Identify the level of brassiness: Before you start treating your hair, you need to know how much brassiness you're dealing with. Hold up a white towel or shirt next to your hair and look at it in natural light. If your hair looks yellow, you have a mild case of brassiness. If it looks orange or red, you have a more severe case. This will help you choose the right product or remedy.
  2. Choose a toner or purple/blue shampoo: If you have mild brassiness, you can use a toner or a purple/blue shampoo to neutralize the warm tones. Toners are semi-permanent dyes that come in different shades of cool blonde or gray. Purple or blue shampoos contain pigments that counteract the yellow or orange tones. Apply the product evenly to your hair, following the instructions on the package. Leave it on for the recommended time (usually 10-20 minutes), then rinse it off with cool water.
  3. Try a DIY remedy: If you have more severe brassiness or want a more natural solution, you can try a DIY remedy. One popular remedy is to mix equal parts of apple cider vinegar and water, apply it to your hair after shampooing, and leave it on for 5-10 minutes. Another remedy is to mix 1/4 cup of baking soda with enough water to make a paste, apply it to your hair, and leave it on for 10-15 minutes. Rinse off the remedy with cool water and condition your hair.
  4. Repeat as necessary: Depending on the severity of your brassiness, you may need to repeat the treatment several times to see results. Be patient and don't overdo it, as toners and DIY remedies can dry out your hair if used too often.

Tips to Prevent and Control Brassiness

Here are some tips to prevent and control brassiness:

  • Avoid overexposure to sunlight, chlorine, and hard water by wearing a hat, using a swim cap, or rinsing your hair with bottled water.
  • Use hair products that are sulfate-free, alcohol-free, and pH-balanced.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends and keep your hair healthy.
  • Consult a professional stylist if you're unsure about how to bleach or color your hair, or if you need a more drastic solution such as a color correction.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting rid of brassy hair:

Q: Can I use regular shampoo instead of purple or blue shampoo?

A: No, regular shampoo doesn't contain the pigments that neutralize brassiness. You need a specialized purple or blue shampoo that's formulated for blonde or gray hair.

Q: Can I use lemon juice to get rid of brassiness?

A: Lemon juice is a natural remedy that can lighten hair and remove some brassiness, but it can also dry out and damage hair if used too often. It's best to use a toner or a purple/blue shampoo that's designed for your hair type.

Q: How often should I use a toner or a purple/blue shampoo?

A: It depends on your hair type and the severity of your brassiness. As a general rule, you can use a toner or a purple/blue shampoo once a week or every other week. Don't use it too often, as it can cause your hair to turn purple or blue instead of neutralizing the warm tones.

Hair Care for Blonde, Gray, or Highlighted Hair

Here are some general tips to care for your blonde, gray, or highlighted hair:

  • Wash your hair every other day or every three days to avoid stripping away natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to avoid pulling and breaking your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often or at high temperatures, as they can damage your hair and cause more brassiness.
  • Protect your hair from UV rays and heat by using a heat protectant spray or serum.
  • Consider getting a gloss or a glaze treatment at the salon to add shine and vibrancy to your hair color.

In conclusion, getting rid of brassy hair at home is possible with the right products and techniques. By following our step-by-step guide and tips, you can neutralize the warm tones and maintain a cool blonde or gray shade. Remember to be gentle with your hair and consult a professional if you need help. Good luck!
