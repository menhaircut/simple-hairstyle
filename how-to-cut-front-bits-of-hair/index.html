How To Cut Front Bits Of Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting the front bits of your hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not familiar with hair cutting techniques. However, with the right tools and a little guidance, you can easily achieve a salon-worthy haircut at home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut the front bits of your hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. Here's what we'll cover:

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Front Bits of Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Front Bits of Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Front Bits of Hair

Description

The front bits of your hair can make or break your hairstyle. Cutting them incorrectly can result in unflattering layers, uneven bangs, and a bad haircut overall. That's why it's essential to learn how to cut them properly.

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over some essential tools you'll need:

  • Sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Water spray bottle

How to Cut Front Bits of Hair

First, decide on the length you want for your front bits of hair. It's best to start with a longer length and work your way down gradually to avoid any mistakes.

Next, dampen your hair with a water spray bottle to make it easier to work with. Comb your hair straight down and separate the front bits of hair that you want to cut from the rest of your hair. Use hair clips or ties to secure the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut the front bits of your hair:

  1. Start with the middle section of your front bits of hair. Hold the hair with your fingers and cut straight across. Make sure to cut a little less than the desired length as hair tends to bounce back when dry.
  2. Move to the left section of your front bits of hair. Hold the hair with your fingers and cut straight across at an angle, following the length of the middle section. This will create a gradual and natural-looking layer.
  3. Repeat the same process on the right section of your front bits of hair.
  4. Check for any uneven parts or stray hairs and trim them carefully.

Tips for Cutting Front Bits of Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting the front bits of your hair:

  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will look shorter when it dries. Instead, dampen your hair with a water spray bottle.
  • Always cut less than the desired length, as hair tends to bounce back when dry.
  • Use sharp hair-cutting scissors to avoid split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Take breaks in between cutting to avoid making any mistakes or over-cutting.
  • Use a mirror to check the length and shape of your front bits of hair from different angles.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting front bits of hair:

1. How often should I cut the front bits of my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

2. Can I cut my hair without hair-cutting scissors?

No, it's not recommended to cut your hair with regular scissors as they can cause split ends and damage to your hair.

3. Can I cut the front bits of my hair myself?

Yes, you can cut the front bits of your hair yourself if you follow the proper techniques and use the right tools.

Hair Care After Cutting Front Bits of Hair

After cutting the front bits of your hair, it's essential to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Apply a hair serum or oil to nourish your hair and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Cutting the front bits of your hair might seem intimidating, but with the right guidance and tools, you can easily achieve a flattering and stylish haircut at home. Follow these steps, tips, and FAQs to cut your hair like a pro!
