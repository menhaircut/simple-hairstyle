How To Cut Side Of Men s Hair

Written by Bardi Jun 11, 2023 · 4 min read
If you're looking to save some money and cut your hair at home, you may be wondering how to cut the sides of your hair. It can be intimidating to take clippers to your own head, but with a little practice and the right technique, you can achieve a great haircut. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting the sides of men's hair and provide tips and tricks for a professional-looking cut.

How to Cut the Sides of Men's Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a set of clippers, a comb, and scissors. You may also want to invest in a mirror or two to ensure you can see all angles of your head.

Step 1: Start with a Longer Guard

Begin by attaching a longer guard to your clippers, such as a #4 or #5. This will allow you to leave a little more length on the sides of your head. Starting with a longer guard can also help you avoid making any drastic mistakes.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Using your comb, section off the hair on the sides of your head. Start at the temple and comb the hair straight down. You can use clips to hold the hair in place if needed.

Step 3: Begin Cutting

With your clippers, start at the bottom of the hairline and work your way up. Use short, even strokes, moving the clippers in an upward motion. Be careful not to go too high up on the head, as this can create an uneven look.

Step 4: Move to the Top

Once you've finished the bottom section, move up to the top section of hair. Use the same technique, starting at the back of the head and working your way forward.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

To create a seamless look, you'll need to blend the hair where the two sections meet. Switch to a shorter guard, such as a #2 or #3, and use the clippers to blend the hair together.

Step 6: Trim the Edges

Using your scissors, trim any stray hairs or uneven edges. Be careful not to cut too much, as this can create an uneven look.

Tips for Cutting the Sides of Men's Hair

- Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard for a seamless look. - Use short, even strokes when using your clippers. - Don't be afraid to ask for help or watch tutorial videos if you're feeling unsure. - Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.

FAQs

How often should I cut the sides of my hair?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and your desired length. On average, most men should trim their hair every four to six weeks.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

While it's possible to use regular scissors, it's best to invest in a pair of hair cutting scissors. These are specifically designed for cutting hair and will give you a more precise cut.

What should I do if I mess up my haircut?

Don't panic! Many haircuts can be fixed with a little time and patience. If you're feeling unsure, it's best to seek out a professional for help.

Hair Care After Cutting the Sides of Men's Hair

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. You may also want to invest in a hair oil or styling product to keep your hair looking its best.

Conclusion

Cutting the sides of men's hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a great haircut at home. Remember to start with a longer guard, section off your hair, and blend the hair for a seamless look. If you're feeling unsure, don't hesitate to seek out professional help. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting your own hair in no time!
