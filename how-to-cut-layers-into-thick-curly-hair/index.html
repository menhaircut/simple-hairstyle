How To Cut Layers Into Thick Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge to cut and style. It can be even more difficult to add layers to thick curly hair without creating a frizzy mess. But fear not! With the right technique and tools, you can achieve a layered look that enhances your curls and adds dimension to your hair. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut layers into thick curly hair.

How to Cut Layers into Thick Curly Hair

Cutting layers into thick curly hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done successfully. Here are the basic steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the natural shape and texture of your curls. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically formulated for curly hair to help enhance your natural curls and reduce frizz.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips or ties to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Start with the Bottom Layer

Starting at the nape of your neck, take a small section of hair and comb it out. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Try to cut the hair at a slight angle to create layers. Repeat this process for the rest of the bottom layer.

Step 4: Move to the Middle Layer

Release the middle section of hair and repeat the same process, starting at the back and working your way to the front.

Step 5: Finish with the Top Layer

Release the top section of hair and repeat the same process, starting at the back and working your way to the front. Be sure to leave the layers longer than the bottom layers to create a more natural-looking effect.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed guide on how to cut layers into thick curly hair:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You'll need the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Wash and condition your hair with products specifically formulated for curly hair. Towel dry your hair and let it air dry until it's about 80% dry.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to part your hair down the middle, then section it into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips or ties to keep each section in place.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom layer, take a small section of hair and comb it out. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Try to cut the hair at a slight angle to create layers.

Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've completed the bottom layer.

Next, move on to the middle layer and repeat the same process, starting at the back and working your way to the front.

Finally, release the top section of hair and repeat the same process, starting at the back and working your way to the front. Remember to leave the layers longer than the bottom layers to create a more natural-looking effect.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers into thick curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Don't be afraid to leave the layers longer than the bottom layers to create a more natural-looking effect.
  • When in doubt, seek the help of a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair?

While it's possible to cut your own hair, it can be difficult to achieve the desired results, especially if you have thick curly hair. It's best to seek the help of a professional stylist.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended that you get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Do I need to use special products for curly hair?

Yes, it's important to use products that are specifically formulated for curly hair to help enhance your natural curls and reduce frizz.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep your thick curly hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing product to help define your curls.

With these tips and techniques, you can successfully cut layers into your thick curly hair and achieve a look that enhances your natural curls and adds dimension to your hair.
