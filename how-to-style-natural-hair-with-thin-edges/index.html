How To Style Natural Hair With Thin Edges

Written by Christine Jul 18, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have natural hair with thin edges, you may find it challenging to style your hair without causing further damage or breakage. But don't worry, there are plenty of styles that you can rock while still protecting your edges. In this post, we'll cover some tips and tricks on how to style natural hair with thin edges.

Description

Natural hair with thin edges can be frustrating to deal with, especially if you're trying to achieve a certain style. Thin edges can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, age, and hair styling practices. It's important to take care of your edges and avoid styles that can cause further damage or breakage.

How to

When it comes to styling natural hair with thin edges, there are a few things to keep in mind:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids, weaves, and ponytails, can cause tension and damage to your edges. Instead, opt for looser styles that don't put too much pressure on your hairline.

2. Protect Your Edges

Use a satin scarf or bonnet when sleeping to protect your edges from rubbing against cotton pillowcases. You can also use edge control products to smooth down your edges and protect them from further damage.

3. Use Gentle Hair Accessories

Avoid using hair accessories that can tug on your edges, such as elastic bands or clips. Instead, use gentle accessories like silk scrunchies or hairpins.

Step by Step

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are gentle and moisturizing. Avoid using harsh shampoos or conditioners that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized and reduce breakage. Be sure to focus on your edges and massage the product gently into your scalp.

3. Choose a Protective Style

Choose a protective style that doesn't put too much pressure on your edges. Some styles to consider include twist-outs, braid-outs, and bantu knots.

4. Use Edge Control

Use edge control products to smooth down your edges and protect them from further damage. Apply a small amount to your fingertips and gently smooth it over your edges.

5. Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a satin scarf or bonnet to protect your hair at night. This will help to reduce frizz and keep your edges in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when styling natural hair with thin edges:

- Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated.

- Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause further damage to your edges.

- Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.

- Be gentle when styling your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your edges.

FAQ

Q: Can I still wear protective styles with thin edges?

A: Yes, you can still wear protective styles with thin edges. Just be sure to choose styles that don't put too much pressure on your hairline.

Q: How can I regrow my edges?

A: There are a variety of products and treatments that can help to regrow your edges, such as castor oil, biotin, and scalp massages. However, it's important to consult with a dermatologist or trichologist before trying any new treatments.

Hair Care

When it comes to taking care of natural hair with thin edges, it's important to keep your hair and scalp moisturized, avoid harsh styling practices, and use gentle hair accessories. With the right care and styling techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy hair without sacrificing your edges.
