How To Cut Natural Curly Hair Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 17, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with long, unruly curls? Cutting your natural curly hair short can be a refreshing change, but it can also be daunting. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of cutting natural curly hair short, including tips and tricks for achieving the perfect cut.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Natural Curly Hair Short
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Natural Curly Hair Short

Description

Cutting natural curly hair short can be a great way to manage your curls and achieve a low-maintenance hairstyle. However, it’s important to approach the process carefully to avoid any mishaps. Short curly haircuts can be tricky, and it’s important to make sure your stylist has experience working with natural curls. In this guide, we’ll provide tips and tricks for cutting natural curly hair short, as well as a step-by-step guide to follow.

How to Cut Natural Curly Hair Short

If you’re considering cutting your natural curly hair short, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it’s important to choose a cut that complements your face shape and hair type. Short curly haircuts can be versatile, but it’s important to choose a style that works for you. Second, it’s important to find a stylist who has experience working with natural curls. Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair, and it’s important to find someone who knows what they’re doing.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your natural curly hair short:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Make sure your curls are detangled and free of any knots.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back.
  3. Starting at the back, use a pair of sharp scissors to trim the ends of your hair. Work in small sections, cutting no more than an inch at a time.
  4. Once you’ve trimmed the back, move on to the sides. Use a mirror to check your progress and ensure that both sides are even.
  5. Finally, trim the front sections. You may want to leave your bangs longer or shorter depending on your preference.
  6. Once you’ve finished cutting, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls. This will help to enhance your natural texture and create volume.

Tips for Cutting Natural Curly Hair Short

When cutting natural curly hair short, there are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a style that complements your face shape and hair type.
  • Find a stylist who has experience working with natural curls.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and ensure that both sides are even.
  • Consider leaving your bangs longer or shorter depending on your preference.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting natural curly hair short:

1. Is it difficult to cut natural curly hair short?

Short curly haircuts can be tricky, but with the right approach, it can be done. It’s important to find a stylist who has experience working with natural curls and to take your time when cutting your hair.

2. What are some good short curly haircuts?

There are many great short curly haircuts to choose from, including pixie cuts, bobs, and shags. Consider your face shape and hair type when choosing a style.

3. How often should I cut my natural curly hair?

How often you should cut your natural curly hair depends on your hair type and the style you choose. Generally, it’s recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your ends healthy.

Hair Care for Short Natural Curly Hair

Once you’ve cut your natural curly hair short, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for caring for short natural curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural texture and create volume.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Cutting natural curly hair short can be a great way to manage your curls and achieve a low-maintenance hairstyle. By following these tips and tricks, as well as our step-by-step guide, you can achieve the perfect short curly haircut.
