How To Find Out Your Face Shape Male

Knowing your face shape is crucial when it comes to choosing the right hairstyle, beard style, and even glasses. But how do you find out your face shape as a male? In this article, we will guide you through the process of identifying your face shape step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Measure Your Face
  • Step 2: Identify Your Face Shape
  • Tips for Identifying Your Face Shape
  • FAQs About Face Shapes for Men
  • Hair Care Tips for Different Face Shapes

Step 1: Measure Your Face

The first step in finding out your face shape is to measure your face. You will need a tape measure or ruler for this. Measure the following:

  • Forehead: Measure from the peak of one eyebrow arch to the peak of the opposite arch.
  • Cheekbones: Measure across your cheekbones, starting and ending at the pointiest part below the outer corner of each eye.
  • Jawline: Measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jaw angles upwards. Multiply that number by two to get your jawline measurement.
  • Face length: Measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Write down each of these measurements as you go.

Step 2: Identify Your Face Shape

Once you have your measurements, you can use them to determine your face shape. There are six main face shapes for men:

  • Oval
  • Square
  • Round
  • Heart
  • Diamond
  • Triangle

Compare your measurements to the descriptions below to see which face shape matches your measurements:

  • Oval: Your face length is greater than your cheekbones, and your forehead is greater than your jawline. The angle of your jaw is rounded rather than sharp.
  • Square: Your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all similar in measurement. The angle of your jaw is sharp.
  • Round: Your cheekbones and face length have a similar measurement, and your jawline is rounded rather than sharp.
  • Heart: Your forehead is wider than your cheekbones, and your chin tapers to a point.
  • Diamond: Your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your forehead and jawline are narrower.
  • Triangle: Your jawline is wider than your forehead, and your chin is the most prominent feature of your face.

Once you have identified your face shape, you can start experimenting with different hairstyles, beard styles, and glasses that will complement your features.

Tips for Identifying Your Face Shape

If you are having trouble identifying your face shape, here are some tips to help you:

  • Look in the mirror and trace the outline of your face with a bar of soap or lipstick. Step back and see which shape it most closely resembles.
  • Ask a friend or family member for their opinion on your face shape.
  • Take a selfie and use a photo editing app to draw a line around your face. Compare the shape to the descriptions above.

FAQs About Face Shapes for Men

Here are some frequently asked questions about face shapes for men:

What is the most common face shape for men?

The most common face shape for men is a square face.

Can your face shape change over time?

No, your face shape will remain the same throughout your life. However, changes in weight, hair loss, or aging can alter the appearance of your face.

Can two people have the same face shape?

Yes, it is possible for two people to have the same face shape. However, facial features such as skin tone, eye color, and hair texture can vary widely.

Hair Care Tips for Different Face Shapes

Now that you know your face shape, you can choose a hairstyle that will flatter your features. Here are some hair care tips for different face shapes:

  • Oval: Almost any hairstyle will suit an oval face, so experiment with different lengths and styles.
  • Square: Short, neat haircuts work best on a square face. Avoid styles that are too long or messy.
  • Round: A hairstyle with height on top and short sides will elongate a round face. Avoid styles that add more width to the face.
  • Heart: A fringe or textured top can balance out a heart-shaped face. Avoid styles that are too short or spiky.
  • Diamond: A textured or messy style can soften the angles of a diamond-shaped face. Avoid styles that are too sharp or geometric.
  • Triangle: A hairstyle with volume on top and short sides can balance out a triangle-shaped face. Avoid styles that are too flat or slicked back.

By following these tips, you can find a hairstyle that enhances your face shape and makes you look your best.
