How To Cut Layers In Your Own Hair Brad Mondo A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle? Do you want to try something new without spending a lot of money at the salon? Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you've never done it before. But don't worry, with some guidance and practice, you can learn how to cut layers in your own hair like a pro, with the help of YouTube hair expert, Brad Mondo. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut layers in your own hair, along with some helpful tips and FAQs to ensure that you get the best results.

Description

Brad Mondo is a YouTube hair expert who has been sharing his knowledge and expertise with his audience for years. He has over 7 million subscribers and is known for his entertaining and informative hair tutorials. In this tutorial, he will show you how to cut layers in your own hair, which is a great way to add dimension and movement to your hair without having to go to the salon.

How to Cut Layers in Your Own Hair Brad Mondo

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting layers in your own hair is to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's important to use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut and avoid damaging your hair. You can purchase hair cutting scissors online or at a beauty supply store.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair, making it easier to cut. You can also use a hair straightener to straighten your hair if you have curly or wavy hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair down the middle and then from ear to ear, creating four sections. Clip the top section of hair away from the bottom section. This will make it easier to cut the layers and ensure that they are even.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom section of hair, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle finger and cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process for each section of hair, making sure to cut at an angle to create layers.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use your comb to blend them together. Comb your hair from the roots to the ends to make sure that the layers are even and blended nicely.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your layers, it's time to style your hair. You can use a hair straightener, curling iron, or hair products to create the look you want.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make sure the layers are even.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and go slowly to ensure a clean and even cut.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQ

Can I cut layers in my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. Just be sure to cut the hair when it's dry and use a hair pick to separate the curls while cutting.

How often should I cut my layers?

It's recommended to cut your layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your hair.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix it or wait for it to grow out. Remember, hair grows back!

Hair Care

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent damage and maintain healthy hair. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims and hair masks can also help keep your hair looking its best.

There you have it, a step-by-step guide on how to cut layers in your own hair like Brad Mondo. With some practice and patience, you can achieve a fresh new look without having to leave your home. Just remember to gather your tools, wash and dry your hair, section your hair, start cutting, blend the layers, and style your hair. Happy cutting!
