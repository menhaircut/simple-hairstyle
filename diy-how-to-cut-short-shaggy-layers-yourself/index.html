Diy How To Cut Short Shaggy Layers Yourself

Written by Frank Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a fun and easy way to change up your hairstyle, cutting short shaggy layers yourself might be just what you need. Not only is it an affordable option, but it's also a great way to express your creativity and personality. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps of cutting short shaggy layers yourself, and provide you with tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect look.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Short shaggy layers are a trendy and low-maintenance hairstyle that can be achieved by cutting different lengths of hair. The layers create texture and volume, making it a great option for those with thin or fine hair. Whether you want to add some edge to your look or just want to switch things up, cutting short shaggy layers yourself is a great way to do it.

How To

If you're new to cutting hair, it's important to start with a simple style and work your way up as you gain confidence. Short shaggy layers are a great option for beginners because they're forgiving and easy to customize. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle
  • Clips to section your hair

Step By Step

Follow these steps to achieve the perfect short shaggy layers:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. It's easier to cut hair when it's clean and dry.
  2. Section your hair. Use clips to separate your hair into four sections: top, back, and two sides.
  3. Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut into it at an angle. Repeat this step until you've cut all the hair in the back section.
  4. Move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair from the side and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut into it at an angle. Repeat this step until you've cut all the hair in the side sections.
  5. Move on to the top section. Take a small section of hair from the top and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut into it at an angle. Repeat this step until you've cut all the hair in the top section.
  6. Once you've cut all the sections, comb your hair out and check for any uneven spots. If you see any, just trim them up.
  7. Style your new shaggy layers however you like!

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect short shaggy layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to control and you'll be less likely to make a mistake.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and make your hair look uneven.
  • Don't cut too much at once. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring, so take a break if you start to feel fatigued.
  • Watch tutorial videos. There are plenty of tutorial videos on YouTube that can help you learn the technique.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short shaggy layers yourself:

Can I cut shaggy layers on curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may want to cut your hair a little longer than you want it to be.

How often should I cut my shaggy layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers lose their shape. As a general rule, you should aim to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape.

What if I mess up?

Don't worry! Hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed. If you mess up, you can always go to a professional stylist to fix your hair.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your short shaggy layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to maintain the health of your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your shaggy layers.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

We hope you found this guide helpful in learning how to cut short shaggy layers yourself. Remember to take your time and have fun with it!
