How To Curl Hair Backwards A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle every day? Why not try something new and curl your hair backwards? This style is perfect for those who want to add some volume and texture to their hair while still keeping it sleek and stylish. In this article, we will guide you through the steps on how to curl your hair backwards. Let's get started!

How to Curl Hair Backwards

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before curling your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair and applying a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the curling iron. Blow dry your hair until it's completely dry, and then brush it to remove any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into manageable pieces. Use hair clips to separate your hair into sections, starting from the back of your head and working your way to the front. The smaller the section, the tighter the curl will be.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair Backwards

Take a small section of hair and clamp it with the curling iron close to the roots. Twist the curling iron away from your face and hold for a few seconds. Release the curling iron and let the curl cool in your hand before letting it go. Repeat this process with the rest of your hair until all sections are curled.

Step 4: Brush Out the Curls

Once all your hair is curled, brush out the curls with a wide-toothed comb or your fingers. This will help loosen the curls and create a more natural, relaxed look.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

To keep your curls in place all day, finish with a light mist of hairspray. Hold the can about 10 inches away from your head and spray lightly all over your hair. This will help set the curls and keep them looking great all day.

Tips for Curling Hair Backwards

Curling your hair backwards can take a bit of practice to get it right. Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect look:

  • Use a curling iron with a small barrel for tighter curls, or a larger barrel for looser waves.
  • Don't curl all the way to the ends of your hair, as this can create a frizzy look. Instead, leave the ends straight or lightly curl them.
  • Be careful not to burn your scalp or ears with the curling iron. Use a heat protectant spray and always keep the iron moving to prevent overheating.
  • If you have thick or long hair, you may need to curl smaller sections at a time for best results.

FAQs about Curling Hair Backwards

1. How long do the curls last?

The length of time the curls last will depend on your hair type and the products you use. Generally, the curls should last at least a few hours or until your next wash.

2. Can I curl my hair backwards with a straightening iron?

Yes, you can use a straightening iron to curl your hair backwards. Simply clamp the straightening iron close to the roots, twist away from your face, and pull down the length of the hair to create a curl.

3. Will curling my hair backwards damage it?

Curling your hair with heat tools can cause damage over time, especially if you don't use a heat protectant spray. To minimize damage, use a low heat setting on your curling iron and limit the amount of time you spend curling each section.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

In addition to curling your hair backwards, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when it's wet.
  • Limit your use of heat tools like curling irons and straightening irons.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.

Now that you know how to curl your hair backwards, it's time to try it out for yourself! With a bit of practice and the right tools, you can create a stunning look that's sure to turn heads.
