How To Bring A Frontal Wig Back To Life Tips For Reviving Your Wig

Written by Daniel Jul 24, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you struggling with a lifeless frontal wig that no longer looks as good as it did when you first got it? Wigs are a great investment that can last for months or even years with proper care, but over time, they can lose their shine and bounce. In this article, we'll show you how to bring a frontal wig back to life and restore its beauty. Whether it's a synthetic or human hair wig, these tips will help you revive it and make it look like new again.

How to Bring a Frontal Wig Back to Life

There are several ways you can bring a frontal wig back to life, depending on its condition and the type of wig you have. Here are some tips on how to revive your wig:

1. Wash Your Wig

Washing your wig is the first step to restoring its beauty. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to clean it gently. Rinse it thoroughly with cold water and avoid rubbing it vigorously. This will help remove any dirt, dust, or oils that have accumulated on the wig, making it look dull and lifeless.

2. Detangle Your Wig

Detangling your wig can be a daunting task, especially if it's been neglected for a while. Use a wide-tooth comb or a wig brush to gently remove any knots or tangles. Start at the tips and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull too hard or damage the hair fibers. If the wig is severely tangled, you may need to soak it in a conditioning solution before detangling it.

3. Style Your Wig

Styling your wig can help bring it back to life and give it a fresh look. You can use a curling iron, flat iron, or hot rollers to add some waves, curls, or volume to your wig. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to avoid damaging the hair fibers. You can also try different hairstyles, such as a ponytail, bun, or braids, to create a new look.

4. Trim Your Wig

If your wig has lost its shape or length, you may need to trim it to revive it. You can do this yourself or take it to a professional hairstylist. Trim the ends to remove any split or damaged hair fibers and create a neat, fresh look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Bring a Frontal Wig Back to Life

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you bring your frontal wig back to life:

Step 1: Wash Your Wig

Fill a sink or basin with cold water and add a small amount of shampoo. Immerse your wig in the water and gently swirl it around for a few minutes. Rinse it thoroughly with cold water and repeat with conditioner. Rinse again and gently squeeze the excess water out of the wig. Avoid rubbing it vigorously or wringing it out.

Step 2: Detangle Your Wig

Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to gently remove any knots or tangles. Start at the tips and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull too hard or damage the hair fibers. If the wig is severely tangled, you may need to soak it in a conditioning solution before detangling it.

Step 3: Style Your Wig

Use a curling iron, flat iron, or hot rollers to add some waves, curls, or volume to your wig. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to avoid damaging the hair fibers. You can also try different hairstyles, such as a ponytail, bun, or braids, to create a new look.

Step 4: Trim Your Wig

If your wig has lost its shape or length, you may need to trim it to revive it. You can do this yourself or take it to a professional hairstylist. Trim the ends to remove any split or damaged hair fibers and create a neat, fresh look.

Tips for Bringing a Frontal Wig Back to Life

Here are some tips to help you bring your frontal wig back to life:

Tip 1: Use the Right Products

Use products that are specifically designed for wigs, such as mild shampoo, conditioner, and styling products. Avoid using regular hair products, as they may contain harsh ingredients that can damage the hair fibers.

Tip 2: Store Your Wig Properly

Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape and prevent tangles. Cover it with a satin or silk scarf or wig cap to protect it from dust, dirt, and moisture.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Damage

Avoid using high heat on your wig, as it can damage the hair fibers and cause them to become brittle and lifeless. Use a heat protectant spray when styling your wig with hot tools.

FAQs

1. Can You Revive a Synthetic Wig?

Yes, you can revive a synthetic wig by washing it, detangling it, and styling it. However, you should avoid using high heat on a synthetic wig, as it can melt the fibers and ruin the wig.

2. How Often Should You Wash Your Wig?

You should wash your wig every 6-8 wears, or as needed, depending on its condition and how often you wear it. Avoid washing it too frequently, as it can damage the hair fibers and shorten its lifespan.

3. Can You Dye a Wig to Bring It Back to Life?

You can dye a human hair wig to bring it back to life, but you should avoid dyeing a synthetic wig, as it can damage the fibers and ruin the wig. If you're not sure how to dye a wig, it's best to take it to a professional hairstylist.

Hair Care for Your Frontal Wig

Proper hair care is essential to maintaining the beauty and longevity of your frontal wig. Here are some tips to help you care for your wig:

1. Brush or Comb Your Wig Daily

Brush or comb your wig daily to prevent tangles and maintain its shape. Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to gently remove any knots or tangles.

2. Store Your Wig Properly

Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape and prevent tangles. Cover it with a satin or silk scarf or wig cap to protect it from dust, dirt, and moisture.

3. Avoid Excessive Heat

Avoid using high heat on your wig, as it can damage the hair fibers and cause them to become brittle and lifeless. Use a heat protectant spray when styling your wig with hot tools.

By following these tips, you can bring a frontal wig back to life and enjoy its beauty for months or even years to come. Remember to take good care of your wig, and it will reward you with a stunning, natural look that will turn heads wherever you go!
