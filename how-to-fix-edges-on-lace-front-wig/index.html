How To Fix Edges On Lace Front Wig

Written by Frank Nov 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with unruly edges on your lace front wig? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with getting their edges to lay flat and look natural. Thankfully, there are several things you can do to fix this issue. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect edge on your lace front wig.

Description

A lace front wig is a type of wig that has a sheer lace panel along the front hairline. This panel allows the wearer to create a natural-looking hairline by blending the wig with their own hair. However, getting the edges of the wig to look natural can be a challenge. Many factors can contribute to this issue, including the type of wig, the shape of your head, and the products you use.

How to Fix Edges on Lace Front Wig

Step 1: Clean Your Skin

The first step in fixing your edges is to clean your skin. This will remove any oils or dirt that may be preventing the wig adhesive from sticking properly. Use a gentle cleanser and warm water to wash your forehead and the area behind your ears. Rinse thoroughly and pat dry with a clean towel.

Step 2: Apply Wig Adhesive

Next, apply a small amount of wig adhesive to the edge of the lace front wig. Use a brush or your fingers to spread the adhesive evenly along the edge. Be sure to avoid getting any adhesive on your hair or skin. Wait a few seconds for the adhesive to become tacky.

Step 3: Place the Wig

Carefully place the wig on your head, making sure the edges are lined up with your natural hairline. Gently press the edge of the wig onto your skin, starting from the middle and working your way outwards. Use a comb or your fingers to smooth down any bumps or bubbles in the lace.

Step 4: Style Your Edges

Once the wig is in place, you can style your edges as desired. Use a small amount of edge control or gel to smooth down any flyaways or frizz. You can also use a scarf or headband to help hold the edges in place while the adhesive dries.

Tips

- Use a high-quality wig adhesive to ensure a strong hold.

- Avoid using too much adhesive, as this can cause the wig to lift or slide.

- Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type and the shape of your head.

FAQ

Q: How long will the wig stay in place?

A: The length of time the wig stays in place will depend on the type of adhesive used and how well it is applied. Generally, a good wig adhesive should hold for several days.

Q: Can I swim or shower with a lace front wig?

A: It is not recommended to swim or shower with a lace front wig, as this can cause the adhesive to loosen or the wig to become damaged. If you need to wash your hair, it is best to remove the wig first.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the look and feel of your lace front wig. Be sure to wash and condition the wig regularly, using products designed for synthetic or human hair wigs. Avoid using hot styling tools or excessive heat, as this can cause the wig to melt or become damaged. Store the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when not in use to help maintain its shape.
