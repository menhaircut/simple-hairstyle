How To Cut Your Long Hair Yourself At Home

Written by Bardi Jun 20, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive haircuts? Do you want to learn how to cut your own long hair at home? With a little bit of practice and some helpful tips, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut your long hair yourself at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Long Hair Yourself at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Long Hair at Home

How to Cut Your Long Hair Yourself at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, it’s important to gather the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair ties. Follow these steps to cut your long hair yourself at home:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It’s important to start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would, and then dry your hair thoroughly.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into four even parts: two at the front and two at the back. Tie each section with a hair tie to keep it separate.

Step 3: Cut the Back Sections

Start with one of the back sections. Take the hair tie out and comb the hair straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and then cut the hair in a straight line. Repeat this on the other back section.

Step 4: Cut the Front Sections

Move on to the front sections. Take out the hair ties and bring the hair to the front. Comb the hair straight and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Cut the hair in a straight line.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

After cutting all sections, check for evenness. Compare the length of each section to make sure they are even. If you notice any unevenness, trim the longer sections until they are even.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to cut your long hair yourself at home:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair ties. Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid split ends.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would, and then dry your hair thoroughly. Wet hair is more difficult to cut, and it’s harder to see the length and shape of the hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Step 4: Cut the Back Sections

Step 5: Cut the Front Sections

Move on to the front sections. Take out the hair ties and bring the hair to the front. Comb the hair straight and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Cut the hair in a straight line.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

After cutting all sections, check for evenness. Compare the length of each section to make sure they are even. If you notice any unevenness, trim the longer sections until they are even.

Tips for Cutting Your Long Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve a salon-worthy haircut when cutting your long hair at home:

  • Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid split ends.
  • Use a comb to section your hair evenly.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Take breaks to step back and check the length and shape of your hair.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you with hard-to-reach areas.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Cutting curly hair can be more difficult than cutting straight hair. It’s important to cut curly hair when it’s dry and to cut each curl individually to avoid unevenness.

What do I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don’t panic. You can always trim the longer sections to make them even. If the mistake is significant, you may need to visit a salon to fix it.

How often should I cut my long hair?

It’s recommended to cut your long hair every 8-12 weeks to avoid split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Long Hair

After cutting your long hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain healthy hair. Here are some tips for hair care after cutting your long hair:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid breakage and frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends.

Now that you know how to cut your long hair yourself at home, you can save money and achieve a salon-worthy haircut. With a little bit of practice and some helpful tips, you can become a pro at cutting your own hair. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting it to maintain healthy hair.
