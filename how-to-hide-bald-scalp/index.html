How To Hide Bald Scalp Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Robby Jul 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Is your bald scalp causing you some distress? Worry no more, as we've got you covered with our tips and tricks on how to hide bald scalp. Whether you're experiencing hair loss due to genetics, age, or illness, our guide will help you feel more confident and comfortable with your appearance. Below are some steps to follow and hair care tips to keep in mind.

How to Hide Bald Scalp

1. Use Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are a quick and easy way to conceal bald spots. They are made of keratin protein, which is the same material as your hair. Simply sprinkle the fibers onto your scalp, and they will attach to your existing hair, giving the appearance of fuller hair. Hair fibers come in a variety of colors to match your natural hair color. Make sure to choose the right color to avoid an obvious look.

2. Try a Hairpiece or Toupee

If you're comfortable with wearing a hairpiece, it can be a great way to hide bald spots. A toupee is a small hairpiece that can be attached to your scalp with adhesive. They come in different types and styles, so you can choose one that suits your preference. Make sure to choose a toupee that matches your hair color and texture.

3. Use Concealer

Concealer is a quick and easy way to hide bald spots. You can use a concealer that is specifically made for the scalp, or you can use a regular concealer that matches your skin tone. Apply the concealer directly to the bald spot, and blend it in with your fingers. Make sure to choose a concealer that is waterproof and sweat-resistant.

4. Style Your Hair

Styling your hair can be an effective way to hide bald spots. You can try a comb-over or a side-swept style to cover the bald spot. You can also try a slicked-back style, which can give the illusion of fuller hair. Make sure to use a good quality styling product to keep your hair in place.

Step by Step Guide to Hide Bald Scalp

Step 1: Choose Your Method

Decide which method you want to use to hide your bald scalp. Consider your comfort level, budget, and how much coverage you need. Hair fibers, hairpieces, and concealers are all great options to consider.

Step 2: Choose the Right Color

Make sure to choose a product that matches your natural hair color. If you have gray hair, choose a product that matches the shade of your gray hair. Choosing the wrong color can make your hair look fake and obvious.

Step 3: Apply the Product

Follow the instructions for your chosen method. For hair fibers, sprinkle the fibers onto your scalp, and use a comb to distribute them evenly. For hairpieces, attach the adhesive to the hairpiece, and press it onto your scalp. For concealers, apply the product directly to the bald spot, and blend it in with your fingers.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once you've applied the product, style your hair to cover the bald spot. Use a comb or brush to create the desired look. You can also use a styling product to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Hiding Bald Scalp

- Choose a product that is easy to apply and doesn't require a lot of maintenance.
- Avoid products that are heavy or greasy, as they can make your hair look oily.
- Choose a product that is waterproof and sweat-resistant, so it stays in place throughout the day.
- If you're using a hairpiece, make sure it fits properly and is comfortable to wear.
- Be patient and experiment with different products and styles to find what works best for you.

FAQs

Q: Can wearing a hat cause hair loss?

A: No, wearing a hat does not cause hair loss. However, wearing a hat that is too tight or wearing it for long periods of time can cause temporary hair loss from friction.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss. It is called telogen effluvium and occurs when stress causes hair follicles to shift into the resting phase, leading to hair loss.

Q: Can diet affect hair loss?

A: Yes, diet can affect hair loss. A diet that is low in protein, iron, and other nutrients can lead to hair loss. Make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and whole grains.

Hair Care for Bald Scalp

- Keep your scalp clean and moisturized.
- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free of harsh chemicals.
- Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage your hair.
- Protect your scalp from the sun by wearing a hat or using a sunscreen with a high SPF.
- Consider taking supplements like biotin and vitamin D to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips, tricks, and hair care advice, you can easily hide your bald scalp and feel more confident and comfortable with your appearance. Remember to choose a method that works best for you and experiment with different products and styles to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
