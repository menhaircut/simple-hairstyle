How To Cut Wavy Boy Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your child's hair can be a daunting task, especially if they have wavy hair. However, with a little bit of patience and practice, you can easily give your little one a fresh new haircut right at home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting wavy boy hair at home, from start to finish.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Wavy Boy Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Wavy Boy Hair at Home
  • FAQs

How to Cut Wavy Boy Hair at Home

Before you begin cutting your child's hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools, including a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's also a good idea to have a towel or cape to protect your child's clothing.

When it comes to cutting wavy boy hair, you'll want to start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut and style, so make sure your child's hair is completely dry before you begin.

Step 1: Section the Hair

Using your comb, divide your child's hair into sections. Start by creating a part down the middle of the head, then divide each side into two sections (front and back). Clip each section out of the way using hair clips.

Step 2: Trim the Bangs

Starting with the front section, comb the hair straight down and trim the bangs to the desired length. For wavy hair, it's best to cut the bangs a little longer than you want them, as the waves can cause the hair to bounce up and shorten once it's dry.

Step 3: Trim the Sides

Working on one side at a time, unclip the front section and comb the hair straight down. Using your scissors, trim the hair to the desired length, making sure to cut in a straight line. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Trim the Back

Unclip the back sections and comb the hair straight down. Starting at the nape of the neck, trim the hair to the desired length, making sure to cut in a straight line. Gradually work your way up the back of the head, making small snips as you go.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you have trimmed all sections of the hair, it's time to blend the layers together. Use your scissors to make small, vertical snips around the head, blending the layers together for a more natural look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a quick recap of the steps involved in cutting wavy boy hair at home:

  1. Section the hair
  2. Trim the bangs
  3. Trim the sides
  4. Trim the back
  5. Blend the layers

Tips for Cutting Wavy Boy Hair at Home

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting wavy boy hair at home:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use sharp scissors
  • Work in small sections
  • Cut hair a little longer than desired
  • Blend the layers for a natural look

FAQs

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It's recommended to cut your child's hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking neat and healthy.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! Hair grows back, and there are ways to minimize the damage. For example, if you accidentally cut too much hair, try blending the surrounding hair to make it look more natural.

Can I cut my child's hair when it's wet?

It's generally best to cut hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut and style.

Hair Care for Wavy Boy Hair

Once you've cut your child's hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for caring for wavy boy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep hair moisturized
  • Brush hair gently to avoid breaking the waves

Cutting wavy boy hair at home may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great-looking haircut right in the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, work in small sections, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
