What Causes Hair To Become Thinner

Written by Bardi Aug 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you noticing that your once thick and luscious locks are becoming thinner? You're not alone. Hair thinning is a common problem that affects both men and women. There are several reasons why hair can become thinner, and in this article, we will explore the most common causes.

Table of Contents

  • Genetics
  • Age
  • Hormonal Changes
  • Stress
  • Nutritional Deficiencies

Genetics

One of the most common causes of hair thinning is genetics. If your parents or grandparents had hair thinning or hair loss, it's likely that you will too. This type of hair loss is known as androgenetic alopecia, and it is caused by a combination of genes and hormones.

Age

As we age, our hair naturally becomes thinner. This is because the hair follicles shrink, and the hair that grows back is finer and less dense. Age-related hair thinning is more common in women than men, and it usually starts in the 40s or 50s.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also cause hair thinning. This is why women often experience hair thinning during pregnancy or menopause. Hormonal imbalances can also cause hair thinning in men, especially if they have high levels of DHT, a hormone that can cause hair follicles to shrink.

Stress

Stress can cause a number of health problems, including hair thinning. When we are under stress, our body produces cortisol, a hormone that can cause hair follicles to shrink. This can lead to hair thinning or hair loss.

Nutritional Deficiencies

Our hair needs certain nutrients to grow and stay healthy. If we don't get enough of these nutrients, our hair can become thin and brittle. Common nutritional deficiencies that can cause hair thinning include iron, zinc, and biotin.

Heat Styling

Using heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and hair dryers can damage the hair shaft and cause hair to become thin. The heat can weaken the hair, making it more prone to breakage and thinning. It's important to use heat styling tools sparingly and to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments like hair dye, perms, and relaxers can also damage the hair and cause it to become thin. These treatments can weaken the hair shaft, making it more prone to breakage and thinning. It's important to use these treatments sparingly and to always follow the instructions carefully.

Medical Conditions

There are several medical conditions that can cause hair thinning, including thyroid problems, autoimmune disorders, and scalp infections. If you are experiencing sudden hair thinning or hair loss, it's important to see a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Medications

Some medications can also cause hair thinning as a side effect. This includes medications used to treat high blood pressure, acne, and depression. If you are experiencing hair thinning while taking medication, it's important to speak to your doctor about alternative options.

Hair Care Practices

Finally, certain hair care practices can also cause hair to become thin. This includes brushing the hair too aggressively, wearing tight hairstyles like braids or ponytails, and using hair elastics that are too tight. It's important to be gentle with your hair and to use hair care products that are gentle and nourishing.

FAQ

What can I do to prevent hair thinning?

To prevent hair thinning, it's important to maintain a healthy diet, avoid heat styling and chemical treatments, and be gentle with your hair. You can also take supplements like biotin and zinc to support healthy hair growth.

What treatments are available for hair thinning?

There are several treatments available for hair thinning, including minoxidil, finasteride, and hair transplant surgery. It's important to speak to a doctor or dermatologist about which treatment is right for you.

Can stress cause hair thinning?

Yes, stress can cause hair thinning. When we are under stress, our body produces cortisol, a hormone that can cause hair follicles to shrink. This can lead to hair thinning or hair loss.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and prevent hair thinning, follow these tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Avoid heat styling and chemical treatments
  • Be gentle when brushing and styling your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Take supplements like biotin and zinc to support healthy hair growth
  • Avoid tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails
  • Use hair elastics that are gentle and don't pull on the hair

Conclusion

There are several reasons why hair can become thinner, from genetics to hormonal changes to heat styling and chemical treatments. By following a healthy hair care routine and being gentle with your hair, you can prevent hair thinning and keep your locks looking luscious and healthy.
