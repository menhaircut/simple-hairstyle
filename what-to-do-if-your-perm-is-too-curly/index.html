What To Do If Your Perm Is Too Curly A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Sep 23, 2023 · 6 min read

If you've recently gotten a perm and it's turned out to be much curlier than you expected, don't worry! While it may seem like a disaster at first, there are several things you can do to tame your curls and get the look you want. In this guide, we'll walk you through step by step on what to do if your perm is too curly.

How to Determine if Your Perm is Too Curly

Before you can fix your too-curly perm, you need to determine whether it's actually too curly or if it's just taking some time to settle. Here are a few signs that your perm might be too curly:

  • Your curls are very tight and bouncy
  • Your hair is frizzy and difficult to manage
  • Your hair feels dry and damaged
  • Your curls are too close together and there is no definition

If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, it's likely that your perm is too curly and needs some attention.

Why Did My Perm Turn Out Too Curly?

There are several reasons why your perm may have turned out too curly. Here are a few of the most common:

  • Your hair was too fine or too damaged to hold the perm
  • Your stylist left the perm solution on for too long
  • Your stylist used rods that were too small for your hair length or texture
  • Your hair was not properly neutralized after the perm

If you're unsure why your perm turned out too curly, talk to your stylist to see if they can help you identify the problem.

How to Fix Your Perm if It's Too Curly

If your perm is too curly, there are several things you can do to fix it. Here are a few options:

  • Get a haircut: If your curls are too tight and you're unhappy with the way they look, consider getting a haircut to remove some of the length and volume. This can help your curls look more manageable and defined.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo: A clarifying shampoo can help remove any excess product buildup or residue from your hair, which can help your curls look more defined and less frizzy.
  • Stretch your curls: You can stretch out your curls by pulling them gently while they're still wet or by using a hair dryer with a diffuser attachment.
  • Use a curling iron: If you want to loosen up your curls a bit, you can use a curling iron with a large barrel to create bigger, looser curls.
  • Get a reverse perm: A reverse perm is a process where your stylist will use a different type of perm solution to loosen up your curls and make them more manageable.

Step-By-Step Guide to Fixing a Too-Curly Perm

Here's a step-by-step guide to fixing a too-curly perm:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or residue.
  2. Towel dry your hair gently, then apply a curl-defining product to your hair while it's still damp.
  3. Use a hair dryer with a diffuser attachment to dry your hair, using a low heat setting.
  4. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron with a large barrel to create bigger, looser curls.
  5. If you want to stretch out your curls even more, gently pull on them while they're still warm from the curling iron.
  6. Finish off with a light hairspray to help hold your curls in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Perm After Fixing It

Once you've fixed your too-curly perm, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep your curls looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment to keep your hair moisturized and healthy
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz and breakage
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

FAQs About Fixing a Too-Curly Perm

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing a too-curly perm:

How long should I wait before fixing my too-curly perm?

It's generally best to wait at least a week or two before trying to fix your too-curly perm. This will give your hair time to settle and adjust to the perm solution.

Can I fix my too-curly perm at home?

While it's possible to fix your too-curly perm at home, it's generally best to seek the help of a professional stylist. They will have the knowledge and experience to safely and effectively fix your perm without causing further damage to your hair.

Will fixing my too-curly perm damage my hair?

Fixing your too-curly perm shouldn't cause any additional damage to your hair, as long as you take good care of your hair afterwards. Be sure to use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair.

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Fixing a Too-Curly Perm

Here are a few common mistakes to avoid when fixing a too-curly perm:

  • Using too much product: Using too much product can weigh down your curls and make them look greasy or limp.
  • Using a high heat setting on your hair dryer: High heat can damage your hair and cause frizz, so be sure to use a low heat setting or a diffuser attachment.
  • Brushing your hair when it's dry: Brushing your hair when it's dry can cause frizz and breakage, so be sure to brush your hair when it's damp or use a wide-toothed comb.

The Importance of Hair Care After Getting a Perm

Getting a perm can be a great way to add volume and texture to your hair, but it's important to take good care of your hair afterwards. Be sure to use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair. And don't forget to get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

If your perm is too curly, don't worry! With a little patience and the right techniques, you can tame your curls and get the look you want. Whether you choose to get a haircut, use a clarifying shampoo, or try a reverse perm, there are several options for fixing a too-curly perm. Just be sure to take good care of your hair afterwards to keep your curls looking great.
