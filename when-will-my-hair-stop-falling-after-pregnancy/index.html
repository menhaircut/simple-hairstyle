When Will My Hair Stop Falling After Pregnancy Everything You Need To Know

Written by Eveline Oct 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you a new mom experiencing hair loss after pregnancy? Don't worry, you're not alone. Postpartum hair loss is a common issue among women. In fact, up to 50% of women may experience hair loss after giving birth. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about when your hair will stop falling after pregnancy and how to handle it.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Handle Hair Loss
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Hair Care
  5. FAQs

Description

Hair loss after pregnancy is caused by hormonal changes in the body. During pregnancy, high levels of estrogen prevent hair from shedding, resulting in thicker and fuller locks. However, after giving birth, estrogen levels drop, and the hair that was retained during pregnancy begins to fall out. This can happen anywhere from one to six months postpartum, and it can last for several months.

While postpartum hair loss can be alarming, it's important to note that it's a natural and temporary process. Your hair will eventually stop falling out, and new hair will grow in its place.

How to Handle Hair Loss

If you're experiencing hair loss after pregnancy, there are several things you can do to help manage it:

  • Be gentle with your hair: Avoid tight hairstyles, excessive brushing, and heat styling.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner: These products can help make your hair appear fuller.
  • Try hair-thickening products: There are several products on the market that claim to thicken hair, such as thickening sprays and mousses.
  • Consider a shorter haircut: A shorter haircut can make your hair appear fuller and more voluminous.

Tips for Hair Care

Here are some additional tips to help care for your hair during postpartum hair loss:

  • Eat a healthy diet: A diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth.
  • Take a hair supplement: Biotin, vitamin D, and iron are all supplements that can help promote healthy hair growth.
  • Stay hydrated: Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid stress: Stress can contribute to hair loss, so try to find ways to relax and manage stress.

FAQs

Q: When will my hair stop falling after pregnancy?

A: Hair loss after pregnancy can last for several months, but it will eventually stop. It typically occurs one to six months postpartum.

Q: Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after postpartum hair loss. It may take several months for new hair to grow in, but it will eventually return to its pre-pregnancy state.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

A: Breastfeeding can contribute to hair loss, as it can cause hormonal changes in the body. However, not all women experience hair loss while breastfeeding.

Remember that postpartum hair loss is a natural and temporary process. While it can be alarming, it's important to take care of your hair and know that it will eventually stop falling out. If you're concerned about your hair loss or if it's not improving, consult with your healthcare provider or a dermatologist.
