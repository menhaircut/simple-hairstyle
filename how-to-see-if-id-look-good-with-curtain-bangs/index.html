How To See If You d Look Good With Curtain Bangs

Written by Daniel Jul 23, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you want to try something new with your hair? Curtain bangs, also known as Bardot bangs, have been trending in recent years. They are a versatile hairstyle that can suit many face shapes and hair types. But how do you know if they'll look good on you? In this article, we'll give you some tips on how to see if you'd look good with curtain bangs.

Table of Contents

  1. Determine Your Face Shape
  2. Consider Your Hair Type
  3. Check Your Forehead Size
  4. Try Wigs or Virtual Try-Ons
  5. Hair Care Tips for Curtain Bangs
  6. FAQs

Determine Your Face Shape

The first thing to consider is your face shape. Curtain bangs can look good on most face shapes, but they're particularly flattering on oval and heart-shaped faces. If you have a round face, you might want to avoid them as they can make your face look wider. If you have a square face, you can still wear curtain bangs, but they should be longer and softer.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and look at the outline of your face. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Oval: your face is longer than it is wide, with a slightly curved jawline.
  • Round: your face is about the same length and width, with a rounded jawline.
  • Square: your face is about the same length and width, with a square jawline.
  • Heart: your face is wider at the temples and narrows at the jawline.
  • Diamond: your face is widest at the cheekbones, with a pointed chin and forehead.

Consider Your Hair Type

Curtain bangs can work with most hair types, but they're best suited for medium to thick hair. If you have fine hair, curtain bangs can make your hair look even thinner. They also require some maintenance, as they tend to get greasy faster than the rest of your hair. If you have oily hair, you might want to avoid them.

Check Your Forehead Size

Another thing to consider is your forehead size. Curtain bangs can look good on both large and small foreheads, but they can make a small forehead look even smaller. If you have a small forehead, you might want to opt for longer bangs that can be swept to the side.

Try Wigs or Virtual Try-Ons

If you're still not sure if you'd look good with curtain bangs, you can always try wigs or virtual try-ons. Many websites and apps offer virtual try-on features where you can upload a photo of yourself and see what you'd look like with different hairstyles. You can also try wigs in different styles and lengths to see what suits you best.

Hair Care Tips for Curtain Bangs

If you decide to go for curtain bangs, here are some hair care tips to keep them looking their best:

  • Wash them frequently: Curtain bangs tend to get greasy faster than the rest of your hair, so you might need to wash them more often.
  • Blow-dry them: Use a round brush and a blow dryer to style your curtain bangs. This will give them volume and shape.
  • Trim them regularly: Curtain bangs require regular trims to keep them looking neat and tidy.
  • Style them with products: Use some styling products like mousse or hairspray to keep your curtain bangs in place.

FAQs

Can I cut curtain bangs myself?

We recommend going to a professional hairstylist to get your curtain bangs cut. They can give you the right length and shape that suits your face shape and hair type.

How long should curtain bangs be?

The length of your curtain bangs depends on your face shape and forehead size. Generally, they should be between chin and cheekbone length.

Do curtain bangs require a lot of maintenance?

Curtain bangs require some maintenance, as they tend to get greasy faster than the rest of your hair. They also require regular trims to keep them looking neat and tidy.

Now that you know how to see if you'd look good with curtain bangs, you can decide if this is the hairstyle for you. Remember to consider your face shape, hair type, and forehead size before making the cut. With the right maintenance and styling, curtain bangs can be a chic and stylish addition to your look.
