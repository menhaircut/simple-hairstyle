How To Cut Your Own Hair With Wahl Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every few weeks? Do you want to learn how to cut your own hair with Wahl clippers? Look no further than this step-by-step guide!

Description

Cutting your own hair with Wahl clippers can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, anyone can do it. In this guide, we will break down the process into easy-to-follow steps and provide tips for achieving a professional-looking haircut at home.

How to

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Wahl clippers with guards in various sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Cape or towel to protect clothing

Once you have gathered your tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Choose the Right Guard

The key to a successful haircut with Wahl clippers is choosing the right guard. Different guards provide different lengths, so be sure to choose the one that will give you your desired length. If you're unsure, start with a longer guard and work your way down.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you begin cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will ensure an even cut and prevent damage to the clippers.

Step 3: Start with the Sides

Begin by using the clippers with the chosen guard to trim the sides of your hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using short, upward strokes. Be sure to keep the clippers level with your head to avoid creating uneven patches.

Step 4: Move to the Back

Once you've finished the sides, move to the back of your head. Use the same technique as before, starting at the bottom and working your way up.

Step 5: Trim the Top

Now it's time to tackle the top of your hair. Use scissors to trim any longer pieces, then switch back to the clippers with the appropriate guard. Start at the front and work your way to the back, using the comb to lift each section of hair and trim as necessary.

Step 6: Blend the Sides and Top

To create a seamless transition between the sides and top of your hair, blend the two together using the clippers without a guard. Use short, upward strokes to blend the hair together, being careful not to create any harsh lines.

Step 7: Check for Any Uneven Spots

Once you've finished the haircut, use a mirror to check for any uneven spots or missed areas. Make any necessary touch-ups with scissors or clippers.

Step 8: Clean Up

After you've finished cutting your hair, clean up any stray hairs with a brush or vacuum. Wash your clippers and store them in a safe place for future use.

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use the comb to lift each section of hair for a more precise cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to rest your arms or hands.
  • Invest in quality Wahl clippers for a more professional-looking cut.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair with Wahl clippers if I have long hair?

Yes, you can! Simply choose a longer guard and use scissors to trim any longer pieces before using the clippers.

Do I need to wet my hair before cutting it with Wahl clippers?

No, it's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly and can damage your clippers.

How often should I cut my hair with Wahl clippers?

It depends on your hair type and desired style. Generally, it's best to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair with Wahl clippers, be sure to wash and condition it to keep it healthy and shiny. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner and avoid using heat styling tools too often.

Cutting your own hair with Wahl clippers can be a rewarding experience and save you money in the long run. With the right tools and techniques, anyone can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Give it a try and see the results for yourself!
