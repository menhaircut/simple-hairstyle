Low Fade Haircut Long Curly Hair The Perfect Combination

Written by Oliver Sep 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have long curly hair, you might think that a fade haircut is not for you. However, a low fade haircut can be the perfect choice for those with curly locks who want to keep their hair well-groomed and stylish. In this article, we will show you how to achieve the perfect low fade haircut for long curly hair, including tips and hair care advice.

Description

A low fade haircut is a hairstyle that gradually fades from longer hair on the top to shorter hair on the sides and back. The fade can start at different lengths, but for long curly hair, a low fade is the best choice. This is because it creates a more natural and gradual transition from the long curls on top to the shorter hair on the sides and back.

How to

If you want to achieve the perfect low fade haircut for your long curly hair, follow these steps:

Step 1: Find a Good Barber

The first step is to find a good barber who has experience with curly hair. Ask around for recommendations or do some research online to find a barber in your area who specializes in curly haircuts. It's important to find someone who understands the unique challenges of cutting curly hair and can give you the best advice.

Step 2: Discuss Your Options

Once you have found a good barber, discuss your options for a low fade haircut. Your barber can recommend the best fade length and style for your hair type and face shape. You can also bring in pictures of hairstyles you like to give your barber a better idea of what you are looking for.

Step 3: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before your haircut, make sure to wash and condition your hair. This will help to remove any product buildup and make your hair easier to work with. Use a conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair to help define your curls and reduce frizz.

Step 4: Cut the Top of Your Hair

The next step is to cut the top of your hair to the desired length. Your barber may use scissors or a razor to achieve the best results. It's important to communicate with your barber throughout the process to ensure that you get the look you want.

Step 5: Blend the Sides and Back

After cutting the top of your hair, your barber will start to blend the sides and back of your hair with the clippers. The fade will gradually get shorter as it moves down your head, creating a natural and seamless transition from the long curls on top to the short hair on the sides and back.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

After your haircut, style your hair with a product that is designed for curly hair. This will help to define your curls and keep them in place throughout the day. You can also use a diffuser on your hair dryer to help enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain your low fade haircut:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to help preserve your hair color and reduce frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut and prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap when swimming.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about low fade haircuts for long curly hair:

Can I get a low fade if I have thinning hair?

Yes, you can still get a low fade if you have thinning hair. Your barber can adjust the length of the fade to suit your hair type and create a more natural look.

How often should I get a haircut to maintain my low fade?

You should get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape of your low fade and prevent split ends.

What products should I use to style my curly hair?

You should use a product that is specifically designed for curly hair, such as a curl defining cream or gel. Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it frizzy.

Hair Care

Maintaining long curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right care and styling products, you can keep your hair healthy and well-groomed. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to help preserve your hair color and reduce frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut and prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap when swimming.

By following these tips and getting a low fade haircut, you can achieve the perfect combination of style and functionality for your long curly hair.
