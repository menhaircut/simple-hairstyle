How To Cut Your Hair Shorter At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Whether you're looking to save money, avoid the hassle of going to a hair salon, or simply need a quick trim, cutting your hair at home can be a great option. In this post, we'll show you how to cut your hair shorter at home in a relaxed and easy-to-understand way. We'll cover everything from how to prepare your hair, to what tools you'll need, to how to style your new shorter do. So, let's get started!

How to Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. This means washing and drying it thoroughly, so that it's clean and free of tangles. You should also make sure that your hair is completely dry before you start cutting, as wet hair can be more difficult to work with and may result in uneven cuts.

What Tools You'll Need

To cut your hair at home, you'll need a few basic tools, including:

  • A pair of sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror

You may also want to use hair clips or elastics to section off your hair, and a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp while you're cutting.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into manageable sections. Use hair clips or elastics to separate your hair into four equal sections: two in the front and two in the back. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 2: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the back of your hair first. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, making sure to cut straight across. Repeat this process until you've cut all of the hair in the back of your head.

Next, move on to the sides of your head. Use your comb to comb the hair straight down, and then cut it at an angle, making sure to keep the hair as straight as possible. Work your way around your head, cutting small sections of hair at a time.

Step 3: Trim the Ends

Once you've cut your hair to the desired length, use your scissors to trim any uneven ends. Hold your hair between your fingers and snip off any straggly ends, making sure to cut straight across.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. Use your comb to part your hair in your desired style, and then use your fingers to shape and style your hair. You can add some hairspray or styling product if you'd like, but keep in mind that less is often more when it comes to styling short hair.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair at home:

  • Start with small cuts: It's always better to start with small cuts and work your way up, rather than cutting too much off at once.
  • Take your time: Cutting your hair can be a time-consuming process, so make sure you have enough time set aside to do it properly.
  • Be patient: If you're new to cutting your hair, it may take some time to get the hang of it. Don't get discouraged if your first few attempts aren't perfect.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Cut My Hair with Regular Scissors?

While it's possible to cut your hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Hair cutting scissors are specifically designed to cut hair cleanly and evenly, whereas regular scissors can damage your hair and result in uneven cuts.

How Often Should I Cut My Hair?

How often you should cut your hair depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. As a general rule, most people should aim to cut their hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking healthy and well-maintained.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. This means washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a heat protectant if you use hot tools, and avoiding harsh chemicals or treatments that can damage your hair.

By following these simple steps, you can successfully cut your hair shorter at home and achieve the look you want. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
