What Color Blush Looks Good On Medium Skin

Written by Daniel Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to makeup, blush is one of the most important products for achieving a glowing, healthy-looking complexion. But with so many different shades and formulas available, it can be difficult to know which color blush looks good on medium skin. In this article, we'll explore how to choose the right blush for your skin tone and provide some tips for achieving a flawless finish.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Right Blush
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Applying Blush
  • Expert Tips for Flawless Blush Application

Description

Medium skin tones can range from warm to cool, and it's important to choose a blush shade that complements your undertones. If you have warm undertones, look for blushes with peach, coral, or bronze tones. If you have cool undertones, opt for blushes with pink, berry, or mauve tones. Neutral undertones can wear a variety of shades, so experiment to find the perfect match.

How to Choose the Right Blush

When shopping for blush, it's important to swatch the product on your skin to see how it looks in natural light. You should also consider the formula of the blush. Cream and liquid blushes tend to look more natural on medium skin tones, while powder blushes can sometimes appear too harsh. Matte and satin finishes are great for daytime looks, while shimmery and glittery finishes can add a glamorous touch for evening wear.

Some popular blush shades for medium skin tones include:

  • Peach
  • Coral
  • Bronze
  • Pink
  • Berry
  • Mauve

Step-by-Step Guide to Applying Blush

Now that you've chosen the perfect blush shade, it's time to learn how to apply it for a flawless finish. Follow these simple steps:

  1. Start by applying foundation and concealer to even out your skin tone.
  2. Use a fluffy blush brush to apply the blush to the apples of your cheeks.
  3. Blend the blush upwards and outwards towards your temples.
  4. If you've applied too much blush, use a clean, fluffy brush to blend out the excess.
  5. Finish with a setting spray to lock in your makeup.

Expert Tips for Flawless Blush Application

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve a flawless blush application:

  • Use a light hand when applying blush, and build up the color gradually.
  • Blend, blend, blend! This will help prevent harsh lines and create a seamless finish.
  • Consider your hairstyle when choosing a blush shade. If you're wearing your hair down, a warm peach or coral shade can complement your locks. If you're wearing your hair up, a cool pink or berry shade can add a pop of color to your complexion.

FAQs

Is cream blush better than powder blush for medium skin tones?

Cream and liquid blushes tend to look more natural on medium skin tones, but powder blushes can also work well if applied with a light hand and blended well.

What's the best way to apply blush?

Use a fluffy blush brush to apply the blush to the apples of your cheeks, then blend upwards and outwards towards your temples. Use a clean brush to blend out any excess.

What's the difference between matte and shimmery blush?

Matte blushes have a flat finish with no shimmer or glitter, while shimmery blushes have a luminous finish with added sparkle.

How Blush Can Complement Your Hairstyle

Your hairstyle can have a big impact on how your blush looks. If you're wearing your hair down, a warm peach or coral shade can complement your locks. If you're wearing your hair up, a cool pink or berry shade can add a pop of color to your complexion. Consider your hairstyle when choosing a blush shade to achieve a cohesive, polished look.

Choosing the right blush shade for medium skin tones can be a game-changer for achieving a healthy, glowing complexion. Follow these tips and tricks to find the perfect shade and apply it flawlessly for a natural, radiant finish.
