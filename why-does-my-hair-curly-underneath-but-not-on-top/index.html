Why Does My Hair Curly Underneath But Not On Top

Have you ever wondered why your hair looks curly underneath but straight on top? This is a common problem that many people face, and it can be frustrating to deal with. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide some tips on how to manage it.

Reasons Why Hair is Curly Underneath But Not On Top

There are several reasons why hair can be curly underneath but not on top:

1. Genetics

Genetics plays a significant role in determining hair type, texture, and curl pattern. If your parents or grandparents have curly hair, there is a high chance that you will have curly hair as well. However, the curl pattern may not be the same all over your head, which can explain why your hair is curly underneath but not on top.

2. Hair Length

The length of your hair can also influence its curl pattern. Hair that is shorter tends to be curlier because the curls have less weight to pull them down. On the other hand, longer hair may be straighter on top because the curls have more weight to pull them down, resulting in less volume and curl.

3. Hair Care Products

The type of hair care products you use can also affect your hair's curl pattern. If you use products that are too heavy or contain ingredients that weigh down your hair, it can result in straighter hair on top and curlier hair underneath.

4. Heat Styling

Heat styling can also affect your hair's curl pattern. If you use heat tools such as flat irons or curling irons on a regular basis, it can cause damage to your hair, resulting in straighter hair on top and curlier hair underneath.

How to Manage Curly Hair Underneath

Managing curly hair underneath can be challenging, but there are several things you can do to make it more manageable:

1. Use the Right Hair Products

Using the right hair products is essential for managing curly hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl defining cream or mousse. These products can help enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

2. Detangle Your Hair Gently

When detangling your hair, be gentle and use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. Avoid using a brush, as it can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, using a detangling spray if necessary.

3. Style Your Hair When Wet

Curly hair is easier to style when it's wet, so try to style your hair after washing it. Apply your hair products to damp hair and scrunch your hair upward to enhance your curls.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Curly Hair Underneath

Here is a step-by-step guide to styling curly hair underneath:

Step 1:

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that is designed for curly hair.

Step 2:

Apply a curl defining cream or mousse to your damp hair and distribute it evenly with your fingers.

Step 3:

Scrunch your hair upward to enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

Step 4:

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer and dry your hair on a low heat setting. Alternatively, let your hair air dry.

Step 5:

Once your hair is dry, use a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz.

Tips for Managing Curly Hair Underneath

Here are some additional tips for managing curly hair underneath:

1. Sleep with a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce frizz and prevent your hair from getting tangled while you sleep.

2. Avoid Touching Your Hair

Avoid touching your hair throughout the day, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your curl pattern.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help prevent split ends and keep your curls looking healthy and bouncy.

FAQs About Curly Hair Underneath

1. Can I Straighten My Hair on Top?

Yes, you can straighten your hair on top if you prefer a straighter look. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis, as this can cause damage to your hair.

2. Can I Get Perm to Make My Hair Curlier on Top?

Yes, you can get a perm to make your hair curlier on top. However, be sure to go to a professional stylist who specializes in curly hair to avoid damage to your hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some general tips for hair care for curly hair:

1. Use Sulfate-Free Shampoo and Conditioner

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Look for shampoo and conditioner that are sulfate-free and designed for curly hair.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning can help nourish your hair and keep it hydrated. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, leaving it on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing it off.

3. Avoid Using Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools can cause damage to your hair, resulting in split ends and breakage. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using them on a daily basis.

In conclusion, having curly hair underneath but not on top is a common problem that many people face. However, with the right hair care products and styling techniques, you can manage your curly hair and keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
