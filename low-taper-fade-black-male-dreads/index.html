Low Taper Fade Black Male Dreads A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Nov 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a black male looking to rock some stylish dreadlocks with a low taper fade? This look is a popular choice for men who want to keep their hair neat and tidy while still having a unique and trendy style. In this guide, we'll give you all the information you need to achieve the perfect low taper fade black male dreads.

Description

Low taper fade black male dreads are a combination of two popular hairstyles: dreadlocks and a low taper fade. The hairstyle is characterized by a short fade on the sides and back of the head, with the hair on top and in the back left to grow into long dreadlocks. The low taper fade helps to keep the hairstyle neat and clean, while the dreads add a unique texture and style.

How to Achieve Low Taper Fade Black Male Dreads

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to achieve the perfect low taper fade black male dreads:

Step 1: Grow Your Hair Out

The first step in achieving this hairstyle is to grow your hair out. You'll need at least 6-8 inches of hair on top and in the back to create the dreadlocks. Be patient, as it may take several months to a year to grow out your hair to the desired length.

Step 2: Choose Your Taper Fade

The next step is to choose the type of taper fade you want. A low taper fade starts just above the ear and gradually fades into the hairline. You can also choose a high taper fade or a mid taper fade, depending on your preference.

Step 3: Visit a Barber

Visit a barber who has experience with creating low taper fade haircuts. Show them a picture of the style you want and discuss any specific details or preferences you have.

Step 4: Create the Dreadlocks

Once you have the taper fade, it's time to create the dreadlocks. This can be done by backcombing, twisting, or using a crochet hook. You can also use dread wax to help the dreads stay in place and maintain their shape.

Tips for Maintaining Low Taper Fade Black Male Dreads

Maintaining your low taper fade black male dreads requires some effort and care. Here are some tips to keep your hairstyle looking fresh:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy. Use a shampoo specifically designed for dreadlocks to avoid buildup and maintain the integrity of your dreads.

Tip 2: Moisturize Your Scalp

Use a natural oil, such as coconut oil or jojoba oil, to moisturize your scalp and prevent itchiness and dryness.

Tip 3: Retwist Your Dreads

Retwist your dreads regularly to maintain their shape and prevent them from unraveling. This can be done every 4-6 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows.

FAQ

How long does it take to create low taper fade black male dreads?

It can take several hours to create the taper fade and several more hours to create the dreadlocks. The entire process can take anywhere from 4-8 hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

How often should I wash my hair with dreadlocks?

You should wash your hair with dreadlocks every 1-2 weeks to avoid buildup and maintain the integrity of your dreads.

Can I style my dreads in different ways?

Yes, you can style your dreads in different ways, such as tying them back, braiding them, or wearing them loose. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Hair Care Products for Low Taper Fade Black Male Dreads

Here are some recommended hair care products for low taper fade black male dreads:

  • Dreadlock shampoo
  • Natural oils, such as coconut oil, jojoba oil, or argan oil
  • Dread wax
  • A detangling brush or comb

With these tips and products, you'll be able to achieve and maintain the perfect low taper fade black male dreads. Rock your unique style with confidence and pride!
