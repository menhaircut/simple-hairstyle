Why Does Only The Top Of The Head Go Bald

Written by Petter Jul 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

As we age, hair loss is a common problem that affects both men and women. However, it is often noticed that only the top of the head goes bald while the sides and back remain unaffected. This can be a cause of worry for many individuals who wish to maintain a full head of hair throughout their lives. In this blog post, we will explore the reasons behind why only the top of the head goes bald and what you can do to prevent or slow down hair loss.

Genetic Factors

One of the primary reasons why only the top of the head goes bald is due to genetic factors. Male and female pattern baldness, also known as androgenetic alopecia, is an inherited condition that affects the hair follicles on the scalp. This type of hair loss is more common in men and is characterized by a receding hairline and baldness on the top of the head. Women may also experience hair thinning on the crown of the head, but it is less noticeable than in men.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also contribute to hair loss on the top of the head. Dihydrotestosterone (DHT) is a hormone that is responsible for shrinking hair follicles, which can lead to hair loss. As we age, our bodies produce more DHT, which can affect the hair on the top of our heads. Hormonal changes during pregnancy, menopause, or thyroid disorders can also cause hair loss on the top of the head.

Age

Age is another factor that can contribute to hair loss on the top of the head. As we get older, our hair follicles shrink and produce thinner, weaker hair. This can lead to hair loss or baldness on the top of the head. Men are more likely to experience age-related hair loss than women, although women may also experience hair thinning with age.

Stress

Stress can also contribute to hair loss on the top of the head. When we are under stress, our bodies produce cortisol, a hormone that can affect the hair growth cycle. Cortisol can disrupt the natural hair growth cycle and cause hair to fall out prematurely, leading to baldness on the top of the head.

Poor Nutrition

Poor nutrition can also contribute to hair loss on the top of the head. Our hair needs a variety of vitamins and nutrients, such as biotin, iron, and zinc, to grow and stay healthy. If we do not consume enough of these nutrients, our hair may become weaker and more prone to falling out.

Medical Conditions

Medical conditions such as alopecia areata, scalp infections, and autoimmune disorders can also cause hair loss on the top of the head. These conditions can affect the hair follicles and cause hair to fall out in patches or all over the scalp.

Mechanical Factors

Mechanical factors such as tight hairstyles, constant pulling or tugging on the hair, or wearing hats or helmets frequently can also cause hair loss on the top of the head. This type of hair loss is called traction alopecia and can be prevented by avoiding tight hairstyles or frequent pulling on the hair.

Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments such as hair dyes, relaxers, and perms can also contribute to hair loss on the top of the head. These treatments can damage the hair follicles and cause hair to fall out. Avoiding these treatments or using them sparingly can help prevent hair loss on the top of the head.

Hair Care Practices

Improper hair care practices such as using hot styling tools frequently, not washing the hair regularly, or using harsh shampoos or conditioners can also contribute to hair loss on the top of the head. Using gentle hair care products, washing the hair regularly, and avoiding hot styling tools can help prevent hair loss on the top of the head.

Prevention and Treatment

Preventing hair loss on the top of the head involves maintaining a healthy lifestyle, avoiding tight hairstyles, using gentle hair care products, and avoiding harsh chemical treatments. If you are experiencing hair loss on the top of your head, there are several treatment options available, such as medications, hair transplants, or scalp micropigmentation. Consult with a medical professional to determine which treatment option is best for you.

FAQ

Why does only the top of the head go bald?

Several factors can contribute to hair loss on the top of the head, including genetic factors, hormonal changes, age, stress, poor nutrition, medical conditions, mechanical factors, and chemical treatments.

Can hair loss on the top of the head be prevented?

Preventing hair loss on the top of the head involves maintaining a healthy lifestyle, avoiding tight hairstyles, using gentle hair care products, and avoiding harsh chemical treatments.

What treatment options are available for hair loss on the top of the head?

Treatment options for hair loss on the top of the head include medications, hair transplants, or scalp micropigmentation. Consult with a medical professional to determine which treatment option is best for you.

Conclusion

Now that you know why only the top of the head goes bald, you can take steps to prevent or slow down hair loss. Maintaining a healthy lifestyle, avoiding tight hairstyles, using gentle hair care products, and avoiding harsh chemical treatments can all help prevent hair loss on the top of the head. If you are experiencing hair loss, consult with a medical professional to determine the best treatment option for you.
