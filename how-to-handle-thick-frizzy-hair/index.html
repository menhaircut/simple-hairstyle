How To Handle Thick Frizzy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Aug 16, 2023 · 4 min read
If you have thick frizzy hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. From taming flyaways to avoiding breakage, it can feel like there's always something to worry about. But fear not! With the right techniques and products, you can get your hair under control and achieve the sleek, smooth locks you've always wanted. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about how to handle thick frizzy hair.

Description

Thick frizzy hair is characterized by strands that are coarse, rough, and prone to breakage. It can be difficult to style and often requires a lot of maintenance to keep it looking its best. If you have thick frizzy hair, you may struggle with dryness, frizz, and flyaways. But with a few simple changes to your hair care routine, you can transform your unruly locks into a sleek and shiny mane.

How to Handle Thick Frizzy Hair

Step 1: Choose the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

The first step in taming thick frizzy hair is to choose the right shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are designed to hydrate and nourish your strands, such as those that contain natural oils like argan or coconut. Avoid shampoos and conditioners that contain harsh chemicals like sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and exacerbate frizz.

Step 2: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

In addition to using a hydrating shampoo and conditioner, it's also important to use a deep conditioning treatment on a regular basis. This will help to nourish your hair from the inside out, reducing frizz and improving overall texture. Look for a treatment that contains ingredients like shea butter or keratin, which are known for their moisturizing and strengthening properties.

Step 3: Invest in a Good Hairbrush

When it comes to styling thick frizzy hair, the right hairbrush can make all the difference. Look for a brush with natural bristles, which will help to distribute your hair's natural oils and prevent breakage. Avoid brushes with synthetic bristles, as these can cause static and exacerbate frizz.

Step 4: Use a Heat Protectant

If you use heat styling tools like a curling iron or straightener, it's important to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage and breakage. Look for a product that contains ingredients like argan oil or keratin, which will help to nourish and protect your hair while you style.

Tips for Handling Thick Frizzy Hair

- Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and damage. - Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair, as these materials are gentler on strands than traditional towels. - Consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage. - Experiment with different hairstyles, like braids or updos, to find a look that works for you. - Don't be afraid to use styling products like mousse or hairspray to help control frizz and flyaways.

FAQ

What causes thick frizzy hair?

Thick frizzy hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, humidity, and damage from heat styling tools.

What are some common mistakes people make when trying to handle thick frizzy hair?

Some common mistakes include using harsh shampoos and conditioners, brushing hair when it's wet, and using heat styling tools without a heat protectant.

Can you permanently straighten thick frizzy hair?

Yes, there are a variety of treatments available that can permanently straighten thick frizzy hair, such as Brazilian keratin treatments or Japanese straightening.

Hair Care for Thick Frizzy Hair

In addition to following the steps and tips outlined above, it's important to take good care of your hair on a daily basis. This includes avoiding excessive heat styling, using gentle hair ties, and protecting your hair from the elements. By following a consistent hair care routine and using the right products, you can keep your thick frizzy hair looking healthy and beautiful.
