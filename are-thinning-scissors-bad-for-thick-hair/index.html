Are Thinning Scissors Bad For Thick Hair

Written by Daniel Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning scissors are a popular tool used by hairstylists to create more texture and volume in hair. However, some people with thick hair wonder if using thinning scissors is a good idea. In this blog post, we will explore whether thinning scissors are bad for thick hair and provide tips on how to use them properly.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Thinning Scissors on Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Thinning scissors are designed to remove some of the bulk from thick hair, making it easier to style and manage. They work by cutting some of the hairs without removing their entire length. This technique creates a more natural, blended look and prevents the hair from looking too bulky or heavy. However, using thinning scissors on thick hair can be tricky and requires some skill and knowledge.

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Thick Hair

Using thinning scissors on thick hair requires some practice and knowledge of the technique. Here are some tips on how to use them properly:

1. Choose the Right Scissors

Not all thinning scissors are the same. Some are designed for fine hair, while others are better suited for thick hair. Look for scissors with larger teeth and wider gaps between them. This will allow you to remove more hair at once without damaging the hair.

2. Section the Hair

Before you start thinning the hair, divide it into sections. This will make it easier to work with and prevent you from accidentally removing too much hair. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

3. Start at the Bottom

When thinning the hair, start at the bottom and work your way up. This will ensure that you remove the right amount of hair and avoid creating any unevenness. Use the scissors to cut a few hairs at a time, working in a diagonal motion.

4. Don't Overdo It

Thinning scissors should be used sparingly, especially on thick hair. Removing too much hair can make the hair look thin and stringy. Be conservative and only remove a small amount of hair at a time.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning scissors on thick hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before you start thinning the hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to work with and can cause the scissors to slip.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to work with and prevent you from accidentally removing too much hair.

Step 3: Start at the Bottom

Start at the bottom of the hair and work your way up. Use the thinning scissors to cut a few hairs at a time, working in a diagonal motion. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Blend the hair by running your fingers through it. This will help to create a more natural, blended look.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Style the hair as desired using a blow dryer, straightener, or curling iron.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors on Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for using thinning scissors on thick hair:

  • Use the scissors sparingly to avoid removing too much hair.
  • Make sure the scissors are sharp to prevent damage to the hair.
  • Practice on a small section of hair before thinning the entire head.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on already thin or damaged hair.

FAQs

Can Thinning Scissors Damage Thick Hair?

Thinning scissors can damage thick hair if they are not used properly. Removing too much hair can make the hair look thin and stringy. It is important to use the scissors sparingly and only remove a small amount of hair at a time.

Can Thinning Scissors Make Thick Hair Thinner?

Thinning scissors can make thick hair look thinner if too much hair is removed. It is important to use the scissors sparingly and only remove a small amount of hair at a time.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for thick hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner designed for thick hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

In conclusion, thinning scissors can be a great tool for creating more texture and volume in thick hair. However, it is important to use them properly and sparingly to avoid damaging the hair. With the right technique and care, thinning scissors can be a useful tool for achieving the perfect hairstyle.
