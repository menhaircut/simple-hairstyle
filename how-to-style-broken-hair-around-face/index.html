How To Style Broken Hair Around Face Tips And Tricks

Broken hair around the face can be a frustrating issue to deal with. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including heat damage, chemical treatments, and excessive styling. But don't worry, there are plenty of ways to style your hair and make it look fabulous! In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to style broken hair around the face.

  1. How to Style Broken Hair Around Face
  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Style Broken Hair Around Face

Styling broken hair around the face can be tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, you can make it work. Here are some tips on how to style broken hair:

1. Use a Heat Protectant

If you're using heat on your hair, make sure to use a heat protectant spray or cream. This will help protect your hair from further damage and breakage.

2. Choose the Right Hairstyle

Choose a hairstyle that works well with your broken hair. Avoid styles that require a lot of pulling or tugging, as this can cause more breakage. Instead, opt for loose, natural-looking styles that don't require a lot of heat or products.

3. Use the Right Products

Use products that are specifically designed for broken hair. Look for products that contain keratin or other strengthening ingredients. Avoid products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

4. Be Gentle When Brushing

When brushing your hair, be gentle and use a wide-tooth comb. Avoid using a brush that pulls or tugs at your hair, as this can cause more breakage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style broken hair around the face:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. If you need to wash your hair, use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for broken hair.

2. Apply a Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or cream to your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas that are particularly damaged.

3. Choose Your Style

Choose a style that works well with your broken hair. This could be a loose, wavy style, a messy bun, or a simple ponytail. Avoid styles that require a lot of pulling or tugging.

4. Use the Right Tools

Use the right tools for your chosen style. For example, if you're doing a messy bun, you'll need bobby pins and a hair elastic. If you're doing loose waves, you'll need a curling wand or iron.

5. Be Gentle When Styling

Be gentle when styling your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging, and use a light touch when applying products or using tools.

Tips for Styling Broken Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling broken hair:

1. Don't Overdo It with Heat

Avoid using heat on your hair every day. This can cause further damage and breakage. Instead, opt for heatless styles or use heat sparingly.

2. Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. This will also help prevent further breakage.

3. Use Hair Masks

Use hair masks or deep conditioning treatments to nourish your hair and keep it healthy. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, protein, or oils.

FAQs

Q: Can I still use heat on my broken hair?

A: Yes, but use heat sparingly and always use a heat protectant spray or cream.

Q: Should I cut my broken hair?

A: It depends on the severity of the damage. If your hair is severely damaged, it may be best to cut it and start fresh. If it's just a few split ends, regular trims should suffice.

Q: How can I prevent further breakage?

A: Use gentle products, avoid over-styling, and get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain natural ingredients.

2. Avoid Chemical Treatments

Avoid chemical treatments like perms, relaxers, and coloring. These can damage your hair and cause breakage.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays. This can help prevent dryness and breakage.

Styling broken hair around the face can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can make it work. Use these tips and tricks to keep your hair healthy and looking fabulous!
