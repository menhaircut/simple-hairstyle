How To Make Fine Hair Fluffy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your fine hair looking flat and lifeless? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people with fine hair struggle to achieve that full, fluffy look. But with the right techniques and products, it's possible to make your fine hair look voluminous and bouncy. In this article, we'll show you how to make fine hair fluffy in 10 easy steps.

Step 1: Start with a Clarifying Shampoo

The first step to making your fine hair fluffy is to start with a clean slate. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup from your hair. This will allow your hair to better absorb the products you use later on.

Recommended Products:

  • Living Proof Perfect Hair Day Dry Shampoo
  • Matrix Total Results Pro Solutionist Alternate Action Clarifying Shampoo
  • The Inkey List Zinc PCA Dandruff Shampoo

Step 2: Use a Volumizing Conditioner

Next, use a volumizing conditioner to add moisture to your hair without weighing it down. Apply the conditioner from mid-length to the ends of your hair, avoiding the roots.

Recommended Products:

  • Cake Beauty Full Bloom Volumizing Conditioner
  • Tigi Bed Head Fully Loaded Volume Conditioner
  • Biolage Volumebloom Conditioner

Step 3: Apply a Thickening Mousse

After towel-drying your hair, apply a thickening mousse to your roots. This will help to give your hair lift and hold throughout the day.

Recommended Products:

  • Oribe Royal Blowout Heat Styling Gel
  • Redken Pillow Proof Blow Dry Express Primer with Heat Protection
  • Redken Guts 10 Volumizing Spray Mousse

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Flip your head upside down and blow dry your hair in sections, starting from the roots. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help add volume and prevent frizz.

Step 5: Use a Round Brush

Using a round brush, curl the ends of your hair under to create a bouncy, voluminous effect. Make sure to lift your hair at the roots as you brush through each section.

Step 6: Tease Your Hair

For even more volume, tease your hair at the crown using a teasing comb. Be gentle and don't overdo it, as this can damage your hair.

Step 7: Spray with Texturizing Spray

To add texture and hold to your hair, spray it with a texturizing spray. This will help prevent your hair from falling flat throughout the day.

Recommended Products:

  • Oribe Dry Texturizing Spray
  • Cake Beauty The Wave Maker Texturizing Salt Spray
  • OGX Moroccan Sea Salt Spray

Step 8: Use Dry Shampoo

If your hair starts to look oily throughout the day, use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and add volume. Spray the dry shampoo at your roots and massage it in with your fingers.

Recommended Products:

  • Living Proof Perfect Hair Day Dry Shampoo
  • Moroccanoil Dry Shampoo Light Tones
  • Lush No Drought Dry Shampoo

Step 9: Sleep with Your Hair in a Bun

To wake up with even more volume, sleep with your hair in a loose bun on top of your head. This will help prevent your hair from getting flat while you sleep.

Step 10: Maintain Your Hair's Health

Lastly, make sure to take care of your hair's health by getting regular trims, eating a healthy diet, and using hair products that are free from harsh chemicals. Healthy hair is more likely to be full and voluminous.

Tips for Making Fine Hair Fluffy

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, as brushes can cause breakage and static.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles and techniques to find what works best for your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make my fine hair fluffy without using heat?

Yes, you can achieve volume without using heat. Try using a volumizing mousse and letting your hair air dry, or use a texturizing spray and scrunch your hair with your fingers.

Can I use a volumizing shampoo instead of a clarifying shampoo?

You can use a volumizing shampoo, but it's important to use a clarifying shampoo every once in a while to remove any buildup from your hair.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have fine hair that gets oily quickly, you may need to wash it every day or every other day. If you have thicker hair that doesn't get oily as quickly, you can probably get away with washing it every few days.
