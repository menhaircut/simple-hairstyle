What Do I Do If My Hair Is Falling Out After Bleaching

Written by Frank Aug 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss after bleaching your hair? This is a common problem that many people face. Bleaching your hair can cause damage, breakage, and even hair loss if not done properly. If you’re struggling with hair loss after bleaching, don’t worry. In this article, we’ll provide you with helpful tips and solutions to get your hair back to its healthy state.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Bleaching your hair can be a great way to achieve a new look or color. However, it can also cause significant damage to your hair. Bleaching can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. This can then lead to hair loss. If you’re experiencing hair loss after bleaching, there are several things you can do to help your hair recover.

How To

The first step in dealing with hair loss after bleaching is to identify the cause. There are many reasons why your hair may be falling out, including:

  • Over-processing
  • Using too high of a developer
  • Leaving bleach on for too long
  • Not conditioning your hair enough
  • Chemical damage from previous treatments

Once you’ve identified the cause of your hair loss, you can then take steps to address it. This may include:

Step by Step

  1. Stop bleaching your hair immediately. Give your hair a break from any further chemical treatments.

  2. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for damaged hair. This will help to nourish and strengthen your hair.

  3. Avoid heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. These can further damage your hair and cause breakage.

  4. Consider getting a haircut. Cutting off damaged ends can help to promote healthy hair growth.

  5. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. This will help to restore moisture to your hair and prevent further breakage.

  6. Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet. Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so it’s best to let it air dry and then comb it gently.

  7. Take a break from tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns. These can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

  8. Consider taking supplements like biotin, which can help to promote healthy hair growth.

  9. Consult with a professional hairstylist or trichologist. They can help you identify the cause of your hair loss and provide you with personalized solutions.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you prevent hair loss after bleaching:

  • Always follow the instructions on your bleach kit carefully.
  • Do a strand test before applying bleach to your entire head.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can also strip it of its natural oils.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to help protect your hair from damage.

FAQ

Can hair loss from bleaching be reversed?

Yes, hair loss from bleaching can be reversed with proper care and treatment. It’s important to identify the cause of your hair loss and take steps to address it, such as using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and getting regular haircuts.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after bleaching?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for your hair to grow back after bleaching. The rate of hair growth varies from person to person and depends on factors like genetics, diet, and overall health.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, strong hair. Here are a few tips to help you care for your hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye.
  • Get regular haircuts to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

In conclusion, if your hair is falling out after bleaching, don’t panic. There are steps you can take to restore your hair’s health and prevent further damage. By following the tips and advice in this article, you can get your hair back to its healthy state and enjoy a beautiful, vibrant look.
