How To Fix A Ruined Wig Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jul 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Wigs have become an essential accessory for many people, whether it’s for fashion, convenience, or medical reasons. However, sometimes wigs can get ruined, and it can be frustrating to figure out how to fix them. In this article, we will go over some tips and tricks on how to fix a ruined wig.

How to

If you have a ruined wig, don't worry; it’s not the end of the world. Here are some steps to help you fix your wig:

Step 1: Detangle Your Wig

If your wig is tangled, you need to detangle it first. Start from the bottom and work your way up. Use a wig brush or a comb to gently detangle your wig. Don't pull too hard, or you could damage the wig fibers.

Step 2: Wash Your Wig

Wash your wig with a wig shampoo and conditioner. Fill a sink with cold water and add a small amount of shampoo. Soak your wig for a few minutes, then rinse it with cold water. Gently squeeze out the excess water, then apply the conditioner. Rinse it again with cold water and squeeze out the excess water.

Step 3: Dry Your Wig

To dry your wig, use a towel to squeeze out the excess water gently. Don't twist or wring it, as this could damage the fibers. Instead, lay your wig flat on a towel and let it air dry.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you fix your ruined wig:

Tip 1: Avoid Heat

Avoid using heat on your wig, as it could damage the fibers. Don't use a hairdryer, a straightener, or a curling iron on your wig. Instead, let it air dry naturally.

Tip 2: Store Your Wig Properly

Store your wig on a wig stand or a mannequin head to help it maintain its shape. Avoid storing your wig in direct sunlight or near a heat source, as this could damage the fibers.

Tip 3: Don't Use Regular Hair Products

Don't use regular hair products on your wig, as they could damage the fibers. Use wig-specific products, such as wig shampoo, conditioner, and styling products.

FAQ

Q1: Can I fix a melted wig?

A: It depends on the extent of the damage. If the wig fibers are melted together, it may be impossible to fix. However, if only a few strands are melted, you may be able to fix it with a blow dryer and a comb.

Q2: Can I dye my wig if it's ruined?

A: If your wig is ruined, dyeing it may not be the best solution. Dyeing a wig can be tricky, and it could further damage the fibers. It's best to consult a professional wig stylist if you want to dye your wig.

Q3: How often should I wash my wig?

A: It depends on how often you wear your wig. If you wear it daily, you should wash it once a week. If you wear it less frequently, you can wash it every two weeks.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to maintaining the quality and lifespan of your wig. Here are some hair care tips:

Tip 1: Brush Your Wig Daily

Brush your wig daily to prevent tangles and matting. Use a wig brush or a comb to gently brush your wig.

Tip 2: Avoid Sleeping in Your Wig

Avoid sleeping in your wig, as this could cause tangles and matting. Instead, remove your wig before going to bed and store it on a wig stand or mannequin head.

Tip 3: Protect Your Wig from the Sun

Avoid exposing your wig to direct sunlight for prolonged periods, as this could cause fading and damage to the fibers. Wear a hat or a scarf to protect your wig from the sun.

By following these tips and tricks, you can fix a ruined wig and keep it looking beautiful for longer.
