How To Style Thick Short Hair A Guide For 2023

Thick short hair can be a challenge to style, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a stunning look. In this guide, we'll show you how to style your thick short hair in a relaxed, easy-to-follow manner. From messy buns to sleek pixie cuts, you'll find plenty of inspiration here.

Description

Thick short hair can be a blessing or a curse, depending on how you look at it. On one hand, you have a lot of volume and texture to work with. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. However, with the right styling tools and techniques, you can create a variety of looks that showcase your thick short hair.

How to Style Thick Short Hair

The key to styling thick short hair is to work with the natural texture and shape of your hair. Here are some popular styles to try:

Pixie Cut

A pixie cut is a classic short hairstyle that never goes out of style. It's perfect for those with thick hair, as it allows you to show off your natural texture. To style a pixie cut, apply a small amount of styling cream to damp hair and blow dry using a round brush. Use a flat iron to smooth out any frizz or flyaways, and finish with a light hold hairspray.

Messy Bun

A messy bun is a great way to dress up or down your thick short hair. To create a messy bun, gather your hair at the crown of your head and secure with an elastic band. Twist your hair into a bun shape and secure with bobby pins. Pull out a few strands to frame your face and finish with a light hold hairspray.

Half-Up, Half-Down

A half-up, half-down style is a great way to show off your thick short hair while keeping it out of your face. To create this look, gather the top section of your hair (from your temples to the crown of your head) and secure with an elastic band. Twist the ponytail into a bun shape and secure with bobby pins. Finish with a light hold hairspray.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style your thick short hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of styling cream to damp hair and distribute evenly.
  3. Blow dry hair using a round brush, starting at the roots and working your way to the ends.
  4. Use a flat iron to smooth out any frizz or flyaways.
  5. Choose your desired style (pixie cut, messy bun, half-up, half-down, etc.) and follow the steps outlined above.
  6. Finish with a light hold hairspray to set your style in place.

Tips for Styling Thick Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you style your thick short hair:

  • Invest in a high-quality blow dryer and flat iron to make styling easier.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before blow drying or flat ironing your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

FAQs

Q: Can I still style my thick short hair if it's curly?

A: Absolutely! In fact, curly hair can be easier to style than straight hair because it already has a lot of volume and texture. Try using a curl-enhancing cream or mousse to define your curls and create a more polished look.

Q: What products should I use to style my thick short hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and desired style. For a pixie cut, use a styling cream and light hold hairspray. For a messy bun, use a texturizing spray and bobby pins. Experiment with different products to find what works best for you.

Hair Care for Thick Short Hair

Maintaining healthy hair is key to achieving a great style. Here are some tips for caring for your thick short hair:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from becoming too dry.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using hot tools (like blow dryers and flat irons) too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

By following these tips and techniques, you can style your thick short hair with ease. Whether you prefer a sleek pixie cut or a messy bun, there's a style out there that's perfect for you. So go out there and rock that thick short hair!
