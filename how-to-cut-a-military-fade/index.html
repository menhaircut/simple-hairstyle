How To Cut A Military Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a clean and stylish haircut, you might want to consider a military fade. This popular hairstyle has been a staple in the military for years, and it's now becoming increasingly popular among civilians. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut a military fade in simple steps, including tips and FAQs. So, let's get started!

How to Cut a Military Fade

Before you start cutting, it's important to gather all the necessary tools, including clippers, scissors, combs, and a mirror. You should also prepare your hair by washing and drying it beforehand. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Determine the length

The first step is to determine the length of the fade. Generally, military fades are short on the sides and back, and longer on top. You can choose how short you want the fade to be, but a good rule of thumb is to start with a longer guard size (e.g., 4 or 5) and work your way down.

Step 2: Create a guideline

Next, create a guideline by trimming around the edges of the hairline. Use a comb to lift the hair up and trim it with scissors or clippers. This will help you create a clean and defined line.

Step 3: Start cutting the sides

Now it's time to start cutting the sides. Use a clipper with a low guard size (e.g., 1 or 2) and start from the bottom, working your way up. Create a gradual fade by blending the hair as you go up. Use a comb to lift the hair and trim any stray hairs.

Step 4: Cut the back

Repeat the same process for the back of the head. Start from the bottom and work your way up, blending the hair as you go. Use a comb to lift the hair up and trim any stray hairs.

Step 5: Trim the top

Finally, trim the top of the hair with scissors or clippers, depending on the desired length. You can leave it longer or shorter, depending on your preference.

Tips for Cutting a Military Fade

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting a military fade:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually
  • Use a comb to lift the hair up and trim any stray hairs
  • Take your time and be patient
  • Use a mirror to check your progress
  • Ask for help if you're not sure

FAQs

Is a military fade suitable for all hair types?

Yes, a military fade can be suitable for all hair types, including thick, thin, curly, or straight hair.

How often should I get a military fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain it. Generally, you should get a military fade every 2-4 weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Can I cut a military fade myself?

Yes, you can cut a military fade yourself if you have the necessary tools and skills. However, it's always a good idea to seek professional help if you're not sure.

Hair Care

To maintain a military fade, you should wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using hot water and harsh chemicals that can damage your hair. You should also use a comb or brush to style your hair and avoid using excessive heat from blow dryers or styling tools.

Cutting a military fade can be a fun and rewarding experience, and with these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a stylish and clean look. So, grab your tools and start cutting!
