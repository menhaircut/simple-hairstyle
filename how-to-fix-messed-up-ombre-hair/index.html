How To Fix Messed Up Ombre Hair

Written by William Nov 27, 2023 · 5 min read
How to Fix Messed Up Ombre Hair - Tips and Tricks | Hair Care

Messed up ombre hair can be frustrating, especially if you’ve spent time and money to achieve the look. But don’t worry, there are ways to fix it without having to start from scratch. In this article, we’ll show you how to fix messed up ombre hair with some tips and tricks.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fix Messed Up Ombre Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Ombre hair is a popular style that involves blending two different colors together. It can be done with any hair color, but it’s most commonly seen with blonde or brown hair. When ombre hair is done correctly, it looks beautiful and natural. However, sometimes things can go wrong and you end up with messed up ombre hair.

There are a few reasons why ombre hair can end up looking messy. One reason is that the colors weren’t blended properly, which can result in harsh lines between the two colors. Another reason is that the colors may have been applied too high or too low on the hair, which can make the ombre look uneven.

If you’re dealing with messed up ombre hair, don’t panic. There are ways to fix it and get the look you want.

How to Fix Messed Up Ombre Hair

Here are some tips and tricks for fixing messed up ombre hair:

1. Go to a Professional

If your ombre hair is really messed up, it’s best to go to a professional stylist to fix it. They’ll have the skills and knowledge to correct the color and blend it properly.

2. Use a Color Correcting Shampoo

If your ombre hair is only slightly messed up, you can try using a color correcting shampoo. These shampoos are designed to neutralize unwanted tones in the hair. Look for a shampoo that’s specifically made for the color you’re trying to fix (e.g. purple shampoo for blonde hair).

3. Tone the Hair

If your ombre hair has too much warmth or brassiness, you can tone it to neutralize the color. A toner is a semi-permanent hair color that can be used to adjust the tone of your hair. If you’re not sure what toner to use, consult with a professional stylist.

4. Add Highlights or Lowlights

If your ombre hair is too harsh or uneven, you can add highlights or lowlights to blend the colors together. Highlights will lighten the hair, while lowlights will darken it. Again, it’s best to consult with a professional stylist to get the right shade and placement.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a step by step guide for fixing messed up ombre hair:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Take a close look at your ombre hair and determine what needs to be fixed. Is the color too harsh? Are there uneven lines between the colors? Are there unwanted tones in the hair?

Step 2: Decide on a Solution

Based on your assessment, decide on the best solution for your hair. Do you need to go to a professional stylist? Do you need to use a color correcting shampoo? Do you need to tone your hair?

Step 3: Gather Your Supplies

If you’re going to fix your ombre hair at home, make sure you have all the necessary supplies. This may include color correcting shampoo, toner, bleach, developer, foil, and a brush.

Step 4: Follow the Instructions

Follow the instructions on the products you’re using. If you’re not sure how to use them, consult with a professional stylist or do some research online.

Step 5: Rinse and Style

Rinse your hair thoroughly and style it as usual.

Tips

Here are some tips for fixing messed up ombre hair:

  • Don’t try to fix your ombre hair with box dye. This can make the problem worse and damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment after fixing your ombre hair to keep it healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and fade the color.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine if you’re going to be outside or swimming.

FAQ

Can I fix messed up ombre hair at home?

Yes, you can fix messed up ombre hair at home, but it’s best to consult with a professional stylist first. They can give you advice on the best products and techniques to use.

How long does it take to fix messed up ombre hair?

The length of time it takes to fix messed up ombre hair depends on the severity of the problem and the solution you choose. It could take a few hours or a few days.

Can I still get ombre hair if I’ve had a bad experience?

Yes, you can still get ombre hair even if you’ve had a bad experience in the past. Just make sure you go to a reputable stylist and communicate your concerns before the process begins.

Hair Care

After fixing your messed up ombre hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain the color and health of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the color.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Fixing messed up ombre hair can be a daunting task, but it’s not impossible. With the right products and techniques, you can get the look you want and feel confident in your hair once again.
