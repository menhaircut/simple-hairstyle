How To Cut Layers On Short Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your short curly hair looking limp and lifeless? Adding layers to your hair can give it more texture and volume, creating a bouncy and dynamic look. However, getting layers on curly hair can be tricky. In this article, we will discuss how to cut layers on short curly hair, step-by-step.

Description

Cutting layers on short curly hair can be a challenging task. However, with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a stunning and voluminous look. Layers can add movement and texture to your hair and help to define your natural curls.

How to Cut Layers on Short Curly Hair

The first step to cutting layers on short curly hair is to gather your tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. If you have particularly thick or coarse hair, you may also want to invest in thinning shears.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to cut layers on short curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. It is easier to cut wet hair, and this will also help to define your natural curls.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles, and then part it in the middle or on one side, depending on your preference.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head, about an inch thick, and twist it around your finger. This will create a natural curl pattern and help you to see where the hair falls.
  4. Use your scissors to cut the twisted section of hair, starting at the ends and working your way up. Be sure to cut at an angle to create a layered effect.
  5. Repeat this process, taking small sections of hair and cutting them at an angle. Always cut less than you think you need, as you can always go back and trim more if necessary.
  6. Once you have completed one side of your head, repeat the process on the other side.
  7. When you are finished, shake out your hair and admire your new layered look.

Tips for Cutting Layers on Short Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers on short curly hair:

  • Always cut less than you think you need. It is easier to go back and trim more than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different layer lengths and angles to find what works best for your hair type and face shape.
  • If you have thick or coarse hair, consider using thinning shears to remove some of the bulk.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers on short curly hair:

  • How often should I cut my hair? It is recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers and keep your hair healthy.
  • Can I cut my own hair? While it is possible to cut your own hair, it is generally recommended to go to a professional stylist, especially if you are cutting layers for the first time.
  • What if I make a mistake? If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always go to a stylist to fix it, or try to even out the length yourself by trimming the longer sections.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers on Short Curly Hair

After cutting layers on your short curly hair, it is important to take care of your hair to maintain your new look. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, as these can damage your hair and undo your layers.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying to help define your curls and add volume.
  • Consider using a hair serum or oil to add shine and reduce frizz.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stunning and voluminous layered look on your short curly hair. Don't be afraid to experiment and try new things to find what works best for you.
