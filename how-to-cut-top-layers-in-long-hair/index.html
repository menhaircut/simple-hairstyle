How To Cut Top Layers In Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Long hair can be gorgeous, but without proper layering, it can also look flat and lifeless. One way to add movement and volume to long hair is by cutting top layers. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut top layers in long hair, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Top Layers in Long Hair

How to Cut Top Layers in Long Hair

Cutting top layers in long hair is a relatively simple process, but it's important to have the right tools and techniques. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting shears
  • Clips to section off hair
  • A comb
  • A mirror

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash and dry your hair. It's best to cut hair when it's clean and dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.
  2. Section off the top layer. Use clips to separate the top layer of your hair from the rest of your hair.
  3. Decide on the length. Determine how much hair you want to cut off, and use your comb to measure the length.
  4. Hold the hair between your fingers. Take a small section of hair between your fingers and hold it straight up. This will be the hair you'll cut.
  5. Start cutting. Use your hair cutting shears to cut the hair straight across. Make sure to cut at an angle, as this will create a softer, more natural look.
  6. Repeat. Continue to take small sections of hair and cut at an angle until you've cut all the top layers.
  7. Blend. Use your comb to blend the top layers with the rest of your hair. This will create a seamless look.
  8. Style. Once you've cut your top layers, style your hair as desired. You'll likely notice more volume and movement in your hair.

Tips for Cutting Top Layers in Long Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting top layers in long hair:

  • Start with small sections. It's easier to cut more hair than it is to put hair back, so start with small sections and work your way up.
  • Use sharp shears. Dull shears can damage hair and create split ends.
  • Take breaks. If you're cutting a lot of hair, take breaks to avoid hand fatigue.
  • Don't cut too much. It's better to cut a little at a time and assess the results than to cut too much and regret it later.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting top layers in long hair:

Q: Can I cut my own top layers?
A: Yes, you can cut your own top layers, but it's important to have the right tools and techniques. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to see a professional stylist. Q: How often should I cut my top layers?
A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. As a general rule, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair. Q: Will cutting top layers damage my hair?
A: No, cutting top layers shouldn't damage your hair if done correctly. In fact, it can help promote healthy hair growth by removing split ends.

Hair Care Advice for Long Hair

In addition to cutting top layers, there are some things you can do to keep your long hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis to prevent damage.
  • Protect hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and techniques, you can successfully cut top layers in long hair and enjoy beautiful, voluminous locks.
