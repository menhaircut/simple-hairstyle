What Colours Suit What Skin Tones A Guide To Flattering Shades

Written by Robby Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of wearing colours that don't seem to suit you? Whether you're shopping for clothes, makeup, or accessories, it can be challenging to figure out what colours look best on your skin tone. But don't worry, we're here to help! In this guide, we'll show you how to determine your skin tone and which colours will complement it perfectly.

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in figuring out what colours suit you is to determine your skin tone. Your skin tone can be warm, cool, or neutral, and it's based on the undertones of your skin. Here's how to figure out your skin tone:

Step 1: Look at Your Veins

Take a look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue or purple, you have cool undertones. If they appear green, you have warm undertones. If you can't tell, you likely have neutral undertones.

Step 2: Consider Your Sunburn and Tan

Think about how your skin reacts when you're in the sun. If you burn easily and don't tan, you likely have cool undertones. If you tan easily, you likely have warm undertones. If you burn and then tan, you likely have neutral undertones.

Step 3: Look at Your Jewelry

Consider which type of jewelry looks best on you. If you look better in silver, you likely have cool undertones. If you look better in gold, you likely have warm undertones. If you look good in both, you likely have neutral undertones.

Colours for Warm Skin Tones

If you have warm undertones, you'll want to stick to colours that are equally warm. Think earthy tones like brown, orange, and yellow. You'll also look great in shades of red, coral, and peach. When it comes to neutrals, go for ivory, cream, and beige.

Colours for Cool Skin Tones

If you have cool undertones, you'll want to stick to colours that are equally cool. Think shades of blue, green, and purple. You'll also look great in pink, magenta, and ruby. When it comes to neutrals, go for white, black, and navy.

Colours for Neutral Skin Tones

If you have neutral undertones, you're lucky! You can wear both warm and cool colours. Stick to shades that are not too bright or too muted. Think shades of gray, taupe, and off-white. You'll also look great in soft pinks and blues.

Tips for Choosing Colours

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing colours:

  • Consider the occasion. Bright, bold colours are great for parties, but you might want to stick to neutrals for a job interview.
  • Think about your hair colour. If you have blonde hair, you might want to avoid wearing yellow, which can clash. If you have red hair, you'll look great in green.
  • Experiment with different shades. Just because you have warm undertones doesn't mean you'll look good in every shade of orange. Try on different colours to see what works best for you.

FAQs

What if I have a tan?

Your skin tone is based on your undertones, not your current level of tan. If you have cool undertones, you'll still have cool undertones even if you have a tan.

What if I have multiple undertones?

Some people have multiple undertones, which can make it a bit more challenging to determine your skin tone. In this case, it's best to experiment with different shades to see what works best for you.

Can I wear black if I have warm undertones?

Absolutely! While black is traditionally a cool colour, it can still look great on warm undertones. Just make sure to pair it with warm accessories and makeup to balance it out.

Hair Care for Flattering Colours

When it comes to choosing colours that suit your skin tone, your hair can play a big role. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • If you have warm undertones and blonde hair, go for warm shades like peach and coral.
  • If you have cool undertones and blonde hair, go for cool shades like pink and blue.
  • If you have warm undertones and brown hair, go for warm shades like red and orange.
  • If you have cool undertones and brown hair, go for cool shades like green and purple.
  • If you have black hair, you can wear both warm and cool shades.
  • If you have red hair, you'll look great in green and blue.

Remember, the most important thing is to feel confident and comfortable in what you're wearing. Use this guide as a starting point, but don't be afraid to experiment with different colours until you find your perfect match.
