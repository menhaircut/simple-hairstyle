How To Keep Your Hair While On Chemo Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Chemotherapy is a common treatment for cancer, but it often comes with side effects such as hair loss. Losing hair can be a traumatic experience, and it can affect a person's self-esteem and confidence. However, there are ways to keep your hair while on chemo. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to keep your hair while on chemo.

  • How to Keep Your Hair While on Chemo
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Keeping Your Hair While on Chemo

How to Keep Your Hair While on Chemo

The first step in keeping your hair while on chemo is to talk to your doctor. They can tell you if there are any drugs or treatments available that can help prevent hair loss during chemo. Some drugs, such as scalp cooling caps, can help reduce hair loss by cooling the scalp and reducing blood flow to the hair follicles.

Another way to keep your hair while on chemo is to maintain a healthy diet. Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help strengthen your hair and prevent hair loss. Some foods that are good for your hair include eggs, nuts, beans, and leafy greens.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to keep your hair while on chemo:

  1. Talk to your doctor about drugs or treatments that can help prevent hair loss.
  2. Maintain a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals.
  3. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for sensitive scalps.
  4. Avoid using hot styling tools such as curling irons and flat irons.
  5. Avoid harsh chemicals such as hair dyes and relaxers.
  6. Wash your hair less frequently to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  7. Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids and ponytails that can put stress on your hair.
  8. Consider using a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent hair breakage.
  9. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or brush.
  10. Consider wearing a wig or a headscarf to cover up hair loss.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair While on Chemo

Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair while on chemo:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid tight hats and headbands that can put pressure on your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel after washing, as it can cause hair breakage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about keeping your hair while on chemo:

Can I dye my hair while on chemo?

No, it is not recommended to dye your hair while on chemo. Hair dyes can be harsh on your scalp and can cause further damage to your hair follicles.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after chemo?

It can take several months for your hair to grow back after chemo. The length of time it takes depends on the type of chemotherapy you received and your individual hair growth rate.

Can I use hair products while on chemo?

Yes, you can use hair products while on chemo, but it is important to use products that are gentle and designed for sensitive scalps.

Hair Care During Chemo

During chemo, it is important to take care of your hair and scalp. Here are some tips for hair care during chemo:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for sensitive scalps.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools such as curling irons and flat irons.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals such as hair dyes and relaxers.
  • Wash your hair less frequently to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids and ponytails that can put stress on your hair.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or brush.
  • Consider using a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent hair breakage.
  • Consider wearing a wig or a headscarf to cover up hair loss.

Keeping your hair while on chemo can be a challenge, but with the right care and treatment, it is possible. Talk to your doctor about your options for preventing hair loss, and take care of your hair and scalp during chemo with gentle products and styling techniques.
