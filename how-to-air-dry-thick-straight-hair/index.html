How To Air Dry Thick Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of using heat on your thick straight hair every day? Do you want to give your hair a break and let it dry naturally? Air drying your hair can be a great way to maintain its health and minimize damage. However, it can be a challenge to air dry thick straight hair without it becoming frizzy or flat. In this article, we will provide you with a step by step guide on how to air dry your hair, along with some tips and FAQs to help you achieve the perfect look.

  1. How to Air Dry Thick Straight Hair
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips for Air Drying Thick Straight Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Thick Straight Hair

How to Air Dry Thick Straight Hair

Air drying your hair can be a simple process when you follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step to air drying your hair is washing it with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any residue.

Step 2: Remove Excess Water

After washing your hair, gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair with a towel. Do not rub your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz and damage.

Step 3: Apply Hair Products

Apply hair products, such as a leave-in conditioner or hair oil, to your damp hair. This will help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.

Step 4: Part Your Hair

Part your hair the way you prefer and then comb it with a wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles.

Step 5: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Let your hair air dry naturally. Do not touch your hair while it's drying as this can cause frizz. If you need to leave the house before your hair is dry, you can gently tie it back in a loose ponytail or braid to prevent it from becoming too frizzy.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step by step guide on how to air dry your hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any residue.

Step 2: Towel Dry

Gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair with a towel. Do not rub your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz and damage. Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt instead of a regular towel, as these materials are gentler on your hair.

Step 3: Apply Products

Apply hair products to your damp hair, such as a leave-in conditioner, hair oil or styling cream. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Part Your Hair

Part your hair the way you prefer and then comb it with a wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles.

Step 5: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Let your hair air dry naturally. Do not touch your hair while it's drying as this can cause frizz. If you need to leave the house before your hair is dry, you can gently tie it back in a loose ponytail or braid to prevent it from becoming too frizzy.

Tips for Air Drying Thick Straight Hair

Here are some tips to help you air dry your hair:

Tip 1: Use the Right Products

Use hair products that are specifically designed for air drying. These products will help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.

Tip 2: Avoid Touching Your Hair

Do not touch your hair while it's drying, as this can cause frizz. If you need to move your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently move it into place.

Tip 3: Use a Diffuser

If you want to speed up the drying process, you can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. This will help to distribute the heat evenly and prevent frizz.

Tip 4: Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and breakage. Satin is a smooth material that won't cause friction on your hair, which can lead to frizz and damage.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to air dry thick straight hair?

A: It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to a few hours to air dry thick straight hair, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Q: Can I use a hair dryer to speed up the drying process?

A: Yes, you can use a hair dryer with a diffuser attachment to speed up the drying process. However, be sure to use it on the lowest heat setting to prevent damage.

Q: How often should I air dry my hair?

A: You can air dry your hair as often as you like. However, it's best to give your hair a break from heat styling and air dry it at least once a week.

Hair Care for Thick Straight Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your thick straight hair:

Tip 1: Use a Clarifying Shampoo

Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup of hair products and oils. This will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent buildup.

Tip 2: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and prevent damage. Look for products that contain natural oils, such as coconut oil or argan oil.

Tip 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage. This will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent damage.

Tip 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as much as possible. Heat can cause damage to your hair and lead to breakage and split ends.

By following these tips and steps, you can achieve the perfect air dried look for your thick straight hair. Remember to be patient and let your hair dry naturally to avoid frizz and damage. Happy air drying!
