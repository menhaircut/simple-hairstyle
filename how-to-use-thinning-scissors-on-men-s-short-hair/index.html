How To Use Thinning Scissors On Men s Short Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning scissors are an essential tool for any hairdresser or barber. These scissors are designed to remove bulk and weight from the hair, creating a natural and more textured look. In this article, we will be discussing how to use thinning scissors on men's short hair. We will cover everything from the basics of using thinning scissors to some tips and tricks to achieve the best results.

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of hair-cutting scissors that have teeth on one blade and a straight edge on the other. These teeth are designed to remove hair in a more gradual and subtle way than regular scissors. Thinning scissors are commonly used on thick hair to create a more natural and textured look. They can also be used to remove bulk and weight from the hair, making it easier to style and manage.

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Men's Short Hair

Using thinning scissors on men's short hair can be a bit tricky, but with some practice and patience, anyone can achieve a great look. Here are some basic steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Before using thinning scissors, make sure the hair is clean and dry. Comb the hair to remove any tangles or knots. Divide the hair into sections using hair clips.

Step 2: Choose the Right Scissors

There are many types of thinning scissors available, each with their own unique features. It's important to choose the right scissors for the job. For short hair, choose a pair of thinning scissors with fewer teeth and wider gaps between them. This will create a more subtle and natural look.

Step 3: Start Thinning

Starting at the back of the head, hold a section of hair with your non-dominant hand and use the thinning scissors to cut the hair in a diagonal direction. Hold the scissors at a 45-degree angle and make small cuts along the hair shaft. Take care not to cut too much hair at once. Continue thinning the hair in small sections, working your way around the head.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After thinning the hair, use regular scissors to blend the hair and create a more natural look. Hold the hair between your fingers and make small cuts to blend the hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning scissors on men's short hair:

  1. Wash and dry the hair.
  2. Divide the hair into sections.
  3. Choose the right thinning scissors.
  4. Hold a section of hair with your non-dominant hand.
  5. Use the thinning scissors to cut the hair in a diagonal direction.
  6. Make small cuts along the hair shaft.
  7. Take care not to cut too much hair at once.
  8. Continue thinning the hair in small sections.
  9. Use regular scissors to blend the hair.
  10. Style the hair as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to achieve the best results when using thinning scissors on men's short hair:

  • Practice on a mannequin head or a willing friend before trying it on a client.
  • Take your time and work in small sections.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut only the ends.
  • Use the scissors at an angle to create a more natural look.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning scissors on men's short hair:

Q: Can I use thinning scissors on all types of hair?

A: Thinning scissors are best suited for thick hair. If you have thin or fine hair, it's best to avoid using thinning scissors as they can make the hair look even thinner.

Q: How often should I thin my hair?

A: It's best to thin your hair every 6 to 8 weeks, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Q: Can I use thinning scissors to create a specific hairstyle?

A: Thinning scissors are best used to create a more natural and textured look. If you want to create a specific hairstyle, it's best to use regular scissors or clippers.

Hair Care after Thinning

After thinning your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as blow dryers and straighteners, too often.
  • Use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished.
  • Visit your stylist regularly for touch-ups and trims.

Using thinning scissors on men's short hair can be a great way to create a more natural and textured look. With some practice and patience, anyone can achieve a great result. Just remember to choose the right scissors, work in small sections, and take care of your hair properly after thinning.
