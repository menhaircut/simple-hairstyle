Short Cuts For Curly Hair Over 50 A Guide For Effortless Style

Written by Aprilia Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending hours styling your curly hair every morning? Do you want a low-maintenance haircut that still looks stylish and flattering? Short cuts for curly hair over 50 might be the answer you're looking for. In this guide, we'll go over how to find the perfect short cut for your curls, how to style it, and how to take care of your hair. Let's get started!

How to Find the Right Short Cut for Your Curls

The first step in getting a short cut for your curly hair is to find the right style for your face shape and hair texture. Here are some options to consider:

Bob

A bob is a classic short cut that can work well for curly hair. Opt for a chin-length bob for a timeless look, or go shorter for a more modern vibe. This cut works well for oval, heart, and square face shapes.

Pixie

A pixie cut is an edgy and playful option for curly hair. Ask your stylist to add layers to enhance your natural texture. This cut works well for round and heart face shapes.

Shag

A shag is a layered cut that can add volume and movement to your curls. This cut works well for oval and heart face shapes.

How to Style Your Short Curly Hair

Short curly hair is easy to style and can look effortless and chic. Here are some tips:

Use a Diffuser

A diffuser attachment for your hair dryer can help enhance your curls and reduce frizz. Apply a styling cream or mousse to damp hair, then use the diffuser on a low heat setting.

Air Dry

Air drying your hair can help maintain your natural curl pattern and reduce heat damage. Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to damp hair, then let it air dry. You can also scrunch your curls with your hands to enhance their shape.

Accessorize

Add some flair to your short curly hair with accessories like headbands, scarves, or hair clips. These can add a pop of color or texture to your look.

Short Curly Hair Care Tips

Curly hair requires some extra care to keep it healthy and frizz-free. Here are some tips:

Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep it hydrated. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Try to air dry your hair as much as possible, and if you do use heat tools, use a heat protectant spray first.

Get Regular Trims

Short haircuts require more frequent trims to maintain their shape. Aim to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

FAQ

Can I dye my short curly hair?

Yes, you can dye your short curly hair. However, keep in mind that dyeing your hair can dry it out and make it more prone to frizz. Make sure to use a dye that's specifically formulated for curly hair and follow up with a deep conditioning treatment.

What's the best way to detangle short curly hair?

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair when it's wet. Apply a leave-in conditioner or detangling spray to help loosen any knots. Avoid using a brush, as it can break your hair and cause frizz.

How often should I wash my short curly hair?

Everyone's hair is different, but generally, you should wash your hair every 2-3 days. Washing too often can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and frizz.

Final Thoughts

Short cuts for curly hair over 50 can be a stylish and low-maintenance option for those who want to embrace their natural texture. With the right cut and styling techniques, you can have effortless, chic hair that looks great at any age. Remember to take care of your hair with moisturizing products and regular trims, and have fun experimenting with different accessories and styles!
