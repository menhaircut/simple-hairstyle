How To Cut Layered Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Layered hair is a popular hairstyle that can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair. Whether you want a subtle or dramatic change, cutting your hair into layers can give you a fresh new look. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut layered hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Why Should You Cut Layered Hair?

Layered hair can make your hair look fuller, thicker, and more voluminous. Layers can also add texture and movement to your hair, making it appear more natural and effortless. Additionally, layered hair is versatile and can be styled in many different ways, which can give you a new look every day.

How to Cut Layered Hair

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

The first step in cutting layered hair is to wash and dry your hair. This will remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that may be in your hair. Once your hair is clean, blow-dry it straight using a round brush. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair, which will make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into four equal parts using hair clips. Divide your hair into two sections horizontally and then divide each of those sections in half vertically. This will create four equal sections.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start by cutting the bottom layer of your hair. Take one of the back sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut straight across. Repeat this step for the other back section. Then, move to the front sections and repeat the same process.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you’ve cut the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut at a slight angle towards the ends. This will create a softer, more blended look. Repeat this step for the other back section and then move to the front sections.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

The top layer is the shortest layer and adds the most volume to your hair. Take a small section of hair from the top and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut straight across. Repeat this step for the other sections.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut all the layers, it’s time to blend them together. Comb your hair straight down and check for any unevenness or choppy layers. Use your scissors to blend any uneven sections together.

Tips for Cutting Layered Hair

  • Start with a small amount of hair and cut slowly. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as wet hair is longer and can cause you to cut off more than you intended.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring, so don’t rush yourself.

FAQs About Cutting Layered Hair

1. How often should I cut my layered hair?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and length of your layers.

2. Can I cut my own layered hair?

Yes, you can cut your own layered hair, but it’s important to take your time and start with small sections. If you’re not confident in your ability to cut your own hair, it’s best to visit a professional stylist.

3. What products should I use to care for my layered hair?

Use a shampoo and conditioner that’s appropriate for your hair type, and consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to prevent breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat by wearing a hat or using a heat protectant spray.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals, as this can promote healthy hair growth.

By following these simple steps and tips, you can easily cut your own layered hair and achieve a fresh new look. Remember to take your time and start with small sections, and don’t hesitate to consult a professional stylist if you’re not confident in your ability to cut your own hair. With the right care and maintenance, your layered hair can look healthy, voluminous, and beautiful for years to come.
