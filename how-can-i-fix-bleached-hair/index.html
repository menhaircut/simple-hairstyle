How Can I Fix Bleached Hair Tips Steps And Hair Care

Written by Eveline Nov 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you one of those people who tried bleaching their hair at home and ended up with a disaster? Bleached hair can be a fashion statement, but it can also damage your hair if not done properly. Over-bleached hair can become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Don't worry, though, there are ways to fix bleached hair and restore its health and shine. In this blog post, we'll show you how to fix bleached hair step-by-step and provide you with tips and hair care advice to prevent further damage.

How to Fix Bleached Hair

Before we get into how to fix bleached hair, it's essential to understand what happens when you bleach your hair. Bleaching removes the natural pigments from your hair, which can leave it looking pale and damaged. Bleaching can also weaken your hair, making it prone to breakage and split ends. Here are some ways to fix bleached hair:

1. Use a Hair Mask

One of the easiest ways to fix bleached hair is to use a hair mask. Hair masks are deep conditioning treatments that can help repair damaged hair. Look for a hair mask that contains ingredients like keratin, argan oil, or shea butter. These ingredients can help restore your hair's health and shine. Leave the hair mask on for at least 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out with cool water.

2. Cut Off the Damaged Ends

If your hair is severely damaged from bleaching, it may be time to cut off the damaged ends. Cutting off the damaged ends can help prevent further breakage and split ends. It can also help your hair grow back healthier and stronger.

3. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Look for a protein treatment that contains hydrolyzed keratin or collagen. These ingredients can help repair damaged hair and prevent further damage.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can further damage bleached hair. If possible, avoid heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.

5. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioners can help moisturize and protect your hair from further damage. Look for a leave-in conditioner that contains ingredients like argan oil or shea butter. Apply the conditioner to your hair after washing it and leave it in to dry naturally.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix bleached hair:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step in fixing bleached hair is to assess the damage. Look at your hair and determine how much damage has been done. If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to cut off the damaged ends.

Step 2: Use a Hair Mask

Apply a deep conditioning hair mask to your hair. Leave the hair mask on for at least 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out with cool water.

Step 3: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair. Leave the protein treatment on for at least 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out with cool water.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.

Step 5: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it. Leave the conditioner in to dry naturally.

Tips for Fixing Bleached Hair

Here are some tips for fixing bleached hair:

  • Don't over-wash your hair. Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair. Hot water can further damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet. Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so wait until your hair is dry to brush it.
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends and prevent further damage.

FAQ

Can I fix bleached hair at home?

Yes, you can fix bleached hair at home. However, it's essential to understand what caused the damage and how to fix it properly. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips to fix bleached hair at home.

How long does it take to fix bleached hair?

The time it takes to fix bleached hair depends on the extent of the damage. Some people may see results in a few weeks, while others may take several months to see significant improvements.

Can I dye my hair after bleaching it?

Yes, you can dye your hair after bleaching it. However, it's essential to wait until your hair is healthy and strong enough to handle the dye. Wait at least a few weeks after fixing your bleached hair before dyeing it.

Hair Care for Bleached Hair

Here are some hair care tips for bleached hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater, as they can further damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or relaxers.

Fixing bleached hair can be a daunting task, but with the right steps and hair care tips, you can restore your hair's health and shine. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and you'll be on your way to healthy, beautiful hair in no time.
