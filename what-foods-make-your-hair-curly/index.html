What Foods Make Your Hair Curly A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 25, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking for a natural way to enhance your curly hair, you may want to consider adding certain foods to your diet. While genetics play a major role in determining your hair's texture, there are several foods that can help make your hair more curly. In this article, we'll explore which foods make your hair curly and how to incorporate them into your diet.

Table of Contents

  • What Makes Hair Curly
  • Foods for Curly Hair
  • How to Incorporate These Foods into Your Diet
  • Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Conclusion

What Makes Hair Curly

Your hair's texture is determined by the shape of your hair follicles. Straight hair has round follicles, while curly hair has oval-shaped follicles. The more oval-shaped your follicles are, the curlier your hair will be. The amount of curl in your hair is also affected by the amount of disulfide bonds between the hair proteins.

Genetics

Genetics play a major role in determining your hair texture. If your parents have curly hair, it's likely that you will too. However, it's not just your parents' hair that matters. Hair texture is a complex trait that is influenced by many different genes.

Environmental Factors

Environmental factors can also affect your hair's texture. Humidity, for example, can make curly hair frizzy, while dry air can make it brittle. Chemical treatments, such as relaxers or perms, can also affect the shape of your hair follicles.

Foods for Curly Hair

While there's no magic food that will make your hair curly overnight, there are several foods that can help enhance your hair's natural curl.

Salmon

Salmon is high in omega-3 fatty acids, which can help keep your hair healthy and strong. It also contains vitamin D, which is important for hair growth. Strong, healthy hair is more likely to hold a curl.

Avocado

Avocado is rich in healthy fats, which can help moisturize your hair and prevent breakage. It's also a good source of vitamin E, which can help promote hair growth. Moisturized, healthy hair is more likely to stay curly.

Blueberries

Blueberries are packed with antioxidants, which can help protect your hair from damage. They're also a good source of vitamin C, which is important for collagen production. Collagen helps keep your hair strong and elastic, which can help maintain your natural curl.

Spinach

Spinach is high in iron, which is essential for healthy hair growth. It's also a good source of vitamin A, which can help keep your scalp healthy. A healthy scalp is essential for healthy hair, and healthy hair is more likely to hold a curl.

Sweet Potatoes

Sweet potatoes are rich in beta-carotene, which is converted into vitamin A in the body. Vitamin A is important for healthy hair growth, and a deficiency can lead to dry, brittle hair. Healthy, moisturized hair is more likely to hold a curl.

How to Incorporate These Foods into Your Diet

Now that you know which foods can help enhance your hair's natural curl, you may be wondering how to incorporate them into your diet. Here are a few tips:

Salmon

Try grilling or baking a salmon fillet for dinner. You can also add canned salmon to salads or sandwiches for a quick and easy lunch.

Avocado

Add sliced avocado to your morning omelet or smoothie. You can also mash it up and use it as a healthy spread on sandwiches or toast.

Blueberries

Add blueberries to your morning oatmeal or yogurt. You can also snack on them throughout the day for a healthy, antioxidant-rich treat.

Spinach

Add spinach to your morning smoothie or omelet. You can also use it as a base for salads or sauté it with garlic and olive oil for a healthy side dish.

Sweet Potatoes

Bake sweet potatoes in the oven and serve them as a healthy side dish. You can also mash them up and use them as a healthy alternative to mashed potatoes.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

In addition to incorporating curly hair-friendly foods into your diet, there are several other things you can do to maintain your natural curl:

Avoid Over-Washing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can make it dry and brittle. Try washing your hair every other day, or even less frequently if your hair is particularly dry.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Avoid using brushes on curly hair, as they can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair after washing.

Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help moisturize and strengthen your hair, which can help maintain your natural curl. Try using a deep conditioner once a week, or more frequently if your hair is particularly dry.

FAQs

Can food really make my hair curly?

While there's no guarantee that eating certain foods will make your hair curly, a healthy diet can help keep your hair strong and healthy, which can enhance your natural curl.

Can I make my hair curly if I have straight hair?

If you have naturally straight hair, there's no way to permanently make it curly. However, you can use curling tools or get a perm to achieve a curly look.

What should I do if my hair is naturally curly but I want to straighten it?

If you want to straighten your hair, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible. You can also use a straightening treatment, such as a keratin treatment, to achieve a straight look.

Conclusion

While there's no magic food that will make your hair curly overnight, there are several foods that can help enhance your hair's natural curl. Incorporating foods such as salmon, avocado, blueberries, spinach, and sweet potatoes into your diet can help keep your hair strong, healthy, and moisturized. In addition to eating a healthy diet, be sure to follow the tips for maintaining curly hair, such as avoiding over-washing, avoiding heat styling, and deep conditioning regularly. With the right care and nourishment, you can enhance your natural curl and enjoy healthy, beautiful hair.
