How to Look Pretty Wearing a Mask

Wearing a mask is now an essential part of our daily lives. It helps protect ourselves and others from the spread of COVID-19. However, it can be challenging to maintain our beauty routines while wearing a mask. In this article, we will provide you with tips on how to look pretty wearing a mask.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first step to looking pretty while wearing a mask is to focus on your eyes. Since your mouth and nose are covered, your eyes are the main feature that people will see. You can use eye makeup to enhance and accentuate your eyes. Use eyeshadow, eyeliner, and mascara to create a beautiful look.

Choose the Right Color

When choosing eye makeup, select colors that complement your eye color. For example, if you have blue eyes, use warm browns or copper tones. If you have green eyes, use purples or plums. If you have brown eyes, use golds or bronzes.

Apply Mascara

Mascara can make a huge difference in making your eyes pop. Use a lengthening or volumizing mascara to add volume and length to your lashes. Don't forget to curl your lashes before applying mascara to make them look even longer.

Step by Step

  1. Start by applying eye primer to your eyelids to make your eye makeup last longer.
  2. Use a light eyeshadow shade as a base on your eyelids.
  3. Use a darker eyeshadow shade on the outer corners of your eyes to create depth.
  4. Apply eyeliner to your upper lash line to define your eyes.
  5. Curl your lashes and apply mascara.

Tips

  • Use a waterproof mascara to prevent smudging.
  • Try using false eyelashes for a more dramatic look.
  • Use a concealer to cover any dark circles under your eyes.

FAQ

Can I still wear lipstick while wearing a mask?

It's not recommended to wear lipstick while wearing a mask as it can smudge and transfer onto the mask. Instead, use a lip balm or lip gloss to keep your lips hydrated.

What type of face mask should I wear?

It's recommended to wear a cloth face mask with a filter pocket or a surgical mask. Avoid wearing masks with valves as they do not provide adequate protection.

Hair Care

Since your hair may touch your face while wearing a mask, it's important to keep it clean and away from your face. Try tying your hair back in a ponytail or bun to keep it away from your face. You can also use a headband or hair clips to keep your hair out of the way.
