What Causes Excessive Hair Fall A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jun 14, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are experiencing excessive hair fall, you are not alone. Hair fall is a common problem that affects millions of people all over the world. There are several factors that can cause excessive hair fall, including genetics, age, hormonal changes, stress, and poor nutrition. In this article, we will discuss the various causes of hair fall and provide you with some tips on how to prevent it.

Causes of Excessive Hair Fall

Genetics

One of the most common causes of hair fall is genetics. If your parents or grandparents have a history of hair loss, you are more likely to experience it as well. This type of hair fall is also known as androgenetic alopecia or male/female pattern baldness. It usually starts with a receding hairline or thinning at the crown and can progress to complete baldness.

Age

As we age, our hair growth slows down, and the hair follicles become weaker. This can lead to thinning and hair fall. Menopause is a common time for women to experience hair fall due to hormonal changes.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also cause hair fall. Thyroid disorders, pregnancy, and menopause are all conditions that can affect hormone levels and lead to hair fall. Hormonal imbalances can also cause hair fall in men.

Stress

Stress can have a significant impact on our physical and mental health, including our hair. When we are stressed, our body produces cortisol, which can lead to hair fall. Stress can also cause scalp problems like dandruff and itchy scalp, which can worsen hair fall.

Poor Nutrition

Our hair needs a balanced diet to grow and stay healthy. If we don't get enough vitamins and minerals in our diet, it can lead to hair fall. Lack of protein, iron, and vitamin D are some of the most common nutritional deficiencies that can cause hair fall.

How to Prevent Hair Fall

Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help prevent hair fall. Include foods like eggs, nuts, leafy greens, and fish in your diet to ensure that you are getting enough nutrients.

Manage Stress

Managing stress is crucial for preventing hair fall. Try to practice relaxation techniques like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises to reduce stress levels.

Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and hair dryers can damage your hair and lead to hair fall. Try to avoid using them as much as possible, and if you must use them, use them on a low heat setting.

Avoid Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments like hair color, perms, and relaxers can weaken your hair and cause hair fall. Try to avoid them or limit their use as much as possible.

FAQ

How Much Hair Fall is Normal?

It is normal to lose 50-100 hairs per day. If you are losing more than that, you may be experiencing excessive hair fall.

Can Hair Fall be Reversed?

Depending on the cause of the hair fall, it may be reversible. If it is due to genetics or aging, it may be more difficult to reverse.

When Should I See a Doctor?

If you are experiencing sudden or severe hair fall, it is best to see a doctor. They can help determine the cause of the hair fall and recommend treatment options.

Hair Care Tips

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Using a gentle shampoo can help prevent hair fall. Look for shampoos that are free of harsh chemicals and sulfates.

Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can improve blood circulation and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp gently for a few minutes every day.

Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Using a wide-toothed comb can help prevent hair fall. It is less likely to cause breakage and damage to your hair.

Conclusion

Excessive hair fall can be a frustrating and embarrassing problem. However, there are several things you can do to prevent it. By eating a balanced diet, managing stress, avoiding heat styling tools and chemical treatments, and following some simple hair care tips, you can keep your hair healthy and prevent hair fall.
