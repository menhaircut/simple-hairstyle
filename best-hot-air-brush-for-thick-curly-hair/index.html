Are you tired of fighting with your thick curly hair every morning? Do you want to achieve a salon-worthy blowout at home without spending hours with a blow dryer and round brush? A hot air brush might be the solution to your hair woes!

Hot air brushes are a combination of a hair dryer and a round brush. They blow hot air while you brush your hair, creating a smooth and voluminous look. However, not all hot air brushes are created equal, especially when it comes to thick curly hair. In this article, we'll share with you the best hot air brush for thick curly hair.

How to Use a Hot Air Brush for Thick Curly Hair

Using a hot air brush is fairly simple, but it does require some practice if you're new to it. Here are the basic steps:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair. Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the hot air.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. For thick curly hair, it's best to work in small sections to ensure that every strand is properly dried and styled.
  3. Take a section of hair and place the hot air brush at the roots.
  4. Slowly glide the brush through your hair, following the brush with the hot air.
  5. Continue brushing and drying until the section is completely dry.
  6. Repeat on the rest of your hair, working in small sections.
  7. Once you're done, use a cool shot of air to set the style and add shine.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a more detailed guide on how to use a hot air brush for thick curly hair:

  1. Start with freshly washed hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls healthy and moisturized.
  2. Towel-dry your hair to remove excess water. Don't rub your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz. Instead, gently squeeze the water out.
  3. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair. This will help prevent damage from the hot air and keep your curls looking healthy.
  4. Divide your hair into small sections. Use hair clips to keep the sections separate.
  5. Start with the bottom sections and work your way up. Take a small section of hair and place the hot air brush at the roots.
  6. Slowly glide the brush through your hair, following the brush with the hot air. Use the brush to smooth out any tangles or knots.
  7. Move the brush down the section of hair, keeping it close to the scalp. This will create volume at the roots.
  8. Continue brushing and drying until the section is completely dry. Use the cool shot button on your hot air brush to set the style and add shine.
  9. Repeat on the rest of your hair, working in small sections. Take your time and be patient!
  10. Once you're done, use a lightweight hair oil or serum to add extra shine and moisture to your curls.

Tips for Using a Hot Air Brush

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your hot air brush:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the hot air.
  • Work in small sections to ensure that every strand is properly dried and styled.
  • Start at the roots and work your way down to create volume and smoothness.
  • Use the cool shot button on your hot air brush to set the style and add shine.
  • Be patient and take your time. It might take a few tries to get the hang of using a hot air brush.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about using a hot air brush for thick curly hair:

  • Q: Can I use a hot air brush on dry hair?
  • A: It's best to use a hot air brush on damp hair. However, you can use it on dry hair if you're in a hurry, but make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  • Q: Can a hot air brush damage my hair?
  • A: Any heat styling tool has the potential to damage your hair if not used properly. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray and work in small sections to prevent damage.
  • Q: How often should I use a hot air brush?
  • A: It's best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Curly Hair

In addition to using a hot air brush, here are some hair care tips for thick curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they're wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair to add moisture and definition.
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out the water.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
