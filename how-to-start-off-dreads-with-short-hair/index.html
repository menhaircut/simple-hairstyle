How To Start Off Dreads With Short Hair

Written by Frank Jun 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in starting dreadlocks but have short hair? Don't worry, it's still possible! In this article, we will guide you through the process of starting off dreads with short hair. From how to section your hair to how to care for your new dreads, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

How to Section Your Hair

The first step in starting off dreads with short hair is to section your hair. You can do this by parting your hair into small squares or rectangles. It's important to make sure that each section is the same size to achieve a uniform look. You can use hair clips or rubber bands to keep each section in place while you work on the rest of your hair.

Tip:

Use a rat tail comb to help you create clean, even sections.

How to Start Your Dreads

Once you have sectioned your hair, it's time to start your dreads. You can do this using a variety of methods, including backcombing, twist and rip, or a combination of both. Backcombing involves teasing the hair towards the scalp to create knots, while twist and rip involves twisting sections of hair and then pulling them apart to create knots.

Tip:

It's best to do this process with damp hair so that it's easier to work with. You can also use a dreadlock wax or gel to help hold the knots in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to starting off dreads with short hair:

  1. Section your hair into small squares or rectangles.
  2. Dampen your hair with water or a dreadlock shampoo.
  3. Choose your method of starting your dreads (backcombing, twist and rip, or a combination of both).
  4. Work on each section of hair until it's knotted and secure.
  5. Use a dreadlock wax or gel to help hold the knots in place.
  6. Repeat the process until all sections of hair have been transformed into dreads.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting off dreads with short hair:

Q: Can I start dreads with relaxed hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreads with relaxed hair. However, it's important to wait until your hair is fully grown out from the relaxer before starting dreads to avoid breakage and damage to your hair.

Q: How long does it take for dreads to form?

A: It can take several months to a year for dreads to fully form, depending on your hair type and the method you use to start your dreads.

Q: How do I maintain my dreads?

A: To maintain your dreads, you should wash them regularly with a dreadlock shampoo and palm roll them to help keep their shape. You can also use a dreadlock wax or gel to help hold them in place.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you have started your dreads, it's important to take care of them to ensure they stay healthy and strong. Here are some tips for hair care for dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a dreadlock shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils on your dreads, as they can cause buildup and make your dreads heavy.
  • Palm roll your dreads regularly to help keep their shape.
  • Avoid using rubber bands or clips on your dreads, as they can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a dreadlock wax or gel to help hold your dreads in place.

Starting off dreads with short hair may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely possible. Follow these steps and tips to achieve your desired dreadlock look and don't forget to take care of your new dreads to maintain their health and strength.
