How To Thin Thick Long Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Luffy Jul 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick long hair can be gorgeous, but it can also be difficult to manage. If you have thick long hair, you may find that it can be heavy and unmanageable at times. One way to make your hair more manageable is to use thinning shears to thin it out. In this article, we will discuss how to thin thick long hair with thinning shears.

Description

Thinning shears are a special type of scissors that have teeth on one blade and a straight edge on the other. When you use thinning shears on your hair, the teeth on the blade will cut some of the hair, while the straight edge will leave the rest of the hair intact. This will create a layered effect that will make your hair look thinner and more manageable.

How to Thin Thick Long Hair with Thinning Shears

Before you begin, make sure you have a good pair of thinning shears. You can purchase them at most beauty supply stores or online. Once you have your thinning shears, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Before you begin thinning your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you get the best results.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 3: Start at the bottom

Begin thinning your hair at the bottom, near the ends. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair, starting at about halfway up the section. Close the shears and open them again, moving them up the section of hair. Repeat this process until you reach the top of the section.

Step 4: Work your way up

Move up to the next section of hair and repeat the process. Make sure you blend the sections together as you go to create a natural look.

Step 5: Check your work

Once you have thinned your hair, check your work in a mirror. Make sure everything looks even and natural. If you notice any areas that need more thinning, go back and thin them out.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when thinning your hair with thinning shears:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to larger sections
  • Don't cut too much hair at once
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet hair
  • Use a comb to help guide the thinning shears through your hair
  • Be patient and take your time

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning hair with thinning shears:

Is it possible to thin hair too much?

Yes, it is possible to thin hair too much. If you thin your hair too much, it may look stringy and damaged. It's best to thin your hair gradually and stop when you achieve the desired look.

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears can be used on most hair types, but they are best suited for thick hair. If you have fine or thin hair, thinning shears may not be the best option.

How often should I thin my hair with thinning shears?

Thinning your hair with thinning shears should be done every 6-8 weeks or as needed. It's important not to thin your hair too often, as this can lead to damage and breakage.

Hair Care

After thinning your hair with thinning shears, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair when brushing or styling
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

Thinning your thick long hair with thinning shears can be a great way to make it more manageable and give it a fresh new look. Follow these tips and steps to achieve the best results and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
