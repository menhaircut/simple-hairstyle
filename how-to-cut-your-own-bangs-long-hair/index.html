How To Cut Your Own Bangs Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 29, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without getting a full haircut, cutting your own bangs is a great option. However, cutting your own bangs can be intimidating, especially if you have long hair. But fear not, with the right tools and techniques, you can cut your own bangs with confidence. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut your own bangs long hair, with tips and tricks to help you achieve a salon-worthy look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Bangs Long Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Bangs

How to Cut Your Own Bangs Long Hair

The first step in cutting your own bangs is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also helpful to have a handheld mirror or a friend to help you with the back of your head.

Next, decide on the length and style of your bangs. Do you want them blunt or wispy? Do you want them to fall straight across your forehead or at an angle? Once you've decided on the style, it's time to start cutting.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair straight down and separate the section of hair that will become your bangs.

2. Use hair clips to secure the rest of your hair out of the way.

3. Hold the section of hair between your index and middle fingers, and use the comb to hold it taut.

4. Decide on the length you want your bangs to be, and make a small snip with your scissors. Remember, it's always better to start too long and trim more if needed.

5. Continue making small snips, moving from the center of your bangs outwards. Be sure to cut in a straight line and avoid creating choppy layers.

6. Once you've reached your desired length, check your work in the mirror. If you need to make any adjustments, do so carefully and slowly.

7. If you want wispy bangs, use your scissors to create small, diagonal cuts into the ends of your hair.

8. Release the rest of your hair from the clips and style as desired.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to your desired length.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to avoid creating split ends.
  • When in doubt, go to a professional stylist.
  • Consider using hair-thinning scissors for a more natural look.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short, as they will likely shrink up when they dry.

FAQs

Q: Can you cut bangs on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut bangs on curly hair. However, it's important to remember that curly hair shrinks up when it dries, so be cautious when cutting.

Q: How often should you trim your bangs?

A: It's recommended to trim your bangs every 2-3 weeks to maintain their shape.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Bangs

After cutting your bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain their shape. Use a nourishing hair oil to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Consider using a dry shampoo to keep your bangs looking fresh in between washes. And finally, be sure to trim your bangs regularly to maintain their shape.

Cutting your own bangs can be nerve-wracking, but with these tips and tricks, you can achieve a salon-worthy look at home. Remember to start with small snips and work your way up to your desired length, and always use a sharp pair of scissors. Happy cutting!
