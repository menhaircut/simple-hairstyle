Will I Suit A Fringe A Comprehensive Guide To Find Out

Written by William Sep 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering getting a fringe but not sure if it will suit you? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people have this dilemma before making a big change to their hairstyle. In this article, we will guide you through the process of determining whether a fringe will suit you or not. Read on to find out more.

Description

A fringe, also known as bangs, is a hairstyle where the hair is cut short and straight across the forehead. It can be styled in many different ways, such as straight, side-swept, or parted in the middle. Fringes have been a popular hairstyle for many years and can suit a variety of face shapes and hair types.

How to Determine If You Will Suit a Fringe

Step 1: Consider Your Face Shape

The first thing to consider when deciding if a fringe will suit you is your face shape. Different fringes suit different face shapes. Here are some general guidelines:

  • If you have a round face, try a side-swept fringe that is longer on the sides.
  • If you have a square face, try a soft, wispy fringe that is shorter in the middle and longer on the sides.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, try a long, side-swept fringe that is tapered at the ends.
  • If you have an oval face, you're lucky! You can pretty much pull off any type of fringe.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Type

The next thing to consider is your hair type. Fringes work best on hair that is thick and straight. If you have curly hair, a fringe may be harder to maintain and style. However, if you have wavy hair, a fringe can work well if styled properly.

Step 3: Consider Your Lifestyle

Another thing to consider is your lifestyle. If you're constantly on the go and don't have much time to style your hair, a fringe may not be the best option for you. Fringes require regular trimming and styling to look their best. If you have a busy lifestyle, consider getting a low-maintenance style instead.

Tips for Getting a Fringe

  • Bring pictures to your stylist to show them the type of fringe you want.
  • Ask your stylist for their opinion on whether a fringe will suit you.
  • Start with a longer fringe and gradually go shorter if you're unsure.
  • Invest in good quality styling tools to make styling your fringe easier.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Will a fringe make my face look rounder?

It depends on the type of fringe you get. If you have a round face, a side-swept fringe that is longer on the sides can actually make your face appear slimmer.

Can I still wear a fringe if I have glasses?

Absolutely! Glasses can actually complement a fringe and vice versa. Just make sure you choose glasses that suit your face shape and the type of fringe you have.

How often do I need to trim my fringe?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short your fringe is. On average, you should trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care Tips for Fringes

Frizz and oil can make your fringe look messy and unflattering. Here are some tips to keep your fringe looking its best:

  • Wash your fringe regularly to keep it clean and oil-free.
  • Blow-dry your fringe with a round brush to smooth out any frizz.
  • Use a small amount of styling product, such as hair wax or pomade, to keep your fringe in place.
  • Avoid touching your fringe too much as this can transfer oil from your hands to your hair.

Now that you know how to determine if a fringe will suit you, it's time to take the plunge and try out this popular hairstyle. Remember, if you're unsure, start with a longer fringe and work your way up to a shorter one. And always consult with your stylist for their opinion and advice. Good luck!
