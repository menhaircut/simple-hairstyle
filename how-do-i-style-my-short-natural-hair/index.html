How Do I Style My Short Natural Hair A Guide To Effortless Hairdos

Written by Robby Jul 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Short natural hair is stunning and versatile, and it can be styled in numerous ways to suit your personality and mood. Whether you have a TWA (teeny weeny afro) or a slightly longer length, you can create chic and stylish looks with just a few tweaks. In this guide, we'll show you how to style your short natural hair and give you tips on how to maintain healthy and luscious locks.

How to Style Short Natural Hair

Styling short natural hair can be as easy or as complex as you want it to be. You can go for a simple wash-and-go style, rock a funky twist-out or braid-out, or experiment with different hair accessories to add some flair to your look. Here are some popular styles that you can try:

The Wash-and-Go

The wash-and-go is a great style for those who want a low-maintenance, fuss-free look. To achieve this style, simply wash your hair, apply your favorite leave-in conditioner, and scrunch your hair to enhance your natural curls. You can let your hair air dry or use a diffuser to speed up the drying process.

The Twist-Out

The twist-out is a classic style that never goes out of fashion. To create this style, divide your hair into small sections, apply your favorite styling product, and twist each section tightly. Once your hair is completely dry, unravel the twists and fluff your hair to create volume.

The Braid-Out

The braid-out is similar to the twist-out, but instead of twisting your hair, you braid it. This style creates a more defined curl pattern and can be worn for several days with minimal touch-ups.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Short Natural Hair

Now that you know some of the popular styles for short natural hair, here's a step-by-step guide to achieving these looks:

The Wash-and-Go

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Scrunch your hair to enhance your curls.
  4. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser to speed up the drying process.

The Twist-Out

  1. Divide your hair into small sections.
  2. Apply a twisting cream or gel to each section.
  3. Twist each section tightly, making sure to twist all the way to the ends.
  4. Let your hair air dry or use a hooded dryer to speed up the drying process.
  5. Once your hair is completely dry, unravel the twists and fluff your hair to create volume.

The Braid-Out

  1. Divide your hair into small sections.
  2. Apply a braiding cream or gel to each section.
  3. Braid each section tightly, making sure to braid all the way to the ends.
  4. Let your hair air dry or use a hooded dryer to speed up the drying process.
  5. Once your hair is completely dry, unravel the braids and fluff your hair to create volume.

Tips for Maintaining Short Natural Hair

Maintaining short natural hair requires a bit of effort, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your hair healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin bonnet or sleeping on a satin pillowcase.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Experiment with different styles to keep your look fresh and exciting.

FAQs About Styling Short Natural Hair

Q: Can I straighten my short natural hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your short natural hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant and avoid using high heat settings. Also, keep in mind that frequent heat styling can damage your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my short natural hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have dry hair, you may want to wash it less often to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. If you exercise frequently or live in a humid climate, you may need to wash your hair more often to prevent sweat and oil buildup.

Hair Care Tips for Short Natural Hair

Keeping your short natural hair healthy and nourished requires a bit of TLC. Here are some tips for maintaining gorgeous locks:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using products with harsh chemicals, such as sulfates, parabens, and silicones.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and stimulate hair growth.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to support healthy hair growth.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.

With these tips and techniques, you can create stunning and effortless hairstyles for your short natural hair. Whether you prefer a wash-and-go style or a more elaborate twist-out, you can rock any look with confidence and flair.
