How Long Are Knotless Braids Supposed To Last A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're thinking of getting knotless braids, one of the questions you may have is how long they're supposed to last. Knotless braids have become increasingly popular in recent years due to their natural look and low tension on the scalp. In this article, we'll talk about how long knotless braids are supposed to last and provide some tips on how to make them last longer.

Table of Contents

  • How To Get Knotless Braids
  • How Long Knotless Braids Are Supposed To Last
  • Tips To Make Knotless Braids Last Longer

How To Get Knotless Braids

Knotless braids are a type of protective style that involves braiding hair without using a knot at the base. This reduces tension on the scalp and can prevent breakage and hair loss. Here's how to get knotless braids:

  1. Wash and condition your hair thoroughly.
  2. Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb.
  3. Part your hair into sections using a rat-tail comb.
  4. Take a small section of hair and begin braiding it, starting at the roots.
  5. Add extensions to the braid as you go, using a crochet hook.
  6. Braid all the way to the ends of the hair and secure with a rubber band.
  7. Repeat the process until all sections of hair are braided.

It's important to note that knotless braids can take several hours to complete, depending on the length and thickness of your hair. It's best to have a professional stylist do them for you.

How Long Knotless Braids Are Supposed To Last

Knotless braids are designed to last for several weeks, typically 4-6 weeks. However, their lifespan can vary depending on a few factors:

  • The quality of the extensions used
  • The thickness of your natural hair
  • Your hair care routine
  • Your lifestyle (e.g. swimming, exercise)

If you take good care of your knotless braids and avoid activities that can cause them to become damaged or tangled, they can last even longer than 6 weeks.

Tips To Make Knotless Braids Last Longer

If you want to make your knotless braids last as long as possible, here are some tips to follow:

  • Avoid getting your braids wet as much as possible. If you must wash your hair, use a spray bottle to dampen your scalp and use a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Sleep with a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and tangling.
  • Avoid heat styling your braids, as this can damage the extensions and your natural hair.
  • Keep your scalp moisturized using a lightweight oil or leave-in conditioner.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles or accessories that can pull on your braids.

Hair Care Tips For Knotless Braids

Even though knotless braids are a protective style, it's still important to take care of your hair while it's in braids. Here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Moisturize your scalp regularly using a lightweight oil or leave-in conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your braids and cause them to become frizzy.
  • Don't leave your braids in for too long. Even though knotless braids can last for several weeks, it's important to take them out after a certain amount of time to allow your hair and scalp to breathe.
  • When taking out your braids, be gentle to avoid damaging your natural hair.

FAQs About Knotless Braids

Q: Are knotless braids better for your hair than traditional braids?

A: Yes, knotless braids are generally considered to be better for your hair than traditional braids because they put less tension on the scalp and can prevent breakage and hair loss.

Q: How long do knotless braids take to install?

A: Knotless braids can take several hours to install, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Q: Can you swim with knotless braids?

A: While it's possible to swim with knotless braids, it's not recommended as it can cause the braids to become tangled and damaged.

Q: How do you take care of your hair while it's in knotless braids?

A: To take care of your hair while it's in knotless braids, moisturize your scalp regularly, avoid using heavy styling products, and don't leave your braids in for too long.

Overall, knotless braids can last for several weeks with proper care and maintenance. By following the tips and hair care advice in this article, you can make your knotless braids last as long as possible while keeping your natural hair healthy and strong.
