How To Cut Layers Around Face At Home Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 18, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your old, boring hair and want to switch things up? One way to do that is by cutting layers around your face. This hairstyle is perfect for those who want to frame their face and add some volume to their hair. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut layers around your face at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layers Around Face at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers Around Face
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Cut Layers Around Face at Home

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips.

Follow these steps to cut layers around your face:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Part your hair down the middle and clip the two sections. This will make it easier to cut layers around your face.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the front of your face and hold it in between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process for the other side of your face.

Step 4: Create Layers

Once you've cut the hair in front of your face, create layers by taking small sections of hair from the back and cutting them at an angle. Make sure to blend the layers with the hair in front of your face.

Step 5: Check Your Work

After you've finished cutting, check your work in the mirror to make sure your layers are even.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to cutting layers around your face:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

As mentioned before, you'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips. Make sure your scissors are sharp to prevent split ends.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 3: Part Your Hair

Part your hair down the middle and clip the two sections. This will make it easier to cut layers around your face.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: the front left, front right, back left, and back right.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Start with the front sections first. Take a small section of hair from the front of your face and hold it in between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process for the other side of your face.

Step 6: Create Layers

Once you've cut the hair in front of your face, create layers by taking small sections of hair from the back and cutting them at an angle. Make sure to blend the layers with the hair in front of your face.

Step 7: Check Your Work

After you've finished cutting, check your work in the mirror to make sure your layers are even. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim the sections that need it.

Tips for Cutting Layers Around Face

Here are some additional tips to help you cut layers around your face:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a comb to separate the hair and make it easier to cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Take breaks to stretch your hands and avoid cramps.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes.

FAQs

Can I cut my layers with regular scissors?

While you can use regular scissors to cut your hair, it's best to use hair-cutting scissors. They are sharper and designed specifically for cutting hair, which will result in a cleaner cut and fewer split ends.

How often should I cut my layers?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the health of your hair and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my layers when my hair is wet or dry?

It's best to cut your layers when your hair is dry. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven cuts.

Hair Care Tips

After you've cut your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to hydrate your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to promote hair growth and health.

Cutting layers around your face can be a fun and easy way to switch up your hairstyle. By following this step-by-step guide and using the right tools, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and take care of your hair after you're done.
