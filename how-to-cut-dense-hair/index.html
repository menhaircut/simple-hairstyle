How To Cut Dense Hair Tips Steps And Hair Care

Written by Oliver Sep 29, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have dense hair, you know how challenging it can be to manage and style. But cutting dense hair can be equally challenging. If you’re planning to cut your dense hair at home, this guide will help you achieve salon-worthy results. Read on to learn how to cut dense hair, step-by-step, and how to take care of your hair after the cut.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Dense Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Dense Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting Dense Hair

How to Cut Dense Hair

Cutting dense hair is different from cutting thin or medium hair. Dense hair has more volume and thickness, so it requires a different approach. Here are some tips for cutting dense hair:

1. Use Sharp Scissors

Sharp scissors are a must when it comes to cutting dense hair. Dull scissors can make the cut uneven and can damage your hair. Invest in a good pair of scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair.

2. Section Your Hair

Sectioning your hair before cutting it is important, especially if you have dense hair. Divide your hair into small sections and clip them together. This will make it easier to cut your hair and ensure that you don’t miss any sections.

3. Start with Small Cuts

When cutting dense hair, it’s best to start with small cuts. This will make it easier to control the amount of hair you’re cutting and ensure that you don’t cut too much at once. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.

4. Cut at an Angle

Cutting dense hair at an angle can help to create layers and add dimension to your hair. Hold your scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut your hair in small sections. This will create a natural-looking layered effect.

5. Trim the Ends

Trimming the ends of your hair is important, especially if you have dense hair. This will help to remove split ends and keep your hair healthy. Use the point-cutting technique to trim the ends of your hair. This involves cutting into the ends of your hair with the tips of your scissors.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of cutting dense hair, here’s a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any dirt or buildup and make it easier to cut your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections and clip them together. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair in small sections, starting at the back of your head. Use sharp scissors and cut at a 45-degree angle. Remember to start with small cuts and work your way up.

Step 4: Trim the Ends

After you’ve cut your hair, trim the ends using the point-cutting technique. This will help to remove split ends and keep your hair healthy.

Tips for Cutting Dense Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you cut your dense hair:

1. Don’t Cut Too Much at Once

Cutting too much hair at once can lead to uneven cuts and damage your hair. Start with small cuts and work your way up.

2. Use the Right Scissors

Invest in a good pair of scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. This will ensure that you get a clean, even cut.

3. Cut Your Hair When It’s Dry

Cutting your hair when it’s dry can help you see how your hair will look once it’s styled. This will help you achieve the look you want.

4. Take Breaks

Cutting dense hair can be time-consuming and tiring. Take breaks to rest your hands and prevent cramping.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting dense hair:

1. Can I Cut My Own Dense Hair?

Yes, you can cut your own dense hair. However, if you’re not comfortable cutting your hair, it’s best to leave it to a professional.

2. How Often Should I Cut My Dense Hair?

It’s recommended to cut your dense hair every 8-12 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

3. Can I Use Regular Scissors to Cut My Dense Hair?

No, regular scissors can damage your hair and make the cut uneven. Invest in a good pair of scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Dense Hair

After cutting your dense hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Deep Conditioner

Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, as they can damage your hair.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 8-12 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

4. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage.

Cutting dense hair can be challenging, but with the right tools, techniques, and hair care, you can achieve salon-worthy results at home. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and take breaks as needed. Happy cutting!
