Is Selling Cigarettes Haram

Written by Bardi Oct 22, 2023 · 3 min read

As a Muslim, it is our responsibility to follow the teachings of Islam and abide by its laws. One of the most discussed topics among Muslims is whether selling cigarettes is haram or not. In this article, we will discuss this topic in detail to provide you with a better understanding of this issue.

What is Haram?

Haram is an Arabic term that means “forbidden” in Islam. It refers to anything that is prohibited by Islamic law. Muslims are obliged to avoid all haram activities and practices in their daily lives.

Is Selling Cigarettes Haram?

The answer to this question is not straightforward. Some scholars believe that selling cigarettes is haram because they are harmful to health and can cause severe diseases such as cancer. Moreover, smoking is considered a bad habit that Muslims should avoid. Therefore, selling cigarettes could be seen as promoting a harmful activity.

On the other hand, some scholars argue that selling cigarettes is not haram because it is a legal product that is allowed by the government. Therefore, as long as the seller is not promoting smoking and is not selling cigarettes to minors, it could be considered halal.

What Does Islam Say About Smoking?

Islam encourages Muslims to take care of their bodies and avoid anything that could harm them. Smoking is considered a bad habit that can cause severe damage to health. Therefore, smoking is discouraged in Islam, and Muslims are advised to avoid it.

What Should Muslims Do?

Muslims should always be careful about what they consume and promote. While selling cigarettes may not be haram, it is essential to consider the harmful effects of smoking on health. Therefore, Muslims should avoid promoting smoking and encourage others to quit smoking for their own good.

Conclusion

Is selling cigarettes haram? It is a complicated question that requires a nuanced answer. While some scholars argue that selling cigarettes is haram, others believe it is not. However, as Muslims, we should always prioritize our health and well-being and avoid anything that could harm us.

Therefore, even if selling cigarettes is not haram, we should avoid promoting smoking and encourage others to quit smoking for their own good. As Muslims, it is our responsibility to follow the teachings of Islam and abide by its laws to live a healthy and fulfilling life.
