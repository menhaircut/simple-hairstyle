Why Does Curly Hair Make My Face Look Fat? - Hair Care Tips

Have you ever noticed that your face looks wider or rounder when you wear your hair curly? If so, you may be wondering why curly hair has this effect on your facial appearance. In this article, we will explore why curly hair can make your face look fat and provide some tips on how to style your hair to flatter your face shape.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Style Your Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Flattering Your Face Shape
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Description

Curly hair has a natural tendency to expand and take up space. This means that when you wear your hair curly, it can create the illusion of a wider or rounder face. This effect is particularly noticeable if you have a round or square face shape, as the curls can emphasize the width of your cheekbones and jawline.

How to Style Your Curly Hair

If you have curly hair and want to avoid making your face look fat, there are a few styling tricks you can try. Here are some options:

1. Blow Dry with a Diffuser

Using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer can help to dry your curls without creating too much volume. This can help to keep your hair closer to your head, which can make your face look slimmer.

2. Use a Smoothing Serum

Using a smoothing serum can help to weigh down your curls and prevent them from expanding too much. Apply a small amount of serum to your damp hair before styling.

3. Try a Half-Up Style

Wearing your hair half-up can help to create the illusion of a longer face. Pull the top half of your hair back and secure it with a clip or elastic band.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to styling your curly hair:

1. Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to keep your curls soft and defined.

2. Apply a Smoothing Serum

Apply a small amount of smoothing serum to your damp hair, focusing on the ends.

3. Blow Dry with a Diffuser

Using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer, dry your hair on a low heat setting. Be sure to hold the diffuser close to your head to prevent your curls from expanding too much.

4. Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to tousle your curls and create the shape you want. You can also try pulling the top half of your hair back and securing it with a clip or elastic band.

Tips for Flattering Your Face Shape

In addition to styling your hair in a way that flatters your face shape, there are a few other tips you can try:

1. Wear V-Neck Shirts

V-neck shirts can help to elongate your neck and create the illusion of a slimmer face.

2. Contour Your Cheekbones

Using a contouring powder or cream can help to define your cheekbones and make your face look slimmer.

3. Avoid Round Glasses

If you wear glasses, avoid round frames, as they can make your face look wider. Instead, try rectangular or square frames.

FAQ

Q: Does curly hair always make my face look fat?

A: Not necessarily. The effect of curly hair on your facial appearance will depend on your face shape and the way you style your hair.

Q: Can I still wear my hair curly if I have a round face?

A: Absolutely! Just be mindful of the way you style your hair. Try using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer and avoid creating too much volume.

Q: What other hairstyles can I try to flatter my face shape?

A: If you have a round face, try a long bob or a side-swept fringe. If you have a square face, try a layered cut or a side part.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Keeping your curly hair healthy and moisturized is essential for maintaining its shape and preventing frizz. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair is more prone to dryness, so using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner is essential. Look for products that contain natural oils like coconut or argan oil.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb can help to prevent breakage and preserve the shape of your curls. Avoid using brushes, as they can cause frizz.

3. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to keep your curls soft and defined. Try using a deep conditioner once a week.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause frizz. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.


By following these tips, you can rock your curly hair without worrying about making your face look fat. Remember, the key is to find a style that flatters your face shape and makes you feel confident.
