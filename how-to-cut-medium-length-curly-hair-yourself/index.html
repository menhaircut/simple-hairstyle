How To Cut Medium Length Curly Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting curly hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're doing it yourself. But with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. In this post, we'll walk you through the steps on how to cut medium length curly hair yourself, so you can achieve the look you want without having to leave your home.

Table of Contents

  • How to prepare for cutting curly hair
  • Step-by-step guide on cutting medium length curly hair
  • Tips for cutting curly hair
  • FAQ for cutting medium length curly hair
  • Hair care tips for maintaining curly hair

How to Prepare for Cutting Curly Hair

Before you start cutting your curly hair, it's important to prepare yourself and your tools. Here are some things you should do:

  • Wash your hair and let it dry naturally. This will help you see the natural shape of your curls.
  • Get a good pair of scissors. Make sure they're sharp and have a pointy tip for precision.
  • Have a mirror and a comb on hand. You'll need these to see the back of your head and to section your hair.
  • Wear a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on.

Step-by-Step Guide on Cutting Medium Length Curly Hair

Now that you're prepared, here's how to cut your medium length curly hair:

Step 1: Section your hair

Use the comb to section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back.

Step 2: Cut the back

Start with the back sections. Take a small section of hair and twist it. Use the scissors to cut the ends of the twisted hair. Repeat this process for all the sections in the back.

Step 3: Cut the front

Move on to the front sections. Take a small section of hair and twist it away from your face. Cut the ends of the twisted hair. Repeat this process for all the sections in the front.

Step 4: Check for balance

Use the mirror to check for balance. If one side is longer than the other, make adjustments as needed.

Step 5: Refine the shape

Take small sections of hair and cut at an angle to create layers. This will give your curls more shape and movement.

Step 6: Trim the ends

Finally, trim the ends of your hair to clean up any split ends or unevenness.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting your curly hair:

  • Always cut your hair when it's dry. Curly hair can shrink when it dries, so cutting it when it's wet can result in a shorter length than you intended.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut small sections at a time to ensure precision.
  • Don't pull your hair too tight when cutting, as this can result in shorter hair than you intended.

FAQ for Cutting Medium Length Curly Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting medium length curly hair:

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself. Just make sure you have the right tools and follow the proper technique.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my curly hair wet or dry?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, so you can see the natural shape of your curls.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your curly hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can cause breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent tangles and breakage.

Cutting medium length curly hair yourself can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Just remember to prepare yourself and your tools, follow the proper steps, and take care of your hair afterwards. Happy cutting!
