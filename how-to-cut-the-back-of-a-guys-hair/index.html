How To Cut The Back Of A Guy s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts for your guy? Or maybe you just want to try your hand at cutting hair? Whatever the reason, cutting the back of a guy's hair can be a bit intimidating. But fear not! With the right tools and techniques, you can give your guy a fresh cut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut the back of a guy's hair in easy-to-understand steps.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning the Hair
  • Cutting the Hair
  • Blending the Hair
  • Clean-Up
  • Tips for a Great Cut
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Cape or towel (to protect clothing)
  • Mirror

Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure your guy's hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be harder to cut evenly. Have your guy put on the cape or wrap a towel around his neck to protect his clothes. Then, use the comb to comb the hair straight down from the crown of the head.

Sectioning the Hair

Sectioning the hair will make it easier to cut and ensure a more even cut. Start by creating a part down the center of the head, from the hairline to the nape of the neck. Then, create a horizontal part about an inch above the nape of the neck. Clip the top section of hair up and out of the way. This will be the hair you cut later.

Cutting the Hair

Starting at the nape of the neck, use the clippers to create a baseline. This is the shortest length of hair you want to cut. Use a comb to lift a section of hair up and away from the head, and run the clippers over the hair in an upward motion. Continue doing this, working your way up the head, until you reach the top of the horizontal part you created earlier.

Blending the Hair

Now it's time to blend the hair between the baseline and the top section you clipped up earlier. To do this, use your scissors to cut small, diagonal sections of hair. Hold the scissors vertically and snip into the hair, rather than cutting straight across. This will create a more natural look. Work your way around the head, blending the hair until it all looks even.

Clean-Up

Once you've finished cutting, use the clippers to clean up any stray hairs around the nape of the neck and the sideburns. Then, remove the clips or elastics holding the top section of hair up and blend any longer pieces into the rest of the hair.

Tips for a Great Cut

  • Start with a longer guard on the clippers and work your way down to a shorter guard as you go. This will give you more control over the length of the cut.
  • Use your fingers to gauge the length of the hair. Don't rely solely on the guards on the clippers.
  • If you're unsure about how much to cut, start with a little and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't glue hair back on!

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my guy's hair?

A: This depends on the length of the hair and how fast it grows. As a general rule of thumb, guys with shorter hair should get a cut every 4-6 weeks, while guys with longer hair can go 6-8 weeks between cuts.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut hair?

A: While you can use regular scissors, it's best to use scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. These will be sharper and give you a cleaner cut.

Hair Care After the Cut

After the cut, make sure to shampoo and condition the hair to keep it healthy. Use a high-quality hair product to style the hair, if desired. And don't forget to schedule your guy's next haircut!

Now that you know how to cut the back of a guy's hair, you can save money and give your guy a fresh cut whenever he needs it. Remember to take your time and use the right tools, and you'll have a great-looking cut in no time.
