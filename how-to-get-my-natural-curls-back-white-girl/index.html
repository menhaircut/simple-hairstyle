How To Get My Natural Curls Back A Guide For White Girls

Written by William Aug 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of straightening your hair every day and missing your natural curls? As a white girl, it can be challenging to embrace your natural curls, especially if you've been relaxing your hair for years. But don't worry, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to get your natural curls back.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Before we dive into the details, let's first understand what we mean by "natural curls." Natural curls are the curls that your hair naturally forms without any heat styling or chemical treatments. If you've been relaxing your hair, it means that you've been chemically straightening it, which can damage your hair and alter its natural texture.

Getting your natural curls back will require some patience and effort, but the results are worth it. Embracing your natural curls can also help improve the health of your hair and increase your confidence.

How to

The first step to getting your natural curls back is to stop relaxing your hair. If you're used to getting your hair relaxed at a salon, you'll need to find a new hairstyle while you're transitioning to your natural curls. You can try protective hairstyles like braids, twists, or wigs to give your hair a break and protect it from damage.

Next, you'll need to start taking care of your hair differently than you did when it was relaxed. Natural curls require more moisture, so you'll need to use hydrating products like leave-in conditioners, oils, and creams. You'll also need to avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and blow dryers, as they can damage your hair and alter its natural texture.

Step by step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get your natural curls back:

  1. Stop relaxing your hair
  2. Find a new hairstyle while you're transitioning
  3. Use hydrating products like leave-in conditioners, oils, and creams
  4. Avoid using heat styling tools
  5. Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo
  6. Deep condition your hair once a week
  7. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry
  8. Detangle your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb
  9. Air dry your hair instead of using a blow dryer
  10. Be patient and give your hair time to adjust to its natural texture

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get your natural curls back:

  • Get regular trims to remove damaged ends
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz
  • Use a diffuser attachment when blow drying your hair to enhance your curls
  • Experiment with different natural hairstyles to find what works best for you

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to get my natural curls back?

A: It can take anywhere from a few months to a year or more, depending on how long you've been relaxing your hair and how often you get trims.

Q: Can I still dye my hair while transitioning to my natural curls?

A: Yes, but it's best to use a gentle, ammonia-free hair dye and avoid lightening your hair too much, as it can damage your curls.

Hair Care

To maintain the health of your natural curls, it's important to follow a consistent hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Deep condition your hair once a week
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Detangle your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz

By following these tips and being patient, you can get your natural curls back and embrace your unique hair texture. Remember, every curl is beautiful, and it's time to show yours off!
