How To Cut Hair At Home Easy A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have no experience. However, with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily at home. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut hair at home easy, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • What You’ll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

What You’ll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you’ll need a few tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause uneven cuts and damage to your hair.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. Brush out any tangles or knots with a comb. If your hair is curly, it’s best to cut it while it’s dry, as curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair makes it easier to cut and ensures that you don’t miss any spots. Start by parting your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, divide each side into two sections, creating four sections in total.

Clip or tie each section out of the way, leaving one section loose to work on at a time.

Cutting Your Hair

Start with the section at the back of your head, near the nape of your neck. Comb through the section, making sure it’s smooth and tangle-free.

Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut off the desired length. Be sure to cut straight across, and avoid angling the scissors or cutting in a curve.

Move on to the next section, working your way around your head. For the side sections, pull the hair down and cut to the desired length, making sure both sides are even.

Finishing Touches

Once you’ve cut all the sections, release your hair and check for any uneven spots or sections that need touching up. Use your scissors to fix any mistakes, and make sure everything is even.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up, rather than cutting off too much at once.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair up and away from your scalp, creating tension that makes it easier to cut.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as wet hair is heavier and longer than dry hair, which can lead to uneven cuts.
  • Take breaks if you get tired, as cutting your hair can be a lengthy process.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair with regular scissors?

While you can technically use regular scissors to cut your hair, it’s not recommended. Regular scissors are often dull and can cause damage to your hair. It’s best to use sharp hair-cutting scissors for the best results.

How often should I cut my hair?

It’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and looking its best. However, the frequency of haircuts can vary depending on your hair type and personal preference.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim any split ends regularly, and consider using a hair mask or oil treatment to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Now that you know how to cut hair at home easy, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. Follow these steps and tips for a successful at-home haircut.
