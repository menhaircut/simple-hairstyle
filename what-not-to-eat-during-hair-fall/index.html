What Not To Eat During Hair Fall A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Oct 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair fall and wondering what could be the cause of it? While there could be several factors contributing to hair fall, your diet plays a crucial role. Eating certain foods can worsen your hair fall and make it difficult for you to maintain your luscious locks. In this article, we bring you a complete guide on what not to eat during hair fall. Read on to find out more.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Hair Fall
  • What Not to Eat During Hair Fall
  • Healthy Alternatives to Promote Hair Growth
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Causes of Hair Fall

Hair fall is a common problem that affects people of all ages and genders. Some of the common causes of hair fall include:

  • Genetics
  • Age
  • Nutritional deficiencies
  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Stress and anxiety
  • Medical conditions such as thyroid disorders, alopecia, and scalp infections

While some of these causes are beyond our control, we can certainly take steps to improve our diet and promote healthy hair growth.

What Not to Eat During Hair Fall

Here are some of the foods that you should avoid if you are experiencing hair fall:

1. Processed Foods

Processed foods are usually high in sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats. They offer little to no nutritional value and can contribute to hair fall. Replace processed foods with fresh fruits and vegetables to ensure that you are getting essential vitamins and minerals that promote healthy hair growth.

2. Fried and Fatty Foods

Fried and fatty foods are high in unhealthy fats that can cause inflammation in the body. Inflammation can damage hair follicles and lead to hair fall. Instead, opt for lean sources of protein such as fish, chicken, and turkey.

3. Sugary Foods

Sugar can cause insulin spikes that can lead to hormonal imbalances. Hormonal imbalances can contribute to hair fall. Avoid sugary foods and opt for natural sweeteners such as honey, maple syrup, and stevia.

4. Alcohol and Caffeine

Alcohol and caffeine can dehydrate the body and lead to hair fall. They can also cause hormonal imbalances that can contribute to hair fall. Limit your intake of alcohol and caffeine and opt for herbal teas and water instead.

Healthy Alternatives to Promote Hair Growth

Here are some of the healthy foods that you can include in your diet to promote hair growth:

  • Leafy green vegetables such as spinach and kale
  • Fruits such as berries, oranges, and bananas
  • Lean sources of protein such as fish, chicken, and turkey
  • Healthy fats such as nuts, seeds, and avocado
  • Whole grains such as quinoa and brown rice
  • Water and herbal teas

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Here are some tips that can help you maintain healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly and use a gentle shampoo
  • Avoid using hot water and opt for lukewarm water instead
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as straighteners and curling irons
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Avoid pulling your hair too tightly in hairstyles such as braids and ponytails

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can stress cause hair fall?

Yes, stress can cause hair fall. It can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle and lead to hair fall.

2. How long does it take for hair to grow back after hair fall?

It can take several months for hair to grow back after hair fall. However, with a healthy diet and proper hair care, you can promote healthy hair growth.

3. Can nutritional deficiencies cause hair fall?

Yes, nutritional deficiencies can cause hair fall. Deficiencies in vitamins such as B12, D, and E, as well as minerals such as iron and zinc, can contribute to hair fall.

In conclusion, what you eat plays a crucial role in maintaining healthy hair. Avoid processed foods, fried and fatty foods, sugary foods, alcohol, and caffeine. Instead, opt for fresh fruits and vegetables, lean sources of protein, healthy fats, whole grains, and water. Follow the tips mentioned in this article and take steps to maintain healthy hair. If you are still experiencing hair fall, consult a dermatologist for further evaluation.
