How to Achieve Best Short Hairstyles for Thin Hair 2021

Before we dive into the hairstyles, let's take a look at some tips to keep in mind while styling thin hair.

1. Avoid Heavy Products

Heavy products can weigh down your thin hair, making it look flat and lifeless. Opt for lightweight styling products that provide volume and texture without weighing down your hair.

2. Use a Round Brush

Using a round brush while blow-drying your hair can add volume and create a fuller look. Make sure to hold the brush at the roots and lift your hair while blow-drying.

3. Add Layers

Layers can create the illusion of volume and add texture to your hair. Ask your hairstylist to add layers to your hair while keeping the length short.

Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic short hairstyle that works wonders for thin hair. It is a low-maintenance hairstyle that requires minimal styling. The short length adds volume to your hair while making it look fuller.

To achieve the pixie cut, ask your hairstylist to keep the length short, with the hair at the back of your head slightly longer than the hair at the front. Style your hair with a lightweight styling product to add texture and volume.

Bob Cut

The bob cut is another popular short hairstyle for thin hair. It is a versatile hairstyle that can be customized to suit your face shape and hair texture.

To achieve the bob cut, ask your hairstylist to keep the length short, with the hair at the back of your head slightly longer than the hair at the front. You can opt for a blunt cut or add layers to create texture and volume.

Layered Bob

The layered bob is a variation of the classic bob cut that adds layers to create texture and volume. It is a great hairstyle for thin hair as it creates the illusion of fullness.

To achieve the layered bob, ask your hairstylist to add layers to your hair while keeping the length short. Style your hair with a lightweight styling product to add texture and volume.

Angled Bob

The angled bob is a modern take on the classic bob cut that adds an edgy twist to your hairstyle. It is a great hairstyle for thin hair as it adds volume and creates the illusion of thickness.

To achieve the angled bob, ask your hairstylist to keep the length short, with the hair at the back of your head slightly longer than the hair at the front. The hair at the front should be angled towards your chin to create a face-framing effect. Style your hair with a lightweight styling product to add texture and volume.

Tips for Styling Thin Hair

1. Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can add texture and volume to your hair. Use it to refresh your hair and add volume between washes.

2. Avoid Overheating

Excessive heat can damage your hair and make it look lifeless. Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis and use a heat protectant spray when you do.

3. Embrace Your Natural Texture

Your natural hair texture can add volume and create a unique look. Embrace your natural texture and work with it instead of against it.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I dye my thin hair?

Yes, you can dye your thin hair, but make sure to use a gentle hair dye that won't damage your hair. Opt for a hair dye that contains natural ingredients and doesn't contain harsh chemicals.

2. How often should I wash my thin hair?

It is recommended to wash your hair every other day or every two days to prevent excessive oil buildup. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look dry and lifeless.

Hair Care for Thin Hair

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is specifically formulated for thin hair. Avoid shampoos that contain harsh chemicals and sulfates that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Condition Your Hair

Conditioning your hair can add moisture and prevent breakage. Use a lightweight conditioner that won't weigh down your hair.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can prevent split ends and breakage, making your hair look fuller and healthier. It is recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.

With these tips and hairstyles, you can rock a fabulous look with your thin hair. Remember to embrace your natural texture and work with it to achieve the perfect hairstyle.
