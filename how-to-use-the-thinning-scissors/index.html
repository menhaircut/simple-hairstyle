How To Use The Thinning Scissors Like A Pro A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning scissors are a must-have tool for any hairdresser or home stylist. They are designed to help you create a more natural and blended look by removing bulk and weight from the hair. However, using them can be tricky if you don't know what you're doing. In this guide, we'll show you how to use the thinning scissors properly and give you some tips on how to care for your hair afterwards.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Use the Thinning Scissors
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Thinning Scissors
  • FAQs

How to Use the Thinning Scissors

Thinning scissors are used to remove bulk from the hair without shortening the length. They have a serrated edge that cuts some of the hair while leaving some uncut. This results in a more natural and layered look. Here are the steps to follow when using thinning scissors:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb it out to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Section off the hair that you want to thin. It's best to work in small sections so that you have more control over the hair.
  3. Hold the thinning scissors with the serrated edge facing upwards. Starting from the bottom of the hair section, cut into the hair with the scissors. Make sure you don't cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven finish.
  4. Continue cutting into the hair, working your way up the section. Use a comb to check that the hair is blending in nicely and that there are no harsh lines.
  5. Repeat the process on the other sections of hair that you want to thin. Remember to work in small sections and to check your progress as you go.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your thinning scissors:

  • Always start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be more difficult to work with and can result in an uneven finish.
  • Use a comb to help you blend the hair as you go. This will ensure that you don't leave any harsh lines.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to work in small sections so that you have more control over the hair.
  • Be careful not to cut too close to the scalp. This can result in a choppy and uneven finish.
  • Remember that thinning scissors are designed to remove bulk and weight from the hair. They are not meant to be used to shorten the length of the hair.

FAQs

Q: Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

A: Thinning scissors can be used on all hair types, but they work best on thick and heavy hair. If you have fine or thin hair, it's best to use them sparingly to avoid thinning out the hair too much.

Q: How often should I use thinning scissors on my hair?

A: It's best to use thinning scissors every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your hair. However, if you have very thick hair, you may need to use them more often.

Q: Can I use thinning scissors on my own hair?

A: It's possible to use thinning scissors on your own hair, but it can be tricky. It's best to have someone else do it for you, especially if you're not experienced with using them.

Hair Care After Thinning

After using thinning scissors on your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heated styling tools too often, as this can damage the hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair in good condition.
  • Avoid using harsh chemical treatments, such as perms or relaxers, as these can weaken the hair.

Now that you know how to use thinning scissors properly, you can create a more natural and blended look for your hair. Just remember to work in small sections, blend the hair as you go, and take care of your hair afterwards. Happy styling!
