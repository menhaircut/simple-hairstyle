How To Explain The Haircut You Want To Your Stylist

Are you tired of leaving the salon with a haircut that's nothing like what you envisioned? It can be frustrating when you're not sure how to communicate the haircut you want to your stylist. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll provide tips on how to explain the haircut you want in relaxed English language, so your stylist can understand exactly what you're looking for.

Description

When you're going to get a haircut, it's important to have a clear idea of what you want. Do you want a choppy bob or a sleek pixie cut? A layered look or a blunt cut? By having a specific style in mind, you'll be able to communicate your wishes more clearly to your stylist.

However, it can be challenging to describe a haircut in a way that your stylist will understand. Using technical jargon or industry terms might confuse them. To avoid any misunderstandings, you need to use relaxed English language and communicate what you want in a clear and simple way.

How To Explain the Haircut You Want to Your Stylist

When explaining your desired haircut to your stylist, there are a few key points to keep in mind:

  • Be specific. Use descriptive words to explain the style you want. For example, if you want a bob, mention whether you want it to be chin-length or shoulder-length.
  • Show pictures. Bring pictures of the style you want to show your stylist. This will give them a visual reference and make it easier for them to understand what you're looking for.
  • Discuss your hair type. Your hair's texture, thickness, and natural wave pattern can affect how your haircut looks. Make sure your stylist knows your hair type so they can adjust the cut to suit your hair.
  • Communicate your lifestyle. Your haircut should suit your lifestyle, whether you're a busy professional or a stay-at-home parent. Let your stylist know how much time you're willing to spend on styling and maintenance.
  • Ask questions. Don't be afraid to ask your stylist questions about the cut they're suggesting. Make sure you're both on the same page before they start cutting.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to follow when explaining the haircut you want to your stylist:

  1. Research the style you want and find pictures to show your stylist.
  2. Think about your hair type and how the style will work with it.
  3. Consider your lifestyle and how much time you're willing to spend on maintenance.
  4. Book your appointment and communicate your desired style to the receptionist.
  5. Show your stylist the pictures you found and describe the style in your own words.
  6. Discuss any concerns or questions you have with your stylist.
  7. Relax and let your stylist work their magic!

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you explain the haircut you want to your stylist:

  • Bring multiple pictures to show your stylist from different angles.
  • Use descriptive words to explain the style you want, such as "choppy," "layered," or "blunt."
  • Consider your face shape when choosing a style.
  • Be honest about your styling habits and how much time you're willing to spend on maintenance.
  • Don't be afraid to speak up if you're not happy with the cut. Your stylist wants you to be satisfied with their work.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about explaining haircuts to stylists:

What if I don't know what style I want?

That's okay! Your stylist can help you choose a style that suits your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle.

Is it okay to bring pictures of celebrities or models?

Absolutely! Celebrity and model pictures can be a great reference for your stylist.

What if I'm not happy with the cut?

Speak up and let your stylist know what you don't like. They may be able to make adjustments to fix the issue. If you're still not satisfied, most salons have a policy for fixing unsatisfactory haircuts.

Hair Care

After getting a new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Use hair products that are designed for your hair type.

By following these tips and communicating clearly with your stylist, you can get the haircut of your dreams!
