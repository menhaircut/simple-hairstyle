Why Is My Hair So Curly In The Back

Written by Aprilia Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be beautiful and unique, but it can also be frustrating to manage. If you have noticed that your hair is particularly curly in the back, you may be wondering why. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide some tips for managing and caring for your curly locks.

Description

Many people with curly hair experience variations in curl pattern throughout their hair. Some areas may be straighter or wavier than others, while other areas may be tighter or more defined. This is often due to differences in hair texture, density, and growth patterns. In the case of curly hair in the back, it may be due to the way that the hair grows and falls on the head.

How to Determine Your Curl Type

Before you can effectively manage your curly hair, it is important to determine your curl type. This can help you choose the right products and styling techniques for your hair. There are several different curl typing systems out there, but one of the most popular is the Andre Walker Hair Typing System. This system categorizes hair into four types based on curl pattern and texture:

Type 1:

Straight hair

Type 2:

Wavy hair

Type 3:

Curly hair

Type 4:

Coily hair

If you have curly hair in the back, you likely fall into the Type 3 or Type 4 category. Within these categories, there are also subtypes that can help you pinpoint your exact curl pattern.

Step-by-Step: How to Manage Curly Hair in the Back

Here are some tips for managing and styling curly hair in the back:

Step 1: Start with a good haircut

A good haircut can help your hair fall and curl more evenly. Look for a stylist who is experienced with curly hair and can help you achieve the shape and length that you want.

Step 2: Use the right products

Curly hair requires moisture and hydration to look its best. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as shampoos, conditioners, and styling creams. Avoid products that contain harsh sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step 3: Experiment with styling techniques

There are many different techniques for styling curly hair, such as plopping, diffusing, and scrunching. Experiment with different techniques to find out what works best for your hair type and curl pattern.

Step 4: Protect your hair at night

To prevent your hair from getting tangled and frizzy while you sleep, consider using a silk or satin pillowcase. You can also try wrapping your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet.

Tips for Caring for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for curly hair:

Tip 1: Don't over-wash your hair

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it doesn't need to be washed as often. Aim to wash your hair no more than once or twice a week, and use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.

Tip 2: Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair

Curly hair is prone to tangling, so it's important to be gentle when detangling your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently work out any knots or tangles.

Tip 3: Deep condition your hair regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture and hydration to your hair. Look for a deep conditioning treatment that is specifically designed for curly hair, and use it once a week or as needed.

FAQs

Q: Is curly hair genetic?

A: Yes, curly hair is largely determined by genetics. If your parents have curly hair, you are more likely to have curly hair as well.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten curly hair using heat tools such as a flat iron or blow dryer. However, it's important to use these tools sparingly and to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: How can I prevent frizz in my curly hair?

A: To prevent frizz, avoid over-washing your hair, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using harsh styling products. You can also try using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair more gently.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair can be challenging, but with the right products, techniques, and care, you can achieve healthy, beautiful curls. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and techniques until you find what works best for you.
