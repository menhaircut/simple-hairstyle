How To Reduce Hair Fall Post Pregnancy Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Sep 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a new mom, you may have noticed that you're experiencing more hair fall than usual. This is completely normal - in fact, it affects up to 50% of women after giving birth. However, if you're worried about the amount of hair you're losing, there are plenty of ways to reduce hair fall post pregnancy. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about preventing hair fall and maintaining healthy locks.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Reduce Hair Fall Post Pregnancy
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Fall
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for New Moms

How to Reduce Hair Fall Post Pregnancy

There are a few steps you can take to help reduce hair fall after pregnancy:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a healthy, balanced diet is essential for maintaining healthy hair. Focus on foods that are rich in protein, iron, and vitamin C, as these are all important for hair growth. Good options include lean meats, leafy greens, and citrus fruits.

2. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water is important for overall health, and it can also help keep your hair healthy. Aim to drink at least 8 glasses of water per day to stay properly hydrated.

3. Get Plenty of Rest

Sleep is essential for overall health, and it can also help reduce stress - which is a common cause of hair fall. Aim to get at least 7-8 hours of sleep per night to help keep your hair healthy.

Tips to Prevent Hair Fall

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair fall:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns can put a lot of strain on your hair, which can lead to breakage and hair fall. Opt for looser styles instead, and avoid tying your hair up too tightly.

2. Use Gentle Hair Care Products

Harsh chemicals in hair care products can damage your hair and lead to hair fall. Look for gentle, natural products that are free of sulfates and parabens.

3. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid brushing your hair too roughly, and be gentle when you're towel-drying your hair. Wet hair is more delicate than dry hair, so it's important to be extra careful when you're handling it.

FAQs

Q: How long does post-pregnancy hair fall last?

A: Post-pregnancy hair fall can last up to 6 months. However, for most women, hair fall will start to slow down after the first few months.

Q: Will cutting my hair help prevent hair fall?

A: Cutting your hair won't necessarily prevent hair fall, but it can help make your hair appear thicker and healthier. If you're experiencing a lot of hair fall, a shorter haircut can help make your hair more manageable.

Hair Care Tips for New Moms

Being a new mom can be overwhelming, but it's important to take care of yourself - including your hair. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy locks:

1. Keep Your Hair Clean

Washing your hair regularly can help keep it clean and healthy. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Avoid using a brush on wet hair, as this can cause breakage and hair fall. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

3. Embrace Your Natural Texture

If you're short on time, embrace your natural hair texture. Use a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to enhance your hair's natural texture, and avoid using heat styling tools whenever possible.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help reduce hair fall post pregnancy and maintain healthy, beautiful locks. Remember to be patient - it may take a few months for your hair to fully recover, but with a little TLC, your hair will be back to its pre-pregnancy glory in no time!
