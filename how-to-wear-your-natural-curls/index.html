How To Wear Your Natural Curls With Confidence

Written by Petter Jul 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of trying to tame your curls with heat styling tools? Embrace your natural texture instead! Wearing your curls can be a liberating experience that showcases your unique beauty. In this article, we’ll share tips and tricks on how to wear your natural curls with confidence.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear Your Natural Curls
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Natural Curls
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Curls

How to Wear Your Natural Curls

The first step to wearing your natural curls is to embrace them. This means accepting your curls’ unique shape, texture, and volume. It also means letting go of the idea that your hair needs to be perfectly smooth and straight.

One way to wear your natural curls is to simply wash and condition your hair, then let it air dry. This will give you a natural, effortless look that showcases your curls’ full potential. However, if you want to define and enhance your curls, there are a few styling techniques you can try.

Styling Products

Using styling products is key to getting the most out of your natural curls. Look for products specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl creams, gels, and mousses. These products can help define your curls, reduce frizz, and add shine.

When applying styling products, start with a small amount and work it through your hair evenly. Be careful not to overload your curls with product, as this can weigh them down and make them look greasy.

Diffusing

If you want to speed up your drying time and add volume to your curls, try diffusing your hair. A diffuser is a special attachment that fits onto your hair dryer and distributes heat evenly. To use a diffuser, turn your hair dryer on to a low or medium setting and place the diffuser close to your scalp. Gently scrunch your curls with the diffuser, lifting them up towards your roots.

Pineappling

Pineappling is a technique that can help preserve your curls overnight. To pineapple your hair, gather it in a loose, high ponytail on top of your head. Use a scrunchie or soft hair tie to secure it in place. This will help prevent your curls from getting flattened or frizzy while you sleep.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to wear your natural curls:

  1. Wash and condition your hair using products designed for curly hair.
  2. Towel-dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub or pull on your curls.
  3. Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair, working it through evenly.
  4. Diffuse your hair on a low or medium heat setting, scrunching your curls gently with the diffuser.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to gently separate and fluff your curls.

Tips for Maintaining Your Natural Curls

Here are some tips to help you maintain your natural curls:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it’s wet, being careful not to pull or tug on your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls and cause frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Get regular trims to keep your curls healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

What should I do if my curls are frizzy?

If your curls are frizzy, try using a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum. You can also use a diffuser to dry your hair, which can help reduce frizz.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair prefer to wash their hair every day, while others only wash it once or twice a week. Experiment to find a washing schedule that works for you.

Hair Care for Curls

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful curls. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using hot water, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it’s wet.

Now that you know how to wear your natural curls, it’s time to embrace your unique texture and rock your curls with confidence!
