How To Know You Have Thick Hair A Guide To Understanding Your Hair Type

Written by Luffy Oct 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you unsure if you have thick hair? Thick hair can be a blessing or a curse, depending on how you manage it. If you don’t know your hair type, it can be challenging to find the right products or styling techniques that work for you. In this article, we’ll help you understand how to know you have thick hair, and provide you with tips on how to care for it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Know You Have Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Determining Your Hair Thickness
  • Tips for Taking Care of Thick Hair
  • FAQs About Thick Hair
  • Hair Care for Thick Hair

How to Know You Have Thick Hair

Thick hair refers to the density of hair follicles on your scalp. If you have a lot of hair strands on your head, you likely have thick hair. Here are some signs that you may have thick hair:

  • Your hair feels heavy
  • Your hair takes longer to dry than others
  • You have to use more product than others to style your hair
  • Your hair looks voluminous even when you don’t style it

If you experience any of these symptoms, you may have thick hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Determining Your Hair Thickness

If you’re unsure whether you have thick hair, you can follow these steps to determine your hair thickness:

  1. Wash your hair with your regular shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Gently towel dry your hair and let it air dry for a few hours.
  3. Comb your hair with a wide-toothed comb or a detangling brush.
  4. Divide your hair into sections and count the number of strands in each section.
  5. If you have more than 100 strands in each section, you likely have thick hair.

Keep in mind that this is just a general guideline, and your hair thickness can vary depending on genetics, age, and other factors.

Tips for Taking Care of Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are some tips for taking care of thick hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup from hair products.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh down your hair.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush or comb to detangle your hair without causing breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

FAQs About Thick Hair

Is thick hair the same as coarse hair?

No, thick hair refers to the number of hair strands on your head, while coarse hair refers to the texture of your hair. You can have thick hair that is fine or coarse.

Can you thin out thick hair?

Yes, you can thin out thick hair by getting a haircut or using thinning shears. However, it’s essential to go to a professional stylist who can thin out your hair without causing damage or creating uneven layers.

Can you have thick hair and still have hair loss?

Yes, thick hair doesn’t necessarily mean you’re immune to hair loss. Hair loss can occur due to genetics, stress, medication, or other factors.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair can be challenging to manage, but with the right care, you can keep it healthy and beautiful. Here are some hair care tips for thick hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as they can cause breakage and split ends.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to deeply condition your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat when blow-drying your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

By following these tips, you can keep your thick hair healthy and looking its best.

Conclusion

If you’re unsure whether you have thick hair, look for signs like heaviness, slow drying time, and voluminous appearance. If you determine that you have thick hair, take care of it by using sulfate-free products, avoiding heat damage, and getting regular trims. With the right care, you can rock your thick hair and feel confident in your hairdo!
