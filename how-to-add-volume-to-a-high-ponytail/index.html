How To Add Volume To A High Ponytail Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Aug 11, 2023 · 5 min read

If you want to add some volume to your high ponytail, you've come to the right place! In this blog post, we'll go over some easy tips and tricks to make your ponytail look fuller and more voluminous. Whether you have thin or thick hair, these methods will work for you. Let's get started!

How to Add Volume to a High Ponytail

If you're wondering how to add volume to a high ponytail, there are a few things you can do. Here are some tips:

1. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair can give it a lot of volume, and it's an easy technique to do. To tease your hair, take a small section of it and backcomb it with a comb. Repeat this process on different sections of your hair until you get the desired volume. Then, smooth out the top layer of your hair with a brush to hide the teasing.

2. Use Hair Extensions

If you want to add volume to your ponytail quickly and easily, hair extensions are a great option. Clip-in extensions can add length and fullness to your ponytail, making it look more voluminous. You can also use a ponytail extension to create a fuller look.

3. Curl Your Hair

Curling your hair can also add volume to your high ponytail. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to add curls to your hair before putting it up in a ponytail. This will create a lot of volume and make your ponytail look more full.

4. Use a Volumizing Product

Using a volumizing product like mousse or hairspray can help give your hair more volume. Apply the product to your roots and massage it in to create lift. Then, blow dry your hair upside down to add more volume.

Step by Step: How to Add Volume to a High Ponytail

Now that you know some tips on how to add volume to a high ponytail, let's go over the steps on how to do it:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start. This will help your ponytail stay in place and look more polished.

2. Tease Your Hair

Take a small section of your hair and tease it with a comb. Repeat this process on different sections of your hair until you get the desired volume.

3. Smooth Out Your Hair

Smooth out the top layer of your hair with a brush to hide the teasing. This will make your ponytail look more polished and put-together.

4. Secure Your Hair in a High Ponytail

Use a hair tie to secure your hair in a high ponytail. Make sure it's tight enough to stay in place, but not so tight that it hurts.

5. Pull Out Some Strands of Hair

Pull out some strands of hair from the front of your face to frame it. This will give your ponytail a softer look.

6. Apply Hairspray

Apply hairspray to your ponytail to keep it in place and add extra volume. Spray it from a distance to avoid making your hair look stiff or crunchy.

Tips for Adding Volume to a High Ponytail

Here are some additional tips for adding volume to a high ponytail:

1. Use a Hair Donut

A hair donut is a great tool to use if you want to create a fuller-looking ponytail. Simply wrap your hair around the donut and secure it with bobby pins.

2. Use Bobby Pins

Bobby pins can be used to create volume in your ponytail. Take small sections of your hair and twist them before pinning them up. This will create a lot of volume and make your ponytail look more full.

3. Use a Texturizing Spray

A texturizing spray can help create volume in your hair. Apply it to your roots and massage it in to create lift.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I make my high ponytail look fuller?

You can make your high ponytail look fuller by teasing your hair, using hair extensions, curling your hair, using a volumizing product, or using a hair donut.

2. Can I add volume to a high ponytail if I have thin hair?

Yes, you can add volume to a high ponytail even if you have thin hair. Using a volumizing product, teasing your hair, or using hair extensions can all help create a fuller look.

Hair Care for a Voluminous High Ponytail

To keep your hair healthy and voluminous, make sure you're taking good care of it. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Heat Protectant

If you're going to be using heat tools on your hair, make sure you're using a heat protectant to prevent damage.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair every 2-3 days instead.

3. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week can help keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

4. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

Now that you know how to add volume to a high ponytail, you can rock this hairstyle with confidence! Whether you're going to work, a party, or just running errands, a voluminous high ponytail is a great way to look put-together and stylish.
