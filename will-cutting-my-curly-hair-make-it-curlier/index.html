Will Cutting My Curly Hair Make It Curlier

Written by Robby Oct 06, 2023 · 5 min read

As someone with curly hair, you may have heard the old myth that cutting your hair will make it curlier. But is there any truth to this claim? In this article, we’ll explore the science behind curly hair and answer the question: will cutting my curly hair make it curlier?

Description

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it’s beautiful and unique, it can also be difficult to manage and style. Many people with curly hair are constantly searching for ways to enhance their curls and make them more defined. One of the most common questions people with curly hair have is whether cutting their hair will make it curlier.

How To

The answer to this question is both yes and no. Cutting your hair won’t actually make your curls curlier, but it can give the illusion of more curl. This is because cutting your hair can help remove weight that is pulling your curls down and causing them to look less defined. When you remove this weight, your curls will bounce back and look more voluminous.

However, it’s important to note that cutting your hair too short can actually have the opposite effect. When hair is too short, it can’t form curls properly and may end up looking frizzy or undefined. So, if you want to enhance your curls by cutting your hair, it’s important to work with a stylist who understands curly hair and can help you find the right length and style for your hair type.

Step by Step

If you’re considering cutting your curly hair to make it curlier, here are some steps you can take to ensure the best results:

Step 1: Find a curly hair specialist

Look for a stylist who specializes in cutting and styling curly hair. They will have the expertise to help you find the right length and style for your hair type and can give you tips on how to care for your curls after the cut.

Step 2: Discuss your goals

Talk to your stylist about your goals for your hair. Do you want more volume? More defined curls? Make sure your stylist understands what you’re looking for so they can help you achieve the look you want.

Step 3: Consider the length

When cutting curly hair, it’s important to consider the length. While shorter hair can give the illusion of more curl, cutting your hair too short can make it difficult for your curls to form properly. Work with your stylist to find the right length for your hair type and face shape.

Step 4: Embrace the layers

Layers can be a great way to enhance your curls and give them more volume. Work with your stylist to add layers that will help lift your curls and create a more defined look.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for enhancing your curls:

  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help enhance your curls
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help hydrate and define your curls
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause frizz

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair:

Will cutting my hair make it grow faster?

No, cutting your hair won’t actually make it grow faster. However, it can help remove split ends and promote healthier hair, which can make it look and feel better.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. In general, it’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and promote growth.

Should I cut my own curly hair?

It’s generally not recommended to cut your own hair, especially if you have curly hair. Curly hair requires a special technique to cut and style properly, so it’s best to leave it to the professionals.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can enhance your curls and keep them healthy. Here are some tips for curly hair care:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can cause frizz and dryness
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help hydrate and nourish your curls
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your curl pattern

In conclusion, cutting your curly hair won’t actually make it curlier, but it can help remove weight and enhance your curls. To achieve the best results, work with a stylist who specializes in curly hair and follow the tips and techniques outlined in this article for optimal hair care.
