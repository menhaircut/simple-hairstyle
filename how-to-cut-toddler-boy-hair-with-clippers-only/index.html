How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair With Clippers Only

Written by Robby Jul 22, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of taking your toddler to the barber every time he needs a haircut? Do you want to save money and time by cutting his hair at home? Well, you're in luck because cutting toddler boy hair with clippers only is easier than you think. In this article, we'll provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to do it, along with some tips and FAQs to help you along the way.

How to

Before we begin, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's also a good idea to have a towel or cape to protect your child's clothes.

Step-by-Step

1. Start by wetting your child's hair with the spray bottle. This will make it easier to cut and reduce the amount of hair flying around.

2. Use the clippers to trim the sides and back of your child's head. Start with a longer guard and work your way down until you reach the desired length.

3. Use scissors to cut the top of your child's hair. Comb the hair upward and trim any excess length. Be careful not to cut too much, as you can always go back and trim more if needed.

4. Blend the sides and top together using the clippers. Use a shorter guard and go over the areas where the sides and top meet until they look natural.

5. Use the comb and scissors to trim any stray hairs and make sure everything looks even.

6. Clean up the neckline and around the ears using the clippers and scissors.

Tips

- Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once.

- Use the comb to lift the hair and make sure you're cutting evenly.

- Take breaks if your child gets restless or uncomfortable.

- Practice makes perfect, so don't be too hard on yourself if it's not perfect the first time.

FAQ

Q: What if my child moves around too much?

A: Try distracting them with a favorite toy or snack. If that doesn't work, take a break and try again later.

Q: Can I use regular scissors instead of hair cutting scissors?

A: It's not recommended, as hair cutting scissors are designed to cut hair cleanly and evenly. Regular scissors can cause split ends and make the hair look uneven.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your child's hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for children's hair, and avoid using too much product.

Cutting toddler boy hair with clippers only can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it's easier than you think. Follow these steps, tips, and FAQs, and you'll be a pro in no time.
