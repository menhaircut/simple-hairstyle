Shoulder Length Haircuts For Thick Curly Hair Tips And Ideas

Written by Christine Aug 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it's beautiful and voluminous, it can also be difficult to manage, especially if you have thick hair. One of the best ways to make your thick curly hair more manageable is by getting a shoulder-length haircut. Not only does it help to reduce the weight of your hair, but it also gives you plenty of styling options. In this article, we will discuss some of the best shoulder-length haircuts for thick curly hair, along with tips on how to care for and style your curls.

How to Choose the Right Shoulder Length Haircut for Your Thick Curly Hair

Choosing the right shoulder-length haircut for your thick curly hair can be challenging. Here are some tips to help you make the right choice:

1. Consider Your Face Shape

When choosing a shoulder-length haircut, it's important to consider your face shape. If you have a round face, opt for a shoulder-length cut with layers to add definition. If you have a square face, go for a shoulder-length cut with soft, wispy layers to soften your features. Oval faces can pull off almost any shoulder-length cut.

2. Think About Your Hair Type

When choosing a shoulder-length haircut for your thick curly hair, it's important to think about your hair type. If you have coarse hair, consider a layered cut to help reduce the weight of your hair. If you have fine hair, a blunt cut may be better suited for you.

3. Consult Your Stylist

Your stylist is your best resource when it comes to choosing the right shoulder-length haircut for your thick curly hair. Bring in pictures of styles you like and discuss what will work best for your hair type and face shape.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Shoulder Length Haircut

Now that you've chosen the perfect shoulder-length haircut for your thick curly hair, it's time to style it. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get the perfect look:

1. Start with Clean, Moisturized Hair

Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated. Avoid using products with sulfates or alcohol, which can dry out your hair.

2. Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your damp hair to help define your curls. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl creams or gels.

3. Use a Diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls. This will help to prevent frizz and keep your curls defined.

4. Finish with a Hairspray

Finish your style with a light hold hairspray to keep your curls in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Shoulder Length Haircut for Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you maintain your shoulder-length haircut for thick curly hair:

1. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

2. Deep Condition Once a Week

Deep conditioning once a week will help to keep your curls hydrated and healthy. Look for deep conditioning treatments that are specifically designed for curly hair.

3. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase will help to prevent frizz and breakage. The smooth surface of the silk will help to protect your curls while you sleep.

FAQ About Shoulder Length Haircuts for Thick Curly Hair

1. Is a shoulder-length haircut suitable for all face shapes?

While shoulder-length haircuts can work for most face shapes, it's important to consider your individual features when choosing a style. Consult with your stylist to find the best style for your face shape.

2. How often should I wash my thick curly hair?

It's best to wash your hair once or twice a week to avoid stripping your curls of their natural oils.

3. Can I straighten my thick curly hair?

Yes, you can straighten your thick curly hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant and not straighten your hair too frequently, as this can cause damage.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for thick curly hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. Avoid using brushes, which can cause breakage.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as much as possible, as these can cause damage to your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to protect your hair from the damaging effects of the sun.

With these tips and ideas, you can rock a gorgeous shoulder-length haircut for your thick curly hair. Remember to consult with your stylist for the best haircut for your face shape and hair type, and take good care of your curls for healthy, beautiful hair.
