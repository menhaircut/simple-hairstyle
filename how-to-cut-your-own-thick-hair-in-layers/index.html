How To Cut Your Own Thick Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on salon visits to maintain your thick hair? Do you want to try cutting your own hair at home but don't know how? In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your own thick hair in layers. We'll cover everything from the tools you'll need to the proper technique and care tips to ensure your hair looks salon-worthy.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your own hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Clips
  • Mirror

How to Cut Your Own Thick Hair in Layers

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. It's important to have clean, dry hair before you start cutting it.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Use your comb to divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then from the center of your forehead to the back of your head. Clip each section to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the bottom of one of the sections and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and cut the ends at an angle. Continue working your way up the section until you've cut all the hair. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all your hair into layers, use your scissors to blend the layers together. This will give your hair a more natural look.

Step 5: Check Your Work

After you've finished cutting your hair, use a mirror to check the back and sides. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure your hair is even and the layers are blended.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Thick Hair in Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will appear longer when it dries.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the layers.

FAQ

Can I cut my own thick hair in layers?

Yes, you can cut your own thick hair in layers as long as you have the right tools and follow the proper technique.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the layers and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

Now that you know how to cut your own thick hair in layers, you can save time and money by doing it at home. Just remember to gather the right tools, follow the proper technique, and take care of your hair to ensure it looks its best.
