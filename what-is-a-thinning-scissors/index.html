What Is A Thinning Scissors And How To Use It

Written by Oliver Jul 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of having thick hair that is hard to manage? Or perhaps you want to create some texture and layers in your hair without losing too much length? A thinning scissors may be just what you need! In this article, we will explain what a thinning scissors is, how to use it, tips and FAQs, and hair care advice.

What is a Thinning Scissors?

A thinning scissors, also known as a texturizing scissors, is a type of haircutting tool that is used to thin out hair. It has a serrated edge that cuts hair in a way that creates texture and removes bulk. Unlike regular scissors which cut hair straight across, thinning scissors cut hair at different lengths, resulting in a more natural and layered look.

How to Use a Thinning Scissors?

Using a thinning scissors is fairly easy, but it does require some practice and skill. Here are the basic steps:

  1. Start with dry and detangled hair.
  2. Section the hair and clip away the parts you don't want to cut.
  3. Hold the thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair, using a sliding motion.
  4. Repeat the process on different sections of hair, but be careful not to overdo it as it can result in uneven and choppy hair.
  5. Blend in the cut sections with the rest of the hair using regular scissors if needed.

Remember to only cut a small amount of hair at a time and to use the scissors sparingly. It's always better to cut less and adjust as needed than to cut too much and end up with a bad haircut.

Tips for Using a Thinning Scissors

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your thinning scissors:

  • Choose the right type of thinning scissors for your hair type and desired style. There are different types of thinning scissors, such as double-sided and single-sided, with varying teeth sizes and shapes.
  • Practice on a mannequin head or on someone else before cutting your own hair.
  • Use the thinning scissors to create layers and texture, but avoid using them to cut bangs or shape the hairline.
  • Clean and oil your thinning scissors regularly to keep them sharp and in good condition.

FAQs About Thinning Scissors

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning scissors:

Q: Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

A: Thinning scissors can be used on most hair types, but they work best on thick and coarse hair. If you have fine or thin hair, use caution and only cut a small amount of hair at a time.

Q: Can thinning scissors be used on wet hair?

A: Thinning scissors are designed to be used on dry hair, as wet hair can be harder to cut and can result in uneven cuts.

Q: How often should I use thinning scissors on my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and desired style, but in general, it's best to use thinning scissors sparingly and only when you want to remove bulk or create texture.

Hair Care Advice

Using thinning scissors can be a great way to add some dimension and style to your hair, but it's also important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that is suited for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat and styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals that are good for your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking and feeling healthy.

Now that you know what a thinning scissors is and how to use it, you can experiment with different styles and textures in your hair. Remember to always use caution and practice before cutting your own hair, and to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
