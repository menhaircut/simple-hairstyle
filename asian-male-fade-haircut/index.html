Asian Male Fade Haircut How To Get The Perfect Look

Written by Aprilia Nov 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a trendy haircut that will make you stand out from the crowd? Look no further than the Asian male fade haircut! This popular hairstyle has been gaining popularity in recent years, and for good reason. It's a versatile style that can work for any occasion, from formal events to casual hangouts. In this article, we'll show you how to get the perfect Asian male fade haircut, step by step.

What is an Asian Male Fade Haircut?

The Asian male fade haircut is a style that involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back, while keeping the top longer. The hair is then gradually tapered, or "faded," from short to long. This creates a clean, sharp look that is popular among men of all ages. The fade can be high, medium, or low, depending on your preference. This style can also be paired with a variety of other haircuts, such as the quiff, pompadour, or slick back.

How to Get an Asian Male Fade Haircut: Step by Step

Step 1: Choose Your Fade

The first step in getting an Asian male fade haircut is to decide on the type of fade you want. Do you want a high, medium, or low fade? A high fade starts at the temples and goes up to the crown of the head. A medium fade starts at the ear and goes up to the crown. A low fade starts at the hairline and goes up to the ear. Consider your face shape and hair texture when choosing your fade.

Step 2: Decide on the Length of the Top

Next, decide on the length of the top of your hair. This will depend on your personal style and the shape of your face. If you have a round face, you may want to keep the top longer to create the illusion of length. If you have a square face, a shorter top may be more flattering. Talk to your stylist about your options.

Step 3: Get a Clean Start

Before your stylist starts cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will allow them to see your natural hair texture and make more accurate cuts. Your stylist will then start cutting the hair on the sides and back, using clippers to create the desired fade. They will gradually increase the length of the guard as they move up the head.

Step 4: Blend the Top and Sides

Once the sides and back are cut, your stylist will blend the top and sides using scissors. This will create a smooth transition between the two lengths. They may also use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk and make the hair easier to style.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, it's time to style your hair! The Asian male fade haircut is versatile and can be styled in many different ways. You can use a pomade or wax to create a slick back or a messy, textured look. If you want a more formal look, you can use a comb to create a side part or a sleek, polished look.

Tips for Maintaining Your Asian Male Fade Haircut

Once you have your Asian male fade haircut, it's important to maintain it regularly to keep it looking sharp. Here are a few tips:

  • Visit your stylist every 3-4 weeks to keep your fade looking fresh.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can make your hair look greasy or weighed down.
  • Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for you.

FAQ

What face shape is best for an Asian male fade haircut?

The Asian male fade haircut can work for any face shape, but the length and style of the top should be adjusted to flatter your features. Talk to your stylist about your options.

Can I style my Asian male fade haircut in different ways?

Yes! The Asian male fade haircut is versatile and can be styled in many different ways, from slicked back to messy and textured.

How often do I need to get a trim?

It's recommended to visit your stylist every 3-4 weeks to keep your fade looking fresh.

Hair Care for Asian Male Hair

Asian male hair can be thick and coarse, so it's important to use products that will keep it healthy and hydrated. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are designed for your hair type, and use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized. Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons, as these can damage your hair over time.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock the Asian male fade haircut with confidence. Whether you're going for a casual or formal look, this versatile style is sure to turn heads. Book an appointment with your stylist today and get ready to show off your new look!
