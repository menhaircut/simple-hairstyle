How To Cut Your Own Hair Layer Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 05, 2023 · 3 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, cutting your hair into layers can add dimension and movement. But with salons being expensive and time-consuming, why not try cutting your own hair at home? In this article, we'll go over how to cut your own hair layer style in a relaxed and easy-to-follow manner.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Cut Your Own Hair Layer Style
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for a Successful Haircut
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care After Your Haircut

How to Cut Your Own Hair Layer Style

Cutting your own hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it's definitely doable. Here are the things you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Large mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve a layered haircut:

  1. Start with dry, clean hair. Comb through any knots or tangles.
  2. Part your hair down the middle and clip one side away.
  3. Starting at the front of your hair, take a small section and comb it straight. Hold the scissors vertically and make small snips to create a soft and natural-looking layer.
  4. Continue this process, working your way back and taking small sections of hair. Make sure to keep the length even as you go.
  5. Once you've completed one side, repeat on the other side.
  6. Check your layers in the mirror to make sure they're even. Make any necessary adjustments.
  7. To add more layers, follow the same steps and take smaller sections of hair.
  8. To blend the layers, take a section of hair from the crown of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the scissors horizontally and make small snips, blending the layers together.
  9. Trim any split ends or flyaways.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid any damage to your hair.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and you don't want to make any mistakes due to fatigue.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair layer style:

Can I cut my hair into layers if I have curly hair?
Yes! Just keep in mind that curly hair can shrink when it's dry, so make sure to cut less than you think you need.
How often should I cut my hair?
It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.
Do I need to use any products after cutting my hair?
It's always a good idea to use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

After you've completed your haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use hair masks or deep conditioners to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layered style.

With these tips and techniques, cutting your own hair layer style can be a fun and rewarding experience. Just remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll end up with a beautiful new hairstyle!
