How To Edge Up A Receding Hairline Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling with a receding hairline and looking for a way to edge it up? Edging up a receding hairline can give you a fresh and stylish look. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to edge up a receding hairline.

Description

A receding hairline can be frustrating and can affect one's confidence. Edging up your hairline can help you regain your confidence and give you a sharp look. Edging up involves cutting the hairline in a straight line and shaping it to your desired shape. It is important to note that edging up should be done by a professional barber or hairstylist.

How to Edge Up a Receding Hairline

Step 1: Choose the Right Barber

Choosing the right barber or hairstylist is crucial when it comes to edging up a receding hairline. Look for someone who has experience with edging up hairlines and has a good reputation. You can ask for recommendations from friends or family members or look for reviews online.

Step 2: Decide on the Shape

Decide on the shape you want for your hairline. A straight line is the most common shape, but you can also opt for a curved or rounded hairline. Your barber or hairstylist can help you decide on the best shape for your face shape and hairline.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before edging up your hairline. This will make it easier for your barber or hairstylist to work with your hair.

Step 4: Trim the Hairline

Your barber or hairstylist will use clippers to trim your hairline in a straight line or the shape you have decided on. They will also use scissors to shape the hairline and remove any stray hairs.

Step 5: Clean and Style

After edging up your hairline, your barber or hairstylist will clean up any stray hairs and style your hair. They may use a hair gel or pomade to give your hair a polished look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when edging up a receding hairline:

  • Choose a barber or hairstylist who has experience with edging up hairlines.
  • Decide on the shape you want for your hairline before getting it edged up.
  • Make sure your hair is clean and dry before edging up your hairline.
  • Consider using a hair gel or pomade to style your hair and keep it in place.

FAQ

Can I edge up my own hairline?

It is not recommended to edge up your own hairline as it requires skill and experience. It is best to leave it to a professional barber or hairstylist.

How often should I get my hairline edged up?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your hairline. It is recommended to get your hairline edged up every 2-3 weeks.

Will edging up my hairline damage my hair?

Edging up your hairline should not damage your hair if done correctly by a professional barber or hairstylist.

Hair Care

After edging up your hairline, it is important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and use a heat protectant before using hot tools. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Conclusion

Edging up a receding hairline can give you a fresh and stylish look. Remember to choose the right barber, decide on the shape you want, wash and dry your hair, trim the hairline, and clean and style your hair. Follow these tips and take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance.
