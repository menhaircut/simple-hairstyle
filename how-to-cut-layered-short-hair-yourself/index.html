How To Cut Layered Short Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a new hairstyle that's easy to maintain and looks great, layered short hair is a perfect option. But going to the salon for a haircut can be expensive and time-consuming. That's why we've put together this step-by-step guide to help you cut your own layered short hair at home. With a few basic tools and some patience, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut that will save you time and money.

How To

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror so you can see the back of your head.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can be misleading, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using the comb, divide your hair into sections. Start by sectioning the top of your hair and clipping it up. Then, divide the sides and back of your hair into sections and clip them up as well.

Step 3: Cut the Top Section

Start with the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut it at an angle. The length of the hair you cut will determine the length of the layers. Repeat this process for the entire top section of your hair.

Step 4: Cut the Side Sections

Next, move on to the side sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, following the length of the hair you cut in the top section. Repeat this process for the entire side section of your hair.

Step 5: Cut the Back Section

Finally, move on to the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, following the length of the hair you cut in the top and side sections. Repeat this process for the entire back section of your hair.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head. If you notice any uneven sections, use the scissors to trim them until you're satisfied with the result.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Cut your hair in a well-lit area so you can see what you're doing.
  • Use a comb to help guide the scissors and ensure a straight cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes.

FAQ

Can I cut my own short hair?

Yes, you can cut your own short hair. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home.

How do I know what length to cut my hair?

The length of your hair will depend on your personal preference and the style you're going for. Start with a longer length and gradually cut it shorter until you're happy with the result.

Do I need to use a specific type of scissors?

Yes, it's important to use sharp scissors designed for cutting hair. Using dull scissors can lead to split ends and an uneven cut.

Hair Care

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking great. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. And don't forget to get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy layered short haircut at home. With a little practice and patience, you'll be able to cut your own hair with confidence.
