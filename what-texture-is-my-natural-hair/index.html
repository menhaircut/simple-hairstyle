What Texture Is My Natural Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're a naturalista, you've probably wondered about your hair texture. Understanding your hair texture is important because it helps you choose the right products and styling techniques. In this article, we'll discuss what texture is my natural hair, how to determine it, and how to take care of it. Let's dive in!

What is Hair Texture?

Hair texture refers to the diameter of each hair strand. There are three main types of hair texture: fine, medium, and coarse. Fine hair has a small diameter, medium hair has a medium diameter, and coarse hair has a large diameter. It's important to note that hair texture is different from hair type, which refers to the curl pattern.

How to Determine Your Hair Texture

Determining your hair texture is easy. All you need is a single strand of hair and a ruler. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help you get accurate measurements.

Step 2: Air Dry Your Hair

Let your hair air dry completely. Don't use a blow dryer or any styling products.

Step 3: Take a Strand of Hair

Take a single strand of hair from the crown of your head. Make sure it's clean and dry.

Step 4: Measure the Diameter

Place the strand of hair on a flat surface and measure its diameter using a ruler. Fine hair is less than 2 inches in circumference, medium hair is between 2 and 4 inches in circumference, and coarse hair is over 4 inches in circumference.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair Texture

Now that you know your hair texture, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Use the Right Products

Use products that are specifically designed for your hair texture. Fine hair needs lightweight products that won't weigh it down, while coarse hair needs heavier products that will hydrate and nourish it.

Tip 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. These can damage your hair and make it brittle.

Tip 3: Deep Condition Regularly

Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week or as needed.

FAQs

Q: Can Hair Texture Change Over Time?

A: Yes, hair texture can change over time. Factors like hormones, pregnancy, and medication can affect your hair texture.

Q: Can Hair Texture Affect Hair Growth?

A: No, hair texture does not affect hair growth. Hair growth is determined by genetics and overall health.

Q: Can You Have More Than One Hair Texture?

A: Yes, it's possible to have more than one hair texture. For example, you may have fine hair at the roots and coarse hair at the ends.

Final Thoughts

Understanding your hair texture is important for maintaining healthy hair. Once you know your hair texture, you can take the necessary steps to care for it properly. Use the tips in this article to keep your hair looking and feeling its best!
