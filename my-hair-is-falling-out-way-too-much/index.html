My Hair Is Falling Out Way Too Much Causes And Solutions

Written by Luffy Oct 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you noticing an abnormal amount of hair fall lately? Is your hair thinning and losing volume? If yes, then you are not alone. Hair fall is a common problem that affects millions of people worldwide. However, excessive hair shedding can be alarming and may indicate an underlying health issue. In this article, we will discuss the causes and solutions of hair fall and provide you with some tips to help you take better care of your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Hair Fall
  • Solutions to Hair Fall
  • How to Take Care of Your Hair

Causes of Hair Fall

Hair fall can be caused by several factors, ranging from genetics to lifestyle. Some of the most common causes of hair fall are:

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes due to pregnancy, menopause, or thyroid imbalances can cause hair fall. Hormonal imbalances can affect the hair growth cycle and lead to hair fall.

Stress

Stress can cause hair fall by increasing the production of cortisol, a hormone that can affect the hair growth cycle. Stress can also lead to scalp inflammation, which can cause hair fall.

Dietary Deficiencies

A diet lacking in essential nutrients like vitamins, minerals, and proteins can cause hair fall. Nutrients like biotin, iron, and zinc are essential for healthy hair growth.

Medications

Some medications like chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and antidepressants can cause hair fall as a side effect.

Hairstyling and Chemical Treatments

Excessive hairstyling, heat styling, and chemical treatments like hair dyeing and perming can damage the hair and cause hair fall.

Solutions to Hair Fall

There are several solutions to hair fall that can help you maintain healthy hair. Some of the most effective solutions are:

Medical Treatment

If your hair fall is caused by an underlying health issue, seek medical treatment to address the issue. Hormonal imbalances, thyroid problems, and scalp infections can be treated with medication.

Dietary Changes

Make dietary changes to include more nutrient-rich foods like fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins. Nutrients like biotin, iron, and zinc are essential for healthy hair growth.

Reduce Stress

Reduce stress by practicing relaxation techniques like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing. Exercise regularly to boost endorphins and reduce stress.

Hairstyle and Chemical Treatment Changes

Avoid excessive hairstyling, heat styling, and chemical treatments like hair dyeing and perming. Opt for natural hair care products that are free from harsh chemicals.

How to Take Care of Your Hair

Taking care of your hair is essential to maintain healthy hair growth. Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to remove dirt and oil buildup. Don't wash your hair too frequently as it can strip the hair of its natural oils.

Condition Your Hair

Use a conditioner after shampooing to keep your hair moisturized and nourished. Conditioner helps to detangle your hair, reducing the risk of hair breakage.

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling tools like blow dryers, straighteners, and curling irons as they can damage the hair and cause hair fall. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf. The sun's UV rays can damage the hair and cause hair fall.

Tips for Healthy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage. Regular trims help to maintain healthy hair growth.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair and reduces the risk of hair breakage.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids as they can pull on the hair and cause hair fall. Opt for loose hairstyles instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much hair fall is normal?

It is normal to lose 50-100 hairs per day. However, if you notice an abnormal amount of hair fall, you should seek medical advice.

Can stress cause hair fall?

Yes, stress can cause hair fall by increasing the production of cortisol, a hormone that can affect the hair growth cycle. Stress can also lead to scalp inflammation, which can cause hair fall.

Can diet affect hair fall?

Yes, a diet lacking in essential nutrients like vitamins, minerals, and proteins can cause hair fall. Nutrients like biotin, iron, and zinc are essential for healthy hair growth.

In conclusion, hair fall is a common problem that can be caused by several factors. However, with proper care and treatment, you can maintain healthy hair growth. Seek medical advice if you notice an abnormal amount of hair fall and make dietary and lifestyle changes to promote healthy hair growth. Follow our tips to take better care of your hair and reduce the risk of hair fall.
