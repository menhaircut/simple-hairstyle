What To Do When Hair Is Damaged By Bleach Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Oct 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who likes to experiment with hair color, then you're probably familiar with bleach. Bleach is a powerful chemical that is used to lighten hair, but it can also cause serious damage if not used correctly. If you've recently bleached your hair and are now dealing with dryness, breakage, and other damage, don't worry - there are steps you can take to restore your hair's health.

  • How to Repair Damaged Hair

How to Assess Damage

Before you begin any hair repair treatments, it's important to assess the extent of the damage. Look for signs of breakage, dryness, and split ends. You may also notice that your hair feels rough or brittle to the touch. If your hair is severely damaged, you may want to consider cutting it and starting fresh. However, if the damage is mild to moderate, you can work to repair it.

How to Repair Damaged Hair

There are several ways to repair damaged hair, and the right approach will depend on the severity of the damage. Here are a few options:

1. Deep Conditioning Treatments

Deep conditioning treatments can help to restore moisture to damaged hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, avocado oil, and shea butter. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for at least 30 minutes (or as directed on the packaging) before rinsing it out.

2. Protein Treatments

Protein treatments can help to strengthen damaged hair. Look for products that contain hydrolyzed protein, which can penetrate the hair shaft and help to rebuild damaged strands. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time before rinsing it out.

3. Hair Oils

Applying hair oils to your strands can help to lock in moisture and prevent breakage. Look for oils like argan oil, coconut oil, and jojoba oil. Apply a small amount to your hair and work it through your strands, focusing on the ends.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're not sure where to start with repairing your damaged hair, follow this step-by-step guide:

1. Assess the Damage

Take a close look at your hair and determine the extent of the damage. This will help you to choose the right treatment approach.

2. Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse thoroughly.

3. Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the treatment on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

4. Apply a Hair Oil

Apply a hair oil to your strands, focusing on the ends. This will help to lock in moisture and prevent breakage.

5. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up, being gentle to avoid causing further damage.

6. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, as these can further damage your hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible.

Tips for Healthy Hair

If you want to keep your hair healthy and prevent damage in the future, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

1. Limit Bleaching

Bleaching can cause serious damage to your hair, so it's best to limit how often you do it.

2. Use Gentle Products

Choose gentle hair care products that are formulated for your hair type. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates.

3. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or scarf. You can also use products that contain UV protectants.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help to promote healthy hair growth. Be sure to eat plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables.

FAQ

1. Can I repair severely damaged hair?

If your hair is severely damaged, you may want to consider cutting it and starting fresh. However, if the damage is mild to moderate, you can work to repair it.

2. How often should I use deep conditioning treatments?

You can use deep conditioning treatments as often as once a week, depending on the condition of your hair.

3. Can I use hair oils on my scalp?

Hair oils are generally best used on the ends of your hair, as they can be too heavy for your scalp.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important if you want to keep it healthy and looking great. Be sure to choose gentle hair care products, avoid over-processing your hair, and protect it from environmental factors. With the right care and attention, you can keep your hair looking its best.
