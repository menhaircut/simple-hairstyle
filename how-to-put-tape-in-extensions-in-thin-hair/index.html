How To Put Tape In Extensions In Thin Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Tape-in extensions are a popular choice for adding length and volume to hair. However, if you have thin hair, you may be hesitant to try them because you're not sure how to make them look natural. The good news is that tape-in extensions can work for thin hair, as long as you know how to put them in properly. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps for putting tape-in extensions in thin hair, along with some tips for caring for your extensions.

Description

Tape-in extensions are pieces of hair that have a strip of adhesive tape attached to one end. The tape is placed on either side of a small section of your natural hair, creating a sandwich effect. The result is a natural-looking extension that blends in seamlessly with your own hair. Tape-in extensions are a great option for people with thin hair because they lay flat against the scalp and are less likely to cause damage than other types of extensions.

How to Put Tape in Extensions in Thin Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start putting in your extensions, you need to prepare your hair. Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. It's important to make sure your hair is completely dry, as wet hair can cause the extensions to slip. Next, section off a small piece of hair near the nape of your neck. This will be where you start applying the extensions.

Step 2: Apply the Extensions

Take one of the tape-in extensions and remove the protective backing from the adhesive tape. Place the extension underneath the small section of hair you just sectioned off, with the adhesive side facing up. Take another extension and place it on top of the section of hair, sandwiching your natural hair between the two extensions. Press down firmly to make sure the extensions are secure.

Step 3: Repeat the Process

Continue this process, working your way up your head in small sections. Make sure you leave enough space between each extension so that they can move freely. You don't want them to be too close together, as this can cause the extensions to tangle and pull on your natural hair.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once you've applied all of the extensions, you can style your hair as desired. You can curl or straighten your hair, but be careful not to apply too much heat to the extensions. Use a heat protectant spray to help protect your extensions from damage.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your tape-in extensions:

  • Choose high-quality extensions made from human hair. Synthetic extensions can look fake and may not blend as well with your natural hair.
  • Don't wash your hair for at least 48 hours after applying the extensions. This will give the adhesive time to set and prevent the extensions from slipping.
  • Avoid using heavy products, as these can weigh down your extensions and make them look unnatural.
  • Use a soft-bristled brush to gently brush your extensions, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • When sleeping, tie your hair up in a loose bun or braid to prevent tangling.

FAQ

Can tape-in extensions cause damage to thin hair?

Tape-in extensions are less likely to cause damage than other types of extensions, but they can still cause damage if they're not applied properly. It's important to make sure the extensions are not too heavy and that they're not placed too close together.

Can I color my tape-in extensions?

Yes, you can color your tape-in extensions. However, it's best to have a professional colorist do it for you to avoid damaging the extensions.

Hair Care

To keep your tape-in extensions looking their best, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips for caring for your extensions:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the extensions of their natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools on the extensions too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep the extensions hydrated.
  • When swimming or exercising, tie your hair up in a loose bun or braid to prevent tangling.

By following these tips and steps, you can confidently put tape-in extensions in thin hair and enjoy the benefits of longer, fuller hair.
