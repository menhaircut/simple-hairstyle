Hair Loss After Covid Infection Understanding And Dealing With It

Written by Luffy Jul 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're one of the millions of people who have contracted Covid-19, you may have experienced various symptoms, including fever, fatigue, cough, and loss of taste and smell. But did you know that hair loss after Covid infection is also a possible side effect? In this article, we'll discuss what causes hair loss after Covid infection, how to deal with it, and some tips for hair care.

Causes of Hair Loss After Covid Infection

Hair loss after Covid infection is known as telogen effluvium, a condition where hair follicles go into a resting phase prematurely. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • The physical stress of Covid-19 on the body
  • The emotional stress of dealing with the pandemic
  • The side effects of medications used to treat Covid-19
  • The nutritional deficiencies that can occur during illness

It's important to note that hair loss after Covid infection is usually temporary and should grow back within a few months.

Symptoms of Hair Loss After Covid Infection

The most obvious symptom of hair loss after Covid infection is a noticeable thinning of the hair, especially on the scalp. Other symptoms may include:

  • Increased shedding of hair when brushing or washing
  • A decrease in hair volume or fullness
  • The appearance of bald spots or patches

Diagnosis of Hair Loss After Covid Infection

To diagnose hair loss after Covid infection, your doctor may perform a physical exam and ask questions about your medical history and any medications you may have taken. They may also order blood tests to check for nutritional deficiencies or underlying health conditions that could be contributing to hair loss.

Treatment for Hair Loss After Covid Infection

Fortunately, hair loss after Covid infection is usually temporary and doesn't require any specific treatment. However, there are some steps you can take to help your hair grow back faster:

  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals
  • Avoid hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as tight braids or ponytails
  • Use gentle hair care products that don't contain harsh chemicals
  • Manage stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques

Tips for Hair Care After Covid Infection

Whether you're dealing with hair loss after Covid infection or just want to keep your hair healthy, there are some simple tips you can follow:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers, curling irons, or straighteners too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, which can damage hair

FAQs

Q: Will my hair grow back after Covid?

A: Yes, hair loss after Covid infection is usually temporary and should grow back within a few months.

Q: What can I do to help my hair grow back faster?

A: Eat a healthy, balanced diet, avoid tight hairstyles, use gentle hair care products, and manage stress through relaxation techniques.

Q: Should I see a doctor if I'm experiencing hair loss after Covid infection?

A: If you're concerned about your hair loss or it's causing you distress, talk to your doctor. They can help diagnose the underlying cause and suggest appropriate treatment.

Dealing with hair loss after Covid infection can be frustrating, but it's important to remember that it's usually temporary and can be managed with proper hair care and stress management techniques. By following these tips and taking care of your overall health, you can help your hair grow back stronger and healthier than ever.
