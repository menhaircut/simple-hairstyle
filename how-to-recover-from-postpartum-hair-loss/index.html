How To Recover From Postpartum Hair Loss Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Oct 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a new mom, you may have noticed that your hair is thinning or falling out in clumps. Don't worry, you're not alone! Postpartum hair loss is a common side effect of giving birth, and it can be frustrating to deal with. However, there are several steps you can take to help your hair grow back and regain its strength. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to recover from postpartum hair loss.

How Does Postpartum Hair Loss Occur?

During pregnancy, your body produces higher levels of estrogen, which can cause your hair to become thicker and more lustrous. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels return to normal, which can cause your hair to fall out at a faster rate than usual. This typically happens around three months after giving birth and can last for several months.

How to Recover from Postpartum Hair Loss: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Eat a Healthy Diet

One of the best ways to promote hair growth is to eat a healthy, balanced diet. Make sure you're getting plenty of protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and E. These nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth and can help prevent further hair loss.

Step 2: Take Supplements

In addition to eating a healthy diet, you may also want to consider taking supplements to promote hair growth. Biotin, iron, and folic acid are all important for hair health and can help stimulate hair growth.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling

If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to avoid heat styling your hair. Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a low-heat setting on your blow dryer.

Step 4: Use Gentle Hair Care Products

Harsh shampoos and conditioners can also contribute to hair loss. Look for products that are gentle and free of sulfates and parabens. You may also want to consider using a deep conditioning treatment to help nourish your hair.

Tips for Recovering from Postpartum Hair Loss

In addition to the steps outlined above, there are several other things you can do to help your hair grow back and regain its strength. Here are a few tips:

Tip 1: Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help improve blood flow to your hair follicles, which can promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

Tip 2: Try a Hair Mask

A hair mask can help nourish your hair and promote growth. Try mixing together avocado, honey, and coconut oil and applying it to your hair for 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

Tip 3: Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can contribute to hair loss. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

FAQs about Postpartum Hair Loss

Q: How long does postpartum hair loss last?

A: Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for a few months, but it can vary from person to person.

Q: Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

A: Unfortunately, postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented. However, you can take steps to promote healthy hair growth and minimize hair loss.

Q: Will my hair return to its pre-pregnancy state?

A: Yes, in most cases, your hair will eventually return to its pre-pregnancy state. However, it may take several months or even a year for your hair to fully recover.

Final Thoughts

Postpartum hair loss can be a frustrating and stressful experience, but it's important to remember that it's completely normal. By following the tips and tricks outlined in this article, you can help your hair grow back and regain its strength. Be patient and kind to yourself, and remember that your hair will eventually return to its pre-pregnancy state.
