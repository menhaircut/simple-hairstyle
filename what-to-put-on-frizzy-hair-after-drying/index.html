What To Put On Frizzy Hair After Drying A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Dec 01, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have frizzy hair, you know how difficult it can be to keep it under control. Frizz can ruin any hairstyle, and it can be frustrating to deal with. Fortunately, there are a few things you can put on your hair after drying to help tame frizz and keep your locks looking smooth and shiny. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about what to put on frizzy hair after drying.

Table of Contents

  • How to Put on Frizzy Hair After Drying
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Taming Frizzy Hair

Description

Frizzy hair is often caused by a lack of moisture in the hair. When the hair is dry, it can become brittle and prone to breakage, which can lead to frizz. Adding moisture to the hair can help to tame frizz and keep the hair looking healthy and shiny. There are several products that you can put on your hair after drying to help moisturize and control frizz.

How to Put on Frizzy Hair After Drying

Putting products on frizzy hair after drying can be a bit tricky. You don't want to overdo it and end up with greasy, weighed-down hair. Here are a few tips for putting products on frizzy hair after drying:

  • Start with a small amount of product and work your way up if necessary.
  • Apply product evenly throughout your hair.
  • Pay special attention to the ends of your hair, which tend to be the driest and most prone to frizz.
  • Avoid applying product to your roots, as this can make your hair look greasy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you put any products on your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. You don't want to apply products to wet hair, as this can dilute the product and make it less effective. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean and moisturize your hair, then dry it thoroughly with a towel or blow dryer.

Step 2: Choose the Right Product

There are several products that can help to tame frizzy hair after drying. Here are a few of the most popular options:

  • Hair oil: Hair oils are great for adding moisture and shine to frizzy hair. Apply a small amount of oil to the ends of your hair, then work it through with your fingers.
  • Serum: Hair serums are designed to smooth and tame frizz. Apply a small amount of serum to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  • Leave-in conditioner: Leave-in conditioner is a great option for adding moisture and detangling frizzy hair. Apply a small amount to your hair, then comb through with a wide-toothed comb.

Step 3: Apply the Product

Once you've chosen your product, it's time to apply it to your hair. Start with a small amount of product and work it through your hair, focusing on the ends. Avoid applying product to your roots, as this can make your hair look greasy. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once you've applied the product, you can style your hair as usual. If you're using a hair oil, you may want to avoid using heat styling tools, as this can cause the oil to evaporate and leave your hair looking dull.

Tips for Taming Frizzy Hair

Here are a few additional tips for taming frizzy hair:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as this can help to minimize breakage.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel after washing, as this can cause frizz. Instead, gently squeeze out excess water with a towel.

FAQs

What causes frizzy hair?

Frizzy hair is often caused by a lack of moisture in the hair. Other factors that can contribute to frizz include humidity, heat styling tools, and chemical treatments.

Can I put oil on my hair after drying?

Yes, hair oil is a great option for taming frizzy hair after drying. Apply a small amount of oil to the ends of your hair, then work it through with your fingers.

Can I use a leave-in conditioner on frizzy hair?

Yes, leave-in conditioner is a great option for adding moisture and detangling frizzy hair. Apply a small amount to your hair, then comb through with a wide-toothed comb.

Hair Care Tips for Frizzy Hair

If you have frizzy hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips for caring for frizzy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too often, as this can cause breakage and make your hair more prone to frizz. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead.

Now that you know what to put on frizzy hair after drying, you can start taming your locks and keeping them looking smooth and shiny. Remember to choose the right product for your hair type, apply it evenly, and avoid overdoing it. With a little bit of care and attention, you can keep your frizzy hair under control and looking its best.
