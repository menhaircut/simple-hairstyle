My Hair Is Falling Out In Clumps After Covid 19 How To Cope

Written by Aprilia Sep 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're reading this, chances are you've recently recovered from COVID-19 and are now experiencing hair loss. You're not alone. Many people around the world have reported losing hair in clumps after battling the virus. This phenomenon is known as post-COVID hair loss and can be quite distressing. In this article, we'll discuss what causes hair loss after COVID-19, how to cope with it, and some tips for hair care.

What Causes Hair Loss After COVID-19?

One of the most significant factors that lead to post-COVID hair loss is stress. When the body is under stress, it produces cortisol, a hormone that can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss. COVID-19 can also cause inflammation in the body, which can affect hair growth. Additionally, many COVID-19 patients experienced high fevers, which can cause temporary hair loss.

How to Cope with Hair Loss After COVID-19

Dealing with hair loss after COVID-19 can be challenging, but there are things you can do to cope. Here are a few tips:

1. Give Your Hair a Break

If you're experiencing hair loss, it may be a good idea to give your hair a break. Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, and opt for gentle hair products that won't further damage your hair.

2. Get Enough Sleep

Getting enough sleep is essential for overall health and can help reduce stress levels. Try to get at least 7-8 hours of sleep each night.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help promote hair growth. Make sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

4. Manage Your Stress

Reducing stress levels is crucial for preventing hair loss. Try practicing relaxation techniques, such as meditation or yoga, to help manage stress.

5. Talk to Your Doctor

If you're concerned about your hair loss, talk to your doctor. They may be able to recommend treatments or supplements that can help promote hair growth.

Hair Care Tips for Post-COVID Hair Loss

If you're experiencing hair loss after COVID-19, there are some things you can do to help care for your hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a mild shampoo that won't further damage your hair. Look for shampoos that are free of sulfates and parabens.

2. Condition Your Hair

Conditioning your hair can help keep it healthy and prevent breakage. Choose a conditioner that's designed for your hair type.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as tight braids or ponytails. These can cause further damage to your hair.

4. Be Gentle When Brushing Your Hair

When brushing your hair, be gentle to prevent further damage. Use a wide-tooth comb or brush and avoid pulling on your hair.

FAQs

Q: Is hair loss after COVID-19 permanent?

A: In most cases, hair loss after COVID-19 is temporary and will grow back within a few months.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss. When the body is under stress, it produces cortisol, a hormone that can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Q: Are there any supplements that can help promote hair growth?

A: Yes, there are supplements that can help promote hair growth, such as biotin and vitamin D.

Conclusion

Dealing with hair loss after COVID-19 can be challenging, but there are things you can do to cope. Give your hair a break, get enough sleep, eat a healthy diet, manage your stress, and talk to your doctor if you're concerned. By following these tips and caring for your hair, you'll be on your way to regaining your locks in no time.
