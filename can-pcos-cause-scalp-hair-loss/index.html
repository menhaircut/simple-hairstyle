Can Pcos Cause Scalp Hair Loss

Written by Frank Aug 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common hormonal disorder that affects about 10% of women of reproductive age. One of the most distressing symptoms of PCOS is hair loss. Hair loss can occur on the scalp, face, and body, and it can be a significant source of anxiety and frustration for many women who have this condition.

What is PCOS?

PCOS is a hormonal disorder that affects a woman's menstrual cycle, fertility, and overall health. Women with PCOS have higher levels of androgens (male hormones) than normal, which can cause a range of symptoms, including:

  • Irregular periods
  • Acne
  • Weight gain
  • Hirsutism (excess hair growth on the face and body)
  • Scalp hair loss

How Does PCOS Cause Scalp Hair Loss?

PCOS can cause scalp hair loss in several ways. One of the main mechanisms is through the overproduction of androgens, such as testosterone. High levels of androgens can lead to a condition called androgenic alopecia, which is characterized by thinning hair on the crown of the head and a receding hairline.

In addition to androgenic alopecia, PCOS can also cause scalp hair loss through insulin resistance. Insulin resistance is a common feature of PCOS, and it can lead to high levels of insulin in the blood. These high levels of insulin can cause inflammation in the hair follicles, which can lead to hair loss.

How to Manage Scalp Hair Loss with PCOS

If you are experiencing scalp hair loss due to PCOS, there are several steps you can take to manage the condition:

1. Consult with a Healthcare Professional

If you have not already done so, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional who specializes in PCOS. They can help you understand the underlying causes of your hair loss and recommend appropriate treatments.

2. Maintain a Healthy Lifestyle

A healthy lifestyle can help manage the symptoms of PCOS, including scalp hair loss. This includes maintaining a healthy weight, eating a balanced diet, and getting regular exercise.

3. Use Hair Loss Treatments

There are several hair loss treatments that can help manage scalp hair loss due to PCOS. These include:

  • Topical minoxidil
  • Oral finasteride
  • Low-level laser therapy
  • Hair transplant surgery

4. Consider Hormone Therapy

Hormone therapy, such as birth control pills or anti-androgen medications, can help manage the hormonal imbalances associated with PCOS and may help reduce scalp hair loss.

5. Practice Good Hair Care

Practicing good hair care can help minimize further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. This includes using gentle shampoos, avoiding harsh chemical treatments, and avoiding hairstyles that pull on the hair.

FAQs

Can PCOS cause permanent hair loss?

In some cases, PCOS-related hair loss can be permanent. However, with appropriate treatment, it is often possible to manage the condition and promote healthy hair growth.

Can diet help with PCOS-related hair loss?

A healthy diet can help manage the symptoms of PCOS, including hair loss. Eating a balanced diet that is rich in nutrients and low in processed foods may help promote healthy hair growth.

Is hair transplant surgery a good option for PCOS-related hair loss?

Hair transplant surgery can be a good option for women with PCOS-related hair loss who have not responded to other treatments. However, it is important to discuss the risks and benefits of this procedure with a healthcare professional before making a decision.

Conclusion

Scalp hair loss is a common symptom of PCOS, but it can be managed with appropriate treatment. If you are experiencing scalp hair loss due to PCOS, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional and explore your treatment options. With the right approach, it is often possible to promote healthy hair growth and regain your confidence.
