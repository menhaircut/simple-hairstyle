How To Cut A Wavy Bob Haircut Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 26, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a chic and low-maintenance haircut, a wavy bob might be just what you need. This trendy haircut is perfect for those who want to show off their natural waves while keeping their hair short and stylish. In this article, we'll show you how to cut a wavy bob haircut in easy steps.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut a Wavy Bob Haircut
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting a Wavy Bob Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for a Wavy Bob Haircut

How to Cut a Wavy Bob Haircut

The wavy bob is a versatile haircut that can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair types. Here's what you need to know before you start cutting:

  • Choose the right length: The length of your wavy bob will depend on your face shape, hair type, and personal preference. Generally, a wavy bob falls between the chin and shoulder.
  • Decide on the layers: Layers can add volume, movement, and texture to your wavy bob. You can opt for long layers or choppy layers depending on your hair type and desired look.
  • Prepare your tools: You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you're ready to start cutting, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use your comb to section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Clip each section away.

Step 3: Cut the back section

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to cut the hair to your desired length. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head.

Step 4: Cut the side sections

Move on to the side sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Cut the hair to the same length as the back section. Repeat this process for each section of hair on both sides.

Step 5: Cut the top section

Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Cut the hair to your desired length, making sure to blend it with the rest of your hair. Repeat this process for each section of hair on the top of your head.

Tips for Cutting a Wavy Bob Haircut

Here are some tips to help you get the perfect wavy bob:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. This will help you achieve an even cut.
  • Use the point-cutting technique to create a softer, more textured look.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take off a little and then cut more if needed.
  • Use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your wavy bob.

FAQs

How often do I need to trim my wavy bob?

You should trim your wavy bob every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my wavy bob myself?

We recommend that you visit a professional hairstylist to cut your wavy bob. However, if you have experience cutting hair, you can try doing it yourself.

Can I style my wavy bob differently?

Yes, you can style your wavy bob in different ways depending on the occasion. You can wear it straight, curly, or in a messy bun.

Hair Care for a Wavy Bob Haircut

Here are some tips to keep your wavy bob looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and frizz-free.
  • Get regular trims to keep your wavy bob looking fresh and healthy.

Now that you know how to cut a wavy bob haircut, it's time to give it a try! With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a chic and low-maintenance hairstyle that's perfect for any occasion.
