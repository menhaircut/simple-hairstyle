How To Cut Natural Black Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending hours at the hair salon, waiting for your stylist to cut your natural black curly hair? Or maybe you're looking to save some money by giving yourself a DIY haircut at home. Whatever your reason may be, cutting natural black curly hair can be a daunting task. But fear not, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a fabulous haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut natural black curly hair.

Description

Natural black curly hair can be beautiful, but it can also be challenging to maintain. One of the biggest challenges is finding the right stylist who understands how to cut curly hair. Many stylists are not trained to work with curly hair, which can result in uneven and unflattering haircuts. That's why it's essential to know how to cut your hair yourself or find a stylist who specializes in cutting curly hair. In this article, we will provide a comprehensive guide on how to cut natural black curly hair.

How to Cut Natural Black Curly Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting natural black curly hair is to gather your tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.

Step 2: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's essential to wash and condition it. This will make it easier to detangle and section your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for curly hair to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

Step 3: Detangle and Section Your Hair

After washing and conditioning your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it. Then, separate your hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to cut your hair in even sections.

Step 4: Start Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. Take one section of your hair and trim the ends using your scissors. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up, cutting small sections at a time. Make sure you cut your hair evenly and avoid cutting too much at once. Repeat this process for each section of your hair.

Step 5: Check Your Haircut

After cutting all sections of your hair, check your haircut in the mirror. Make sure your hair is even and the length is what you desired. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim the sections that need it.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your haircut, it's time to style your hair. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and add moisture. You can also use a diffuser on your hair dryer to dry your hair and enhance your curls.

Tips for Cutting Natural Black Curly Hair

Cutting natural black curly hair can be tricky, but with these tips, you can achieve a fabulous haircut:

  • Start small and cut less hair than you think you need to. You can always go back and trim more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry to see the true length of your hair.
  • Section your hair to ensure an even haircut.
  • Check your hair in the mirror frequently to avoid overcutting.

FAQs

Can I cut my natural black curly hair when it's wet?

It's recommended to cut natural black curly hair when it's dry to see the true length of your hair. However, if you prefer to cut your hair when it's wet, make sure to cut less hair than you think you need to avoid overcutting.

How often should I cut my natural black curly hair?

It's recommended to cut your natural black curly hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy ends and promote hair growth.

Hair Care for Natural Black Curly Hair

To keep your natural black curly hair healthy and beautiful, it's essential to follow a hair care routine. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and add moisture.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as relaxers or perms, on your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain healthy ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a fabulous haircut for your natural black curly hair. Remember to take your time, start small, and check your haircut frequently to avoid overcutting. With these tips, you can save time and money by cutting your hair at home and still achieve a beautiful and healthy hairstyle.
