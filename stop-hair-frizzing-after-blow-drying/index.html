Stop Hair Frizzing After Blow Drying Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jun 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your hair frizzing up after blow-drying? It can be frustrating to spend time and effort styling your hair, only to have it ruined by frizz. But don't worry, we've got some tips and tricks to help you stop hair frizzing after blow drying.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Hair Frizzing After Blow Drying
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Hair Frizz
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products to Prevent Frizz

How to Stop Hair Frizzing After Blow Drying

Before getting into the details, it's important to understand what causes hair frizz. Frizz occurs when the hair cuticles are lifted and exposed to moisture in the air. Blow-drying can contribute to frizz, as the high heat can dry out the hair and cause the cuticles to lift. Here are some ways to prevent hair frizz:

1. Use a Heat Protectant

Before blow-drying your hair, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. This will help to protect your hair from the high heat of the blow dryer and prevent damage that can lead to frizz. Make sure to evenly distribute the product throughout your hair.

2. Use the Right Brush

The type of brush you use can also contribute to frizz. Avoid using plastic or metal brushes, as they can create static electricity that leads to frizz. Instead, use a natural bristle brush that will help to distribute the oils in your hair and smooth the cuticles.

3. Use the Right Hair Dryer

Investing in a high-quality hair dryer can make a big difference in preventing frizz. Look for a dryer with a diffuser attachment, which will help to distribute the heat evenly and prevent hot spots that can lead to frizz. Also, choose a dryer with adjustable heat and speed settings, so you can use a lower heat and speed setting to prevent damage to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to prevent frizz after blow-drying your hair:

1. Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair to remove excess water, then apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair.

2. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections, using hair clips or ties to keep the sections separate. This will make it easier to blow dry your hair evenly.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a natural bristle brush and a high-quality hair dryer with a diffuser attachment, start blow-drying your hair. Use a low heat and speed setting, and work from the roots to the ends of your hair. Continue until your hair is completely dry.

4. Finish with a Cold Shot

After blow-drying your hair, finish with a blast of cold air from your hair dryer. This will help to seal the hair cuticles and prevent frizz.

Tips for Preventing Hair Frizz

In addition to the steps above, here are some additional tips to prevent hair frizz:

1. Don't Over-Dry Your Hair

Blow-drying your hair for too long can dry out your hair, causing the cuticles to lift and leading to frizz. Make sure to only blow dry your hair until it's just dry, then let your hair air dry the rest of the way.

2. Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

Touching your hair too much can cause the cuticles to lift and lead to frizz. Avoid touching your hair too much, and try to keep your hands away from your hair as much as possible.

3. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent frizz, as the smooth surface of the silk won't create friction that can lead to frizz.

FAQs

1. Can I prevent frizz without blow-drying my hair?

Yes, you can prevent frizz without blow-drying your hair. Try air-drying your hair, or using a towel to gently blot your hair dry instead of rubbing it vigorously.

2. Can I use a hair straightener to prevent frizz?

While a hair straightener can help to smooth your hair and prevent frizz, it can also damage your hair if used too often. Make sure to use a heat protectant and only use a hair straightener occasionally.

Hair Care Products to Prevent Frizz

Here are some hair care products that can help to prevent frizz:

1. Moroccan Oil

Moroccan oil is a great product for preventing frizz, as it helps to nourish and moisturize your hair while also smoothing the cuticles.

2. Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair moisturized and prevent frizz. Look for a leave-in conditioner that is lightweight and won't weigh down your hair.

3. Frizz Control Serum

A frizz control serum can help to smooth your hair and prevent frizz, while also adding shine to your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can prevent hair frizzing after blow drying and enjoy smooth, silky hair every day.
