What Serious Illnesses Cause Hair Loss

Written by Oliver Oct 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you been noticing more hair fall than usual? Are you experiencing a thinning hairline or bald patches? Hair loss can be a sign of serious illnesses that require medical attention. In this article, we will explore the various serious illnesses that cause hair loss and how to manage it.

Table of Content:

  1. Cancer
  2. Thyroid Disorders
  3. Lupus
  4. Diabetes
  5. Iron Deficiency Anemia
  6. Stress and Anxiety
  7. Medication
  8. Autoimmune Diseases
  9. Pregnancy
  10. Genetics

Cancer

Cancer and its treatments such as chemotherapy and radiation can cause hair loss. Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells, which include cancer cells and hair follicle cells. This results in hair loss, including eyebrows and eyelashes. Hair loss due to cancer treatment is usually temporary, and hair growth resumes after the treatment is over.

Thyroid Disorders

Thyroid disorders such as hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism can cause hair loss. The thyroid gland produces hormones that regulate metabolism, and an imbalance in these hormones can affect hair growth. Hair loss due to thyroid disorders is usually reversible with proper treatment and medication.

Lupus

Lupus is an autoimmune disease that affects various parts of the body, including the skin and hair. Hair loss due to lupus can be patchy or diffuse and can occur anywhere on the scalp. Proper medication and treatment can help manage hair loss due to lupus.

Diabetes

Diabetes can cause hair loss due to poor circulation and high blood sugar levels. High blood sugar levels can damage blood vessels that supply blood to the hair follicles, leading to hair loss. Proper management of diabetes can help prevent hair loss.

Iron Deficiency Anemia

Iron deficiency anemia is a condition where the body lacks sufficient iron to produce hemoglobin, which carries oxygen to the body's tissues. Hair follicles require oxygen to grow, and a lack of oxygen can lead to hair loss. Proper supplementation and diet can help manage hair loss due to iron deficiency anemia.

Stress and Anxiety

Stress and anxiety can cause hair loss by disrupting the hair growth cycle. Stress can push hair follicles into a resting phase, leading to hair loss. Proper stress management and relaxation techniques can help prevent hair loss due to stress and anxiety.

Medication

Medications such as antidepressants, blood thinners, and blood pressure drugs can cause hair loss as a side effect. Hair loss due to medication is usually reversible after the medication is stopped or changed.

Autoimmune Diseases

Autoimmune diseases such as alopecia areata and scleroderma can cause hair loss. Alopecia areata is a condition where the immune system attacks hair follicles, leading to hair loss, usually in patches. Scleroderma is a condition where the body's immune system attacks its own tissues, including the skin and hair. Proper medication and treatment can help manage hair loss due to autoimmune diseases.

Pregnancy

Pregnancy can cause hormonal changes that can affect hair growth. During pregnancy, high levels of estrogen can prolong the anagen or growth phase of the hair cycle, leading to thicker and fuller hair. However, after childbirth, hormone levels drop, and hair can enter the telogen or resting phase, leading to hair loss. Hair loss due to pregnancy is usually temporary and resolves on its own.

Genetics

Genetics can play a significant role in hair loss. Male and female pattern baldness are genetic conditions that affect hair growth in specific patterns. Male pattern baldness usually starts with a receding hairline, while female pattern baldness leads to thinning hair all over the scalp. Proper management and treatment can help slow down hair loss due to genetics.

Conclusion

Hair loss can be a sign of serious illnesses, and it's essential to seek medical attention if you notice excessive hair fall. Proper management and treatment of the underlying condition can help prevent or manage hair loss. Incorporating a healthy diet, stress management, and proper hair care can also promote hair growth and prevent hair loss.
