How To Check What Hairstyle Suits You

Written by Christine Nov 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your usual hairstyle and want to switch things up? Choosing a new hairstyle can be overwhelming, especially if you're not sure what will suit your face shape, hair type, and personal style. In this article, we'll guide you through how to check what hairstyle suits you, step by step. Whether you're looking for a drastic change or a subtle tweak, we've got you covered.

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding a hairstyle that suits you is to determine your face shape. There are six main face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and stand in front of a mirror. Use a measuring tape or ruler to measure the following:

  • Forehead: Measure across the widest part of your forehead, from one hairline to the other.
  • Cheekbones: Measure across your cheekbones, starting and ending at the pointiest part below the outer corners of your eyes.
  • Jawline: Measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jaw angles upward. Then, double that measurement.
  • Face length: Measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Once you have your measurements, use this guide to determine your face shape:

  • Round: Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width. Face length is shorter than the other dimensions.
  • Oval: Face length is greater than the width of the cheekbones, and forehead is greater than the jawline.
  • Square: Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width, and face length is relatively short.
  • Heart: Forehead is wider than the cheekbones and jawline, and the chin is pointed.
  • Diamond: Forehead and jawline are narrower than the cheekbones, and the chin is pointed.
  • Oblong: Face length is greater than the width of the cheekbones and forehead, and the jawline is rounded.

Knowing your face shape will help you choose a hairstyle that flatters your features and balances your proportions.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Type

Once you've determined your face shape, consider your hair type. Is it fine or thick? Straight or curly? Oily or dry? Knowing your hair type will help you choose a hairstyle that works with your natural texture and is easy to maintain.

If you have fine hair, avoid heavy, blunt cuts that can make your hair look flat and lifeless. Instead, opt for layers and texture that add volume and movement. If you have thick hair, a blunt cut can help control the volume and make your hair look sleek and polished. If you have curly hair, embrace your natural texture with a cut that works with your curls, such as long layers or a bob. If you have straight hair, a blunt cut or a sleek, straight style can be chic and sophisticated.

Step 3: Think About Your Lifestyle and Personal Style

Your hairstyle should reflect your personal style and lifestyle. If you have a busy schedule and don't have a lot of time to style your hair, choose a low-maintenance cut that you can easily wash and go. If you work in a conservative environment, opt for a classic, polished style. If you're more adventurous with your fashion choices, consider a bold, statement-making cut or color.

It's also important to consider your hair's natural texture and how it will behave in different weather conditions. If you live in a humid climate, for example, a sleek, straight style may be difficult to maintain. Instead, embrace your natural texture with a curly or wavy style.

Step 4: Use Virtual Try-On Tools

If you're still not sure what hairstyle suits you, try using virtual try-on tools. Many hairstyling apps and websites allow you to upload a photo of yourself and try on different hairstyles virtually. This can give you a better idea of how a certain cut or color will look on you without committing to it.

Tips for Finding Your Perfect Hairstyle

  • Bring photos to your hairstylist: If you have a specific style in mind, bring photos to your hairstylist to show them what you're looking for.
  • Consider your hair's growth pattern: If you have cowlicks or other natural quirks in your hair, choose a style that works with them rather than against them.
  • Try temporary changes: If you're not ready for a permanent change, try clip-in extensions or temporary hair color to experiment with different looks.
  • Be realistic: Choose a style that you can realistically maintain and style on a daily basis.

FAQs

What haircut suits a round face?

A round face looks best with a cut that adds height and lengthens the face, such as a long layered cut or a side-swept fringe.

What haircut suits an oval face?

An oval face can pull off almost any hairstyle, but a middle part and long layers can accentuate your face shape.

What haircut suits a square face?

A square face looks best with a cut that softens the angles of the face, such as a long layered cut with side-swept bangs.

Hair Care Tips for Your New Hairstyle

Once you've found your perfect hairstyle, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips for your new 'do:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for your hair type.
  • Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools every day, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to find a hairstyle that suits you and keep it looking great. Happy hairstyling!
