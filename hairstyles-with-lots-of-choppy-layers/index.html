Hairstyles With Lots Of Choppy Layers A Trendy Look For 2023

If you're looking for a hairstyle that's both edgy and chic, then you might want to try out hairstyles with lots of choppy layers. This trend has been around for a while now, and it's still going strong in 2023. In this article, we'll explore everything you need to know about this style, including how to achieve it, tips to maintain it, and more. So, let's dive in!

How to Achieve Hairstyles with Lots of Choppy Layers

Hairstyles with lots of choppy layers are characterized by their uneven and textured look. This style works for all hair types, from thick to thin, and it's perfect for those who want to add some movement and dimension to their hair. To achieve this style, you'll need to follow these steps:

1. Choose Your Length

The first step in achieving a choppy layered look is to decide on your desired length. This style works for short, medium, and long hair, but keep in mind that the longer your hair, the more difficult it is to achieve a choppy look. If you have long hair, you may need to cut off some length before adding the layers.

2. Decide on Your Layers

The next step is to decide on the number and thickness of your layers. Generally, the more layers you have, the choppier your hair will look. However, you don't want to go overboard with the layers, as this can make your hair look too thin and stringy. Your hairstylist can help you determine the best number of layers for your hair type and face shape.

3. Add Texture

To achieve a choppy look, you'll need to add texture to your hair. This can be done through various techniques, such as razor cutting or point cutting. Your hairstylist can help you determine which technique will work best for your hair type and desired look.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you want to try achieving a choppy layered look at home, here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair before cutting. This will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair, which will make it easier to cut.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will help you cut your hair evenly.

3. Cut the Bottom Layer

Start by cutting the bottom layer of your hair. This layer should be the longest, and it will serve as the foundation for the rest of your layers.

4. Create the Middle Layer

Next, create the middle layer by cutting your hair at a slightly shorter length than the bottom layer. This will create a layered effect.

5. Finish with the Top Layer

Finally, cut the top layer to the desired length. This layer should be the shortest, and it will add texture and movement to your hair.

Tips to Maintain Your Choppy Layers

Once you've achieved your desired choppy layered look, it's important to maintain it properly. Here are a few tips:

1. Use the Right Products

Use products that will enhance the texture and movement of your hair, such as sea salt spray or texturizing spray. Avoid heavy products that will weigh down your hair and make it look flat.

2. Get Regular Trims

To keep your layers looking fresh and edgy, get regular trims every 6-8 weeks. This will also prevent split ends and breakage.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Using heat styling tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, can damage your hair and make it look frizzy. Use a heat protectant spray before styling to prevent damage.

FAQ

What face shapes work best with choppy layers?

Choppy layers work well with all face shapes, but they're particularly flattering for those with round or square faces. The layers will help frame your face and soften your features.

Can I achieve a choppy layered look with curly hair?

Absolutely! In fact, curly hair can look particularly stunning with lots of choppy layers. Your hairstylist can help you determine the best way to cut your curly hair to achieve a choppy look.

Hair Care Tips for Choppy Layers

Here are a few additional tips to keep your choppy layers looking their best:

1. Wash Your Hair Less Frequently

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils and make it look dull. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, and use a dry shampoo in between washes if needed.

2. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Choppy layers can make your hair look dry and damaged, especially if you use heat styling tools. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and healthy.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can damage your hair and make it look frizzy and dry. Use a hat or a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve and maintain a trendy and edgy look with hairstyles with lots of choppy layers. So, go ahead and give it a try!
