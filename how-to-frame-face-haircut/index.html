How To Frame Face Haircut Creating A Flattering Look

Are you tired of your hair just hanging around your face with no shape or structure? A framed face haircut can give your locks the dimension and style they need to really pop. Not sure where to start? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll go over how to frame face haircut, step by step. We'll also provide tips and FAQs to make sure you get the best results.

Table of Contents

  • How to Frame Face Haircut
  • Step by Step

How to Frame Face Haircut

A framed face haircut is all about creating angles and structure around the face. It involves cutting layers that start at the jawline or cheekbones and angle down towards the ends of the hair. This creates a flattering look that highlights your best features.

When getting a framed face haircut, it's important to consider your face shape. Different face shapes require different types of framing. For example, a round face might benefit from longer layers that start at the chin, while a square face might look best with shorter layers that start at the cheekbones.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve a framed face haircut:

  1. Consult with your hairstylist about the best framing for your face shape.
  2. Start with clean, dry hair.
  3. Section off the top layer of your hair and clip it out of the way.
  4. Take a section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down.
  5. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the jawline or cheekbones and angling downward towards the ends.
  6. Continue cutting sections of hair, working your way around the head.
  7. Release the top layer of hair and repeat the process, cutting at the same angle as the bottom layer.
  8. Blend the layers together by combing through the hair and cutting any uneven pieces.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when getting a framed face haircut:

  • Bring pictures of haircuts you like to show your hairstylist.
  • Consider getting highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to ask your hairstylist to make adjustments if you're not happy with the initial cut.
  • Regular trims are important to maintain the shape of your framing.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about framing face haircuts:

How often should I get a trim?

You should get a trim every six to eight weeks to maintain the shape of your framing.

Can I still wear my hair up?

A framed face haircut can be styled in a variety of ways, including updos. Talk to your hairstylist about the best updo options for your hair length and framing.

Will a framed face haircut work with curly hair?

A framed face haircut can work with any hair texture. Your hairstylist can adjust the angle of the layers to suit your curly locks.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the shape and health of your framing. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

With these tips and steps, you'll be able to achieve a gorgeous, flattering framed face haircut that will turn heads. Remember to communicate with your hairstylist and take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
