  • Hydrating ingredients: Look for shampoos that contain moisturizing ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil. These ingredients will help keep your hair hydrated, which is key for combating frizz.
  • Sulfate-free: Sulfates are harsh detergents that can strip curly hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and frizz. Look for shampoos that are sulfate-free.

Step-by-Step Guide to Washing Curly Frizzy Hair

  1. Prep your hair: Before getting in the shower, detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or brush to prevent further tangling and breakage.
  2. Wet your hair: Wet your hair thoroughly with warm water. Avoid using hot water, as this can dry out your hair and lead to frizz.
  3. Apply shampoo: Apply a quarter-sized amount of shampoo to your scalp and massage it in with your fingertips.
  4. Rinse: Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water to remove all shampoo.
  5. Apply conditioner: Apply a quarter-sized amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.
  6. Rinse: Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water to seal the cuticle and prevent frizz.

  8. Style: Apply your preferred styling products to damp hair and style as desired.

Tips for Caring for Curly Frizzy Hair

In addition to using the right shampoo and washing your hair properly, there are a few other tips to keep in mind when caring for your curly frizzy hair:

  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage curly hair and lead to frizz. Try to air dry your hair and avoid heat styling as much as possible.
  • Use a diffuser: If you must use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the heat evenly and reduce frizz.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase: Cotton pillowcases can absorb moisture from your hair, leading to frizz. Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help reduce frizz and prevent breakage.
  • Deep condition: Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to hydrate and nourish your hair.
  • Trim regularly: Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy and strong.

A: No, regular towels can cause friction and lead to frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry.
