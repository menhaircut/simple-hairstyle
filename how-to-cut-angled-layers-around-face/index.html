How To Cut Angled Layers Around Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 02, 2023 · 5 min read

Cutting angled layers around the face is a great way to add dimension and movement to your hair. Whether you have long or short hair, this technique can help frame your face and give you a fresh new look. In this article, we will guide you through the steps of cutting angled layers around the face, as well as provide tips and answers to common questions.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Angled Layers Around Face
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Angled Layers Around Face
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips

How to Cut Angled Layers Around Face

Cutting angled layers around the face involves creating layers that are shorter at the front and longer towards the back. This technique is great for adding volume and movement to your hair, as well as framing your face. Here are the steps you can follow to achieve this look:

Step 1: Prepare Your Equipment

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary equipment. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. It is also recommended to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place. This will make it easier for you to cut your hair without getting confused about which section you are working on.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how short or long you want your layers to be. Keep in mind that the shorter the layers, the more volume and movement you will have. Use a comb to separate the section of hair you want to cut, then hold it between your index and middle fingers at the length you desire.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Start by cutting the hair at the front of your face. Hold the section of hair in front of your face, then cut it at an angle towards your chin. This will create layers that frame your face. Work your way towards the back, cutting each section of hair at the same angle. Repeat this process on the other side of your face.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

After you have cut all the sections of hair, blend the layers by cutting the ends of your hair at a slight angle. This will create a seamless transition between the layers and give you a natural look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide to cutting angled layers around the face:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back.
  3. Decide how short or long you want your layers to be.
  4. Use a comb to separate the section of hair you want to cut, then hold it between your index and middle fingers at the length you desire.
  5. Cut the hair at the front of your face at an angle towards your chin.
  6. Work your way towards the back, cutting each section of hair at the same angle.
  7. Repeat this process on the other side of your face.
  8. Blend the layers by cutting the ends of your hair at a slight angle.
  9. Use a mirror to check the back of your hair and make any necessary adjustments.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Angled Layers Around Face

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting angled layers around the face:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you are unsure about cutting your own hair.
  • If you have curly hair, cut your hair when it is dry to avoid any surprises.
  • Take your time and be patient. Rushing the process can lead to uneven layers.

FAQs

Can I cut angled layers around my face if I have short hair?

Yes, you can cut angled layers around your face regardless of your hair length. Just make sure to adjust the length of your layers accordingly.

What is the best way to maintain angled layers?

To maintain your angled layers, trim your hair every 6-8 weeks. This will prevent split ends and keep your layers looking fresh.

Should I cut my hair when it is wet or dry?

If you have straight hair, it is best to cut your hair when it is wet. If you have curly hair, it is best to cut your hair when it is dry.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting angled layers around your face, it is important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips to help you maintain your hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suited for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

Cutting angled layers around the face is a great way to add dimension and movement to your hair. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a gorgeous new look that frames your face and adds volume to your hair. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
