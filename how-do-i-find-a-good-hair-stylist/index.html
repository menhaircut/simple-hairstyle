How Do I Find A Good Hair Stylist

Written by Frank Nov 12, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking for a new hair stylist, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. With so many options out there, it's important to know how to find a good hair stylist that will give you the look you want. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for finding the perfect stylist for you.

Step 1: Ask for Recommendations

The first step in finding a good hair stylist is to ask for recommendations from people you know and trust. This could be friends, family members, coworkers, or even strangers with great hair that you compliment. Ask them who they go to for their hair and if they've had a good experience.

Once you have some recommendations, do your own research. Look up the stylist's website or social media pages to get a better sense of their style and expertise.

Step 2: Check Online Reviews

Online reviews can be a great resource for finding a good hair stylist. Check websites like Yelp, Google, or Facebook for reviews from past clients. Look for stylists with high ratings and positive comments about their skills, communication, and customer service.

Keep in mind that not all reviews are created equal. Look for reviews that are detailed and specific, rather than generic or brief. Also, pay attention to how the stylist responds to negative reviews. Do they take responsibility and try to make things right, or do they dismiss the client's concerns?

Step 3: Look at Their Portfolio

A good hair stylist should have a portfolio of their work that showcases their skills and creativity. Look for photos of clients with hair types and styles similar to yours. This will give you a better idea of what the stylist is capable of and whether their aesthetic matches your own.

If the stylist doesn't have a portfolio, ask to see some before-and-after photos of their clients. This can give you a sense of their ability to transform hair.

Step 4: Schedule a Consultation

Before committing to a new stylist, schedule a consultation to discuss your hair goals and get a sense of their communication style. This is also a good opportunity to ask questions about their training, experience, and approach to hair care.

During the consultation, pay attention to how the stylist listens to you and whether they ask questions to clarify your needs. A good stylist should be able to offer suggestions and advice based on your hair type, face shape, and personal style.

Step 5: Ask About Their Experience and Training

When choosing a hair stylist, it's important to consider their experience and training. Ask about their education and any specialized certifications or training they've received. This can give you a sense of their level of expertise and whether they're up-to-date on the latest trends and techniques.

It's also a good idea to ask how long they've been working as a stylist and what types of clients they typically work with. If you have curly hair, for example, you may want to find a stylist who has experience working with textured hair.

Step 6: Consider the Salon's Atmosphere

The salon's atmosphere can play a big role in your overall experience. Look for a salon that feels clean, welcoming, and comfortable. Consider the lighting, music, and overall vibe of the space. If you feel relaxed and at ease, you're more likely to enjoy your appointment.

If you're unsure about a salon, ask to take a tour before scheduling an appointment. This can give you a better sense of the space and whether it feels like a good fit for you.

Step 7: Pay Attention to Their Communication Skills

Good communication is key to a successful stylist-client relationship. Look for a stylist who is attentive, listens to your needs, and responds to your questions and concerns. They should be able to explain their techniques and products in a way that you can understand.

If the stylist seems dismissive or uninterested in your needs, it may be a sign that they're not the right fit for you.

Step 8: Discuss Pricing and Payment

Pricing can vary widely between salons and stylists. Before scheduling an appointment, make sure you understand the stylist's pricing structure and what services are included. Ask about any additional fees for things like blowouts or deep conditioning treatments.

It's also important to discuss payment options. Find out whether the salon accepts credit cards or if you'll need to bring cash. Make sure you understand the salon's cancellation policy as well.

Step 9: Trust Your Gut

Ultimately, choosing a hair stylist is a personal decision. Trust your instincts and choose a stylist who makes you feel comfortable and confident. If you feel uneasy or unsure about a stylist, keep looking until you find someone who feels like a good fit.

FAQs

How often should I get my hair cut?

How often you get your hair cut depends on your hair type and the style you're going for. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep hair healthy and prevent split ends.

What should I bring to my hair appointment?

Bring any inspiration photos or examples of hairstyles you like. You may also want to bring your own styling products or tools if you have specific preferences.

How can I maintain my hair between salon appointments?

Maintaining healthy hair between appointments is key to achieving your desired look. Use high-quality hair products and avoid over-styling or using heat tools too often. Regular trims and deep conditioning treatments can also help keep hair healthy and strong.

Hair Care Tips

Maintaining healthy hair is an ongoing process. Here are some tips to keep your locks looking their best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid over-styling or using heat tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and strengthen hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.
