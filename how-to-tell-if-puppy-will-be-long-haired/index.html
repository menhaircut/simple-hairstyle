How To Tell If Your Puppy Will Be Long Haired

Written by Daniel Sep 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're considering getting a new puppy, one of the factors you might be considering is whether or not they will have long hair. Long-haired dogs can be beautiful and fluffy, but they also require more grooming and maintenance than short-haired breeds. In this article, we'll discuss how to tell if your puppy will be long haired, and provide tips for caring for their coat.

Table of Contents

  • How to Tell if Your Puppy Will Be Long Haired
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Caring for Your Puppy's Hair
  • FAQ

How to Tell if Your Puppy Will Be Long Haired

There are a few ways to determine if your puppy will have long hair:

1. Look at the Breed

Some dog breeds are known for having long hair, such as the Shih Tzu, Maltese, and Yorkshire Terrier. If you know the breed of your puppy, you can research whether or not they typically have long hair.

2. Look at the Puppy's Parents

If you're getting a puppy from a breeder, you can ask to see the puppy's parents. If the parents have long hair, there's a good chance the puppy will as well.

3. Look at the Puppy's Fur

Finally, you can examine the puppy's fur. Long-haired puppies will typically have longer, softer fur than short-haired puppies. You can also look for feathering on their legs, ears, and tail.

Step by Step Guide

If you're still unsure whether or not your puppy will have long hair, follow these steps:

Step 1: Examine the Fur

Look at the length and texture of your puppy's fur. Long-haired puppies will have longer, softer fur than short-haired puppies.

Step 2: Look for Feathering

Check for feathering on your puppy's legs, ears, and tail. This is another indication that they may have long hair.

Step 3: Research the Breed

If you know the breed of your puppy, research whether or not they typically have long hair. This can give you a better idea of what to expect.

Tips for Caring for Your Puppy's Hair

If your puppy does have long hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

1. Brush Their Hair Regularly

Long-haired dogs need to be brushed regularly to prevent matting and tangling. Aim to brush your puppy's hair at least once a week, if not more.

2. Give Them Regular Baths

Long-haired dogs can get dirty easily, so it's important to give them regular baths. Aim to bathe your puppy every 2-3 months, or more often if they get particularly dirty.

3. Trim Their Hair as Needed

Long-haired dogs may need to have their hair trimmed occasionally to prevent it from getting too long and unmanageable. Take your puppy to a professional groomer for a trim if needed.

FAQ

Q: Can a short-haired puppy's fur grow longer over time?

A: No, a puppy's fur will not change length as they grow older. If a puppy has short hair, it will stay short.

Q: Can you predict exactly how long a puppy's hair will be?

A: No, there's no way to predict the exact length of a puppy's hair. However, by examining their fur and researching their breed, you can get a good idea of what to expect.

Q: Can long-haired dogs be hypoallergenic?

A: Yes, some long-haired dog breeds are hypoallergenic. However, it's important to note that all dogs produce dander, which is what triggers allergies. So even if a dog is hypoallergenic, they may still cause allergies in some people.

Conclusion

By examining your puppy's fur, researching their breed, and looking at their parents, you can get a good idea of whether or not they will have long hair. If they do, make sure to take good care of their coat by brushing and bathing them regularly, and taking them to a professional groomer as needed.
