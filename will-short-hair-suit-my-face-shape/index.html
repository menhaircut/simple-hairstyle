Will Short Hair Suit My Face Shape A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're considering a change in hairstyle, you may be wondering if short hair will suit your face shape. It's a common concern, as the wrong haircut can leave you feeling less confident and less like yourself. But don't worry, we've got you covered! This guide will help you determine if short hair is right for you, and provide tips for finding the perfect cut.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in determining if short hair will suit your face shape is to figure out what your face shape actually is. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back away from your face and examine the shape of your jawline, cheekbones, and forehead.

If your face is roughly the same width at the forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, you likely have an oval face shape. If your face is widest at the cheekbones and has a rounded chin and forehead, you have a round face shape. If your face is roughly the same width at the forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, but has a strong, angular jawline, you have a square face shape. If your face is widest at the forehead and gradually narrows at the jawline, you have a heart face shape. If your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at both the forehead and jawline, you have a diamond face shape. If your face is longer than it is wide and has a long, straight jawline, you have an oblong face shape.

Is Short Hair Right for You?

Now that you know your face shape, it's time to decide if short hair is right for you. The answer depends on a few factors. First, consider your hair texture. If you have fine, thin hair, short hair may not be the best choice, as it can make your hair look even thinner. If you have thick, heavy hair, short hair can help lighten the load and make your hair more manageable.

Next, think about your personal style. Are you someone who likes to experiment with new trends and looks, or do you prefer a classic, timeless style? Short hair can be a bold statement, so it's important to make sure it aligns with your personal style and the image you want to project.

Short Hairstyles for Different Face Shapes

Once you've determined that short hair is right for you, it's time to find the perfect cut. Here are some guidelines for short hairstyles that flatter different face shapes:

  • Oval: Almost any short hairstyle will suit an oval face shape, as this shape is considered the most versatile.
  • Round: A short, layered bob can help elongate the face and create a more angular look.
  • Square: Soft, wispy layers around the face can help soften the angles of a square face shape.
  • Heart: A chin-length bob with layers that start at the jawline can help balance a heart-shaped face.
  • Diamond: A pixie cut with volume on top can help balance a diamond-shaped face.
  • Oblong: A short, textured bob can help add width to an oblong face shape.

Tips for Choosing the Perfect Cut

When choosing a short haircut, it's important to consider not just your face shape, but also your hair texture and personal style. Here are some tips for finding the perfect cut:

  • Bring photos to your stylist to help communicate the look you want.
  • Consider your lifestyle and how much time you're willing to spend styling your hair each day.
  • Be open to suggestions from your stylist, who can help you tailor a haircut to your specific features and preferences.

FAQs about Short Hair and Face Shape

Will short hair make my face look rounder?

Not necessarily. A well-chosen short haircut can actually help elongate your face and create a more angular look. It's all about finding the right cut for your face shape.

Can I still wear a ponytail with short hair?

Depending on the length of your hair, you may still be able to pull it back into a small ponytail or bun. Talk to your stylist about options for styling your short hair.

Do I need to change my makeup if I get a short haircut?

Not necessarily, but it's a good idea to experiment with different makeup looks to find what works best with your new haircut. Short hair can draw more attention to your face, so you may want to play up your eyes or lips to create a balanced look.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Short hair requires different care than long hair, so it's important to adjust your hair care routine accordingly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every other day to avoid stripping it of natural oils.
  • Use a lightweight conditioner to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair texture.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your cut.

With these tips and guidelines, you can confidently determine if short hair will suit your face shape and find the perfect cut for you. Remember, the most important thing is to choose a haircut that makes you feel confident and beautiful!
