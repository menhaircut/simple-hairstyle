How To Cut Shoulder Length Hair Step By Step

Written by Christine Nov 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every time you need a haircut? Or are you looking for a way to save some money? Cutting your own hair may seem like a daunting task, but it is possible to achieve a salon-worthy haircut at home. In this guide, we will take you through the step-by-step process of how to cut shoulder length hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Shoulder length hair is a popular choice for many women because it is versatile and easy to style. However, regular haircuts are necessary to maintain the length and shape of the hair. If you are looking to cut your own shoulder length hair, it is important to have the right tools and techniques to achieve the desired result.

How To

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure that you have the following tools:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

It is also important to choose a well-lit area with a large mirror. You can even set up two mirrors to get a full view of your hair.

Step by Step

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and removing any tangles. Towel dry your hair and make sure that it is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Trim the Sides

Start with the sides of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Unclip the back section of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length. Make sure that the length is even on both sides.

Step 5: Trim the Top

Unclip the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length. Make sure that the length is even on both sides.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Use a mirror to check that the length of your hair is even on both sides. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 7: Finishing Touches

Use your scissors to add layers or texture to your hair as desired.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and adjust as necessary
  • Don't rush - take your time to ensure an even cut
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
  • Trim your hair when it is dry to get a more accurate length
  • Use hair clips to keep sections of hair out of the way

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair, but it is important to be cautious. Curly hair tends to shrink when it is dry, so make sure that you cut it longer than the desired length.

How often should I cut my shoulder length hair?

It is recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and shape of your hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, make sure to take good care of it to maintain its health and shine. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized. Avoid heat styling tools and use a heat protectant when necessary.

Cutting your own hair may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to achieve a salon-worthy haircut at home. Follow these step-by-step instructions and you'll be on your way to a new look in no time!
