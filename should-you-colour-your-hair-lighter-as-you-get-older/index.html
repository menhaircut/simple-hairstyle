Should You Colour Your Hair Lighter As You Get Older

Written by Eveline Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, our hair naturally starts to lose its pigment, resulting in grey or white hair. While some embrace their natural hair colour, others prefer to continue colouring their hair. In this article, we will discuss whether you should colour your hair lighter as you get older.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Colour Your Hair Lighter
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Colouring Your Hair Lighter
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Coloured Hair

Description

Many people believe that as they age, they should lighten their hair colour to look more youthful. While lighter hair can brighten up your face and give you a younger appearance, it's not always the best option for everyone.

When it comes to hair colour, it's essential to consider your skin tone, hair texture, and personal style. While some people can pull off blonde hair effortlessly, others may look better with darker hair colours.

How to Colour Your Hair Lighter

Colouring your hair lighter can be done at home or in a salon. If you're doing it at home, it's essential to choose the right hair dye and follow the instructions carefully. However, if you're uncertain, it's best to visit a professional hairdresser to avoid any mishaps.

It's also important to consider the health of your hair before colouring it. If your hair is damaged or brittle, it's best to avoid any harsh chemicals and wait until your hair is in better condition.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to colour your hair lighter:

  1. Choose the right hair dye for your hair type and colour.
  2. Wash your hair and dry it completely.
  3. Section your hair and apply the hair dye evenly, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Leave the hair dye on for the recommended time (usually 20-30 minutes).
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water and apply conditioner.
  6. Dry and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Colouring Your Hair Lighter

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when colouring your hair lighter:

  • Choose a hair colour that complements your skin tone.
  • Do a patch test before applying the hair dye to avoid any allergic reactions.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment before and after colouring your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair for 48 hours after colouring to allow the colour to set.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat styling tools to prevent damage and fading.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about colouring hair lighter:

  1. Will colouring my hair lighter damage it?
    Colouring your hair lighter can damage it, especially if you're using harsh chemicals or over-processing your hair. It's essential to use a gentle hair dye and follow the instructions carefully to avoid any damage.
  2. Can I colour my hair lighter at home?
    Yes, you can colour your hair lighter at home, but it's essential to choose the right hair dye and follow the instructions carefully. If you're uncertain, it's best to visit a professional hairdresser.
  3. How often should I colour my hair?
    It depends on your hair growth and the rate at which your hair colour fades. Generally, it's recommended to colour your hair every four to six weeks to maintain the colour.

Hair Care Tips for Coloured Hair

Here are some tips to help you care for your coloured hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for coloured hair to prevent fading.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair as it can strip the colour and dry out your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible and use a heat protectant spray when necessary.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

In conclusion, whether you should colour your hair lighter as you get older depends on your personal preference, hair type, and skin tone. If you decide to colour your hair lighter, it's essential to choose the right hair dye, follow the instructions carefully, and take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain the colour.
