How To Cut A Pixie Cut On Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be tricky to style, but when it comes to a pixie cut, it can look absolutely stunning. Pixie cuts are low-maintenance and perfect for those who want a chic and edgy look. In this guide, we'll teach you how to cut a pixie cut on curly hair, step-by-step.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut a Pixie Cut on Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that is usually cut to about an inch or two in length. It's a daring style that can accentuate your best features and make a bold statement. Cutting a pixie cut on curly hair can be a bit more challenging than straight hair, but with the right technique, it can be done easily.

How to Cut a Pixie Cut on Curly Hair

The first thing you'll need to do is decide on the length of your pixie cut. Keep in mind that curly hair will shrink when it dries, so it's better to go a bit longer than you think you want. Once you've decided on the length, it's time to gather your tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

It's best to start with clean, dry hair so you can see the natural texture and shape of your curls.

Step 2: Section your hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Use clips to keep each section separated.

Step 3: Cut the sides

Starting with the sides, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, following the shape of your head. Cut the hair in small sections until you've completed one side.

Step 4: Cut the back

Move on to the back section, and follow the same process as the sides. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, following the shape of your head.

Step 5: Cut the top

Finally, move on to the top section. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, following the shape of your head. Keep in mind that the top section should be slightly longer than the sides and back.

Tips for Cutting a Pixie Cut on Curly Hair

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Work in small sections to ensure evenness
  • Take your time and be patient
  • Don't cut too much at once
  • Use a spray bottle to keep hair damp while cutting

FAQs

Can I cut a pixie cut on my own?

While it's possible to cut a pixie cut on your own, we recommend going to a professional stylist who has experience with curly hair.

How often do I need to trim my pixie cut?

A pixie cut should be trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Will a pixie cut work with my face shape?

Pixie cuts can work with any face shape, but it's important to consult with a stylist to determine the best cut for your features.

Hair Care for Curly Pixie Cuts

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and hydrated. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping natural oils from your hair. Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define and moisturize your curls. Avoid using heat styling tools, and let your hair air dry whenever possible. Finally, schedule regular trims to keep your pixie cut looking sharp and stylish.

With this step-by-step guide, you can confidently cut a pixie cut on curly hair. Remember to be patient, take your time, and consult with a stylist if you're unsure. With the right technique and care, your curly pixie cut will be a head-turning style that you'll love.
